Siaya Governor James Orengo publicly confronted Homa Bay Governor Gladys Wanga during a recent regional event, criticizing her for her recent derogatory remarks toward Deputy President Rigathi Gachagua and warning her against assuming a self-appointed role as the dominant figure in Nyanza politics.

## James Orengo publicly rebukes Gladys Wanga over comments on Rigathi Gachagua

### The Old Guard versus the New Guard
The political landscape in Nyanza experienced a significant shift following a direct confrontation between the seasoned legal mind, James Orengo, and the Homa Bay governor. Known for his oratorical skills and deep-rooted influence, Orengo challenged Wanga to abandon her growing sense of entitlement. For many observers, this served as a classic example of a veteran statesman reminding a younger politician that political success should not be accompanied by arrogance or the unnecessary alienation of national leaders like the Deputy President.

### The Cost of Political Arrogance
The tension originated from Wanga’s recent verbal attacks against Rigathi Gachagua, which many political analysts labeled as unprovoked and beneath the dignity of her office. While younger politicians often view aggressive rhetoric as a sign of strength, Orengo characterized these comments as a reckless display of ego. His intervention highlighted the reality that in the high-stakes environment of government leadership, political power is often fleeting and dependent on maintaining strategic alliances rather than engaging in inflammatory personal disputes.

### A Lesson in Humility
Political insiders suggest that Orengo’s intervention was a calculated effort to maintain regional order and hierarchy. By publicly accusing Wanga of acting as though she owns the local political agenda, he sought to humble a leader who has gained significant influence due to her proximity to power. This rebuke underscores the importance of decorum among local leadership. As the government shifts its focus toward national economic development and internal growth, the message from the veteran governor is clear: political capital is a fragile resource that should not be squandered on petty insults.
