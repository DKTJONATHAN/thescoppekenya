Nollywood stars Funke Akindele and Toyin Abraham dominated industry conversations following a public incident at Iyabo Ojo's "The Return of Arinzo" premiere in Lagos on Sunday night, March 29, 2026. Akindele appeared to ignore Abraham's greeting in full view of cameras and guests, reigniting their long-standing rivalry ahead of the film's April 3 release.

Nollywood Fallout: Akindele and Abraham's Renewed Tensions

The Premiere Snub The highly anticipated premiere of Iyabo Ojo's film drew a significant gathering of Nollywood's elite, including Rita Dominic, Femi Adebayo, and Fathia Balogun. However, a 20-second clip from the event quickly went viral, showing Toyin Abraham approaching Funke Akindele, touching her arm, and leaning in to speak for several seconds. Akindele reportedly maintained a forward gaze, offering no visible acknowledgement of Abraham's greeting. Abraham then moved to greet others seated nearby before attempting a second interaction with Akindele, which was met with the same unresponsiveness. The tension was palpable even earlier in the evening, as Akindele and Mercy Aigbe, seated at the same table, reportedly arrived simultaneously without acknowledging each other.

Digital Aftermath and Historical Roots The incident rapidly escalated from the physical event to the digital realm. Following the premiere, Toyin Abraham unfollowed Funke Akindele on Instagram and posted, then deleted, a vow to never greet her again. Akindele responded by unfollowing Abraham, adding fuel to a rivalry that had shown signs of cooling as recently as September 2025 when they were observed interacting warmly at another premiere. Days before Sunday's confrontation, Akindele had publicly defended both herself and Abraham against online body-shaming, a gesture that now highlights the importance of fostering informed prevention against harmful online narratives. The deep-seated animosity between the two actresses sharpened publicly in 2021 during a box office clash involving Akindele's "Omo Ghetto: The Saga" and Abraham's "The Ghost and the Tout Too." Further tension arose around the "Anikulapo season 2" release, when filmmaker Kunle Afolayan publicly criticised Akindele. Abraham subsequently created a skit in solidarity with Afolayan, a move widely interpreted as aligning herself against Akindele and reinforcing a perception within industry circles that Akindele is often the difficult party in these recurring conflicts.

Industry and Public Reactions Online reactions to the latest incident have been sharply divided. Some observers have criticised Akindele for what they perceive as a public display of disrespect towards a colleague. Others have defended her right to personal boundaries, arguing that forced cordiality serves no one. Toyin Abraham, in contrast, has garnered widespread praise for her composed handling of the moment in real time. The events of Sunday night, March 29, 2026, have effectively reset any goodwill previously established between the two Nollywood giants, leaving the industry to speculate on the future of their tumultuous relationship.
