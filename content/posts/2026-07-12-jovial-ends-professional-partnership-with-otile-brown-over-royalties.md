### The Missing Split-Sheet Crisis

At the center of this controversy is the split-sheet agreement, a document that serves as the bedrock of any musical collaboration. In technical terms, a split-sheet is a legal record detailing exactly who wrote what percentage of a song, defining how royalties are divided among songwriters, producers, and performers. Without this signed documentation, artists are essentially building a house on quicksand. 

Jovial reports that she never received a copy of such an agreement during her collaborations with Otile Brown. This is a recurring ghost in the industry. Many musicians operate on mutual trust, assuming that verbal understandings or long-standing friendships suffice. However, when the revenue starts flowing from digital streaming platforms and performance rights organizations, that trust often evaporates under the weight of poor record-keeping. By speaking openly about the absence of signed paperwork, Jovial is exposing a vulnerability that affects countless other creatives who remain silent for fear of professional blacklisting.

### Protecting The Creative Future

Relying on an informal structure leaves creators with little recourse when royalty payments go missing or become subject to opaque accounting practices. 

When documentation is absent, the burden of proof falls on the artist, a difficult position to hold when the other party controls the publishing or distribution channels. The Kenya Copyright Board provides the regulatory framework to protect these rights, but the responsibility to initiate the paperwork rests with the talent.
