## Manchester United's Desperate Alex Scott Pursuit Manchester United to push for Alex Scott transfer is the central subject of the update.

Manchester United, fresh from missing out on multiple midfield targets, now reportedly pivot their attention to AFC Bournemouth's Alex Scott. This belated push suggests a reactive transfer strategy, compounded by an astronomical £80m asking price for a player who appears to be an alternative rather than a primary focus.

### A Strategy Built on Second Choices

United's pursuit of Scott follows a familiar pattern this summer: frantic reaction to missed opportunities. Having already secured Ederson Silva for £38m, the club's ambition for "at least one more midfielder" has clearly hit a few roadblocks. This reactionary approach comes after failed attempts to sign Elliot Anderson, Matheus Fernandes, and Sandro Tonali, with Fabrizio Romano explicitly naming Scott as an "alternative" for Michael Carrick's side. Such a scramble for second-choice options leaves many questioning United's strategic foresight - a concern not new to observers of their transfer dealings. For a deeper look into their wider midfield hunt, one might recall reports on potential [Fernandes alternatives Man Utd have considered](https://zandani.co.ke/article/man-utd-ready-to-sign-real-madrid-star-on-one-condition-as-ineos-turn-to-three-fernandes-alternatives) earlier this window.

### The Bournemouth Blockade and United's Wallet

Bournemouth, predictably, are playing hardball. Reports indicate they are demanding a staggering £80m for Scott, hoping to deter United and other interested parties like Arsenal. This valuation, for a player United are targeting as a clear alternative, speaks volumes about the desperation at Old Trafford. Paying such an inflated fee for a prospect who wasn't their initial priority is a dubious proposition, especially after committing £38m elsewhere. It's the kind of reactive overspending that has plagued United's transfer windows for years, suggesting little has changed in their negotiating tactics.

### Ageism in the Transfer Market

Adding another layer of questionable decision-making is United's apparent age policy. The club reportedly prefers targets aged between 22 and 26. While Scott fits this arbitrary window at 22, it also means potentially viable options are dismissed out of hand. Tyler Adams, for instance, a player mentioned as a "possible option," is seemingly overlooked because he "turns 28 next season." This rigid adherence to an age profile, particularly when already struggling to land primary targets, severely limits their options and could force them to overpay for players simply because they fit an arbitrary demographic, rather than being the best solution for the squad's immediate needs. It's less about building a cohesive team and more about ticking boxes.
