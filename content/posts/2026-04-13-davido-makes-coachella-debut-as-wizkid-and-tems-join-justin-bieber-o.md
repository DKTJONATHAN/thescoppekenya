Davido made his long-awaited Coachella debut on Saturday, April 11, delivering a 45-minute set on the Gobi Stage in Indio, California. Later that evening, Wizkid and Tems surprised fans by joining Justin Bieber on the main stage for the "Essence" remix, marking a notable evening for Nigerian music at the 2026 festival.

Nigerian Stars Shine at Coachella 2026 Davido's performance from 7:50 to 8:35 p.m. was a highlight, opening with "Dami Duro" and moving through a catalogue of hits supported by dancers and a live band. The set included tracks like "If," "Skelewu," and "With You," with Adekunle Gold joining for a medley that featured "High." This marked Davido's first appearance at the Coachella Valley Music and Arts Festival, which celebrates its 25th edition this year, running across two weekends from April 10-12 and April 17-19. Headliners for the festival include Sabrina Carpenter, Justin Bieber, and Karol G.

Wizkid and Tems' Surprise Appearance The excitement continued on the main stage when Wizkid and Tems joined Justin Bieber. Their performance of the "Essence" remix sent the crowd into a frenzy, particularly when Bieber announced Wizkid's arrival. Neither Wizkid nor Tems were on the official 2026 Coachella lineup, yet their unbilled appearance garnered some of the loudest reactions of the weekend. This unexpected collaboration showcased the global reach of African music.

Representation and Historical Context While Davido was the only Nigerian-based artist and the sole dedicated Afrobeats representative officially billed on the 2026 Coachella lineup, his debut adds to a rich history of Nigerian acts at the festival. Seun Kuti was the first Nigerian to perform at Coachella in 2012 with his father's band, Egypt 80. Burna Boy made his debut in 2019, followed by Mr Eazi that same year. CKay appeared at the 2022 edition, and DJ Spinall became the first Afrobeats DJ to perform in 2024, bringing out Fireboy DML and Teni. Tems and Rema also had dedicated sets in 2024 and 2025, respectively. By the time Davido stepped onto the Gobi Stage, Nigerian artists had already established a significant presence at the event. The contrast between Davido's solo billed slot and the unannounced, high-impact appearance of Wizkid and Tems with Bieber did not go unnoticed, sparking discussions about Nigerian representation at the festival. Davido is scheduled to return for his second weekend performance on Saturday, April 18. The global recognition of African talent extends beyond music, with Funke Akindele's film "Behind The Scenes" announced for a Netflix release, further amplifying the continent's vibrant entertainment sector.
