## NTSA records disprove Njugush asset transfer rumors after split

Social media users recently claimed comedian Njugush had moved his assets to his mother following his high profile separation from Celestine Ndinda. However, public vehicle registration records reveal these assertions are entirely baseless, confirming that the couple maintains shared ownership of their business property.

The Kenyan digital space is currently obsessed with the "Hakimi effect." Ever since the international footballer made headlines for his divorce proceedings, local audiences have developed a habit of projecting global celebrity drama onto every domestic breakup. When Njugush and Celestine Ndinda announced the end of their fourteen year relationship and eight year marriage last month, the internet immediately assumed a strategic asset shuffle. Keyboard warriors insisted the comedian had preemptively moved his fleet of buses to his mother to avoid a payout. 

### The paper trail vs the narrative

The speed at which misinformation travels is matched only by the persistence of the myths it creates. In this case, the viral claim suggested a classic "move" to shelter wealth. Yet, the reality stored in the National Transport and Safety Authority (NTSA) database tells a different story.

Records obtained by digital researchers show that the high capacity vehicles associated with the TTNT brand remain legally registered under the joint names of the former couple. The NTSA logs show no recent transfer of title, no sudden switch to a parent's name, and no divestment of interest. While the public wanted to see a calculated legal maneuver, the actual documents show a clean, shared business interest.

The timeline of this digital circus is telling:

*   **April 2026:** The pair publicly addresses their separation, citing the end of their marital journey after eight years of formal marriage and fourteen years of history together. 
*   **Early May 2026:** Anonymous accounts on social media platforms begin sharing screenshots and unverified claims, suggesting Njugush engaged in aggressive asset protection measures.
*   **May 08, 2026:** Verified NTSA ownership records hit the public domain via investigative reports, proving that the ownership status of their 33-seater bus fleet has remained unchanged and joint throughout the separation period.
