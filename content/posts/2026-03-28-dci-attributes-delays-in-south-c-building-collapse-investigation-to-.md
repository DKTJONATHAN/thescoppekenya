The Directorate of Criminal Investigations (DCI) on Saturday, March 28, 2026, attributed delays in submitting investigation files for the South C building collapse in Nairobi to the probe's inherent complexities. This follows strong criticism from the Office of the Director of Public Prosecutions (ODPP) regarding the slow pace of the inquiry.

Probe Complexity Cited The DCI's explanation came in response to allegations from Director of Public Prosecutions (DPP) Renson Ingonga, who blamed Inspector General of Police Douglas Kanja for failing to submit investigation findings. Ingonga stated that his office had issued repeated directives, including a '2nd Reminder', yet no file had been received almost three months after the tragedy that claimed two lives.

DCI Outlines Investigative Scope Responding on Saturday, March 28, the DCI confirmed that investigations into the collapse of the 14-storey Manzil Towers remain active. The agency highlighted that the inquiry involves multiple technical processes requiring considerable time to complete. The DCI noted that the probe depends on critical reports and analysis from several government institutions, including those responsible for approvals, inspections, and compliance in the construction sector. This scrutiny mirrors other government efforts to ensure accountability and proper procedure, such as the [EACC probing Embu MCAs over an alleged Ksh95 million tender scandal](https://zandani.co.ke/article/eacc-probes-embu-mcas-over-alleged-ksh95-million-tender-scandal). Investigators are currently examining the building's entire development lifecycle, from initial approvals and inspections to construction processes and structural integrity assessments. The DCI reassured the public that progress updates have been shared with the ODPP to ensure transparency and coordination, acknowledging the public interest and urgency of the matter. The DCI reaffirmed its commitment to a meticulous, evidence-based investigation to identify and appropriately charge all culpable parties once the finalised file is resubmitted to the ODPP for legal action.
