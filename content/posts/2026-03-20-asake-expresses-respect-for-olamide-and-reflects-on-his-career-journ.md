Celebrated artist Asake recently shared profound insights into his career journey and unwavering respect for music icon Olamide during an appearance on Korty EO's widely followed YouTube podcast. He openly reflected on his early struggles and the pivotal role Olamide played in his monumental ascent to stardom, a trajectory that has seen him achieve significant global milestones in 2025 and 2026.

## Asake's Enduring Reverence for Olamide

During the candid discussion, Asake recounted the challenges he faced in the initial stages of his career before his breakthrough. He described 2020 as a profoundly humbling period, personally and economically, after a brief taste of success with his song 'Mr Money'. While the music industry paradoxically thrived, Asake noted a time when many businesses faced collapse across various sectors, reflecting widespread economic jitters [https://zandani.co.ke/article/mass-layoff-registrar-of-companies-imminent-closure-117-companies]. For nearly two years following that flicker of visibility, Asake navigated numerous trials and errors, striving to establish himself in an industry that had briefly acknowledged him before seemingly moving on. His earlier signing with the now-defunct TFT Records in 2018 and subsequent releases, including 'Kanipe' in 2019, did not lead to the sustained breakthrough he sought.

### The Pivotal YBNL Breakthrough

Asake's career trajectory dramatically shifted in February 2022 when Olamide signed him to YBNL Records. That same month, Asake released his debut EP, *Ololade*, which featured the collaborative track ‘Omo Ope’ with Olamide and the hit single ‘Sungba’. This marked the beginning of one of the most remarkable ascents in recent Nigerian music history, transforming Asake into a global Afrobeats powerhouse. Between 2022 and 2024, Asake accumulated over 20 awards, solidifying his position as one of Nigeria's most streamed and celebrated artists. His influence continued to grow, with 2025 seeing him solidify his status as a global sensation, surpassing 2 billion career streams on Spotify and Audiomack, and earning a Grammy nomination for Best African Music Performance. YBNL Records, founded by Olamide in 2012, remains an active and influential force in the African music industry, instrumental in shaping the Nigerian music scene and fostering new talent.

### Unwavering Loyalty and Respect

Asake's profound loyalty and respect for Olamide are consistent and publicly acknowledged. During his recent podcast appearance, Asake unequivocally stated, "Till the end of time, Baddo will always be Baddo. The same respect I had for Baddo yesterday is the same respect I have for him today. Even if I become the president of America or the president of Zimbabwe, Baddo will always be Baddo." This sentiment underscores the deep gratitude Asake holds for Olamide, who provided crucial support and a platform when he most needed it. Olamide himself continues to be a prominent figure in the music scene, headlining major events like the Mainland Block Party's "Mission 26K" concert on January 1, 2026. Asake's journey from a young artist who experienced a brief moment of fame before facing renewed struggles to becoming one of Nigeria's biggest musical exports highlights the enduring impact of mentorship and loyalty in the competitive music industry.
