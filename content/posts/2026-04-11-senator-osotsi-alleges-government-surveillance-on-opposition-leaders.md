Vihiga Senator Geofrey Osotsi, discharged Saturday, April 11, after a week's hospitalisation, alleges government-linked surveillance preceded a coordinated attack on him in Kisumu. The Senator recounts being beaten at a coffee shop, claiming assailants threatened assassination, raising serious concerns about political targeting.

Allegations of Coordinated Assault and Surveillance Speaking for the first time since his hospital release, Senator Osotsi detailed the incident, which he described as a coordinated assault. He claimed a group of men approached him at a Kisumu coffee shop, addressed him as "Senator," and then began assaulting him. Osotsi stated that as he fell, the attack continued, with one assailant allegedly drawing a firearm and threatening to assassinate him. He specifically refuted claims circulated by "paid bloggers" suggesting a "love triangle" motive, asserting his wife was present during his account.

Interior Ministry Implication Osotsi further alleged that leaders within the Linda Mwananchi movement are under constant surveillance. He accused unnamed senior officials within the Interior Ministry of orchestrating this scheme. The Senator directly linked Interior Principal Secretary Raymond Omollo to both the alleged trailing of leaders and the Kisumu attack, stating the ministry had been transformed into what he termed a "goon empire."

Concerns Over Information Circulation and Political Motives The Senator also expressed concerns regarding the rapid online circulation of incident details before his own media team was informed. This, he suggested, indicates that individuals close to him might have been used to track his movements. Osotsi issued a warning, stating, "You are not going to get Western Kenya using goons despite having recruited goons in the whole of Nyanza." He questioned the specific location of the attack, posing, "Why must I be attacked in Kisumu? Why not any other town?" He concluded that the aim was to "sell a narrative that the Western people and the Nyanza people are at war," a claim he firmly dismissed.

Linda Mwananchi's Unwavering Resolve Despite the severity of the incident and the alleged threats, Senator Osotsi affirmed that the Linda Mwananchi leaders remain undeterred. He maintained that their planned tour from Nakuru to Kisumu will proceed as scheduled on April 19.
