When an Instagram Story outpaces a formal press release from the Ministry of Health, the nature of public reaction inevitably changes. Kenya is currently experiencing a moment where the personality behind the message carries more weight than the verification of the content itself. This trend introduces a significant risk of desensitization, as followers may find it increasingly difficult to separate a legitimate crisis from a personality-driven content loop. While officials manage critical infrastructure tasks like restoring water supply following a pipeline burst, they seem to be losing the battle for the public attention span to figures who optimize for engagement metrics.
