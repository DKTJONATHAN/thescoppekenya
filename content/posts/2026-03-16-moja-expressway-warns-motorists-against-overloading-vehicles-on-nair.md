MOJA Expressway Company Limited has issued a stern warning to motorists using the Nairobi Expressway against vehicle overloading, citing the dangerous and unlawful practice that has become prevalent in recent weeks. The company, managing the highway's daily operations, emphasized the strict prohibition to ensure public safety and smooth traffic flow.

## Overloading Risks on Nairobi Expressway

### Enforcement and Prevalent Practices
MOJA Expressway has flagged the practice of carrying excessive passengers as both dangerous and unlawful. The warning specifically targets Public Service Vehicles (PSVs) that have been observed exceeding passenger capacity, particularly those joining the expressway from the Airport North Road connecting the Eastern bypass. Some PSVs reportedly carry up to 15 passengers in excess, often with minimal immediate consequences due to the absence of traffic officers on the expressway.

### Safety and Infrastructure Concerns
Overloaded vehicles significantly disrupt the expressway's intended conditions for high-speed, heavy-volume traffic, creating risks for all road users. Excess weight raises a vehicle's center of gravity, making it dangerously prone to rollovers, especially at expressway speeds. Furthermore, overloading forces brakes to work beyond their design capacity, leading to overheating and potential failure. For heavy commercial vehicles, this practice also causes structural damage to the expressway infrastructure, which can result in substantial fines.

### Penalties for Non-Compliance
Motorists found overloading vehicles face severe repercussions. MOJA Expressway stated that carrying passengers in excess of legal capacity is strictly prohibited. Beyond immediate dangers, persistent infractions like overloading could lead to a ban from using the expressway, an action that would prove costly to overturn.
