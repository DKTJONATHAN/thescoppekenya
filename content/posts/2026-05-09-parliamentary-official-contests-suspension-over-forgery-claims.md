Kenyans are currently locked in a fierce digital debate over the Parliamentary Service Commission integrity protocols after a secretary challenged her suspension. Allegations of a fake Gakeo College certificate have sparked deep concerns regarding document tampering within the nation's most powerful institution.

Social media platforms are ablaze with frustration as Loice Nyambura Wamburu continues her legal fight against a suspension that has left many questioning the vetting processes at Parliament. While the official dispute centers on a certificate allegedly flagged in December 2024, the public discourse has moved toward a more uncomfortable question: how exactly do mystery documents appear in personal files without the employees' knowledge? 

For many Kenyans, this case is not just about one individual. It is about the lingering trust deficit that haunts public service recruitment. If a staffer with a career dating back to 2016 can suddenly find their employment threatened by a document they claim they never submitted, who is actually safe?
