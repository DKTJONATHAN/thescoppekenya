The Senate Standing Committee on Finance and Budget has invited the public to submit views on the Division of Revenue Bill, 2026. This crucial legislation, released via public notice on Monday, March 23, will dictate the financial allocation between national and county governments for the 2026/27 financial year.

## Revenue Allocation at a Crossroads

### Proposed 2026/27 Budget Framework

The government has proposed one of the country’s largest budgets in history, estimated at Ksh 4.7 trillion. Of this, approximately Ksh 2.9 trillion has been set aside for division between the national and county governments. Under the proposed framework, the national government is slated to receive Ksh 2.4 trillion, while the 47 county governments have been allocated Ksh 420 billion. An additional Ksh 10 billion has been earmarked for the Equalisation Fund, intended to provide basic services such as water, roads, health facilities, and electricity to marginalised areas. The substantial budget not only dictates public service delivery but also influences employment opportunities across various sectors. Discussions around the digital divide in application processes for roles such as those at the KRA Affordable Housing Board highlight the ongoing need for equitable access to government-supported initiatives [KRA Affordable Housing Board Mass Jobs: Unpacking the Digital Divide in Application](https://zandani.co.ke/article/kra-affordable-housing-board-mass-jobs-digital-divide-application).

### Governors Challenge Allocation

The proposed allocation to the counties has received pushback from the Council of Governors. They have rejected the Ksh 420 billion figure, arguing that the amount would be insufficient to meet the financial needs required for the effective operation of devolved units and the delivery of essential services. The governors have instead demanded that the National Treasury review the proposed amount and consider a higher share.

### Public Participation Details

The Division of Revenue Bill was read in the Senate on March 18, 2026, and subsequently committed to the Finance and Budget Committee for consideration. In accordance with standing order 145(5) of the Senate Standing Orders, the Committee is mandated to facilitate public participation and incorporate public views and recommendations into its report to the Senate. Written representations are to be submitted to the Clerk of the Senate. These can be hand-delivered to the Office of the Clerk in the Main Parliament Buildings in Nairobi or sent via email to clerk.senate@parliament.go.ke. The deadline for receiving these memoranda is Thursday, March 26, 2026, at 4:00 p.m.
