President William Ruto announced the dualling of the 169-kilometre Makutano-Meru highway through the new National Infrastructure Fund (NIF) on Sunday, April 26, 2026, during a church service in Tharaka Nithi County. This critical project aims to significantly improve trade and transport between Meru and Nairobi.

## National Infrastructure Fund to Drive Key Projects

President Ruto affirmed that the establishment of the National Infrastructure Fund provides a dedicated financial mechanism for large-scale infrastructure developments, eliminating reliance on foreign debt. The government's recent deposit of over Ksh103 billion, obtained from the sale of the Kenya Pipeline Company, directly into the NIF account, underscores the fund's immediate operational capacity. The NIF aims to raise over Ksh5 trillion within the next decade to transform the country's economic standing.

### Makutano-Meru Highway Dualling Initiated

The Makutano-Meru highway dualling project is set to enhance connectivity across four counties: Kirinyaga, Embu, Tharaka Nithi, and Meru. This expansion will reduce travel times and improve safety, particularly at notorious accident black spots. The corridor is deemed vital for the movement of agricultural products, bolstering trade efficiency.

### Nithi Bridge Remediation Efforts Underway

Addressing the long-standing safety concerns at Nithi Bridge, President Ruto confirmed that both engineering solutions and necessary funds are in place. He urged all involved stakeholders to expedite land compensation and collaborative efforts to ensure the bridge's reconstruction is completed swiftly. While such improvements address specific dangers, infrastructure resilience remains a national priority, especially in light of continued challenges such as the recent Nairobi floods where 25 individuals lost their lives, underscoring the broader need for robust infrastructure development across the nation.

### Broader Infrastructure Development Mandate

Beyond the Makutano-Meru highway, the National Infrastructure Fund is earmarked for other crucial projects, including the construction of dams nationwide to bolster food security. The beautification of the Jomo Kenyatta International Airport (JKIA) was previously announced as the inaugural project to be funded under the new initiative, making the highway project the second major undertaking by NIF.
