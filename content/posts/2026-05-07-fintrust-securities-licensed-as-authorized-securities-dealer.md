## Fintrust Securities and the Shift in Your Savings Strategy

If you have kept your extra savings in a standard bank account or a SACCO for years, you might wonder why the news of Fintrust Securities becoming an Authorized Securities Dealer matters to your personal wallet. It signals a move away from traditional borrowing toward direct investment.

Fintrust Securities, a subsidiary of Fincorp Credit, has received an Authorized Securities Dealer license from the Capital Markets Authority. This shift allows the firm to trade government securities directly, moving beyond the group's historical focus on credit and lending. For the everyday worker, this means the landscape for how you earn interest on your money is about to get more competitive and potentially more accessible.

### How does this change the competition for your money?

For a long time, Kenyan banks have held the primary gatekeeper role for government bond investments. They often set the terms, the minimum entry amounts, and the fees for retail clients who want to participate in the fixed-income market. When a non-bank entity like Fintrust enters the space as an Authorized Securities Dealer, that dynamic shifts.

These dealers do not have the same overheads as traditional commercial banks. By focusing specifically on securities rather than retail banking branches, they can offer more streamlined digital platforms to buy and sell Treasury bonds. When more players are fighting for your investment, they often compete on service quality, ease of access, and lower entry barriers. This forces traditional banks to rethink how they present their own investment products, which is a win for the person looking for better options than a basic savings account.

### Will this help you earn more interest?

When competition in the fixed-income market increases, the primary result is often better efficiency. Because Fintrust comes from a background in credit expertise, they understand how to manage risk and liquidity. They can use this knowledge to create investment products that feel more approachable than the complex auctions managed by the Central Bank.

If you have previously felt that government bonds were only for big institutional investors or the ultra-wealthy, you might find that new retail products appear soon. These companies are likely to bundle smaller amounts of capital into meaningful bond trades. While interest rates are largely determined by macroeconomic factors, a more accessible market means you can capture those rates more easily. If you are tired of waiting for the latest news on [Omah Lay’s sophomore album](https://zandani.co.ke/article/omah-lay-releases-sophomore-album-clarity-of-mind) or catching up on the weekend sports scores, you might want to spend a moment looking at how these dealers plan to simplify bond purchases.

### Why it matters now

The cost of living in Nairobi continues to fluctuate, and relying solely on a fixed salary is no longer enough to build wealth. By diversifying the types of entities authorized to trade securities, the regulator is essentially opening the door for more retail participation. When financial firms stop focusing only on lending you money and start focusing on helping you grow it, the balance of power shifts slightly back to the saver.

### What to watch next

Keep a close eye on the minimum investment thresholds set by these new dealers. Often, the barrier to entry for government securities is the biggest hurdle for a regular earner. If Fintrust and similar entities start allowing lower minimums, it will confirm that they are targeting the average Kenyan saver. 

Also, watch for the integration of mobile money platforms into their trading interfaces. The easier it is to move funds from your mobile wallet into a secure government bond, the more likely you are to see higher returns than what you are currently earning in a stagnant savings account. Whether they launch a dedicated app or integrate with existing finance tools, the goal for them is volume, and the goal for you should be finding the lowest fee structure for your long term wealth goals.
