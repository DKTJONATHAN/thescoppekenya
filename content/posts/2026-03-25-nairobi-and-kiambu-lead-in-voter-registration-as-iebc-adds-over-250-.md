The Independent Electoral and Boundaries Commission (IEBC) reports over 250,000 new voters added to the national roll since September 2025, with Nairobi and Kiambu counties leading the exercise. Data released on March 23, 2026, highlights active registration efforts across the country, ahead of the upcoming Enhanced Continuous Voter Registration (ECVR) phase.

Voter Registration Momentum Builds

County Performance Highlights Figures released on March 23 indicate Nairobi leads the voter registration drive with 49,055 new registrations. Kiambu County closely follows with 20,404 new voters. These two counties collectively account for 27 per cent of all new voters recorded thus far, underscoring the substantial turnout in urban and peri-urban regions. The stronger participation in these areas is driven by higher population density and increased access to voter awareness campaigns. Such growth often correlates with significant infrastructure development, including projects like the construction of the Kitengela-Rongai Road and the dualling of the Isinya-Konza City Road, which enhance regional connectivity and facilitate access. Mombasa County has registered 15,140 new voters, while Machakos recorded 11,687, and Nakuru added 10,432. Other counties demonstrating notable registration numbers include Kitui at 9,401, Kisii at 8,871, and Kakamega at 8,078. Meru County registered 7,499 voters, with Murang’a following closely at 7,267. Conversely, counties such as Mandera (994), Elgeyo Marakwet (552), Lamu (578), Tana River (241), and Isiolo (112) show the lowest registration figures.

Demographic Trends Demographic analysis of the ongoing registration reveals a near gender balance among new registrants, with men accounting for 50.9 per cent and women for 49.1 per cent. A significant trend indicates that older voters are dominating the exercise; those aged between 36 and 40 years constitute over 67 per cent of new registrants, compared to approximately 32 per cent among the 18 to 35 age group. IEBC Commissioner Alutalala Mukhwana noted that the ‘Niko Kadi’ youth mobilisation effort is now beginning to influence these numbers, with reports of a surge in young people turning up to register.

Expanding Registration Capacity The IEBC has scaled up its operational capacity in anticipation of increased demand. Approximately 12,450 personnel, including voter registration clerks, voter assistants, and ICT assistants, have been recruited to manage the expected surge in registrations across the country. Commissioner Mukhwana emphasised the adoption of flexible, locally responsive strategies to maintain an accessible and efficient registration process. The commission is set to roll out the Enhanced Continuous Voter Registration (ECVR) on March 30, 2026, which will run for 30 days nationwide.

Targets for 2027 General Election The commission is targeting 2.5 million new voters during the first phase of the ECVR. This initiative is part of a broader strategy to enlist at least 6.3 million new voters, aiming for a projected total of 28.5 million registered voters ahead of the 2027 General Election. Kenya’s total registered voter population stood at about 22.3 million at the start of the renewed mass voter registration efforts in September 2025.
