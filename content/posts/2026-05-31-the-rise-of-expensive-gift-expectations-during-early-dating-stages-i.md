Dating norms in Nairobi are currently undergoing a significant shift as an increasing number of men report facing demands for luxury electronic devices and high-end gifts during the initial stages of courting, sparking a heated national conversation regarding transactional romantic expectations.

## The Rise of Expensive Gift Expectations in Modern Romance

### The New Gold Standard of Affection
Modern courting in Kenya now appears to involve a rigorous financial vetting process that begins long before an emotional bond is established. It is no longer considered sufficient to offer dinner or thoughtful gestures. Many suitors report that potential partners now view a new smartphone or a premium gadget as the basic entry fee for a relationship. This demand for immediate material compensation is being viewed by many as a clear indicator that the primary motivation behind the union is not genuine love, but rather a calculated effort to secure lifestyle upgrades at the expense of the suitor.

### Spiritual Parallels and Betrayal
Cultural critics are drawing sharp comparisons between these mercenary dating habits and historical accounts of betrayal. Some observe that the pressure to provide expensive items early on mirrors the thirty pieces of silver famously associated with the betrayal of Jesus. When a partner prioritizes a premium device over mutual understanding, it suggests that the relationship is merely a transactional arrangement. As the government tightens its grip on financial movements, including the [Treasury Proposes New Digital Tax Framework for Mobile and Crypto](https://zandani.co.ke/article/treasury-proposes-new-digital-tax-framework-for-mobile-and-crypto), these expensive gift demands are becoming increasingly difficult to conceal from financial scrutiny.

Consequently, many men are deciding that if love carries a price tag higher than their monthly rent, they would rather remain single than act as a financier in a scripted romance.
