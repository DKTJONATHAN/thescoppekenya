Nairobi City Water and Sewerage Company (NCWSC) announced a temporary water supply interruption today, Saturday, March 21, 2026, across several city estates, including the President's official residence. The disruption follows a major pipeline burst on the Kabete-Kilimani transmission line. Repairs are currently underway to restore services.

## Pipeline Burst Disrupts Nairobi Water Supply

### Incident Details and Impact
The water supply disruption stems from a burst on the main Kabete-Kilimani transmission pipeline, which occurred in the early hours of Friday, March 20, 2026, at approximately 1 AM. Nairobi Water technical teams responded immediately to the incident, initiating repair efforts to address the damaged section.

The interruption impacts a significant portion of the city. Areas experiencing water shortages include State House, Kileleshwa, Kilimani, Ngumo, KEMRI, Highrise, Nairobi West, Upper Hill, Madaraka, and their surrounding vicinities.

### Restoration Efforts and Timeline
Nairobi Water has confirmed that repair works are actively progressing. These efforts involve replacing the damaged pipe section, conducting pressure testing, and ensuring the system's integrity and safety before resuming normal supply.

According to the notice issued, water supply is expected to be fully restored by Sunday, March 22, 2026, at 6 PM. This timeline allows for thorough repairs on the critical transmission line while prioritizing safety and infrastructure integrity for a reliable return to service.

### Resident Advisory and Emergency Services
Residents affected by the outage are urged to use any stored water sparingly and prioritize essential needs during the restoration process. To support vulnerable groups and critical institutions, Nairobi Water has made water tanker services available for emergency and priority requirements. Customers needing water tanker services can dial *260# on their phones and select Option 5 to submit a request.
