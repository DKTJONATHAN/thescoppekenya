**By Celestine Nzioka** The Ministry of Interior has announced a significant salary increment for police officers, prison warders, and National Youth Service personnel, marking a major milestone in the ongoing security sector reforms. Under the new pay structure set to take effect this July, police constables in the lowest cadre will see their monthly basic pay rise by up to Ksh18,000. In a statement released on Wednesday, the Ministry confirmed that this adjustment is the final phase of a three year salary review cycle that began in July 2024. The government describes these cumulative adjustments as the most substantial pay rise for security officers since independence. A constable at the entry level of the National Police Service who previously earned a basic salary of Ksh38,975 will now receive a maximum of Ksh57,700. This represents a 48 percent increase in basic earnings. A similar pay adjustment has been applied to equivalent ranks within the Kenya Prisons Service to ensure parity across the disciplined forces. Fresh graduates from police training colleges are also set to benefit from the new policy. Their starting salary has been adjusted from Ksh20,390 to Ksh29,296, reflecting a 44 percent increase. For the National Youth Service, officers in the lowest cadre will now earn between Ksh26,222 and Ksh37,912, up from the previous range of Ksh19,800 to Ksh32,315. The pay review also extends to the highest ranks of the services. The top serving police officer will see their maximum monthly basic pay rise to Ksh345,850 from the previous Ksh289,090. Senior officers in the Prisons Service will now earn between Ksh301,548 and Ksh584,903 depending on their specific grade and years of service. Interior Principal Secretary Raymond Omollo, who chairs the National Steering Committee on the implementation of these reforms, noted that the government has already achieved over 50 percent of the planned reform actions. The National Police Service currently leads the implementation progress at 57.2 percent. The Ministry emphasized that these reforms are built upon a four pillar transformation framework. These pillars focus on institutional capacity development, human resource management, oversight and accountability, and operational preparedness. The government maintains that improving the welfare of officers is central to enhancing the overall security and stability of the nation. While the increments have been implemented in phases since July last year, the upcoming July payout represents the completion of the promised salary adjustments. This move is expected to boost morale within the security agencies as the government continues to roll out logistical and housing improvements alongside the financial rewards.
