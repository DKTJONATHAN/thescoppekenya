Former Chief Justice David Maraga has sparked significant national debate after publicly criticizing current political party leaders. His recent statements indicate a desire to challenge the status quo, prompting speculation regarding his potential presidential bid and his lack of an established political foundation.

## David Maraga and the presidential bid puzzle

Maraga has openly expressed frustration with the current political leadership, yet his strategy for mounting a presidential campaign remains unclear. While the former head of the judiciary occupies a position of perceived moral authority, his approach appears to rely on the expectation that established parties, such as ODM, Wiper, or Jubilee, will offer him their political infrastructure. The reality of domestic politics is that campaign machinery is built through extensive groundwork rather than bestowed upon newcomers as a reward for past judicial service.

### The missing foundation of support

In the competitive arena of national elections, a candidate without a grassroots network faces significant hurdles. Critics note that Maraga lacks a dedicated political base or the formal backing of a single elected member of parliament. Expecting established political entities to hand over their hard-won mobilization structures to an outsider is a strategy that ignores the competitive nature of the system. Party leaders typically prioritize their own political survival and the preservation of their existing coalitions over yielding control to those who have not invested in the internal development of the party.

### Institutional movement beyond the bench

While Maraga considers a transition from the judiciary to the campaign trail, the legal system he once led continues to evolve independently of his influence. Recent administrative updates show that Chief Justice Martha Koome has established new Court of Appeal and Small Claims Court stations to address the growing backlog of cases across the country. Such structural changes highlight that the machinery of justice continues to function regardless of any individual’s tenure. Much like the government’s efforts to improve physical infrastructure, such as the directive to build cycling lanes on the Nyeri-Nyahururu Highway, the political landscape requires concrete planning and tangible investment before a candidate can successfully navigate a path to the presidency.

### Challenges of public transition

Transitioning into the political spotlight is a complex endeavor that requires more than a distinguished reputation. Public figures often face significant hurdles when attempting to pivot from institutional roles to electoral politics, where success depends on established alliances and voter trust. For Maraga, the primary challenge remains proving that his political strategy is as robust as his legal record. Without building his own political vehicle or securing genuine grassroots support, he faces the risk of being viewed as an outsider attempting to occupy a space without having contributed to its foundation. Ultimately, voters are waiting to see if the former judge will develop a genuine political movement from the ground up or if he will continue to wait for an invitation to an established party that has little incentive to hand him the keys.
