## Industrial Fuel Switch Accelerates Deforestation Concerns Busia Senator Okiya Omtatah has issued a strong warning regarding escalating deforestation, citing a concerning trend where large manufacturing companies are transitioning from gas and diesel to cheaper firewood and wood-based biomass fuel. The senator is pressing for urgent government intervention to implement sustainable sourcing and environmental safeguards to protect natural resources.

Growing Reliance on Wood Fuel Speaking before the Standing Committee on Land, Environment and Natural Resources at the Senate today, March 12, Senator Omtatah highlighted industrial zones in Thika, Limuru, Nakuru, and Mukurweini, where numerous major companies have reportedly shifted away from furnace oil, industrial diesel, and gas. This transition, he explained, is driven by rising energy costs, leading industries to embrace firewood and wood briquettes.

Environmental Safeguards Questioned While acknowledging that a move to biomass fuel is not inherently problematic, Senator Omtatah stressed that the current shift is occurring too rapidly and without adequate environmental checks or proper safeguards to ensure sustainable sourcing. The substantial volume of wood required to power large industrial boilers continuously raises fears that this demand is quietly accelerating the destruction of natural forests and community lands across the country. Omtatah cautioned that unchecked industrial wood consumption risks undoing years of forest recovery efforts and could severely impact critical water towers.

Broader Ecological and Climate Impacts The ripple effects of this trend extend beyond forests. Degraded water towers could lead to reduced river flows, directly affecting farming communities dependent on these sources for irrigation, drinking water, and livestock. Omtatah further warned that the country faces potentially devastating climate consequences if this issue is not urgently addressed, especially for a nation already contending with erratic weather patterns.

Accountability and Regulatory Scrutiny The senator is demanding that the government disclose the number of companies that have officially converted to biomass fuel and their full consumption of firewood energy. He questioned whether the National Environment Management Authority (NEMA) conducted proper environmental impact assessments before approving biomass fuel use at an industrial scale, a legally mandated process for major operational changes. Omtatah also challenged the Kenya Forest Service regarding the tree harvesting permits used by these companies, seeking assurance that the wood originates from sustainable commercial plantations and not from gazetted forests or protected community lands, which are legally off-limits.

Call for Government Action Senator Omtatah emphasized that existing legal frameworks, including the Forest Conservation and Management Act and the Environmental Management and Coordination Act, are robust enough to effectively regulate industrial biomass use. He is now pressing the government to outline the urgent steps it is taking to prevent what he described as a looming environmental crisis, driven not by poverty but by large corporations seeking to cut operational costs.
