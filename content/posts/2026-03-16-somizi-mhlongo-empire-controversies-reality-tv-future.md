Somizi Mhlongo, a ubiquitous figure in South African entertainment, continues to dominate headlines with his flamboyant persona and multifaceted career, all while navigating a labyrinth of public scrutiny and personal controversies. The media personality's enduring presence on screens and stages, coupled with his significant financial standing, positions him as an undeniable candidate should 'I'm A Celebrity South Africa' seek truly 'local' star power for its 2026 season.

Somizi Mhlongo's Enduring Empire and Financial Clout Born Somizi Buyani Mhlongo on December 23, 1972, in Soweto, South Africa, his trajectory into the limelight was almost predetermined, given his parentage to late veteran actors Ndaba Mhlongo and Mary Twala. Mhlongo's career, spanning decades, kicked off in the late 1980s, notably featuring in the 1992 musical film "Sarafina!" as Fire, also known as Whacko. Since then, he has meticulously built an empire that encompasses choreography for major events, including the 2010 FIFA World Cup and the 2013 Africa Cup of Nations opening and closing ceremonies, acting roles, presenting gigs, and a successful entrepreneurial portfolio. His estimated net worth stands at an impressive $5 million, which equated to approximately R93 million in 2024. This substantial wealth is derived from his diverse roles as a media personality, choreographer, actor, and entrepreneur. Mhlongo's income streams have included significant earnings from his stint as a judge on "Idols South Africa," where his monthly salary reportedly surged from R48,700 in 2020-2021 to R200,000 in 2022. Beyond television, he has secured lucrative brand ambassador deals with entities like DStv and the Department of Water and Sanitation, and collaborated with McDonald's South Africa as the face of McCafé Coach Sessions. His entrepreneurial spirit further extends into the culinary world, with a cookware line and a published cookbook adding to his financial portfolio.

A Career Marred by Public Controversies Despite his glittering success, Mhlongo's career has been repeatedly overshadowed by a string of high-profile controversies, many of which mainstream media often treats with a soft touch. In 2021, the South African National Editors' Forum (SANEF) publicly condemned Mhlongo for his attacks and insults against journalists reporting on his alleged infidelity and relationship issues. This episode highlighted a pattern of volatile public engagement when faced with scrutiny. Perhaps the most damaging was the abuse allegations leveled against him by his estranged husband, Mohale Motaung. In 2021, Motaung alleged physical and psychological abuse, including claims of broken ribs, threats with a kitchen knife, and an attempt to drive him off the road in 2020. These serious accusations led to Mhlongo's temporary departure from "Idols SA" and his exit from Metro FM, as both the SABC and MultiChoice cited their firm stance against abuse. While Mhlongo denied these allegations, the scandal cast a long shadow over his public image. Further complicating his narrative were plagiarism allegations in 2020, with claims that the concept for his TV series "Dinner at Somizi's" was stolen from another creative, Hastings Moeng. In February 2025, Mhlongo faced significant public backlash over claims by his baby mama, Palesa Madisakwane, that he had failed to provide child maintenance and demanded a DNA test for their daughter, Bahumi, years prior. His response, a cryptic song shared on social media, only intensified the outrage. Most recently, in March 2026, Mhlongo found himself embroiled in another financial dispute. He publicly distanced himself from Search Light (Pty) Ltd, a cleaning services company accused of owing over R165,000 in unpaid salaries to former employees. The Commission for Conciliation, Mediation and Arbitration (CCMA) ruled in favour of the employees, with a sheriff attempting to seize assets, only to find the company's former offices empty. Mhlongo vehemently denied any ownership or association with Search Light, stating it was never registered under his name. This denial, coming amidst a CCMA ruling and the company's apparent disappearance, has raised questions about corporate accountability in the celebrity sphere.
