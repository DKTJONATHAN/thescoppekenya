**By Celestine Nzioka** The Council of Governors (CoG) on Tuesday, March 31, 2026, demanded the immediate withdrawal of alleged arrest warrants and all coercive measures targeting its members. This follows the attempted arrest of Nairobi Governor Johnson Sakaja, which the CoG condemned as politically motivated and an undermining of the rule of law.

CoG Demands Halt to Coercive Measures CoG Chair Ahmed Abdullahi, in a media briefing on Tuesday, March 31, stated that any action against governors must be based on verified facts rather than political or personal interests. "We demand the immediate withdrawal of the alleged warrants of arrest or any coercive measures against governors, pending an impartial determination," Ahmed stated. The governors specifically condemned the attempted arrest of Nairobi Governor Johnson Sakaja on Monday, March 30, emphasizing that such actions deepen mistrust and undermine the rule of law. In light of these events, the CoG has called upon the Inspector General of Police, Douglas Kanja, to diligently verify the facts and truthfulness of any allegations or cases against governors before taking action. This demand underscores a broader expectation for all governmental processes to uphold fairness and due process, aligning with principles that ensure public officials, like those noted in recent directives where CS Wandayi ordered oil marketers to release fuel at gazetted prices, operate within established legal frameworks.

Senate Committee Impasse and Sakaja's Appearance The CoG's statement comes days after the Senate County Public Accounts Committee (CPAC) ordered Inspector General Kanja to produce Governor Sakaja and his Samburu counterpart, Jonathan Lelelit, for failing to appear before the committee. Kanja had been given until Monday, March 30, to ensure the governors appeared, with the Senate threatening to declare them unfit for public office through a letter to the Independent Electoral and Boundaries Commission (IEBC) if they failed to comply. Attempts to arrest Governor Sakaja at his office on Monday evening were unsuccessful as police could not locate him. Hours after the incident, Sakaja issued a statement condemning the attempted arrest, clarifying that the issue was institutional rather than personal. He explained, "The issue at hand is not a personal matter concerning Governor Sakaja Johnson, but one that is being handled collectively by the Council of Governors. It is therefore misleading to single out one Governor in a matter that is collective and consultative in nature." Despite the earlier standoff, Sakaja appeared before the CPAC on Tuesday afternoon, where he faced questioning over alleged misappropriation of county funds, including claims that seven advisors each receive over Ksh 203,000 monthly. The CoG has reiterated Sakaja's position, with governors maintaining they will not appear before the Senate Committee until their demands, including addressing allegations of intimidation and extortion by specific senators during oversight sessions, are met. The governors affirmed their commitment to consultative engagements, accountability, transparency, and the rule of law once these claims are resolved.
