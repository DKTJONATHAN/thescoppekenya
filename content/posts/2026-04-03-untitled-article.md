<p>As April 2026 settles in, many of us are already feeling that special buzz that comes with Easter. Forget just bunnies and chocolate; this year, let's really dig into what this time means for us, especially here in Kenya, beyond the long weekend.</p>

<h2>This Weekend: The Dates and The Deep Meaning</h2>
<p>Today, Friday, April 3rd, 2026, marks Good Friday. For us, this isn't just another public holiday; it's a day heavy with reflection, leading into a weekend of profound significance. Easter Sunday, the climax of it all, falls on April 5th this year. And then, we get to stretch out the celebrations and reflections just a little longer with Easter Monday on April 6th. Three full days to pause, breathe, and truly consider what we're celebrating.</p>

<p>I've seen Easter evolve over my ten-plus years watching Kenya's pulse. For many, it’s about that much-needed break from the city's hustle. Roads out of Nairobi, Mombasa, Kisumu, all choked with cars headed upcountry, families reuniting. It’s the aroma of nyama choma grilling in the village, the laughter of cousins who haven't seen each other in months. And yes, the kids absolutely love the brightly coloured eggs and the idea of a fluffy bunny delivering treats. These are beautiful parts of our culture, woven into the fabric of the holiday. But scratch beneath that surface, and you find a story far deeper, far more powerful.</p>

<p>What are we truly celebrating? At its core, Easter is about life. It’s about a new beginning so monumental, it changed everything. We're marking the resurrection of Jesus Christ. Two millennia ago, a man died on a cross, was buried, and then, three days later, came back to life. This isn't just a historical event; it’s the cornerstone of Christian faith and the very reason for the season.</p>
