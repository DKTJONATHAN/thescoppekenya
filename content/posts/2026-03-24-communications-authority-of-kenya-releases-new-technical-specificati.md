The Communications Authority of Kenya (CA) has released updated technical specifications for mobile cellular devices. The directive, announced on Tuesday, March 24, 2026, aims to regulate the sale and use of type-approved mobile phones, ensuring consumer safety, enhanced network performance, and a better user experience across the nation.

Ensuring Device Safety and Network Integrity

Updated Regulatory Framework The CA's new standards require all individuals, companies, and organizations intending to market, sell, or use communication equipment to comply with the revised guidelines. The Authority emphasized that only duly licensed vendors are permitted to sell communication equipment that meets established safety standards and contributes to optimal network performance. This move reinforces the commitment to protecting consumers from potentially harmful devices.

Compliance and Enforcement The Authority urged all stakeholders, including ICT equipment vendors, local mobile phone assemblers, and manufacturers, to access the full specifications through the CA website, ca.go.ke, to ensure adherence during the type-approval process. The CA issued a stern warning against the sale of mobile phones and other ICT devices without proper authorization, noting that such actions could lead to legal penalties, fines, and confiscation of equipment. This regulatory vigilance is paramount in upholding market integrity.

Protecting Consumers from Substandard Devices The updated type-approval process is specifically designed to guarantee that communication devices meet national technical and safety benchmarks. This initiative seeks to shield consumers from substandard, counterfeit, or potentially hazardous devices that could disrupt communication networks or compromise user safety. Consumers are strongly advised to purchase phones and accessories exclusively from licensed dealers to avoid acquiring uncertified devices that may be blocked from accessing local networks. This guidance follows the CA's February 10 notice this year, which banned 21 mobile phone brands over licensing and safety concerns, citing an influx of non-type-approved devices posing health risks and network interference.
