Kajiado Governor Joseph Ole Lenku successfully secured the release of journalist Joshua Mungai from Kamiti Maximum Prison this May after leading a public fundraiser. The governor paid KSh 16 million in defamation damages owed to State House Comptroller Katoo Ole Metito.

## Governor Joseph Ole Lenku pays KSh 16 million to free journalist Joshua Mungai

### A brief stay at the government Airbnb
After a short stint at a government owned Airbnb in the gated community of Kamiti Maximum Prison, Joshua Mungai is finally tasting freedom again. The journalist found himself behind bars after failing to satisfy a court order to pay damages for articles written during the 2022 general election cycle. While the official term for his stay is civil jail, the severity of the punishment highlights the irony of a writer being hosted at the most notorious high security residence in the country over a matter of words and unpaid bills.

### High stakes political chess in Kajiado
The KSh 16 million figure remained non negotiable as Katoo Ole Metito insisted on every cent, claiming the funds would be directed toward community projects. This move is widely viewed as a strategic opening gambit for the gubernatorial campaign Metito is planning for 2027. By refusing to waive the debt, Metito attempted to frame the financial ruin of a journalist as a moral lesson for the media. Such intense legal maneuvers underscore why there is a constant call for the government to strengthen election security planning to ensure that the fallout from political contests does not continue to spill into the corridors of justice years later.

### Governor Lenku plays the hero card
Governor Joseph Ole Lenku did not miss the chance to play the savior, leading a rapid public fundraiser to settle the debt and walk Mungai out of the prison gates. By writing the check, Lenku effectively neutralized a political weapon Metito intended to use to show strength. The journalist became a human trophy in a proxy war between the two Kajiado heavyweights, with the intervention by Lenku being as calculated and high stakes as a major investment in the green economy.

### Philanthropy through financial ruin
The attempt by Metito to paint this windfall as a charitable venture for Kajiado residents has been met with sharp sarcasm across the region. Critics find it difficult to reconcile the image of a philanthropist with someone who sent a scribe to Kamiti over a debt. The narrative that destroying the livelihood of a professional serves the public good is a tough sell, even for a State House insider. As Mungai returns to his family, the consensus on the ground is that the only thing philanthropic about the situation was the speed at which his freedom was bought by a political rival. In the streets of Kajiado, the takeaway is clear: one man got his millions, another got his hero moment, and the journalist is just glad his host at the government Airbnb finally processed his check out.
