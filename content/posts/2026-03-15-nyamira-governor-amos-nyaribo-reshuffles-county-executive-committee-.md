Nyamira Governor Amos Nyaribo has restructured his County Executive Committee, reassigning several members and chief officers in changes formalized by a gazette notice on March 13, 2026. These adjustments, effective March 4, aim to optimize service delivery across Nyamira County departments following recent political challenges.

Nyamira Cabinet Reshuffle Details

Key Reassignments Within County Dockets Stephen Oboso has transitioned from the Lands, Housing, Urban Development, and Physical Planning docket to lead the Finance and Economic Planning department. He replaced Jones Moko Onwenga, who now heads the Trade, Tourism, and Cooperative Development docket. John Omambia Matiangi, previously the Trade, Tourism, and Cooperative Development CECM, now steers the Gender, Culture, Sports, and Social Services ministry. Bernard Mbere Maina moved from Trade, Tourism, and Cooperative Development to the Lands, Housing, Urban Development, and Physical Planning portfolio. Alice Manoti was reassigned from Gender, Culture, Sports, and Social Services to the Environment, Water, Energy, Natural Resources, and Climate Change department. Rael Momanyi moved from Public Service Management to serve as Chief Officer for Gender, Culture, Sports, and Social Services, replacing Mercy Moraa Motanya, who assumed the Public Service Management docket.

Governor Nyaribo's Recent Political Standing The reshuffle by Governor Nyaribo follows his survival of an impeachment attempt almost three months prior. Nyamira MCAs had sought his removal over allegations of illegal appointments and abuse of office. Senate Speaker Amason Kingi confirmed on Wednesday, December 3, 2025, that Nyaribo would continue serving after a preliminary objection argued the impeachment was invalid due to the Nyamira County Assembly not meeting the constitutional threshold for approval.

Broader County Executive Adjustments These changes in Nyamira coincide with similar administrative adjustments in neighboring counties. Two weeks ago, Kakamega Governor Fernandes Barasa also announced modifications affecting three key chief officers in his county executive. In a statement on March 2, 2026, Governor Barasa confirmed Anselm Mulinya moved from Education, Science, and Technology to become Chief Officer for Cooperatives. Daniel Seth Sumba, formerly leading Cooperatives, now oversees County Administration. Samuel Ambrose Makokha was reassigned from County Administration to the Education, Science, and Technology department, completing Kakamega's three-way reshuffle.
