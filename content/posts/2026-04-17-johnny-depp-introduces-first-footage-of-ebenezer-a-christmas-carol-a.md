## Johnny Depp Unveils Gruesome Scrooge at CinemaCon Johnny Depp is back with a bang, premiering the first look at his ghastly Ebenezer Scrooge in *Ebenezer: A Christmas Carol* at CinemaCon. This marks his return to a major American studio, with Ti West directing a spooky, comedic reimagining hitting theaters November 13, 2026.

Depp's Triumphant Return After his high-profile 2022 legal battle, Johnny Depp is making a significant comeback, stepping back into the spotlight with a major American studio film. His appearance at CinemaCon on Thursday, April 16, 2026, was a moment fans have eagerly anticipated, marking his first major project since the much-discussed trial. This return signals a new chapter for the beloved *Pirates of the Caribbean* star, who captivated the audience with his personal introduction to the project.

A Scrooge Like No Other Depp's transformation into Ebenezer Scrooge is nothing short of radical. The footage screened at Paramount's presentation revealed a truly ghastly take on the classic miser. Expect to see Scrooge with grimy mutton chops, greasy gray hair, and nasty, frequently exposed teeth, all framed by a constant snarl. Depp shared his long-standing obsession with the story and his initial intimidation by Alastair Sim's iconic 1951 portrayal, but ultimately found a unique angle for his performance. The footage also shows a glimpse of his signature comic mania, promising shrieks, yelps, and goofy expressions amidst the supernatural encounters.

Horror Director's Holiday Twist Helming *Ebenezer: A Christmas Carol* is Ti West, known for his chilling horror films like *X* and *Pearl*. West's influence is clear, as the trailer showcased a decent amount of spooky imagery, merging his horror sensibilities with the festive tale. The film features a stellar supporting cast, including Ian McKellen as Jacob Marley, Andrea Riseborough as the Ghost of Christmas Past, and Tramell Tillman as another spirit. Rupert Grint takes on the role of Bob Cratchit, with Daisy Ridley, Sam Claflin, Charlie Murphy, Arthur Conti, and Ellie Bamber also joining the ensemble. One particularly intriguing scene depicted Scrooge in a room full of bones, hinting at the darker elements West brings to this holiday classic.

Mark Your Calendars Get ready for a unique holiday experience as *Ebenezer: A Christmas Carol* is set to hit theaters on November 13, 2026. This film promises to be a memorable cinematic event, blending the timeless charm of Dickens with a fresh, slightly macabre twist, all anchored by Johnny Depp's captivating return to the big screen.
