## Kamariny Stadium Confirmed for Mashujaa Day 2025 Celebrations

Interior Cabinet Secretary Kipchumba Murkomen announced that Kamariny Stadium in Elgeyo Marakwet County will host Mashujaa Day 2025 celebrations on October 20. This decision marks a significant step in the government's plan to decentralize national events and finalize long-delayed infrastructure projects, confirming active construction at the historic venue.

### Construction Progress and Government Commitment

Murkomen, speaking on Saturday, May 2, during a Community Empowerment Initiative at Kamwosor Grounds in Elgeyo Marakwet, confirmed that construction works at Kamariny Stadium are actively underway. A contractor has been moved to the site, indicating a renewed government commitment to complete the stadium after years of stalled progress and public debate over its delayed delivery. The CS linked this development to President William Ruto's earlier pledges, assuring that the promise to complete the stadium is now being fulfilled. Murkomen stated, "We will be in Kamariny Stadium celebrating Mashujaa Day, and by then all the construction works will have been completed."

### Historic Event for Elgeyo Marakwet

The announcement means that October 20, 2025, will mark the first time a national holiday with full military participation is held in Elgeyo Marakwet County. The region, which previously faced insecurity and banditry challenges, has recently experienced improved stability. This progress follows multi-agency operations under Operation Ondoa Uhalifu North Rift, active across Elgeyo Marakwet, Baringo, and West Pokot counties. Murkomen noted that the security situation is steadily improving due to ongoing government interventions and community cooperation, despite isolated concerns.

### Addressing Past Delays

The Kamariny Stadium project traces its origins to a controversial 2017 campaign promise made during the Jubilee era, when Ruto, then President Uhuru Kenyatta’s running mate, pledged its completion within six months. Years later, the project became a symbol of stalled sports infrastructure, drawing criticism from Kenyans and stakeholders over lost opportunities in sports development and tourism. This public debate and criticism often engage various sectors, much like how Bien responded to a critic recently [Bien responds to critic Sabato as Finale reaches 20 million views]. However, the government states that renewed construction efforts and fresh prioritization of national infrastructure are now correcting these past delays, with multiple counties benefiting from new infrastructure investments.
