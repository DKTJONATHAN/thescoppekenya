### A Champion for Transparency
Maribel Kapolon became a defining figure for a generation of Kenyans who chose to confront their mental health struggles openly. By sharing the realities of living with bipolar disorder, she worked to dismantle the social stigma that forces many individuals to suffer in isolation. Her advocacy served as a vital resource for those navigating their own psychological challenges.

### Highlighting Internal Realities
The sudden loss of such a dedicated advocate has drawn attention to the disparity between national political discourse and the urgent, private struggles occurring within many households. Kapolon consistently emphasized that mental health crises remain a significant reality for citizens, regardless of their outward appearance or perceived strength. Her work highlighted the necessity of acknowledging these struggles as a standard part of the human experience.

### A Call for National Reflection
Her passing has initiated a countrywide conversation regarding the adequacy of current support systems for mental health. Many are now examining whether enough resources are being directed toward psychiatric care and accessible health services. As the nation reflects on her life, the focus has shifted toward the importance of human capital and the need for more robust, empathetic care structures to support those in the greatest need of assistance.
