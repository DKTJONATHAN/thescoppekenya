Photojournalist and activist Allans Ademba has publicly alleged government threats and intimidation following the suspension of his prominent social media accounts. On March 29, 2026, Ademba reported waking to find his TikTok account, with millions of views and thousands of followers, unexpectedly suspended, linking it directly to state actions.

Ademba Alleges State Machinery Against Activism

Social Media Account Suspension Details Ademba provided screenshots of the suspension notice for his TikTok account, which he discovered on March 29, 2026, at 1 am. The ban notice stated, “We suspended your account, Ademba Allans. You have 180 days left to appeal, or we’ll permanently disable your account.” He claims this suspension is part of a series of threats after the government "released its state machinery" on him. Ademba highlighted his account's significant reach, including 18 million post views, 325,000 profile views, 1.6 million likes, 52,000 comments, and 63,000 shares, suggesting these metrics are being targeted.

Broader Intimidation Concerns The activist further indicated that he is experiencing other forms of intimidation by "state machinery" and fears for his life should he disclose the full scope of these threats. He stated that revealing more details would put his life in danger. Due to being on the state radar, Ademba has resorted to using a basic feature phone, commonly known as a 'Kabambe,' which lacks internet capabilities. While these phones lack internet-based GPS trackers, their International Mobile Equipment Identity (IMEI) numbers remain registered with networks, making them traceable when powered on, though more challenging for hackers to track compared to smartphones. President William Ruto's administration has faced widespread accusations from human rights groups, activists, and international bodies regarding the use of digital surveillance to facilitate the abduction and disappearance of its critics.

Niko Kadi Initiative and Voter Registration Ademba Allans founded the viral Niko Kadi initiative in February 2026, aiming to mobilize young citizens to register as voters ahead of the 2027 General elections. The movement, which originated from a single tweet seeking 100,000 unregistered voters in Kasarani, rapidly expanded nationwide via WhatsApp and social media, registering 641 people on its first day. According to Ademba, Niko Kadi promotes civic education and youth participation, boosts nationwide registrations, and seeks to influence millions of Gen-Z voters while inspiring music, dance, and activism across regions. Ademba has explicitly warned politicians against "hijacking" the movement, asserting its citizen-funded and citizen-led nature to prevent political interference. Meanwhile, the Independent Electoral and Boundaries Commission (IEBC) is scheduled to commence a nationwide mass voter registration drive starting Monday, April 30, 2026. This initiative targets millions of voters, with a particular focus on youth participation.
