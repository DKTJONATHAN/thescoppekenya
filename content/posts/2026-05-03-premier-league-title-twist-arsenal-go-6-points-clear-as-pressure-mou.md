Arsenal has seized a commanding six-point lead over Manchester City in the Premier League title race, with only three games remaining for the Gunners. This dramatic turn has ignited intense debate among pundits, as Mikel Arteta's squad aims to end a 22-year drought.

## Premier League Title Momentum Shifts

The 2025/2026 Premier League season is reaching a thrilling climax, with Arsenal establishing a significant advantage. Following their convincing 3-0 victory over Fulham, Mikel Arteta's side now sits six points clear of second-placed Manchester City. Arsenal has just three league fixtures left, while Pep Guardiola's squad has five, creating a high-stakes scenario where City has minimal room for error. The Gunners' recent form, including wins against Newcastle and Fulham, has steadied their campaign after a challenging period that saw them briefly lose the top spot.

Opta's latest analysis gives Arsenal a 79.7% chance of securing their first Premier League title since 2004. Manager Mikel Arteta expressed his satisfaction with the team's performance against Fulham, highlighting their dominance and hunger, especially after their Champions League semi-final first-leg draw against Atletico Madrid. The goal difference could prove pivotal, and Arsenal's three unanswered goals against Fulham extended their advantage over City by four, which could be critical in a tight finish.

### Punditry Weighs In on the Race

The football world is divided on who will lift the trophy. Five-time Premier League winner Wayne Rooney believes Arsenal are now the definitive favourites, even predicting a "comfortable five points" victory due to their more favourable fixtures. He expects Arsenal to win every remaining game while Manchester City might slip up.

Conversely, former City goalkeeper Joe Hart remains confident his old club will triumph, citing their experienced "spine" of multiple winners like Rodri, Bernardo Silva, and Erling Haaland. Paul Merson, a former Arsenal forward, told Sky Sports that if Arsenal win their next three Premier League matches, Manchester City will face an immense challenge. He leans towards Arsenal due to their fixture list, anticipating the title could come down to goal difference if both teams win all their remaining games. Jamie Redknapp, another former England midfielder, also sees Arsenal holding a significant goal difference advantage in their remaining matches, though he believes the outcome is still highly unpredictable.

### Decisive Fixtures Ahead

Arsenal's remaining Premier League schedule appears favourable. After their Champions League return leg against Atletico Madrid on Tuesday, they visit strugglers West Ham next Saturday, host relegated Burnley on May 18, and conclude their season away at Crystal Palace on May 24.

Manchester City faces a more congested and challenging run-in. They visit Everton on Monday, followed by an away fixture against sixth-placed Brentford. Their schedule also includes a home game against Crystal Palace, an away trip to eighth-placed Bournemouth, and a final-day clash at home against Aston Villa, currently in fifth position. The demanding nature of City's fixtures, coupled with Arsenal's current momentum, sets the stage for a truly unforgettable conclusion to the Premier League season for Za Ndani.
