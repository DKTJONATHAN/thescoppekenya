Nairobi woke up to a watery wonderland on Tuesday, April 28, 2026, as torrential overnight rains transformed the city's roads and residential areas into unexpected rivers. The deluge proved a formidable foe, testing the city's infrastructure and leaving even the most prepared residents, including our beloved celebrities, navigating unforeseen challenges.

## Nairobi's A-Listers Caught in the Deluge

### Star-Studded Estates Submerged
Residential havens often favoured by Nairobi's elite found themselves battling rising waters. In Utawala, sources close to a popular media personality reported their meticulously manicured garden gates were swept away by a swollen stream, threatening their palatial home. Meanwhile, the usually bustling Seasons Road near Clay City, a route frequented by musicians and actors heading to downtown studios, became a standstill, turning a quick commute into an unexpected aquatic adventure. Drivers of sleek SUVs and luxury sedans were seen attempting to ford the new "rivers," some with less success than others. It is a reminder that even the most exclusive addresses are not immune to nature's whims. Perhaps it is a good time to revisit essential red flags to watch for in Nairobi's rental market when properties face such vulnerabilities.

### Traffic Snarls and Unwanted Splashes
Major transit arteries across the city became epicentres of gridlock, proving that Nairobi's legendary traffic can always find a new gear, even underwater. The Ruai Bypass, near its intersection with Kangundo Road, transformed into a vast, unmoving lake, stranding commuters for hours. Whispers suggested some prominent influencers might have missed crucial morning appearances. More alarmingly, the Kariokor Roundabout saw its drainage systems overwhelmed, resulting in raw sewage spewing onto the streets. This created a hazardous situation, particularly at the Kariokor Market junction, where pedestrians, including those potentially en route to high-profile appointments, were forced to navigate contaminated waters, and passing vehicles inadvertently splashed the unsavoury mix onto unsuspecting individuals.

### More Rain on the Horizon?
The Kenya Meteorological Service, known for its crucial advisories, has issued a stark warning that the city is not out of the woods yet, forecasting additional rainfall for the remainder of the week. Residents, especially those in low-lying areas or near struggling drainage systems, are urged to remain extra vigilant. This means our beloved Nairobians, famous or not, might need to trade their designer heels for gumboots for a little while longer, as the city's resilience continues to be tested by Mother Nature's relentless downpour.
