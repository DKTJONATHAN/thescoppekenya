Ten people are dead following a severe road accident on the Nairobi-Nakuru highway near Gilgil early Saturday, April 4, 2026. The tragic incident at Kariandusi involved a speeding 14-seater matatu that lost control and rammed into a stalled trailer, resulting in immediate fatalities and injuries.

Kariandusi Collision Claims Lives The devastating accident occurred on Saturday morning when a matatu, reportedly speeding, collided with a trailer that had developed mechanical problems and stalled along the busy highway. Gilgil Sub-County Police Commander Winston Mwakio confirmed the deaths of ten occupants from the matatu at the scene due to the extensive impact of the collision. Only two people survived the crash and have been transported to a nearby health facility for urgent medical attention. Emergency response teams, comprising police officers and local residents, worked to retrieve the bodies from the wreckage and clear the accident site. Images from the scene show the matatu severely damaged at the front, underscoring the force of the impact. The Nairobi-Nakuru highway is a critical transport route but has unfortunately been the location of numerous road accidents in recent years.

Heightened Road Safety Measures This tragic event follows a recent travel advisory issued by the National Transport and Safety Authority (NTSA), cautioning drivers against reckless driving, especially during the current Easter holiday period. On Thursday, April 2, 2026, NTSA Director General Nashon Kondiwa announced a widespread crackdown targeting dangerous driving habits and unsafe vehicles to mitigate road accidents. Kondiwa stated that the NTSA would collaborate closely with the National Police Service (NPS) to intensify enforcement operations across major highways and urban centers during this busy travel period. Motorists have been urged to prioritize safety, with warnings that reckless behaviors such as speeding, drunk driving, and driver fatigue remain primary causes of accidents across the country. Drivers are advised to strictly adhere to speed limits, avoid operating vehicles under the influence of alcohol, and take regular breaks during long journeys. For vehicles that experience breakdowns, the NTSA Director General stressed the importance of parking safely off the road and placing warning triangles at an adequate distance to alert other road users. Additionally, unroadworthy vehicles, including those with defective brakes, worn-out tires, or faulty lighting, face immediate impoundment.
