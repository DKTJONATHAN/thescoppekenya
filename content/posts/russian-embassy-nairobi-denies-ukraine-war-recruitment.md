## Is the Russian Embassy Recruiting Kenyans for War?

The Russian embassy in Nairobi has vehemently denied any involvement in recruiting Kenyan citizens to fight in the ongoing war in Ukraine, dismissing such accusations as a "dangerous and misleading propaganda campaign". This comes after a Kenyan intelligence report revealed over 1,000 Kenyans are reportedly fighting on the front lines.

**The Scoop**
Well, gather 'round, because this one's a right proper head-scratcher! You've got the Russian embassy in Kenya, right, absolutely fuming, denying they're behind any dodgy schemes to get Kenyans to fight in Ukraine. "A dangerous and misleading propaganda campaign!" they cried on Friday, insisting they're not involved in any "rogue recruitment schemes". What a kerfuffle! They even said they "refute any collusion with entities or individuals who would force or entrap Kenyans under false pretences". But, honestly, can you even imagine? It's like something out of a spy novel, isn't it?

**The Evidence**
But here's where it gets juicy. Because while the embassy is doing its best to look innocent, a bombshell report from Kenyan intelligence officers has landed, suggesting over 1,000 Kenyans have actually gone to Russia and ended up fighting in that dreadful war. That's way, way more than the 200 the authorities were blathering on about before. Apparently, some poor chaps were lured with promises of whopping salaries, like Sh350,000 a month, and even bonuses. What a nightmare! Many left on tourist visas, often going via Turkey or the UAE, before sneaky recruiters started sending them through Uganda, South Africa, and even the Democratic Republic of Congo when surveillance got tighter at Nairobi airport.

And it's not just Kenyans, oh no! An investigative group, "All Eyes on Wagner," said this very month that 1,417 fighters from 35 African countries joined the Russian army between 2023 and mid-2025, with 316 sadly dying. There was even a Ukrainian intelligence report in 2024 saying Moscow had recruited folks from Somalia, Rwanda, Congo, Uganda, and even Nepal, India, and Cuba. The French Institute of International Relations reckons Africa is just "fertile ground" for these Russian recruiters, targeting "poor urban youth" desperate for a better life because Europe's getting too hard to reach. South Africa even had "distress calls" from 17 of its men trapped in Donbas in November 2025 after being tricked. And just this week, four South Africans were returned home, part of a group allegedly tricked into fighting. Honestly, it's a shocking situation, making one wonder about the whole "Skendo Kubwa!
