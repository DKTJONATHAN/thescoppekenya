Digital creator Fatma Banj recently confirmed her single status, announcing her separation from fellow digital personality Xtian Dela. The news, shared across her social media platforms, quickly drew attention from fans. Banj is now eagerly anticipating the upcoming Old School Bongo 6.0 event in Nairobi, scheduled for 2026, where she looks forward to seeing Harmonize perform.

Fatma Banj's Fresh Start Fatma Banj’s announcement signals a definitive end to her widely followed relationship with Xtian Dela. While the reasons for their parting were not disclosed, Banj’s message conveyed a distinctly optimistic and forward-looking tone, emphasizing a renewed focus on personal growth and embracing new experiences. This new phase for Banj highlights a common thread among public figures seeking fresh starts, reminiscent of recent news surrounding Willis Raburu's visit to Chemelil for a prayer session with local clerics.

Anticipation Builds for Old School Bongo 6.0 As part of her new chapter, Banj has expressed considerable excitement for the Old School Bongo 6.0 event. The popular Nairobi series, known for celebrating both classic and contemporary Bongo Flava, consistently features top East African artistes. Harmonize, famed for hits like “Kwangwaru” and “Single Again,” is a highly anticipated performer, expected to draw a large audience.
