Kylian Mbappe's commitment to Real Madrid is under brutal scrutiny. A record-breaking fan petition and Spanish media firestorm follow his injury-enforced absence and a controversial Italian getaway. ### The Illusion of a Superstar Signing
Real Madrid made a grand statement signing Kylian Mbappe in 2024. The expectation was immediate, undeniable glory. Fast forward to May 2026, and what do we have? A staggering 41 goals and six assists in 41 appearances this season - individually impressive, yes. Yet, Madrid is out of the Champions League, humbled by Bayern Munich in the quarter-finals, and Barcelona are on the verge of claiming another La Liga title, potentially even at the Bernabeu this Sunday. Two seasons, zero major trophies since Mbappe's arrival. This isn't just bad luck; it exposes the myth that one player, no matter how talented, can single-handedly guarantee success when their focus is elsewhere.

### The Million-Signature Indictment
The 'Mbappe out' petition collecting nearly 32 million signatures isn't just a record; it's a global indictment. While not every signee is a Madrid die-hard, it signals a universal frustration with the player's perceived attitude. Fans don't just want goals; they demand passion, commitment, and a shared sense of struggle, especially during a season that has seen Madrid falter on the biggest stages. His recent hamstring injury, followed by a conspicuously timed 'rehabilitation break' in Italy, poured gasoline on an already smoldering fire. This isn't just about an injury; it's about optics, and Mbappe's optics right now are terrible.

### World Cup Priorities vs. Club Loyalty
Spanish journalists Antonio Romero and Julio Pulido from Cadena SER aren't just speculating when they suggest Mbappe is "saving himself for France for the 2026 World Cup finals this summer." That's the cold, hard reality of elite-level football today. Romero points out Mbappe's previous bad luck at the European Championship and his clear desire to arrive at the World Cup in "top form." It's a pragmatic, if selfish, approach. Why risk a muscle strain in a likely dead-rubber Clásico when the ultimate prize, a World Cup, is just weeks away? The club's predicament, it seems, is secondary to national glory and personal legacy.
