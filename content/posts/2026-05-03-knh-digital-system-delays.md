## KNH Digital System Faces Delays, Hospital Confirms Stability

Kenyatta National Hospital (KNH) confirmed delays in its new KNH digital system for patient data capture on Sunday, May 3, leaving many patients stranded. The facility experienced temporary service disruptions in discharge and billing processes, attributed to minor teething problems during its large-scale digital transition, impacting operational flow.

### System Challenges and Resolution

KNH addressed widespread social media reports, dismissing claims that its new digital system had entirely collapsed. The hospital clarified that challenges experienced were minor teething problems common in large-scale digital transitions, not compromising patient care and safety. While temporary service delays occurred, particularly in clearance and billing, KNH stated these issues have been resolved. The institution affirmed the system remains operational and is stabilizing as deployment continues. The hospital regretted any inconvenience caused but assured the public of its commitment to patient care.

### Transition to Afya Apex

KNH is actively transitioning from a manual, paper-based record system, which has been in use for over 125 years, to a fully digitized platform known as Afya Apex. This new KNH digital system aims to automate and integrate clinical and administrative services across all departments. The rollout of Afya Apex began on April 15 and is slated for completion within 90 days. The hospital emphasizes that this modernization is crucial for improving efficiency, transparency, and the overall quality of care, aligning with contemporary health trends. KNH urged patients and stakeholders to support this significant digital transformation. KNH stated that such opposition would be dealt with decisively as the institution pushes forward with its reforms for modern healthcare delivery.
