The 12th Africa Magic Viewers' Choice Awards will see Bovi Ugboma and Nomzamo Mbatha co-host its prestigious ceremony on May 9, 2026, in Lagos. This marks a significant shift, ending IK Osakioduwa's remarkable eleven-year tenure as the face of the Pan-African awards.

A New Era for AMVCA Hosting

Introducing the Co-Hosts Nigerian comedian and actor Bovi Ugboma is set to bring his signature wit and stage presence to the AMVCA stage. Joining him is South African actress, producer, and UNHCR Goodwill Ambassador Nomzamo Mbatha, celebrated for her international roles, including *Coming 2 America* and her current portrayal of Queen Nandi in *Shaka iLembe*. Their pairing promises an evening where "mastery meets African excellence," according to organisers. Bovi expressed his understanding of the occasion's significance, stating, "The AMVCA stage is one of the biggest in Africa, and I don't take that lightly."

IK Osakioduwa's Legacy IK Osakioduwa concludes an uninterrupted eleven-year run as the AMVCA host since the awards commenced in 2013. His consistent presence has been a defining feature of the ceremony for over a decade. This transition represents the most substantial change to the presentation of the awards since its debut, with Bovi and Nomzamo Mbatha stepping into the prominent roles.

Leading Nominees and Key Sponsors The 2026 edition of the AMVCAs features a robust field of nominees, with *Gingerrr* and *The Herd* leading the pack, each securing nine nominations across various acting, directing, and technical categories. *To Kill A Monkey* follows closely with eight nominations, while *My Father's Shadow*, from the Davies brothers, earned seven nods. Veteran actress Joke Silva has been appointed head judge for this year's awards. The 12th AMVCA is supported by a strong roster of sponsors including Guinness, MTN, Indomie, QuickTeller, Verve, Lush Hair, and several Diageo brands like Johnnie Walker and Smirnoff Ice.
