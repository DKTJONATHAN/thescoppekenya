## White House Pulls the Plug on Anthropic, Reshaping US AI Landscape

The United States government has dramatically altered its stance on artificial intelligence partnerships, effectively banning Anthropic from federal contracts while simultaneously bolstering rival firm OpenAI. This decision, announced by the Trump administration, marks a significant moment for the American AI industry, sparking debate over innovation, national security, and ethical AI deployment.

### The Administration's Standoff with Anthropic

In a move unprecedented for a homegrown technology company, the Trump administration has designated Anthropic, developer of the Claude AI, a "supply chain risk to national security". This classification, typically reserved for foreign adversaries, came after Anthropic reportedly declined the Department of War's request for unrestricted access to its technology. Dario Amodei, Anthropic's chief executive, sought written guarantees that its AI would not be used for mass surveillance of American citizens or in autonomous weapons systems capable of making life-or-death decisions without human authorization. The administration viewed these stipulations as insubordination, with President Donald Trump dismissing Anthropic as "radical left" and "woke".

Defense Secretary Pete Hegseth escalated the matter, leading to directives ordering all federal agencies to cease using Anthropic's products immediately. The ban extends beyond the public sector, impacting private companies holding government contracts that utilise Anthropic's technology. Anthropic, founded in 2021 by former OpenAI members, is known for its focus on AI safety and its Constitutional AI principles, which guide its Claude family of large language models. Its Claude Opus 4.6 model recently topped AI benchmarks, demonstrating significant capabilities in reasoning and coding.

### OpenAI Hands the Keys to the Pentagon

Hours after the directive against Anthropic, OpenAI, a long-standing competitor, emerged as the clear beneficiary. OpenAI's chief executive, Sam Altman, announced an agreement with the Department of War to deploy its AI models across the Pentagon's classified computer networks. This partnership includes OpenAI staff working directly within military facilities. Altman stated that the Department of War demonstrated "a deep respect for safety" and agreed to principles similar to those Anthropic had requested, including bans on domestic mass surveillance and maintaining human responsibility over the use of force. OpenAI plans to build technical safeguards into its systems and limit deployments to cloud networks.

Sam Altman, who has led OpenAI since 2019, has been a prominent figure in the AI boom, overseeing the launch of models like ChatGPT. OpenAI has recently secured massive funding rounds, including a reported $110 billion in February 2026, valuing the company at hundreds of billions of dollars. This significant capital injection underscores the intense competition and investment in advanced AI technologies.

### Implications for AI Governance and Innovation

The rapid turn of events raises critical questions about the future of AI governance and the role of private tech firms in national security. While OpenAI has committed to safeguards, the enforceability and long-term implications of these agreements remain under scrutiny. The US Congress has, so far, played a minimal role in shaping these arrangements, leaving a vacuum in legislative oversight.

This shift in federal AI strategy highlights the increasing demand for advanced AI capabilities across various sectors. As AI continues to evolve, its applications are expanding, from enhancing productivity in businesses to revolutionising creative fields. For instance, the recent acquisition of ProducerAI by Google is set to transform how prompts are turned into songs, showcasing the diverse and innovative paths AI is taking in the creative economy.

The administration's decision to effectively blacklist a leading AI developer while embracing another underscores a growing tension between rapid technological advancement, corporate ethics, and governmental control. The long-term impact on the American AI industry's competitiveness and ethical development will be a critical area to watch in the coming months.
