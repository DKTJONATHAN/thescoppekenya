## President Ruto Appoints Ida Odinga to UNEP, Francis Meja as PSC Chair Amidst Key State Restructuring President William Ruto has formally appointed Mama Ida Odinga as Kenya's Ambassador and Permanent Representative to the United Nations Environment Programme (UNEP) and named Francis Meja as the new Chairperson of the Public Service Commission (PSC). These significant appointments, alongside numerous others across various government agencies and state boards, were made public in a gazette notice dated February 27, 2026. The restructuring highlights the government's ongoing efforts to streamline public service leadership and enhance national representation on the global stage.

Ida Odinga's Diplomatic Role at UNEP Mama Ida Odinga's appointment as Kenya's Ambassador and Permanent Representative in the Foreign Service, specifically to UNEP, follows her recent parliamentary approval. Her nomination was approved by the National Assembly's Departmental Committee on Defence, Intelligence, and Foreign Relations on February 24, after a vetting process where she articulated her vision for Kenya's environmental diplomacy. With UNEP's headquarters based in Nairobi, her role is deemed critical in advancing Kenya's environmental agenda and strengthening global partnerships in climate action.

Francis Meja's Leadership as PSC Chair Concurrently, President Ruto also appointed Francis Meja as the Chairperson of the Public Service Commission for a six-year term. Meja, who has a distinguished background in both public and private sectors, previously served as a PSC member since January 2025. His appointment, which replaces Ambassador Anthony Muchiri, was approved by the National Assembly's Departmental Committee on Labour on February 25, 2026, after parliamentary vetting. His mandate will involve guiding recruitment, oversight, and policy implementation across government ministries and agencies to promote efficiency and accountability within the public service.

Broader Government Appointments and Reappointments Beyond the President's directives, several Cabinet Secretaries also effected appointments and reappointments to various state corporations and boards, all gazetted on February 27, 2026. These appointments aim to bolster leadership and strategic direction within their respective dockets. The Cabinet Secretary for the National Treasury and Economic Planning, John Mbadi, appointed Abdi Ali Mohamed as a Member and Non-Executive Chairperson of the Board of Directors of the Retirement Benefits Authority for a three-year term. This move is expected to contribute to the oversight and strategic direction of Kenya's pensions sector, which is navigating a defining year in 2026 with new regulations and expanded contribution obligations. The Cabinet Secretary for Health, Aden Duale, appointed six members to the Board of Directors of the Clinical Officers Council of Kenya for a three-year term. The appointees include Kipkoech Geoffrey, Joseph Macharia Wachira, Shadrack M. Asena, Pauline Sirote Korere, Abdirahman Noor Mohamed, and Salma Mohamed Abdi, under various provisions of the Clinical Officers Training, Registration and Licensing Act. For the Ministry of Investments, Trade and Industry, Cabinet Secretary Lee Kinyanjui reappointed Francis Kuria Karu, Grace Nyabonyi Momanyi, and Stephen Kipkosgei Yego as Members of the National Standards Council for an additional three years. This continuity in leadership is vital for maintaining and developing national standards that underpin industrial growth. The Cabinet Secretary for Youth Affairs, Creative Economy and Sports, Salim Mvurya, reappointed Fatma Bakari Barayan as Non-Executive Chairperson of the Board of the Youth Enterprise Development Fund for three years. The focus on youth empowerment through such funds is a testament to the government’s commitment to fostering opportunities for young Kenyans. This emphasis on nurturing talent and providing platforms for growth resonates deeply within Kenyan society, much like the journey of individuals achieving national recognition, such as [Toxic Lyrikali, whose rise from Kayole to stardom has become a significant narrative in the country's creative economy](https://zandani.co.ke/article/toxic-lyrikali-real-name-age-kayole-to-stardom). Further appointments saw the Cabinet Secretary for Water, Sanitation and Irrigation, Eric Muuga, name Isack Abduba Fayo as a Member of the Board of Directors of the Northern Water Works Development Agency for three years, revoking the earlier appointment of Elias Shill to the same position. In the agricultural sector, Cabinet Secretary for Agriculture and Livestock Development, Mutahi Kagwe, appointed Joseph Kiplangat Too, Frederick North-Coombes, Kizito Wangalwa, and Vasey N. Mwaja as members of the Kenya Sugar Research and Training Institute for a three-year term. Finally, Attorney General Dorcas Agik Oduor made two appointments to the Nairobi Centre for International Arbitration, with Miriam Chepchumba Bomett joining as a new member for three years and Samuel Mbiriri Nderitu being re-appointed for a further three-year term. These appointments underscore the nation's commitment to strengthening alternative dispute resolution mechanisms.
