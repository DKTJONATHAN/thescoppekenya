The ruling United Democratic Alliance party is facing internal instability as Secretary General Cleophas Malala confronts intensifying calls for his resignation. Following recent cabinet reshuffles, high-ranking officials are criticizing his management, warning that his leadership is damaging President William Ruto’s political support base.

## Cleophas Malala Faces Potential Ouster Amid UDA Leadership Turmoil

### A Leadership Style Under Fire
Political observers describe Malala’s management as a growing liability rather than the strategic asset the ruling party requires. Critics argue that his combative approach to party administration has alienated long-term allies and fostered unnecessary friction. Instead of creating a unified front, his tenure has been characterized by intense confrontations that have eroded the party's cohesion. There is a prevailing sentiment that his tactics are counterproductive to the stability needed to advance the executive’s legislative and political agenda.

### The Mt. Kenya Marginalization Factor
The internal friction is most visible in the Mt. Kenya region, where influential political leaders have expressed profound dissatisfaction. These stakeholders contend that the current party administration has sidelined their interests, creating a dangerous perception of neglect. This disconnect between the national party office and its most critical voting bloc is causing alarm. Many local leaders believe that Malala’s leadership is driving a wedge between the presidency and its constituents, thereby threatening the durability of the political coalition that secured victory in the previous election.

### Failed Consolidation in the West
Beyond the central region, there is mounting concern regarding Malala's inability to secure support in his own home region of Western Kenya or expand the party's influence into the Coast. The original objective of his appointment was to broaden the party’s national reach and lock in emerging regions. However, the current reality on the ground indicates that the party’s footprint is instead shrinking in these vital areas. By failing to consolidate grassroots support in his home base, Malala has inadvertently highlighted his lack of political utility, leading many to conclude that the party’s management structure requires an immediate and decisive overhaul to prevent further fragmentation.
