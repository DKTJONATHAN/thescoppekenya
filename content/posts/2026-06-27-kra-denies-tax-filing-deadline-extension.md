By holding the line on the cutoff date, the authority mandates that all outstanding obligations must be satisfied through the iTax portal before the clock runs out on Tuesday.

The agency asserts that these portal instabilities have been identified and resolved, encouraging users to proceed with their submissions immediately to avoid final-hour server congestion.

For those requiring urgent technical support or guidance with the portal interface, the revenue agency has advised the use of the mobile short-code *222*5#. This channel serves as the primary point of contact for troubleshooting errors that prevent the successful upload of tax returns.

### Implications for Non-Compliance

Taxpayers who fail to complete their filings by the midnight deadline on June 30 face the risk of default tax assessments. Under current regulations, the authority is empowered to generate automated assessments based on available financial data for those who do not report their earnings independently.

Default assessments often result in additional penalties and interest charges that accrue over time. By forcing compliance through the threat of these assessments, the agency is prioritizing the collection of accurate revenue data over the temporary technical frustrations expressed by the public.

Regional support centers across the country have responded to the high demand by implementing extended operating hours. These stations are now processing inquiries and offering direct assistance to taxpayers struggling to navigate the online requirements, aiming to clear the backlog before the deadline expires.
