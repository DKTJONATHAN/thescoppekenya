The National Transport and Safety Authority (NTSA) announced comprehensive reforms effective Tuesday, March 31, 2026, mandating regular health checks for commercial and PSV drivers and introducing digital monitoring systems. These measures aim to enhance road safety along major highways, particularly the Nairobi-Mombasa and Nairobi-Kisumu-Malaba routes.

NTSA Unveils Driver Health and Digital Surveillance

Mandatory Medical Assessments Under the new NTSA regulations, all drivers, especially those operating commercial and Public Service Vehicles, must undergo mandatory regular medical checks. These assessments will identify health conditions that could impede driving ability, including vision problems, hearing challenges, fatigue-related disorders, and chronic illnesses. The Authority emphasized the critical role of addressing vision impairments, which affect 20 to 30 percent of adults, and conditions like diabetes, with a prevalence of 3 to 4 percent, in mitigating risks linked to fatigue and delayed reaction times. To support driver well-being, the NTSA has also established resting stations along the Northern corridor, ensuring long-distance motorists receive adequate rest. This initiative aligns with broader efforts to ensure driver fitness, complementing ongoing advancements in medical training, such as the [special courses offered through the KMTC](https://zandani.co.ke/article/kmtc-special-courses-new-deal-how-the-namibia-mou-changes-medical-trai).

Real-Time Digital Monitoring In a significant technological advancement, the NTSA plans to introduce enhanced digital monitoring systems to track driver behavior in real time. These systems will incorporate telematics, dashboard cameras, and GPS tracking to monitor critical aspects such as speed, braking patterns, driver alertness, and adherence to traffic rules. The Authority highlighted the adoption of the "Safe System Approach," a strategy embraced by leading road safety nations, which operates on the principle that human error is inevitable, and systems should be designed to anticipate mistakes and minimize their consequences. Vehicles will be required to install NTSA-approved digital systems that relay data to centralized monitoring platforms, allowing for early detection of risky driving patterns. These systems will integrate with the NTSA's existing databases to create comprehensive driver profiles, facilitating law enforcement and accountability.

Addressing Accident Causation The NTSA noted that fatigue, existing medical conditions, and reckless driving continue to be primary contributors to road accidents. According to its Road Safety Status Report 2025, commercial vehicles are an increasingly significant factor in road traffic accidents, accounting for 1,087 cases in 2024, compared to 3,645 for all other vehicle categories. This latest regulatory move is part of the government's wider commitment to implementing data-driven and preventative measures to enhance road safety.
