Commuters across Nairobi and the wider region face increased transport costs as the Matatu Owners Association implemented a 25 percent fare hike today, Wednesday, April 15, 2026. This adjustment follows the Energy and Petroleum Regulatory Authority's recent significant increase in petrol and diesel prices.

Fuel Price Hike Prompts Fare Adjustments

Operators Cite Unsustainable Costs The Matatu Owners Association announced the fare increase during a press briefing held on Wednesday, April 15, 2026. This decision comes a day after the Energy and Petroleum Regulatory Authority (EPRA) raised petrol prices by Ksh28 and diesel prices by Ksh40. Operators argue that the substantial increase in diesel costs, in particular, has made current operating models unsustainable, severely impacting their daily earnings. A representative of the association noted that an increase of Ksh40 in diesel price could reduce a vehicle's daily profit by approximately Ksh2,400 from an average daily earning of Ksh8,000. They appealed to the public for understanding, stating the move was necessitated by external pressures and urged the government to reinstate fuel subsidies.

Immediate Impact on Nairobi Routes The fare adjustment is set to immediately affect common routes within Nairobi and its surrounding estates. For instance, trips from the Nairobi Central Business District (CBD) to areas such as Kawangware, Kibera, and Mathare, previously costing Ksh80, are now expected to rise to Ksh100. Similarly, routes from the CBD to Nyayo Estate, which cost Ksh100, will now climb to approximately Ksh130. Longer trips, such as those to Rongai and Thika from the CBD, could see fares jump from Ksh150 to Ksh190, reflecting the implemented 25 percent increment. This rise translates into hundreds of shillings in additional weekly transport expenses for many daily commuters, compounding existing financial strains on households.

Upcountry Travel Also Affected The fare adjustments extend beyond city commutes, impacting several operators on the Nairobi-to-upcountry routes. Travel costs from Nairobi to Migori, for some bus companies, have increased from approximately Ksh1,400 to Ksh1,800 following EPRA's latest fuel price review. Similarly, the cost of travel from Nairobi to Mombasa has risen to Ksh2,000 from around Ksh1,500, while journeys from Mombasa to upcountry destinations now cost Ksh3,000, up from Ksh2,500. Prior to the official announcement, commuters had already reported instances of fares exceeding a 25 percent increase, raising concerns about the enforcement of the new pricing structure.

Call for Government Intervention The Matatu Owners Association has formally urged the government to consider reinstating fuel subsidies. They contend that this measure is crucial to shield both operators and commuters from further financial strain caused by fluctuating fuel prices. The association cautioned that without such intervention, fare hikes could become a regular occurrence whenever fuel prices rise, placing an increasing burden on residents who rely on public transport for daily activities like work, school, and business. The government is already addressing significant challenges, including the President's recent statement on the inevitability of daily load shedding due to an energy deficit, highlighting a period of various economic pressures on the administration.
