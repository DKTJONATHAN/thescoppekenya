### Why are Joey and Kelsey rejecting commercial brand partnerships?

To understand why this choice is so unusual, it helps to look at how reality TV weddings usually work. Typically, a couple from the franchise is offered a high-end engagement ring for free, often from famous designers like Neil Lane. In exchange, the couple must follow specific rules about how and when they show the ring. If they decide to televise their wedding, the network often pays for the event, but the couple loses control over the guest list, the timing, and even the decor. The wedding essentially becomes a television production rather than a private celebration.

Joey and Kelsey have decided to walk away from these deals. In various interviews, including a conversation with Brianna LaPaglia on her podcast, they explained that they want their wedding to feel like a real life event. When a brand pays for your flowers, your dress, or your venue, you are often required to post about those brands on social media or include them in the ceremony. This can make a wedding feel like a long advertisement. By saying no to these sponsorships, the couple is keeping their wedding private. They want to be the only people making decisions about their big day, even if it means leaving millions of dollars in free goods and services on the table.
