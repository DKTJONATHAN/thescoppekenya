State House has received a 14-day directive to dismantle sections of its perimeter wall bordering the Kirichwa Kubwa River, as part of an intensified effort to reclaim riparian land in Nairobi. The ultimatum was issued on Tuesday, April 14, 2026, by Water Resources Authority (WRA) Chief Executive Officer Mohamed Moulid Shurie during an inspection alongside Nairobi Governor Johnson Sakaja.

## Enforcement on Nairobi Waterways

Officials identified and marked portions of the State House perimeter wall encroaching on the riparian reserve, with a formal notification issued for demolition within two weeks. Governor Sakaja lauded the move as a significant display of leadership, noting President William Ruto's commitment to comply with environmental regulations.

### Presidential Compliance and Programme Scope
President Ruto publicly announced on Thursday, April 9, 2026, that State House would be among structures on riparian land subject to removal, urging voluntary evacuation before enforcement. The President affirmed that a multi-agency notice indicated the wall along Kirichwa Kubwa River falls within the riparian reserve and must be removed, specifying a 15-meter setback from the high-water mark.

This demolition is integral to the Nairobi River Regeneration Programme, a comprehensive initiative aimed at restoring riparian reserves, improving environmental conditions, and developing modern urban infrastructure along Nairobi's rivers. Over KES 50 billion has been allocated to fund this transformation. The program includes river restoration, construction of pedestrian and cycling corridors, and development of public spaces to enhance mobility and the city's environment. Walkways will feature lighting and security for safe movement.

### Urban Development and Past Successes
The broader strategy involves connecting these corridors to various parts of Nairobi, facilitating movement from areas like Eastleigh to Westlands via scenic river pathways, aiming to ease congestion and provide new recreational and transport alternatives. Major developments within the program encompass the construction of a modern Gikomba Market and the revitalization of areas such as Globe Roundabout.

Previous land reclamation efforts have demonstrated success, such as the restoration of the Kibagare River near Westgate, where a public park is currently under development. This regeneration effort aligns with other significant infrastructure projects, similar to how affordable housing projects create 1,000 jobs daily in Nyanza Region, contributing to economic growth. The Nairobi River Regeneration Programme itself has created over 40,000 jobs through various activities.
