By Celestine Nzioka, Za Ndani

President William Ruto, Health Cabinet Secretary Aden Duale, Transport Cabinet Secretary Davis Chirchir, and Water Cabinet Secretary Eric Mugaa have announced a series of new appointments across various government institutions. These significant changes, detailed in a gazette notice dated March 27, 2026, reflect strategic shifts in key leadership roles within the nation's public service.

## Government Leadership Changes

### Presidential Directives

In a gazette notice dated March 27, 2026, President William Ruto formally appointed Kosiom Frank Ole Kibelejenya as an ambassador within the foreign service. This strategic placement marks a significant step in the nation's diplomatic representation. Reports from February 2026 indicated his nomination as Ambassador to Denmark, following parliamentary approval earlier in March 2026.

### Health Sector Restructuring by CS Duale

Health Cabinet Secretary Aden Duale enacted extensive changes within the health sector. He appointed Riyak Ahmed Mohamed and Zablon Mokua as members of the Jaramogi Oginga Odinga Teaching and Referral Hospital board for a three-year term, effective March 27, 2026. The Health CS also reappointed Catherine Kathure Samwel to the Kenya National Hospital Board for another three years, commencing March 27, 2026. Nazim Jiwa Mitha and David Kipchirchir Rotich also joined the Kenyatta National Hospital Board as members for a three-year period.

Furthermore, Duale designated Godana Sora as the Non-Executive Chairperson of the Physiotherapy Council of Kenya. Hilda Cheserem, Washington Atho Waindi, Anjali Bajali, Harrison Gicheru, and Wallace Karuguti were simultaneously named as members of the council, all serving three-year terms from March 27, 2026.

Appointments continued within the Kenya Medical Practitioners and Dentists Council, with Tonnie Mulli joining as a member for three years. Moses Kiptanui, Marion Mutugi, and Rose Jepchumba Kosgey received reappointments to the same board. David Muthaka Ichere was appointed as a board member of the Mathari National Teaching and Referral Hospital for three years, alongside the reappointments of Bernard Kaparo, Japhet Ouru Ogwangi, and Obadia Kipkurui as members of the board.

The Health CS further appointed Solomon Kariuki, Stanley King'e, Nancy Jabungei, Joel Stephen Mukaya, Josephine Nyabeta, Damaris Katsutsu, and Hashim Musa Haji as members of the Board of Directors of the Kenya Medical Laboratory Technicians and Technologists Board for three years. Reappointed to this board for three-year terms were Hadija Bisharo, Warda Mohamed, Kennedy Kuria, and Safari Kali Kithi. Lastly, Lyndah Kemunto Manoti was appointed by Duale as a member of the Pharmacy and Poisons Board for three years, effective March 27, 2026.

### Transport Sector Adjustments

Transport Cabinet Secretary Davis Chirchir made appointments to the National Transport and Safety Authority. Peter Kibe Maina and Kenneth Limo Toroitich have been designated as members of its Board of Directors for a three-year period, effective March 27, 2026.

### Water Sector Appointment

Water Cabinet Secretary Eric Mugaa concluded the series of appointments by naming Beatrice Kanuthu Muchina as a member of the Central Rift Valley Water Works Development Agency Board, for a period of three years. These extensive leadership changes reflect ongoing efforts to streamline public service delivery, aligning with broader governmental fiscal strategies as previously reported by Za Ndani regarding the [Govt Leans on Local Lenders as Debt Jumps by 1.3T in 2025: Kenya's Evolving Fiscal Strategy](https://zandani.co.ke/article/govt-leans-on-local-lenders-debt-jumps-by-1-3t-in-2025-fiscal-strategy).
