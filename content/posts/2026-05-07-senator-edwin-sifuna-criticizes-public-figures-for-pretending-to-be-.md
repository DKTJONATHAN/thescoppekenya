Nairobi Senator Edwin Sifuna publicly criticized prominent figures this week for claiming political neutrality while actively supporting the current administration. Speaking in the capital, the legislator argued that fence-sitting is a calculated attempt to mask loyalty to government interests during a period of intense polarization.

## The Politicization of Neutrality

### The Myth of Institutional Independence
Senator Sifuna’s remarks take aim at public figures who invoke the reputation of former Chief Justice David Maraga to project an image of impartial constitutionalism. By aligning themselves with the legacy of the former Chief Justice, these individuals attempt to insulate themselves from public criticism. However, Sifuna argues that this neutrality is performative. He asserts that these figures are often silent participants in the current government agenda, using the guise of independence to protect their relationships with the executive branch while avoiding the scrutiny reserved for overt political actors.

### Strategic Silence and Corporate Interests
The Senator posits that this feigned neutrality serves as a protective shield for those with significant commercial ties to the state. By maintaining a facade of impartiality, these public figures aim to secure government contracts and maintain corporate stability without alienating their constituencies. Their public statements are strategically designed to sound like objective analysis while consistently mirroring the policy objectives of the administration. This approach allows them to operate as extensions of the regime while evading the accountability that comes with formal political affiliation.

### The Demand for Political Clarity
As political discourse becomes more rigorous, the public is increasingly rejecting the diplomatic language of the middle ground. Senator Sifuna maintains that the current political environment demands transparency and that the practice of straddling both sides of the aisle is no longer sustainable. He warns that the public is adept at identifying those who serve the interests of the administration while masquerading as independent observers. According to the Senator, the era of the neutral observer has concluded, as citizens now require public figures to explicitly define their allegiances rather than relying on the reputations of independent institutions to deflect responsibility.
