## Arteta's Star Boy Dilemma: Loyalty Over Merit?

Mikel Arteta faces a genuine headache with Noni Madueke's undeniable impact overshadowing Bukayo Saka's recent performances. Pundits openly question if the Arsenal coach has the courage to drop his favoured forward, despite Madueke actively changing games while Saka struggles to influence play. The upcoming Everton clash will reveal if merit or reputation guides selection.

### The Uncomfortable Truth About Saka's Form

It's becoming increasingly difficult for anyone with eyes to deny Bukayo Saka is not firing on all cylinders. Against Bayer Leverkusen, Arsenal were "far from their scintillating best", and Saka's 59 minutes on the pitch yielded little. Meanwhile, summer signing Noni Madueke, brought on in the final half an hour, "transformed" the match, earning a crucial penalty. Charlie Austin frankly stated that Madueke's performances "have warranted starting", a stark contrast to the stagnant displays from Arsenal's supposed "star boy."

### Keown's Contradiction and Arteta's Test

Even Arsenal legend Martin Keown, while praising Madueke's "dynamic" and game-changing contribution against Leverkusen, bizarrely concluded it's "not time" for him to start ahead of Saka. This kind of contradictory punditry is exactly why managers often make the wrong calls. Admitting Saka "was not having one of his best nights" yet refusing to countenance a change reeks of clinging to a narrative rather than addressing current form. The real test for Arteta against Everton isn't tactical genius; it's the simple act of choosing the player who is demonstrably performing better right now. Failing to do so only breeds complacency and ignores a clear opportunity for impact.
