**For Za Ndani (zandani.co.ke) by Jona Munyi**

The Premier League relegation battle has reached a fever pitch, with Tottenham orchestrating a dramatic escape. Their recent victory propelled Spurs out of the drop zone, leaving West Ham in a precarious position. As the season nears its climax, the fight for survival promises an intense conclusion for these storied clubs.

## Tottenham's Timely Revival

Tottenham's 2-1 victory at Aston Villa on Sunday night was more than just three points; it was a statement of intent. The win marked their second consecutive success, a rarity for the club since the early stages of the 2025-26 campaign, and crucially moved them out of the Premier League relegation zone. This resurgence follows a dire period for Spurs, who endured a 15-game winless run since the new year, a streak that cost Igor Tudor his job and saw them plunge into the bottom three under new manager Roberto de Zerbi after a loss to Sunderland.

### De Zerbi's Midas Touch

Since a crucial win against bottom club Wolves, De Zerbi appears to have instilled a newfound belief. Pundits have credited his brave tactical setup and the players' unwavering desire as pivotal in their recent upturn. The team's commitment to winning every tackle and second ball, along with a pragmatic shift away from playing out from the back in risky situations, highlights a side now fully committed to the fight. This psychological uplift could be Tottenham's greatest asset heading into the final three weeks of the season.

## West Ham's Perilous Plunge

While Spurs found their footing, West Ham suffered a significant setback. Their 3-0 defeat by Brentford on Saturday was a severe blow, ending a three-match unbeaten run that had seen them gain momentum since the international break. Despite hitting the woodwork four times and arguably deserving more from the frantic contest, the result, compounded by Tottenham's subsequent win, saw Nuno Espirito Santo's side drop back into the relegation zone.

### Brentford Blow and Tough Road Ahead

The psychological ramifications of this sudden shift cannot be understated, especially as West Ham prepares to host league leaders Arsenal in their next fixture. The Hammers must quickly regroup and demonstrate the fighting spirit they have shown in recent weeks to claw their way back to safety. Their remaining fixtures, including a trip to Newcastle and a home game against Leeds, will test their resolve to the limit.

## Forest's European Distraction

Nottingham Forest, currently 16th with a game in hand, presents an intriguing case. Vitor Pereira's side is the most in-form of the three battling to avoid the drop, boasting an unbeaten run of six league games with three wins. However, unlike West Ham and Spurs, Forest also has a demanding European campaign to contend with.

### In-Form but Divided Focus

Forest are halfway to securing a place in the Europa League final, having beaten Aston Villa 1-0 in the first leg of their semi-final on Thursday. A potential major European final in Istanbul on Wednesday, 20 May, could prove a significant distraction, particularly with a potentially must-win Premier League fixture awaiting them just four days later on the final day of the season. Their demanding run-in also includes fixtures against Chelsea, Newcastle, Manchester United, and Bournemouth.

## The Numbers Game: Opta's Verdict

The battle for survival is tight, with the traditional benchmark of 36 points for safety now insufficient. West Ham currently sit on 36 points, Spurs on 37, and Forest on 39, with three weeks left in the season. Data experts Opta have weighed in with their predictions: West Ham are now strong favourites (75.1%) to be relegated this term, with Tottenham at 22.5% and Forest just 2.3%. The coming weeks will reveal if these statistical probabilities hold true or if the Premier League delivers another dramatic twist.
