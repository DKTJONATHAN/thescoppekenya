The National Assembly has approved new Salaries and Remuneration Commission (SRC) Regulations of 2025, standardising civil servant salaries and allowances this year. This significant move aims to promote fairness and transparency within the public sector wage bill, addressing long-standing inconsistencies across government institutions.

Standardised Public Sector Salaries

New Framework for Civil Servant Compensation The reforms, introduced through the SRC Regulations of 2025, establish structured job grades, standardised pay scales, and clear guidelines on allowances. Members of Parliament noted these changes directly address inconsistencies where employees in comparable roles across different government institutions previously received varied salaries due to disparate allowance structures. This new framework represents a fundamental shift from fragmented payment systems to uniform salary bands directly linked to job evaluations. Nyando MP Jared Okello emphasised the necessity of the changes, stating, "There has been a haphazard way of apportionment of salaries. Public officers have had to undergo a lot of disparities. The Regulations create a level of uniformity and certainty." Under the new regulations, each role will be assessed based on qualifications, responsibilities, and experience, then assigned to a defined pay grade.

Rationalising Allowances and Legal Precedent Beyond addressing the fragmented pay system, the regulations introduce a critical rationalisation of allowances. Historically, allowances have been utilised to disproportionately increase earnings in certain government offices. The new rules provide clear guidelines on the maximum allowance a civil servant can receive. This means an employee previously benefiting from high allowances may experience downward adjustments, though potentially offset by an increase in basic pay. For example, an employee earning Ksh100,000 with substantial allowances might have their pay reviewed, with allowances slightly reduced and basic pay rising. Ainabkoi MP Samwel Chepkonga, in moving the motion for approval, informed the House that the revised regulations successfully remedied past legal and constitutional defects that led to their earlier annulment. He affirmed that the approved regulations would empower the SRC to fulfill its mandate in a fair, transparent, and responsible manner. Nairobi County Woman Representative Esther Passaris endorsed the structured approach, calling it essential for effective public wage bill management and ensuring consistency across all government entities. This is particularly relevant as the nation continues to monitor its economic indicators, including [Kenya's Foreign Reserves Fall Amid Iran War and Mounting Pressures](https://zandani.co.ke/article/kenya-s-foreign-reserves-fall-amid-iran-war-and-mounting-pressures). This parliamentary approval effectively resolves a prolonged dispute between the SRC and key state agencies regarding control over public servants' remuneration.
