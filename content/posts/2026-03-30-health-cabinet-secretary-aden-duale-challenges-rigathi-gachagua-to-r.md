Health Cabinet Secretary Aden Duale on Sunday, March 29, 2026, dared former Deputy President Rigathi Gachagua to publicly release an alleged dossier detailing misuse of funds within the Social Health Authority (SHA). Duale spoke at a Waqf empowerment event in Wajir South, strongly refuting Gachagua's criticisms regarding SHA operations and its financial health.

Duale Challenges Gachagua on SHA Dossier

Allegations by Former Deputy President Gachagua Former Deputy President Rigathi Gachagua has consistently voiced strong criticisms against the Social Health Authority, alleging irregularities such as phantom claims and fraudulent payouts. During a Sunday church service in Ndia Constituency, Gachagua claimed that the government was undermining faith-based hospitals through SHA, further asserting that the government-mandated health insurance body would face a complete collapse within six months. He cited billions in unpaid dues to hospitals and urged private facilities to demand outstanding funds or implement upfront collections. These exchanges highlight the ongoing political debates surrounding critical public services, a landscape also recently exemplified by incidents such as [Journalists from Standard and Mediamax attacked during UDA meeting in Cherangany].

Health CS Duale's Rebuttal and Operational Update In response to the allegations, Health Cabinet Secretary Aden Duale dismissed Gachagua's claims as hearsay, maintaining that SHA remains fully functional. Duale challenged Gachagua to release his dossier by 10:00 AM on Monday, March 30, 2026. He stated that KSh 21 billion has been paid to health facilities across the country, including faith-based, private, and public institutions. The Health CS also accused Gachagua of exploiting faith-based and private medical institutions for political gain. Duale further provided detailed statistics on SHA's operations, noting that over 30.1 million individuals are now registered under the scheme, with 12,000 new registrations recorded daily. He outlined significant disbursements under the new health financing framework, totaling approximately Ksh92.5 billion. Of this, Ksh75 billion has been disbursed through the Social Health Insurance Fund (SHIF), while Ksh13 billion has been allocated to support primary healthcare services. Additionally, Ksh3.5 billion has been released for civil servants and teachers under the public officers' scheme, with about Ksh1 billion designated for emergency, chronic, and critical illness care. These disbursements, Duale affirmed, aim to strengthen service delivery across all health facilities. The CS also reported that the government has mobilized approximately Ksh156 billion over the same period to support the programme. This mobilization includes roughly Ksh115 billion from SHIF contributions, Ksh19.8 billion for primary healthcare, Ksh12 billion from civil servants and teachers, and Ksh8.3 billion earmarked for the emergency, critical, and chronic illness fund.
