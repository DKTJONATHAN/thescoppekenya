## Governor Natembeya Calls Shotgun on Presidential Convoys

Trans Nzoia Governor George Natembeya is making waves, boldly challenging the grand spectacle of presidential motorcades and the disruptive road closures they cause. He argues that these elaborate displays of power alienate leaders from everyday Kenyans, questioning their necessity and impact on public life.

### The Governor's Gavel Drop

Natembeya, never one to mince words, spoke on Wednesday, March 11, 2026, about the dizzying array of vehicles accompanying the Head of State. He openly questioned the logic of needing dozens of escort vehicles for a "small function" and why roads become ghost towns for "45 minutes" for an airport trip. The irony of leaders enjoying open roads while citizens languish in "man-made gridlock" was not lost on him, making one wonder about the concept of public service.

### Wheels of Power

The presidential motorcade has certainly seen some impressive upgrades recently. President William Ruto now rolls in an armored Toyota Land Cruiser 300 series VX-R, a KSh 23 million fortress on wheels, integrated into his fleet a few years ago. This formidable addition joins a lineup that already boasts multiple Land Cruiser 200 series, a sleek Lexus 570, high-end Mercedes-Benz sedans, and the classic presidential limousine. While these machines are undeniable symbols of state power, and security is paramount, Natembeya's bold critique hints at a deeper conversation regarding public resources and their allocation. After all, similar discussions have arisen concerning how essential services are funded, like the recent debates around delayed salary increments for doctors after budget allocations.

### Traffic Jam Trouble

Beyond the glitz and the glamour, Governor Natembeya's point is sharp: the sheer scale and execution of these movements profoundly affect the daily lives of Kenyans. He suggests it is time for a serious rethink on how these convoys operate, ensuring they better respect the time and needs of the public. After all, what good is a leader who cannot share the road with the very people they lead?
