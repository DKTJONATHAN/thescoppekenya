## The Hidden Emotional Toll: Why Romantic Roles Impact Nollywood Personal Lives

Nollywood actress Sandra Okunzuwa recently shared the personal cost of her profession, revealing that a romantic role contributed to the collapse of a long-term relationship. Her experience highlights a common dilemma: is it possible for actors to keep their professional performances separate from their personal lives when the work requires such intense intimacy?

### The Technical Nature of On-Screen Romance

Viewers frequently assume that the chemistry seen in Nigerian films is genuine or indicates a real connection between actors. However, Okunzuwa addressed this misconception during an appearance on the BTS Reality podcast. She explained that filming romantic moments is far from an intimate experience. Instead, it is a clinical, technical process that demands significant stamina.

From an actor's viewpoint, a kissing scene is essentially choreography. The set is rarely private. It is filled with camera operators adjusting angles, lighting technicians moving equipment, and directors providing constant feedback. These shoots often occur in the middle of the night, stretching from 12 AM to 1 AM to ensure every frame meets the director's vision. Okunzuwa emphasized that her priority during these sessions is hitting the right marks and staying true to the character’s motivations. By the time the director calls "cut," the focus is on finishing the workday rather than enjoying the moment. Unfortunately, explaining the clinical reality of a film set to a partner outside the industry can be a significant challenge.

### Navigating Relationship Boundaries

The primary conflict arises from the disconnect between how actors view their work and how their partners perceive those performances. Okunzuwa disclosed that a former boyfriend ended their relationship because he could not accept her portraying affection with a co-star.

This situation exposes the strain professional obligations place on personal boundaries. When actors sign a contract, they agree to fulfill the script's requirements, regardless of how those scenes might be interpreted by loved ones. The job demands a specific type of emotional labor that frequently clashes with conventional relationship expectations. While societal pressures regarding these scenes often differ between men and women, the resulting emotional fallout is a burden shared by many professionals in the field. Consequently, the work that drives an actor’s professional success can simultaneously threaten their personal stability.

### The Perspective of the Audience

Understanding this tension is important for viewers. Fans often engage with film content under the assumption that performers are living out a fantasy. Recognizing that actors sacrifice their personal comfort and relationships to create this entertainment changes the perception of their work. It serves as a reminder that on-screen romance is a manufactured product rather than a lifestyle, and that these performers often pay a high price in their real lives to entertain the public.

### Looking Ahead

The industry is seeing a shift in how performers handle these concerns. Many now prioritize transparent communication with their partners before committing to specific projects. As Nollywood continues to expand globally, it is likely that more performers will discuss the mental health and relationship challenges inherent in their career choices. 

Ultimately, professional intimacy in cinema remains a job requirement. While it is essential for the craft of storytelling, it continues to extract an emotional tax from those who perform it.
