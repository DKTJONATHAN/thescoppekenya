Autopsy Reveals Brian Njunge Was Strangled To Death Contradicting is central to this update for Kenyan readers.

On May 27, 2026, a post-mortem examination by Chief Government Pathologist Johansen Oduor revealed that 24-year-old Brian Njunge was strangled to death at Kiambu Police Station. The forensic findings directly contradict official police claims that the young man committed suicide.

## Forensic Autopsy Disproves Kiambu Police Suicide Narrative

The official explanation provided by authorities regarding the death of Brian Njunge suggested a standard case of self-harm within a cell. Officers on duty claimed that the 24-year-old man used a piece of a mattress cover to end his life. However, the family rejected this narrative immediately after viewing the body at the scene. The presence of blood on the clothing and nose of the deceased indicated that a violent encounter had taken place prior to his death.

### Discrepancies in the Official Police Report

The family of the deceased remained suspicious of the suicide claims due to the physical evidence observed at the station. While the police narrative focused on a self-inflicted injury using bedding, the visible trauma on Njunge suggested otherwise. The family noted that the condition of the body was inconsistent with a hanging, prompting them to demand a professional forensic evaluation to uncover the truth behind the incident.

### Pathologist Confirms Manual Strangulation

Chief Government Pathologist Johansen Oduor conducted the autopsy and provided a report that fundamentally changed the direction of the investigation. The examination determined that the cause of death was strangulation rather than hanging. This medical evidence suggests that Njunge was a victim of physical assault while in custody. The forensic details provided by Dr. Oduor have focused the case on the possibility of foul play by those responsible for the safety of the detainees.

### Demands for Accountability and Justice

The gap between the forensic findings and the initial police report has raised serious questions about accountability within the Kiambu Police Station. This incident is being viewed as a significant example of police brutality and a subsequent attempt to mask the facts. The family of Brian Njunge is now calling for a thorough investigation to identify the individuals responsible for the killing. Public demand for justice continues to grow as the case highlights ongoing concerns regarding the safety of citizens in police custody.
