Boxers Protifas "Majembe" Odipo and Mbavu Destroyer ignited Nairobi's entertainment scene during a TikTok Live on Sunday, March 29, 2026. Majembe sternly warned his opponent to cease contacting his girlfriend, dramatically escalating their personal rivalry ahead of their “Vurugu” bout at Kasarani Stadium Indoor Arena.

Boxer Rivalry Turns Personal as "Vurugu" Bout Nears

Majembe's Public Warning Shakes Social Media What began as a promotional livestream for their upcoming fight swiftly transformed into a direct confrontation on Sunday night, March 29, 2026. Protifas Odipo, known in the ring as Majembe, made it unequivocally clear that he would not tolerate interference in his romantic relationship. He directly accused Mbavu Destroyer of reaching out to his partner, shifting the narrative from boxing theatrics to his personal affairs. Majembe issued a stern caution during the session: “Stop texting my woman. I saw you trying to text her; you need to stop that, or I will beat you up.” He further emphasized his girlfriend’s disinterest in "tough guys" or drama, stating, “My woman said she doesn’t want someone who is full of trouble (ngori); she wants someone polite.”

Mbavu Destroyer's Provocative Challenge Majembe’s public outburst follows a series of provocative statements made by Mbavu Destroyer earlier in March. On March 8, 2026, the “Weka Mawe” creator took to Instagram, not only boasting of his intention to defeat Majembe in the boxing ring but also vowing to "snatch" his girlfriend right before his eyes. Mbavu’s assertive message declared, “Majembe, I’m going to break your ribs and take your girlfriend… whether she likes me or not, I don’t care. You must pay for your mistakes.” He reinforced his image as someone "full of trouble" and ready for a contentious clash, further fueling the animosity between the two amateur boxers.

"Vurugu" Bout: A High-Stakes Showdown The escalating bad blood between Majembe and Mbavu Destroyer has transformed their upcoming match into one of Nairobi’s most eagerly awaited sporting events. Scheduled for April 4, 2026, at the Kasarani Stadium Indoor Arena, with the action commencing at 3:00 pm, the “Vurugu” bout promises more than just a boxing match. The high-stakes drama surrounding Majembe and Mbavu Destroyer ensures the "Vurugu" bout will draw massive attention, solidifying its place among Nairobi's most anticipated events. Much like the enduring influence of popular music groups, as explored in [Wakadinali's Vicious Grip: No Fading Echo in 2026], this boxing rivalry is making a significant mark on the cultural calendar. With Pastor Kanyari publicly endorsing Mbavu Destroyer, the intensity surrounding this personal and professional rivalry has reached new heights as both fighters prepare to settle their differences with gloves on.
