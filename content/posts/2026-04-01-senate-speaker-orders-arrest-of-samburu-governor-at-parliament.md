Senate Speaker Amason Kingi today ordered the immediate arrest of Samburu Governor Lati Lelelit at Parliament buildings. The directive, issued on Wednesday afternoon, April 1, 2026, followed an active warrant against the governor for his repeated failure to appear before the Senate's watchdog committee regarding audit queries.

Governor Arrest Ordered

Speaker's Directive Speaker Amason Kingi issued the directive at approximately 3:25 PM on Wednesday, instructing the Sergeant-at-Arms to promptly apprehend Governor Lelelit and escort him to the Parliament Police Station. This action was taken after confirming the governor's presence within the Parliament precincts. Reports indicate Governor Lelelit was addressing the press outside the buildings, having just arrived to meet Senate leadership in an attempt to prevent his arrest for missing previous summons.

Calls for Accountability Senate Majority Leader Aaron Cheruiyot initiated the demand for action on the floor of the House, urging Speaker Kingi to intervene before the governor could depart. His sentiments were echoed by Kitui Senator Enoch Wambua, who condemned governors for their open defiance of Senate summons. Senator Wambua particularly singled out Nairobi Governor Johnson Sakaja for alleged disobedience and resistance to summons and potential arrest. He emphasized the necessity of ending the disrespect towards Parliament.

Escalating Tensions with Governors The Senate's watchdog committee, responsible for county government oversight, has repeatedly expressed deep frustration over numerous governors' consistent absence, viewing it as a direct challenge to the Senate's authority. This incident follows closely after police officers reportedly camped outside Nairobi Governor Johnson Sakaja's City Hall office yesterday, Tuesday, March 31, 2026, in an attempt to arrest him for similar failures to honor Senate summons.
