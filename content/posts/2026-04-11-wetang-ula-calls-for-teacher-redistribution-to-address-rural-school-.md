National Assembly Speaker Moses Wetang'ula advocated for immediate, systematic teacher redistribution to alleviate critical staffing deficits in rural schools. Speaking in Trans Nzoia County on Saturday, Wetang'ula highlighted the alarming national disparity in teacher deployment, calling for urgent intervention from the Ministry of Education.

Addressing Rural Teacher Imbalance

Persistent Shortages Despite Recruitment Speaker Wetang'ula voiced concern over the acute teacher shortage in rural institutions, despite the government's employment of over 100,000 teachers since President William Ruto assumed office, with an additional 20,000 slated for recruitment in June and July 2026. He emphasized that such significant recruitment efforts should preclude an unfair teacher ratio. The disparity persists because many teachers, once employed and posted to rural areas, seek transfers to urban centers, leaving rural schools understaffed.

Case Study: Kolongolo Secondary School The dire situation in some rural schools was underscored by Wetang'ula, who cited St Francis Kolongolo Girls Secondary School in Kwanza Constituency, Trans Nzoia County, where there is a shortage of up to 28 teachers. He deemed this staffing gap unjustified, particularly under President Ruto’s administration, which has improved the national teacher-to-learner ratio. Equitable teacher distribution is now more crucial than recruitment itself, asserting no region should suffer from poor deployment policies. This aligns with broader efforts to refine educational frameworks and transitions, as seen in discussions regarding [KNEC to KNEAC: A Welcome Reform with Caution - Navigating Kenya's Educational Transition](https://zandani.co.ke/article/knec-to-kneac-welcome-reform-with-caution).

Call for Fair Postings Wetang'ula specifically urged Education Cabinet Secretary Julius Ogamba to ensure teacher postings are handled fairly, without personal preferences for urban stations influencing decisions. He noted that teachers' tendency to favor urban postings has significantly contributed to the national staffing imbalance. This call comes as the Teachers Service Commission (TSC) prepares to upgrade 200 offices across the country, enhancing connectivity and digital infrastructure ahead of a major teacher recruitment drive.
