Jubilee Deputy Party Leader Fred Matiang'i has decisively ruled out any political alliance with President William Ruto ahead of the 2027 General Election. Speaking recently on his move to the United Opposition, Matiang'i dubbed a partnership with the Head of State as "counter-productive," citing a pattern of fractured relationships between the President and his former allies.

## Matiang'i Declares No Alliance with Ruto for 2027

### Why the ‘Super CS’ Won't Join Camp Ruto

When questioned about a potential invitation to collaborate with the UDA party leader, Matiang'i was notably candid, questioning the rationale of aligning with an administration he claims is already grappling with internal dissent. "What would we be discussing? Work with him on what? Look at his history, the people he has wanted to work with," Matiang'i remarked. "He is already quarrelling with the ones he has been working with. Surely, am I from Mars? What difference is it going to make?" His remarks paint a vivid picture of a political figure deeply skeptical of the President's ability to maintain alliances.

### Fueling the Fire: Matiang'i's Economic Critique

The Jubilee chief also directed sharp criticism at the Kenya Kwanza administration's management of the energy sector, particularly the recent controversies surrounding fuel prices. He accused the government of succumbing to "vested elite interests" that, in his view, are manipulating policy and engineering a crisis. "If I were Head of State, I would not engage and allow cartels to operate in the whole sector… I don’t understand why we got into these agreements with private companies," he asserted, suggesting current agreements disproportionately benefit private entities over ordinary citizens. This sentiment resonates with broader concerns raised by various factions regarding the administration’s decisions and expenditures, as lobby groups have also raised concerns over President Ruto’s remarks and State House expenditure.

### Betting Big: Matiang'i's Presidential Path

Despite a robust opposition lineup featuring political heavyweights such as Kalonzo Musyoka, Martha Karua, Eugene Wamalwa, and even former DP Rigathi Gachagua, Matiang'i remains steadfast in his ambition for the nation's top office. He insists that his bid is supported by "scientific work" and a profound conviction in his victory. "I have not thought about not being President. I would not invest in something if I am not like 70-80% sure about it," he declared, adding, "I can’t see President Ruto’s pathway to election victory." Addressing his strong ties to the previous administration, Matiang'i squarely refuted claims that he is merely a "project" of 4th President Uhuru Kenyatta, affirming his loyalty to the Jubilee Party but emphasizing his independent candidacy is rooted in his professional record. With the 2027 General Election approaching, Matiang'i's statements undeniably signal a tightening of political battle lines within the United Opposition.
