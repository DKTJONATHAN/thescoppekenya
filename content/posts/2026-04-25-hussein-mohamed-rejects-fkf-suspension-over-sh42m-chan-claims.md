Hussein Mohamed has vehemently rejected his suspension by the Football Kenya Federation (FKF), declaring the move unjustified and asserting that no funds were lost during the 2025 African Nations Championship (CHAN) preparations. His statement, released today, challenges allegations of financial mismanagement amounting to Ksh42 million.

## Mohamed Refutes FKF Claims

The FKF's National Executive Committee (NEC) announced Mohamed's suspension yesterday, following allegations of misappropriating Ksh42 million earmarked for CHAN preparations. The NEC also directed Abdullahi Yussuf Ibrahim and Acting CEO Dennis Gicheru to step aside as part of their resolution.

### Insurance Procurement Clarification

Mohamed dismissed the accusations, stating that the procurement of insurance for CHAN 2025 was conducted strictly within the regulations set by the Confederation of African Football (CAF). He emphasized, "The procurement of insurance for CHAN 2025 was conducted in line with the CAF Host Agreement, which defines the structures and processes governing tournament operations." Mohamed clarified that CAF, not FKF, was responsible for securing and maintaining the necessary insurance coverage for players throughout the three-week tournament period. He explicitly stated, "FKF did not transfer or pay any money to any insurance company for CHAN competitions," confirming that all services adhered to CAF requirements.

### Claims of a Smear Campaign

The embattled FKF boss claimed these allegations constitute a targeted smear campaign designed to undermine his leadership and reform initiatives. He described the situation as "a desperate act of revenge for the steadfast positions I have taken in the past. This also explains the choice of using me as the poster child in the so-called CHAN scandals." Mohamed affirmed his readiness to cooperate with investigative agencies but criticized what he termed 'unfounded and malicious reports' circulating publicly. He reiterated his commitment to ongoing reforms within the FKF, despite encountering significant resistance in his efforts to combat corruption.

### Interim Leadership and Financial Directives

Following the NEC resolution, Deputy President Macdonald Mariga has been installed as the Acting FKF President, pending the outcome of ongoing investigations. Additionally, the committee ordered the freezing of all FKF bank accounts linked to Mohamed in his official capacity. This measure was taken to preserve evidence and prevent any potential interference, aligning with a broader focus on financial accountability and transparency.
