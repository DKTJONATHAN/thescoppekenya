Goldberg, a prominent beverage brand, launched its "Back to Bar" campaign on Friday, April 17, 2026, at the Lagos Brewery Bar. The initiative aims to reward loyal football fans across Nigeria with all-expense-paid trips to the United Kingdom or Lisbon to experience Super Eagles matches live.

## Goldberg Launches Super Eagles Campaign

The "Back to Bar" campaign offers a straightforward path to significant rewards for football enthusiasts. Consumers who purchase two bottles of Goldberg at participating establishments instantly receive airtime rewards and automatically qualify for weekly raffle draws. Over the campaign's duration, ten fortunate winners will secure an all-expense-paid journey to either the United Kingdom or Lisbon, providing an unparalleled opportunity to watch the Super Eagles compete live. This direct approach emphasizes immediate value and transparent participation, crucial elements in fostering consumer trust and engagement.

### Reinforcing a Rich Football Legacy

The campaign's launch event, held at the Lagos Brewery Bar, drew media representatives, dedicated football fans, and respected figures from the sport. The attendance of former Super Eagles players, including Friday Elahor, Victor Agali, Ifeanyi Udeze, Jonathan Akpobore, Precious Dede, and Ebi Onome, underscored Goldberg's long-standing commitment to Nigeria's vibrant football culture. This connection highlights the brand's role in supporting and celebrating the nation's passion for the beautiful game, integrating itself deeply within the fabric of local sports entertainment.

### Meeting Modern Consumer Realities

Kunle Aroyehun, Senior Brand Manager for Goldberg at Nigerian Breweries Plc, articulated that the campaign was specifically crafted to align with current consumer spending patterns. "We understand what it means to go to a bar right now. People are more intentional with how they spend, but they still want to enjoy football and the moments that come with it," Aroyehun stated, emphasizing the campaign's design to offer tangible value from the very first purchase. This strategy ensures that consumers feel rewarded for their choices, offering both instant benefits and the chance for a transformative football experience.

### The Heart of Fan Culture

Sandra Amachree, Head of Marketing Communications at Nigerian Breweries Plc, highlighted the campaign's roots in the authentic local experience of football. "Football in Nigeria is not something people experience occasionally; it is part of everyday life. And for many fans, that experience is shaped in bars," Amachree noted. This perspective recognizes bars as vital spaces where fan culture is forged and shared. This understanding of localized passion extends across the continent, where fans eagerly engage with various forms of entertainment, from sports to reality television. For instance, the excitement around programs like I'm A Celebrity South Africa 2026 [https://zandani.co.ke/article/im-a-celebrity-south-africa-2026-kenya-watch-vote] demonstrates the strong regional appetite for shared viewing experiences. By supporting these communal viewing environments, Goldberg aims to become an integral part of the fan experience. The campaign is now active across participating bars nationwide, featuring weekly draws and real-time winner announcements to maintain visibility and encourage continuous participation.
