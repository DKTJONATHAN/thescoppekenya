The Kenya Revenue Authority (KRA) is set to implement the EAC Customs Bond starting March 23, 2026, standardizing regional customs processes for East African Community (EAC) cross-border traders. This new unified guarantee system, officially launched by President William Ruto, aims to enhance compliance and simplify cargo transit throughout the region.

## Regional Customs Bond to Boost Trade Efficiency

The decision to roll out the EAC Customs Bond follows the 47th Sectoral Council on Trade, Industry, Finance & Investment (SCTIFI) of the East African Community. President William Ruto, in his capacity as the outgoing Chairperson of the EAC Summit Heads of State, officially launched the bond on March 7, 2026. The KRA has informed all Customs Agents that the EAC Bond will become effective on March 23, 2026.

### Unifying Cross-Border Trade Guarantees

This initiative introduces a unified financial guarantee system recognized across multiple East African countries, eliminating the previous requirement for separate bonds in each jurisdiction. This shift is expected to significantly reduce administrative burdens for traders, who previously faced delays, duplication, and increased operational costs due to individual country guarantees.

### Streamlining Operations for Stakeholders

The new system is anticipated to directly impact customs agents, freight forwarders, and transporters handling cross-border cargo, requiring them to quickly adapt to streamlined trade operations within the region. KRA, in collaboration with the EAC, will jointly oversee the implementation and provide necessary support and sensitization for customs agents and other relevant stakeholders. This reform promises lower compliance costs and reduced administrative delays for logistics firms and manufacturers moving goods from ports such as Mombasa. This streamlined process will benefit various sectors, including the robust agricultural export market, exemplified by Kenya’s finest coffees shining at Nairobi Taste of Harvest 2026 Africa.

### Enhanced Visibility Through Technology

At the core of the new system is technology akin to the Regional Electronic Cargo Tracking System. This integration allows for real-time tracking, monitoring, and reporting of goods as they traverse borders. Revenue authorities like KRA and the Ugandan Revenue Authority (URA), along with insurers and logistics players, will gain improved visibility over cargo, thereby enhancing risk management and mitigating instances of diversion, fraud, or non-compliance during transit.

### Facilitating Trade and Mitigating Risks

Customs bonds are available to importers, exporters, customs brokers, and freight forwarders involved in international trade. These bonds facilitate trade by offering assurance to customs authorities and mitigating financial risks associated with cross-border goods movement. In cases of non-compliance or default, these bonds can cover unpaid duties or penalties. KRA confirms that all essential system upgrades have been completed, ensuring a smooth transition as stakeholders adopt the new bond platform for cargo movement across East African borders.
