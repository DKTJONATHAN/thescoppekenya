Reigning world champions Emmanuel Wanyonyi and Beatrice Chebet once again seized the coveted Sportsman and Sportswoman of the Year titles at the 22nd SOYA Awards gala held on Friday night at Uhuru Gardens. Their repeated victories firmly establish their dominance in Kenyan athletics, thrilling fans nationwide.

SOYA Awards Crowns Wanyonyi and Chebet Again

A Night of Double Glory The 22nd edition of the SOYA Awards unfolded with dazzling fanfare at Uhuru Gardens, celebrating the pinnacle of Kenyan sporting achievement. Emmanuel Wanyonyi, a formidable force on the track, successfully defended his Sportsman of the Year title, a win that underscores his consistent excellence. Similarly, Beatrice Chebet, whose prowess has captivated audiences globally, retained her Sportswoman of the Year crown, affirming her status as a leading figure in women's athletics. The atmosphere was charged with excitement as athletes, celebrities, and dignitaries gathered to honor the nation's best. The event was graced by Ethiopian athletics legend Haile Gebrselassie, among other distinguished guests.

Cementing Athletic Legacies Wanyonyi and Chebet’s consecutive wins are more than just accolades; they are definitive statements of enduring legacy within the highly competitive world of professional sports. As reigning world champions, their ability to maintain such high levels of performance year after year serves as profound inspiration for aspiring athletes across the country. Wanyonyi, the world 800 metres champion, clinched top honors at the World Athletics Championships in Tokyo, Japan, while Chebet secured a historic double gold in the 5,000m and 10,000m at the same championships.
