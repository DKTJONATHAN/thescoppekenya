Transport operators and matatu saccos nationwide have implemented an immediate fare increment effective Wednesday, April 15, 2026. This move follows the Energy and Petroleum Regulatory Authority (EPRA)'s latest fuel price review for April to May 2026, which saw significant increases in key petroleum products.

Transport Operators Adjust Fares After EPRA Fuel Price Hike

EPRA's Latest Price Review Details The Energy and Petroleum Regulatory Authority (EPRA) released its latest review on Tuesday, April 14, increasing the price of Super Petrol by Ksh28.69 per litre and Diesel by Ksh40.30 per litre. Kerosene prices remained unchanged. Following these adjustments, Super Petrol will retail at Ksh206.97 per litre, Diesel at Ksh206.84 per litre, and Kerosene at Ksh152.78 per litre. These new prices are in effect from April 15 to May 14, 2026.

Significant Rise in Transport Operating Costs The Kenya Transporters Association (KTA) issued an advisory to its members, highlighting the substantial impact of the diesel price increase on overall transport expenses. The association noted that the Ksh40 per litre surge in diesel represents an approximate 24.5 per cent increase, a significant shock to the cost structure of road freight services. According to KTA, fuel constitutes about 55 per cent of total operating costs in road transport. The association's analysis estimates that the recent fuel hike translates to an overall increase of approximately 13 to 14 per cent in transport operating costs. KTA has advised its members to promptly review their pricing structures and adjust transport rates to ensure business sustainability.

Industry Leaders Confirm Fare Increments Albert Karakacha, President of the Matatu Owners Association, confirmed that fare adjustments would take effect from Wednesday, April 15. He stated that the significant rise in diesel prices, a primary fuel for public transport, necessitated the upward revision of fares for commuters. Operators were urged to engage clients and contractual partners transparently to explain the basis of these fare increments.

Government Measures to Cushion Consumers EPRA indicated that the government has taken steps to mitigate the impact of the price hikes on consumers. This includes a reduction in Value Added Tax (VAT) on petroleum products from 16 per cent to 13 per cent. Additionally, approximately Ksh6.2 billion from the Petroleum Development Levy has been deployed to cushion consumers from the full extent of the price increases.
