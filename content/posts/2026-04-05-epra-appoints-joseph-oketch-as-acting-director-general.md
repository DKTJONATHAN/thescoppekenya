The Energy and Petroleum Regulations Authority (EPRA) has appointed Joseph Oketch as its acting Director General, effective immediately, following the resignation of Daniel Kiptoo. Oketch’s appointment, announced Sunday, April 5, 2026, comes amidst a scandal involving substandard fuel importation.

Leadership Transition at EPRA

Oketch Assumes Leadership Role Joseph Oketch, formerly head of the Electricity and Renewable Energy Directorate, will now steer EPRA as its acting Director General. EPRA Board Chairman Adan Ali expressed confidence in Oketch’s ability to lead, citing his 25 years of experience within the energy sector, including prior roles at Kenya Power and the Rural Electrification Authority (REA). Oketch, who joined EPRA approximately 10 years ago, holds a Bachelor of Science in Electrical Engineering, a Master's degree and Postgraduate Diploma in Project Planning and Management from the University of Nairobi, and a PhD in Strategic Management from Kenyatta University. He will continue to oversee the Electricity and Renewable Energy Directorate in his new capacity.

Predecessor's Scandal and Resignation Oketch's appointment follows the resignation of his predecessor, Daniel Kiptoo, on Saturday, April 4, 2026. Kiptoo's departure was prompted by his arrest Friday evening in connection with the irregular importation of substandard fuel valued at over Ksh 4 billion. He resigned alongside former Petroleum Principal Secretary Mohammed Liban and former Kenya Pipeline Company (KPC) Managing Director Joe Sang. Investigations by the Office of the President indicate that falsified in-country fuel stock data was used to justify an emergency fuel procurement by the Ministry of Energy and Petroleum. This shipment, acquired outside the established Government-to-Government (G-2-G) oil supply agreement with Gulf nations, reportedly involved prices exceeding contracted rates, violated emergency procurement protocols, and consisted of fuel below mandated quality standards.
