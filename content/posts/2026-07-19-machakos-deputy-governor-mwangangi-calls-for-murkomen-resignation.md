## County Security Crisis Triggers National Cabinet Demands Machakos Deputy Governor Mwangangi Calls for Murkomen Resignation is the central subject of the update.

Machakos Deputy Governor Francis Mwangangi today demanded the immediate resignation of Interior Cabinet Secretary Kipchumba Murkomen and Principal Secretary Raymond Omollo, citing a critical collapse of law and order following a surge in organized gang violence across the county.

This ultimatum marks a significant escalation in the ongoing friction between devolved units and the national security apparatus. Mwangangi asserts that the deliberate failure to curb goon activity at public events threatens the foundations of local democracy. His public stance mirrors growing frustration among county executives who feel abandoned by state enforcement agencies tasked with maintaining public safety.

The demand follows a string of security breaches in the region, including two reported assaults on media personnel attempting to cover public meetings. Recent weeks saw the disruption of developmental forums by unidentified groups, leaving residents vulnerable and the local administration unable to guarantee the safety of its own officials. These incidents reflect broader patterns seen previously in [Ol Kalou](https://zandani.co.ke/article/police-declare-saba-saba-protests-illegal) where political gathering safety has remained a contested point of national policy.

Constitutional experts point out that while national security remains a function of the central government, the County Governments Act requires close coordination between governors and local security committees. Under the current legal framework, county officials have an oversight role in identifying security gaps, though they lack direct command over the National Police Service. This legislative divide often forces local leaders to seek political solutions when administrative channels fail to yield results.

Previous governors have frequently challenged the Interior Ministry regarding the allocation of resources and the slow response time to regional threats. Mwangangi's call is unique because it shifts the target from departmental policy to the personal tenure of the Cabinet Secretary. This departure from diplomatic engagement toward public ultimatums suggests that the existing communication lines between the national security council and regional administrations are failing.

### Ministry Response and Escalating Tensions

The Ministry of Interior issued a brief statement this afternoon dismissing the allegations of complicity. A spokesperson for the ministry maintained that security enforcement remains governed by national protocols and is not subject to the political timelines of county officials. Officials further emphasized that the government continues to investigate the incidents reported in Machakos, cautioning that premature political demands undermine the professional work of active intelligence units.

Public sentiment in Machakos remains polarized. Residents who have witnessed the recent rise in violence have expressed support for stronger accountability measures, echoing a recent national conversation on safety that gained traction after [notable judicial rulings](https://zandani.co.ke/article/court-sentences-4-men-to-death-over-murder-of-ex-mp-george-muchai) reinforced the importance of tackling systemic insecurity. For the victims of the recent goon attacks, the political debate highlights a long-standing disconnect between how security is managed in the capital and how it is experienced on the ground.

### Institutional Deadlock

The tension underscores a larger structural problem within the security sector. When county leaders publicly attack national cabinet members, it signals a breakdown in the inter-governmental cooperation necessary for maintaining stability. Critics argue that until the role of county governments in security enforcement is clearly defined by new legislation, these confrontations will likely continue to disrupt local governance.

Political analysts suggest the Machakos ultimatum will force a reaction from the Office of the President. If the Interior Ministry cannot demonstrate a tangible reduction in gang-related activity, more governors may join the call for a leadership overhaul. All eyes now shift to the upcoming security briefing scheduled for mid-week, where the national government is expected to present a revised strategy for regional security coordination.
