The family of Maureen Gathigia Kinyua has formally petitioned the Office of the Director of Public Prosecutions (ODPP) for a fresh inquest into her 2019 death. Their lawyers wrote on March 19, 2026, insisting her death in the Mataga area was not a road accident as officially recorded, but a planned killing.

## Petition for Fresh Inquest

Maureen Kinyua's family, led by her father James Kinyua Macharia, has hired a law firm to press for a new inquest. Their formal letter to the ODPP details six primary reasons why they believe her death was a premeditated murder disguised as a boda boda accident. The family has presented witness accounts and physical evidence that directly contradict the initial official narrative.

### Contradictory Evidence Emerges

The family's letter explains that Maureen was reportedly lured to Mataga by a female friend suspected of having connections to a prominent political figure from the Mount Kenya region, where she remained until late evening. When she attempted to leave, the friend allegedly contacted her brother, a boda boda operator, providing specific instructions on where to transport Maureen. Following the incident, the boda boda rider reportedly vanished from the scene, only returning when personally summoned by the area base commander. This sequence of events, according to the family, indicates prior coordination.

Crucially, the motorcycle purportedly involved in the accident exhibited no dents or visible damage. This detail, alongside the discovery of what appeared to be a penetration wound in Maureen's forehead, is cited by the family as sufficient to dismiss the road accident explanation as a fabrication. They contend the official autopsy report failed to acknowledge the distinct forehead injury.

Further raising suspicions, the wife of the senior political figure allegedly linked to Maureen's female friend reportedly arrived at the scene shortly after the incident, surveyed the area, and departed. Maureen's mobile phone also disappeared post-mortem, which the family believes contained vital call logs and communication that could expose those behind what they term a coordinated murder plot.

### Political Figures Under Scrutiny

The Maureen Kinyua case has resurfaced nearly seven years after her death, with the family expressing their demand for immediate answers and justice. The former Deputy President and Democracy for Citizens Party (DCP) leader, Rigathi Gachagua, has been drawn into the spotlight concerning alleged involvement in Maureen's death. This development follows recent public disputes between the United Opposition and the Kenya Kwanza administration.

On March 17, 2026, President William Ruto, while on a development tour in Busia County, publicly accused former Deputy President Rigathi Gachagua of having a hand in Maureen's death. Maureen, a blogger affiliated with the Jubilee party, died near Sagana Catholic Church on May 24, 2019, in what was initially termed a hit-and-run accident. Preliminary investigations at the time did not link Gachagua to the incident. Gachagua has publicly denied any involvement, maintaining it was an accident.

The lawyers are urging the ODPP to consider the matter a potential homicide. They are calling for a comprehensive fresh investigation, a review of the original autopsy, and a forensic re-examination of the initial scene evidence. Questions surrounding legal procedures often arise in such cases, as highlighted by other recent developments in the justice system.
