Super Metro, you are a disgrace. The cold-blooded murder of Joseph Mureithi, hurled from your moving bus and crushed like refuse, is not just an incident; it is a damning indictment of your entire operation and the criminal negligence that defines Kenya's public transport.

## Super Metro's Bloody Legacy in Athi River

Let us be clear: the so-called "Super Metro" brand has become synonymous with terror, not transport. The super metro athi river incident that claimed Joseph Mureithi’s life on February 24, 2026, around 11 PM, is not a mishap. It is the predictable outcome of an industry steeped in thuggery and an insatiable lust for profit over human decency. Mureithi, a 25-year-old petrol attendant, deserved to go home after his shift, not to be discarded like garbage on the Kitengela-Namanga Highway. Witnesses, brave enough to speak, paint a horrifying picture: an overcrowded bus, a crew "chaotic and confrontational," and then the unthinkable. These are not accidents; these are acts of calculated brutality.

### The Cowardice of KDK 060H's Crew

The individuals on board Super Metro bus KDK 060H, particularly the driver and the conductor, are not just employees; they are accessories to murder. To allegedly push a young man from a speeding vehicle, then to run him over with the very same bus, and then to simply speed off into the night? That is the act of monsters, devoid of conscience, driven by a barbarism that shocks even the most hardened Kenyan. This kitengela bus kdk 060h accident, as Super Metro absurdly labels it, was a grotesque display of inhumanity. They didn't just kill Joseph Mureithi; they desecrated his dignity and spat on the value of every passenger's life. Their immediate flight from the scene is proof of their guilt, their utter depravity.

### Joseph Mureithi's Family Demands Justice - A Mockery?

Joseph Mureithi's family demands justice, and who can blame them? Patrick Muthomi, a family member, rightly condemned this barbaric act, stating it "could have been avoided." But will they get it, or will this be another statistic swallowed by Kenya's notoriously compromised justice system? Super Metro has the audacity to issue statements "expressing regret" and claiming "surprise" when their bus was stopped by an "irate mob of boda boda riders." Surprise? What an insult! Their hollow condolences are nothing but cynical damage control, a pathetic attempt to whitewash blood from their hands. If they were so surprised, why were their crew members already in police custody? The family's cries for justice should echo through every corrupt office that allows these bandits to operate.

### Super Metro's 'Surprise'? When a Passenger is Thrown Out!

Super Metro’s claim that the incident came as a "surprise to all on board" is a bald-faced lie, insulting the intelligence of every Kenyan. This isn’t a one-off. Just last year, in March 2025, Gilbert Thuo Kimani met a similar, gruesome end on Thika Road, allegedly thrown from another Super Metro bus over a measly fare dispute and then crushed. Super Metro, then, too, denied wrongdoing, claiming he "fell while attempting to alight." What a convenient narrative! Are Kenyans to believe their passengers just have a collective tendency to spontaneously "fall" from moving Super Metro buses, only to be coincidentally run over by the same vehicle? This pattern screams of systemic rot, not isolated incidents. When a super metro passenger is thrown out, it is a crime that reflects directly on the corporate culture. The audacity of their management to deflect blame, rather than take responsibility, is sickening. Read more about their consistent failure to uphold passenger safety here: Super Metro On The Spot Again After Passenger Is Thrown Out Of Moving Bus And Run Over In Athi River.

### Where Are the Regulators? NTSA's Deafening Silence

And where, pray tell, is the National Transport and Safety Authority (NTSA) in all this? Their silence is deafening, their inaction criminal. They are the supposed custodians of road safety, yet they permit these rogue operators to flourish, to spill blood on our highways with impunity. The public outcry for Super Metro to be cancelled or suspended is not merely emotional; it is a rational response to regulatory failure. How many more Joseph Mureithis, how many more Gilbert Thuos, must be sacrificed at the altar of their bureaucratic incompetence and alleged corruption before they stir from their slumber? Their mandate is to protect citizens, not to provide cover for corporate killers.

### The Super Metro Driver Conductor Arrested - But What of the System?

Yes, a Super Metro driver conductor arrested is a start. The DCI patrol vehicle that swiftly pursued and stopped the bus deserves credit for acting where Super Metro’s own conscience apparently failed. But arrests are not enough. This is not about a few bad apples; it's about a poisoned tree. The entire Super Metro operation, from its hiring practices to its purported internal reviews, is a farce. An autopsy is scheduled, detectives are appealing for witnesses. These steps are necessary, but they mask the deeper problem. Until the corporate behemoth itself is held accountable, until its executives are hauled before the courts, until their licenses are revoked and their fleet grounded, these "investigations" are nothing but theatre.

Enough is enough. Super Metro must be stripped bare, its licenses revoked, and every single person responsible for Mureithi's death-from the murderous crew of KDK 060H to the negligent executives at the top-must face the full, unsparing wrath of the law. Anything less is a betrayal of Joseph Mureithi and every Kenyan whose life is cheapened by this monstrous industry.
