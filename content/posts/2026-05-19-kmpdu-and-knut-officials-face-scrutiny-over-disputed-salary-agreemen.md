The Kenya Medical Practitioners, Pharmacists and Dentists Union and the Kenya National Union of Teachers are facing intense internal backlash in Nairobi as members protest against the recent salary agreements and return to work formulas negotiated with the national government this month.

## Union leadership under fire for alleged betrayal of members

### The pattern of compromised negotiations
The ongoing industrial unrest has exposed a recurring cycle where union officials rapidly accept government proposals that deviate significantly from original member demands. Doctors and teachers are increasingly vocal about their dissatisfaction, alleging that their leadership prioritizes personal gain over the collective welfare of the workforce. By finalizing agreements that provide little relief regarding cost of living adjustments or structural salary improvements, these officials have sparked allegations that they are prioritizing private settlements to ensure a swift end to industrial action.

### Disparity between public perception and leadership conduct
Critics are drawing unfavorable comparisons between the perceived boldness of public transport operators and the perceived weakness of union executives. While independent transport workers are often characterized by their chaotic but firm resistance to external pressures, union leaders are now being cast as scavengers. This narrative suggests that officials wait for the government to provide financial incentives before abandoning the picket lines. This atmosphere of distrust has rendered traditional bargaining processes ineffective, as the rank and file no longer believe their representatives are operating in good faith.

### Accusations of manipulation and self-interest
A growing segment of the membership is openly labeling their union representatives as easily manipulated fools who have been compromised by government influence. Workers argue that while their own salary increments remain stalled in administrative bureaucracy, union leadership appears to maintain a lifestyle that does not align with the stagnation faced by the teachers and doctors they represent. This deep-seated suspicion has created a crisis of legitimacy, leaving many to conclude that the primary function of current union leadership has shifted from worker advocacy to the pursuit of personal financial stability at the expense of their constituents.
