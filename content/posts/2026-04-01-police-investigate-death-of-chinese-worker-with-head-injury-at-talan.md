Police are investigating the death of a Chinese worker discovered unresponsive in a latrine at the Talanta Stadium construction site in Jamhuri, Nairobi, on Monday, March 30, 2026. The adult male, found by a chef attached to the Chinese management, was pronounced dead upon arrival at Nairobi Hospital, exhibiting a slight head injury.

Police Investigate Incident at Construction Site

Discovery and Initial Response Security personnel at the Talanta Stadium facility reported the incident on the morning of March 30, 2026. After the discovery by a chef, security officers and other Chinese nationals transported the worker to Nairobi Hospital, where he was declared deceased.

Unclear Circumstances and Police Inquiry The exact circumstances surrounding the worker's death remain unclear. Nairobi police officers later visited the stadium premises to examine the scene where the body was found. Authorities noted a minor injury on the right side of the man’s head. The site is currently under investigation as officers gather evidence and interview witnesses. The body has been moved to Lee Funeral Home for a postmortem examination to determine the official cause of death. Nairobi Police Chief Issa Mohamud stated that authorities are considering both sudden natural death and murder as possibilities in their investigation.

Talanta Stadium Development Talanta Stadium, slated for renaming as Raila Odinga Stadium, is presently under construction. The project employs numerous Chinese nationals, drawing attention to this incident. The 60,000-seat facility, which commenced construction in March 2024, is scheduled to host the 2027 Africa Cup of Nations (AFCON). Following a recent inspection by the Senate Committee on roads and infrastructure, the completion date for the stadium was revised, with works now anticipated to conclude by June 2026.
