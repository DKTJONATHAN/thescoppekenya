Teslah Kenya has ignited a fierce debate after calling out the systemic exclusion of female artists from prime-time DJ rotations. Fans are now demanding accountability from club gatekeepers, questioning why major festival lineups still lack gender parity despite high streaming numbers.

The social media streets are currently on fire. ### The Gatekeeper Effect and Club Economics

The imbalance in our local entertainment sector is no longer a secret. Fans have pointed out the glaring gap between the success of artists like Nikita Kering, Ssaru, and Femi One, and their absence from prime-time festival slots. When compared to streaming chart data, which shows a massive appetite for female-led tracks, recent festival lineups look outdated and exclusionary.

Industry insiders have different takes on this. One prominent Nairobi-based DJ, who requested anonymity, defended current curation practices: "We look for high energy and tracks that maintain specific dance floor momentum. Sometimes it is about the BPM and the crowd engagement rather than the gender of the artist." 

However, another radio programmer countered this by admitting that "safe" playlists are often the default. "Programmers often stick to what they perceive as the proven path, which unfortunately keeps the industry locked in a cycle where male artists get the majority of the airplay," they explained. This admission suggests that the issue is not about the quality of the music, but about a risk-averse industry culture that refuses to trust the audience with a more diverse range of voices.
