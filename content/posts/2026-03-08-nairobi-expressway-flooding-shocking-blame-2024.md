## Nairobi Expressway Blamed for Capital Flooding The Motorists Association of Kenya (MAK) has blamed the Nairobi Expressway for the capital's severe flooding, demanding accountability from its operators. On Sunday, March 8, MAK stated the project's construction disrupted crucial drainage, overwhelming the city's road network during rainfall.

Expressway Impact on Drainage In a statement released on Sunday, March 8, the Motorists Association of Kenya (MAK) directly attributed Nairobi's persistent flooding to the Nairobi Expressway project. MAK asserted that before the mega project along Mombasa Road and Uhuru Highway, these transport corridors did not experience recurrent inundation. The association claims the expressway's construction interfered with numerous drainage channels across the corridor, contributing to major traffic disruptions on the busy route during rainy periods. MAK also noted that the road collects substantial volumes of water, directing them to the lower deck section, which then overwhelms existing drainage infrastructure.

Demand for Accountability and Legal Action MAK has called for accountability from the Nairobi Expressway operators, a private toll enterprise managed under a concession by the China Road and Bridge Corporation in partnership with the Government of Kenya. The association demands these operators take responsibility for impacts associated with the project. Furthermore, MAK has urged legal action against engineers and planners who approved infrastructural projects in the county that contribute to the current drainage issues. These professionals are accused of repeatedly approving recarpeting works that sealed vital drainage vents, which historically allowed rainwater to flow into underground channels.

Affected Roads and Compensation Call According to MAK, roads affected by these drainage problems include Tom Mboya Street, Moi Avenue, River Road, Kirinyaga Road, Sheikh Karume Road, Luthuli Avenue, Ronald Ngala Street, Haile Selassie Avenue, Kimathi Street, and Muindi Mbingu Street. The association has called upon the National Government, in collaboration with the Nairobi City County Government, to ensure compensation for all motorists whose vehicles were destroyed during this weekend's downpour. MAK also stressed the need for an immediate audit and reopening of all blocked drainage systems across Nairobi's road network, advocating for personal accountability for engineers and planners who approved defective roadworks.

Widespread Flooding Across Kenya The accusations against the expressway operators coincide with several parts of Kenya grappling with widespread flooding caused by ongoing heavy rains. These rains have led to significant destruction of property and loss of life, particularly in Nairobi. The National Police Service, in a statement issued on Saturday, March 7, confirmed that the flooding has also resulted in displacement, road closures, and extensive property damage.
