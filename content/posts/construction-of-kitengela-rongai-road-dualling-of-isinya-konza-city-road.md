## Construction of Kitengela-Rongai Road and Isinya-Konza City Road Dualling: Ruto's Bold Infrastructure Agenda President William Ruto, speaking in Kajiado County on Saturday, February 28, 2026, unveiled significant infrastructure commitments, including the **construction of Kitengela-Rongai Road** and the **dualling of Isinya-Konza City Road**. These projects aim to alleviate transport challenges, unlock economic potential, and enhance connectivity within the Nairobi Metropolitan Area and beyond. The President affirmed that funding is secured for these pivotal road works. The proposed 30-kilometre road connecting Kitengela town in Kajiado East sub-County to Ong'ata Rongai township in Kajiado North sub-County has been a subject of public concern for years. The President pledged to personally launch the **construction of Kitengela-Rongai Road**, underscoring its national importance. This vital artery runs parallel to the Nairobi National Park boundary and the Standard Gauge Railway (SGR) line, extending past African Nazarene University to the SGR Ong'ata Rongai station, before linking to Magadi Road.

The Urgency Behind Kitengela-Rongai Road Construction Last rehabilitated in 2014, the Kitengela-Rongai Road is currently in a deplorable condition, causing substantial losses for residents and transporters. Its strategic role as an economic conduit for quarry stones and pozzolana from Sholinke and Nkurunka, materials essential for cement processors, highlights the urgency of its upgrade. The road also touches a section of Machakos County in the Embakasi area, further emphasising its regional significance.

Dualling of Isinya-Konza City Road: Paving the Way for Konza Technopolis In tandem with the Kitengela-Rongai project, President Ruto announced the **dualling of Isinya-Konza City Road**, designated as the B50. This approximately 50-kilometre stretch is critical for linking the Athi River-Namanga Road (A2) at Isinya with Konza Technopolis and the Nairobi-Mombasa Highway (A8) at Malili. The Kenya National Highways Authority (KeNHA) is overseeing the project, which will traverse Kajiado, Machakos, and Makueni counties and is designed to support high speeds of up to 120km/h. This bypass serves as a strategic transport corridor to decongest the main Nairobi-Mombasa highway and provide direct, efficient access to the Konza Technopolis, a flagship Vision 2030 project. Furthermore, the Head of State announced the comprehensive upgrade of the 63-kilometre Isara (Emali Junction)-Mashuuru-Imaroro road to a B2 standard. The tendering process for this project is slated to commence in March 2026, with the President asserting that the road will be entirely rebuilt, not merely recarpeted. This signals a departure from previous maintenance approaches that have seen similar roads quickly deteriorate due to inadequate planning and funding for long-term upkeep. These infrastructure investments in Kajiado County represent a concerted effort by the government to stimulate regional economic growth and improve the quality of life for citizens. By enhancing road networks, the administration aims to facilitate trade, reduce travel times, and attract further investment into key development areas like Konza Technopolis. This forward-looking approach to national development, much like the strategic integration of advanced technologies in other sectors, as seen with the [US Military's deployment of Claude AI in Middle East Operations](https://zandani.co.ke/article/us-military-deploys-claude-ai-middle-east-operations), is fundamental to bolstering national capabilities and resilience. The President's direct involvement in launching these projects underscores the executive's commitment to their timely and successful implementation.
