Za Ndani by Jona Munyi

The 2026 World Cup in the United States is poised to be the most expensive tournament ever for attending fans, with costs soaring to unprecedented levels. Supporters eyeing a trip to North America face substantial bills for flights, accommodation, and match tickets, transforming the dream into a luxury only accessible to those with considerable financial means. Early estimates indicate a family of four could spend well over £22,000 for the group stage alone.

## The Astronomical Price Tag for World Cup 2026

The significant expenses for the 2026 World Cup are a clear departure from previous tournaments, driven by high ticket prices, inflated accommodation rates, and elevated train fares. For England fans, a trip through the group stage could cost approximately £6,500 per person or £13,000 for two. A family of four, including two pre-teen children, would see costs skyrocket to more than £22,000. While Dallas offers more affordable options for England supporters, Scotland fans face eight nights in Boston, one of the most expensive host cities. Two Scottish fans could expect to spend around £7,500 each, with a family's expenses potentially exceeding £25,000. Hotel prices in host cities like Boston have been noted to be particularly high, with some rooms costing over £408 per night. Even with efforts to find value hotels within a four-mile radius of city centres and considering official resale site tickets, the financial burden remains substantial.

### Breaking Down England's Journey Costs

**Flights and Internal Travel**
Return flights from Heathrow to New York's JFK for two England fans are estimated at just over £500 each, considered good value. Upon arrival, a connecting flight to Dallas costs £283 per person. Later, a flight from Dallas to Boston is priced at £378 per person. For the final group game in New Jersey, fans can take an Amtrak train from Boston to New York for £42 each. Return train tickets to MetLife Stadium from Manhattan are priced at $150 (£114) each. Overall, travel within the United States for two friends is estimated at £6,273, while for a couple it is £5,855. A family would need approximately £9,008 for travel and accommodation.

**Accommodation**
In Dallas, four nights at the Hampton Inn & Suites Dallas Market Center cost £624 for a twin room (£156 per night), with breakfast included. The Hilton Dallas Medical District offers double rooms at £560 (£140 per night), also with breakfast. Boston presents a stark contrast, with five nights at the Hampton Inn & Suites Boston Crosstown Center costing £1,650 (£330 per night), including breakfast. A twin-bed option in the city centre could be as high as £2,041 (£408 per night) without breakfast. In New York, a five-night stay at the Truss Hotel Times Square for a double room is £1,184 (£237 per night), while a twin at the AMTD Idea Tribeca Hotel costs £1,275 (£255 per night), though breakfast is not included. Some hotels in Dallas, such as the Comfort Inn Dallas Medical-Market Center, offer free stays for children, costing £627 (£156 per night) for a family. For a family in Boston, the Hampton Inn is £1,763 (£353 per night), and in New York, the Holiday Inn in Times Square is £1,589 (£318 per night).

**Match Tickets**
Securing tickets through the official resale platform is common, often incurring a 15% transaction fee on top of already inflated prices. For England's opener against Croatia, two upper-tier category-two tickets cost £1,406, significantly higher than their face value of £517. Similarly, two tickets for the Ghana match were £1,222 (face value £745), and for the Panama match, £1,396 (original price £494). The total cost for two people to attend all three group games, including fees, is £4,370 (£2,185 per person), more than double the face value of £1,756. For families, the cost is even higher at £8,720 for 12 tickets, due to limited availability for groups of four and no concession pricing.

**Daily Living Expenses**
An estimated £3,000 is needed for two people on a low-to-mid-range budget for meals, drinks, snacks, and local transport over 14-15 days, averaging £107 per person per day for England fans. This budget also accounts for the US tipping culture. A family would require £4,500, which includes excursions for children.

### Scotland's World Cup Financial Hurdles

**Travel Logistical Challenges**
Scotland fans face higher initial travel costs, with a direct flight from Glasgow to New York costing £1,887 for two tickets or £3,390 for a family, nearly double what England fans pay. This often necessitates layovers, such as via Amsterdam. It is more cost-effective to spend the first night in New York, costing £191 for a couple at the Truss Hotel Times Square or £240 for a twin at the Courtyard New York Manhattan/Midtown East. An evening train to Boston on June 12 costs £122 for two tickets. Later, a flight from Boston to Miami for two tickets is £765, and the return flight to New York, connecting to Glasgow, costs £205 for two tickets. Two friends following Scotland would spend approximately £7,205, or £3,603 per person, on travel and accommodation. A family would need £10,491 for this initial part of the trip.

**Accommodation Woes**
Scotland plays two matches in Boston, one of the most expensive host cities, impacting accommodation significantly. The enVision Hotel in Boston costs £2,290 (£286 a night) for a double room or £2,552 (£319 a night) for a twin. Six nights in Miami at the MetroPark Hotel costs £1,228 (£205 a night) for a twin or double. For a family, the Holiday Inn Lower East Side in New York costs £243 for one night, and the enVision Hotel in Boston costs £2,552 (£319 a night). Six nights at the Hampton Inn Miami-Coconut Grove/Coral Gables for a family is £1,559 (£260 a night) with breakfast.

**Ticket Dynamics**
While resale tickets for Scotland's first two group games are cheaper than England's, the final match against Brazil drives prices extremely high. For the Haiti match, two tickets cost £1,151 (face value £477), and for the Morocco game, £1,222 (face value £471). The cost for two tickets to the Brazil match is £2,557.

**Adjusted Daily Expenses**
With an extra day in the United States, Scotland fans' estimated daily living costs are adjusted to £3,214 for two people and £4,820 for a family. Transfers to the Miami stadium are expected to be free from designated train station hubs.
