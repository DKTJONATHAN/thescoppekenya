Activist and journalist Hanifa Adan has joined Radio Generation as a host for "The Generation Report," a weekly segment airing Mondays from 7 to 10 PM. She will co-host with Triciah Wayua, providing news analysis and commentary on current affairs and politics.

Hanifa Adan's New Role at Radio Generation

Weekly News Segment Focus Hanifa Adan will leverage her extensive experience in youth activism and political reporting to provide comprehensive news roundups and insightful analysis. The weekly segment promises expert commentary on pressing political issues and top headlines both within the country and internationally, encompassing a range of subjects from governance to economic insights. For example, the programme might review recent corporate dividend announcements, such as those rewarding shareholders with a Ksh2.50 dividend. The show aims to deliver fresh, unbiased news, as Adan has stated.

A Background in Activism and Journalism Adan, a University of Nairobi journalism alumna, has been a Daily Nation columnist since April 2025, primarily focusing on governance, social justice, and youth activism. Her public profile significantly rose during the 2024 Gen Z-led anti-Finance Bill demonstrations, where she actively protested and championed government accountability through social media. She was also recognised as Human Rights Defender of the Year in 2024, solidifying her standing among leading youth activists. Her involvement extends to recent political movements, including the Okiyah Omtatah team's presidential bid announced earlier this year.

Expanding Influence and Advocacy Her activism began in 2022, addressing decades-long drainage issues in Nairobi's Korogocho slums, her birthplace, which drew Governor Johnson Sakaja's attention. Since then, her advocacy has broadened to include anti-abduction protests, femicide awareness campaigns, youth mobilisation, and broader social justice issues. While facing past scrutiny regarding the 2024 demonstrations and alleged mismanagement of Ksh30 million intended to compensate protest victims-claims she clarified by verifying individual cases and sharing supporting documents-her online presence remains a crucial tool for engaging young people and shaping national discussions on policy and governance. Her new radio platform is expected to amplify these vital youth-focused perspectives and continue her advocacy.
