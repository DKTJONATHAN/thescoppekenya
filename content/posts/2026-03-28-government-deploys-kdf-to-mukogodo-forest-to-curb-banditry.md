The Kenya Defence Forces have been deployed to Mukogodo Forest, Laikipia County, to counter rising banditry. Defence Cabinet Secretary Soipan Tuya announced the deployment on Friday, assuring residents in insecurity-prone areas that the military is prepared to support operations against armed gangs and cattle rustlers.

## Government Intensifies Security Operations

The government's decision to deploy the KDF follows persistent concerns raised by lawmakers regarding delays in establishing a temporary KDF camp in Doldol, Laikipia County. This marks the second time the KDF has been sent to the region, with previous operations drawing scrutiny.

### Strategic Deployment and Funding

Defence Cabinet Secretary Tuya confirmed that KDF officers will collaborate closely with the National Police Service. She emphasized the President's directive to eliminate all banditry activities and insecurity, ensuring a robust, multi-agency presence in all affected areas of Laikipia County. To enhance operational effectiveness, the government has allocated Ksh1 million to develop road networks, thereby improving access for security teams. Residents and leaders in Samburu County have also been urged to support the upcoming security operation.

### Multi-Agency Coordination and Disturbed Areas

Ahead of the deployment, Rift Valley Regional Commissioner Abdi Hassan chaired a multi-agency security meeting at the Doldol Deputy County Commissioner’s Office. The forum, which included KDF personnel and other key security stakeholders, focused on reviewing the prevailing situation and devising practical measures for stability. Discussions highlighted the importance of timely intelligence sharing, joint planning, and synchronized responses to restore peace and order. The Ministry of Interior has officially designated parts of Laikipia, Meru, and Isiolo counties as security-disturbed and dangerous zones.

### Human Rights Concerns

The deployment, while aimed at restoring peace, comes after the Kenya National Commission on Human Rights (KNCHR) criticized previous operations. In February, KNCHR accused the police and KDF of employing scorched-earth tactics during disarmament exercises in the North Rift. The rights group reported that more than 70 cattle, 10 camels, and 50 goats were killed during a January 26, 2026, operation in Silale, Baringo County, leading to significant destruction of property and livelihoods. KNCHR asserted these actions violated human rights, disproportionately affecting vulnerable populations. The commission also warned that the heavy-handed approach resulted in the closure of marketplaces and water points, displacement of residents, and harassment of local chiefs and assistants, particularly in areas like Silale, Riongo, Nginyang, and Naudo.
