Kenya Aviation Workers Union threatens strike over KCAA disp is central to this update for Kenyan readers.

The Kenya Aviation Workers Union (KAWU) has issued another strike threat against the Kenya Civil Aviation Authority (KCAA), alleging a breach of their return-to-work formula. Secretary General Moses Ndiema stated Wednesday, March 18, 2026, that aviation services face suspension from March 25, urging passengers to prepare for disruptions.

Renewed Aviation Standoff

Breach of Agreement Allegations Intensify KAWU Secretary General Moses Ndiema publicly announced on Wednesday, March 18, 2026, that aviation services would be halted in a week. He claims the Kenya Civil Aviation Authority (KCAA) breached the return-to-work formula, an agreement forged barely a month ago. Ndiema cautioned passengers about potential travel disruptions beginning March 25, emphasizing that the union would not be held accountable for any inconveniences stemming from the planned industrial action. This latest threat marks a worrying pattern, following a previous strike suspended by KAWU in February 2026 that had paralyzed aviation operations.

Union Grievances and Legal Interventions The union's core grievances include accusations that KCAA violated agreements outlined in the return-to-work formula, which was intended to guide subsequent discussions. KAWU also alleges victimisation of employees by KCAA, leading to deepening tensions within the sector. In February, as industrial action escalated, the Employment and Labour Relations Court (ELRC) temporarily blocked the strike. Justice Agnes Nzei of the ELRC in Milimani, Nairobi, deemed KCAA's application urgent and directed that KAWU's strike notice, served on February 9, 2026, not be acted upon until the case concluded.

Potential Sector-Wide Disruptions Industrial strikes by aviation workers typically result in severe consequences, often disrupting flights into and out of the country. The Kenya Airline Pilots Association (KALPA) has previously warned that continuous disruptions from such strikes could elevate fatigue risks for flight crews, thereby increasing the potential for catastrophes. KALPA attributes this risk to delays, cancellations, and diversions, which create unpredictable schedules and extend pilot duty periods. Such protracted disputes underscore the constant need for robust frameworks to manage industrial relations and ensure operational stability.
