Deputy President Rigathi Gachagua has formally demanded the nullification of recent United Democratic Alliance grassroots election results. The Deputy President alleges widespread rigging and a lack of transparency within the ruling party, signaling a deepening rift between himself and the current party leadership.

## Political Turbulence Within the Ruling Party

### The Parallels to Opposition Tactics
The Deputy President’s aggressive challenge to internal party results has drawn comparisons to the historical conduct of opposition leader Raila Odinga. Throughout his tenure, Gachagua was a vocal critic of opposition-led protests and the refusal to accept declared electoral outcomes. By adopting a strategy of rejecting results he deems unfavorable, the Deputy President faces allegations of hypocrisy. Political analysts suggest this shift highlights a significant irony, as the leader who once condemned the questioning of democratic processes now employs identical methods to contest his own party’s internal elections.

### Strategic Motivations and Internal Control
Critics argue that Gachagua’s call for nullification is a calculated effort to maintain his influence over the Mt. Kenya political bloc. By questioning the integrity of the polls, the Deputy President is attempting to ensure that his preferred loyalists remain in positions of power. This confrontational approach risks destabilizing the ruling coalition from within. While the official justification for his stance is the pursuit of transparency, observers note that such interventions are often used to consolidate authority and sideline political rivals before the next major electoral cycle.

### The Breakdown of Party Unity
The ongoing dispute reflects a fundamental breakdown in the relationship between the party center and regional power brokers. By publicly challenging the legitimacy of the grassroots elections, Gachagua has effectively signaled his refusal to comply with the party’s established internal procedures. This public pressure puts the UDA leadership in a difficult position: yielding to the demand for nullification could undermine the entire election process, while ignoring the Deputy President threatens to trigger a broader mutiny. As the party grapples with these internal fractures, the focus remains on whether the current leadership will prioritize institutional stability or bow to the demands of its highest-ranking members.
