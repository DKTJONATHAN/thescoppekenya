The event, held on Sunday, March 1, 2026, at Alliance Française de Lagos, has redirected public attention from the film itself to the strategic optics of its promotion. The buzz surrounding Omotola Jalade-Ekeinde’s 'Mother’s Love' was already significant, marking the veteran actress's first foray behind the camera after three decades in Nollywood. The film, scheduled for a March 6 nationwide release, delves into intricate mother-daughter dynamics and classism within Nigeria, themes that have garnered international attention following screenings at the Toronto International Film Festival. However, the conversation shifted dramatically with Obasanjo's arrival, eclipsing the film's artistic merit and the notable attendance of other industry figures like Ruth Kadiri, Ifeanyi Kalu, and Lily Afe.

The Curious Case of Obasanjo's Attendance The former president's public appearances typically revolve around weighty political summits, diplomatic engagements, or crucial business forums, making his presence at a movie premiere a stark departure from the norm. This high-stakes PR play arrives amidst a heated debate within Nollywood regarding film promotion. Just last month, Omotola Jalade-Ekeinde herself weighed in on the growing pressure on filmmakers and actors to produce viral dance content to market their work, candidly stating that such expectations are "exhausting and unnatural". Her stance echoed celebrated filmmaker Kunle Afolayan, who similarly sparked controversy by declaring he found it unflattering for producers to resort to dancing for movie promotion.

This strategic pivot, whether intentional or not, has certainly achieved what every promotional strategy aims for: getting the populace talking.
