## Kenya's Digital Economy Exhibits Robust Mobile-First Acceleration in Q4 2025

Kenya's digital economy recorded significant expansion during the final quarter of 2025, underscored by unprecedented mobile penetration, rising smartphone adoption, and near-universal mobile money usage. This growth solidified the nation's reliance on mobile technology, with data consumption surging across its highly connected populace.

### Unprecedented Mobile Connectivity and Market Dynamics

Kenya concluded 2025 with 78.4 million SIM subscriptions against a population of 52 million, pushing the mobile penetration rate to an extraordinary 149.5%. This indicates that many Kenyans utilize multiple SIM cards, reflecting deep integration of mobile technology into daily life. Safaricom remains the dominant operator, holding two-thirds of all mobile subscriptions, nearly two-thirds of mobile broadband, and approximately nine out of ten mobile money users. Airtel positions as a credible second, yet a substantial gap persists between it and the market leader.

### Smartphone Adoption Fuels Surging Data Consumption

The shift towards smartphones accelerated, with 48.7 million devices connected to Kenya's mobile networks by December 2025, representing a 92.9% penetration rate that continues to climb. Conversely, feature phone usage declined to 56.5%. This transition has directly fueled an increased appetite for mobile data, with subscriptions reaching 62 million in the quarter. While 2G and 3G connections are diminishing, 4G surged to 44.1 million users, and 5G quietly surpassed 1.7 million subscribers. The average user now consumes 14.6 GB of data monthly, with 5G users consuming an average of 46.4 GB, more than three times the typical usage. This 12% quarterly growth in broadband consumption signals sustained infrastructure investment. This commitment is crucial for powering continued digital expansion, especially as the nation addresses broader energy challenges that can lead to load shedding, underscoring the need for resilient infrastructure across all sectors.

### Mobile Money Achieves Near-Universal Reach

Kenya's mobile money market added 2.7 million new users in Q4 2025, reaching a 98% penetration rate by December. The festive season contributed to a 5.6% quarterly jump in transactions as Kenyans engaged in heightened spending and sending of money. M-Pesa continues its undisputed leadership in this sector with an 89% market share, supported by a vast network of 501,399 registered agents nationwide.

### Evolving Communication Patterns

User behavior indicates a gradual shift from traditional SMS to internet-based communication. SMS traffic saw a decline from 14.7 billion to 14.4 billion messages in the quarter, primarily attributed to the rise of platforms like WhatsApp. Despite this, voice calls remain robust, with Kenyans making 31.5 billion minutes of calls, a 5.2% increase from the previous quarter. This suggests a migration to online platforms for texting while maintaining a strong preference for voice communication.

### Expanding Fixed Internet and Global Connectivity

Fixed internet subscriptions grew by 7.4% to 2.46 million, with fiber connections constituting 1.38 million. Starlink satellite internet registered 22,282 subscriptions, a modest but notable entry showcasing creative approaches to last-mile connectivity. Kenya's total international internet bandwidth capacity now stands at 24,161 Gbps, with the PEACE submarine cable experiencing the most significant growth at 43.9% in the quarter.

### Streaming Displaces Traditional Broadcast Media

The traditional broadcast sector saw a decline, with subscriptions falling to 1.66 million from 1.68 million. Both DTT platforms GoTV and StarTimes, along with most satellite TV providers, experienced subscriber losses. Only cable reported marginal growth, reflecting a global trend where streaming services increasingly displace conventional pay TV.
