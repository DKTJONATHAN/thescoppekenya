Odumodublvck has publicly denied accusations that he or his label orchestrated the temporary removal of Blaqbonez's 'ACL' track from streaming platforms. The Nigerian artist clarified his social media posts were mere trolling amidst their ongoing competitive rivalry, affirming the track's disappearance was not his doing as Blaqbonez releases a deluxe edition.

Odumodublvck Clears Air on 'ACL' Takedown

Trolling Versus Takedowns Odumodublvck explicitly denied any role in the temporary removal of Blaqbonez’s track 'ACL' from streaming platforms. Speaking in an X space hosted by Afrobeats To The World, he directly stated that neither he nor his label, YP, was responsible, stressing the need for this clarity. He admitted to "whining" or trolling Blaqbonez on social media, framing his cryptic posts as part of ongoing competitive banter. "Me and the guy we get scores to settle, so me, I just dey whine am," he explained, distinguishing his playful antagonism from actual interference. Odumodublvck argued that the accusation was illogical and damaging to his reputation, pointing out the procedural impossibility of a single individual unilaterally removing a major label's song. He urged his audience to consider the complex mechanics involved in platform operations.

'ACL' Returns Amidst Deluxe Release Despite the drama, Blaqbonez has officially launched the 'No Excuses' deluxe edition, featuring a sonically sharpened version of 'ACL' alongside three brand-new tracks. The revised 'ACL' retains its boom bap foundation and composure but presents a tightened production, giving it a harder hit. The track maintains its calculated, intentional delivery, with its message remaining unchanged, only sharpened. The deluxe edition expands the project to 20 songs, introducing ‘Work in the Morning,’ ‘Fine Shyt’ featuring TML Vibez, and ‘Hustle Kpa $$$’ featuring Artsalghul. This expanded album continues to blur the lines between rap and Afrobeats. The narrative around the mysterious removal, once fueled by online speculation and Odumodublvck's cryptic posts, highlights how digital environments can shape public perception, echoing the broader shift as traditional voice traffic declines and all generations increasingly opt for internet-based calling.
