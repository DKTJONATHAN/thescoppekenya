## Gloria Ntazola's Audi vs. Nairobi Floods: A Soggy Reality Check Nairobi's relentless rainfall has delivered a rude awakening to social media sensation Gloria Ntazola. After a terrifying encounter with flash floods in her Audi, she's decided to bench her luxury ride for the season, candidly declaring it unsuitable for the city's current climate. Her emotional ordeal highlights the severe impact of the ongoing deluges across the capital.

A Near-Miss in the Torrent The usually outspoken content creator found herself in a truly harrowing situation during a night of particularly heavy downpour. She recounted a scene of pure chaos as city roads rapidly transformed into dangerous, raging rivers, almost sweeping her and her Audi away. The emotional toll was immense, leaving her in tears as she navigated the life-threatening waters. "Yesterday we almost got swept away. I cried so much," Ntazola shared with her followers.

The Audi Gets Benched This terrifying experience has prompted a significant shift in Ntazola's perspective on luxury cars and road safety. She expressed deep frustration with her Audi's performance under such extreme weather, deeming it unfit for Nairobi's current conditions. Her blunt assessment? "I'll not be driving... because Audis are the shittiest cars especially during rainy seasons." This bold move by one of Kenya's prominent online personalities, who recently gifted herself an Audi A7, serves as a stark reminder that even celebrity status doesn't exempt one from the realities of the weather. Perhaps it's a blessing she's not heading into the South African bush for "I'm A Celebrity SA 2026" in that particular vehicle!

Nairobi Under Siege Gloria Ntazola's close call mirrors a much wider crisis gripping the capital. The Nairobi River has burst its banks, contributing to at least 23 confirmed deaths in the city alone. Areas like Grogan and major highways have become impassable, with the military even deployed to rescue stranded motorists. The Kenya Meteorological Department has warned that the heavy rains are expected to persist, at least until March 10, advising drivers of low-clearance vehicles to be extremely cautious. Ntazola is no stranger to public discourse, having first gained national attention for a viral video where she "kidnapped" a Nairobi City County askari who had entered her car, often commenting on city governance and infrastructure since then. As she parks her Audi and seeks alternative ways to navigate the flooded streets, her story stands as a vivid cautionary tale for all city dwellers facing the wrath of the current rainy season.
