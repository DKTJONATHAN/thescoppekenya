Murang'a County Governor Irungu Kang'ata has announced extensive inspections and licensing reviews targeting over 7,500 liquor businesses within the county. These actions follow a Murang'a County Alcoholic Drinks Control Committee meeting held on March 23, 2026, with physical premises inspections scheduled from March 30 to April 2, 2026, to enforce new licensing regulations.

Murang'a County Intensifies Liquor Licensing Enforcement

Renewals and Objections Processed Following the close of application and objection windows, 3,200 premises received automatic license renewals through the county's digital system after no public objections were raised against them. These businesses must visit the MurangeeServices portal to confirm their renewal status, print invoices, and complete payments by March 31, 2026. Conversely, 667 premises are currently under review by county licensing authorities due to formal objections from the public.

Pending Reviews and Compliance Issues An additional 1,200 first-time applicants are required to undergo a mandatory review process as the committee assesses the pending applications. Furthermore, 400 existing premises, despite not facing public objections, have been flagged for non-compliance, specifically failing to settle their 2025 license fees. This lapse significantly jeopardizes their renewal prospects for the current year.

Upcoming Physical Inspections A combined total of 2,267 establishments, including those with objections, new applicants, and businesses defaulting on fee payments, will undergo mandatory physical inspections. These inspections are scheduled between March 30 and April 2, 2026, and will be personally led by the County Commissioner of Murang'a and his team. The county emphasizes that the entire process will operate via its digital platform, requiring all affected premises to utilize Murang'a County's eServices prior to inspections to ensure a transparent and verifiable process. Officers are receiving dedicated training on Friday, March 27, 2026, at 10 am at the County Commissioner's Office in Murang'a town, to prepare for the exercise.

Addressing Illicit Alcohol Concerns The enhanced enforcement measures are a direct response to Murang'a County being ranked 15th nationwide in reported cases of illicit and adulterated alcohol abuse. This statistic was highlighted by the Deputy Inspector General of Police, Geoffrey Masengeli. Efforts by the Kenya Revenue Authority (KRA) and the National Authority for the Campaign Against Alcohol and Drug Abuse (NACADA) have already led to the seizure of counterfeit alcohol valued at millions of shillings and the closure of illegal manufacturing facilities in areas such as Kenol.
