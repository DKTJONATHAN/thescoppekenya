Homa Bay Governor Gladys Wanga indicated on Sunday, April 26, that the Orange Democratic Movement (ODM) seeks a 50 percent power share with the United Democratic Alliance (UDA) in a future political alliance. Speaking in Kilifi, Wanga proposed Mombasa Governor Hassan Joho as ODM's preferred candidate for Deputy President ahead of the 2027 General Election.

## ODM's Power-Sharing Demands

ODM chairperson Gladys Wanga emphasized the party's demand for equal partnership in any coalition negotiations with UDA. She asserted that power-sharing arrangements would strictly adhere to parity, with ODM expecting a 50-50 split of executive positions should UDA field a presidential candidate. Wanga stated that ODM considers itself the largest party in the region, justifying its call for equal standing in discussions.

### Joho Proposed for Deputy President

During the ODM Youth Convention in Kilifi, Governor Wanga publicly endorsed Mombasa Governor Hassan Joho as ODM's preferred candidate for Deputy President. She highlighted Joho's qualifications for the role, solidifying his position as a potential running mate for President William Ruto in the 2027 presidential bid. This announcement comes amid previous speculation about other ODM figures, including Wanga herself, eyeing the deputy presidency.

### Broad-Based Management Committee's Role

Wanga's remarks follow the recent formation of a Broad-Based Management Committee between UDA and ODM. Established after a meeting at State House, Nairobi, on April 23, between President William Ruto and ODM leader Oburu Odinga, the committee aims to align legislative and executive priorities. It will also streamline governance coordination and address emerging political tensions within their cooperation framework, including discussions on electoral zoning to minimize internal competition for 2027.

### Critique of Opposition Leaders

Governor Wanga utilized the platform to criticize opposition figures, specifically former Deputy President Rigathi Gachagua. She accused Gachagua of disrespectfully invoking the name of the late former Prime Minister Raila Odinga, urging him to cease such mentions. Wanga also dismissed recent opposition tours along the Coast, deeming them ineffective and lacking local support. She stressed that ODM would only engage with political partners who uphold its principles, warning against alliances with leaders she accused of land grabbing and tribal politics. These political maneuvers unfold as other significant developments also shape the year.
