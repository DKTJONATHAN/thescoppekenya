## Preachers to the Rescue: Kanyari and Kiengei Lend a Hand to Flood-Hit Nairobi When Nairobi's heavy rains turned tragic, transforming homes into waterways and displacing countless residents, some of Kenya's most recognisable spiritual figures stepped up. Pastor Victor Kanyari of Salvation Healing Ministries and Bishop Benson Gathungu, founder of Jesus Compassion Ministries, are among those mobilising aid, bringing much-needed relief and a touch of celebrity compassion to the deeply affected.

Kanyari's Compassionate Crusade in Njiru In a move that saw him trade the pulpit for direct community action, Pastor Kanyari spent considerable time in the Njiru area, where his church is situated. His 'Team Kanyari' distributed essential food and supplies to residents whose daily lives were upended by the severe floods. Photos shared by the popular preacher on social media captured the scene, showing lines of appreciative residents receiving aid, underscoring a practical display of charity from the usually controversial figure. "Today Team Kanyari spent the day at Potmore Njiru providing food to people affected by the floods," Kanyari stated, highlighting his hands-on approach to the crisis.

Bishop Kiengei's Open Doors and Appeal Joining the ranks of compassionate leaders, Bishop Kiengei extended an open invitation to those displaced by the relentless downpour, welcoming them into the sanctuary of his Jesus Compassion Ministries International headquarters. Recognizing the widespread devastation - submerged houses, destroyed properties, and displaced families - Kiengei acted swiftly. His statement on Tuesday, March 10, 2026, laid bare the crisis's impact. Beyond offering shelter, he issued a heartfelt appeal to the public for donations. He urged well-wishers to contribute clothes, bedding, and hygiene items, channeling resources through "the Lord's Store" to reach those most in need as they bravely attempt to rebuild their shattered lives.

Nairobi's Recurring Deluge and Public Outcry The recent deluge, which commenced on Friday, March 6, 2026, tragically claimed over 40 lives in Nairobi, left many injured, and obliterated property worth hundreds of millions of shillings. This recurring nightmare of urban flooding has predictably ignited public frustration, with a significant portion of criticism directed at Governor Johnson Sakaja. Residents are demanding accountability and long-term solutions to the perennial issue. As the city grapples with the aftermath, discussions around infrastructure resilience are prominent, especially considering past assurances, such as when KeNHA allayed fears of flooding at the Nairobi Greenpark Underpass, debunking AI misinformation surrounding its vulnerability. The ongoing situation spotlights the critical need for robust urban planning and emergency preparedness to safeguard Nairobi's citizens from future climate-related adversities.
