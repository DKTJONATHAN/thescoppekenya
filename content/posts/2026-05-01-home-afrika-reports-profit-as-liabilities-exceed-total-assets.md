## Home Afrika Reports Profitability Amidst Significant Liabilities

Home Afrika Limited reported a net profit of KSh 117.89 million for the full year ended December 31, 2025, marking its second consecutive year of profitability. Despite a top-line contraction, the NSE-listed property developer significantly improved its margins through cost reduction, yet faces substantial balance sheet challenges with total liabilities exceeding total assets.

### Financial Performance Overview
Home Afrika Limited achieved a net profit of KSh 117.89 million in 2025, extending its profitable streak. This positive outcome occurred despite a 34.9% revenue contraction to KSh 508.67 million from KSh 781.91 million in 2024, a decline attributed to the timing of deferred income recognition rather than weakened demand. The company demonstrated material improvements across all profitability measures, with the gross profit margin widening to 54.9% from 47.8%, the operating margin expanding to 37.2% from 28.6%, and the net profit margin rising to 23.2% from 17.1%. These margin gains are largely a result of a 20% reduction in overall costs, including a 35.1% drop in administrative expenses to KSh 88.57 million and a 43.9% decline in the cost of sales to KSh 229.30 million. Finance costs also decreased by 10.2% to KSh 70.68 million.

### Diversified Revenue and Enhanced Cash Generation
Other operating income for Home Afrika saw a substantial increase of 48.5% to KSh 46.56 million. This growth was driven by rental income from Mitini Apartments, office subletting at the Morningside headquarters, and increased green fees and tournament activity at the Migaa Golf Course. The golf course, now fully certified and operational, has become a significant secondary revenue stream and a catalyst for adjacent residential plot sales, a dynamic that continued into the first half of 2025 where other income more than doubled year-on-year. Operating cash generation also strengthened, with net cash from operating activities rising 29% to KSh 396.33 million from KSh 307.11 million, providing the business with a degree of self-funding capacity it has largely lacked in its listed history.

### Balance Sheet Imbalance and Capital Structure Concerns
Despite operational improvements, Home Afrika's balance sheet reveals considerable challenges. Total liabilities stand at KSh 4.77 billion, significantly exceeding total assets of KSh 3.74 billion. This indicates a negative equity position, which improved from negative KSh 2.33 billion in 2024 to negative KSh 1.03 billion in 2025, largely due to a KSh 1.18 billion prior year adjustment rather than retained earnings. Borrowings increased by 6.8% to KSh 1.09 billion, and the KSh 680.95 million private placement bond remains unchanged with no visible repayment progress. Additionally, trade receivables nearly doubled to KSh 649.38 million against a backdrop of falling revenue, a divergence that the company's published commentary did not address. The directors have not recommended a dividend for the period, citing the need to rebuild equity and reduce obligations. The board's medium-term target is the recovery of a positive equity position before any return of capital to shareholders.
