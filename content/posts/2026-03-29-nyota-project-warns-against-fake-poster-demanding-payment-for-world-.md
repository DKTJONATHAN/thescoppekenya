The National Youth Opportunities Towards Advancement (NYOTA) project has issued a stern warning against a deceptive poster demanding a Ksh200 fee to access World Bank-backed funds. On Sunday, March 29, 2026, NYOTA clarified that all its services are completely free and exclusively available through the USSD code *254#.

## NYOTA Project Clarifies Fraudulent Payment Claims

The clarification follows the widespread circulation on social media of a fake poster since March 29, asserting that individuals could obtain Ksh36,000 through the NYOTA Project by paying a Ksh200 fee via a specified paybill number. The official statement from NYOTA unequivocally labeled the poster as fake, urging the public to disregard it, and reiterated that the project operates without any till numbers.

### Unpacking the Deceptive Poster

The fraudulent material prominently featured the Daily Nation newspaper logo and an image of President William Ruto, accompanied by bold text proclaiming, “PRESIDENT ANNOUNCES SUPPORT FOR CITIZENS.” This deceptive presentation aimed to lend an air of legitimacy to the scam, attempting to exploit those seeking opportunities from the genuine government initiative. In addition to the paybill number hoax, NYOTA also emphasized that it does not offer any mobile applications or provide loans, reinforcing that all project services remain free of charge.

### Understanding the Authentic NYOTA Initiative

The NYOTA Project stands as a pivotal government initiative designed to empower youth by providing business startup capital and comprehensive support. Launched by President William Ruto in September 2025, the fund is specifically targeted at unemployed individuals aged 18-29, with an age extension to 35 for persons with disabilities. The project offers crucial grants, training, and essential business support, distributing funds in carefully managed phases to ensure accountability. Beneficiaries typically possess a Form 4 education level or below, receiving six-month on-the-job training alongside startup capital for small businesses.

The phased rollout of the program, which President William Ruto oversaw nationwide in November 2025, initially encompassed counties within the Western and Mount Kenya regions, including Kakamega, Vihiga, Busia, and Bungoma. By early January 2026, the initiative had expanded to Uasin Gishu, Nakuru, Nairobi, and Kiambu, among others. The program has since reached Nyanza and Rift Valley counties, with a significant disbursement event held in Kisumu for youth from Siaya, Kisumu, and Homa Bay. In Northern Kenya, NYOTA funds have been distributed in Garissa and Mandera counties, with Wajir slated for the final phase of rollout, further solidifying the project's commitment to supporting youth across the nation.
