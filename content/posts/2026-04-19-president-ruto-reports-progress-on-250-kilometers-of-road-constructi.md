President William Ruto today, April 19, 2026, revealed significant progress on the 250-kilometer road construction project across Nairobi County. Speaking at Karen Africa Gospel Church, the President confirmed 67 kilometers of tarmac roads are already under construction, underscoring ongoing efforts to transform the capital's infrastructure and enhance its national representation.

Nairobi Infrastructure Overhaul Underway

Road Development and Urban Modernization President Ruto affirmed that 67 kilometers of the planned 250-kilometer road network are currently under construction within Nairobi. He indicated that an additional 70 kilometers will commence in the coming week, with the remaining sections to follow. The President stressed the critical importance of Nairobi's infrastructure, noting that many international visitors' perceptions of the entire country are formed through their experience of the capital. Therefore, improving Nairobi is essential to accurately reflect Kenya's overall image and status.

Comprehensive City Lighting Project Beyond road improvements, President Ruto announced a large-scale initiative to install over 40,000 street lights across Nairobi. This project aims to illuminate not only major highways but also residential estates throughout the city. The President emphasized that Nairobi operates as a 24-hour hub, and improved lighting is crucial for fostering continuous business operations and enhancing urban safety.

Joint National-County Partnership These infrastructure developments stem from a landmark Ksh80 billion joint cooperation agreement signed on February 17, 2026, between President Ruto and Nairobi Governor Johnson Sakaja. This partnership, solidified at State House, Nairobi, targets crucial areas including roads, water supply, housing, and waste management. Governor Sakaja clarified that the agreement functions as a collaborative effort to inject national government resources and expertise into city-level projects, without transferring constitutional functions away from the Nairobi County government.

National Infrastructure Vision The Nairobi projects are part of a broader national commitment to tarmac more than 28,000 kilometers of roads over the next seven years. This ambitious plan is central to the government's "Singapore Dream" agenda, aimed at elevating the country from third-world to first-world status. The focus on robust infrastructure, including transparent management of resources, aligns with initiatives such as the NTSA's smart logbook management guidelines, designed to prevent ownership disputes and fraud, ensuring efficiency in related sectors.
