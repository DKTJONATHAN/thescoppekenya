COTU Secretary-General Francis Atwoli recently clarified varying figures regarding a newly announced wage increment, confirming a 12 percent wage increase for all employees. This clarification on the Francis Atwoli wage increase comes amidst public discussion, with a higher 15 percent rate specifically earmarked for agricultural and low-income earners across Kenya, ensuring a vital boost for many.

## Francis Atwoli Wage Increase: Decoding the Latest Figures

### A Dual-Rate Boost for Kenyan Workers

The central announcement confirms a 12 percent general wage increase designed to uplift a broad spectrum of Kenya's workforce. However, the clarification also highlighted a more substantial 15 percent increment specifically targeted at agricultural workers and those in lower income brackets. This strategic differentiation aims to provide more significant relief and improved living standards for segments of the population often most impacted by economic fluctuations. The implications of this Francis Atwoli wage increase are expected to ripple through various sectors as 2026 progresses.
