## Kenya Manufacturing Sector Driven by Industrial and Construction Gains in 2025

Kenya's manufacturing sector experienced modest 2.1% growth in 2025, reaching KSh 3.8 trillion in output. This expansion was uneven, largely driven by industrial and construction-related production, which offset a notable contraction in agro-processing, particularly sugar and tea, impacting overall sector performance.

### Uneven Sector Performance and Funding Dynamics

Overall production volumes in the manufacturing sector increased by 2.1% in 2025, with the value of output rising to KSh 3.8 trillion from KSh 3.6 trillion in 2024. This growth was supported by a 5.7% increase in loans to the manufacturing sector, reaching KSh 596.3 billion, predominantly from commercial banks. Conversely, lending by development finance institutions saw a decline, with approvals from the Development Bank of Kenya falling to KSh 360 million from KSh 559.7 million and by the Kenya Development Corporation to KSh 1.0 billion from KSh 1.9 billion.

### Agro-Processing Contraction Weighs Heavily

Agro-based manufacturing weakened significantly, leading to a 1.2% contraction in total food manufacturing, a reversal from the 7.9% expansion in 2024. This decline was primarily driven by a 24.8% drop in sugar production, which fell to 613.2 thousand tonnes from 815.5 thousand tonnes due to cane shortages. Additionally, the manufacture of food products not elsewhere classified, largely tea and coffee, decreased by 5.5%. Made tea production dropped from 598.5 thousand tonnes to 550.4 thousand tonnes, though milled coffee saw a slight increase from 37.2 thousand tonnes to 37.9 thousand tonnes. Processed fruit volumes contributed to a 5% contraction in prepared and preserved fruits and vegetables, falling by 9.6%.

Despite these declines, some segments within agro-processing demonstrated growth. Dairy products expanded by 12.9%, with processed milk rising to 701.5 million litres from 619.1 million litres, and yoghurt and fermented milk increasing by 4.4%. Meat and meat products grew by 9.8%, prepared animal feeds by 8.9%, grain mill products by 3.4%, and bakery products by 1.8%. Beverages also expanded by 1.3% as soft drink production rose to 703.7 million litres. However, these gains were insufficient to counteract the significant drops in sugar and tea production.

### Industrial and Construction-Linked Manufacturing Drives Growth

The industrial and construction related subsectors showed robust performance. Basic metals grew by 4.9% in 2025, reversing a 6.1% decline in 2024, supported by higher output of flat-rolled steel. Fabricated metal products expanded by 3.3%, structural metal products by 3.7%, and electrical equipment by 3.2%, reflecting increased demand from the construction, energy, and telecommunications sectors. Wood and wood products grew by 5.8%, while plastics products increased by 2.8%, fueled by packaging materials and construction inputs.

The transport equipment sector recorded a strong rebound, with the motor vehicles, trailers, and semi-trailers subsector expanding by 15.2% after a 3.6% contraction in 2024. Vehicle assembly rose by 18.5% to 13,692 units from 11,555 units. Machinery and equipment grew by 3.4%, and furniture by 5.4%. Textiles grew by 2.6%, reversing an 11.4% contraction in 2024, and leather products edged up 0.9%. Chemical products expanded by 1.2%, though constrained by a 20.1% drop in ethyl alcohol production, while pharmaceuticals rose by 2.0%.

### Employment and Export Zones Expand

Employment in manufacturing grew by 5.2% to 388,564 workers, accounting for 11.7% of formal wage employment. Compensation of employees in the sector increased to KSh 322.2 billion from KSh 309.6 billion in 2024. The Export Processing Zones (EPZs) demonstrated significant growth, with employment rising by 16.4% to 104,692 workers, driven primarily by apparel and agro-processing firms. The number of EPZ enterprises increased to 185 from 180, and total sales rose by 4.4% to KSh 142.2 billion, with exports increasing to KSh 136.8 billion from KSh 129.4 billion.
