As Celestine Nzioka, Za Ndani.

Railway Development Levy Fund Established for Infrastructure Expansion President William Ruto today signed legislation establishing the Railway Development Levy Fund, a new vehicle for railway infrastructure financing. This dedicated fund, recently cleared by Parliament, enables the administration to mobilise significant capital for projects beyond the Standard Gauge Railway by leveraging future levy collections for borrowing.

Expanded Scope and Funding Mechanisms On Friday, March 13, President Ruto assented to the Miscellaneous Fees and Levies (Amendment) Act, 2026, officially establishing the Railway Development Levy Fund. This legislative action broadens the application of the Railway Development Levy (RDL) to encompass a wider array of railway transport infrastructure, moving beyond its initial focus on the Standard Gauge Railway (SGR). Over Ksh35 billion collected through the RDL will now be directly channeled into this fund. The new law introduces a critical provision allowing the government to securitise future RDL collections, enabling borrowing against these revenues. Specifically, a clause permits up to 90 percent of the Fund to be used as security for additional financing dedicated to developing railway infrastructure, including the SGR. This move aligns with the government's objective of securing sustainable financing for strategic railway projects and strengthening the management framework for the levy's utilisation, according to a statement from State House. This initiative follows the President's recent statements regarding regional stability and his [condemnation of strikes on allied nations](https://zandani.co.ke/article/ruto-condemns-strikes-uae-qatar-saudi-arabia-urgent-de-escalation).

Securitisation and Future Financing Plans The Treasury previously securitised Ksh7 from the Ksh25 collected per litre of petrol and diesel, following an increase in the levy from Ksh18 in July 2024. This strategy is now being expanded. In January 2026, the government announced plans to securitise Ksh125 billion in short-term syndicated loans to finance ongoing road projects, facilitated through the Railway Development Fund. This initiative is projected to push the total amount raised through fuel levy securitisation to Ksh300 billion. Reporting from Bloomberg indicates that Ksh60 billion of these loans are expected in the first half of 2026, with the remaining balance to be received in the 2026/27 fiscal year, commencing in July. Additionally, the government intends to list a Ksh175 billion road bond on the Nairobi Securities Exchange (NSE) via the Railway Development Levy Fund to refinance earlier bridge loans. The fund's proceeds are also designated to support railway infrastructure safety, economic regulation, and the rehabilitation of existing railway systems, with such rehabilitation projects requiring joint approval from the Cabinet Secretaries for the National Treasury and Transport.
