Housing PS Charles Hinga recently clarified that the platform has not seen a mass exodus of capital. While reports suggested Ksh 2.56 billion in withdrawals, official data indicates that most of this money moved internally to secure specific home deposits.

Many Kenyans monitoring their Boma Yangu accounts have been confused by the terminology used on the user dashboard. When a contributor decides to commit their savings toward a specific project, such as the units at Park Road, the system reflects this as an allocation. This movement of funds is often misread as a withdrawal from the entire housing scheme. To clear the air, the government has requested the Auditor-General to perform a full financial review of the housing project funds. This audit aims to provide a transparent breakdown of how the 1.26 million registered users are engaging with the platform.

### Why do some dashboard entries look like withdrawals?

The core of the recent public concern lies in how the portal displays transactional data. On the dashboard, an internal transfer-where a saver moves money from their general savings pool into a specific project deposit-is logged in a way that can appear identical to an account exit.

In reality, these funds remain within the government-backed housing ecosystem. Of the total figure cited in recent reports, only 31.4 percent represents actual cash returned to contributors who chose to leave the scheme entirely. The remaining portion comprises internal reallocations. Effectively, the dashboard is doing its job by showing the movement of assets, but the interface lacks a clear distinction between a voluntary exit and a project commitment. This is the primary reason why depositors often fear their money has been removed when, in fact, it has been earmarked for a physical unit.

### How are these funds actually being utilized?

Financial transparency is the primary driver behind the call for an independent audit. The government maintains that while some participants have withdrawn their contributions, the bulk of the money is active. Records show that Ksh 788 million has been redirected toward home unit deposits, signaling that a significant number of contributors are transitioning from simple savers to active homeowners.

The platform currently manages varying streams of revenue, including the housing levy and private savings. Distinguishing between these is essential for users who want to track their progress. If you are checking your balance, it is important to look for the "Net Withdrawal" metric rather than the total "Outgoing Transactions" column. The net figure accounts for the 31.4 percent of users who have opted out, providing a clearer view of the platform's stability.
