President William Ruto has directly intervened in the ongoing governance and financial disputes at Nairobi Hospital, as confirmed by State House spokesperson Hussein Mohammed on Monday, March 16, 2026. The President's involvement aims to ensure transparency and stability amidst legal challenges facing the institution.

Presidential Oversight at Nairobi Hospital President Ruto's intervention follows numerous complaints from various stakeholders, including doctors, members of the Kenya Hospital Association (KHA), and patients. These appeals highlighted significant concerns regarding the potential impact of longstanding governance issues and financial mismanagement on patient care, clinical oversight, and the hospital's operational stability. State House confirmed receiving written memoranda detailing these challenges over several months.

Clarifying Presidential Involvement Despite rumours circulating last week about a potential government takeover of Nairobi Hospital, State House clarified that President Ruto's role is strictly supervisory. The President's directive is to allow institutional processes to adhere to the law, safeguard staff, and guarantee uninterrupted services for patients, without influencing the board's composition or daily operations. This aligns with the government's broader commitment to ensuring the effective functioning of key institutions, as seen in other critical sectors.

Investigative Measures and Legal Actions In response to the petitions, the Attorney General invoked Section 800 of the Companies Act to appoint inspectors, initiating an investigation into the hospital's affairs. Facilitated consultative meetings led by Chief of Staff and Head of Public Service Felix Koskei were also part of the early resolution initiatives. A multi-agency team comprising the Business Registration Service, Asset Recovery Agency, Kenya Revenue Authority, DCI, National Intelligence Service, and Financial Reporting Centre submitted its investigative report on March 6, 2026. This report subsequently led to several detentions and, on Monday, the prosecution pressed criminal charges against doctors associated with the management of Kenya Hospital Association Limited, the entity operating Nairobi Hospital. Health Cabinet Secretary Aden Duale further clarified on Tuesday that Nairobi Hospital, structured as a company limited by guarantee, cannot be acquired or privatised, reinforcing the supervisory nature of the President's intervention.
