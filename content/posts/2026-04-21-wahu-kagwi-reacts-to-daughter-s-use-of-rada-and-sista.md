### Unpacking "Sista" and "Rada"

While Wahu Kagwi pondered the origins and implications of "Sista" and "Rada," these terms have solidified their place in the lexicon of Kenyan youth. "Sista" is often used as an informal, friendly address for a female, akin to "sister" but with a more casual, almost universal application among peers. "Rada," on the other hand, generally refers to being aware, in the know, or having the latest information about a situation or event. Understanding these phrases becomes essential for adults hoping to bridge the communication divide with the nation's youth.
