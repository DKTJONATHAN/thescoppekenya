## AFCON 2027 Dates Confirmed Awaiting Venue Decision

CAF has officially confirmed the AFCON 2027 tournament dates, scheduled to commence on Saturday, June 19, 2027, and conclude with the final on Saturday, July 17, 2027. Kenya, alongside Uganda and Tanzania, will co-host the prestigious event. Awaiting decisions on which nation will stage the opening and closing matches, the qualifying draw is set for May 19, 2026, involving 48 teams competing for tournament slots.

### Tournament Calendar and Venue Choices

The official calendar for the Africa Cup of Nations 2027, approved by the FIFA Council, indicates a slightly adjusted end date compared to earlier reports. While the opening match is fixed for June 19, 2027, the final will now occur on July 17, 2027, a day earlier than previously circulated. CAF is yet to announce which of the co-hosting nations will be granted the honor of hosting the tournament's inaugural and concluding matches.

Kenya's newly constructed Ksh45 billion Talanta Stadium in Nairobi, with a 60,000-seat capacity, is a strong contender for these pivotal games under the Pamoja Bid. Other proposed stadiums for the opening and closing matches include Dar es Salaam's Benjamin Mkapa Stadium and Uganda's modernized Mandela National Stadium. For official matches and training, Kenya also lists the refurbished Kasarani and Nyayo stadiums in Nairobi, along with Kipchoge Keino Stadium in Eldoret. Tanzania plans to utilize a new facility in Arusha, named for Samia Suluhu Hassan, and Amaan Stadium in Zanzibar, while Uganda focuses on new constructions in Hoima and Lira, in addition to the revamped Nakivubo Stadium.

### AFCON 2027 Qualifiers Schedule

The journey to the AFCON 2027 tournament involves a rigorous qualification process. CAF will conduct a draw on May 19, 2026, to determine the qualifying groups. Forty-eight teams, including the three co-hosts, will participate, divided into 12 groups of four teams each. The top two teams from each group will secure their place in the final tournament.

The qualifiers are slated across three FIFA International Windows. The first window runs from September 21 to October 6, 2026, followed by the second from November 9 to 17, 2026. The final round of qualifiers will take place from March 22 to 30, 2027.
