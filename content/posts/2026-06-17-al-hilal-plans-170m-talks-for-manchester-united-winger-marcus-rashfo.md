## Al Hilal's €170m Mirage for Rashford's "Dream"

Marcus Rashford's "dream" Barcelona return hinges precariously on Al Hilal's sprawling €170m pursuit of Raphinha. With his €30m option ignored and Manchester United desperate to offload their £40m asset, the England winger faces a reality check: a Saudi payday or a less-than-desirable path back to Catalonia.

### Rashford's Barcelona Delusion

It takes a special kind of optimism, or perhaps delusion, to believe a "dream transfer" to Barcelona is on the cards when the club just signed Anthony Gordon for £70m and let your own €30m option expire without a second thought. Marcus Rashford, currently preparing for England's World Cup opener against Croatia this Wednesday, reportedly "only has eyes for Barcelona." That's admirable commitment to a fantasy, considering Barcelona explicitly refused to meet what even his parent club, Manchester United, deemed a fair price. They wanted another loan. United said no. Simple. The Catalan giants have clearly moved on, leaving Rashford in a rather awkward limbo. This isn't a story of mutual desire; it's a player clinging to a narrative long past its sell-by date.

### United's Desperate Offload Strategy

Manchester United, ever the masters of market confusion, find themselves in a familiar bind. After Rashford's loan spell, they "fully intend to find a new buyer" for the 28-year-old. The pressure is on, particularly with reports suggesting United are actively exploring a move for West Ham's £50m-rated Crysencio Summerville. The cynical truth here is that a new winger, like Summerville, hinges entirely on Rashford making way first. And what about that "secret £40m release clause" David Ornstein conveniently revealed on Monday? A "secret" clause available to any club not named Liverpool or Manchester City sounds less like a strategic move and more like a desperate attempt to offload a player quickly before his value diminishes further. This isn't a club capitalising on an asset; it's a club scrambling to cut losses and clear the wage bill. The future at Old Trafford is certainly shifting, with figures like Michael Carrick potentially shaping a new look if deals like this fall into place.

### Al Hilal's Opportunistic Plays

Enter Al Hilal, the Saudi Pro League side who seem to be the universal solution for any European club looking to cash in on a player, or for a player looking for a life-changing sum of money. While the headline screams of Al Hilal targeting Rashford, the source text quickly pivots to their true, long-standing desire: Barcelona star Raphinha. Two years ago, they tried for Raphinha with a €100m deal. Now, Brazilian outlet GE Globo reports they've made him "one of the main targets" this summer, prepared to offer a staggering €170m across a two-year deal, quadrupling his current Nou Camp salary. Raphinha himself admitted in October 2025 that a previous Saudi offer "shook me a lot, it would solve my personal life, my parents', my son's, many people." That's the cold reality of modern football: personal finances often trump sporting ambition. Raphinha's 'emphatic' refusal to discuss his future before the World Cup effectively translates to: "call me after the tournament, let's talk numbers."

### The World Cup Waiting Game

So, how does Al Hilal's pursuit of Raphinha give Rashford a "glimmer of hope" for Barcelona? It's simple, if brutally cynical. If Al Hilal finally lands Raphinha, Barcelona would be left with a gaping hole on the wing. At that point, a return for Rashford - even one they initially dismissed as too expensive or only worthy of a loan - suddenly becomes a desperate measure. It's not about Barcelona wanting Rashford; it's about potentially needing him when their first, second, and perhaps third choices are gone. For Rashford, his "dream" might not be a direct path, but rather a chaotic ripple effect driven by Saudi oil money and Barcelona's own transfer mishaps. He might get his wish, but it will be a transfer born of opportunism and desperation, not genuine desire.
