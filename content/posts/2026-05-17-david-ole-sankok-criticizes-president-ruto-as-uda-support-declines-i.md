Former nominated Member of Parliament David Ole Sankok has publicly criticized President William Ruto’s administration over worsening governance and corruption. This dissent indicates a significant decline in support for the ruling UDA party within the Rift Valley, signaling growing instability in the President’s inner circle.

## Political Fragmentation Within the Rift Valley

### The Evolving Rift Valley Landscape
The political foundation of the ruling UDA party is experiencing significant disruption. David Ole Sankok, formerly a staunch defender of the administration, has shifted his stance to openly challenge the current leadership. His recent critique highlights a cooling relationship between the President and his traditional support base, suggesting that the administration's initial political momentum has faded as regional leaders reassess their loyalties.

### Strategic Departures Among Political Opportunists
Observers note that this change in rhetoric is part of a calculated survival strategy among regional politicians. Faced with waning public support for the regime, many figures are distancing themselves from the administration to avoid being associated with its perceived failures. These politicians are often characterized as individuals who shift their allegiances based on shifting political fortunes. The current move by loyalists to vocalize their opposition indicates a preemptive effort to secure their own political futures before the next election cycle begins.

### Systematic Challenges and Administrative Scrutiny
The criticism directed at the government focuses heavily on systemic corruption and failures in national management. Beyond minor policy disputes, there is mounting frustration regarding the broader direction of the country. As internal dissatisfaction grows, the administration is finding it increasingly difficult to maintain a unified front. By choosing to speak out, Sankok is setting a precedent that warns other allies of the potential consequences of remaining tied to an administration that is losing its political grip. The movement suggests that many insiders are choosing to abandon their positions rather than remain stranded when the current political tide turns.
