## Insecurity in Mandera County

Six passengers were killed and five others sustained serious injuries on Saturday morning when unidentified gunmen ambushed a 14-seater public service vehicle travelling along the Mandera-Arabia transport route. This latest incident highlights the persistent, structural vulnerability of commuters in Northern Kenya.

The attack occurred near Beer Awayon as the matatu made its way toward Arabia village within the volatile Lafey Constituency. Security forces arrived at the scene shortly after the ambush, but the perpetrators had already fled into the surrounding terrain. Medical staff at the Arabia Sub-County Hospital confirmed the arrival of five injured survivors, who remain under intensive care as the investigation into the security breach continues.

The Mandera-Arabia corridor has become a focal point of concern due to its status as a critical lifeline for local residents. Despite regional stability initiatives, this specific transport route remains consistently exposed to violent disruptions. The reliance on public transport in this border region is driven by a lack of viable alternatives, forcing residents to traverse known danger zones daily to access essential services and markets.
