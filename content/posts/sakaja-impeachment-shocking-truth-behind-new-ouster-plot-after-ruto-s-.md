**Sakaja In Trouble As Nairobi MCAs Launch New Impeachment Plot Following Ksh 80B State Deal** **By Celestine Nzioka** Nairobi Governor Johnson Sakaja is once again fighting for his political life after a defiant group of Nairobi Members of the County Assembly revived a plot to oust him from City Hall. This latest rebellion comes barely two weeks after the Governor signed a monumental Ksh 80 billion cooperation agreement with the national government, a deal that was expected to solidify his standing at the capital. Speaking at a heated press briefing on Tuesday, March 3, the legislators confirmed they are ready to submit a formal impeachment motion to the Clerk of the County Assembly. This move signals a total breakdown of the temporary truce previously brokered by President William Ruto and former Prime Minister Raila Odinga. Nairobi South Ward MCA Esther Waithera Chege, who is leading the current onslaught, revealed that the motion is built on 22 specific grounds. While the fine details of these charges remain under wraps, Chege emphasized that this is a completely fresh process distinct from previous attempts. She noted that the signatures collected so far have already surpassed the required legal threshold. "We have enough signatures for the first round. The second round comes within seven days, and I can assure you we have an overflow of signatures for the first round," Chege stated. She added that the team is now simply counting down the days before the motion hits the floor of the House for formal consideration by the Speaker. This fresh push for removal follows a volatile period in September 2025, when over 70 MCAs signed a petition citing failed campaign promises, bursary delays, and the stalling of the Ward Development Fund. That crisis was only averted after Raila Odinga held a closed door meeting with Sakaja and ODM allied MCAs at the Jaramogi Oginga Odinga Foundation. During that intervention, Sakaja reportedly acknowledged the accusations and apologized to the ward representatives. He was subsequently granted a 60 day window to reform his leadership style and mend his relationship with the Assembly. However, the current move by the MCAs suggests that the cooling off period failed to resolve the underlying friction between the executive and the legislature. The timing of this new ouster plot is particularly significant because it follows closely on the heels of the Ksh 80 billion deal intended to transform city infrastructure. While the Governor has been touting this cooperation as a win for the city, the ward representatives appear more concerned with internal governance issues and the alleged neglect of grassroots priorities. As the Clerk prepares to receive the 22 grounds for impeachment, the political temperature at City Hall has reached a boiling point. For Governor Sakaja, the coming days will be a ultimate test of his political survival skills as he navigates a hostile assembly and a skeptical electorate.
