## High Court Upholds Public Interest in Kenya-US Health Data Agreement Challenge

The High Court has stopped the Consumer Federation of Kenya (COFEK) from withdrawing its legal challenge against the controversial Kenya-US health data agreement. Justice Patricia Nyaundi's recent ruling in Kenya upheld the Katiba Institute's objection, emphasizing that public interest cases on constitutional matters, like data protection and privacy, cannot be privately dismissed.

### Court Blocks Petition Withdrawal

Justice Patricia Nyaundi delivered the ruling, asserting that public interest litigation must remain under the court's control. The decision followed the Katiba Institute's lawyer, Joshua Malidzo, opposing COFEK's attempt to withdraw its petition, which had initially challenged the legality of the Kenya-US health data agreement. Malidzo argued that constitutional issues impacting the public cannot be resolved through private consent between involved parties. Nyaundi also noted Busia Senator Okiya Omtatah's intent to pursue the case in his own 2025 petition, further indicating significant public interest in the matter.

### Details of the Health Data Agreement

The Kenya-US health data agreement, signed in December 2025, sparked controversy due to concerns over Kenyans’ privacy rights, data protection, and insufficient public consultation before its implementation. The deal involves the U.S. channeling over Ksh200 billion in health funding directly through the Kenyan government over five years, shifting away from a previous donor-driven model that relied on non-governmental organizations. This U.S. pledge is contingent on annual increases in Kenya's national and county health budgets, with Kenya expected to allocate Ksh10 billion in 2026/27, Ksh20 billion in 2027/28, Ksh35 billion in 2028/29, and Ksh50 billion in 2029/30. The agreement also mandates the Kenyan government to assume responsibility for health commodities and human resource costs currently funded by the U.S. by 2031. The debate around data protection highlights the ongoing discussions about digital privacy and frameworks, similar to concerns addressed by the [Communications Authority of Kenya releases new technical specifications for mobile devices](https://zandani.co.ke/article/communications-authority-of-kenya-releases-new-technical-specifications).

### Government's Position and Next Steps

President William Ruto has publicly defended the Kenya-US health data agreement, assuring citizens of its strong legal foundation and that their data would not be exploited.
