Deputy Inspector General Gilbert Masengeli confirmed Sunday, April 5, that a man arrested at the Likoni ferry, whose video went viral last week, possessed a fake gun and was diagnosed with mental health challenges. The clarification from Mombasa addresses public concern regarding the incident.

Likoni Ferry Incident Clarified

Details of the Arrest and Findings Speaking to the media in Mombasa, Deputy Inspector General Masengeli revealed that the individual was indeed apprehended and subsequently taken to a hospital for mental health evaluation. Results from medical professionals confirmed that the man was mentally challenged. Furthermore, the firearm found in his possession during the October 2025 arrest at the Likoni ferry was determined to be a fake. Coast Regional Police Commander Ali Nuno had previously clarified that a video circulating on Sunday, March 29, depicting the arrest was not recent, pinpointing the incident to October 2025. Upon searching the man, police recovered what appeared to be a pistol tied around his waist, alongside a sling and other unspecified items.

Police Vigilance and Public Safety Masengeli emphasized that the police could not disregard potential risks, regardless of an individual's mental state. He stated that officers remain on high alert to protect both the public and themselves. The decision to take the man into custody, despite awareness of his street-dwelling status and mental health challenges, stemmed from a commitment to public safety. Instances of criminals collaborating with street individuals to store weapons have been observed in the country, with previous cases such as street youths in Dandora, Nairobi, surrendering several firearms. This operational reality necessitates vigilance from law enforcement in areas like Likoni.

Ongoing Anti-Gang Operations The clarification by the Deputy Inspector General aligns with intensified police operations targeting criminal gangs in the coastal region. The Directorate of Criminal Investigations has reported over 20 arrests in less than a month as part of these efforts. This proactive stance underscores the continuous commitment of law enforcement to bolster security and curb criminal activities in urban areas.
