## The Imenti Forest Siege: A Scripted Participation Charade

The Kenya Forest Service attempted to host a public consultation in Meru today, completely disregarding a subsisting court injunction that halted the process. While officials arrived with prepared presentations, residents arrived with a mounting collection of grievances and a petition containing 7,000 signatures.

### The Fiction of Consent

State machinery in Kenya often operates under the assumption that a microphone and a podium constitute an invitation for dialogue. At the Imenti Forest forum this Wednesday, the Kenya Forest Service (KFS) demonstrated exactly how fragile that assumption is. Despite a court order explicitly suspending the exercise due to a lack of proper notice to stakeholders, organizers proceeded as if the legal system were merely a suggestion.

The strategy was transparent: frame the project as a localized improvement while obscuring the broader ecological catastrophe. The proposed 11-hectare footprint for a State Lodge, airstrip, and golf course is being marketed as a development milestone. Yet, the environmental reality-that this is a protected ecosystem-remains buried under the weight of bureaucratic maneuvering. Similar to the disconnect seen in [Bolt's recent pricing disputes](https://zandani.co.ke/article/bolt-defends-ev-boda-boda-fares-amid-protests), official corporate or state communication continues to ignore the lived experience of those actually bearing the costs on the ground.

### Silencing the Evidence

The event reached its inevitable breaking point when KamaNu M’tuamwari attempted to present a petition reflecting the deep-seated opposition of the Meru community. His presence on the stage lasted mere seconds before he was forcibly removed by security details. This was not merely an eviction from a podium; it was the symbolic silencing of local voices that have consistently challenged the state's unilateral decision-making.

Sherie Gakii and other organizers appeared stunned that the community refused to act as compliant extras in a pre-recorded play. By ejecting the very voices they claimed to be consulting, the KFS invalidated the entire premise of the gathering. It is an old tactic: exclude the dissenters, label the remaining crowd as representative, and file the report as a successful exercise in public participation. This performance mirrors the systemic apathy seen in recent political processes, where [low turnout and candidate victories](https://zandani.co.ke/article/low-voter-turnout-in-ol-kalou-uda-primaries-raises-concerns-after-ca) are treated as democratic confirmation rather than symptoms of a rotting institutional mandate.

### The Cost of a Concrete Playground

The math of this development is increasingly difficult to justify. Converting 11 hectares of protected forest into a luxury retreat creates a long-term deficit in water catchment and biodiversity that no amount of economic projection can offset. The state pushes for a golf course, yet the local population is left to manage the drying springs and shifting climate patterns that follow forest degradation.

Legal injunctions are the only remaining barrier between the Imenti ecosystem and the heavy machinery waiting in the wings. By holding this forum in direct defiance of the court-mandated July 21 deadline, the state is signaling that it views the judiciary as an inconvenient obstacle to be bypassed rather than a branch of government to be respected. The KFS has prioritized the vanity of a State Lodge over the long-term survival of the forest. If the law can be bypassed this brazenly during a public process, what hope is there for the preservation of the environment once the project breaks ground?

If the state is so confident in the benefits of its forest-leveling project, why is it so afraid to let an activist finish a single sentence?
