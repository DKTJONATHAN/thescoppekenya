## Kahuro Office Opening Marred by Administrator Boycott The official opening of the Kahuro Assistant County Commissioner's (ACC) offices in Kiharu constituency proceeded on Monday, March 9, despite a significant boycott by local administrators. Kiharu MP Ndindi Nyoro presided over the ceremony, which was briefly disrupted by police. This event follows a previous boycott last year, highlighting ongoing tensions between the lawmaker and government functionaries.

Administrator Boycott The launch of the Kahuro ACC offices on Monday, March 9, 2026, was notably devoid of local government administrators, who reportedly stayed away following instructions from senior authorities. Police officers briefly attempted to halt the ceremony, but residents gathered for the event confronted the officers, compelling them to leave the venue. MP Ndindi Nyoro publicly criticized a senior officer from Nairobi for attempting to obstruct the launch, challenging the individual to confront him directly.

Political Tensions This recent boycott is not an isolated incident. In March 2025, several administrators and police officers in Muranga County also boycotted a handover ceremony led by Nyoro, involving the opening of a new assistant county commissioner’s office and administration block in the Mjini area. Funded by the Constituency Development Fund (CDF), that project also faced similar administrative non-cooperation. Nyoro dismissed the boycotts as intimidation tactics aimed at hindering his work, vowing to remain unbowed and stating his commitment to development over political animosity. The political environment in Kenya has seen instances where law enforcement agencies, such as the DCI, have accused politicians of arming followers, indicating a broader landscape of complex political dynamics and influence.

Constituency Initiatives The Kiharu lawmaker has recently shown divergence from the agenda of the Kenyan Kwanza government, focusing prominently on development initiatives within his constituency. Among his recent announcements are free primary education and a subsidized secondary school program in Kiharu, where students pay only Ksh 500, along with free lunch for all learners.
