The Ethics and Anti-Corruption Commission (EACC) yesterday arrested a Nakuru Water and Sewerage Company (NAWASCO) leak detection officer in Langa Langa Estate. The apprehension followed a public complaint regarding a Ksh15,000 bribe demanded for a water meter replacement, with investigators recovering Ksh133,000 in suspected graft proceeds during the operation.

Graft Allegations in Nakuru

Suspect Apprehended with Concealed Funds The suspect was apprehended on Monday, March 23, 2026, following an operation initiated by EACC detectives. A resident of Langa Langa Estate had reported that the official demanded Ksh15,000 to facilitate the replacement of a confiscated water meter. Upon his arrest while allegedly receiving the bribe, investigators recovered the initial Ksh15,000 in cash. Further search revealed an additional Ksh100,000 concealed within his socks and Ksh18,000 in his coat pocket, all suspected to be proceeds from corrupt activities.

Commission Launches Extensive Investigations The recovery of the substantial sum of money has intensified suspicions of widespread corrupt practices within the institution. The EACC has confirmed that investigations are actively underway to ascertain if more individuals or transactions are linked to the ongoing case. Following his processing at the commission’s South Rift Regional Office in Nakuru, the suspect was subsequently detained at the Central Police Station.

Recent Victories Against Corruption This arrest closely follows a significant triumph in the fight against graft, where the commission successfully recovered Ksh10.9 million from a former Deputy Director of Health in Homa Bay County. The funds represented salaries unlawfully earned, as determined by a ruling from an anti-corruption court. The EACC disclosed on March 17, 2026, that the recovery stemmed from months of investigation and legal proceedings which confirmed the official, Walter Odhiambo, had been simultaneously employed at two different levels of government, a direct violation of established legal provisions. Justice Benjamin Musyoki of the Milimani Anti-Corruption court had directed that Mr. Odhiambo refund all salaries paid to him between June 2016 and August 2021, citing irregular payments due to dual employment. In what signals a concerning and persistent trend among government employees, the EACC had previously apprehended a Lands Control Board official on March 3, 2025, for soliciting bribes. That arrest was also prompted by a tip-off from a complainant seeking consent from the board to transfer land they had sold.
