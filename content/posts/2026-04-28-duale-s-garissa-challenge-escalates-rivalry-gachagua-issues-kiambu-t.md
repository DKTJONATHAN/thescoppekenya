## Duale-Gachagua Rivalry Heats Up Over North Eastern Development

### North Eastern Development Becomes Battleground

The dramatic fireworks began in February 2026 when Health Cabinet Secretary Aden Duale, speaking at a youth empowerment event in Garissa, publicly challenged former Deputy President Rigathi Gachagua. Duale dared Gachagua to a televised debate concerning development matters in North Eastern Kenya, even proposing a specific TV program for the showdown. Gachagua, however, swiftly dismissed the challenge as "laughable," declaring that he would only engage in such a high-stakes debate with President William Ruto, underlining the profound political chasm between the two figures. This exchange followed Gachagua's prior criticisms regarding the region's development and alleged misuse of devolution funds.

### High Drama: Gachagua Alleges 'Blockade' and Issues 'Tread Carefully' Warning

The saga dramatically intensified in April 2026, with Gachagua announcing his firm intention to undertake visits to Garissa and other North Eastern regions. In a fiery pronouncement, he vehemently alleged that Duale was attempting to orchestrate an audacious blockade of these planned visits, claiming the Health CS sought to conceal purported truths about regional development. This accusation painted a picture of Duale actively attempting to suppress free movement and access to information. Gachagua also issued a sharp counter-warning to Duale, who resides in Kiambu-a region widely considered Gachagua's political stronghold-advising him to "tread carefully". This statement, loaded with territorial undertones, echoed Gachagua's earlier defense of former President Uhuru Kenyatta, where he emphatically stated that Kenyatta "never blocked him [Ruto] from travelling across the country," subtly implying Duale might be engaging in such tactics.
