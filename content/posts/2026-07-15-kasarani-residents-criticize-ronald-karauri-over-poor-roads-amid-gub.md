Kasarani Member of Parliament Ronald Karauri is facing significant backlash from his constituents this week as residents highlight the worsening state of local infrastructure. The criticism follows rising speculation that the lawmaker is preparing a bid for the Nairobi gubernatorial seat.

## The State of Kasarani Infrastructure

### Crumbling Road Networks
The condition of the road network in Kasarani has reached a critical point, with major access routes riddled with deep, hazardous potholes. Motorists and pedestrians describe the daily commute as a significant struggle that causes substantial damage to vehicles and creates constant traffic congestion. Residents assert that these infrastructure failures demonstrate a lack of attention to basic constituency needs, contrasting the state of the roads with the MP’s recent public profile.

### Ambition Versus Local Performance
Political observers and constituents are highlighting the disconnect between the MP’s rumored plans for higher office and his performance within his current jurisdiction. Critics argue that seeking a promotion to lead the entire county is premature when fundamental service delivery at the constituency level remains unaddressed. There is a growing consensus among locals that potential campaign outreach is failing to gain traction because it ignores the immediate, tangible reality of the neglected roads.

### Hyperbolic Responses to Leadership
The frustration within the constituency has led some residents to express their dissatisfaction through sharp comparisons. Some locals have stated that the current state of neglect is so severe that they would prefer the incumbent county leadership to a new administration led by the current MP. While this sentiment is not an endorsement of the existing county government, it serves as a pointed critique of the representative’s record. Voters have indicated that they are increasingly skeptical of political promises, prioritizing clear evidence of local development over future electoral ambitions.
