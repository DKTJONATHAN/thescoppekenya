KeNHA’s pre-dawn assault on Roysambu traders on Wednesday, March 4, 2026, was not a development initiative; it was a cowardly act of state-sanctioned barbarism, stripping hardworking Kenyans of their livelihoods and dignity without a shred of humanity. This regime, through its enforcers at the Kenya National Highways Authority, has once again proven its utter contempt for the common mwananchi, leaving nothing but ruin in its wake.

## The Brutality of the KeNHA Roysambu Stalls Demolition 2026

Let us be brutally clear: what transpired on the Thika Superhighway in the dead of night was an act of economic terrorism. KeNHA, backed by a massive contingent of security personnel, descended with heavy machinery around 1:20 AM, flattening everything in sight. This was not an operation; it was a raid. These are the same tactics deployed by despotic regimes, not a government supposedly serving its people. Hundreds of traders, whose only crime was trying to feed their families, woke up to find their entire lives reduced to rubble. Their businesses, some built over years of sweat and sacrifice, near the Shell petrol station, were obliterated in minutes. The visual evidence of bulldozers tearing down livelihoods is sickening, a testament to KeNHA’s absolute disdain for human suffering.

### The Sham of "Notices" and the Lie of Due Process

KeNHA claims it issued notices. What a laughable, insulting lie! While some reports mention notices dated February 23, 2026, and even January 9, 2026, for various sections of the Thika Superhighway, traders on the ground tell a different story. One trader, Alex Muturi, recounted the chilling moment they were told at "saa nane ya usiku" (2 AM) to remove their belongings with a mere 25-minute warning. Another lamented, "Nilikuwa nafanya biashara ya kuuza manguo, hawa watu wa KeNHA hawakuwa wametupatia notice, tulikuwa tunangoja wakuje watupatie notice ndip tuweze kujipanga". This isn't due process; it’s a strongman routine designed to intimidate and dispossess. A seven-day notice, even if genuinely received by all, offers no real solution for small-scale traders whose capital is tied up in their very structures and inventory. Where was the relocation plan? Where was the support? There was none. Only the cold, unfeeling steel of excavators.

### Roysambu Traders Lose Stalls Compensation: A Gross Injustice

The sheer audacity of KeNHA to execute such a destructive operation without any provisions for compensation is a moral outrage. These `roysambu traders lose stalls compensation`, losing millions of shillings in stock and structures. Families who relied on these small shops and kiosks for their daily bread are now staring into the abyss of poverty. This is not "development"; it is state-sponsored impoverishment. The authority’s glib justification of "road reserve encroachment" rings hollow when weighed against the immediate devastation wrought upon thousands. If the government truly cared about its citizens, a humane exit strategy, complete with fair compensation and viable alternative trading spaces, would have been paramount. Instead, we got brute force.
