Easy Coach, a prominent bus operator, announced revised bus fares effective Monday, April 20, 2026, across its key routes. The adjustments, communicated in a notice dated Saturday, April 18, 2026, directly respond to the recent fuel price increase by the Energy and Petroleum Regulatory Authority (EPRA).

Easy Coach Implements New Fare Structure

Revised Fares for Major Routes Passengers traveling from Nairobi to upcountry destinations such as Kisumu, Maseno, Mbale in Vihiga, Siaya, Butere, and the Ndori-Bondo-Usenge route will now pay Ksh1,900. This represents an increase of Ksh150 from previous fares for these routes. Similarly, fares for Ugunja in Siaya, Bumala, and Busia have increased to Ksh1,950 from Ksh1,800. For Western Kenya destinations, travelers heading to Kakamega, Mumias, Webuye, and Kimilili will be charged Ksh1,800. The fare for Kitale now stands at Ksh1,850. Routes within the Southern Nyanza region show varied adjustments. Passengers bound for Oyugis, Kisii, Kendubay, and Homabay will pay Ksh1,550. Destinations including Rodikopany, Ndhiwa, Sori, Mbita, Migori, Sirare, Kehancha, and Rongo will incur a fare of Ksh1,600. The Nairobi to Eldoret route has been revised to Ksh1,700, reflecting a Ksh100 increase.

Fuel Price Surge Prompts Adjustments The fare revisions directly follow the Energy and Petroleum Regulatory Authority's (EPRA) recent fuel price increment. EPRA raised the cost of super petrol by Ksh17 and diesel by Ksh30. However, other reports indicate a higher increase by EPRA, with super petrol rising by Ksh28.69 per litre and diesel by Ksh40.30 per litre for the period of April 15 to May 14, 2026. This increment impacts operational costs for transport companies significantly. These adjustments by Easy Coach align with a trend observed across the transport sector. Just three days prior, on April 15, 2026, Ena Coach also announced its own fare changes, attributing them to the sudden rise in fuel prices. Ena Coach set its Nairobi to upcountry fare via Narok at Ksh1,700, while the Nakuru route stands at Ksh1,800. The Nairobi-Mombasa route is now Ksh2,000, and travel between Kisii and Kisumu costs Ksh700. Fares between Mombasa and upcountry destinations, in either direction, have been adjusted to Ksh3,000, with these changes implemented immediately. The company stated on April 15, 2026, that an operational review preceded these necessary adjustments to maintain service quality.
