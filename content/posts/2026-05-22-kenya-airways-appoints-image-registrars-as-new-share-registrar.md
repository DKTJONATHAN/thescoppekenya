## Kenya Airways Appointments and the Shareholder Experience

If you hold shares in Kenya Airways, you might wonder why a change in the company's administrative service provider matters to your wallet. While backend shifts often seem invisible, they determine how easily you can vote, receive dividends, or track your investment during a historic year for the national carrier.

Effective June 1, 2026, Kenya Airways is transitioning its registry services to Image Registrars Limited, ending its long-standing contract with Custody and Registrars Services Limited. This move comes at a critical time for the airline as it prepares for its 50th Annual General Meeting on June 12, 2026. For the retail investor, this is more than just a vendor change. It is a logistical shift in how you interact with one of the most recognizable entities on the Nairobi Securities Exchange.

### How does this registry switch impact your voting power?

When a company changes its share registrar, the immediate concern for any shareholder is whether the transition will be seamless. The registrar acts as the bridge between you and the company. They maintain the official list of who owns what, manage the distribution of annual reports, and facilitate your participation in voting processes.

Image Registrars is known for its heavy investment in digital infrastructure. As the airline approaches its 50th AGM, which will be held virtually at 9:00 a.m., the efficiency of this new registrar will be tested immediately. If you have previously struggled with logging into online portals to cast your vote or had difficulty accessing your dividend statements, this transition aims to simplify those pain points. A smoother interface ensures that your voice as an investor is actually counted during the upcoming election of directors and the review of the financial audits ending December 31, 2025.

### Why does the 50th anniversary matter for your investment?

Reaching a 50th year is a significant milestone for any corporate entity. For Kenya Airways, this year serves as a moment of reflection and a push toward future stability. Long-term shareholders who have held the stock through various economic cycles rely on a responsive registry to feel connected to the company's trajectory.

When the administration of your investment is handled by a firm that specializes in virtual engagement, the barrier to participating in the airline's future decreases. You are no longer just a passive observer. Instead, you become an active participant in the governance of the firm. By moving these functions to a registrar that prioritizes digital accessibility, Kenya Airways is signaling that it wants its retail base to stay informed, engaged, and empowered during this landmark year. While we often see headlines about high-level politics, such as the recent debates involving Treasury CS John Mbadi, the quiet, technical work of managing shareholder data remains the foundation of a healthy stock market.

### Why it matters now

The registry switch is happening exactly when shareholders need to be most vigilant. With the AGM agenda featuring vital items like director elections and financial audits, any technical friction in the voting process could lead to voter apathy. If the transition causes a breakdown in communication, retail investors risk being sidelined. By centralizing operations under Image Registrars, the company is attempting to stabilize its administrative backbone to ensure the 50th AGM runs without significant hitches.

### What to watch next

Keep a close eye on your inbox and postal mail as June approaches. You will likely receive updated instructions on how to register for the virtual AGM platform.
