## EPRA Seals Illegal LPG Facility in Kilifi The Energy and Petroleum Regulatory Authority (EPRA) has sealed an illegal Liquefied Petroleum Gas (LPG) bulk storage facility in Mariakani, Kilifi County. The operation, executed on February 20, 2026, targeted the unlicensed site in Shangia due to significant safety violations and its operation within a densely populated area.

Regulatory Breach and Safety Risks EPRA’s Enforcement and Surveillance officers, working with a multi-agency team from the National Police Service and Kilifi County government, inspected and subsequently sealed off the facility. The site was found to have been constructed and operated without the necessary construction permits and operational licences mandated under petroleum sector regulations. This poses a substantial safety risk to the surrounding community.

Hazardous Location Identified The facility's location was a key concern for the energy regulator. It was situated within a busy commercial area, alarmingly close to schools, a mosque, a dispensary, and a major highway experiencing heavy traffic. Further compounding the danger, the site was near a community water point, directly opposite a power substation, and adjacent to a high-voltage electricity transmission line. EPRA warned that a fire or gas explosion in such an environment could lead to catastrophic consequences and mass casualties.

Owner Faces Legal Action Despite an official decommissioning notice being displayed at the facility’s entrance, the owner failed to surrender the premises to regulators. EPRA stated that the owner will face legal consequences for constructing an LPG bulk storage facility without a valid construction permit and for operating an unlicensed gas storage and handling facility, actions contrary to the Petroleum Act.
