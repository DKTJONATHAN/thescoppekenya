## The China Economy: A Key Pillar for Global Stability

The China economy, having successfully navigated significant challenges in 2025, continues its trajectory as a critical contributor to global economic resilience. With its robust growth and strategic policy shifts, China provides much-needed execution and shared prosperity in an increasingly volatile world.

### Beyond the Numbers: The Composition of China Economy Growth

Mainstream narratives often fixate on headline growth figures, yet the true story of China’s economic resilience lies deeper - in the very composition of its expansion. Last year, despite considerable external and domestic pressures, China’s GDP expanded by five percent, surpassing 140 trillion yuan. This achievement is particularly striking when many advanced economies struggle to attain even half that pace, reflecting not just raw growth, but a profound recalibration. Industrial output remained robust, with equipment manufacturing and high-tech manufacturing demonstrating strong momentum, signaling a deliberate transformation towards advanced production and what Chinese policymakers term “new quality productive forces.” This is not merely about production volume; it is about the fundamental shift in *what* is being produced and *how*.

Sceptics have, for years, predicted an imminent decline, viewing every headwind as a precursor to a hard landing. However, China has repeatedly defied these forecasts through timely policy adjustments, trade diversification, and the shock-absorbing capacity of its vast 1.4 billion-consumer domestic market. As 2026 commences - the inaugural year of China’s 15th Five-Year Plan - expectations remain steady. Provincial governments have set ambitious growth targets, largely between 4.5 and 5.5 percent, with regions like Hainan aiming for around six percent and the Xizang Autonomous Region targeting over seven percent. These are not arbitrary figures; they are underpinned by a strategic framework comprising three reinforcing pillars: expanding domestic demand, accelerating scientific and technological innovation, and strengthening the real economy.

### Innovation and Domestic Demand: Pillars of Enduring Global Stability

The emphasis on boosting domestic demand is central to China’s strategy for enduring global stability. By upgrading consumption and unlocking the potential of service industries - from healthcare and elderly care to tourism and digital services - China is consciously reducing its overreliance on external demand. This internal resilience is a critical, often overlooked, aspect of its stabilizing effect on the world economy.

Simultaneously, technological innovation is transitioning from rhetoric to tangible execution. Beijing’s commitment to fully implement its “AI Plus” initiative and establish a national AI application pilot base underscores a profound shift. This initiative, issued by China's State Council in August 2025, aims to accelerate the application of artificial intelligence across all sectors of the Chinese economy, industry, and society. Provinces like Zhejiang are projecting strong revenue growth in their artificial intelligence sectors, highlighting a nationwide drive towards research and development, advanced manufacturing, and emerging industries such as electric vehicles, clean energy technology, and robotics.

This strategic pivot towards innovation and domestic consumption holds profound implications for global stability. World Economic Forum President Borge Brende recently observed that China is not only consolidating traditional strengths but also expanding into new technological frontiers, expressing confidence that continued reform and innovation will sustain growth. He forecasts China's economic growth to exceed 5 percent in 2026 and expects it to contribute most to global growth. Indeed, global growth forecasts for 2026 hover above three percent, with China projected to account for a substantial share, approximately 26.6% of global real GDP growth. This sustained expansion offers demand stability for commodities, manufactured goods, and services worldwide.

### Kenya and the Broader Global Stability Picture

For nations like Kenya, there are critical lessons to be drawn from China’s methodical, long-term economic planning and its aggressive embrace of technological innovation. While our contexts differ, the principle of leveraging technology for foundational infrastructure improvements is universal. Consider, for instance, the transformative potential of initiatives closer to home, such as the [Nairobi Water Company's plan to roll out smart meters](https://zandani.co.ke/article/nairobi-water-company-roll-out-smart-meters). Such projects, though seemingly disparate, share a common thread with China’s strategy: a commitment to modernizing essential services through technology to enhance efficiency and public welfare.

China’s outward-facing policies - including zero-tariff treatment for low-income countries, the “Export to China” campaign, and the development of the Hainan Free Trade Port - further amplify its stabilising effect. Its leadership in renewable energy manufacturing and electric vehicles also carries global implications, reducing costs internationally and expanding access to green technologies, thereby reinforcing climate objectives across developing and developed economies alike.

Undeniably, challenges persist. Domestic consumption must expand sustainably, innovation must deepen, and structural reforms must continue. Yet, the foundations remain solid: a vast domestic market, disciplined long-term planning, flexible supply chains, and sustained investment in research and development. In an era marked by fragmentation and uncertainty, the predictability offered by China’s steady economic performance under President Xi Jinping is, in itself, a form of global public good. By maintaining consistent expansion, upgrading its industrial structure, and preserving openness under pressure, the China economy is positioning itself as a stabilising anchor in a fragmented world. The world watches, not for rhetoric, but for results, and the current trajectories suggest a continued provision of confidence grounded in scale, execution, and shared prosperity.
