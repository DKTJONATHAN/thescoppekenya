## Kirinyaga Church Robbery: Three Sanctuaries Lose Ksh1 Million

Members of three churches in Kirinyaga County are counting losses after a coordinated Kirinyaga church robbery on the night of Saturday, May 2, when unknown intruders stole properties worth over KSh 1 million and desecrated sacred items. St. Joseph Catholic Church, an Anglican church (ACK) in Kiamangunyi, and Fountain of Life Church were all affected by the overnight invasions.

### Details of the Robbery

Intruders gained entry by forcing open windows before accessing various rooms within the church premises. Musical instruments, including microphones, a keyboard, and a sound receiver, were among the items stolen, with one church reporting losses exceeding KSh 500,000 in instruments alone. In addition to material goods, the attackers reportedly drank the Holy Sacrament and made away with cash found inside the churches. Evidence suggests the criminals moved freely within the compounds, heightening fears of escalating insecurity in the region.

### Church Leaders Call for Action

In response to the brazen attacks, church leaders are urgently appealing for enhanced security patrols and improved surveillance to deter future incidents. The widespread nature of the Kirinyaga church robbery has prompted concerns among congregants about the safety of places of worship and their assets. The call for security measures aligns with broader public demands for order and safety across various sectors.

### Pattern of Church Attacks

This incident is not isolated, as churches across the country have recently become targets for thieves, often due to perceived low resistance during criminal operations. Notable past incidents include an August 2025 theft at Nyaguku Catholic Church in Nyamira County, where equipment and instruments were stolen. Another case in July 2019 saw a thief in Nyahururu pen an emotional letter to a Pefa church following a botched robbery, revealing information about money left in the premises.
