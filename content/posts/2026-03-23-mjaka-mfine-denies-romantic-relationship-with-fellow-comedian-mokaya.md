Content creator Mjaka Mfine has emphatically denied a romantic relationship with comedian Mokaya. In a March 23, 2026 interview, she clarified their undeniable on-screen chemistry stems purely from professional collaborations and creative skits, urging fans to put an end to persistent dating rumors.

## Mjaka Mfine Sets the Record Straight on Mokaya Romance

The digital space has been buzzing with speculation, but content creator Mjaka Mfine, known formally as Mitchelle Joyce Akoth Oruko, has officially pressed the brakes on the dating narrative involving fellow comedian Mokaya. Despite their undeniable on-screen spark, Mjaka made it abundantly clear in a recent interview that their bond is meticulously built on scripts and skits, not soulmate aspirations.

### Chemistry Beyond the Camera

It is easy to see why the "shipping" started among their followers. The dynamic duo has mastered the art of viral engagement through frequent appearances in matching outfits and seamless comedic timing that keeps audiences across TikTok, Instagram, and Facebook thoroughly hooked. Furthermore, occasional dance videos have led many fans to interpret their captivating chemistry as something more profound than mere friendship. Mjaka, however, remains firm that the captivating dynamic seen online remains purely part of their professional act.

### Career Over Couples

When confronted with the persistent rumors fueled by their frequent collaborations, Mjaka's response was direct and refreshingly unsentimental. She emphasized that her eyes are firmly on the prize, and that prize is decidedly not a romantic relationship. She stated unequivocally that they are just working and urged fans to drop the story. Even when asked if she would ever consider a romantic shift, she remained steadfast, noting that her focus is entirely on her work and her financial growth. This stance echoes sentiments about prioritizing personal ambition, a topic explored elsewhere, like when [Timaya shared his views on relationships](https://zandani.co.ke/article/timaya-says-marriage-doesn-t-work). Mjaka firmly believes that anything distracting her from her career-including romantic speculation-is the true opposition, noting, "Enemy ni mmoja." This strategic outlook ensures their partnership remains a powerhouse of Kenyan digital entertainment rather than dissolving into tabloid romance.
