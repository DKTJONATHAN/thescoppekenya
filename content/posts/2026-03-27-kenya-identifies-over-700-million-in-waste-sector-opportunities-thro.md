## Kenya's Circular Economy: A $700 Million Investment Frontier The Kenya Investment Authority (Invest Kenya) has unveiled a new investment prospectus at the Kenya International Investment Conference (KIICO 2026), highlighting over $700 million in economic opportunities within the waste management and circular economy sectors. This initiative aims to transform discarded materials into a significant economic driver, boosting GDP and creating jobs across the nation.

Unlocking Economic Value from Waste Kenya currently generates approximately 22,000 tonnes of waste daily, yet only about 4% undergoes recycling. The recently launched prospectus, titled "Waste Management and Circular Economy: Investment Pathways and Opportunities in Kenya," reframes this challenge as a substantial economic opportunity. It identifies that converting this waste into value across five key sectors could unlock over $700 million in economic value by 2030. This transformation is projected to contribute approximately 0.5% to Kenya’s Gross Domestic Product (GDP) within the same timeframe, while also supporting job creation and strengthening domestic supply chains. Kenya already possesses a growing ecosystem of over 120 circular economy businesses operating across various value chains.

Policy Momentum and Collaborative Frameworks The launch of the prospectus coincides with growing momentum for the circular economy, bolstered by recent policy advancements. These include the Sustainable Waste Management Act and the introduction of Extended Producer Responsibility (EPR) frameworks, which are creating a more enabling environment for investment in the sector. The prospectus was developed through a collaborative effort, commissioned by Invest Kenya and undertaken with global system change advisory firm Systemiq, law firm ALN Kenya, and the tech-enabled social enterprise TakaTaka Ni Mali. This multi-stakeholder partnership aims to provide clearer information and visibility, addressing previous constraints on investment in the sector.

Clarifying Investment Pathways The core objective of the prospectus is to clarify investment opportunities across circular economy value chains, encompassing sectors such as packaging, electronics, and textiles. It spotlights emerging companies and pipeline projects, alongside identifying the crucial enabling conditions needed to unlock further private capital and support investor engagement. John Mwendwa, CEO of Invest Kenya, emphasized that circular economy solutions can strengthen manufacturing value chains, create jobs, and attract new investment, positioning Kenya as a regional hub for circular materials and recycling. Ben Dixon, Partner and Head of Materials and Circular Economy at Systemiq, noted Kenya's strong policy momentum, existing business ecosystem, and significant investor interest as key ingredients for a thriving circular economy. Rosa Nduati Mutero from ALN Kenya highlighted the importance of a strong enabling environment, acknowledging Kenya's progress in policy reforms that support waste recovery and circular business models. Mary Ngechu of TakaTaka Ni Mali stressed the need for greater coordination and sustained investment to scale existing solutions within Kenya's vibrant entrepreneurial ecosystem.
