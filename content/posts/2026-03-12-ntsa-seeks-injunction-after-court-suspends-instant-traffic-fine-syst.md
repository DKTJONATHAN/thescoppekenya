## NTSA Seeks Injunction Over Suspended Instant Fines

The National Transport and Safety Authority announced it will seek a temporary injunction after the High Court suspended its instant traffic fine system. Director General Nashon Kondiwa stated on Thursday, March 12, that this action aims to enable continued enforcement of traffic regulations, pending a final court determination next month.

### Court Halts Instant Traffic Penalties

The High Court, through Justice Bahati Mwamuye, issued a conservatory order effectively barring the National Transport and Safety Authority (NTSA) from implementing its recently introduced instant traffic fine system. This ruling follows a petition filed by Lawyer Shadrack Wambui, who challenged the directive. The court has scheduled the matter for mention on April 9 this year for the hearing and determination of the petition. ### NTSA's Enforcement Plans and Justification

Speaking during a press briefing on Thursday, March 12, NTSA's Director General Nashon Kondiwa affirmed the authority's commitment to enhancing road safety. Kondiwa stated that NTSA will seek an injunction order to maintain the status quo, which, if granted, would allow the authority to proceed with enforcing the instant fines until the court fully hears and determines the case next month. This temporary order is intended to prevent further disruption while the legal process unfolds.

### Instant Fines System Overview

The NTSA rolled out the instant fines traffic management system on Monday, March 9. The system was designed to notify motorists instantly via SMS about traffic violations committed. Key violations intended for capture by this system include speeding, lane violations, driving on pavements, and not wearing seatbelts. The proposed fines for these offenses were set to range from Ksh500 to Ksh10,000, depending on the severity of the infraction.

### Tackling Insurance Fraud

Beyond the instant fines system, Director General Nashon Kondiwa also announced that the NTSA will initiate a crackdown targeting fraudulent insurance companies operating across the country. Kondiwa highlighted the significant prevalence of fraud within the local insurance industry, noting that this issue often results in motorists paying elevated premiums while receiving minimal or no tangible benefits from their policies. The authority aims to address this systemic problem to ensure fair practices and protect motorists' interests.
