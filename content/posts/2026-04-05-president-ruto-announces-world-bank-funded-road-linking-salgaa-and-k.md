President William Ruto today announced plans for a World Bank-funded Class B road connecting Salgaa in Nakuru County to Kilgoris in Narok County, a project he affirmed would finally materialize after decades of unfulfilled promises, during a church service in Kilgoris.

World Bank Backs Key Infrastructure Project President Ruto revealed the upcoming construction of a 140 to 160-kilometre Class B road, an inter-county connector spanning Narok and Nakuru counties. The route will encompass Marua, Enkaka, Enosail, Kilgoris, and Cheptuka in Narok County, extending to Salgaa in Nakuru County. As a Class B road, it is designated to link important centres, including county headquarters and major trading hubs, integrating into the national road network. The Head of State confirmed engagement with the World Bank to secure the necessary funding for this extensive project.

Presidential Commitment to Development The President acknowledged that previous administrations had promised this specific corridor without delivering. He personally committed to overseeing its construction, emphasizing the new government's resolve to fulfill long-standing infrastructure pledges. While specific timelines for the Kilgoris-Salgaa road were not disclosed, sources within the Narok County Government anticipate construction could commence in July this year, originating from Impopong. This initiative aligns with the President's broader pledge to tarmac 28,000 kilometres of road during his tenure.

Funding and National Infrastructure Strategy The announcement of World Bank backing for the Salgaa-Kilgoris road follows earlier reports in March 2026 regarding fresh negotiations with the global monetary authority for additional funding towards the 750-kilometre Isiolo-Mandera highway. It remains unconfirmed whether the new Kilgoris road falls under this larger funding umbrella. The government established the Ksh5 trillion National Infrastructure Fund (NIF) to reduce reliance on external borrowing for projects. However, the continued pursuit of World Bank loans for key developments like this road, and programmes such as the Boma Yangu initiative which integrates housing and market infrastructure for small traders, raises questions regarding the current application of the NIF's strategic objectives. Other significant ongoing infrastructure projects include the 175-kilometer Mau-Rironi Summit dual-carriageway and improvements to the Nithi Bridge in Tharaka Nithi County.
