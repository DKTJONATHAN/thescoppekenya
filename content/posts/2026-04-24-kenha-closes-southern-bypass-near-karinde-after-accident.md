The Kenya National Highways Authority has temporarily closed a section of the Nairobi Southern Bypass for Ole Sereni-bound vehicles following a fatal morning accident. The closure affects the stretch between Thogoto and Dagoretti interchanges near the Karinde area today.

## Traffic Disruption and Alternative Routes

### Collision Details and Casualties
The disruption was caused by a collision involving two long-distance trucks along the Gitaru stretch. Preliminary accounts suggest a lorry that had stalled by the roadside was struck from behind by another truck. Early indications point to brake failure as the primary cause of the accident.

One person died on the spot while another sustained serious injuries during the crash. The deceased was a mechanic who was reportedly working on the stalled lorry at the time of the impact. Emergency teams are on-site to assist the survivor and clear the wreckage from the busy highway.

### Travel Advisory and Diversions
Motorists have been warned to brace for significant traffic delays along the affected section. KeNHA has advised drivers to consider using the Kikuyu-Mutuini-Dagoretti (C63 Road) as an alternative route to bypass the congestion. Images and videos obtained by Za Ndani show major traffic snarl-ups affecting both outbound and incoming lanes. The road agency is working with a multiagency team of emergency responders to ensure normalcy returns as quickly as possible.
