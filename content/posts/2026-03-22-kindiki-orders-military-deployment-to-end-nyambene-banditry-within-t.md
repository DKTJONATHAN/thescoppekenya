Celestine Nzioka, Za Ndani Deputy President Kithure Kindiki today ordered a comprehensive military deployment to end banditry in Nyambene, Meru County, within two weeks. Speaking during a tour, Kindiki vowed to utilize all government security resources, including the Kenya Defence Forces, police, and specialized equipment, to restore order in the bandit-prone region.

Intensified Crackdown on Banditry

Strategic Deployment and Operational Shift The government will deploy all instruments of war, including military personnel, police officers, tanks, and aircraft, to confront criminal gangs in Nyambene. President William Ruto has directed both the police and the Kenya Defence Forces to suspend other assignments and prioritize combating these groups. This intensified focus on security comes as the Kenya Meteorological Department recently warned of heavy rainfall across several regions, a factor that could impact ground operations. Interior Cabinet Secretary Kipchumba Murkomen is scheduled to visit the area soon to assess the situation and deploy the necessary workforce. Deputy President Kindiki himself pledged to visit Nyambene this week to personally oversee the operation's progress.

Recent Attacks and Intelligence Gathering The directive follows recent bandit attacks in Makulubaine and Kaongo on Thursday, March 19, 2026, which resulted in two fatalities and the theft of 150 livestock. In response, the National Police Service (NPS) on Saturday, March 21, 2026, urged residents of Meru County, alongside those in Isiolo, Samburu, and Laikipia, to provide any intelligence regarding banditry and related criminal activities. An elaborate multi-agency security operation has been established to recover stolen cattle and confiscate illegal firearms and ammunition.

Affected Areas Identified NPS has identified specific areas most impacted by the attacks. In Meru County, these include Buuri East, Tigania East and West, and Igembe North, Central, and Mutuati. Other targeted regions encompass Mukogondo Forest in Laikipia, Lantana and Sereolipi in Samburu East, and Kipsing and Ngaremara in Isiolo.
