Award-winning Nigerian singer Asake's mother recently revealed her son’s surprising choice to study at Obafemi Awolowo University (OAU) in Ile-Ife, Osun State, over a planned education in the United Kingdom. This personal account, shared on a YouTube podcast, offers new insight into the star’s foundational journey and unwavering determination.

Asake's OAU Journey

Mother's UK Plans and Son's Determination Asake’s mother, whose name the artist adopted for his stage persona, detailed her efforts to send him to the United Kingdom. She had gathered funds and secured an international passport for him, expecting him to pursue higher education abroad. However, Asake, born Ahmed Ololade, expressed a strong desire to attend university in Nigeria, insisting on Obafemi Awolowo University instead.

Muyideen Oladapo's Key Role Initially resistant to her son's change of plans, Asake’s mother was eventually swayed by Yoruba movie actor Muyideen Oladapo, also known as Lala. Oladapo, a student at OAU at the time, facilitated Asake’s admission to the institution and assured her he could help guide the young and determined student during his studies.

Shaping an Artist: OAU's Influence Asake ultimately enrolled in the Performing Arts program at OAU, a decision he credits with significantly shaping his unique artistry. He has often stated that while his talent was a natural gift, his formal education at the university greatly enhanced and refined his creative abilities. This academic foundation has been crucial to his rise as a global music sensation.

A Return to Roots: December 2025 Visit The singer’s deep connection to his alma mater was evident in December 2025 when he returned to the Ile-Ife campus. He was warmly received by Vice-Chancellor Professor Simeon Bamire and senior officials, including Dr Oluwatoyin Ogundeji of the Theatre Arts department, where his academic journey began. Asake performed for a large student audience at the OAU Amphitheatre, drumming and offering a traditional Yoruba prostration, met with enthusiastic cheers. His mother accompanied him on this significant visit. Asake's success as one of Nigeria's biggest music exports continues to highlight the vibrant talent emerging from the continent, a dynamic often reflected in public discussions about prominent figures in the music industry, such as Delta State Governor Sheriff Oborevwori's preference for Davido over Wizkid.
