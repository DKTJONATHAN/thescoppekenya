The government is routing a KSh 1.46 billion water pipeline through Karura Forest to serve Nairobi Eastlands. Implemented by the Athi Water Works Development Agency, this project began its forest section in March 2026, aiming for overall completion by mid-2027, citing cost savings and efficiency.

Gigiri-Karura Pipeline to Boost Eastlands Water Supply

Project Details and Funding The Gigiri-Karura-Outer Ring Road Transmission Pipeline Project, valued at KSh 1.46 billion, is being implemented by the Athi Water Works Development Agency (AWWDA) to alleviate persistent water shortages in Nairobi Eastlands. Once operational, the pipeline is projected to supply an additional 211 million litres of water daily to residents along the Outer Ring Road corridor, including Ruaraka, Mathare, Embakasi North, and Embakasi West constituencies. The project is financed by the French Development Agency (AFD). Joseph Kamau, Chief Executive Officer of AWWDA, stated that the initiative aims to address Nairobi's growing population and escalating water demand, which is presently estimated at 370 million litres per day. The project is expected to sustainably serve a population of approximately three million people by 2045.

Strategic Forest Routing A section of the 8.4-kilometre pipeline has been intentionally routed underground through Karura Forest. Kamau clarified that this decision was made to significantly reduce construction costs and avoid expensive design modifications. He explained that detouring outside the forest would necessitate extensive pumping and extend the pipeline's length by about three kilometres, incurring substantial additional costs. The pipeline's route includes a 4.4-kilometre stretch from Gigiri Reservoir to Karura Reservoir, with another 4 kilometres extending towards Outer Ring Road. Works for the 3-kilometre portion within Karura Forest commenced in March 2026 and are scheduled to conclude by July 2026, proceeding along the Wangari Maathai Cycling Track corridor. The government's commitment to strategic infrastructure projects such as this underscores broader efforts in national development.

Environmental Safeguards and Timeline The Kenya Forest Service (KFS) is overseeing the project within Karura Forest, mandating contractors to align the pipeline along existing pathways to prevent tree felling. Authorities have also committed to restoring any disturbed nature trails once construction concludes. Project completion for the entire pipeline is anticipated by mid-2027.
