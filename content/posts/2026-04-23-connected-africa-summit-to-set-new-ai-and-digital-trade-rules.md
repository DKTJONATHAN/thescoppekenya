## Connected Africa Summit to Forge Africa's Digital Economy Rules

The 15th Annual Connected Africa Summit convenes in Nairobi from April 27 to April 30, bringing together African ministers, private sector, and development partners. The summit aims to establish the continent's own regulatory frameworks for artificial intelligence, cross-border data, and digital trade, asserting Africa's agency in shaping its digital future rather than inheriting external rules.

### Continental Digital Governance Imperative

ICT Cabinet Secretary William Kabogo emphasized the critical need for Africa to define its digital governance. He stated the continent must author the rules governing its digital economy for the coming decade, particularly in artificial intelligence, cross-border data, and digital trade. This year's theme, "Uniting Africa’s Innovation for an Inclusive Digital Market," underscores the ambition to negotiate these frameworks as a unified continent, avoiding fragmented, country-by-country approaches.

### Key Deliverables for Digital Growth

The Ministry has outlined three concrete deliverables for the four-day summit. An AfCFTA Digital Trade Implementation Sprint List will identify eight to twelve measures to alleviate friction in cross-border digital trade. Concurrently, an African Data Space Action Note will establish a working framework for data interoperability, aligning with the African Union Data Policy Framework. Furthermore, the summit seeks to develop a pipeline of bankable projects in connectivity, Digital Public Infrastructure, cybersecurity, and digital skills, each with clear leadership and timelines.

### Strategic Partnerships and Participation

Ministerial delegations from Ethiopia, Malawi, Uganda, Gabon, Guinea, Chad, Zimbabwe, and other African states are confirmed to attend. Her Excellency Deemah AlYahya, Secretary-General of the Digital Cooperation Organization, will also participate. Safaricom is a pivotal partner, committing KES 22 million, including providing internet connectivity at the venue, demonstrating an "ecosystem partner" approach. The summit boasts 47 confirmed sponsors, including industry giants Huawei and Hewlett Packard Enterprise Company (HPE).

### Addressing the Digital Divide

Despite the summit's ambitious agenda, CS Kabogo acknowledged the significant gap between aspirations and current realities. Less than a third of Africa's population currently uses the internet, and broadband access remains expensive relative to household incomes across the continent. He highlighted that artificial intelligence rules are too often crafted without African representation, underscoring the urgency for the continent to actively participate in these crucial global conversations. The ICT Authority, organizer of the event, plans to establish a permanent secretariat to ensure continuity for future summits.
