## Court of Appeal Suspends Ruling on Presidential Advisors The Court of Appeal today suspended a High Court ruling that declared President William Ruto's 21-member Council of Advisors unconstitutional. This decision allows the advisors to continue their service and receive entitlements, averting disruption to the Office of the President while the Attorney General's appeal is heard.

Appellate Court's Rationale A three-judge bench of the Court of Appeal ruled that dismissing the President's advisors at this stage could disrupt the operations of his office. The ruling effectively set aside the High Court's earlier decision, which had declared President Ruto's appointments unconstitutional, pending the full hearing and determination of the appeal filed by the Attorney General. The Attorney General had sought a stay on Justice Bahati Mwamuye's judgment, arguing that the advisors were already "rendering services to the public" by the time the judgment was delivered. The appellate judges, Justice Korir Weldon, Lady Justice Hedwig Ong’udi, and Justice Samson Okong’o, also stated that the argument regarding duplication of roles, raised in the original case, should be addressed during the substantive appeal. They concluded that the applicant had met the necessary threshold for the orders sought.

High Court's Initial Decision and Injunction Previously, on January 22, Justice Mwamuye had issued an injunction preventing the government from processing any salaries or benefits for the appointed advisors. This injunction was a result of a case filed by the Katiba Institute. With the Court of Appeal’s latest decision today, this injunction is no longer in effect, allowing the Council of Advisors to continue serving and receiving their entitlements. However, it remains unclear whether the salaries for the period when the injunction was active will be backdated.

Lawyers Express Concern Over Delivery Following today's ruling, lawyers involved in the case voiced concerns regarding the manner of its delivery. Advocate Joshua Malidzo, representing the Katiba Institute, stated that his team had not been notified in advance of the ruling. Malidzo indicated that they had previously been informed the ruling would be delivered on April 24, 2026, expressing surprise at today's delivery without prior notice or receipt of the ruling itself.

Continuity of Advisory Roles Despite the procedural concerns raised, the Court of Appeal's decision ensures that key advisors, including economist David Ndii, Makau Mutua, Joe Ager, Jaoko Oburu, Kennedy Ogeto, and Joseph Boinnet, among others, will remain in office. These individuals are members of the President’s Council of Economic Advisers and other advisory bodies. The three-judge bench also ruled that the intended appeal raises matters of significant public interest and should therefore be heard on a priority basis.
