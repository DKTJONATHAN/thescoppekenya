## Samsung's "Awesome Intelligence" Lands in Kenya Samsung has launched its Galaxy A57 5G and A37 5G smartphones in Kenya, introducing advanced "Awesome Intelligence" AI features alongside significant hardware and software upgrades. These mid-range devices offer enhanced capabilities for transcription, content extraction, and improved photography, catering to the evolving digital needs of Kenyan consumers with practical, integrated technology solutions.

AI-Powered Productivity Tools Both new Galaxy A series phones run on One UI 8.5, integrating a suite of AI features dubbed "Awesome Intelligence." The Voice Recorder application now provides automatic transcription and translation of recordings, a valuable tool for professionals or students managing meetings and lectures. AISelect allows users to long-press the Edge Panel to extract text or images directly from the screen, streamlining the transfer of information into notes or photo editors. Furthermore, Google's Circle to Search feature has been enhanced to process multiple objects simultaneously, enabling users to circle and search an entire outfit for individual components in a single action.

Next-Generation Imaging for Kenyan Users The camera systems on both devices feature a triple-camera setup, anchored by a 50MP main sensor. Samsung has prioritized improvements in Nightography, its low-light processing technology, on both the A57 5G and A37 5G. The A57 5G receives additional photographic enhancements, including faster shutter speed, superior noise reduction, and a "Best Face" feature. This "Best Face" functionality is optimized for group photos and burst shots, ensuring clearer results where subjects might blink.

Performance and Durability Innovations The Galaxy A57 5G stands out with a Super AMOLED+ display featuring slimmer bezels and is powered by a 5,000 mAh battery, designed to offer up to two days of usage. It supports Super Fast Charging 2.0, capable of recharging the phone to 60% in approximately 30 minutes. Processor upgrades have been implemented across the CPU, GPU, and NPU in both models, contributing to enhanced performance. A 13% larger vapor chamber in the A57 5G improves thermal management, allowing the phone to handle sustained tasks like gaming or extended recording sessions without rapid throttling. Both the A57 5G and A37 5G carry an IP68 rating, signifying resistance to water submersion up to 1.5 meters for 30 minutes.

Enhanced Security and Longevity Commitments Security is reinforced with Knox Vault, providing a dedicated hardware layer for protection. A new Private Album feature within the Gallery app offers users a secure space to lock away sensitive photos and videos. The Privacy Alerts system proactively identifies and flags suspicious access to location data or other sensitive information, empowering users with immediate awareness. Samsung has also made a significant commitment to longevity, promising six generations of Android OS and One UI upgrades, coupled with six years of security updates. This extended support period is a notable offering for mid-range smartphone users in the Kenyan market, reflecting a strong value proposition for device endurance.

Market Positioning and Accessibility The Samsung Galaxy A57 5G is available in Kenya at KES 68,300 for the 8GB + 256GB configuration and KES 60,900 for the 8GB + 128GB version. The Galaxy A37 5G is priced at KES 60,200 for the 8GB + 256GB model and KES 49,000 for the 6GB + 128GB variant. Both devices are currently accessible nationwide at Samsung Experience Stores and authorized dealers, providing Kenyan consumers with readily available options for these latest mid-range innovations.
