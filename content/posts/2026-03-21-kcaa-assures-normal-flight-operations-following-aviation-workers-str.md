The Kenya Civil Aviation Authority (KCAA) has reassured the public of uninterrupted flight operations at Jomo Kenyatta International Airport and other facilities today, Saturday, March 21, 2026. This follows a strike threat from the Kenya Aviation Workers Union (KAWU) over unresolved labor disputes, which KCAA states are being addressed through ongoing negotiations.

KCAA Assures Normalcy Amidst Strike Threats The KCAA confirmed that discussions involving its board, senior management, and a conciliation process led by the Ministry of Labour are actively progressing, having already achieved significant strides. The authority emphasized that measures are in place to uphold safety, security, and service delivery across the nation's aviation services, guaranteeing no disruptions.

Union's Renewed Grievances The KCAA's reassurance comes in the wake of a fresh strike threat issued by KAWU on Wednesday, March 18, 2026. This occurred barely a month after the union had suspended a previous industrial action. KAWU Secretary General Moses Ndiema warned of potential aviation service suspensions within a week, accusing KCAA of violating a previously agreed-upon return-to-work formula and engaging in alleged victimization of workers. The union urged passengers to make early travel arrangements, stating it would not be held accountable for any inconvenience resulting from the proposed industrial action. This latest impasse mirrors a similar dispute in February 2026, which saw the Employment and Labour Relations Court intervene to halt a looming strike at JKIA.

Industry Raises Safety Concerns Industry stakeholders, including the Kenya Airline Pilots Association, have voiced concerns that repeated strike threats could have serious repercussions. These consequences include flight delays, cancellations, and operational uncertainty. Pilots have also cautioned that such disruptions could lead to increased fatigue among flight crews due to extended duty hours and unpredictable schedules, thereby raising significant safety questions. Wilson Airport Incident Under Investigation In a separate but related development within the aviation sector, KCAA has also confirmed it is investigating an aircraft incident that occurred Friday night, March 20, 2026, at Wilson Airport. A plane reportedly made a hard landing and subsequently veered off the runway. While no injuries were reported, the incident has prompted renewed inquiries and concerns regarding aviation safety standards.
