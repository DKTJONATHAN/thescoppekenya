## KMTC Advances Talks for Graduates' Healthcare Employment in Canada The Kenya Medical Training College (KMTC) has commenced discussions with Canadian officials to establish job opportunities for its graduates through a Health Care Assistant program. Following a March 10, 2026, delegation visit, talks are aligning training standards and recruitment, preparing professionals for Canada's healthcare system.

Exploring International Job Pathways KMTC hosted a Canadian delegation on March 10, 2026, marking the second engagement between both parties, coordinated through the Ministry of Labour and Social Protection. These discussions aim to create job opportunities for KMTC graduates abroad, addressing the growing demand for trained caregivers in Canada. Lucy Chebungei, KMTC Director of Finance, Planning and Administration, representing the college's Chief Executive, welcomed the delegation. She affirmed KMTC’s commitment to forging international collaborations for the benefit of its graduates. Ms. Chebungei highlighted KMTC’s role as a critical contributor to the healthcare workforce, locally and globally, by producing competent professionals who serve diverse health systems worldwide.

Aligning Training Standards The Canadian delegation, led by Lara Dyer, travelled to assess KMTC’s Health Care Assistant curriculum. Their mission is to determine if the curriculum meets the professional standards required for healthcare support workers entering Canada’s health system. The team thoroughly reviewed course content, training structure, and practical skills. This evaluation aims to ensure KMTC graduates can integrate smoothly into Canadian care facilities without requiring significant additional training after relocation. Dyer emphasized that the objective is to ensure graduates transitioning to Canada are well prepared and able to integrate seamlessly into the healthcare system. KMTC believes that aligning training with international standards will enable its graduates to transition more easily into overseas jobs, while upholding the quality of care expected in foreign health systems.

Preparing Graduates for Global Demand The discussions occur shortly after more than 22,000 KMTC students graduated across the country, now preparing to join the healthcare workforce. Skilled personnel remain in high demand both locally and internationally. KMTC believes these international partnerships could forge new job pathways for trained health workers. This initiative simultaneously strengthens Kenya’s contribution to the global healthcare workforce, particularly as many countries face critical staff shortages. To support this pathway, KMTC is rolling out a one-year Health Care Assistant course. This program is designed to prepare trainees to provide hands-on support to patients and clients across various care settings, including hospitals, homes, and other healthcare institutions, thus enabling adaptability to diverse environments.
