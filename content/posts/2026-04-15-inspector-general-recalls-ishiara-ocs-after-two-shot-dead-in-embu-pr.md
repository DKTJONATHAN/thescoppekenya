The National Police Service has today, April 15, 2026, recalled the Officer Commanding Station (OCS) in Ishiara, Embu County, following deadly clashes between police and protesters. The confrontation on Tuesday led to two fatalities and several injuries, prompting investigations into the incident.

Ishiara OCS Recalled Amidst Probe

Inspector General Directs Recalls and Redeployments The Inspector General of the National Police Service (IG-NPS) has directed the immediate recall of the Ishiara OCS. This action paves the way for thorough investigations into the fatal shootings. Additionally, officers who had served extended periods at the station have been redeployed to other areas. This measure aims to facilitate fair, transparent, and impartial investigations into the incident, as stated by the NPS.

Protests Erupt Over Hospital Services The deadly clashes on Tuesday, April 14, 2026, occurred during demonstrations in Ishiara, Mbeere North. Residents took to the streets to protest the poor state of services at Ishiara Level 4 Hospital, citing critical shortages of drugs, inadequate staffing, and deteriorating infrastructure. The initially peaceful demonstrations escalated when police officers attempted to disperse the crowd, leading to a confrontation that witnesses allege involved the use of live bullets, resulting in two protester deaths and multiple injuries.

Investigations Underway Following the incident, angry residents reportedly transported the bodies of the victims and deposited them outside the Ishiara Police Station as tensions mounted. The NPS had issued a statement earlier today suggesting that officers were compelled to use force after protesters allegedly threatened their safety with crude weapons. Detectives from the Directorate of Criminal Investigations (DCI) headquarters have been dispatched to conduct thorough investigations. Concurrently, the Independent Policing Oversight Authority (IPOA) has launched its own independent inquiry into the matter. Authorities have urged Mbeere North residents to remain calm, assuring the public that accountability for the incident will be exercised and that the Service will offer maximum cooperation to the independent oversight body.
