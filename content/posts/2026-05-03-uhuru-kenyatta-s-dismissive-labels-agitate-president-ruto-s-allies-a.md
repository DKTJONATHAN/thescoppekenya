President William Ruto's allies have intensified threats regarding former President Uhuru Kenyatta's retirement benefits, following Kenyatta's continued political activity. This renewed friction, evident in public statements and warnings this week, marks another escalation in their ongoing political rivalry.

## Uhuru Kenyatta’s Dismissive Labels Agitate President Ruto’s Allies as Retirement Benefit Threats Escalate

The political theatre in Kenya is currently dominated by the persistent sparring between the incumbent and his predecessor. Every time former President Uhuru Kenyatta graces a public event, a familiar pattern emerges: President William Ruto’s most vocal supporters, often dubbed his "attack dogs," swiftly emerge to issue stern warnings. Their consistent message is that continued political engagement will result in the forfeiture of his much-deserved retirement benefits. This cycle of veiled threats and dismissive retorts has become a staple of Kenya’s political landscape in 2026.

### The ‘Vinyangarika’ Gambit

What makes this ongoing drama particularly compelling is Kenyatta’s calculated silence directed at President Ruto himself. Instead of engaging directly, the former Head of State has a habit of grouping Ruto with figures like Oscar Sudi and Farouk Kibet, then subtly dismissing them all as "Vinyangarika"-a local term understood to mean worthless or inconsequential individuals. This strategic move, insiders say, particularly agitates the President’s allies, who view it as a profound disrespect and a deliberate attempt to belittle their principal by association. It is a classic Kenyatta maneuver, designed to deliver a cutting insult without ever directly confronting the man in the big office.

### The Echo Chamber of Threats

Yesterday, the air was thick with insults hurled across the political divide, and today, another contingent of these political enforcers is expected to follow suit, ensuring the narrative remains heated. Allies like Farouk Kibet have openly cautioned that the government might take drastic measures, including freezing bank accounts, should Kenyatta persist in allegedly funding opposition activities. Meanwhile, Deputy President Rigathi Gachagua has reportedly accused President Ruto of orchestrating these attacks, challenging him to confront Uhuru directly rather than sending proxies. This constant volley of threats and counter-accusations paints a vivid picture of the enduring rivalry, keeping political observers gripped.

### Uhuru's Steadfast Position

Despite the mounting pressure, Uhuru Kenyatta remains steadfast in his conviction that retirement from office does not strip him of his constitutional right to participate in political discourse. Speaking on May 1, 2026, during a delegates conference, Kenyatta reiterated that he is a committed member of the Jubilee Party and has every right to contribute to national discussions, citing examples of global leaders like former US President Barack Obama who remain politically active post-presidency. His stance underscores a broader debate about the role of former heads of state in a vibrant democracy. The unfolding drama also highlights the intense political realignments already underway as Kenya gears up for the 2027 General Election, and the political tug-of-war shows no signs of abating.
