## The Kampala Ebola Alert: Why Kenyan SMEs in Logistics and Cross-Border Trade Face an Imminent Profit Squeeze

For Kenyan small business owners operating along the Northern Corridor, the confirmed Ebola cases in Kampala and Ituri are not just a public health concern. They represent an immediate threat to supply chains, inventory stability, and the bottom line of cross-border trade.

The confirmation of Ebola cases in Kampala and the DRC has triggered regional health surveillance directives from the Africa CDC. While health officials prioritize containment, the secondary effect for Kenyan traders is clear: the Northern Corridor, the lifeline for our regional trade, is about to get much slower and significantly more expensive to use.

### Bottlenecks and Perishable Inventory Risks

When border authorities tighten health screening measures, the first thing to suffer is the movement of goods. For SMEs moving fresh produce, dairy, or other perishables into Uganda or beyond to the DRC, time is money. Expect enhanced health checks for truck drivers and cargo manifests at Malaba and Busia. Even a 24-hour delay at a border crossing can mean the difference between fresh stock and a total loss of investment.

Many small-scale importers rely on lean inventory cycles to avoid high storage costs. If your cargo is caught in a health-mandated queue for disinfection or driver screening, your shop shelves in Nairobi or Kisumu may face gaps. This disruption ripples outward, affecting your ability to meet customer demand and forcing you to scramble for more expensive local alternatives. If you are currently struggling with cash flow because of these logistical hurdles, you might find yourself [rewriting a loan](https://zandani.co.ke/article/rewriting-a-loan-loan-consequences) to keep your operations afloat while waiting for stock to clear customs.

### Rising Costs in Logistics and Insurance

The cost of doing business is set to rise. Transport companies are already signaling potential adjustments to freight rates to cover the risks associated with heightened health compliance. If your contract with a logistics provider does not have fixed pricing, be prepared for "health compliance surcharges."

Furthermore, insurance providers often react to regional health alerts by reassessing the risk profiles of cross-border transport. If the situation in Kampala worsens, premiums for transit insurance may increase. These added costs are difficult for an SME to absorb without raising prices, which might make your goods uncompetitive in a market that is already feeling the pinch of inflation. Businesses must now scrutinize their shipping contracts and understand exactly who bears the cost of a three-day delay at the border.

### Why It Matters Now

The Africa CDC has already issued directives to synchronize surveillance across the region. This means the era of business as usual at our border points is ending for the next several weeks. Trade volume along the Northern Corridor is high, and any friction introduced by these screenings will manifest on your balance sheet as increased operational expenses and decreased inventory turnover.

### What to Watch Next

Keep a close eye on the official guidance from the Ministry of Health regarding transit protocols. If you have pending shipments, contact your clearing agents today to confirm if they have contingency plans for rapid documentation processing during potential screening delays. 

Small business owners should take these three steps immediately:
1. **Audit your supply chain:** Identify which of your suppliers rely on transit through Kampala and determine if you can source stock locally or from alternative routes in the short term.
2. **Review your contracts:** Check your logistics agreements for force majeure clauses related to health emergencies. Know if you are protected against sudden rate hikes or cargo delays.
3. **Buffer your liquidity:** If you are an exporter, remember that your export VAT returns will be tied to your customs data. Delays in customs clearance could delay your tax refund process, so ensure your [KRA customs data integration](https://zandani.co.ke/article/2026-04-26-kra-announces-integration-of-customs-data-for-export-vat-returns) is perfectly aligned to avoid any administrative bottlenecks on top of the health ones.

The coming weeks will require a proactive approach to regional trade. Do not wait for your cargo to be flagged at the border before assessing your financial exposure.
