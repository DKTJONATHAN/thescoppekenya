The Kenya Editors Guild (KEG) has strongly condemned a violent assault on journalists Gilbert Sitati and George Njoroge during a United Democratic Alliance (UDA) meeting in Cherangany, Trans Nzoia County, on Friday, March 20, 2026. KEG President Zubeidah Kananu described the incident as a brazen act against media freedom.

Assault on Journalists Condemned

Incident Details and KEG's Stance Gilbert Sitati of Standard Group and George Njoroge of Mediamax were reportedly attacked while covering a UDA meeting in Cherangany. In a statement released Saturday, March 21, 2026, KEG President Zubeidah Kananu characterized the incident as a "brazen act of violence" and a direct threat to both media freedom and the public's right to information. The journalists were fulfilling their professional duties and were reportedly targeted due to their coverage of a controversial fake ambulance procurement deal linked to Harambee House. Kananu emphasized that such actions undermine democratic principles and erode accountability.

Escalating Hostility KEG expressed deep concern regarding the increasing trend of hostility and violence directed at journalists, especially within politically charged environments. The organization stated that such incidents foster a climate of fear and self-censorship, thereby weakening the media's capacity to serve the public interest. The statement highlighted that this violence against the press is unacceptable and directly challenges the foundational tenets of media freedom and public information access.

Viral Footage and Investigation Demands Video footage circulating widely online on Friday, March 20, 2026, depicted two Standard Group journalists being confronted by several individuals, leading to a physical altercation. Preliminary reports suggest the dispute stemmed from a story published in The Standard, which allegedly connected a Senator to a contentious ambulance tender. One journalist was heard pleading for their safety before managing to escape the assailants. Following these events, KEG has urged Inspector General of Police Douglas Kanja to initiate immediate and transparent investigations. The guild demands that those responsible be identified and prosecuted promptly. KEG warned that a failure to hold individuals accountable would compel the organization to pursue legal action, including private prosecution. Additionally, political leaders and their supporters are called upon to respect the media's role and ensure journalist safety at public events, avoiding the transformation of political gatherings into areas of lawlessness. Kananu affirmed solidarity with the injured journalists and encouraged public cooperation with accountability efforts.
