Former Nominated Senator Millicent Omanga took to X on Wednesday, March 25, 2026, to playfully yet firmly address persistent social media inquiries about her relationship status. She publicly celebrated her husband, a doctor, effectively curbing the influx of DMs from online suitors.

Omanga's Strategic Relationship Reveal Millicent Omanga, known for her vibrant online presence, acknowledged that her inbox has been flooded with messages from men asking about her single status or seeking advice on her success. To manage these inquiries, she shared a rare tribute to her spouse, highlighting his medical profession. “Behind every successful woman kuna mwanaume (there is a man)… and mine happens to be a Doctor. Now, those DMs asking ‘are you single?’ and ‘how did you succeed?’ should reduce a little,” she stated. This personal revelation is viewed as a calculated move to redirect public focus towards her political brand as she prepares for a significant campaign.

A New Political Chapter Unfolds Omanga's public display of affection for her husband comes amidst a notable political transition. She recently defected from President William Ruto’s United Democratic Alliance (UDA) to join Rigathi Gachagua’s Democracy for the Citizens Party (DCP), a party unveiled by Gachagua in May 2025. Her official arrival at the DCP headquarters was marked by a viral moment where she engaged in an energetic dance challenge with supporters. Dressed in party colors, the Nairobi Woman Representative hopeful was seen dancing to booming music alongside supporters. This move, seen as a strategic effort to refocus public attention on her political brand as she gears up for a major campaign, signals a proactive political strategy, much like Kenya's recent move to retire KSh 53.6Bn in 2028 and 2032 Eurobond buyback, signalling a proactive debt strategy.

Setting the Stage for 2027 Omanga’s shift to the DCP and her alignment with the Linda Mwananchi movement-a coalition featuring firebrand politicians like Edwin Sifuna and Babu Owino-sets the stage for a competitive 2027 race. The Linda Mwananchi movement operates as a coalition of opposition figures. “Many people have been asking what my plan is for 2027. I will be on the ballot for the Nairobi Woman Representative position,” she declared in February. Her path to the seat is anticipated to be an "epic clash," as she faces a diverse and formidable field of candidates. These include Karen Nyamu, a Nominated Senator currently aligned with the UDA, and Corazon Kwamboka, a popular social media personality and lawyer. As the incumbent Esther Passaris prepares to defend her record or move to a different seat, Omanga’s early campaigning and strategic personal branding suggest she is positioning herself as the candidate of the people-with a doctor by her side for unwavering support.
