By Celestine Nzioka

Interior Cabinet Secretary Kipchumba Murkomen has provided new details regarding the Friday evening accident in Taita Taveta County involving Kenya Ports Authority (KPA) Managing Director Captain William Ruto. The KPA MD's vehicle collided with an un-signposted stationary trailer. Sadly, his daughter, who was travelling with him and five family members, passed away in the tragic crash.

## KPA MD Accident Details Emerge

### Tragic Collision in Taita Taveta
Interior Cabinet Secretary Kipchumba Murkomen revealed that the Kenya Ports Authority (KPA) Managing Director Captain William Ruto's vehicle struck a stationary trailer lacking adequate signage on Friday evening in Taita Taveta County. Murkomen, speaking in Mombasa, confirmed the tragic loss of Captain Ruto's daughter, who was among five family members travelling with him. Following extensive medical evaluations, Captain Ruto has been reported in stable condition.

The CS was in Mombasa overseeing regional security alongside DCI Director Mohamed Amin and Deputy Inspector General of Police Gilbert Masengeli when informed of the crash. Coast Regional Commander Ali Nuno was also present during the Saturday briefing.

### Enhanced Security Measures and Road Safety Appeal
Capitalizing on the occasion, Cabinet Secretary Murkomen urged all road users to exercise heightened vigilance and strictly adhere to traffic regulations, particularly during the ongoing Easter season. He also announced the government's initiative to deploy digital operation hubs and CCTV surveillance across six major towns, including Mombasa. This expansion aims to establish a comprehensive nationwide security network, bolstering police service operational efficiency and public service delivery.

Concurrently, the National Police Service (NPS) has issued a public appeal for motorists to drive cautiously and observe all traffic laws amidst prevailing incidents of flooding and road carnage. Police Spokesperson Muchiri Nyaga confirmed that 112 lives have been tragically lost this year due to heavy rains. The NPS also advised pedestrians to exercise extreme care, especially in areas susceptible to flooding or poor visibility, to mitigate accident and injury risks. The ongoing concerns about road safety underscore recent recommendations, such as those from the NCAJ, for mandatory defensive driving certification for PSV operators.
