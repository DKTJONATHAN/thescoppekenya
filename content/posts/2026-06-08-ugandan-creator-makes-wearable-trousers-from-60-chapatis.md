## The Heavy Price of Digital Stardom Ugandan Creator Makes Wearable Trousers From 60 Chapatis is the central subject of the update.

Raymond Kahuma stands in a studio, the hum of equipment muffled by his own physical discomfort. He wears trousers crafted from sixty individual chapati flatbreads, a garment tipping the scales at ten kilograms. To a casual social media observer, this is merely a bizarre internet stunt. Yet, for Kahuma, the image captures a man navigating the taxing reality of a career built on constant, escalating spectacle.

### From Culinary Staples to Structural Assembly

The days of straightforward cooking tutorials are long behind him. Kahuma’s evolution from documenting the humble Ugandan Rolex to this current project marks a shift toward performance art rooted in structural engineering. Turning food into apparel demands more than culinary talent. It requires a meticulous, almost industrial process involving specialized adhesives and careful hemming to ensure the dough remains intact despite the tension of his movements.

This transformation is not accidental. By repurposing flour, water, and oil into raw textiles, Kahuma is intentionally challenging the algorithm. Every stitch is a deliberate investment of resources, designed to force a pause in the endless scroll of a modern user. He has pivoted from being a chef to a creator who uses ingredients as his primary medium. In the competitive Ugandan digital landscape, where attention is a finite resource, he understands that technical complexity is the only way to remain visible. He is betting that if he treats his production like an engineering challenge, the audience will view him as something more than a flash-in-the-pan viral star.

The weight of the ten-kilogram trousers serves as a physical manifestation of his career trajectory. He exists in a relentless cycle where each success sets a higher bar for the next endeavor. There is little room for quiet creative reflection when the public demands ever-larger stunts. Much like the intense scrutiny directed at advocates pushing for social funding or the heated debates surrounding high-profile musical releases, Kahuma’s output is subjected to the unforgiving gaze of a global audience. The pressure to outdo his previous work turns every project into a high-stakes performance that leaves little room for error.

### The Precarious Economics of Attention

Operating within this model brings significant risks. The logistics of sourcing materials, funding labor, and maintaining high-quality production gear require a substantial budget. In a market where monetization can be inconsistent, Kahuma is balancing on a financial precipice. He is effectively acting as his own venture capitalist, with his physical endurance and public persona serving as the only collateral.

His career path mirrors the high-intensity atmosphere of the music industry. Just as an artist must constantly generate discourse to stay relevant, Kahuma must manufacture new conversation pieces to secure international partnerships and maintain his standing. The chapati trousers are a bid for global legitimacy, proving he can bridge the gap between niche internet humor and large-scale, technically impressive production. 

However, the strategy is not without its pitfalls. There is a looming danger of audience fatigue. If the threshold for what constitutes a viral hit keeps rising, the physical and financial costs of his stunts will eventually outpace the rewards. The core challenge for Kahuma is no longer just about gaining views; it is about longevity. He must determine how to translate this frantic momentum into a sustainable business model that does not require him to push his limits to the breaking point. For now, he keeps moving forward, carrying the literal weight of his work one project at a time. The true test of his success will be whether he can evolve from a master of the viral moment into an entrepreneur who can scale his influence without needing to defy the laws of physics every month.
