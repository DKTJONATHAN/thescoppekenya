By Grace Mkamburi, Za Ndani

## Kenyan Fuel Scandal Exposes Governance Gaps

Senior Kenyan petroleum officials resigned after an investigation uncovered an alleged scheme to fabricate a national fuel shortage. This led to irregular, overpriced, and substandard emergency fuel procurement, breaching the G2G framework and raising significant economic and public trust concerns ahead of April's fuel price review.

### Allegations of Supply Chain Manipulation

A government investigation has revealed an alleged scheme to manipulate data on in-country fuel stocks, creating a false impression of an impending supply shortfall. This manipulated data was reportedly used to trigger the irregular procurement of an emergency fuel cargo by the Ministry of Energy and Petroleum. This procurement bypassed the established G2G framework, was executed at a price significantly above contracted rates, and involved substandard quality fuel. Administrative proceedings have been initiated against Joseph Wafula, Deputy Director of Petroleum, and Joel Mburu, KPC's Supply and Logistics Manager. Arrests of principal officeholders, including Petroleum Principal Secretary Mohamed Liban, Kenya Pipeline Company (KPC) Managing Director Joe Sang, and Energy and Petroleum Regulatory Authority (EPRA) Director General Daniel Kiptoo Bargoria, were effected on Thursday, April 2, 2026.

The shipment, aboard the vessel MV Paloma, is believed to have docked at the Port of Mombasa between March 27 and 29, 2026, and is suspected to have entered the Kenyan market outside the G2G importation framework. Preliminary findings suggest the fuel originated from Saudi Aramco before being allegedly sold to a separate international firm and redirected through a local Kenyan importer. Quality tests conducted by a KPC quality assurance manager found elevated sulphur levels, rendering the consignment non-compliant with Kenyan fuel specifications. The manager declined to authorize discharge of the shipment and escalated the matter, leading to the involvement of investigators. This conduct is characterized by the government as a serious breach of public trust, potentially constituting economic crimes under the Anti-Corruption and Economic Crimes Act (Chapter 65, Laws of Kenya) and the Penal Code (Chapter 63, Laws of Kenya).

### Market Dynamics and G2G Framework Under Review

Despite the ongoing scandal, the government maintains that current fuel supply remains stable, underpinned by the continued performance of contracted G2G suppliers: Aramco Trading Fujairah, ADNOC Global Trading Ltd, and Emirates National Oil Company Singapore Pte Limited. Kenya currently holds approximately 16 days of petrol cover and 19 days of diesel, with jet fuel and kerosene reserves at around 49 days. The current pricing cycle, running until April 14, 2026, is not expected to be immediately affected. However, pump prices are anticipated to face pressure from mid-April, influenced by climbing global oil prices linked to the Middle East conflict. Energy Cabinet Secretary Opiyo Wandayi has directed oil marketing companies to release any withheld fuel, warning that hoarding violates licensing obligations.

The G2G petroleum import arrangement, established in 2023 following severe fuel shortages in 2022, was designed to stabilize supply against global price volatility and ease acute foreign exchange pressure. The program has been extended to 2027/2028. Nonetheless, the arrangement has faced structural criticism, and the government is reportedly considering a phased wind-down, shifting a portion of procurement to an open tender system. This scandal intensifies scrutiny on market integrity and public sector governance, emerging at a time of rising global oil prices, a looming fuel price review, and eroded public confidence in supply security.

### Corporate Response and Operational Continuity

Following the arrests of key officials, Kenya Pipeline Company's board acted swiftly, naming Pius Mwendwa, General Manager for Finance, as acting Managing Director. Board chairperson Faith Boinett affirmed KPC's operational stability, stating that the board is communicating with relevant institutions to understand the nature and scope of the allegations. All governance actors within the petroleum sector have been directed to provide full access to information to facilitate ongoing investigations. The government has committed to reversing irregular shipment requisitions and realigning them with the contracted G2G framework. This incident, alongside other ongoing investigations into public sector conduct, highlights persistent challenges in ensuring accountability and transparency across Kenyan institutions, reflecting a broader public demand for robust governance, a sentiment echoed in discussions around other national integrity concerns such as the [MP Ng'eno Helicopter Crash: Preliminary Probe Reveals Chopper Flew Low, Hit Trees](https://zandani.co.ke/article/mp-ngeno-helicopter-crash-flew-low-hit-trees).
