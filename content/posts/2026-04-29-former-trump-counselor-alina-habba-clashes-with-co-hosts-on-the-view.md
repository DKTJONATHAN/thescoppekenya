## Alina Habba Ignites The View's Hot Topics Table

Former Trump counselor Alina Habba stirred up a storm on *The View* this Wednesday, engaging in fiery debates with Sunny Hostin and Whoopi Goldberg. From a controversial ex-FBI director's deleted post to economic claims and immigration policies, Habba held nothing back, making for an unforgettable "Hot Topics" segment that left audiences buzzing.

### White House Drama and Cryptic Codes

This Wednesday, Alina Habba joined *The View* panel, fresh from the White House Correspondents' Dinner drama where shots reportedly rang out just last Saturday. While they touched on that startling incident, the conversation quickly veered into deeper waters. Former federal prosecutor Sunny Hostin, ever sharp, steered the discussion to the recent indictment of ex-FBI director James Comey over what she summarized as being "related to an Instagram post of seashells, an Instagram post that he deleted the very next day and apologized for". The controversy? An image of seashells spelling "8647", which many, including Habba, interpreted as a message "to kill" the 47th president. Hostin firmly stated, "The dictionary disagrees with you on that" definition, referencing Merriam-Webster's description of "eighty-six" as "slang meaning 'to throw out,' 'to get rid of,' or 'to refuse service to'," setting the stage for a classic *View* face-off.

### Incitement and Accountability

The temperature in the studio rose as the panel debated public figures' responsibility regarding inciting violence. Habba stressed, "You guys have responsibilities not to call the president certain things or say things that could incite violence." However, things got truly interesting when Hostin brought up past suggestions from former President Trump that some Democrats engaged in behavior that's "punishable by DEATH". Habba claimed she hadn't "seen that post," but maintained that "nobody should be inciting violence, period." She then made a bold comparison, stating, "We are not Jack Smith, we're not Letitia James, we bring real cases against people," a remark that garnered audible groans from the audience.

### Economic Realities: Fact or Fiction?

A particularly spirited exchange unfolded when Habba declared that "prescription drug prices" and "egg prices" are both "going down" in the United States. Whoopi Goldberg, never one to shy from a direct question, shot back, "For who?" Habba confidently told Goldberg to "Look at it," to which Goldberg retorted, "I am looking at it, and a lot of people lost their healthcare." The "Big Beautiful Bill" Goldberg referenced had Habba asking if she meant Obamacare, only to be met with Goldberg's firm "No, babe, I'm talking about your Big Beautiful Bill." Clearly, economic perspectives were poles apart at the Hot Topics table.

### Immigration: Through the Front Door

Towards the interview's close, Goldberg sought Habba's perspective on Trump's immigration stance, especially as Habba herself is the child of Iraqi immigrants. Habba articulated that Trump is "pro-immigration the right way, through the front door, through our systems, through the policy." She clarified his stance, stating, "He's not pro-somebody getting stabbed on a bus, he's not pro-rapists." Goldberg, however, expressed her strong disagreement, highlighting the distinction between fleeing persecution and criminal acts, emphasizing "people who are fleeing their country where their child might be in danger, their child's sexuality might be in danger." Habba concluded by asserting that proper immigration is a "process that must be complied with."

### The View's Conservative Lineup

Alina Habba's appearance is just the latest in *The View*'s ongoing series of interviews with conservative personalities. The show has a history of hosting right-leaning guests, from political figures like Marjorie Taylor Greene and Elisabeth Hasselbeck to celebrities such as Cheryl Hines and Savannah Chrisley, even including comedian Sheryl Underwood. It seems *The View* is consistently looking for diverse voices to ignite those "Hot Topics" debates, ensuring that Wednesday's episode featuring Habba was certainly one for the books.
