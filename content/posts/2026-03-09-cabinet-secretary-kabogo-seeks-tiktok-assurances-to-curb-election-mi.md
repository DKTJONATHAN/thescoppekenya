## Government Demands TikTok Accountability for 2027 Election Integrity Cabinet Secretary William Kabogo has pressed TikTok for explicit assurances regarding its strategies to combat misinformation and coordinated disinformation ahead of the 2027 general election. The government emphasizes platform responsibility, particularly concerning digital safety and compliance with Kenya's Data Protection Act, as discussions unfold.

Assurances Sought from TikTok Leadership Following a meeting with TikTok leadership, Information, Communications, and Digital Economy Cabinet Secretary William Kabogo confirmed that he sought clarity on the platform's tools designed to detect misinformation and curb organized disinformation campaigns. Kabogo underscored the government's stance, welcoming digital innovation while demanding greater responsibility from social media platforms to prevent the spread of false information, especially during critical election periods. He explicitly requested "clear assurances on TikTok’s tools to detect misinformation and prevent coordinated disinformation campaigns."

TikTok Safer Internet Summit 2026 and Regulatory Framework The meeting precedes the TikTok Safer Internet Summit 2026, scheduled for Tuesday, March 10, and Wednesday, March 11. This summit occurs days after the National Assembly rejected a proposed ban on TikTok in Kenya, a decision made on February 18. The Public Petitions Committee concluded that a ban would impede economic growth and infringe on fundamental rights, instead recommending stricter regulations. Kenya's more than 17 million TikTok users represent a significant segment of the nation's creative economy. The Cabinet Secretary further asserted that TikTok's operations must align with Kenya’s regulatory framework, including adherence to the Data Protection Act (Kenya). He reiterated, "digital safety is a shared responsibility. We are strengthening content moderation and age verification to protect our children and vulnerable groups." The government is actively collaborating with the company to enhance content moderation and implement more stringent age-verification protocols.

Expanding Operational Presence and Local Content Moderation Kabogo emphasized the necessity of enhanced collaboration between the government and global technology firms to cultivate a digital ecosystem founded on innovation, trust, and safety. He challenged TikTok to expand its operational footprint within Kenya and dedicate increased resources to moderating content in local African languages. The platform's extensive reach influences various sectors, including the dissemination of African celebrity news and gossip, making robust content moderation vital across all linguistic demographics.
