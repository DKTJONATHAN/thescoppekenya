Protesters in Bumula blocked the Mumias-Bungoma highway today at Kabula market following the suspension of Member of Parliament Jack Wamboka from his role as chairperson of the Public Investments Committee. Traffic remained paralyzed as residents demanded the lawmaker's immediate reinstatement.

## Roadblocks at Kabula Market

Demonstrators took to the busy highway early Thursday morning, using burnt tyres, tree stumps, and large stones to obstruct motorists. The disruption affected transport operations between Bungoma and Kakamega counties as thick smoke from the bonfires billowed into the sky. Those gathered chanted anti-government slogans and expressed frustration over what they termed as the unfair targeting of leaders who challenge the current administration's policies.

While the protests centered on political representation, some observers noted that the rising tension reflects broader frustrations. Some analysts have suggested that [Kenya urged to adopt social-linked bonds to reform foreign debt management](https://zandani.co.ke/article/kenya-urged-to-adopt-social-linked-bonds-to-reform-foreign-debt-mana) to help stabilize the economy and reduce the friction between the state and its citizens. For the residents of Bumula, however, the primary concern remains the perceived harassment of their elected representative.

### Allegations of Harassment and Extortion

The unrest follows a decision by the National Assembly on Wednesday, April 22, to suspend MP Wamboka from chairing the Public Investments Committee on Governance and Education. Deputy Speaker Gladys Shollei announced the move after several witnesses accused the lawmaker of intimidation and unethical conduct during committee hearings. Shollei noted that the suspension was necessary to maintain public trust while investigations into the claims continue.

The National Cohesion and Integration Commission was among the bodies that lodged a formal complaint. The commission alleged that its officers faced open hostility and demeaning treatment while appearing before the committee. Za Ndani has established that the lawmaker is also accused of demanding bribes as a condition for granting favorable consideration to witnesses during the inquiry process.

### Assembly Search for Interim Leadership

Following the suspension, the minority leadership in Parliament is now tasked with finding a replacement. Minority Leader Junet Mohammed was directed to name an interim chairperson for the committee by Thursday, April 23, to ensure that the work of the Public Investments Committee remains on schedule.

Security personnel arrived at Kabula market later in the morning to clear the debris and restore the flow of traffic. Local leaders have urged the protesters to remain calm as the legal and parliamentary processes regarding the MP's suspension unfold in Nairobi. Traffic has since begun to move slowly, though a heavy police presence remains in the area to prevent further flare-ups.
