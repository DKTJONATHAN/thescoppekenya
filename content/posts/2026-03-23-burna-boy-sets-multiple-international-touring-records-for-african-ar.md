Damini Ebunoluwa Ogulu, known professionally as Burna Boy, has systematically redefined the global touring landscape for African artists. His extraordinary career, marked by record-breaking sold-out stadium and arena performances across multiple continents, solidifies his position as a pioneering figure and the highest-grossing African artist internationally through 2025.

Burna Boy's Unprecedented Global Touring Milestones Burna Boy’s live performances have consistently shattered prior limitations for African artists on the global stage, showcasing an expanding audience that spans continents.

North American Dominance Reflecting on his trajectory, Burna Boy achieved the distinction in April 2022 of becoming the first African artist in history to headline and sell out Madison Square Garden in New York City. His "One Night In Space" concert drew over 20,000 fans, positioning him among global icons who have commanded the iconic venue. On July 8, 2023, he further elevated his status by becoming the first African artist to sell out a United States stadium, performing to an audience of 41,800 at Citi Field in New York City. Burna Boy's February 25, 2024, performance at the 19,000-capacity Scotiabank Arena in Toronto, Canada, established him as the first and only African artist to sell out the venue. Combined with his Bell Centre dates in Montreal, reports suggest he grossed $5.7 million from six Canadian shows alone.

European Triumphs During 2023, Burna Boy also made history in the United Kingdom, becoming the first African artist to headline a sold-out show at London Stadium, captivating a crowd of 60,000. His London Stadium show on June 29, 2024, further cemented this, grossing approximately $6.15 million from nearly 59,000 fans in a single night. Also in 2023, he demonstrated his growing influence in France by selling out the 40,000-capacity La Défense Arena in Paris for two consecutive days, a first for an African artist at that venue. Still within 2023, the artist sold out the 40,000-capacity GelreDome Arnhem Stadium in the Netherlands, underscoring his extensive reach across Europe.

Oceania Records and Global Financial Acumen Burna Boy’s recent "No Sign of Weakness Tour" extended his record-setting streak to a new continent, becoming the highest-grossing tour by an African artist in Oceania's history, generating $3.1 million from 31,000 tickets sold across four shows. His performance at Qudos Bank Arena in Sydney on October 18, 2025, notably marked him as the first African artist to gross over $1 million from a single concert in Oceania. These accomplishments across North America, Europe, and Oceania solidify Burna Boy as the first African artist to gross over $1 million from a single concert across three separate continents. His "I Told Them… Tour" stands as the highest-grossing tour ever by an African artist, amassing a total of $30.5 million from 302,801 tickets sold across 22 shows. Burna Boy's continued trajectory indicates more milestones are anticipated.
