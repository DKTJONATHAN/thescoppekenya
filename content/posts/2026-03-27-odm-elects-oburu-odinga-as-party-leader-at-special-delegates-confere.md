By Celestine Nzioka, Za Ndani The Orange Democratic Movement (ODM) officially confirmed Oburu Odinga as its party leader today, Friday, March 27, 2026, during a Special Delegates Conference at Jamhuri Grounds in Nairobi. His election, ratifying five months of acting capacity, affirmed confidence in his leadership for the party's future direction.

ODM Ratifies New Leadership

Odinga's Affirmation Oburu Odinga's leadership was endorsed by delegates during the conference, confirming his role after serving in an interim capacity following internal party changes. Suna East MP Junet Mohamed oversaw the ratification process, declaring Odinga the party leader following a unanimous 'Aye' vote from the assembled delegates. Odinga had assumed the acting party leader position in October 2025 following the death of his brother, Raila Odinga.

Deputy Party Leaders Approved The delegates also approved Mombasa Governor Abdullswamad Sheriff Nassir and Kisii Counterpart Simba Arati as deputy party leaders. Their appointments signify the party's strategic consolidation of regional leadership, aiming to strengthen its presence across various constituencies. Governor Nassir had previously defended the party's leadership decisions, citing adherence to the party constitution and unanimous resolutions.

Osotsi's Disapproval However, the conference saw a dramatic turn when Vihiga Senator Godfrey Osotsi failed to secure ratification as a third deputy party leader. His nomination did not receive the required proposer and seconder from the delegates, consequently leading to his disapproval. Junet Mohamed confirmed the decision, stating that Osotsi's name, brought forth by the National Governing Council, could not proceed without the necessary endorsements. This development follows Osotsi's reported realignment with an ODM breakaway faction, including Edwin Sifuna, Babu Owino, and Siaya Governor James Orengo. Osotsi, who had been serving as a deputy party leader, has been vocal in rejecting calls to support President William Ruto and emphasized internal unity within ODM.

New National Chairperson Appointed Further leadership changes saw the approval of Homa Bay Governor Gladys Wanga as the ODM's official National Chairperson. She will be deputised by Rarieda MP Otiende Amollo alongside his Turkana South counterpart John Ariko Namoit, whose ratifications were also announced during the event.
