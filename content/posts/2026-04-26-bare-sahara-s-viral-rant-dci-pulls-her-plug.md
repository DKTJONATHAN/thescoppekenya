Bare Sahara's Viral Rant: DCI Pulls Her Plug!

The Directorate of Criminal Investigations (DCI) on Sunday, April 26, 2026, swiftly apprehended Bare Sahara Ahmed over a widely circulated video. Her alleged remarks, deemed inciteful and a threat to national cohesion, prompted a robust DCI response, with investigations now delving into the full context of her statements.

## Bare Sahara's Inciteful Video Lands Her in Hot Water

The DCI moved with speed after reviewing a video in which Ms. Ahmed allegedly made pronouncements that could easily stir up trouble and undermine the nation's unity. This swift action aligns with the DCI's mandate to investigate serious criminal cases, including those related to maintaining law and order and preventing crime. Authorities have consistently warned that while freedom of expression is a constitutional right, enshrined in Article 33 of the Constitution of Kenya, it does not extend to incitement to violence, hate speech, or advocacy of hatred. This firm stance comes as the Central Bank of Kenya Governor recently hinted at further interest rate cuts after lending rates fell to 8.75 percent, a sign of ongoing efforts to stabilize the nation's economic landscape.

### The Contentious Clip That Sparked Outrage

The specific content of Ms. Ahmed's video is currently under intense scrutiny, with investigators working to establish the full context and intent behind her utterances. The DCI has made it unequivocally clear that there's a distinct line between expressing an opinion and fanning the flames of division. The National Cohesion and Integration Act, for instance, explicitly outlaws words intended to incite contempt, hatred, hostility, or discrimination against any person or group based on ethnicity or race, with severe penalties for offenders.

### DCI's Stern Warning: Freedom Has Limits

The DCI's statement reiterated that exercising freedom of expression must be done responsibly. Any statements inciting division, hatred, or threatening peaceful coexistence will be met with firm legal action. This serves as a stark reminder to all, public figures and citizens alike, that the privilege of free speech carries with it a weighty responsibility. This principle holds true even as prominent personalities like Wahu and Nameless navigate their own public narratives, having revealed a media agreement used to keep their wedding private.

### Investigations Underway

Za Ndani understands that the DCI's approach in this matter is a straightforward application of the law to a perceived breach of public peace. They aren't looking for hidden meanings or subtle subtexts; rather, their focus is on the literal impact of Ms. Ahmed's statements on national cohesion. This directness highlights the seriousness with which law enforcement in Kenya treats matters that could potentially jeopardize the country's delicate social fabric.

As investigations continue, the streets are buzzing with whispers: in Kenya, you can speak your mind, but you better pick your words wisely or face the music.
