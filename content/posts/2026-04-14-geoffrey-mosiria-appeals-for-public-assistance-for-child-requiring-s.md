Nairobi County Chief Officer for Citizen Engagement, Geoffrey Mosiria, made a heartfelt plea on Tuesday, April 14, 2026, urging Kenyans to rally behind a five-year-old girl at Kenyatta National Hospital. The child urgently requires specialized skull surgery following a horrific incident last year, sparking a nationwide call for compassion and support.

Mosiria Champions Cause for Young Survivor

Child's Harrowing Ordeal Unveiled The gravity of the situation was brought to light by Mosiria, highlighting a case of profound child abuse and remarkable resilience. The young girl's ordeal began in 2025 when she was left under the care of her uncle after her mother moved to Nairobi for work. Tragically, two months later, the mother received devastating news: her daughter had allegedly been assaulted by her uncle, his wife, and their mother. This brutal act resulted in severe head trauma, including a dangerous blood clot in the brain. The child was rushed to the ICU, where doctors initially advised the mother to consider withdrawing life support, a suggestion she courageously refused, granting her child a fighting chance.

A Long Road to Recovery Having now spent nine months at Kenyatta National Hospital, the young survivor's journey has been arduous. While she overcame the immediate life-threatening trauma, the extent of the nerve damage has profoundly impacted her. Currently, the child is unable to see, walk, talk, or sit independently, remaining dependent on machines to breathe. Mosiria, known for his commitment to citizen welfare, echoing the kind of civic engagement seen with initiatives like Governor Sakaja's orders for the rehabilitation of Nairobi Dam, expressed profound admiration for the mother's unwavering dedication throughout this challenging period.

Critical Surgery and The Harambee Spirit The medical team has now recommended a specialized surgical procedure involving the implantation of a bone in the girl's skull. This operation is deemed critical for protecting her brain and establishing a foundation for potential nerve recovery. The family is appealing to the public for assistance in raising Ksh 150,000 to facilitate this essential surgery. Previous appeals for the family saw Kenyans generously contribute financial aid and prayers, enabling the mother to remain by her daughter's side. Mosiria passionately invoked the spirit of "Harambee," urging the nation to once again stand with the young survivor and offer her another chance at life and healing.
