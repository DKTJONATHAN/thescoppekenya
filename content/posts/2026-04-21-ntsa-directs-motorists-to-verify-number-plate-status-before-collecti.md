The National Transport and Safety Authority (NTSA) on Monday, April 20, 2026, issued a directive urging motorists to verify their number plate readiness via the eCitizen platform before collecting them. This move is part of the Authority's strategy to clear a persistent backlog of uncollected reflective number plates at service centres across the country.

## NTSA Addresses Number Plate Backlog

### Verifying Collection Status
Motorists who applied for number plates are now advised to confirm their collection status by logging into the government's digital services platform, eCitizen. The NTSA has provided a direct link, ntsa.go.ke, for users to access the dedicated service portal. This portal allows applicants to track the progress of their number plate applications before visiting any collection point.

Upon successful login, individuals are directed to their NTSA dashboard. This personalized section displays personal details alongside various service categories. To ascertain the number plate's readiness, users must navigate to the vehicle registration search tab. This feature clearly indicates whether the number plate is still undergoing processing or is available for collection.

### Reporting Issues and Contact Information
The digital portal also links each driver’s number plate and driving licence status directly to their profile. For any challenges encountered during the verification process or collection, a 'Report Issue' feature is available. Motorists requiring direct assistance can contact NTSA officials via phone at 0709-932-300, by email at info@ntsa.go.ke, or through the dedicated complaints address at complaints@ntsa.go.ke.

### Addressing Unclaimed Plates and Efficiency
The directive comes in response to reports indicating that thousands of number plates remain unclaimed, leading to congestion in storage facilities and impeding efficient service delivery. As of February 2026, approximately 70,000 number plates were reportedly uncollected across various NTSA service centres nationwide, significantly contributing to the existing backlog. The NTSA states that its systems have enhanced efficiency and reduced long queues at service points. The Authority's announcement also follows mounting pressure from a segment of motorists regarding perceived delays in processing crucial documents. The latest administrative issue underscores the broader responsibilities of vehicle ownership, which also include ensuring adequate [motor insurance coverage](https://zandani.co.ke/article/britam-explains-motor-insurance-coverage-for-submerged-vehicles) for various eventualities.
