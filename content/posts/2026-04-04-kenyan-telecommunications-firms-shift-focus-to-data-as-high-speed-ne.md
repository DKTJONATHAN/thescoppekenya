## Kenya's Telecommunications Shift to Data Dominance

Kenyan telecommunication firms are strategically pivoting towards data services as mobile broadband subscriptions and high-speed network usage surge. This transformation underscores a consumer preference for data-intensive communication and services, reshaping market dynamics and prompting operators to enhance their data offerings.

### Surging Data Consumption and Broadband Growth

The Communications Authority of Kenya (CA) reports a significant shift in the nation's telecommunications sector, with mobile data now a primary revenue generator. Mobile broadband subscriptions increased by 10.5 percent over one year, reaching 61.9 million users. Total data consumption escalated to 755 terabytes, marking a 32.86 percent rise. This substantial growth reflects an accelerating demand for data-centric communications among Kenyans.

### High-Speed Network Adoption

A key trend highlighted in the CA's Q2 FY2025/26 report is the rapid migration to faster networks. 4G subscriptions grew by 10.4 percent, totaling 44.2 million users between Q1 and Q2 of FY24/25. Though still in its early stages, 5G adoption recorded a notable 15.9 percent increase, bringing subscriptions to 1.7 million. This expansion in 4G and 5G usage coincides with a decline in 2G and 3G network numbers, indicating a clear user preference for enhanced connectivity. The data consumption patterns underscore this shift, with 5G subscribers averaging 46.4 gigabytes per month in Q2, significantly higher than 4G users at 14.1 gigabytes and 3G users at 8.8 gigabytes. Such high data usage aligns with demands from bandwidth-intensive applications and activities, including mobile gaming, as exemplified by events like the Road to 2026 PMGO Africa inviting Kenyan PUBG MOBILE Players to compete for substantial prize pools.

### Smartphone Penetration Drives Data Boom

The underlying factor fueling this data surge is the continued widespread adoption of smartphones. By December 2025, smartphone penetration reached 92.9 percent of all mobile devices, up from 85.2 percent just three months prior, marking a 9.1 percent quarterly growth. Concurrently, feature phone connections decreased by 2.6 percent to 29.6 million, suggesting a strong consumer preference for smartphones during device replacement. Kenya now boasts over 48.7 million active smartphones, with the increasing 4G and 5G subscriptions signaling an improvement in the calibre of devices in use.

### Market Competition and Strategic Responses

For Kenyan telcos, data services have transitioned from supplementary offerings to core telecommunications products. Safaricom holds a significant market share in mobile broadband at 64.3 percent, with Airtel Kenya at 32.0 percent, collectively dominating the market. The evident consumer appetite for high-speed mobile internet, coupled with the increasing affordability of 5G phones and a preference for data-reliant communication platforms, intensifies competition. Operators are responding by refining their data offerings and connection reliability. Airtel Kenya, for instance, has introduced home fibre services and recently concluded a pilot program for mobile-to-cell satellite services in Kenya, strategically positioning itself for future growth in this evolving landscape.
