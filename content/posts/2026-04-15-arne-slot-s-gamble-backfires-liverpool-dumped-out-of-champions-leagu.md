Liverpool's 2026 Champions League journey concluded with a decisive 4-0 aggregate defeat to Paris St-Germain, casting a shadow over Arne Slot's tenure. The elimination, coupled with Mohamed Salah's Anfield farewell, leaves the club facing a critical period as questions emerge about their European competitiveness and the manager's immediate future.

## European Ambitions Crushed

Liverpool's aspirations for 2026/27 Champions League football now hinge on their domestic league performance, following a disappointing exit from Europe's elite competition. The 4-0 aggregate scoreline against Paris St-Germain, reigning champions of Europe, highlights a significant step backward for Slot's side. While PSG's quality under Luis Enrique was undeniable, Liverpool's inability to challenge meaningfully across two legs, particularly after a narrow penalty shootout loss to the same opposition in last season's round of 16, signals a concerning regression. The defeat also ensures no silverware for Liverpool this campaign, a situation Slot himself termed "definitely not an acceptable season" without Champions League qualification.

## Slot's Costly Gamble

A focal point of post-match criticism was Arne Slot's decision to start Alexander Isak. Making his first appearance since December after an ankle injury, Isak was reportedly only fit for one half. The gamble proved ill-advised, with the striker managing just five touches in 45 minutes before being replaced by Cody Gakpo at halftime. Football pundit Julien Laurens criticized the lineup as "complete nonsense," suggesting Liverpool's second-half intensity, sparked by Gakpo, should have been the starting approach. Former player Stephen Warnock echoed these sentiments, stating Isak was "non-existent" and "nowhere near fit," arguing that such a crucial match against the "best team in Europe" was not the time for such an experiment. Gakpo's immediate impact post-break underscored the perceived error in Slot's initial tactical choice.

## Anfield Bids Farewell to a Legend

Adding to the somber mood, Mohamed Salah waved goodbye to the Anfield faithful after the match, signifying his final Champions League appearance for the club. Salah's departure marks the end of an era and creates an immense void for Liverpool to fill. His emotional farewell underscores the transitional period the club now faces, with significant changes expected on and off the pitch as they look to rebuild for future European campaigns.

### The Road Ahead for Liverpool

With the Champions League dream extinguished, Liverpool's focus shifts entirely to securing a top-four finish in the domestic league. Failing to qualify for next season's Champions League would intensify pressure on Arne Slot and undoubtedly impact the club's financial and recruitment strategies. While there were some positives, like the improved attacking output at Anfield with an xG of 1.94 compared to 0.18 in Paris, the overall performance suggests considerable work remains before Liverpool can once again be considered serious contenders on the European stage. The club faces a pivotal summer as they navigate the aftermath of a trophy-less season and a legendary player's exit.
