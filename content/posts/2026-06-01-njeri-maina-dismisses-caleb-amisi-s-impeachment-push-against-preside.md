Njeri Maina dismisses Caleb Amisi's impeachment push against Pres is central to this update for Kenyan readers.

Kirinyaga Woman Representative Njeri Maina has publicly rejected calls from Member of Parliament Caleb Amisi to impeach President William Ruto. Maina argues that the motion lacks the necessary legislative support and serves as a performative tactic rather than a genuine constitutional action.

## The Impracticality of the Impeachment Motion

### A performance for the gallery
Political observers are characterizing the recent calls for a presidential ouster as a choreographed spectacle. While some legislators continue to demand a change in administration, Maina contends that this rhetoric is designed to capture public attention rather than influence government policy. The legislative reality suggests that these calls are merely a distraction meant to stir public sentiment without providing a viable path toward removing the head of state.

### The numbers game
Maina emphasizes that the opposition lacks the required parliamentary majority to successfully initiate or pass an impeachment motion. She frames the ongoing narrative as a deceptive political strategy, noting that the proponents of the move are fully aware that the numbers are not in their favor. By continuing to push this agenda, these politicians are allegedly misleading the public, as they understand the effort will fail to gain any traction within the National Assembly.

### Why the talk lacks teeth
The discourse surrounding the president’s potential removal is being dismissed as lacking any serious intent or structural backbone. Critics suggest that the politicians involved are comfortable maintaining the current status quo and have no real plan to execute a leadership change. Instead, the focus remains on keeping the populace engaged with empty promises of political upheaval. This approach is viewed as a cycle of theater where politicians perform the role of the agitator to maintain relevance, despite having no legitimate intention or capacity to shift the balance of power.
