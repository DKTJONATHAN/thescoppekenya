Education Cabinet Secretary Julius Ogamba has initiated a thorough investigation into alleged questionable degrees issued by Masinde Muliro University of Science and Technology. The directive, issued on April 1, 2026, mandates the Commission for University Education (CUE) to verify the legitimacy of all graduands and their qualifications awarded during recent graduation processes, following widespread online claims.

Degree Authenticity Under Scrutiny CS Ogamba's order comes in response to numerous allegations circulating across various social and mainstream media platforms, which have raised significant doubts regarding the authenticity of certificates from Masinde Muliro University of Science and Technology (MMUST). These claims have prompted concerns about the integrity of the country’s higher education system and the value of academic qualifications.

CUE Tasked with Verification The Commission for University Education has been directed to undertake a comprehensive investigation. Ogamba emphasized that the CUE's primary role is to ensure every certificate issued reflects genuine academic effort, proper assessment, and strict adherence to national education standards expected of accredited universities. The Commission is specifically required to ascertain that all graduands were bona fide students and legitimately qualified for their conferred awards.

Protecting Academic Standards This investigation forms part of a broader effort by the Ministry of Education to uphold Kenya’s reputation as a leading regional education hub. Ensuring the integrity of local degrees is critical, especially as they increasingly compete in international academic and professional spheres. Such measures are vital for maintaining confidence in the country's educational framework.

Spreading Disinformation Addressed Amidst the ongoing probe, a separate statement falsely attributed to the Ministry of Education has caused further confusion. This fake document, which discusses examination policies and uses the government's public seal, the Ministry's initials, and CS Ogamba's forged signature, has been debunked by officials. The Ministry has clarified that this misleading communication, which frames invigilation, support, and marking as inherent teacher duties, is intended to distract from the legitimate investigation into MMUST’s certificates and should be dismissed. The Ministry distances itself from the fake statement and urges the public to treat it with contempt, highlighting that such misinformation risks diverting attention from the real issues at hand. This incident underscores the importance of verifying information, much like the rigorous due diligence discussed in safeguarding cross-border payment systems.
