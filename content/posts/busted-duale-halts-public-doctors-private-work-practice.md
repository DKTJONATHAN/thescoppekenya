<h2>Duale Launches Digital Crackdown on Public Doctors' Private Practice</h2> Health Cabinet Secretary Aden Duale has announced a new digital system designed to halt public doctors from engaging in private practice during official working hours. The initiative will automatically flag and reject Social Health Authority (SHA) insurance claims authorised by medical professionals who are simultaneously scheduled for duty in public hospitals and attending to patients in their private facilities. This decisive move is part of broader reforms aimed at ensuring the efficient use of public health funds, guaranteeing quality patient care, and combating fraud within the healthcare system. <h3>Digital System to Combat Dual Practice</h3> The new enforcement mechanism leverages an automated digital system to monitor and control doctors' work practices. According to CS Duale, if a doctor is scheduled to work from 8 am to 5 pm in a public county facility, any claim submitted from their private clinic under their pre-authorisation during these hours will be rejected by SHA. The system will meticulously track the time and location of each claim authorisation, relying on pre-authorisation records submitted to SHA, which are mandatory before claims can be processed. This means that even a patient served at a doctor's private clinic during a public hospital lunch break will have their claim declined if the doctor authorises it while officially on duty. Duale further warned that the system's scrutiny extends to night shifts, ensuring that attempts to authorise private claims during designated public duty hours will be flagged irrespective of the time. <h3>Addressing Misuse of Public Resources</h3> CS Duale emphatically stated that the government will no longer tolerate the misuse of public time and resources by healthcare workers prioritising private practice over their public hospital duties. He highlighted the detrimental impact this practice has on patients reliant on public health services. The digital crackdown comes as the Social Health Authority faces significant fraud allegations, including a recent claim exceeding Ksh 11 billion. This measure is positioned as a critical component of ongoing reforms to safeguard public health funds and elevate the standard of patient care across national and county facilities. <h3>Strengthening Health Systems Through Partnership</h3> The announcement was made during the signing of an Intergovernmental Partnership Agreement (IPA) between the national government and counties. This agreement is expected to foster enhanced collaboration between the two levels of government, particularly in advancing Universal Health Coverage (UHC). The IPA falls under the framework of the Building Resilient and Responsive Health Systems (BREHS) Project, a five-year initiative launched on January 31, 2025, and scheduled to conclude on June 30, 2029. The BREHS Project aims to improve the utilisation and quality of primary healthcare services while simultaneously strengthening institutional capacity across all 47 counties and at the national level.
