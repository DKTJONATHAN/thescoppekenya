## Pupils Hospitalized Following Suspected Mango Poisoning in Kitale

Over thirty Grade 4 pupils from Big Tree Comprehensive School in Kitale were rushed to Wamalwa Kijana Teaching and Referral Hospital today, Thursday, March 12, 2026, after experiencing symptoms consistent with food poisoning. The incident occurred shortly after the learners shared mangoes brought to school by a classmate. Medical professionals confirm the pupils are in stable condition and expected to be discharged soon.

### Incident Details and Medical Response

The pupils began complaining of stomach aches this morning, March 12, following the consumption of mangoes shared amongst them. Reports indicate one student provided two mangoes, which were then distributed among classmates. Initially, 31 pupils reported discomfort, with 23 subsequently transported to Wamalwa Kijana Teaching and Referral Hospital for observation and treatment. Doctors at the facility confirmed the admissions presented with classic food poisoning symptoms. One medical official stated, "Most of them had taken kitchen fruit which had been contaminated with pesticides in the morning. We started our management; we gave them fluids and some medication." The doctor further confirmed the pupils were not severely affected, adding, "They weren't badly affected, so after medication, they got well, and they are ready to be discharged home."

### Ongoing Investigations

Health officials have initiated investigations into the incident. Samples of the suspected mangoes have been collected for laboratory analysis to precisely determine the cause of the illness. Concurrently, police have launched their own inquiry to establish the full circumstances surrounding the event. Parents of the affected learners are currently awaiting an official statement from the authorities regarding the ongoing investigations.
