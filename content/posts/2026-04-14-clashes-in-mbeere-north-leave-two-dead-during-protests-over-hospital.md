By Celestine Nzioka Two individuals are confirmed dead after violent clashes erupted between protesters and police officers in Mbeere North on Tuesday, April 14, 2026. The unrest stemmed from demonstrations against the deplorable conditions at Ishiara Level 4 Hospital.

Mbeere North Protests Turn Deadly The deceased were part of a group of youths who took to the streets to protest the substandard state of Ishiara Level 4 Hospital, a facility classified for primary care. The protests, which rendered roads impassable for a significant part of the day, escalated into deadly confrontations in the afternoon when police attempted to disperse the demonstrators.

Hospital Conditions Spark Outrage Irate residents had blocked the busy Embu Highway with rocks in the build-up to the confrontation. The Ishiara Level 4 Hospital has faced considerable criticism in recent weeks, with ward representatives issuing warnings as recently as April 10, 2026, that the facility's condition was unsustainable. Concerns were amplified by photos circulating on social media, which showcased the hospital's dilapidated infrastructure, including damaged ward roofs. Locals have traditionally held the facility in high regard, intensifying calls for accountability from the county government to upgrade the hospital.

Confrontation and Broader Concerns The situation intensified on Tuesday afternoon when police officers attempted to repel the protesters, who then retaliated, leading to a direct confrontation. Reports indicate that live rounds were fired during the clash, resulting in the two fatalities. This incident marks the second time in April 2026 that protesters have been shot by police. Weeks prior, two protesters were fatally shot by authorities in Siaya during an attempt to storm a police post. That earlier incident occurred amidst demonstrations over an alleged eviction from a gold mining site. The current clash also unfolds amid ongoing calls to address a troubling surge of goonism witnessed across several parts of the country in April. On Sunday, Interior Cabinet Secretary Kipchumba Murkomen announced his approval for Inspector General of Police Douglas Kanja to address goonism with the full backing of the Ministry. This escalation comes amid broader calls to address instances of unrest and goonism across the country, with figures like Uhuru Kenyatta recently reiterating calls for national unity and an end to tribal politics.
