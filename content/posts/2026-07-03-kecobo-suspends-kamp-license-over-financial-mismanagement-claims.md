## Copyright Licensing Chaos Hits Small Businesses

If your bar, restaurant, or event space relies on licensed music to set the mood, you might be facing an unexpected headache this July. KECOBO has pulled the plug on KAMP for the next 90 days, leaving business owners caught in a messy transition that threatens to complicate your upcoming tax and compliance filings.

The Kenya Copyright Board has formally suspended the license of the Kenya Association of Music Producers (KAMP) following allegations of poor governance and internal financial irregularities. This decision, which took effect this week, means that KAMP no longer has the legal authority to act as your point of contact for music rights. They cannot issue new invoices, collect payments, or negotiate any tariff structures until they prove they have cleaned up their house.

### The Payment Pause: Where Your Royalty Money Goes Now

For most business operators, the primary concern is the monthly royalty bill. If you receive an invoice from KAMP dated after the suspension, do not pay it. KECOBO has designated PAVRISK as the authorized entity to manage collections during this interim period. This shift is intended to safeguard funds that were previously being mismanaged. 

If you have already paid for an annual license, your existing agreement is technically still recognized, but you should keep your receipt and a copy of your contract in a safe place. If you are currently in a payment plan or were due to renew this month, stop all transactions directed to KAMP accounts. PAVRISK is now the entity responsible for depositing these funds into a designated trust account. Making a payment to KAMP today would likely result in a financial loss, as those funds may not be recognized by regulators when it comes time for your next compliance audit.

### Protecting Your Business From Legal Exposure

The risk here is not just lost money but potential legal liability. If KAMP representatives or agents continue to knock on your door during this suspension period, you are within your rights to ask for their current authorization letter from KECOBO. If they cannot produce proof of standing, you should inform them that you are waiting for clear guidance from the regulatory board regarding the collection process.

This period of administrative house cleaning follows broader efforts to bring accountability to state-aligned organizations, a trend we have also seen with the [Treasury CS John Mbadi Appoints Selection Panel for State-Owned Enterprise Directors](https://zandani.co.ke/article/treasury-cs-john-mbadi-appoints-selection-panel-for-state-owned-ente). Much like the oversight being applied to state corporations, the goal here is to ensure that the 70:30 ratio for royalty distribution actually reaches the creators rather than disappearing into administrative overhead. For the small business owner, this means keeping meticulous records of every cent paid for music rights. 

### Practical Steps for Compliance

You should take immediate action to protect your operation during the next three months. First, notify your accounts department that all KAMP-related payments are frozen until further notice. Second, reach out to KECOBO directly or check their official portal to confirm the specific payment coordinates for PAVRISK. Third, keep a physical file of all communications received from KAMP during this period. If you receive an aggressive notice or a threat of legal action for non-payment, document the interaction and forward it to the copyright board. 

While governance disputes often dominate the headlines, the real impact lands on your bottom line. Use this 90-day window to audit your own licensing status. If you have any doubts about your current standing, do not sign any new documents presented by KAMP agents, as their authority to enter into binding agreements is currently void. By remaining cautious and strictly following the updated collection instructions from the board, you can avoid becoming a casualty of this institutional dispute. Keep your business operations professional and documented, and remember that regulatory suspensions are meant to protect the system, not to give you a permanent pass on your obligations.
