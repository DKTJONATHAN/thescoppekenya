During a heated campaign period in Emurua Dikirr this week, the political landscape turned into a spectacle when supporters of incumbent MP Johanna Ng’eno received cash handouts from his spouse while rival supporters protested by wearing green plastic carrier bags on their heads.

## The Absurdity of Grassroots Political Pageantry

### A Contrast in Campaign Strategy
The campaign trail in Emurua Dikirr has devolved into a stark survival contest defined by two competing methods of securing voter attention. Supporters of the incumbent candidate were seen accepting cash handouts of 1,000 shillings, a traditional strategy utilized to consolidate grassroots backing. In direct contrast, the opposition camp, citing a lack of financial resources, opted for a visual protest. Supporters of the rival candidate marched through the constituency wearing bright green plastic grocery bags as makeshift headgear, turning common household items into a public political statement.

### Marketing Financial Scarcity as Loyalty
While the optics of the situation suggest a lack of funding, the opposition movement has reframed the use of plastic bags as a deliberate and profound symbolic gesture of unwavering loyalty. This dynamic highlights a sharp irony in local politics, where voters are signaling their commitment to a candidate who cannot afford standard branded merchandise. It serves as a commentary on the performative nature of elections, where participants lean into the absurdity of the process to signal their allegiance, even when the candidates they support lack the traditional capital to run an effective campaign.

### The Reality of Voter Disillusionment
The bizarre imagery of voters wearing shopping bags underscores a deeper disillusionment within the constituency. Residents find themselves caught between candidates who offer small monetary payments and those who lack the basic funds to produce standard campaign materials like t-shirts. This scene serves as a stark reminder that in local elections, the political script often defies standard logic. As the race continues, the green bag movement underscores the reality that in the heat of a campaign, a lack of funding can sometimes be overcome by creative, albeit unconventional, demonstrations of support.
