The Independent Electoral and Boundaries Commission (IEBC) has scheduled by-elections for May 14, 2026, to fill three vacant elective seats. These include the Member of National Assembly position for Emurua Dikirr Constituency and two county assembly wards, Porro Ward and Endo Ward, following recent declarations of vacancy.

By-Election Timeline Set

Vacancies Detailed The three seats declared vacant include the Member of National Assembly for Emurua Dikirr Constituency in Narok County, effective March 10, 2026, due to the death of MP Johana N'geno. Additionally, the Porro Ward seat in Samburu West Constituency, Samburu County, became vacant on January 5, 2026, and the Endo Ward seat in Marakwet East Constituency, Elgeyo/Marakwet County, was rendered vacant on January 27, 2026.

Candidate Submission and Nomination Political parties wishing to participate must submit their candidates' names by Wednesday, March 25, 2026. Independent candidates are required to submit their names and proposed symbols to the commission by the same date. The IEBC will publish the names and symbols of independent candidates within seven days of receipt. The official nomination of political parties and candidates will occur on Wednesday, April 15, and Thursday, April 16. Any disputes arising from these nominations must be lodged within 24 hours and will be determined by Saturday, April 25, 2026. Candidates intending to contest as independents must not have been members of any political party for at least three months preceding the by-election date. Public officers planning to run must resign from their positions within seven days of the vacancy declaration.

Campaign Regulations and Voter Register Campaigns for the by-elections are slated to run from Wednesday, April 15, to Monday, May 11, strictly between 7am and 6pm. This period will see candidates engaging directly with constituents, a form of active public participation essential for representative governance, much like the broader community engagement championed by initiatives like 'Get Out Get Active'. To ensure an orderly electoral process, the registration and revision of the voter register in the affected areas will be suspended until June 12, 2026.
