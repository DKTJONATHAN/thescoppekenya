Treasury Cabinet Secretary and ODM Chairman John Mbadi sparked significant controversy this week by asserting that his political career was built through his own individual efforts, appearing to minimize the long-standing influence and mentorship of opposition leader Raila Odinga in his rise to power.

## The unraveling of ODM internal unity

### Challenging the political narrative
In the competitive landscape of Kenyan politics, the recent remarks by John Mbadi have stirred intense debate. By suggesting that his ascent to the Treasury and his tenure as party chairman were products of personal grit rather than his long-term affiliation with Raila Odinga, Mbadi has challenged the foundation of his own political history. This attempt to rewrite his origin story has been met with skepticism from party stalwarts, who argue that the ODM platform provided the essential infrastructure for his professional success. The narrative shift is being viewed by many as a risky gamble that risks alienating the voter base that remains loyal to the party’s traditional hierarchy.

### Systematic fractures in the opposition
The growing friction within ODM reflects a broader pattern of internal instability that is currently plaguing the opposition. Analysts observe that the internal discord is not isolated, but rather part of a larger trend where prominent figures begin to drift away from the central figurehead to establish independent identities. This trend has created significant vulnerability within the party, as supporters interpret any distance from Odinga as a betrayal of party principles. The resulting chaos demonstrates how fragile political alliances have become when individual ambition begins to outweigh the established party structure.

### The strategic impact of presidential maneuvering
Political observers point to the broader influence of President William Ruto’s administration as a key driver of this internal strife. The current government has strategically courted opposition leaders, creating incentives for figures like Mbadi to adopt more independent stances. By encouraging opposition members to redefine their allegiances, the administration has successfully fractured the once-solid ODM front. Mbadi now finds himself in a precarious position, attempting to navigate a new political reality while his former allies question his loyalty, proving that when politicians choose to diminish the importance of their predecessors, they often find their own political capital rapidly eroding in the process.
