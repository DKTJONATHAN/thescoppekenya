The Energy Petroleum Regulatory Authority (EPRA) has introduced three new charges to electricity bills effective April 2026, impacting countless households and businesses across the nation. These additions stem from foreign exchange rate fluctuations, water resource management levies, and the escalating cost of fuel for power generation.

## New Components Added to Electricity Bills

### Foreign Exchange Adjustment Reflects Market Movements

A Foreign Exchange Fluctuation Adjustment of Plus 123.41 Cents per kilowatt-hour has been applied to all meter readings taken in April 2026, as stated in a Gazette Notice released on April 24. This adjustment accounts for exchange gains and losses. KenGen recorded a gain of Ksh 14.26 million, Kenya Power posted Ksh 453.2 million, and Independent Power Producers (IPPs) contributed Ksh 874.78 million, calculated against 1.3 billion units generated in March 2026. The total exchange figures for March 2026 amounted to over Ksh 1.3 billion.

### Water Resource Levy for Hydropower

Consumers will also incur a Water Resource Management Authority (WRMA) levy of 1.54 cents per kilowatt-hour. This charge is directly tied to electricity purchased from hydropower plants with a capacity of at least one megawatt. Twelve hydropower stations, including Gitaru, Kamburu, Kiambere, Kindaruma, Masinga, Tana, Wanjii, Sagana, Turkwel, Gogo, Sondu Miriu, and Sangoro, collectively supplied 334.69 million units in March 2026 at an approved levy of 5 Kenya cents per kilowatt-hour.

### Fuel Costs Drive Significant Increase

The most substantial addition is the Fuel Energy Cost Charge (FECC), which adds an extra 347 Kenya cents per kilowatt-hour to bills for April 2026. This charge is calculated based on the actual fuel prices paid by power stations utilizing diesel, gas, and geothermal steam for electricity generation. EPRA Acting Director-General Dr. Eng. Joseph Oketch confirmed this charge in the gazette notice.

### Regional Disparities in Energy Costs

Off-grid areas are anticipated to experience the sharpest increase due to their reliance on diesel-powered stations, where fuel costs are considerably higher than in grid-connected regions powered by geothermal or hydropower. Turkana County records the highest diesel fuel price at Ksh 255.48 per kilogram, followed by Lamu County at Ksh 251.58, and Homa Bay at Ksh 238.24. Other high-cost locations include parts of Lamu County, Samburu, Mandera, Takaba, and Wajir, where trucking-in fuel inflates generation expenses. In contrast, areas connected to geothermal plants like Olkaria benefit from significantly lower steam charges, recorded at just Ksh 2.59 per kilogram, offering some insulation to central-grid towns such as Nairobi and Nakuru from the most severe fuel-related surcharges.

### Overall Impact on Consumers

The combined effect of these three charges represents a notable increase in the per-unit cost for consumers in April. The 347-cent fuel charge carries the heaviest weight, followed by the 123.41-cent foreign exchange adjustment, and the 1.54-cent water levy.
