## How many Premier League teams qualify for the 2026/27 Champions League?

For the 2026/27 season, the Premier League typically secures four places in the Champions League through league position. However, due to the new Swiss model and strong English performances in European competitions, it's highly probable that five Premier League teams will qualify, with a potential for six if a Europa League or Champions League winner finishes outside the traditional qualifying spots.

Look, under this "new-ish Swiss model" for the Champions League, we're talking about 36 teams now, not the classic 32. Two of those extra four spots are dished out to the countries with the best performances in European competitions from the previous season. Apparently, in six of the last eight seasons, England would have bagged an extra spot, and that was indeed the case for the 2024/25 season. So, early doors, we're looking at five Premier League clubs heading into the Champions League. That's Arsenal, Manchester City, Aston Villa, Chelsea, and Manchester United, according to the rundown. And then you've got Tottenham, who finished 17th but won the Europa League, effectively making six English teams in the Champions League! Talk about a lifeline!

But it doesn't stop there, does it? Oh no. Then we've got the Europa League and the Conference League. England gets two Europa League berths. One goes to the highest-placed team not already in the Champions League - Aston Villa snagged that last season. The other? That's for the FA Cup winners, unless they've already qualified through their league position or by winning a European trophy. Last season, Crystal Palace won the FA Cup, but then Nottingham Forest got involved because of some multi-club ownership shenanigans, eventually taking the spot. Honestly, trying to figure out these qualification rules is almost as convoluted as trying to untangle some of the major international financial dealings, like those big Eurobonds, eh? And the Carabao Cup winners? They get a ticket to the Europa Conference League play-offs. Newcastle United didn't need it last season because they were already in the Champions League, so it reverted to Nottingham Forest, who finished seventh. But then Forest got promoted to the Europa League, so it eventually landed with Crystal Palace. What a peculiar scenario! But the basic gist is, if the Carabao Cup winners are already in Europe, the spot goes to the next best-placed team in the Premier League. So, with Arsenal and Manchester City duking it out in the Carabao Cup final this season, we can expect that spot to revert to the league as well.

But, at the end of the day, it means more English teams in European competitions, which can only be a good thing for our standing on the continent.
