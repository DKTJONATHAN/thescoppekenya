Homa Bay Governor Gladys Wanga and Suba North MP Millie Odhiambo are embroiled in a bitter public dispute as of May 2026. The two senior ODM party figures have traded accusations regarding the use of organized smear campaigns to discredit political rivals within the party.

## The Growing Divide Between ODM Leaders

### Allegations of Organized Character Assassination
The conflict centers on claims that Governor Wanga has financed a network of writers tasked with silencing her political opponents. Millie Odhiambo contends that these individuals are not organic supporters but a structured unit focused on damaging the reputations of party members who challenge the gubernatorial office. This power struggle has effectively turned local party dynamics into a battlefield where internal unity has been sacrificed for the sake of personal dominance.

### The Hypocrisy of Political Alliances
Observers have highlighted the irony of this public fallout between two leaders who frequently promote sisterhood and collective progress. Despite their public rhetoric regarding women in leadership, both figures are now actively dismantling their professional relationship for political survival. This clash suggests that the mandate of public service is being sidelined in favor of controlling personal narratives, revealing a contradiction between the values these leaders claim to represent and the tactical smear campaigns they allegedly employ.

### Misplaced Priorities in Homa Bay
The infighting has drawn sharp criticism from residents concerned that local development remains stagnant while the political class prioritizes reputation management. Public concern is mounting over whether government resources are being diverted to fund these proxy conflicts rather than addressing essential infrastructure and services. As politicians engage in these ego-driven disputes, the electorate is increasingly demanding that their representatives shift focus from internal party skirmishes toward the urgent developmental needs of the region.
