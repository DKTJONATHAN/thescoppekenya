The National Treasury has received Ksh 103.45 billion following the successful partial sale of the Kenya Pipeline Company. This significant transfer of funds marks the conclusion of the country’s first electronic Initial Public Offering and signals a major shift in state ownership.

## Kenya Pipeline Privatisation Proceeds
The Privatisation Authority of Kenya officially handed over the proceeds to the Treasury today, marking a milestone in the government's efforts to divest from commercial state enterprises. This sale represents the first-ever electronic Initial Public Offering (eIPO) in the country, a move aimed at creating a more inclusive ownership structure for strategic national assets.

Cabinet Secretary John Mbadi accepted the dummy cheque, confirming that the total proceeds have been deposited into the National Infrastructure Fund account. The government sold a 65 per cent stake in the utility company, with the state retaining a 35 per cent share. Local investors purchased over 67 per cent of the available shares, while regional partners from Uganda and Rwanda also secured stakes in the firm.

### Strategic Shift and National Infrastructure Fund
The capital injection comes at a time when the energy sector is facing intense scrutiny. For instance, [Petroleum PS Liban Mohamed, KPC MD Joe Sang and EPRA DG Daniel Kiptoo Arrested Over Substandard Fuel Allegations](https://zandani.co.ke/article/petroleum-ps-liban-mohamed-kpc-md-joe-sang-and-epra-dg-daniel-kiptoo) recently highlighted the governance challenges within the industry. By transitioning to a more diverse ownership model, the government aims to enhance accountability and operational efficiency.

The National Infrastructure Fund will use these resources to finance large-scale projects intended to modernize the economy. A priority project includes the planned expansion and beautification of Jomo Kenyatta International Airport, which is scheduled to commence in June. This aligns with broader efforts to regulate the sector, such as when [EPRA shuts down illegal LPG storage facility in Kilifi over safety concerns](https://zandani.co.ke/article/epra-shuts-down-illegal-lpg-storage-facility-in-kilifi-over-safety-c) to ensure public safety and compliance.

### Revocation of State Entity Status
Following the conclusion of the partial sale, the Treasury officially stripped the company of its status as a national government entity. Through a gazette notice issued on Wednesday, April 22, CS Mbadi revoked the previous designation under the Public Finance Management Act. This legal change formally removes the company from the schedule of National Government Entities, allowing it to operate under a new corporate governance framework.

The Cabinet Secretary noted that the handover symbolises a commitment to transparency and the prudent management of public resources. The move is expected to provide the necessary fiscal space for the government to complete priority infrastructure projects while reducing the burden of state-owned enterprises on the exchequer.
