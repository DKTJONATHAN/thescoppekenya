## The Consequences of Transactional Lifestyles

The discovery of Sabrina Rianga’s body has focused intense scrutiny on the risks associated with transactional relationships among university students. In an environment where the pressure to maintain an image of luxury often eclipses academic focus, many students are entering high-stakes arrangements that promise immediate material gain but frequently carry grave personal consequences. This tragedy serves as a stark reminder that the pursuit of a lavish lifestyle often masks a dangerous undercurrent of exploitation.

### The Risks of Prioritizing Appearance

There is a growing critique of the cultural movement that encourages young women to prioritize superficial social status and expensive outings over building a sustainable career. This fixation on displaying a successful image creates a vulnerability to individuals who view financial support as a business investment rather than a relationship. When these unrealistic expectations of wealth collide with limited resources, the resulting dynamic is often volatile. These arrangements turn human connections into commercial transactions, where the fundamental safety of the individuals involved is frequently disregarded.

### Promoting Self-Reliance Over Shortcuts

This incident has ignited urgent calls for a shift in perspective across campus communities. Rather than seeking rapid wealth through influential or wealthy benefactors, there is a clear need for students to prioritize self-reliance and long-term planning. True stability is achieved through patience and education, not through the pursuit of instant, precarious fixes. The tragic conclusion of this student’s life underscores the necessity for young women to avoid dangerous dependencies and for men to understand that walking away is the only acceptable alternative when a relationship fails, rather than resorting to violence.

### The Need for Emotional Maturity

Violence is never an appropriate response to a failing relationship or a broken arrangement. The circumstances surrounding this case suggest a desperate need for improved conflict resolution and a complete rejection of the dangerous notion that individuals are commodities to be owned or discarded. As the community seeks justice, the message remains firm: the pursuit of vanity and the inability to handle rejection are costs that no one should ever have to pay with their life. If students continue to value the appearance of a fast life over their own fundamental safety, the danger of further tragedies remains high.
