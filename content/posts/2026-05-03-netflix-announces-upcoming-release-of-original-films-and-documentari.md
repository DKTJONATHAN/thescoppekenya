## Netflix's May 2026 Must-Sees: Star-Studded Dramas, Witty Comedies, and More!

Get ready to update your watch list because Netflix is bringing a fresh wave of captivating original films and documentaries this May 2026. From intimate celebrity portraits to thrilling horror and heartwarming tales, the streamer promises a month of diverse and unmissable entertainment.

### Martin Short: An Intimate Portrait

A new "intimate" documentary shines a spotlight on the legendary Martin Short, taking viewers beyond his acclaimed career. Known for iconic characters across *SCTV*, *Saturday Night Live*, and Comedy Central, and his current role in Hulu's *Only Murders in the Building*, this film delves into his personal life. It features never-before-seen home movies and candid interviews with collaborators like Steve Martin, Tom Hanks, John Mulaney, and the late Catherine O'Hara. The ongoing curiosity surrounding his "current relationship" with Meryl Streep also adds a fascinating layer to this portrait. Interestingly, reports from early 2026 suggested that Short and Streep had been quietly dating for nearly a year, despite previous denials of a romantic relationship.

### A Powerful Story of Female Friendship

Transport yourself to the Deep South with this powerful story about female friendship, and perhaps something more. This Oscar-nominated drama, set in the 1930s and 1980s, was a massive box office success upon its initial release. Jessica Tandy stars as Ninny, who enthralls Kathy Bates' lonely housewife with tales of Idgie Threadgoode (Mary Stuart Masterson) and Ruth Jamison (Mary-Louise Parker), two women who forge a deep bond. The film also features Chris O'Donnell and Cicely Tyson.

### Ouija: Origin of Evil

Before Mike Flanagan became Netflix's go-to for small-screen horror hits like *The Haunting of Hill House* and *Midnight Mass*, he directed this surprisingly strong prequel to 2014's *Ouija*. Set in 1967, *Origin of Evil* follows a fake fortune teller, played by Elizabeth Reaser, who unwittingly invites demons when she incorporates a Ouija board into her readings. Child actress Lulu Wilson delivers a particularly strong performance, with her character able to "scream, levitate, and emanate evil with a flair all her own". The cast also includes Lin Shaye, Henry Thomas, Sam Anderson, and Kate Siegel.

### Ollie: A Body-Swapping Animated Adventure

Oscar winner Michael B. Jordan leads the voice cast of this animated comedy, *Ollie*, which draws comparisons to *Freaky Friday*. Jordan voices Ollie, a small woodland creature who swaps bodies with the majestic bird that would typically hunt it. Director Nathan Greno shared in a recent interview that the film explores empathy, offering new perspectives on the world through Ollie's transformation. Juno Temple, Cedric the Entertainer, Justina Machado, and Tracy Morgan also lend their voices to this heartwarming tale. This film is also known as *Swapped*.

### Jonathan Glazer's Under the Skin

For fans of visionary cinema, Jonathan Glazer's *Under the Skin* arrives on Netflix. Known for his rare but impactful features, Glazer's 2013 film, based on Michel Faber's 2000 novel, is a beguiling exploration of violence, fear, seduction, empathy, and the human condition. Scarlett Johansson delivers an "understated" and transformative performance as a nameless extraterrestrial who harvests human men. Her portrayal shows the world through the eyes of someone experiencing it for the first time, evolving from a drone-like entity into a being that understands human emotion. Jeremy McWilliams and Adam Pearson also star.

### Remarkably Bright Creatures

Sally Field stars in this adaptation of Shelby Van Pelt's bestselling novel, *Remarkably Bright Creatures*. The film tells the heartwarming story of a widow who forms an unlikely connection with a younger, down-on-his-luck man and, unexpectedly, a giant Pacific octopus. Octopuses, as the source text reminds us, are indeed "remarkably bright creatures". Directed by Olivia Newman, who previously helmed 2022's *Where the Crawdads Sing*, the movie boasts an impressive cast including rising star Lewis Pullman and veterans like Colm Meaney, Beth Grant, and Joan Chen. The film is set to release on May 8, 2026.

### Ladies First: A Gender-Swapped Comedy

Sacha Baron Cohen takes the lead in *Ladies First*, an adaptation of the French film *I Am Not an Easy Man*. Cohen plays a womanizing cad who wakes up in a world where women hold all the power. This romantic comedy explores gender dynamics in the workplace, forcing Cohen's ad agency hotshot to experience the professional imbalances faced by his female colleagues. Rosamund Pike leads the supporting cast, which also includes the likes of Richard E. Grant, Emily Mortimer, Charles Dance, and Fiona Shaw. *Ladies First* is scheduled to stream on May 22, 2026.
