iPhone users are being cautioned against a sophisticated fraud scheme that targets individuals who have lost their devices. The scam employs meticulously crafted fake Apple websites to steal iCloud credentials, enabling fraudsters to bypass Apple's Activation Lock and effectively wipe stolen phones, thereby making them easily resalable in 2026.

Elaborate Scam Exploits Lost iPhone Owners

The Modus Operandi The scheme initiates when victims receive what appears to be a legitimate SMS from Apple Support, claiming an update on their device's lost status and a new location. This message includes a deceptive link designed to resemble an official Apple iCloud page. Unsuspecting users, often in a state of panic after losing their device, are then tricked into entering their Apple ID and password on this fraudulent site.

Bypassing Activation Lock Apple's Activation Lock typically renders a stolen or lost iPhone unusable without the owner's correct credentials, making it difficult for thieves to resell. However, fraudsters exploit this by waiting for the owner to mark the phone as lost. They then send the convincing SMS, urging immediate login. Once credentials are entered on the fake site, scammers gain full control, promptly logging into the authentic iCloud, removing the device from the owner's account, and wiping it clean. This action effectively disarms the Activation Lock, turning a previously unusable stolen phone into a clean, marketable device.

Expert Warning and Prevention Khalif Kairo, a prominent entrepreneur, has issued a direct warning to iPhone users regarding this escalating threat. He advises against accessing any unsolicited links claiming to be from Apple, even if the sender appears as 'apple' in messages. The critical indicator of fraud is a mismatched URL that does not belong to official Apple domains. Entering credentials on such sites provides scammers direct access to wipe the device. Users are strongly urged to verify URLs independently and only log into iCloud through official Apple channels. This sophisticated scheme underscores the critical importance of digital literacy and vigilance for all iPhone users in 2026.
