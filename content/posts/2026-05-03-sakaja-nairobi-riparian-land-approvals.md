## Sakaja Warns Officials Over Nairobi Riparian Land

Nairobi Governor Johnson Sakaja on Sunday, April 3, warned planning officers and surveyors against approving unlawful developments on Nairobi riparian land, vowing accountability amidst intensified flood mitigation efforts. Speaking at PCEA Evergreen Parish, Sakaja defended ongoing demolitions along waterways, part of a strategy to restore natural drainage and prevent loss of life and property across the city.

### Accountability for Unlawful Approvals
Governor Sakaja stressed that officials who approved structures on Nairobi riparian land and floodplains would face consequences, regardless of when the approvals were granted. He emphasized that planners and surveyors must adhere strictly to development regulations. The governor also cautioned those opposing the demolitions, stating that blocking waterways directly contributes to flood risks and subsequent complaints.

### Reclaiming Encroached Riparian Zones
Enforcement teams are actively reclaiming encroached riparian zones across Nairobi. Operations are ongoing in several areas, including Kirichwa Kubwa in Kilimani, as part of a broader crackdown. Sakaja confirmed that more structures built on Nairobi riparian land are being identified and removed as the rains continue, reiterating that the exercise will spare no one. This proactive approach by the county government aligns with broader governmental efforts to ensure public safety and proper infrastructure development, reminiscent of recent directives such as Kindiki directing KeNHA to build cycling lanes on Nyeri-Nyahururu Highway after a fatal accident. ### Call for Relocation in High-Risk Areas
Sakaja urged residents in flood-prone informal settlements, such as Grogan, to relocate immediately to prevent further loss of life. He highlighted that continued habitation in dangerous zones is a matter of safety, not politics, particularly as heavy rains persist across Nairobi and other parts of the country. The Kenya Meteorological Service Authority has issued continuous warnings regarding intensified heavy rainfall.
