## De Zerbi's Immediate Impact

This turnaround, so emphatic after De Zerbi's short tenure, has prompted questions about the squad's prior underperformance and whether this marks a definitive shift in their fight for survival.

### A Psychological Turning Point

The psychological impact of De Zerbi's leadership cannot be overstated. Following West Ham's 3-0 defeat to Brentford on Saturday, 2 May 2026, the door for Spurs was ajar. De Zerbi’s philosophy, articulated after last weekend’s crucial win at Wolves—"We have to die on the pitch. We have to play, we have to fight"—has instilled a fierce competitiveness previously absent. Self-belief, unity, quality, and sheer bloody-mindedness, concepts foreign to Spurs in this difficult campaign, now surge through the side. Moments like Bentancur’s fist-pumping celebration and Palhinha’s affectionate embrace from Kolo Muani highlighted a squad united and passionate.

De Zerbi’s men exploited this, demonstrating a relentless pressure from the opening seconds. Villa’s fans responded with fury at half-time and the final whistle, highlighting their club’s misprioritization.
