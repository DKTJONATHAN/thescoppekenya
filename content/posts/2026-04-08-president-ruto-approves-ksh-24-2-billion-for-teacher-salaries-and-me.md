President William Ruto, on Wednesday, April 8, 2026, approved the Supplementary Appropriation Bill at State House, releasing Ksh 24.2 billion to the Teachers Service Commission. This crucial funding will address existing salary shortfalls and ensure comprehensive medical cover for educators, easing long-standing financial pressures across the sector.

Significant Boost for Education Sector

Teacher Welfare Prioritised The approved Supplementary Budget specifically allocates Ksh 24.2 billion to the Teachers Service Commission (TSC) to bridge salary gaps and enhance health insurance for teachers. This financial intervention directly responds to years of concerns raised by educators regarding delayed benefits and inadequate remuneration. Additionally, the government has designated Ksh 3 billion to settle pending medical bills, reinforcing its commitment to improving welfare within the teaching profession.

Enhanced University Funding The education sector also benefits from substantial allocations directed towards higher learning institutions and student support. The Higher Education Loans Board (HELB) receives an additional Ksh 4.1 billion, elevating its total funding to Ksh 45.6 billion, crucial for student financing. To mitigate persistent industrial disputes, Ksh 3.88 billion has been set aside to clear university salary arrears stemming from the 2017-2021 Collective Bargaining Agreement. This funding also extends support to programmes like the Wings to Fly initiative via Technical and Vocational Education Training (TVET) institutions.

Institutional Stability Strengthened Further investments include Ksh 6 billion earmarked for higher education institutions, notably Moi University and Kabarak University. An allocation of Ksh 1.5 billion has also been directed to the University Funding Board, aimed at bolstering institutional stability across the higher education landscape.
