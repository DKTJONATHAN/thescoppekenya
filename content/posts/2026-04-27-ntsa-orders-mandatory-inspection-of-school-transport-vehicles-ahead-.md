## Heightened School Transport Safety Measures

### "Watoto Wafike Salama" Initiative Intensified

The NTSA’s latest directive is an integral component of the intensified "Watoto Wafike Salama" initiative, designed to guarantee the safe conveyance of learners as schools recommence. All school transport operators are required to present their vehicles for inspection to confirm mechanical soundness and safety for children.

### Multi-Agency Compliance Checks

Beyond the mandatory vehicle inspections, a multi-agency team will escalate strict road safety compliance checks nationwide. The NTSA urges all motorists to uphold high levels of responsibility while on the roads.

### Collective Responsibility for Child Safety

The authority has called upon parents, school administrations, and transport operators to collectively prioritize children's safety during this school reopening period. Ensuring secure travel for learners remains a shared critical objective.

### Fatal Crash Prompts Immediate Investigations

This comprehensive safety campaign follows a fatal road crash along the Narok-Mahi Mahiu road, where seven lives were lost instantly. The incident, occurring between Ntulele and Duka Moja, involved a trailer and a Toyota Voxy, with all occupants of the Voxy perishing at the scene. Police reports and witnesses indicate the trailer experienced brake failure before colliding with the Voxy.

The NTSA has confirmed its close collaboration with the National Police Service (NPS) to conduct a thorough investigation into the circumstances that led to the collision. Investigators are focused on determining the precise cause of the driver losing control of the vehicle.

### Directives Ahead of Term Two

The NTSA has recently issued a series of directives targeting drivers, particularly as learners begin their return to school for the second term from today, Monday, April 27, 2026. These measures aim to mitigate risks during this period of increased road activity.
