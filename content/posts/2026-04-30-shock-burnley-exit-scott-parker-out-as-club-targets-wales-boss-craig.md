Burnley Football Club faces a pivotal summer after confirming Scott Parker's departure by mutual consent, just eight days following their Premier League relegation. The club is actively seeking a new manager, with Wales boss Craig Bellamy emerging as a prominent candidate for the hot seat at Turf Moor. The search reflects a critical juncture for the Clarets as they prepare for a return to the Championship.

## Parker's Tumultuous Tenure Concludes

Scott Parker's two-year spell at Burnley concluded on April 22, 2026, marking his exit eight days after the club's relegation from the Premier League. Parker, who took charge in July 2024, initially delivered success, guiding the Clarets back to the top flight at the first attempt with a second-place finish in the 2024-25 Championship season. That season saw his side achieve an impressive 30 clean sheets in 46 league matches, a Championship record.

However, the 2025-26 Premier League campaign proved significantly more challenging. Burnley managed only four wins in 34 league games and secured just one point from their last eight matches, ultimately leading to their demotion following a 1-0 defeat to Manchester City. The team's struggles were compounded by conceding 68 goals in 34 league matches, the highest in the division. Their disappointing cup runs, including exits from the FA Cup and Carabao Cup to League One opposition, further underscored the challenging season.

## The Bellamy Enigma

Craig Bellamy, currently the Wales national team manager, is a serious contender for the vacant Burnley managerial position. Bellamy has prior experience at Turf Moor, having coached under former boss Vincent Kompany. He was also considered for the top job before Parker's appointment in 2024.

However, securing Bellamy presents significant hurdles for Burnley. He is reportedly content in his role with Wales, relishing the prospect of leading the nation to a home nations European Championship in 2028. Furthermore, Bellamy is understood to have reservations about the current composition of the Burnley squad and the extensive rebuilding required this summer, with numerous player departures anticipated. Financially, Burnley would also need to activate a release clause of at least £700,000 to extract Bellamy from his contract with the Football Association of Wales.

### Contenders and Complications

While Bellamy remains a primary target, other prominent names have surfaced in connection with the Burnley job. Steven Gerrard has been heavily linked, although no formal contact has been established. Sources indicate that the Clarets have also inquired about Cardiff head coach Brian Barry-Murphy.

In the immediate term, Burnley assistant manager Mike Jackson will assume control for the final four matches of the season, commencing with Friday's trip to Leeds United. The managerial decision carries immense weight for Burnley as they aim for a swift return to the Premier League, navigating a summer of potential squad overhaul and the significant task of reigniting their competitive edge in the Championship.
