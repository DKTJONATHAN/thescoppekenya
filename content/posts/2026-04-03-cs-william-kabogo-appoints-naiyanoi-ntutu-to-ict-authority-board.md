Information, Communications and the Digital Economy Cabinet Secretary William Kabogo has appointed Naiyanoi Ntutu Ng'eno, widow of the late Emurua Dikirr MP Johana Ng'eno, to the Information and Communication Technology Authority (ICTA) board. Her three-year term is effective from Thursday, April 2, 2026.

New Member Joins ICT Authority Board

Appointment Details and Mandate Cabinet Secretary William Kabogo formalized the appointment of Naiyanoi Ntutu Ng'eno via a gazette notice published on Thursday, April 2, 2026. Her tenure on the ICTA board is set for three years, commencing on the date of the notice. Ms. Ntutu Ng'eno is tasked with contributing to the oversight of the authority's operations, which are pivotal in advancing the nation's digital economy and e-government services. This forms part of a larger national strategy to enhance efficiency and public services, complementing efforts in other sectors such as trade facilitation [KRA to Roll Out EAC Customs Bond to Simplify Transit](https://zandani.co.ke/article/kra-to-roll-out-eac-customs-bond-to-simplify-transit). ICTA Board Chairperson Lily Ng'ok Kirui welcomed the new appointee, expressing confidence in her capabilities. In a message shared on social media, Ms. Kirui stated, "Welcome to the Information and Communication Technology Authority Board, Naiyanoi Ntutu Ng'eno. I am looking forward to working closely with you. I have no doubt in your abilities and the contribution you will make to the team. Together, we will fulfil our collective mandate."

Transition from Political Ambition Naiyanoi Ntutu Ng'eno first entered public consciousness following the death of her husband, Emurua Dikirr MP Johana Ng'eno, who tragically died in a helicopter crash on February 28, 2026. The incident, which also claimed five other lives, garnered widespread national attention. Following her husband's passing, Ms. Ntutu Ng'eno initially declared her intention to contest the vacant Emurua Dikirr parliamentary seat. However, she subsequently withdrew from the electoral race, citing personal reasons after consultations with her family. This decision marked a shift from direct political engagement towards public service, culminating in her current appointment to the ICT Authority.
