## President Tinubu Confirms Nairobi Visit for Africa-France Summit

Nigerian President Bola Tinubu will arrive in Nairobi, Kenya, in the coming days to attend the Africa-France Summit starting May 11. This significant Bola Tinubu Nairobi visit is part of a diplomatic tour, following recent public exchanges with President William Ruto that sparked online debate between the two nations. President Tinubu will depart Abuja today, May 2, 2026, first travelling to France before arriving in Kenya for the summit.

### Summit Agenda and Economic Focus

The Africa-France Summit, co-chaired by French President Emmanuel Macron and President William Ruto, is scheduled to begin on May 11 in Nairobi. African leaders and French officials will convene to discuss critical continental priorities, including energy transition, green industrialisation, digital transformation, and reforms in global financing systems. The summit, themed “Africa Forward: Africa-France Partnerships for Innovation and Growth,” will also address climate action, infrastructure development, youth empowerment, technological advancement, and peace-building initiatives across Africa.

President Tinubu is expected to leverage the Bola Tinubu Nairobi visit platform to promote Nigeria’s economic reform agenda and highlight investment opportunities. He will also hold bilateral meetings with global and African business leaders on the sidelines of the summit, according to a Nigerian publication. As African economies seek growth, discussions on financial markets and investment remain key, as seen in reports such as the rise in NSE futures margins. After concluding his engagements in Kenya on May 12, President Tinubu will proceed to Kigali, Rwanda, to participate in the Africa CEO Forum between May 14 and 15, where over 2,000 business leaders and policymakers will deliberate on strategies for accelerating intra-African trade and industrial growth.

### Addressing Diplomatic Tensions

The Nigerian President's visit occurs against the backdrop of recent public exchanges with President William Ruto that generated widespread online reactions. These exchanges followed President Tinubu's earlier comments suggesting Nigerians were "better off than Kenyans" amidst fuel price pressures, remarks that drew strong criticism from Kenyan social media users. The situation escalated after President Ruto's remarks during a diaspora engagement in Italy, where he commented that Nigerian-accented English could be difficult to understand, sparking widespread condemnation in Nigeria and beyond.

Online reactions fueled a wave of cyber exchanges between Kenyan and Nigerian users, with debates focusing on economic conditions, language, and national pride, intensifying a digital rivalry. However, President Ruto later sought to downplay the controversy during a mining conference in Nairobi, stating his remarks were misinterpreted and intended as light comparisons about English proficiency across Africa. He extended greetings to President Tinubu, striking a conciliatory tone and referencing Kenya’s close ties with Nigeria and personal family connections, in an effort to ease tensions ahead of the Bola Tinubu Nairobi visit.
