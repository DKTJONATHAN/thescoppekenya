Kenyan authorities arrested Bare Sahara Ahmed over a viral video featuring alleged inflammatory remarks. Despite public speculation of a quick release, the Directorate of Criminal Investigations (DCI) confirmed she remains in custody and is scheduled to appear in court today, April 27, 2026, to face charges.

## Bare Sahara Ahmed Remains in Custody, Court Appearance Today

### The Controversial Remarks That Led to Arrest
The drama unfolded after a video featuring Bare Sahara Ahmed went viral across the country, showing her allegedly making deeply divisive comments. Among the remarks attributed to her were inflammatory statements referring to non-Muslim police officers as 'nywele ngumu'-a derogatory term literally meaning 'hard hair'-and asserting that Garissa belongs exclusively to Somalis. She reportedly further escalated the situation by threatening action if authorities did not address an alleged shooting incident involving a Somali driver. These comments quickly drew the attention of law enforcement, prompting swift action from the DCI, who apprehended her on Sunday, April 26.

### Whispers of Leniency Quashed by DCI
Following her arrest, a wave of rumors began to sweep through Kenyan circles, suggesting that Bare Sahara Ahmed had been released barely hours after being taken into custody and would not face charges. This public speculation often reflects a broader frustration among Kenyans who perceive a pattern where individuals accused of hate speech and incitement might evade serious accountability, raising questions about the fairness and effectiveness of the justice system. However, the DCI moved quickly to quell these rumors, clarifying that, contrary to popular belief, Ahmed was not granted a swift release. The DCI's firm stance emphasizes that legal processes are fully in motion, a significant departure from scenarios where court appeals might be dismissed on technicalities.

### Court Appearance Scheduled for Today
The DCI explicitly confirmed that Bare Sahara Ahmed remains in custody, countering any notion of a lenient approach or premature freedom. Authorities stated unequivocally that she is scheduled to appear before the Kahawa Law Courts today, April 27, 2026. This unwavering commitment by the DCI aims to reassure the public that those who threaten national cohesion and incite violence will be held to account, reinforcing the importance of maintaining peace and order across the nation. The outcome of today's court proceedings is highly anticipated.
