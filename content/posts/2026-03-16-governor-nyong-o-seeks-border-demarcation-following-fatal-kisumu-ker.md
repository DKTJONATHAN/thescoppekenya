Governor Anyang Nyong’o has formally requested the Ministry of Lands to urgently demarcate the Kisumu-Kericho border following fatal clashes on Sunday, March 15, at Muhoroni Junction. One person died amidst escalating land tensions, prompting the governor's immediate intervention to resolve the long-standing dispute.

Urgent Border Demarcation Sought

Escalating Tensions and Fatalities The recent skirmishes at Muhoroni Junction, occurring on Sunday, March 15, resulted in one fatality and severe property damage. A sugar plantation was reportedly torched, and several motor vehicles were set ablaze during the fracas. Governor Nyong’o attributed the heightened conflict to the absence of clear physical demarcation of the disputed land, which has continuously fueled tensions between communities in the border area.

Governor's Call for Ministry Intervention In response to the escalating unrest, Governor Nyong’o issued a statement on Monday, March 16, confirming he had sent a reminder letter to the national government’s Ministry of Lands. He urged immediate technical support for the Koguta Clan, specifying the need for expedited land surveys, beaconing, and facilitated subdivision in line with the National Land Commission (NLC) directive. The governor also emphasized the orderly settlement of rightful beneficiaries, stating that citizen rights must not be trampled by intimidation or extra-judicial action. The contested land was previously allocated to the clan by the NLC and subsequently affirmed by the High Court of Kenya.

Recurrent Disputes and Failed Resolutions The land dispute along the Kisumu-Kericho border has persisted for years, with claims of ownership and settlement rights frequently flaring up in the agriculturally rich zone near Muhoroni. This region, vital to the sugar industry, makes the land particularly valuable for cane farming. Despite collective efforts in 2025, where Governor Nyong’o and Kericho Governor Erick Mutai, with assistance from the National Cohesion and Integration Commission (NCIC), declared an end to border disputes, tensions have regrettably resurfaced. The Muhoroni area is not isolated; the Sondu region, also bordering the two counties, has historically reported clashes, often triggered by cattle rustling leading to loss of lives.
