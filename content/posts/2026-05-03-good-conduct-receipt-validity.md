## Good Conduct Certificate Payment Validity Confirmed

Huduma Kenya has confirmed that original payments for a Good Conduct Certificate remain valid for up to one year from the date of payment for applicants who missed fingerprint capture. This clarification, issued recently on X (formerly Twitter) following a citizen's social media inquiry, addresses common concerns regarding payment validity when the fingerprint recording step is delayed.

### Payment Validity Details

The agency clarified that individuals who paid for a Certificate of Good Conduct but did not proceed with fingerprint recording can still use their original payment, provided less than one year has passed since the payment date. This means receipts remain valid for up to 12 months from the date of payment if the fingerprint capture has not yet been completed. Consequently, applicants whose payments are more than one year old will be required to make a fresh payment before undergoing fingerprint recording.

### Application Process and Cost

The service for a Good Conduct Certificate currently costs Ksh 1,050. The application process begins on the eCitizen portal, where an appointment is made. Applicants then visit any Huduma Centre across the country, which host Directorate of Criminal Investigations (DCI) desks, to complete the fingerprint capture process on working days. Once processed, the certificate is usually issued within two weeks, and applicants can log in to eCitizen to download it.

The requirement to complete payments via the eCitizen portal and download the certificate online reflects the ongoing digital transformation of government services.
