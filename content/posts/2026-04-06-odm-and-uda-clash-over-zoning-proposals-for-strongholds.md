The Orange Democratic Movement (ODM) and the United Democratic Alliance (UDA) have clashed over zoning ahead of the 2027 general elections. ODM leaders, speaking in Kisumu on Sunday, April 5, 2026, insisted on zoning strongholds as a precondition for any pre-election agreement.

Zoning Standoff Ahead of 2027 Elections The two dominant political parties appear to hold contrasting positions regarding pre-election negotiations for the upcoming 2027 general elections. The UDA has indicated its intention to field candidates across the nation, while ODM firmly advocates for zoning, particularly within areas traditionally considered its strongholds.

ODM Demands Stronghold Respect ODM leaders, spearheaded by party chairperson Gladys Wanga, made their stance unequivocally clear in Kisumu. They asserted that zoning would constitute a fundamental requirement for any pre-election understanding with President William Ruto's UDA party. Wanga emphasized that ODM approaches any negotiations as an equal partner, not from a position of weakness. Other ODM Members of Parliament echoed this sentiment, stating they would not permit UDA to field candidates in their established strongholds. Kisumu County woman representative Ruth Odinga underscored the party's strength, declaring, "And we are not going to sing songs of praise to anybody if they are not going to know that on that table, ODM is very strong, and ODM can feed everybody everywhere." Alego Usonga MP Samuel Atandi further cemented this position, stating there would be no room for negotiation on zoning. He affirmed that UDA must acknowledge and respect ODM's traditional bases, specifically citing Muhoroni as an area reserved solely for ODM candidates. This firm stance aligns with recent calls for an "irresistible offer" to ODM from the ruling coalition, a sentiment previously echoed by Siaya Governor James Orengo, suggesting a broader push for political leverage.

UDA Signals National Ambition Conversely, UDA's approach, indicating a nationwide fielding of candidates, suggests a strategy to broaden its footprint across the country, potentially challenging the traditional stronghold dynamics upheld by ODM. This divergence sets the stage for potentially contentious pre-election discussions as both parties aim to maximize their influence ahead of the 2027 polls.
