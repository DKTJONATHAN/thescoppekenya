When news broke that Dedan Kimani, father of the late journalist Kimani Mbugua, was being held at Nairobi West Prison, the digital streets reacted with predictable fervor. Supporters, led by YouTuber Vickie Vickinah, have called for legal intervention, framing his incarceration as an injustice. However, the charge sheet is specific. Under Section 300 of the Penal Code of Kenya, extortion is a serious felony requiring clear evidence of threat or coercion to obtain property or money.

The state claims that the 2.5 million KSh in question was not a request for assistance but a demand backed by illicit pressure. While the court of public opinion focuses on the tragedy of Kimani Mbugua’s death in Mombasa last October, the criminal court is interested in the forensic audit of these alleged transactions. Kenyan law does not distinguish between a parent and a stranger when the charge is extortion; it relies solely on the presence of mens rea, or criminal intent, coupled with the act of demanding money through unlawful means.
