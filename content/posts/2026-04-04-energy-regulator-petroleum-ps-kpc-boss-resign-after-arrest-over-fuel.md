Energy and Petroleum Regulatory Authority (EPRA) Director General Daniel Kiptoo, Kenya Pipeline Company (KPC) Managing Director Joe Sang, and Petroleum Principal Secretary Mohamed Liban have all resigned following allegations on a fuel importation deal. President William Ruto announced their resignations today, April 4, 2026, amidst an ongoing investigation into the distribution of substandard fuel.

Energy Sector Leaders Resign Amid Fuel Scandal

Presidential Office Confirms Exits The Office of the President confirmed the resignations via a letter dated April 4, 2026, and signed by the Head of Public Service and Chief of Staff, Felix Koskei. The communication stated that President Ruto accepted the exits of Mr. Liban, Mr. Sang, and Mr. Kiptoo Bargoria, which followed decisions by their respective boards.

Disciplinary Actions Initiated Concurrently, the Chief of Staff confirmed that the State Department for Petroleum and KPC have initiated disciplinary proceedings. These actions target Deputy Director Joseph Wafula and Supply and Logistics Manager Joel Mburu as part of ongoing administrative measures related to the allegations.

Fuel Importation Controversy Deepens The resignations follow the arrest of four senior officials by the Directorate of Criminal Investigations (DCI) over allegations of procuring and facilitating the distribution of substandard fuel. Detectives suspect a consignment aboard the vessel MV Paloma, initially destined for Angola, was diverted to the Port of Mombasa under unclear circumstances before being released into local supply chains. Preliminary findings suggest this shipment, reportedly originating from Saudi Aramco, may have been overpriced by over Ksh4 billion and failed to meet quality standards, raising concerns over potential losses and risks to consumers. The vessel MV Paloma is believed to have docked in Mombasa between March 27 and March 29, 2026.

Government Assurances and Future Measures The government has assured thorough investigations, with those found guilty facing charges under the economic crimes framework. Measures will also be taken to correct irregular fuel shipment requisitions, ensuring alignment with the contracted government-to-government (G-to-G) framework. Further updates will be provided as investigations progress, including administrative actions to ensure full accountability.
