## Afrobeats Politics: Governor Oborevwori's Musical Allegiance Delta State Governor Sheriff Francis Orowhedor Oborevwori recently sparked lively debate across Nigerian social media by choosing Davido over Wizkid in a lighthearted game. This preference, shared via the state's official X account, predictably ignited passionate responses from fans and underscored Davido's established political visibility within Nigeria's government circles.

The Enduring Afrobeats Rivalry The "Davido or Wizkid" question has been a central point of discussion in Nigerian pop culture for nearly a decade, drawing in musicians, celebrities, and now, politicians. This spirited rivalry transcends mere musical preference, reflecting a deep cultural engagement with Afrobeats and its leading figures. The public nature of Governor Oborevwori's selection highlights how deeply ingrained this debate is, even reaching figures in governance.

Politics Meets Pop Culture Governor Oborevwori's choice of Davido was, for many, an expected outcome, given Davido's prominent engagement in Nigeria's political landscape. His consistent presence at political events, rallies, and inaugurations, notably influenced by his uncle, Osun State Governor Ademola Adeleke, has established Davido as a familiar face within government circles. This level of political visibility is a distinct characteristic among Nigeria's top artists, setting him apart from peers like Wizkid or Burna Boy.

Humanising Governance Through Social Media The Delta State Government's interactive "This or That" post exemplifies a wider evolution in how Nigerian state governments utilise social media. Moving beyond rigid official statements, these platforms are now employed to humanise leaders and foster connections with younger demographics. Governor Oborevwori's administration, for instance, has actively engaged on this front, showcasing initiatives like the inspection of the College of Health Technology in Ovrode to address skill shortages and publicly affirming commitment to expanding opportunities for youth and women across Delta State. Such strategies aim to build rapport and demonstrate relevance to a digitally savvy populace, much like how young artists such as Lil Maina captivate teenagers and Gen Z fans across Kenya. This digital pivot is crucial for modern engagement, paralleling efforts seen in digital learning initiatives across Kenya's frontier counties.
