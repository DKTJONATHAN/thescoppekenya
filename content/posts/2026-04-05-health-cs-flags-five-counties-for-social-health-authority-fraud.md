Health Cabinet Secretary Aden Duale identified Homa Bay, Bungoma, Mandera, Wajir, and Kisii counties for widespread Social Health Authority fraud during a statement in Garissa on Saturday, April 4, intensifying the government's crackdown on suspicious medical claims from private facilities.

Intensified Scrutiny on Health Claims

Counties Under Audit CS Duale specifically highlighted Homa Bay, Bungoma, Mandera, Wajir, and Kisii as the counties recording the highest number of suspicious medical claims. These claims are predominantly linked to privately owned health facilities. The five flagged counties are now subject to increased scrutiny, with auditors focusing on abnormal billing patterns and repeated claims.

Nationwide Crackdown Expands Since March 30, at least 12 facilities have been shut down as authorities move to investigate fraud that has cost the public billions. Additionally, 24 other facilities are currently undergoing forensic audits, while 250 more are under investigation by the Directorate of Criminal Investigations. So far, 30 case files have been forwarded to the Office of the Director of Public Prosecutions, and 18 facilities are now facing legal proceedings. In total, over 1,000 facilities have been closed. Za Ndani investigations have established that some hospitals inflated claims or billed for services not rendered in an attempt to siphon funds from the Social Health Authority system. There are also suspicions of facilities collaborating with insiders within the Authority to process these false claims. The government's concerted efforts to curb fraud in the Social Health Authority align with a broader national agenda to enhance accountability and regulatory compliance across various public-facing sectors, a commitment also evident in detailed explanations of procedures such as the Kenya Revenue Authority's luggage screening procedures and regulations for arriving passengers.

Identified Facilities and Measures The crackdown, which commenced in late March, has impacted several healthcare providers. Among these is Bungoma West Hospital, which was suspended from the Social Health Authority provided panel on March 30 for 90 days over suspected fraudulent claims. Other facilities flagged include Baypharm Medical Centre in Homa Bay, Dawida Annex Hospital Ltd in Taita Taveta, Kerugoya Medical Centre in Kirinyaga, Raycos Health Centre in Mombasa, Calvary Hope Medical Centre in Bungoma, Shamberere Dispensary in Kakamega, Kurafa Medical Centre in Kiambu, Umoja Three Medical Centre in Nairobi, and The Pro Elite Westlands General and Specialist Hospital Ltd in Nairobi.
