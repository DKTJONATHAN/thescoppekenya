## Equity Bank's Digital Leap Redefines Kenyan Banking

Equity Bank's FY2025 results highlight a profound shift in customer banking behavior, with 98.2% of transactions now occurring outside physical branches. Digital channels alone processed 88.4% of all transactions, reflecting a consistent growth trajectory from 77.8% in FY2018 and substantial investment in customer-centric digital infrastructure.

### Digital Channels Drive Transaction Volumes

Equitel, launched in 2014, leads Equity Bank's digital channels, recording a 30% year-on-year growth to 400.9 million transactions in FY2205. This platform primarily facilitates low-value transactions. Pay With Equity (PWE), designed for merchant payments across major mobile money platforms, processed 403.1 million transactions, marking a 16% year-over-year increase. Equity Mobile and USSD channels together contributed 174.3 million transactions, an increase of 17%, and generated Kshs 4.36 trillion in transaction value, a 37% jump from the previous year.

### The Specialized Role of Physical Branches

While digital platforms dominate transaction volumes, physical branches maintain a crucial role in high-value transactions. In FY2025, branches processed 19.5 million transactions with a significant value of Kshs 4.96 trillion, nearly matching the entire Equity Mobile and USSD channel's value. The average branch transaction is approximately Kshs 254,523, vastly exceeding Equitel's Kshs 3,577 and PWE's Kshs 11,313. This indicates that one branch visit equates to about 71 transactions on Equitel. Despite a decline in both volume and value compared to FY2024, branches have not become obsolete; instead, they have specialized in handling corporate deposits, loan drawdowns, and complex transactions that customers prefer or need to complete in person.

### Innovation Amidst Diverse National Landscapes

The sustained growth in digital transaction adoption by Equity Bank underscores the increasing reliance on technological solutions within Kenya's financial sector. This evolution toward digital self-care platforms and agency banking for routine services positions physical branches as specialized hubs for intricate financial needs. This digital transformation underscores Kenya's evolving technological landscape, a narrative unfolding amidst diverse national developments, from financial innovation to ongoing challenges in sectors like sports.
