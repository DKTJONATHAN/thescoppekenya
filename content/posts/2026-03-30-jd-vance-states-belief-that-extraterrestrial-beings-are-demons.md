## White House Alien Buzz: Demons, UFOs, and Area 51

U.S. Vice President JD Vance has dropped a bombshell, claiming the "aliens" he's so eager to investigate at Area 51 are actually demons, not E.T. This wild take joins a chorus of high-profile figures, from Barack Obama to Whoopi Goldberg, debating what truly lurks beyond our skies and political corridors.

### Vice President Vance's Demonic Obsession

JD Vance, currently the U.S. Vice President, has admitted to being "obsessed with the UFO files" since taking office. Speaking on conservative podcaster Benny Johnson's show on Friday, Vance revealed his repeated but thwarted attempts to visit Area 51 and New Mexico to "get to the bottom of this." He hasn't even had a "peek" at the classified documents related to "alien and extraterrestrial life" that President Donald Trump called to release in February, citing demands from the economy and national security.

However, Vance's curiosity takes an unexpected turn. He doesn't believe they are aliens at all, but rather "demons." He explained that he views "celestial beings, who fly around and do weird things to people" through a Christian understanding, suggesting that "every great world religion... has understood that there are weird things out there." Vance, paraphrasing Verbal Kint from *The Usual Suspects*, believes one of the devil's greatest tricks is convincing people he doesn't exist. Despite admitting he hasn't spent enough time on the subject, he vowed, "I've still got three more years as vice president. I will get to the bottom the UFO files."

### From Presidents to EGOT Winners: The Alien Debate

Vance isn't the only public figure weighing in on extraterrestrial life. Just days before Trump's call for declassification, his predecessor, Barack Obama, created a stir by claiming aliens are "real, but I haven't seen them" on a podcast. He quickly clarified, stating, "I saw no evidence during my presidency that extraterrestrials have made contact with us. Really!"

Adding to the celebrity chatter, EGOT-winner Whoopi Goldberg emphatically claimed on a recent episode of *The View* that aliens are "already here... They've been here for quite some time." Her pronouncement carried extra weight, given her iconic role on *Star Trek*.

### Spielberg's "Disclosure Day" Ready for Landing

Perhaps this surge in alien discourse is all a sophisticated promotional campaign for *Disclosure Day*, E.T. director Steven Spielberg's much-anticipated return to the subject of little green men. The first teaser for the film, starring Emily Blunt and Josh O'Connor, dropped in December, setting the stage for its global release on June 12, 2026. Whether it's demons, real aliens, or just brilliant marketing, the conversation about what lies beyond our world is certainly taking off.
