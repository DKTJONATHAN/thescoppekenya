Former Nairobi Governor Mike Sonko orchestrated the arrest of Elvis Opiyo, the primary suspect in the acid attack on gospel singer Claire-Mary, through a deceptive tactic. Sonko lured Opiyo to Nairobi on March 22, 2026, leading to his apprehension as Claire-Mary recovers in Kenyatta National Hospital following the March 17 assault.

Arrest Follows Calculated Ruse Mike Sonko detailed on March 24, 2026, how he successfully orchestrated the arrest of Elvis Opiyo. Following a visit to Claire-Mary at Kenyatta National Hospital, Sonko revealed his plan to trick Opiyo into traveling to Nairobi, which culminated in the arrest on Sunday, March 22. Sonko stated, "We tricked him by lying to him to come to Nairobi, and that’s how we made the arrest. So, justice will be served."

Suspect's Alleged NIS Affiliation Addressed During his hospital interaction with Claire-Mary, it was disclosed that Elvis Opiyo allegedly works for the National Intelligence Service (NIS). Despite this claim, Sonko assured Claire-Mary that Opiyo is not exempt from the law. "Elvis Opiyo says that he works for the NIS. Even if so, he is not above the law; no one sent him to come and burn you with acid," Sonko reaffirmed, vowing that the matter would be handled. Individuals in civil service roles, including those in intelligence, are subject to the nation's legal framework, and avenues like the [[HELB invites applications for civil servants training revolving fund]](https://zandani.co.ke/article/helb-invites-applications-for-civil-servants-training-revolving-fund) exist for their professional development, not for impunity.

Details of the Acid Attack Kenyan gospel singer Claire-Mary, a member of the Redemption Ministers SDA choir, suffered severe facial and body injuries after being ambushed near Prestige Plaza on Ngong Road, Nairobi, on March 17, 2026. She was doused with a corrosive substance in an incident captured by surveillance cameras. Claire-Mary remains in critical condition, receiving specialized treatment at Kenyatta National Hospital.

Legal Proceedings and Past Reports Elvis Opiyo, identified as Claire-Mary's estranged former partner, was arraigned at Kibera Law Courts on March 23, 2026. He is accused of orchestrating the acid attack by hiring an assailant, allegedly after Claire-Mary ended their eight-year relationship. Investigations indicate that Claire-Mary had previously reported Opiyo to the police in November 2025 for assault and death threats. Reports also link Opiyo to another assault dating back to November 25, 2025. Sonko has pledged to facilitate expert legal counsel and cover both Claire-Mary's legal fees and hospital bills for the ongoing case.
