Wiper Democratic Movement leader Kalonzo Musyoka expressed sharp disapproval over the extensive police presence surrounding former Cabinet Secretary Raphael Tuju at a Nairobi hospital on Tuesday, March 24, 2026. The former Vice President condemned the deployment, citing it as an extravagant use of public funds and a disrespectful intrusion on Tuju's privacy.

Tuju's Hospital Guard Detail Under Fire

Taxpayer Burden and Dignity Concerns During his visit to the medical facility, Kalonzo Musyoka voiced strong objections to the assignment of three police officers to guard Tuju’s hospital room. He characterized the deployment as a clear “waste of taxpayers’ resources,” suggesting that such security assets could be better utilized across the country. The opposition leader emphasized that the heavy security infringed upon the dignity of a veteran politician recovering from health challenges.

Officers' Plight and Dr. Gikonyo's Generosity Kalonzo painted a vivid picture of the conditions faced by the officers on duty, claiming they were forced to rely on the hospital management for sustenance. "What is the need for spending taxpayers’ money keeping three policemen here?" Kalonzo questioned, highlighting that the officers were "very hungry" and eating "food meant for patients" thanks to Doctor Gikonyo's kindness. This revelation adds a layer of human interest to the political drama.

Tuju: Statesman or 'Terrorist'? The Wiper leader vehemently criticized the treatment of Raphael Tuju, who served in the cabinets of both Mwai Kibaki and Uhuru Kenyatta. Kalonzo argued that Tuju was being handled like a "terrorist," despite his established constitutional rights. "Tuju is not a terrorist. The way he was handled yesterday in our presence showed nothing short of a terrorist attack on a Kenyan," Kalonzo declared. He linked this heightened security to Tuju’s ongoing legal and personal battles concerning his property, vowing opposition support to protect him from "vicious vultures" eyeing his assets.

Political Chessboard: Ruto's Remarks and Opposition's Stance Kalonzo Musyoka's statement served as a direct challenge to President William Ruto, who had earlier suggested Tuju needed "honest friends" during his current predicament. The Wiper leader urged the President to demonstrate genuine friendship by ordering the immediate withdrawal of the police officers. Kalonzo maintained that opposition figures rallying around Tuju were his true allies, presenting their solidarity as a stand against perceived state-led intimidation. The political theatre surrounding Tuju's hospitalization remains a hot topic, especially as President Ruto continues to make key appointments, such as those detailed in the recent gazette notice concerning [Ruto Appoints Ida Odinga to UNEP, Francis Meja as PSC Chair in Sweeping Gazette Notice](https://zandani.co.ke/article/ruto-appoints-ida-odinga-to-unep-francis-meja-as-psc-chair). As of Wednesday morning, the heavy security detail remains at the facility, with no official government response regarding the officers' welfare.
