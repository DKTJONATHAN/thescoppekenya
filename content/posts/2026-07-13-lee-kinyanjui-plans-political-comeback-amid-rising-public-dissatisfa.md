Former Nakuru Governor Lee Kinyanjui is actively preparing for a political comeback ahead of the 2027 general election. His potential return is fueled by widespread public dissatisfaction with the current county administration regarding failing sanitation services and deteriorating waste management standards across Nakuru.

## The Shift in Nakuru Political Sentiment

### Deteriorating Urban Sanitation
Nakuru, previously recognized for its cleanliness and orderly environment, is now facing a significant waste management crisis. Piles of uncollected garbage and overflowing disposal bins have become common features on city streets, raising serious public health concerns among residents. Many local observers are now highlighting the contrast between the current state of the city and the maintenance standards achieved during the tenure of the former governor. As the refuse continues to accumulate, citizens are increasingly viewing the past administration as a period of higher operational competence.

### Public Discontent as a Catalyst
The growing frustration among residents has shifted the focus of local politics toward service delivery rather than party affiliation. While national fiscal concerns frequently dominate the news cycle, voters in Nakuru are prioritizing the immediate quality of their living environment. Analysts suggest that the upcoming election cycle may serve as a direct referendum on the current administration's inability to maintain basic city hygiene. For the electorate, the demand for improved sanitation has become a decisive issue that threatens to displace the current leadership if local infrastructure remains neglected.

### Evaluating Administrative Records
The prevailing public mood reflects a sense of nostalgia for the strategies utilized during the previous government. This sentiment is effectively positioning Kinyanjui as a viable alternative for voters who feel that the city's standards have declined under the current regime. Every failure in waste collection is intensifying scrutiny of the incumbent governor, as the electorate compares present management to past performance. Consequently, the former governor is gaining momentum by simply being the benchmark against which the current leadership is failing to measure up. The message from the public remains firm: if the city does not see a drastic improvement in cleanliness, the voters are prepared to seek a change in leadership.
