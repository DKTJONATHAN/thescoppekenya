## KNH Digital System Delays Confirmed, Patients Affected

Kenyatta National Hospital (KNH) on Sunday, May 3, confirmed delays with its new digital system for patient data capture, leaving many patients stranded. The hospital attributed the issues to minor teething problems during its transition from a manual system, dismissing reports of a complete collapse while assuring patient care was not compromised.

### KNH Addresses System Challenges

KNH's statement clarified that challenges experienced by the KNH digital system were minor teething problems common in large-scale digital transitions, impacting patient discharges and billing. While acknowledging temporary service delays, particularly in clearance processes, the hospital firmly stated that patient care and safety were never compromised. "The system has not collapsed; it remains operational and is stabilising as deployment continues," KNH affirmed, regretting any inconvenience caused to patients and their families. ### Transition to Afya Apex

The hospital is transitioning from a manual approach to a fully digitised KNH digital system known as Afya Apex, designed to automate and integrate clinical and administrative services across all departments. The rollout commenced on April 15 and is slated for completion within 90 days. KNH highlighted that its operations have relied heavily on paper-based records for over 125 years, a method now deemed outdated and incompatible with modern healthcare delivery standards.

### Call for Support and Warning Against Resistance

KNH called upon patients and stakeholders to support this transformative shift, describing it as crucial for enhancing efficiency, transparency, and the overall quality of care. The hospital, however, issued a stern warning against any resistance to the digitisation process, whether from internal staff or external parties, asserting that such actions would be dealt with firmly as it progresses with these essential reforms.
