The Ministry of Interior, led by Cabinet Secretary Kipchumba Murkomen, has officially designated sections of Laikipia, Meru, and Isiolo counties as security-disturbed and dangerous. This declaration, made in a special gazette notice on Thursday, March 26, 2026, facilitates a major security operation to dismantle criminal elements in the affected regions.

Major Security Operation Underway The declaration by Interior Cabinet Secretary Kipchumba Murkomen will take effect on Thursday, March 26, 2026, at 6 PM, for an initial period of 30 days. This period may be extended or withdrawn earlier based on the evolving security situation in the counties. Murkomen stated that the measure is intended to "weed out perpetrators of recent incidents of criminal activities in the areas" and involves a mop-up of illegal firearms. This operation follows an earlier deployment of 400 additional police officers to reinforce those already on the ground.

Targeted Areas in the Declaration Specific regions within the three counties have been identified as disturbed zones. In Laikipia County, the affected areas include parts of Laikipia North, such as Mugogo Forest, Mugogodo, Sieku, Makuriani, and Doldol. For Meru County, sections of Tigania East and Igembe North sub-counties, including Mlima Rasta, Mlima Lombolio, Mworontoi Belt, Nyambene Game Reserve, and the Ngatho Dam area, have been marked. Isiolo County's declaration covers parts of Isiolo North, encompassing Sarova Game Reserve, Lowangishu Hills, Mutuyni, and Loruku Hills.

Multi-Agency Approach and Road Reopening The security operation will be a multi-agency effort, involving various security agencies. The government intends to deploy the Kenya Defence Forces (KDF) and the National Intelligence Service (NIS) to aid in eradicating banditry in these counties. Roads in Samburu, Isiolo, and Laikipia, which were previously closed due to insecurity, will also be reopened to enhance accessibility for security operations. Speaking in Isiolo on Tuesday, February 3, 2026, Murkomen emphasized that the President, as Commander-in-Chief, has given "marching orders to restore security."

Crackdown on Abetting Officers Security chiefs in the affected counties have been directed to initiate a crackdown on police officers suspected of abetting insecurity. This includes officers who fail to act against criminals or collude with them in criminal activities. This measure underscores the government's commitment to addressing internal challenges within the security apparatus while simultaneously combating external threats.
