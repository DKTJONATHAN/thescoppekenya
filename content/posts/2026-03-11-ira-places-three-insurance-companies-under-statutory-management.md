## Insurance Authority Places Three Companies Under Statutory Management The Insurance Regulatory Authority (IRA) has placed KUSCCO Mutual Assurance Ltd, Trident Insurance Company Ltd, and Corporate Insurance Company Ltd under statutory management, effective March 10, 2026. The directive prohibits new policy issuance and urges existing policyholders to seek alternative coverage to safeguard against potential losses.

Statutory Management Initiated The Insurance Regulatory Authority announced today, Wednesday, March 11, 2026, that it has placed KUSCCO Mutual Assurance Ltd, Trident Insurance Company Ltd, and Corporate Insurance Company Ltd under statutory management. This action, effective March 10, 2026, was taken in accordance with Section 67C(2)(i) of the Insurance Act. The IRA has appointed the Policyholders Compensation Fund (PCF) to oversee the operations of the three insurers.

Implications for Policyholders As a direct consequence of this directive, KUSCCO Mutual Assurance Ltd, Trident Insurance Company Ltd, and Corporate Insurance Company Ltd are prohibited from issuing any new insurance policies from March 11, 2026. Existing policyholders are strongly advised to review their current coverage immediately and seek alternative insurance from licensed providers to mitigate potential financial risks. The PCF will manage the insurers' operations and ensure that eligible claims are handled under the provisions of the Insurance Act, Cap 487 Laws of Kenya. Policyholders will receive guidance on how to manage their insurance contracts moving forward.

Rationale Behind the Directive Statutory management is a temporary measure typically implemented when an insurer is deemed financially unstable or fails to meet regulatory compliance requirements. The IRA's decision aims to protect the integrity of the insurance sector and the public from potential financial exposure. This move reflects a commitment to upholding stability within critical financial services, much like the preparations underway in the education sector as the KNEC recently opened its 2026 KJSEA registration, warning schools against last-minute rushes amidst systemic hurdles.

Broader Industry Crackdown This development comes as the government, alongside independent institutions such as the Association of Kenya Insurers (AKI), continues a nationwide crackdown on fake motor insurance certificates. A multi-agency team, including AKI, the Traffic Police Department, the IRA, and the Insurance Fraud Investigation Unit, announced on February 19, 2026, that it is actively probing such cases. Recent efforts have uncovered numerous instances of insurance fraud, with reports indicating at least 44 forged motor insurance certificates detected across two regions within the past year.
