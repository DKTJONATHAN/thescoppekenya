## Entertainment Icons Depart: Remembering the Stars of 2026

The entertainment world is mourning significant losses in 2026. From "Dawson's Creek" heartthrob James Van Der Beek to "Schitt's Creek" icon Catherine O'Hara, and music legend Bob Weir, Hollywood says goodbye to beloved figures who left indelible marks across film, television, and music.

### Film and Television Legacies

Hollywood said farewell to several familiar faces from the big and small screens. Acclaimed actress Mary Beth Hurt, a star in films by Martin Scorsese and her husband Paul Schrader, passed away on March 29 at 79. Her impressive career included roles in Oscar-winning films such as "The Age of Innocence" and "The World According to Garp." Comedian and actor Alex Duong, known for "Blue Bloods" and his vibrant presence in the Los Angeles comedy circuit, died on March 28 at 42 following a battle with tissue cancer.

Veteran actor James Tolkan, whose commanding performances graced "Back to the Future" and "Top Gun," left us on March 27 at 94. His extensive credits stretched back to the 1960s. Pioneering model and actress Judy Pace, who broke barriers for Black women in Hollywood with roles in "Peyton Place" and "Brian's Song," died on March 11 at 83. Jennifer Runyon Corman, recognizable as Scott Baio's college crush on "Charles in Charge" and a student in "Ghostbusters," passed away on March 6 at 65. The stage and screen lost Jane Lapotaire, known for "The Crown" and "Downton Abbey," and a Tony Award winner for "Piaf," on March 5 at 81. Corey Parker, who charmed audiences as Grace's boyfriend on "Will & Grace" and appeared in "Friday the 13th: A New Beginning," died on March 5 at 60.

Earlier in the year, fans also grieved the passing of "Dawson's Creek" heartthrob James Van Der Beek, "Schitt's Creek" and "Home Alone" icon Catherine O'Hara, "Grey's Anatomy" and "Euphoria" star Eric Dane, and "The Thing" and "Punky Brewster" actor T.K. Carter. Robert Carradine, beloved for his roles in the "Revenge of the Nerds" films and the "Lizzie McGuire" series, died on February 23 at 71.

### Music and Animation Maestros

The world of music and animation also felt profound losses. Animation legend Barry Caldwell, a creative force behind "Animaniacs," "Pinky and the Brain," and "Tiny Toon Adventures," died on March 24 at 68. His final project was Disney Jr.’s "Ariel." Bluegrass musician Ronnie Bowman, known for his work with the Lonesome River Band and collaborations with stars like Alison Krauss, passed away on March 22 at 64 after a motorcycle accident.

Tommy DeCarlo, who remarkably ascended from being a longtime fan to the lead singer of Boston, died on March 9 at 60. Woodstock legend "Country" Joe McDonald, whose band Country Joe and the Fish defined the '60s counterculture with anthems like "I-Feel-Like-I’m-Fixin’-To-Die Rag," died on March 7 at 84. The iconic New York salsa musician and social activist Willie Colón, celebrated for fusing salsa with diverse rhythms across over 60 albums, died on February 21 at 75. Grateful Dead founding member Bob Weir was also among the music luminaries who passed earlier in 2026. Los Angeles rapper Luci4, whose real name was James Dear, known for his certified gold track "Bodypartz," died on February 22 at just 23.

### Notable Personalities and Pop Culture Figures

Beyond the silver screen and concert halls, several other public figures left their mark. Mel Schilling, the psychologist and dating expert from Australia's reality series "Married at First Sight," died on March 24 at 54 after battling colon cancer. KiKi Shepard, the long-reigning host of "Showtime at the Apollo," passed away on March 16 at 74, having also graced Broadway and television. John Alford, known for British series like "Grange Hill," was found dead in his jail cell on March 13 at 54.

The racing world mourned Chase Pistone, a former NASCAR driver and grandson of "Tiger" Tom Pistone, who died on March 2 at 42. "Deadliest Catch" deckhand Todd Meadows died on February 25 at 25 after a tragic accident at sea. Robert Cosby Jr., son of "The Real Housewives of Salt Lake City’s" Mary Cosby, passed away on February 23 at 23. Fashion icon Valentino Garavani, who made a memorable appearance in "The Devil Wears Prada," and "Dilbert" creator Scott Adams were also among the notable figures whose deaths were announced at the beginning of 2026.
