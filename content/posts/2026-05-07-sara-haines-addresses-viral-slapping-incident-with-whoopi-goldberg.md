In the current media climate, a three-minute interview segment often carries less weight than a five-second clip of a host doing something unexpected or provocative. This push for unfiltered interaction mirrors broader trends in the industry, where public figures aim to humanize themselves through casual, off-the-cuff behavior. For a show like The View, these physical antics act as a signal to the audience that the production is alive, reactive, and capable of breaking its own rules.
