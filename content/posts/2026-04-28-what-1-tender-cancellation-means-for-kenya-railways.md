## Kenya Railways Cancels DMU Tender

Kenya Railways has cancelled an international tender for 24 air compressors for Diesel Multiple Unit (DMU) trains, part of the World Bank-funded Ksh65 billion Kenya Urban Mobility Improvement Project. The abrupt cancellation, just 12 days before the May 11, 2026 submission deadline, casts uncertainty over the timeline for improving Nairobi's commuter rail infrastructure.

### Tender Cancellation Details

The corporation announced the cancellation on Tuesday, April 28, having previously invited bids through an international competitive procurement process aligned with World Bank regulations. The tender aimed to upgrade critical DMU train components, with delivery expected at Kenya Railways' central workshops in Nairobi. Bidders were required to submit proposals by May 11, with a Ksh1.5 million bid security, and bid openings were scheduled for the same day.

### Impact and Speculation

This sudden halt creates significant uncertainty for the procurement process and the broader timeline for enhancing commuter rail services within Nairobi and surrounding counties. Kenya Railways has not publicly disclosed specific reasons for the cancellation, prompting speculation among stakeholders regarding potential administrative reviews or funding adjustments. Such unexpected tender cancellations are rare in large-scale, donor-funded projects and can lead to implementation delays and impact donor confidence. ### Future of the Project

It remains unclear whether Kenya Railways will re-advertise the tender or restructure it under revised terms for improved affordability and faster implementation. The need for upgraded components is critical, especially as reports indicate several DMU trains suffer frequent breakdowns, compelling the corporation to incur significant repair costs. The ongoing challenges with train maintenance underscore the urgency of a clear path forward for the urban mobility project.
