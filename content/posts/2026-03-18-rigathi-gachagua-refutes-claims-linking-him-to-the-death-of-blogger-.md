Rigathi Gachagua, leader of the Democracy for Citizens Party (DCP), has vehemently denied recent allegations by President William Ruto linking him to the 2019 death of blogger Maureen Gathigia Kinyua. Gachagua's camp maintains Kinyua died in a hit-and-run road accident near Sagana Catholic Church, with no official evidence connecting him to the incident.

DCP Clarifies Blogger's Death The Democracy for Citizens Party, through a statement on its affiliate X account, categorically refuted claims suggesting Gachagua's involvement in Kinyua's death. The statement clarified that Maureen Gathigia Kinyua tragically passed away on May 24, 2019, in a road traffic accident near Sagana Catholic Church in Mathira, Nyeri County. Investigations into the incident classified it as a hit-and-run, with no official evidence linking her death to murder or foul play.

Circumstances of Kinyua's Passing According to the DCP, the driver involved in the May 2019 incident was subsequently charged in Karatina Court with causing death by dangerous driving and later released on a Ksh150,000 bond while awaiting further legal proceedings. This legal process, the party emphasized, leaves no plausible way for Gachagua to be linked with the blogger's death. Information circulating online suggesting Maureen's passing was a murder, rather than an accident, is deemed misinformation and baseless. Such claims risk causing unnecessary distress to Kinyua's family and associates, highlighting the pervasive nature of unverified narratives, a subject often examined by Za Ndani in its analysis of [Annie Macaulay: Not a Single Mum, But a Master of Narrative](https://zandani.co.ke/article/annie-macaulay-not-a-single-mum).

Origin of Renewed Allegations The cause of the popular blogger's death resurfaced online, sparking confusion after remarks made on March 17, 2026, where President William Ruto accused former Deputy President Rigathi Gachagua of involvement while on a development tour in Busia County. This accusation is part of an ongoing public spat between the United Opposition and the Kenya Kwanza Administration, marked by fierce exchanges and personal attacks. Notably, at the time of Maureen's death in 2019, then-Deputy President William Ruto personally called her mother to offer condolences, describing Kinyua as a "keen, dedicated and thorough" worker for the Jubilee fraternity.

Gachagua's Call for Responsible Discourse Gachagua's statement proceeded to caution media experts and civil society leaders, stressing that public figures, including President William Ruto, should exercise prudence and refrain from making statements based on unverified claims. He called for accurate reporting to be maintained, not only to uphold journalistic integrity but also to respect the family's privacy and prevent the spread of rumors that can mislead the public. This plea for responsible communication underscores the significant impact of public statements, particularly amidst intense political exchanges.
