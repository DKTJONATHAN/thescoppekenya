Reggae legends UB40 featuring Ali Campbell are set to tour South Africa in June 2026, with highly anticipated performances scheduled for Cape Town, Durban, and Pretoria. The acclaimed UK group will play three dates across the nation, offering fans a chance to experience their iconic hits live.

## UB40 Featuring Ali Campbell's 2026 South Africa Tour

The tour itinerary includes a performance at Cape Town's Grand Arena on June 2, 2026, followed by Durban's ICC on June 4, 2026. Music enthusiasts in Pretoria will have two opportunities to see the band at the SunBet Arena, with concerts on June 6 and June 7, 2026. The initial Pretoria date has already sold out, highlighting the immense demand for the group's return to the South African stage. Tickets for the remaining shows are available through Big Concerts and Ticketmaster.

### The Group's Unique Line-up and Legacy

This touring entity is known as UB40 featuring Ali Campbell, a distinct group not to be confused with the original UB40. Ali Campbell, the original lead singer, departed the band in 2008 due to management and business disputes, with keyboardist Mickey Virtue and later Astro following suit. These three musicians subsequently formed UB40 featuring Ali Campbell.

A legal dispute over the use of the UB40 name arose between the factions, particularly with Ali Campbell's brother, Robin Campbell, who remained with the original group. Both groups continue to operate. The reggae band UB40 originally formed in December 1978 in Birmingham, England. Their unique name, Unemployment Benefit, Form 40, was chosen because all original members were unemployed when the group was established. The original UB40 released over 21 studio albums, crafting hits like "Red Red Wine" and "Can't Help Falling in Love."
