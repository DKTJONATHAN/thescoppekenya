Prime Cabinet Secretary and Cabinet Secretary for Foreign Affairs Musalia Mudavadi departed for Moscow, Russia, on Sunday, March 15, 2026, to engage top Russian government officials. His mission is to address the urgent situation of citizens caught in the Russia-Ukraine war and to facilitate their safe repatriation.

## Diplomatic Push for Citizen Repatriation

Mudavadi's visit focuses on the welfare of individuals who have been voluntarily or involuntarily recruited into the Russian military, and consequently trapped in the ongoing conflict. Reports indicate that over 18 citizens have died in the war, with more than 30 currently missing. The diplomatic efforts aim to prevent further risks associated with misleading recruitment or false promises of employment. A key objective of the Moscow visit is to facilitate a safe repatriation process for those affected individuals back to their home country.

### Bilateral Engagements and Economic Ties

During his time in Moscow, the Prime Cabinet Secretary will hold bilateral meetings with high-ranking officials. These include Sergey Lavrov, the Minister of Foreign Affairs of the Russian Federation, and subsequently, Ministers of Education and Labour. Mudavadi is also scheduled to deliver a public lecture at the Moscow State Institute of International Relations (MGIMO).

Beyond addressing the immediate grievances concerning citizens in Russia, the visit aims to strengthen diplomatic relations between Nairobi and Moscow across various sectors. These areas of focus include Education, Labour Mobility, Health, Infrastructure, and Energy. There is also a significant push to champion robust economic ties. Mudavadi highlighted the substantial potential for exporting products such as coffee, tea, and floriculture to the Russian market. This initiative comes at a time of global economic shifts, emphasizing the importance of diverse trade partnerships. The broader engagement seeks to establish a clear and transparent framework to safeguard the welfare of nationals residing, studying, and working in Russia.
