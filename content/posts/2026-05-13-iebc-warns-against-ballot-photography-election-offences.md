## The Selfie-Ban Dilemma: Why Rural Voters Feel Polling Booth Surveillance Threatens Democratic Expression

The IEBC is effectively turning polling booths into digital dead zones for tomorrow’s by-elections. By outlawing ballot photography, the commission hopes to protect the secrecy of the vote, yet in rural heartlands, this move is sparking fears that a vital tool for informal political accountability is being stripped away.

Ahead of the May 14 by-elections, the Independent Electoral and Boundaries Commission has signaled a hard crackdown on voters carrying mobile devices into the marking booths. Under Section 7(3)(e) of the Election Offences Act, taking a photo or video of a marked ballot is now treated as a serious breach, carrying the threat of a 1 million shilling fine or three years in prison. This directive comes as voters across Narok, Elgeyo Marakwet, and Nandi counties prepare to settle high-stakes legislative contests, including the intense political showdown in Emurua Dikirr. The commission claims the ban is necessary to curb potential vote-buying schemes where patrons demand photographic evidence of loyalty.
