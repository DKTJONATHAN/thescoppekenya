## President Ruto Awards Sabastian Sawe for Marathon World Record

President William Ruto today awarded Kenyan marathoner Sabastian Sawe Ksh 8 million at State House, Nairobi. This recognition followed Sawe's historic achievement at the London Marathon on Sunday, where he broke the world record, becoming the first man to complete the race in under two hours. The substantial reward underscores the government's commitment to incentivising athletic excellence and celebrating national sporting triumphs.

### Record-Breaking Achievement and Financial Incentives

Sawe completed the London Marathon in an astounding 1:59:30, officially surpassing the previous marathon world record of 2:00:35 set by the late Kelvin Kiptum at the 2023 Chicago Marathon. President Ruto presented Sawe with two cheques: Ksh5 million for breaking a world record and an additional Ksh3 million for winning a gold medal in a major competition. These awards align with the government's established policy framework for rewarding athletes and sports personalities.

Beyond the monetary compensation, President Ruto also honoured Sawe with a customised Kenyan number plate bearing the numbers 1:59:30, commemorating his **Sabastian Sawe world record**. The athlete will also have the liberty to choose a car of his preference, on which the unique number plate will be installed.

### Acknowledging Sporting Excellence

During the State House event, which followed Sawe's return from London, the marathoner himself presented President Ruto with an autographed pair of the running shoes he wore during his record-setting race. The President lauded Sawe's achievement, stating, "Sabastian, you have not only broken a record; you have expanded the horizon of human potential. You have made the impossible possible. You have inspired a nation, a whole generation and the world."

The London Marathon proved to be a historic event, with Ethiopia’s Yofin Kejelcha also running under two hours, clocking 1:59:41. President Ruto further acknowledged Uganda’s Jacob Kiplimo, who finished third with a new personal best of 2:00:28. The recognition of the **Sabastian Sawe world record** is a clear signal of the government's support for national athletes. The government's dedication to sports is further demonstrated through efforts such as ensuring timely payment of AFCON hosting fees.
