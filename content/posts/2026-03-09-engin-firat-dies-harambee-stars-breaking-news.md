## Engin Firat's Sudden Passing Former Harambee Stars coach Engin Firat passed away on Monday, March 9, 2026, at Istanbul airport after suffering a heart attack. Firat, the first team coach for Lebanon's Nejmeh Football Club, was traveling to Adana. His sudden death concludes a career impacting both club and national football.

Career and Coaching Legacy Engin Firat's death was confirmed by Nejmeh Football Club, where he had been appointed head coach on February 8, 2026. At Nejmeh SC, he quickly established an unbeaten record in his debut matches, successfully guiding the team into a domestic cup final. Firat had reunited with Kenyan players Anthony "Teddy" Akumu, Masud Juma, and Austin Odhiambo at the Lebanese club, having previously coached them during his tenure with the Harambee Stars. Firat served as the head coach of Kenya's men's national team, Harambee Stars, for three years, from approximately 2022 to 2025. His time with the national squad ended in December 2025 after Kenya failed to qualify for the 2026 Africa Cup of Nations (AFCON).

Tributes and Professional Ethos Nejmeh Club issued a statement mourning Coach Firat, describing him as a committed professional with high ethics. "With great sadness and sorrow, Star Athletic Club mourns first team coach captain Engin Firat, who passed away following a sudden heart attack while at Istanbul airport on his way to Adana a few hours after leaving Beirut for his country," the statement read.
