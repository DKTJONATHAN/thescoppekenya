Afrobeats titan Davido recently declared a renewed focus on African sounds for his upcoming sixth studio album, signaling a deliberate pivot back to the continent after extensive global success. Speaking on BBC 1Xtra's Official UK Afrobeats Chart Show, the artist confirmed his musical direction for 2026.

## Davido's African Homecoming

### A Return to Roots
During an appearance on BBC 1Xtra's Official UK Afrobeats Chart Show, hosted by Congolese-British comedian and presenter Eddie Kadi, Davido provided clear insight into his future musical direction. He emphasized plans to decenter Western influences, stating his current recordings are "very, very Davido African" and not focused on Western sounds. The artist asserted that his next project, subtly announced on his birthday in November 2025 as his sixth studio album, will be "very African". This statement follows years of establishing a formidable global presence, including performing in and selling out numerous arenas, attending various award shows, and making three Grammy appearances in the past three years. Davido expressed a desire to "go back home" to Africa, indicating a strategic shift towards his continental audience.

### Building on Past Success
This pivot is not unprecedented for the artist. His fifth studio album, "5ive", released in 2025, achieved significant commercial success by firmly grounding itself in Nigerian identity. The album resonated deeply with markets Afrobeats had nurtured across Africa for a decade, demonstrating Davido's understanding that the genre's most loyal and responsive audience remained at home. This strategic focus on African audiences continues to be a crucial element for artists seeking sustained impact, much like the ongoing discussions surrounding the future of groups like Wakadinali. Last year, his "5ive Tour" further solidified this commitment, with performances across five major Nigerian cities, including Lagos, Ibadan, and Enugu. This domestic tour was widely recognized as a clear statement of intent, especially at a time when many major acts had largely bypassed the Nigerian touring circuit. These concerted efforts-the 2025 album, the tour, and the recent interview-underscore a consistent strategy. Davido has achieved global music industry milestones, and his trajectory now points towards a deliberate return to his foundational roots. The upcoming landmark London festival scheduled for August 2026 further illustrates his continued engagement with a diverse audience while maintaining an African core.
