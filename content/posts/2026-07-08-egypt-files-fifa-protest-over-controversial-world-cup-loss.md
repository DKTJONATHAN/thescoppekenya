## The Scripted Myth of the Argentine God

FIFA has officially received Egypt's formal protest following their 3-2 defeat in Atlanta, yet the global media remains fixated on the glory of the result rather than the mechanics of the robbery. While the Egyptian Football Association screams into the void, the reality is that the World Cup has evolved from an athletic competition into a premium marketing vehicle where the primary product is the continued viability of the Lionel Messi brand.

### The VAR Theater of the Absurd

The match officiating under Francois Letexier was not merely incompetent; it was curated. Consider the 79th minute, when Mostafa Zico was denied a goal that was ostensibly chalked off for a phantom infringement, a decision so flimsy it would not have survived a scrutiny test in any league that actually valued integrity. Contrast this with Argentina, who seem to operate under a different set of physics when they enter the penalty area. If Marwan Attia had been wearing an Argentine shirt, his contested challenges would have been treated as standard physicality rather than the high crimes they were deemed to be in the eyes of the officials.

We have reached a point where VAR serves as the ultimate gatekeeper of the tournament narrative. It is no longer a tool for correcting human error but a sophisticated device for shielding commercial assets from the embarrassment of early elimination. Much like the way some celebrities insist on airbrushing their own publicity photos, FIFA uses VAR to polish the rough edges of matches to ensure the biggest names remain in the draw until the final weekend. If you think the officiating bias is purely coincidental, you are as naive as those who believe the [national transport strikes](https://zandani.co.ke/article/kenyan-transport-sector-launches-nationwide-strike-over-fuel-costs) are merely about fuel prices and not deep-seated governance failure.

### The Illusion of Fair Play

Hossam Hassan, Egypt’s manager, was correct to point out the obvious bias, even if his words fell on ears paid to ignore them. During the post-match press conference, his frustration was palpable, yet the mainstream press painted him as a sore loser rather than a man watching his team get dismantled by a system rigged to favor the house. The rejection of the penalty claim involving Mohamed Salah was the final piece of evidence required to confirm that the tournament officials are not just watching the game, they are managing the outcome.

The commercial reality is simple. FIFA has spent decades and billions of dollars positioning Messi as the face of the sport. Allowing Egypt to knock them out via a legitimate late-game push would have been a catastrophic failure for the tournament’s sponsors. It would have signaled that the sport is truly unpredictable, a notion that conflicts with the sanitized, profitable experience the federation needs to maintain. While [Sanaipei Tande](https://zandani.co.ke/article/sanaipei-tande-speaks-out-against-societal-marriage-pressures) faces her own battles against rigid societal scripts, football fans are forced to watch a screenplay that was written before the first whistle even blew in Kansas City.

### The Integrity Deficit

The Egyptian association, led by Hany Abou Rida, is currently calling for the officiating staff to be removed. It is a hollow gesture. Even if these specific referees are sidelined, the culture that empowered them to make those decisions remains firmly in place. The conflict of interest is baked into the foundation. When your organization is entirely dependent on the marketability of specific icons, you do not let sporting fairness interfere with the bottom line. The protest is a necessary performance of anger, but everyone in the FIFA boardroom knows the complaint will be buried in a drawer alongside common sense and fair play.

**The committee will dismiss the Egyptian appeal within 48 hours to ensure the march toward the final remains undisturbed.**
