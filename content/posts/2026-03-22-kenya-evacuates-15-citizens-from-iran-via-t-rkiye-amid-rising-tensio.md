The government has confirmed the evacuation of fifteen citizens from Iran, routing them through Türkiye amidst escalating Middle East tensions. The State Department for Diaspora Affairs announced Saturday, March 21, 2026, that the evacuees were expected to arrive in the country early Sunday, March 22.

## Regional Crisis Prompts Citizen Evacuation

### Coordinated Repatriation Efforts
The evacuation was meticulously coordinated with Kenya Missions in Ankara and Tehran. A multi-agency team initiated emergency protocols across the country's missions in the region. An earlier communication on Friday had indicated an arrival in the early hours of Saturday, March 21. The State Department for Diaspora Affairs clarified on X that the delay was due to "uncertainty in the delicate evacuation process."

### Heightened Middle East Hostilities
The evacuation unfolds amidst escalating hostilities between Iran and the United States, characterized by ongoing airstrikes, retaliatory attacks, and threats of further military action. Iran recently launched missile strikes near an Israeli nuclear facility, following an alleged targeting of one of its own sites. US President Donald Trump issued a new 48-hour deadline on Saturday for Iran to reopen the Strait of Hormuz, threatening attacks on Iranian power plants if no action is taken. This development intensifies pressure on oil markets, leading to looming shortages and surging global prices. Iran's state media has warned of retaliation against US-linked energy infrastructure in the Gulf if its fuel and energy facilities are attacked.

### Ongoing Monitoring and Advisory
The government maintains continuous monitoring of the Middle East situation. A multi-agency team has activated emergency protocols across Kenya Missions in the region, reflecting a comprehensive approach to citizen safety. This level of preparedness is critical in times of crisis, similar to the coordinated efforts seen in domestic emergencies where the Kenya flood death toll reached 62 as Nairobi recorded the highest fatalities. [https://zandani.co.ke/article/kenya-flood-death-toll-reaches-62-as-nairobi-records-highest-fatalit] Citizens in the affected region have been advised to strictly adhere to guidance issued by the country's embassies, host governments, and official communication channels. Those requiring assistance are urged to contact the nearest mission or the State Department for Diaspora Affairs.
