## Internal Strife and Institutional Instability

### A House Divided Against Itself
The current political atmosphere in Nairobi has moved into an unusual phase where ruling party members are staging protests against their own administration. The escalating tension between the President and the Deputy President has paralyzed the government agenda, shifting the focus from national development to internal supremacy battles. This infighting has distracted leadership from critical administrative duties and systemic governance concerns, leaving citizens to navigate the resulting political vacuum. 

### The Hypocrisy of State Protest
The decision by the UDA Women’s League to demonstrate against their own government represents a striking contradiction in political accountability. By framing themselves as victims demanding justice from the very leadership they occupy, these officials are highlighting the administrative disconnect within the current regime. This performative opposition suggests a government more concerned with protesting its own shadow than addressing the foundational instability of the state. As officials focus on externalizing blame for internal failures, the public is left to deal with the consequences of a leadership team that is effectively at war with itself.

### Navigating the Leadership Void

The ongoing friction within the ruling party has served as a reminder that stability is rarely a byproduct of political alliances. As the rivalry between the top two leaders persists, it becomes increasingly clear that the burden of maintaining national progress falls on citizens rather than the divided executive.
