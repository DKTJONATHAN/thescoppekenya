The 2002 handover of power from President Daniel Arap Moi to Mwai Kibaki at Uhuru Park was truly historic. Amidst the charged atmosphere, Moi's bodyguard found himself in an extraordinary position, desperately catching objects hurled by jubilant but agitated crowds, a living barrier in a moment of national transition.

KAZI NGUMU: Moi's Bodyguard's Legendary Saves at Uhuru Park! That infamous day in 2002 saw Uhuru Park transformed into a cauldron of emotion, a mix of hope, relief, and raw sentiment as the sun set on President Moi’s 24-year rule. While Mwai Kibaki’s supporters cheered wildly, a significant portion of the crowd felt no compunction about expressing their displeasure directly to the outgoing Head of State. It was a baptism by fire, or rather, by flying objects, for Moi’s personal security detail.

The Human Goalkeeper For any presidential bodyguard, every day holds inherent risks, but that December 2002 afternoon presented a challenge unlike almost any other in recent Kenyan history. Perhaps only the tumultuous events of 1982, when Moi’s government faced a coup attempt, could compare in terms of sheer unpredictability and danger for those tasked with his protection. His lead bodyguard, a man whose face registered unwavering focus, became a human goalkeeper, expertly deflecting or catching every item lobbed towards the President as he prepared for his final national salute. From shoes to soda bottles, the loyal officer ensured nothing touched Moi, a display of unparalleled dedication under extreme pressure.

A Nation's Unfiltered Voice The scenes at Uhuru Park were a vivid illustration of a nation finally finding its collective voice after decades of single-party rule. The jubilation for Kibaki was palpable, but so too was the pent-up frustration directed at the outgoing regime. The bodyguard’s heroic efforts weren't just about physical protection; they symbolized the intense political and social currents swirling that day. It was a moment of release, both for the public and, arguably, for the very system that was undergoing such a dramatic change. As we reflect in 2026, the aspirations for governance, infrastructure, and public welfare remain critical, underscoring Dr. Chitunga’s stern warnings about political control threatening Kenya’s infrastructure fund.

The Aftermath and Lasting Legend Years later, tales of that bodyguard’s agility and resolve continue to circulate in hushed tones and knowing smiles across the country. It was *kazi ngumu* - truly hard work - performed with an almost superhuman calm amidst a storm of public sentiment. That day cemented his legend, an unsung hero whose unshakeable professionalism shielded a president during his most vulnerable public moment, illustrating the extreme demands placed upon those in the inner circle of power. The streets today still buzz about the day Uhuru Park decided to give Moi a send-off that no bodyguard, no matter how seasoned, could have fully rehearsed for.
