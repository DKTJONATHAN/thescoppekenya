## One Night Only: Monica Barbaro & Callum Turner’s Wild Rom-Com Ride

Get ready for a high-concept romantic comedy! Monica Barbaro and Callum Turner star in Will Gluck's "One Night Only," where premarital intimacy is restricted to a single, chaotic night each year. Two abandoned strangers navigating New York City might just find an unexpected connection when the stakes are impossibly high.

### Love's Annual Curfew

Imagine a world where premarital sex is illegal, save for one wild, designated night from 7 p.m. to 7 a.m. This is the outlandish premise of "One Night Only," helmed by writer-director Will Gluck, known for his witty takes on romance like "Anyone But You" and "Easy A." Gluck playfully suggests no one even remembers why this rule exists, much like "all things political," but the annual exception sets the stage for utter chaos and, surprisingly, deep connection. While some might draw parallels to "The Purge," Gluck clarifies his film focuses less on the act itself and more on the thrilling, frantic pursuit of connection on what happens to be the most unromantic night of the year.

### Dating in the Extreme

Monica Barbaro, portraying Allie, and Callum Turner, as Owen, find themselves thrust together after their original plans for the special night fall apart. Allie's best friend abandons her for a stranger, and Owen's fiancée ditches him for someone else. These two lost souls, ghosted and friendless in a bustling New York City, are forced to navigate the peculiar rules of this "one night only" event alone - and then, together. Barbaro astutely notes the film is "an allegory for dating and how intense it can be when you're seeking true connection with somebody," highlighting its deeply relatable core despite the fantastical premise.

### Soulmates by Cosmic Joke

The journey of Owen and Allie is far from straightforward. Turner hints that their spark isn't immediate, but fate, or perhaps a cosmic joke, keeps throwing them together. "The universe [is] banging both of them over the head," Turner reveals, describing how they repeatedly encounter each other on this night they both despise. This serendipitous, high-stakes situation, where they stumble into romance amidst the chaos, is exactly what makes their connection so profound, according to Gluck. Turner even goes so far as to call them "soulmates," with Barbaro adding a playful "no spoilers" caveat to their eventual romantic destiny.

### Mark Your Calendars: August 7

"One Night Only" promises the raunchy humor expected from a sex comedy, but with a heartfelt pivot toward intimacy and genuine love, a signature style Gluck has perfected in films like "Friends With Benefits." Both Barbaro and Turner credit Gluck’s unique talent for elevating the romantic comedy genre, making audiences eager to see his work on the big screen. Prepare for a uniquely charming and utterly chaotic romantic adventure when "One Night Only" hits theaters on August 7, 2026.
