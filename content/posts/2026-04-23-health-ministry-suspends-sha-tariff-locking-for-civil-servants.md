Health Ministry Suspends SHA Tariff Locking for Civil Servan is central to this update for Kenyan readers.

The Ministry of Health Cabinet Secretary Aden Duale announced the immediate suspension of the Social Health Authority's (SHA) tariff-locking mechanism today, Thursday, April 23. This directive follows widespread reports of civil servants being denied medical services or compelled to pay out of pocket at contracted health facilities, aiming to resolve payment friction.

## Immediate Changes to Civil Servant Healthcare

Health Cabinet Secretary Aden Duale confirmed the withdrawal of the tariff locking currently configured in the SHA system after chairing a meeting focused on improving healthcare services for civil servants. The announcement, made today, April 23, dictates that all SHA-contracted facilities are now barred from charging civil servants any fees for medication, effective immediately.

### Suspension of Strict Tariff Application

Provisions enforcing strict tariff application have also been suspended. This suspension will remain in effect until nationwide tariff negotiations are successfully concluded. Clause 10.2(c) of the Addendum, which governs strict tariff applications, will remain suspended in adherence to the binding obligations in existing contracts between facilities and SHA.

### Rapid Response and "Walk-In, Walk-Out" Policy

To address urgent situations, a joint rapid response desk will be established. This desk, comprising representatives from SHA, the State Department of Public Service, and the Union of Kenya Civil Servants (UKCS), will facilitate the release of civil servants detained in hospitals over outstanding bills and process refunds for any unauthorised charges. The Ministry also agreed to enforce a strict “walk-in, walk-out” policy, explicitly prohibiting co-payments at SHA-contracted facilities. Only facilities within the SHA network are permitted to offer services under the Public Officers Medical Scheme Fund (POMSF), with violations leading to penalties, including de-contracting.

### Impact on Vulnerable Patients

The resolution addresses a crisis that significantly impacted public servants, particularly those with chronic conditions such as requiring dialysis, cancer treatment, and diabetes management, alongside accident and emergency cases. Many reported being turned away or forced to pay cash for services they already fund through salary deductions. Earlier widespread reports suggesting a suspension of SHA services for civil servants were dismissed by the Ministry, which reiterated that contracted facilities should not demand cash payments.
