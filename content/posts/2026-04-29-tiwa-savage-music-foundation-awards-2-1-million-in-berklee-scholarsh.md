The Tiwa Savage Music Foundation has awarded $2.1 million in Berklee College of Music scholarships to eighteen talented African students in 2026. This significant initiative, culminating in a grand finale in Nigeria, provides unparalleled global music education opportunities for emerging artists across the continent, bridging local talent with international standards.

## Tiwa Savage Foundation Empowers African Music Talent with Berklee Scholarships

### Intensive Program Elevates Skills

From April 22 to 26, 2026, the Berklee in Nigeria: Tiwa Savage Intensive Music Program offered immersive, world-class training to 120 emerging artists, songwriters, producers, and music professionals in Lagos. This intensive, a first for Berklee on the African continent, focused on live performance, songwriting, music production, and the business of music. Participants directly engaged with distinguished Berklee faculty through workshops, collaborative sessions, and mentorship, gaining both technical skills and critical industry insight. The program also underscored collaboration, creative exploration, and long-term career development.

### Grand Finale Showcases Emerging Artists

The program culminated in a landmark Grand Finale Concert and Award Ceremony at the National Theatre, hosted by Darey Art Alade and Kie Kie. During this event, participants presented original works developed throughout the intensive. The live concert featured diverse band-backed performances spanning Afrobeats, R&B, Hip-Hop, Gospel, Jazz, and fusion genres. Ensembles led by Berklee faculty performed a range of music from Stevie Wonder and Miles Davis to Masego, Ayra Starr, and Ne-Yo, alongside original compositions. Electrifying guest performances by Loud Choir and Teni the Entertainer further highlighted the depth and global potential of Nigeria's next generation of music talent. The finale welcomed over 1,000 attendees, including members of the U.S. Consulate, industry executives, cultural leaders, media, government representatives, and music enthusiasts such as Yeni Kuti.

### Tiwa Savage's Vision for Global African Creatives

Speaking on the program's successful completion, Tiwa Savage expressed deep pride in seeing the young musicians take the stage. She reaffirmed the Tiwa Savage Music Foundation's goal to bring the world's highest standards of music education to Nigeria, viewing the eighteen life-changing scholarships to Berklee in Boston as proof of African talent's global competitiveness. Savage emphasized that this is merely the beginning of the mission to provide African creatives with the necessary tools, structure, and platform to excel on the world stage. This initiative also aligns with broader efforts to expand access to structured, world-class music education, removing barriers and creating new pathways for African creatives to compete and lead globally. ### Berklee Faculty Led Transformative Training

Berklee faculty members who led the program included Dennis Montgomery (Academic Director, Professor of Ensembles), Yoron Israel (Chair of Percussion), Tyrone Chase (Assistant Chair of Ensembles), Nichelle Mungo (Associate Professor of Voice), and Anthony Nembhard (Assistant Professor of Ensembles). Jason Camelio, Program Director and Assistant Vice President of Global Programs and Partnerships, who oversees Berklee’s global initiatives, also played a crucial role. The evening concluded with a scholarship awards presentation, where the eighteen students received fully funded undergraduate places at Berklee College of Music in Boston. All 120 participants also received graduation certificates.
