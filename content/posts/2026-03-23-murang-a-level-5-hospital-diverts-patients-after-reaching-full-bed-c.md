Murang'a Level 5 Hospital is diverting patients to alternative facilities after reaching its full 500-bed capacity, Governor Irungu Kang'ata announced on Monday, March 23, 2026. The main referral hospital is currently overwhelmed by a significant surge in patient numbers, necessitating urgent county intervention.

Murang'a Hospital Reaches Full Capacity Murang'a County residents have been advised to seek medical services at nearby health facilities due to the overwhelming patient numbers at Murang'a Level 5 Hospital. The hospital is managing between 1,500 and 2,000 outpatient visits daily, with admissions consistently at its 500-bed capacity. This surge is compounded by patients arriving from neighboring regions, straining existing resources despite recent infrastructure expansion.

Alternative Care and County Measures To alleviate pressure on the primary referral facility, patients are encouraged to visit alternative healthcare providers. These include Muriranja's Hospital, Mugoiri, Mukuyu, and Kiangage dispensaries, which have received additional medical staff to manage the increased patient load. The county government has implemented several urgent measures to address the crisis. These include reorganizing emergency staff, recalling personnel on leave, opening new wards to create additional space, and deploying marshals to manage patient flow efficiently. Furthermore, links with Level 4 hospitals have been strengthened to ensure continuity of care and ease congestion.

Staffing Concerns and Policy Fast-tracking Governor Kang'ata has raised concerns regarding staffing levels within the county's health sector. Plans to recruit additional health workers face potential limitations due to budget constraints tied to the wage bill. The governor has therefore urged the county assembly to fast-track policies designed to mitigate such emergency situations in the future. Residents are also encouraged to cooperate with healthcare staff and managers to ensure positive outcomes for all patients. The county has disseminated phone numbers for patients to voice concerns directly to government officials. This situation is not unprecedented, as the county hospital experienced a similar patient surge in October 2025, which also led to the recall of doctors on leave and the opening of new wards.
