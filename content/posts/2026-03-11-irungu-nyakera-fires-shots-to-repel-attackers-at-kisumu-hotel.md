## Irungu Nyakera Repels Attackers at Kisumu Hotel

In the early hours of Wednesday, March 11, Irungu Nyakera, a Democracy for Citizens Party Patron, reportedly discharged his firearm twice to deter over 100 alleged goons who stormed his Kisumu hotel, causing property damage and injuring staff. Nyakera attributes the incident to political opponents and criticises police inaction.

### Early Morning Confrontation
On Wednesday, March 11, 2026, gun drama unfolded in Kisumu when Irungu Nyakera allegedly fired two warning shots to disperse a mob attacking his hotel. Nyakera, the Democracy for Citizens Party Patron and a Nairobi Gubernatorial candidate, stated that more than 100 individuals stormed the premises at 5 AM, assaulting workers and restraining a female security guard before vandalising property. Nyakera confirmed his personal presence during the incident on his social media platform, X, asserting he acted to repel goons he believes were dispatched by political adversaries.

### Allegations of Official Complicity
Nyakera claimed that the attack resulted in the destruction of property and injuries to several staff members, despite hotel management's attempts to secure assistance from the area OCS, who purportedly failed to respond. He recounted calling the OCS for backup and, after an hour of no response, warning of further action if intruders remained on his property. Nyakera has directly linked some government officials to the plot, suggesting instructions were issued for him to vacate his tenancy at the Lake Basin Development Mall due to his opposition affiliation. He quoted the Nyanza Region DCI boss informing him of a Principal Secretary's personal interest in the property. As of now, the government has not issued an official statement regarding these allegations. Kenyans.co.ke has not independently verified claims of a senior PS instructing the attackers.

### Previous Incidents and Political Tensions
This recent assault follows a similar incident at the same Kisumu hotel three weeks prior, where goons reportedly stormed the facility, absconding with merchandise and temporarily locking the management out. These developments coincide with Nyakera's recent public criticism of the government's approach to urban development in Nairobi, particularly regarding demolitions impacting traders along the Thika Superhighway. He previously highlighted a perceived disconnect between government pledges of modernising Nairobi and the displacement of income sources through abrupt demolitions. Nyakera commented on the "cooperation agreement between Nairobi City County Government and the National Government," stating it enables them to "demolish everything in their sight without empathy". Such political statements underscore the broader context of opposition figures like Nyakera raising concerns over governance.
