## Kangemi Slip Road Cracks Expose Structural Flaws

Kangemi residents have voiced serious concerns following the appearance of a wide, deep crack on the Kangemi inbound slip road along Waiyaki Way. The visible damage, suggesting compromised road integrity and underlying ground movement, has prompted an alarm regarding public safety and the lack of alternative routes for daily commuters.

### Visible Damage and Commuter Risk

Footage reveals a crack extending several meters down the middle of the road, visibly splitting the surface. Large concrete blocks lining the road have shifted, creating wide gaps and showing signs of chipping and crumbling. This lateral displacement of protective barriers suggests significant underlying ground movement, indicating the visible damage may be part of a deeper structural problem. Despite the evident danger, matatus, boda bodas, and pedestrians continue to use the compromised stretch daily, traveling to and from Kangemi, Westlands, Uthiru, Kabete, and surrounding areas without an available alternative route.

### Critical Junction and Public Frustration

The Kangemi interchange, a vital connection along the Nairobi-Nakuru highway (Waiyaki Way), links the main road to local streets like Loresho Road, Muthanga Road, and Hinga Road. These slip roads are crucial shortcuts, enabling residents to access or exit the main highway and avoid heavy traffic during peak hours. The current state of the Kangemi slip road cracks has ignited widespread frustration online, with many netizens blaming the government for substandard road construction and alleging that contracts are primarily for personal enrichment.

One netizen criticized the approval process, stating, "Honestly, whoever is approving these roads needs to go to jail together with the contractors. Just check out the Ruai new dual carriage expansion, it's full of craters and wells." Another commented on the quality of the tarmacking, noting, "The beams are intact, the problem is the contractor who did the tarmacking did a poor job, he didn't account for vibrations." Concerns over road quality extend beyond Kangemi, with another netizen alleging that roads in Konza Technopolis, a tech hub city, are already developing potholes despite minimal use. "Most roads done during this term by the national government or counties are totally substandard. Barely one year old, the service lane in Kitengela looks like a farm. The new road in Konza Technopolis has potholes. Suswa road is a death trap," the netizen expressed. This public outcry echoes other instances of citizen dissatisfaction with government projects, reminiscent of recent clashes in Mbeere North during protests over hospital conditions.

### Calls for Accountability

At the time of this report, the Kenya National Highways Authority (KeNHA) had not yet responded to the raised concerns regarding the Kangemi slip road cracks. KeNHA has previously announced disruptions and construction works at the Kangemi Interchange, including bridge construction, with diversions implemented to manage traffic flow. However, the current situation highlights ongoing challenges in road infrastructure maintenance and public safety.
