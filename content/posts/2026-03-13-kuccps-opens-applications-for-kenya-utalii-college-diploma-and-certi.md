## KUCCPS Opens Kenya Utalii College Applications for Diploma and Certificate Courses The Kenya Universities and Colleges Central Placement Service (KUCCPS) has opened applications for diploma and certificate courses at Kenya Utalii College (KUC). Form Four students who sat the 2025 Kenya Certificate of Secondary Education (KCSE), alongside previous candidates, can apply for 13 programmes until April 1, 2026, targeting careers in hospitality and tourism.

Application Window and Eligibility KUCCPS officially announced the opening of its online application portal on Friday, March 13, 2026. The system is now accepting submissions for 13 distinct diploma and certificate programmes at Kenya Utalii College. These applications are open to Form Four leavers who undertook the 2025 KCSE examination, as well as candidates from prior years. The application window is set to close on April 1, 2026.

Diverse Programme Offerings Kenya Utalii College offers a comprehensive range of courses designed to equip students for the hospitality and tourism sectors. Available diploma programmes include Hospitality Management, Culinary Arts, Front Office Operations, Food and Beverage Management, Housekeeping and Laundry Management, Travel and Tourism Management, and Tour Guiding and Administration. Certificate courses cover Front Office Operations, Pastry and Bakery, Housekeeping and Laundry Operations, Food and Beverage Service and Sales, Travel and Tour Operations, and Tour Guiding and Administration.

Integration with National Placement System Established 47 years ago, Kenya Utalii College has a distinguished record, having trained over 60,000 graduates who contribute to the hospitality and tourism industries both locally and internationally. Until last year, KUC managed student admissions directly under the Ministry of Tourism. Its recent integration into KUCCPS marks a significant shift, aligning it with 155 other colleges across various ministries that have adopted the central placement system, following the precedent set by institutions such as KMTC and the Kenya School of Law. This move follows a collaboration between KUCCPS and KUC aimed at expanding access to the institution’s globally recognised tourism and hospitality programmes. The partnership was formally announced on January 19 by KUCCPS CEO Dr. Agnes Mercy Wahome and KUC Principal Mark Rachuonyo Ogendi. Dr. Wahome emphasized that the KUCCPS placement system ensures fairness, merit, and equity, allowing institutions to attract students from all regions while maintaining national representation. The expanded access to education aligns with broader national development efforts, including significant infrastructure projects designed to improve connectivity across the country.

Submitting Applications and Support Applicants are advised to submit their applications online through the KUCCPS Student Portal, which is accessible via the official KUCCPS website at kuccps.ac.ke. For students requiring assistance with their applications, support is available at the KUCCPS offices located at ACK Garden House, 1st Ngong Avenue, Nairobi. Additionally, students can seek help at Kenya Utalii College itself or utilize the services offered at any of the 58 Huduma Centres situated nationwide.
