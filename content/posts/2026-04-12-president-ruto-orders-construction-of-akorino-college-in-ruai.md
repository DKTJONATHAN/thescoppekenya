President William Ruto, speaking today, April 12, 2026, at State House, ordered the immediate construction of an Akorino community college in Ruai. Valued at Ksh 120 million and situated on a six-acre plot, the government has finalized the title deed handover, with building scheduled to commence next week.

Akorino College Construction Underway The Head of State confirmed that construction for the college would begin next week, with an anticipated completion timeframe of one year. President Ruto expressed optimism about holding a future meeting at the completed Ruai facility next year. He acknowledged that earlier political disagreements had caused delays but noted that current unity among stakeholders has now cleared the path for the project's progress.

Project Oversight and Key Personnel A contractor is slated for assignment next week to initiate works. President Ruto announced the formation of a trustee structure, designed to bring together all relevant parties to oversee the institution's development. This structure will include Principal Secretary Beatrice Inyangala from higher education, tasked with guiding the college's academic requirements, and Attorney General Dorcas Oduor, who will provide legal counsel for potential disputes.

Developing Talent and Community Capacity Beyond academic pursuits, the college is set to feature a music production studio, aiming to foster talent development within the Akorino faithful community. This initiative aligns with broader efforts to support local talent in the music industry. Additionally, a road leading to the college land is currently under construction to enhance accessibility. This commitment by President Ruto builds upon similar pledges made nearly two years ago, including a Ksh 50 million pledge for the Akorino Africa Bible College during a conference in Nakuru on June 9, 2024. The college will mark the first formal training school for the Akorino religious group, offering biblical studies and church leadership training.

Government Support Initiatives In addition to the college construction, the government has committed to collaborating with the church on training and capacity building for the Akorino Sacco, pledging millions to promote financial independence within the community. Furthermore, President Ruto ordered the reservation of 50 teaching slots for the Akorino faithful during the next Teachers Service Commission (TSC) recruitment drive, recognizing their capabilities in the education sector.
