## Kenyan Shilling Performance Sees 4.1% Gain Against Dollar in 2025

The Kenya National Bureau of Statistics (KNBS) reported the Kenyan Shilling gained 4.1 percent against the U.S. dollar in 2025, easing to Ksh129.30 from Ksh134.82 in 2024. This recovery, detailed in the KNBS Economic Survey Report 2025 released on April 29, was primarily driven by steadily improving foreign exchange conditions throughout the year.

### Shilling's Sustained Recovery

The Kenyan Shilling sustained its recovery momentum in 2025. The Trade Weighted Index (TWI) improved from 130.0 in 2024 to 127.6 in 2025, indicating a strengthening of the currency in real effective terms. This appreciation of 4.1 percent against the United States dollar was largely fueled by improved foreign exchange conditions. The shilling closed 2025 trading at 129.0 units against the U.S. dollar.

### Economic Growth and Broader Currency Performance

The country's economic growth registered 4.6 percent in 2025, a slight drop from the 4.7 percent growth recorded in 2024. This growth fell short of President William Ruto’s anticipated target of 5.6 percent. Beyond the dollar, the Kenyan Shilling performance also saw gains against other major currencies. It appreciated by 1.1 percent against the Pound Sterling, moving from Ksh172.27 in 2024 to Ksh170.40 in 2025. The shilling gained 3.1 percent against the Japanese Yen and posted its sharpest performance against the Indian Rupee at 8.0 percent.

Within the East African Community, the picture was mixed. The Rwanda Franc gained sharply against the shilling by 12.6 percent, and the Uganda Shilling edged ahead slightly. Conversely, the Tanzania Shilling lost ground against the Kenyan Shilling during the same period.

### Future Outlook for Kenyan Shilling Performance

Despite the positive 2025 performance, Commercial Bank of Kenya Governor Dr. Kamau Thugge expressed scepticism regarding the currency's behavior against the dollar this year. He cited various global factors, particularly the Middle East conflict, as potential influences pushing the Kenyan Shilling towards a weaker position. In an interview on April 9, Governor Thugge stated that the Central Bank had built up reserves to manage such shocks, asserting that exchange rate volatility remains manageable. The Kenyan shilling has remained broadly stable, supported by foreign exchange reserves providing more than five months of import cover and continued inflows such as diaspora remittances.
