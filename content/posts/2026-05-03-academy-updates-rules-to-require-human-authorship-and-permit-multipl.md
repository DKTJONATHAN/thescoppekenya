## Academy Reshapes Awards with AI Ban and Acting Category Twist

The Academy is turning Tinseltown on its head with groundbreaking new Oscars rules. Major changes include clamping down on artificial intelligence, demanding human authorship for screenplays, and allowing actors to snag multiple nominations in the same category. These shifts mark a bold evolution for the prestigious awards, signaling a future where human creativity is undeniably front and center.

### Hollywood's Human Touch

Hold up, robots! The Academy is drawing a clear line in the sand, ensuring the magic behind the silver screen remains distinctly human. New rules explicitly state that screenplays must be "human-authored" to even be considered for Best Adapted Screenplay or Best Original Screenplay. Similarly, acting roles must be "demonstrably performed by humans with their consent." This proactive stance battles the rising tide of AI, with the Academy reserving the right to request more information on the nature of any AI use. It is a clear message: human talent reigns supreme.

### Double the Talent, Double the Nods

Prepare for a shake-up in the acting races! For decades, a strict rule prevented actors from receiving more than one nomination in the same category within a single year. Now, that stipulation is history. Actors can potentially earn multiple nominations for stellar performances, provided those roles place in the top five votes. Imagine your favorite star going head-to-head with themselves for a gold statuette - the drama just intensified! This exciting change brings acting categories in line with other award sections, finally recognizing when a performer delivers not just one, but two or more unforgettable turns.

### The Future of the Gold Statuette

Beyond the creative regulations, the Academy is also mapping out a new path for the awards ceremony itself. The 101st Oscars in 2029 will usher in a new era, moving its annual broadcast from ABC to the YouTube streaming platform. Before that major shift, fans can mark their calendars for the 99th telecast on Sunday, March 14, 2027, and the 100th Academy Awards on Sunday, March 5, 2028, both airing live from the Dolby Theatre in Los Angeles. Expect more clarity on other categories too, like end credit song eligibility, with a new rule specifying requirements for songs submitted as the first new music cue when credits roll.

### Remembering the Recent Victories

Rewinding to the most recent ceremony this past March, cinephiles celebrated Paul Thomas Anderson's *One Battle After Another*, which swept the competition, clinching both Best Picture and Best Director. The acting categories saw a vibrant mix of talent take home the gold, with Michael B. Jordan recognized for *Sinners*, Jessie Buckley for *Hamnet*, Sean Penn for his powerful turn in *One Battle After Another*, and Amy Madigan for her work in *Weapons*. These celebrated performances set a high bar, reminding everyone of the human brilliance these new rules aim to protect and celebrate.
