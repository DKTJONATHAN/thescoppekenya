But while the pros were minting money, our precious junior players, after bravely battling world beaters in Karen, walked away with... inspiration. Honestly, just inspiration!

But here’s the kicker, mate: not a single one of them won the fully funded Absa Junior Account worth Sh50,000 this year! (Can you even imagine?). <h3>Battling World Beaters: Just a Photo Op for the Junior Players at Magical Kenya Open?</h3>
They call them "world beaters," these professional golfers, and our juniors certainly had a go at them. Muthoni Kioi, a plucky 13-year-old, even took on pros from Spain, England, and the Netherlands. She said she learnt how to "stay focused" and "be emotionally balanced." Bless her heart! Jemile Gacheru, a Form 3 student, played alongside international pros too, noting their "consistency." And Alpha Ndungu, playing off a handicap of 3.0 (very respectable!), was "elated" to be pitted against a former MKO champion. They all said it was "eye-opening" and "grateful for the experience." But honestly, while the exposure is lovely, when you're battling world beaters, surely a little bit of prize money wouldn't go amiss, would it? It’s all well and good to talk about "talent development," but a bit of tangible reward never hurt anyone, did it?

He said the "Beat the Pro" challenge is all about inspiring future champions. And yes, the juniors themselves spoke of great lessons and encouragement. They got golf balls and tips! But as a gossip columnist, I'm thinking: is this truly setting them up for success, or is it a bit of a budget-friendly way to get good PR?
