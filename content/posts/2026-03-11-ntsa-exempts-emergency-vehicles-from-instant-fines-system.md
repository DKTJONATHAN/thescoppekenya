## NTSA Exempts Emergency Vehicles from Instant Fines The National Transport and Safety Authority (NTSA) has announced that emergency service vehicles will be exempt from the newly implemented instant fines system. This exemption applies to ambulances, police, and military vehicles, which frequently exceed speed limits during urgent responses. All other motorists, including drivers of four-wheelers, three-wheelers, and motorcycles, remain subject to the system.

Emergency Service Exemption Details NTSA Director General Nahashon Kondiwa, speaking on March 10, confirmed the exemption for emergency vehicles. He acknowledged that these services often necessitate higher speeds, making their inclusion in the instant fines system impractical. This clarification ensures critical response times for public safety and security are not hindered by automated penalties.

Universal Application for Other Motorists Kondiwa emphasized that the instant fines system is otherwise comprehensive, applying to the majority of vehicles on the nation's major highways. The advanced cameras deployed possess the capability to read both front and rear license plates, ensuring no motorist, regardless of vehicle type, escapes detection. The system aims for broad enforcement of traffic regulations to enhance road safety.

Mechanism for Appeals and Evidence Motorists who receive fines under the new system will have a clear process to challenge penalties they believe are wrongly issued. Kondiwa stated that an appeal process is integral, acknowledging that errors can occur. Courts will retain the authority to review and resolve these disputes. The automated system generates verifiable evidence, including images and short GIF clips of the vehicle at the moment an offense occurs, which will be crucial for both enforcement and appeals.

Continued Manual Enforcement and Clear Road Signage Despite the technological advancements of the instant fines system, Kondiwa noted that certain traffic offenses cannot be captured by cameras. Consequently, enforcement officers, including the National Police Service, will continue to patrol roads and manually enforce regulations where necessary. Additionally, NTSA assures motorists that all camera-monitored sections of roads will be distinctly marked. Clear signage indicating legal speed limits will accompany every speed camera location, informing drivers as they enter designated speed-monitoring zones. Speed limits will be determined by road design and surrounding land use.
