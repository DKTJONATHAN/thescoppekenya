Nairobi Governor Johnson Sakaja is actively being sought by police for contempt of parliament following a Senate Watchdog committee's recommendations. A manhunt for the county boss commenced late Monday, March 30, 2026, as authorities pursued his arrest outside City Hall. Preliminary reports suggest the governor may have evaded initial capture.

Law Enforcement Initiates Manhunt Regional Police Commander Issa Mohamud confirmed that the operation stems from a resolution passed last week by the Senate County Public Accounts Committee (CPAC). Mohamud stated that law enforcement is "following orders issued by the Inspector General of Police and will take the necessary steps." Authorities reportedly camped outside City Hall for a significant portion of Monday evening, March 30, though initial efforts to locate Governor Sakaja proved unsuccessful.

Senate Ultimatum and Fines On March 25, 2026, the Senate CPAC issued an ultimatum to Inspector General of Police Douglas Kanja, demanding Sakaja's appearance before the committee by Monday, March 30. CPAC chair Moses Kajwang' emphasized that failure to produce the governor within the stipulated timeframe would lead to a resolution declaring Sakaja unfit for public office. The committee also imposed a KSh 500,000 fine on Governor Sakaja for his failure to honor previous summons.

Scrutiny Over Audit Irregularities Governor Sakaja's current predicament intensifies the ongoing scrutiny over Nairobi's audit report. His absence from the committee hearing on March 25 led the CPAC to adopt the Auditor General's report as presented. The report flagged Nairobi County for significant irregularities and misappropriation of public resources. A particular point of contention from the audit report was the controversial revelation that the governor allegedly employed seven advisors, each reportedly earning a monthly salary of KSh 203,000. These issues underscore a broader need for accountability in public service, impacting various sectors from governance to educational opportunities, as seen in efforts like those by KUCCPS to identify marketable degree programmes.

Similar Orders Issued Nairobi's situation is not isolated. Similar orders have also been directed towards Samburu Governor Jonathan Lati Lelelit, who is likewise being sought to address pressing questions regarding his administration. Both cases highlight the Senate's commitment to upholding parliamentary oversight and ensuring accountability from county executives.
