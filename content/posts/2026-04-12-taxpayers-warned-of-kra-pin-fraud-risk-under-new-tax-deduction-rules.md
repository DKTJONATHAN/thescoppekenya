Individuals are cautioned against unwarranted sharing of their KRA Personal Identification Numbers, following a rise in fraudulent expense claims ahead of the 2025 tax filing period. This urgent warning comes amidst new Kenya Revenue Authority guidelines that expose taxpayers to exploitation by fraudsters seeking PINs for illicit deductions.

Taxpayer PIN Fraud Warning The Kenya Revenue Authority (KRA) has introduced new tax deduction guidelines that, while aiming to streamline processes, have inadvertently created a loophole exploited by dishonest individuals. The warning emerged from a concerned taxpayer who observed an alarming trend of tax cheats actively seeking KRA PINs for use in fraudulent expense claims pertaining to the 2025 tax year. Many do not possess eTIMS receipts to substantiate expenses for 2025, and KRA now permits the deduction of such expenses under specific conditions.

New Deduction Rules Create Loophole Under the updated provisions linked to KRA's electronic tax invoice system, eTIMS, taxpayers can now claim certain business expenses even in the absence of electronic receipts. However, this is contingent upon submitting a detailed list of these expenses to KRA before filing returns, critically including the supplier's KRA PIN for each transaction. This requirement has been identified as the primary vulnerability, as it allows individuals to utilize randomly obtained PINs from unsuspecting individuals to fabricate expense justifications.

Consequences of PIN Misuse The implications of a compromised KRA PIN are significant. If an individual's PIN is erroneously used in another person's expense records, KRA systems may interpret this as undeclared sales by the legitimate PIN owner, subsequently triggering tax compliance inquiries. Affected individuals could receive official communications from KRA indicating millions in alleged undeclared sales, necessitating lengthy explanations and visits to tax offices to resolve the discrepancies. For example, individuals might face substantial tax bills for commercial activities they never conducted but which were linked to their identification number. Furthermore, stolen KRA PINs are also employed by fraudsters to register fictitious companies for purposes of money laundering and sophisticated tax evasion schemes. Even the uncancelled PINs of deceased individuals are frequently exploited for similar criminal undertakings.

Safeguarding Your Information Taxpayers are strongly encouraged to exercise extreme caution with their sensitive documents and login credentials. A common point of vulnerability involves leaving tax information with cyber attendants, which compromises the security chain and complicates accountability in cases of fraud. It is imperative that individuals secure their tax information diligently. Fraudsters additionally leverage social media platforms, often impersonating KRA officials to trick individuals into divulging private information or transferring funds to "clear" penalties. KRA has repeatedly cautioned against sharing credentials on social media, advising individuals to always confirm the legitimacy of any agency requesting information. Just as the public evaluates the sincerity behind high-profile charity walks like Eric Omondi’s 466km Busia journey, critical assessment is paramount when approached with unsolicited requests online.
