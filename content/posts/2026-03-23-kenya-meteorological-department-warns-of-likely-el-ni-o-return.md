The Kenya Meteorological Department warns of a likely return of the El Niño weather phenomenon later this year, with probabilities rising to 58-61 percent by mid-to-late 2026. The department's climate outlook report indicates this could intensify rainfall during the October-November-December short rains, impacting central and eastern regions.

Projected El Niño Impact

Timing and Probabilities The El Niño-Southern Oscillation (ENSO) outlook indicates that while the first half of 2026 will remain relatively stable, the transition toward El Niño conditions could begin around the middle of the year. Projections suggest a 58-61 percent probability of El Niño conditions dominating from June to December 2026, intensifying during the October-November-December (OND) short rains. El Niño, a natural climate phenomenon, is characterized by warmer-than-average sea surface temperatures in the central and eastern equatorial Pacific Ocean, occurring every two to seven years and disrupting global atmospheric circulation.

Anticipated Effects Enhanced rainfall during the OND short rains is projected, particularly in eastern and central regions, potentially alleviating dry season deficits. However, this also raises risks of flooding in vulnerable river basins. Such conditions underscore the importance of robust infrastructure, as seen with past directives for evacuations when [Nairobi Dam risks bursting](https://zandani.co.ke/article/government-orders-evacuation-of-kibera-and-nearby-estates-as-nairobi). Projected advantages include boosted crop yields and hydropower generation. Challenges could involve increased vector-borne disease outbreaks and strain on existing infrastructure.

Regional Rainfall Outlook For the March-April-May (MAM) season, near-average to above-average rainfall is expected over the Lake Victoria Basin, the Highlands West of the Rift Valley, the Highlands East of the Rift Valley (including Nairobi), the Rift Valley, and parts of North-western Kenya. Near-average to below-average rainfall is anticipated over the Southeastern Lowlands, Northeastern, and parts of Northwestern Kenya. The Coastal region is expected to experience below-average rainfall.

Mitigation and Preparedness The report emphasizes that the forecast remains probabilistic, contingent on evolving ocean temperatures. For drier regions, measures such as drought-tolerant crops, water harvesting, conflict resolution, and livelihood diversification programs like cash transfers are recommended to bolster food security and reduce migration. In wetter areas, focus on early warnings, infrastructure reinforcement including dikes and drainage systems, disease surveillance, and value addition for surpluses can minimize losses, while promoting integrated pest management and efficient water use supports resilience.
