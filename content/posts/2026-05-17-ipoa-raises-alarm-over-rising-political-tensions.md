## Election security and the ghosts of our past

As the 2027 general elections approach, the Independent Policing Oversight Authority has sounded an alarm concerning the resurgence of organized political violence. We currently stand at a dangerous juncture where only immediate, decisive intervention can prevent a total failure of our institutions. 

To the Inspector General of Police:

The era of issuing sterilized briefings and guarded press statements has concluded. Your office sits at a critical threshold. The actions you take in the coming 48 hours will determine whether our national security framework prioritizes the public good or facilitates the instability currently germinating within political circles.

### The 1992 precedent

This nation has traversed these treacherous grounds previously. The structural violence that left deep scars on our country in 1992 originated in the same manner as current events: through the systematic recruitment of youth militias masked as political mobilization. History serves as more than a collection of memories; it acts as a strategic blueprint for those intent on destabilizing the state for electoral leverage. By failing to address the early indicators of gang formation and the bravado displayed by political strongmen, the security sector inadvertently suggests that the law is a flexible concept. One cannot feign shock when an inferno consumes the house after ignoring the sparks for weeks. The warnings from 1992 are not merely historical records; they represent a direct order for you to dismantle these criminal enterprises before they evolve into large-scale conflict.

### Patterns of recruitment

Field intelligence points to a worrying surge in gang recruitment linked to political interests. These groups are not emerging from organic frustration. Instead, they are receiving funding, transport, and various incentives from individuals who perceive the approaching election as a fight for survival. While the government directs significant energy toward recovering public assets, such as the effort to reclaim Ksh 3 billion from importers, there exists a far more perilous deficit in the erosion of our collective safety. When political actors offer protection or financial kickbacks in exchange for intimidation, they effectively privatize the use of force. You must acknowledge these maneuvers for exactly what they are: an assault on the state’s constitutional monopoly on power. If your officers fail to disrupt the recruitment phase, subsequent waves of electoral violence will prove far more difficult to suppress.

### The cost of silence

Citizens are exhausted by the cycle of reactive policing. We have witnessed too many preventable tragedies, including the recent, horrific instance of violence in Nakuru where a life was lost over an triviality, leaving a child to witness the brutality. That incident stands as a brutal reminder of the consequences when security institutions fail the most vulnerable. If the police continue to grant political figures immunity, you are effectively signaling to every aspiring warlord that the 2027 election is open for exploitation. The Inspector General holds a clear duty to look past the political leanings of these agitators and prioritize the security of the people who will suffer most from rising instability.

### Why it matters now

The next 13 months are vital. If the police do not establish a credible deterrent presence immediately, the momentum of these gangs will become self-sustaining. Permitting the normalization of violence as a campaign tool creates a precedent that will be nearly impossible to reverse once the election reaches its peak intensity.

### What to watch next

Monitor the actions of regional commanders over the next two days. Look for genuine, thorough investigations into reported gang leaders, as opposed to the typical cycle of issuing polite, ineffective warnings. Should there be a lack of arrests regarding those currently organizing these groups, it will become evident that the security sector has prioritized political expediency over its legal obligation to maintain the peace.

I urge the Inspector General to set aside the pretense of neutrality when confronting criminal activity. The public requires a tangible, concrete plan to identify and neutralize these emerging political gangs within the next 48 hours. If you fail to halt the recruitment today, you will certainly be unable to stop the war tomorrow.
