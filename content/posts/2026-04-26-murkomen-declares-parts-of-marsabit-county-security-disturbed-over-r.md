Interior Cabinet Secretary Kipchumba Murkomen has officially declared parts of Marsabit County as security-disturbed and dangerous due to escalating banditry and inter-communal violence. This declaration, made in consultation with the National Security Council and National Police Service, was formally published in the Kenya Gazette on April 24, taking effect on April 23, 2026, at 6:30 p.m. The order is set to last thirty days, with potential for extension or withdrawal based on the region's security status.

## Heightened Security Measures in Marsabit

The declaration encompasses thirteen specific areas, all designated with the "Hillo" prefix, which are particularly affected by the unrest. These include Hillo Gorgora, Hillo Orofa, Hillo Walkite, Hillo Tanzania, Hillo Irress Abamartille, Hillo Gootu, Hillo Tessum Qalicha, Hillo Karray, Hillo Hudda, Hillo Qoranjido, Hillo Irress Shindia, Hillo Rabaalee, and Hillo Godde Haroressa. These zones collectively represent a deeply unsettled part of northern Kenya.

### Legal Framework and Enforcement

The legal foundation for this action is Section 106(6) of the National Police Service Act, 2011, a provision that grants the Cabinet Secretary significant authority to address severe security deterioration. This exercise of executive power comes amidst a period where public officials face increased scrutiny and calls for accountability. On April 23, Inspector General Douglas Kanja reinforced the declaration by ordering all residents within the named areas to immediately surrender any firearms in their possession, with strict enforcement measures to follow.

### Drivers of Conflict

At the core of the persistent unrest in Marsabit are inter-ethnic tensions and retaliatory attacks. Communities in the region have repeatedly clashed over land and livestock, perpetuating a cycle of violence that profoundly impacts local families.

### Border Facility Controversy

Adding to the regional tension is an ongoing controversy surrounding the construction of a new security facility at the Marsabit-Wajir border. Interior Cabinet Secretary Kipchumba Murkomen has halted the construction of a multi-agency security installation at Basir over a boundary tussle between Wajir and Marsabit counties. This project, initially launched on April 4, 2026, aimed to establish a permanent security presence in a corridor identified for arms smuggling, narcotics, and human trafficking.

### Travel Advisories and Prior Declarations

Current travel advisories warn of high risks from armed banditry, especially within 110 kilometers of the South Sudan and Ethiopia borders, urging extreme caution or avoidance of travel to these areas. This recent declaration for Marsabit marks the second such notice in less than a month. Weeks prior, CS Murkomen also declared parts of Laikipia, Meru, and Isiolo counties as security-disturbed and dangerous. That declaration took effect on March 26, 2026, for a similar 30-day period, subject to extension or withdrawal.
