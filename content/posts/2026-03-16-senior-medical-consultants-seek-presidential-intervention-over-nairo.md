Prominent medical specialists, long-serving at Nairobi Hospital, have escalated their fervent pleas to President William Ruto, urgently seeking his intervention in the unfolding drama. Allegations of monumental financial impropriety and governance chaos now threaten to dismantle Kenya’s esteemed 70-year-old medical pillar.

Presidential Patronage Called Upon Amid Hospital Turmoil The core of the matter centers on the Nairobi Hospital's unique public character and the President's role as Patron of the Kenya Hospital Association (KHA). Senior consultants, some with over two decades of practice at the institution, report they approached the Office of the Chief of Staff and Head of Public Service, Mr. Felix Koskei, in March 2026, detailing years of mismanagement and financial impropriety. This appeal led the Attorney General to obtain Search and Seizure Warrants under Section 800 of the Companies Act to investigate corporate fraud. The resulting operation recovered a treasure trove of documents, some hidden and others dramatically rescued from the hospital's incinerator during an alleged attempt to destroy evidence. In November 2026, with governance challenges persisting, the consultants sought intervention from the Cabinet Secretary for Health, Hon. Aden Duale, outlining continued mismanagement and an insurance boycott that crippled the hospital. Despite these efforts, the crisis persisted, leading to the direct appeal to the President in March 2026, as the institution teetered on the brink of collapse.

The Vanishing Billions and Startling Deficits The financial health of Nairobi Hospital is a staggering concern. Audited statements reveal the hospital has suffered losses exceeding KES 3 billion, with a deficit of approximately KES 2 billion in 2026 alone. Suppliers are reportedly owed in excess of KES 4 billion, causing crucial deliveries of essential drugs and medical equipment to be suspended. Further adding to the financial mystery, cash and cash equivalent reserves of KES 9.1 billion, accumulated through depreciation, have allegedly vanished or been siphoned out over the years.

A Courtroom Saga: Litigation Fever Grips the Board The hospital's Board has plunged the institution into a slew of litigations, with legal fees skyrocketing to over KES 680 million in 2026. These substantial legal battles are reportedly solely focused on retaining leadership rather than addressing crucial aspects of patient care, raising questions about the priorities at play.

The Curious Case of the Stacked Members' Register A highly publicized allegation involves the manipulation of the Members' Register, a bold move to allegedly capture control of the hospital. Over 300 individuals were reportedly loaded onto the register, their membership paid for through a single lump-sum payment of KES 5 million, bypassing valid nomination processes, vetting, or Board approval. These newly added members were then transported to the Hospital in buses to vote at the Annual General Meeting, a clear attempt to rig votes and entrench specific individuals in power.

Incinerator Escapes and Defiance of Justice The attempted destruction of documents, with some rescued from the incinerator and others found hidden, points to a potential "consciousness of guilt," according to the consultants. Furthermore, in November 2026, Hon. Justice Prof. (Dr.) Sifuna issued conservatory orders in the case of Milimani HCCC No. E293 of 2025, Peter Wainaina, Maurice Ambani & 8 Others v. Felix Osano, Gilbert Nyamweya & 11 Others. These orders explicitly restrained the Board of Directors, including the CEO and Company Secretary, from transacting with the hospital’s financial investments, convening any Board meetings or AGMs, and procuring new or ongoing capital projects. Despite these clear directives, the CEO and Company Secretary have reportedly violated these injunctive orders, even calling an AGM in an apparent attempt to solidify their positions.

The Consultants' Plea and a Question of Guilt While the recovered documents form the basis of ongoing criminal proceedings, the consultants expressed concern regarding the arrest of Dr. Job Obwaka. They highlighted that Dr. Obwaka is neither a current member of the Board nor was he a member when the register of members was allegedly manipulated, advocating for his exoneration. The consultants maintain that this is not a government takeover, but a necessary step to address incompetence and corruption, asserting that the hospital belongs to its members and must be saved for the thousands of patients who rely on it.
