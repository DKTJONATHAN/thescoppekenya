## Kenya's Digital Migration: Internet Calls Reshape Communications Traditional circuit-switched voice traffic is declining globally and in Kenya, superseded by internet-based calling platforms like WhatsApp. This technological shift, spanning all generations, demonstrates that connectivity has outpaced conventional telephony. Data reveals a consistent drop in standard voice calls, indicating a permanent value transfer from network operators to Over-the-Top (OTT) platforms.

Global Communications Restructure International voice traffic experienced significant growth for nearly a century, peaking in 2014 before beginning an annual decline. TeleGeography, a prominent telecommunications data firm, reported a 7.0% drop in 2024 alone, with wholesale carrier traffic falling to approximately 284 billion minutes from a peak exceeding 500 billion minutes. The introduction of WhatsApp voice calls in 2015 marked a pivotal moment, with OTT traffic surpassing international carrier voice traffic by 2016. This global trend underscores that the reduction in traditional voice calls signifies a shift towards alternative communication methods rather than decreased human interaction.

Safaricom's Revenue Transformation In Kenya, the global trend is precisely mirrored by Safaricom, the dominant telco holding over 63% mobile market share. Safaricom's voice revenue steadily climbed from Kshs 77.3 billion in FY2013 to a peak of Kshs 95.6 billion in FY2018, but has since declined, reaching Kshs 80.8 billion by FY2025-a 15.5% drop from its peak. This decline closely parallels the global trajectory of traditional voice erosion. Conversely, Safaricom’s mobile data revenue has surged dramatically, increasing eleven-fold from Kshs 6.6 billion in FY2013 to Kshs 72.9 billion in FY2025. Based on this trajectory, mobile data revenue is projected to surpass voice revenue for the first time in the company's history within the next financial year.

Connectivity Fuels OTT Dominance Kenya's aggressive expansion of mobile connectivity has created an environment ripe for widespread OTT adoption. The Communications Authority of Kenya (CA) reported 78.3 million mobile SIM subscriptions in the first quarter of FY2025/26, reflecting a 149.4% penetration rate, with 59.5% being smartphones. The country also boasts over 2.27 million fixed data subscriptions. This robust infrastructure has directly facilitated the proliferation of OTT platforms, with 54.4% of Kenyans using WhatsApp in the fourth quarter of FY2024/25. Meta's platforms continue to expand their footprint, with Facebook reaching 17 million users, Instagram at 3.95 million, and Messenger at 2.80 million users. These platforms offer cost-effective voice and video calls, making them the preferred, often sole, option for many Kenyan users communicating locally or internationally. The shift reflects a fundamental restructuring of global communications, transferring significant value from network operators to OTT platforms.
