## Runway Ready for The Devil Wears Prada 3?

The fashion world is still buzzing from *The Devil Wears Prada 2*'s theatrical debut, but the star-studded cast and visionary director are already eyeing a possible third installment. Meryl Streep, Anne Hathaway, Stanley Tucci, Emily Blunt, and David Frankel hint at more couture drama, leaving fans eager for Miranda Priestly's next reign.

### The Stars Are Aligning

Fresh off the sequel's success, the iconic ensemble, including Streep, Hathaway, Tucci, and Blunt, along with director David Frankel, shared their optimism for a *Devil Wears Prada 3*. Streep, at 76, playfully quipped to Entertainment Weekly about the twenty-year wait between the 2006 original and its 2026 successor, a sentiment echoed by Tucci. "I don't think we can do it in another 20 years. We have to do it sooner than that, because I'll be dead," Tucci joked to EW, adding that a third film might even require their "exhumation" from the grave.

### More Screentime for This Duo

Emily Blunt and Stanley Tucci, who are related by marriage as Tucci wed Blunt's sister Felicity in 2012, expressed a desire for their characters to share more scenes in a future film. Blunt noted, "We love what we got to do, but we didn't get enough to do together," indicating their hope for "a little more to chew on together" in a third chapter.

### Box Office Speaks Volumes

Anne Hathaway grounded the high-fashion dreams with a dose of reality, suggesting that *The Devil Wears Prada 2*'s box office performance will ultimately determine if they return for another round. "I hope so, I feel like there's more road to explore," she told EW, adding, "But, we'll probably have a better idea of that [when the film comes out]." The first film, released in 2006, commanded global grosses of $327 million and earned two Oscar nominations, including one for Streep.

### Director's Vision for the Future

Director David Frankel, who helmed both the original and the recent sequel, believes the beloved characters have stories beyond the current narrative. "There's a life for the characters beyond this story," Frankel stated. While he previously dismissed sequel talks after the 2006 film and even after author Lauren Weisberger's follow-up book, he acknowledges the unpredictable nature of show business. "Let's see what happens. It's show business. It's anybody's guess. We said no 20 years ago, and here we are," Frankel admitted. "I don't want to say yes now. They are very rich characters, they're amazing actors, there's great screenwriting, so I could imagine that these characters might live on." *The Devil Wears Prada 2* is currently enjoying its run in theaters nationwide.
