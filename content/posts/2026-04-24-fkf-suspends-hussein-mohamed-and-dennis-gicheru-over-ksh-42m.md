Football Kenya Federation President Hussein Mohamed, along with NEC member Abdullahi Yussuf Ibrahim and Acting CEO Dennis Gicheru, were suspended today, April 24, over alleged financial impropriety involving Ksh42 million meant for the African Nations Championship. Deputy President Macdonald Mariga now leads in an acting capacity.

## FKF Leadership Shake-Up

### Allegations and Immediate Actions

The decision, announced on April 24, saw the trio ordered to step aside to allow for comprehensive investigations into claims of financial impropriety. The allegations centre on the misappropriation of approximately Ksh42 million from accounts designated for the African Nations Championship (CHAN). This sum raises significant concerns regarding the management of funds related to the continental tournament.

The National Executive Committee (NEC), with the resolution signed by 9 of its 14 members, directed the immediate freezing of all FKF bank accounts linked to President Mohamed in his official capacity. This measure aims to preserve evidence and prevent any interference with the ongoing probe. The committee further called for the appointment of an independent forensic auditor to scrutinise all funds received by the federation from key stakeholders, including the Confederation of African Football (CAF), FIFA, and the Ministry of Sports. The NEC stated that the allegations facing the suspended officials include financial impropriety, conflict of interest, and non-competitive procurement of goods and services.

### Mariga Takes Interim Helm

In a sweeping resolution, the NEC mandated that Deputy President Macdonald Mariga immediately assume the role of FKF President in an acting capacity. His appointment is pending the outcome of investigations and a forensic audit. Mariga has been tasked with convening an urgent NEC meeting to chart the way forward and stabilise the federation's operations, ensuring continuity amidst the leadership transition.

### Mohamed Denies Claims

The embattled Hussein Mohamed on Thursday pushed back against the allegations surrounding the disputed Ksh42.4 million insurance payment. He termed the claims a malicious campaign intended to damage his reputation. "Once I started the process of cleaning house, it was inevitable that corruption would fight back," Mohamed stated. "Through such a malicious campaign, a lifetime of sacrifice, dedication, and investment to help develop our social fabric through sports can go up in smoke, just like that. Reputation ruined forever. I shall not allow it."

### Safeguarding Football's Integrity

The NEC meeting, which three members-Charles Njagi, Ahmedqadar Mohammed Dabar, and Kenneth Rungu-did not attend, underscored the necessity of restoring public confidence in the federation. Safeguarding the integrity of Kenyan football was cited as a key reason behind the drastic action. This move aligns with broader calls for accountability in public and institutional leadership, mirroring the scrutiny applied to other prominent figures, such as when [Kimani Ichung’wah explained why he blocked President William Ruto](https://zandani.co.ke/article/kimani-ichung-wah-explains-why-he-blocked-president-william-ruto).
