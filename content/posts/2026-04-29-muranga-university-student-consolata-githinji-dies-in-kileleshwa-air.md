Consolata Githinji, a 22-year-old Muranga University student, died on April 26, 2026, after allegedly falling from a sixth-floor Airbnb apartment in Kileleshwa, Nairobi. The incident led to the arrest of 33-year-old Tony Odhiambo, sparking a police investigation.

## Muranga University Student Consolata Githinji Dies in Kileleshwa Airbnb Fall, Tony Odhiambo Arrested

The tragic incident unfolded when Consolata Githinji was captured on CCTV footage entering the Kileleshwa apartment with Tony Odhiambo. Reports suggest a commotion may have occurred inside the residence before her fatal fall. Odhiambo, who was with Githinji at the time, claims she jumped after an argument, though police have apprehended him in connection with her death. Authorities are currently piecing together the events of that fateful night, investigating whether foul play was involved or if her death was a suicide. The incident has left many questioning the safety of short-term rentals and the circumstances surrounding young Kenyans' tragic demises.

As investigations continue, a familiar, unsettling narrative has begun to surface within certain circles. The tragedy of Consolata Githinji's death has, for some, become an unfortunate platform for scrutinizing her lifestyle choices rather than focusing on the actual events leading to her demise. The insinuation then, as now, is that women's choices somehow justify or mitigate the circumstances of their misfortune.

Beneath the surface of seemingly concerned statements like "anyway, we are not justifying gender-based violence, but ladies need to love themselves and be careful," lies a sharp satirical jab.
