### Ecological Triggers Behind the Reptile Influx

The recent uptick in snake sightings across Mombasa is a direct byproduct of the ongoing rainy season. Kenya Wildlife Service (KWS) experts explain that heavy precipitation causes significant ecological displacement. As floodwaters submerge subterranean dens and burrows, snakes are instinctually driven to seek refuge in drier, elevated locations. Unfortunately, human residential zones often provide exactly the type of elevated cover these animals require.

Furthermore, the migration of small mammals-the primary food source for many snake species-acts as a secondary attractant. When these rodents move toward human structures to escape the damp, snakes naturally follow the trail. Construction sites are particularly prone to these incursions. Piles of building materials, discarded lumber, and unmanaged drainage systems offer the perfect micro-habitats for reptiles. By occupying these zones, humans inadvertently provide a sanctuary for wildlife, leading to the frequent, high-stakes encounters that have left many residents feeling anxious and vulnerable.

### Proactive Safety and Habitat Modification

To mitigate these risks, KWS officials are urging coastal homeowners to move beyond reactive measures and focus on environmental hygiene. The most effective way to deter snakes is to remove their food and shelter. Residents are advised to clear all vegetation within at least ten meters of their dwellings, as tall grass provides cover for predators and prey alike. Furthermore, the removal of rubble, woodpiles, and general debris is essential, as these items create the dark, cool crevices that snakes find inviting.

Structural integrity also plays a vital role. Homeowners should inspect their properties for potential entry points, particularly gaps beneath external doors and vents. Installing fine-mesh screens over drainage outlets and ventilation ducts can effectively block transit routes. If an encounter occurs, experts stress the importance of restraint. Attempting to kill or trap a snake significantly increases the risk of a defensive bite. Instead, the public is encouraged to retreat to a safe distance and contact professional wildlife removal services, which are equipped to handle the animals without jeopardizing human safety.
