Senator Allan Chesang today, March 20, 2026, initiated legal action against Standard Group PLC, serving a cease-and-desist notice over a report he terms false and malicious. The publication allegedly links him to a fraudulent ambulance tender scheme at Harambee House, demanding a retraction within 48 hours.

Senator Chesang Issues Ultimatum to Standard Group

Allegations of Fraudulent Scheme Involvement Trans Nzoia Senator Allan Chesang has threatened legal action against Standard Group PLC concerning a story published in The Standard newspaper and broadcast on KTN. The report, titled 'The Masterminds of the Harambee House Tender Scam', allegedly implicated Senator Chesang in a fraudulent ambulance tender scheme. His legal representatives assert the story was published without any consultation with their client, directly linking him to the scheme alongside two individuals identified as his allies. Senator Chesang's law firm maintains that their client "categorically denies any involvement whatsoever in the said alleged scheme and further denies any knowledge of, or association with, the individuals referred to as his aides." The legal team argues the publication moved beyond fair reporting, painting the senator as a "mastermind and willing participant in corrupt dealings," thereby stripping him of his presumption of integrity.

Disputed Past Accusations and Amplified Damage The legal notice also addressed an older accusation within the story, referencing a 2021 case involving a businessman and a Ksh180 million laptop supply to the deputy president's office. The publication claimed Senator Chesang was implicated, arrested, and arraigned in January 2021 over alleged tender forgery. These claims have been dismissed by his lawyers as "wholly false, unsubstantiated, and published with deliberate malicious intent." The broad dissemination of the story across KTN's Facebook, YouTube, and national television platforms is cited by Chesang's advocates as having "dramatically amplified the damage," potentially reaching millions and associating him with the scandal. "Our client’s attention has been drawn to an article published in the Standard Newspaper dated Friday, 20th March 2026 in both print and online formats, as well as the KTNnews broadcast on Facebook, YouTube and nationwide televisions disseminated on 20th March 2026, including but not limited to the headline," the lawsuit stressed.

Unresolved Standoff and Demands The legal notice further alleges that Standard Group abandoned basic journalistic standards, proceeding with the story without verifying facts or allowing the senator an opportunity to respond to the allegations. Senator Chesang is demanding the immediate removal of both the newspaper article and the KTN broadcast from all platforms, including their website and active social media channels. As of March 20, 2026, Standard Group PLC has not issued a public response to the senator's demands, leaving the situation unresolved.
