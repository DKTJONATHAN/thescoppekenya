**Murang’a County, KALRO Initiate Digital Soil Fertility Mapping Training for Countywide Rollout** **By Timothy Muli** **Nairobi, Kenya** The Kenya Agricultural and Livestock Research Organization (KALRO), in close partnership with the Murang’a County Government, has officially launched a comprehensive training programme designed to equip local agricultural extension officers, IT specialists, and agripreneurs. This critical preparatory phase precedes a countywide Digital Soil Fertility Mapping Exercise, which commenced on February 23, 2026. The extensive mapping initiative is set to cover all 35 wards within Murang’a County. Its primary objective is to generate precise, ward-specific soil fertility data. Officials anticipate this information will be instrumental in providing farmers with tailored guidance on nutrient management, thereby bolstering evidence-based agricultural planning and policy formulation at the county level. Technical leadership for the exercise is provided by KALRO experts, who are overseeing data accuracy, digital efficiency, and quality control. The technical team is spearheaded by Dr. Daniel Gikaara, a distinguished soil scientist and cluster team leader, alongside James M. Mureithi, KALRO’s Research Extension Liaison Officer for Murang’a County. They receive support from Reuben Mwangi of Natural Resource Management (NRM) and Gloria Amati, the IT expert responsible for the digital components of the project. Speaking at the launch, Paul Kimani Mugo, the County Executive Committee Member for Agriculture, Trade and Cooperatives, affirmed the initiative's significance as a major stride towards modernizing agricultural decision-making processes. "Reliable soil data is essential for tailoring fertilizer recommendations to local conditions, reducing input waste and improving farm productivity," stated Mugo, underscoring the practical benefits for farmers. Other notable attendees at the event included Dr. Apollo Maina, Peter Nyamora, and Regina Thumaine, all of whom reiterated the paramount importance of data-driven approaches in effective soil health management. Both county and KALRO leadership expressed strong optimism that the digital soil fertility mapping will serve a dual purpose: guiding fertilizer policy at both county and national levels, while simultaneously strengthening soil health management, enhancing crop nutrition, and ultimately boosting overall agricultural productivity across Murang’a County.
