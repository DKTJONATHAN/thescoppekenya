Nigerian music stars Ice Prince, Duncan Mighty, and Peruzzi energized the All Progressives Congress national convention at Abuja's Eagle Square this Friday, March 27th, and Saturday, March 28th, 2026, performing for thousands of delegates and party loyalists. The two-day event blended political discourse with vibrant musical performances, drawing over 8,000 attendees from across the nation.

Music Takes Centre Stage at APC Convention

A Charged Political Gathering The atmosphere inside Eagle Square was notably charged, reflecting the energy typical of live performances by popular artistes. Supporters gathered to pledge allegiance to President Tinubu, whose address from the podium touched on national issues. Tinubu paid tribute to former President Muhammadu Buhari and addressed concerns about political dominance, stating, "Let me reiterate, especially to those who often lament and misunderstand us, we do not seek a one-party state." He also claimed his administration is actively tackling Nigeria's persistent electricity crisis, despite ongoing power supply challenges. For the youth, he offered a message of hope: "You are not only the future of Nigeria, you are our hope for a more glorious and assured future."

Artists Face Public Scrutiny The appearances of Ice Prince, Duncan Mighty, and Peruzzi at the government event quickly ignited a wave of public criticism across social media. Many Nigerians voiced their disapproval, arguing that lending their platforms to the ruling party amounted to an endorsement of an administration some hold responsible for current national hardships. Comments online called for an end to their music careers, accusing the artistes of prioritizing personal gain over national solidarity. This debate surrounding the involvement of notable artistes in government functions is a recurring conversation across Africa, highlighting the complex relationship between entertainment, public perception, and political landscapes. This dynamic often puts public figures under intense scrutiny, reminiscent of other significant legal and political discussions across the continent, such as the recent judicial review concerning presidential advisors in Kenya.
