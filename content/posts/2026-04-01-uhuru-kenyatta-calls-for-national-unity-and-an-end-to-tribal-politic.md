Former President Uhuru Kenyatta called for national unity and an end to tribal politics on Wednesday, April 1, at the burial service of the late Daniel Karaba in Kirinyaga County. Kenyatta urged leaders to rise above divisive politics, stressing that cohesion is vital for the country's progress and stability.

Kenyatta Urges Unity, End to Tribalism In his address, Kenyatta referenced his past political partnership with Raila Odinga, stating their shared vision had always aimed to unite the country beyond ethnic and political divisions. He affirmed, "Let us unite. This is what Raila and I wanted. What we really desired was for this country to be united. To be led by policies and principles, not by tribe, ethnic bitterness and hatred." The former Head of State underscored that national unity remains central to the nation's identity and long-term stability, noting that ethnic divisions continue to undermine progress.

Prioritizing Service Over Politics Kenyatta used the platform to call on leaders to shift their focus from political competition to service delivery. He articulated, "Be leaders. That is what the people want. They want leadership, not politics. They don't want insults. They want direction. They want leadership that will see them through to the other side." He further noted that the populace is increasingly demanding accountability and leadership that prioritizes their welfare over personal interests, echoing ongoing public conversations about economic policies that directly impact daily life, such as how [EPRA details the process of calculating fuel prices in Kenya](https://zandani.co.ke/article/epra-details-the-process-of-calculating-fuel-prices-in-kenya).

Integrity and Principled Leadership The former President stressed that integrity and principle-driven leadership are critical, particularly during heightened political activity. "Our country needs unity in order for us to have peace, stability and progress. Our country needs leaders who have integrity, leaders who are principled, who are there to serve their people," he added. Such stability is crucial for fostering an environment where businesses thrive and contribute to national growth, as exemplified by [Family Bank reports record profit ahead of Nairobi Securities Exchange debut](https://zandani.co.ke/article/family-bank-reports-record-profit-ahead-of-nairobi-securities-exchan). His remarks at the burial service were delivered alongside those of former Deputy President Rigathi Gachagua and other opposition leaders, who also addressed the current political climate and leadership concerns, all against a backdrop of heated political rivalry and growing calls for leaders to prioritize national cohesion and policy-driven governance ahead of the 2027 election.
