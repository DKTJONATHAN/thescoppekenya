Interior Cabinet Secretary Kipchumba Murkomen met with UK Minister of Border Security and Asylum Alex Norris in London on Thursday, March 19, 2026, to secure significant British investment for developing world-class sports infrastructure. The discussions focused on strengthening bilateral ties and adopting the UK's globally recognized expertise to modernize sports facilities across the nation.

Kenya Courts UK Investment for Sports Infrastructure

Boosting Bilateral Ties and Economic Growth The engagement between CS Murkomen and Minister Norris on Thursday, March 19, centered on enhancing bilateral relations and identifying investment opportunities across various sectors, with sports infrastructure highlighted as a crucial area of mutual interest. Murkomen stated that expanding investment at the country's borders will strengthen relations with the United Kingdom, following his courtesy call at Norris's London office. He emphasized that increased investment partnerships between the two nations could substantially boost economic growth, including the modernization of critical sectors such as sports.

Leveraging UK Expertise for Modern Facilities The UK's global recognition for its advanced sports infrastructure and expertise presents a valuable model for the nation as it endeavors to elevate its facilities to international standards. The country aims to attract private investors, particularly within the football sector, to develop modern facilities including stadiums, high-performance training centers, and community-based sports hubs. Murkomen encouraged football investors to consider the nation as a strategic investment destination, citing the UK's world-class infrastructure, strong leagues like the Premier League, and extensive grassroots sports development.

Government's Investor-Driven Approach This investor-driven approach is poised to mark a significant turning point in the country's ambition to become a leading sports powerhouse in Africa. The government is undertaking a comprehensive overhaul of the sports sector through improved governance and infrastructure. This includes the introduction of the Sports Bill 2026 and the National Sports Policy, which aim to facilitate the completion of at least 22 stadiums nationwide. This modernization drive is part of a broader government commitment to national development and efficiency, aligning with other initiatives such as the National Transport and Safety Authority's rollout of 1,000 speed cameras for digital road enforcement.
