Nollywood actor Kunle Remi has vigorously denounced Nigeria's escalating petrol prices and economic strain this week. Leveraging his platform, he called for widespread public discussions and collective action, asserting that public figures must not remain silent on critical national issues impacting everyday life across the continent.

Kunle Remi Tackles Economic Hardship

Actor's Passionate Outcry Against Fuel Costs Kunle Remi's frustration was evident in a series of online videos where he challenged the notion that public figures should avoid political or economic discourse. "Usually I say things like I don’t really get involved with politics… No, that’s the most stupid statement from anyone in Nigeria right now," he declared. The actor advocated for active participation, stating, "We should be discussing, we should be talking about it, we should be trying to fix… There’s nothing like sitting on the fence." He linked his concerns directly to the impact of fuel costs on daily living, noting how businesses, from shopping malls to small barber shops, rely heavily on petrol to operate. Remi also highlighted a personal stake, adding, "I have a child, so I’m thinking not just for myself."

Geopolitical Tensions and Local Impact The actor further questioned Nigeria's responsiveness to global oil market shifts, particularly ongoing tensions in the Middle East. Expressing deep anger, he lamented, "I don’t understand why Nigeria is one of the first countries to be affected by the war in Iran. My spirit is very angry. All the things I’ve been working for is for what?" Remi's remarks underscored a broader sentiment of exasperation over how international events appear to disproportionately affect the local economy.

Call for Collective Action In a subsequent video, Remi escalated his appeal, urging collective action among Nigerians. "They can’t kill everybody. If we move in our numbers, forgetting which region the next person comes from, we will conquer our oppressors," he asserted. He also acknowledged varying perspectives within his professional circle, saying, "I have some colleagues… saying Tinubu is fixing things, I hope you’re asking the right questions." This outspokenness highlights a growing trend among public figures across the continent to use their platforms to address critical societal issues, adding to the diverse expressions of African artistry and public engagement seen in works like the recent "Agaba Romantic" EP from Joeboy and Wizard Chan.

Sustained Pressure on Fuel Prices Remi's comments arrive amidst persistent pressure on petrol prices throughout Nigeria this year. Despite the commencement of domestic refining operations, including the Dangote Refinery, pump prices continue to mirror global market volatility. Industry stakeholders point to international crude oil price movements and geopolitical tensions as significant factors limiting any immediate relief for consumers. Recent market data shows that a nearly 20 percent increase in petrol prices implemented last week remains in place, with a national average of about N1,300 per litre. A decline in crude oil prices earlier in the week has yet to result in lower pump prices, intensifying consumer concerns.

Public and Celebrity Support Online, Remi’s remarks have garnered widespread support, with many users commending his courage in addressing an issue directly impacting daily life. His comments are seen by many as reflecting a broader public sentiment, particularly as more Nigerians grapple with rising transportation and operational costs. Several users echoed his sentiments, with some recalling similar frustrations that led them to leave Nigeria years ago. Other prominent figures like Okunnu, MC Macaroni, Hauwa, Iyabo Ojo, Falz, and Laide Olabanji (Laiban Films) have also been noted for openly speaking out on pressing national issues, putting their public standing on the line. Remi's intervention amplifies the growing chorus of voices demanding accountability and clearer communication regarding fuel pricing, as economic pressures continue to shape public discourse.
