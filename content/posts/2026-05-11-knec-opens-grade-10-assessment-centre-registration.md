## KNEC Opens Online Registration for Grade 10 Assessment Centres

As the Competency-Based Curriculum moves into its next phase, many Kenyan parents are asking one vital question: Is my child’s school officially recognized to host the upcoming Grade 10 national assessments? The answer hinges on a registration process closing soon.

The Kenya National Examinations Council has officially opened its online portal for schools to register as Grade 10 assessment centres. This step is a mandatory administrative requirement under the new senior school framework. While the task falls on school principals to complete the digital submission, the implication for families is significant. Ensuring that a school is a registered assessment centre is the only way to guarantee that a learner’s progress and final results will be legally recognized within the national education database.

### Why is the May 22 deadline so important for families?

The clock is ticking. Schools have until May 22 to finalize their status on the KNEC platform. This period is not merely a formality for bureaucrats. It is a critical window during which every institution hosting Grade 10 learners must prove it meets the criteria set by the council to conduct assessments.

If a school fails to register by this date, its learners face a serious hurdle. National assessments are the primary gateway for progression in the CBC system. Without the school being confirmed as an official centre, the assessment process for those students could face unnecessary delays or, in worst-case scenarios, administrative complications that could jeopardize their certification. Parents should treat this like checking for a child's name on a school register. It is the fundamental step to ensure the child is properly accounted for in the national record.

### How can parents verify their school status?

Parents do not need to access the KNEC portal themselves, as this is a secure system reserved for school administrators using official school codes and verification protocols. However, parents have a responsibility to be informed. If you are concerned about your child’s school, the best approach is to have a direct conversation with the school administration.

Ask simple, clear questions: Have we been registered as a Grade 10 assessment centre? Has the principal confirmed the institution's data on the KNEC portal? For schools that do not have existing KNEC codes, the guidance is specific: administration must reach out to their local Sub-County Directors of Education to receive instructions on how to proceed. If a school cannot explain their status or seems unsure of these requirements, it is a sign that you should seek clarity immediately before the May 22 cutoff.

### Why it matters now

Education is the most significant investment for most Kenyan households. As the country settles into the demands of the CBC, the administrative requirements are becoming more rigorous. By checking with your child's school now, you avoid the panic of discovering a registration gap later. While the world focuses on other events, like the recent recovery of bodies in a [Kericho cemetery](https://zandani.co.ke/article/detectives-recover-33-bodies-from-kericho-cemetery-during-exhumation) or the latest turns in local sports, this quiet administrative task will dictate whether your child’s school year ends without a hitch. Education requires both academic effort and careful verification of status.

### What to watch next

The next few weeks will reveal which schools have successfully navigated the digital transition. Watch for announcements from the Ministry of Education regarding schools that may have encountered technical challenges in registering their centres. It is also a time to observe how smoothly the shift is handled in rural versus urban areas. For now, the priority remains the May 22 deadline. If you are a parent, pick up the phone or walk into the administration office this week. It is a small action that secures a large part of your child’s future.

Verify your child's school registration today to ensure their Grade 10 results are legally secured.
