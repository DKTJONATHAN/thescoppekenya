President William Ruto officially appointed 37 judges to the High Court and Environment and Land Court on April 24, 2026. This significant move aims to expedite justice delivery by addressing the substantial case backlog across the nation's judicial system.

## Judicial Expansion Addresses Case Backlog

The appointments, gazetted under Article 166(1)(b) of the Constitution, empower the President to formally designate judges, directly tackling the thousands of pending cases that have caused prolonged delays for justice seekers.

### High Court Appointments Detailed

Of the 37 new judicial officers, 24 have been assigned to the High Court. Notable appointees include Robinson Ondieki Kebabe, Joyce Mkambe Gandani, Joseph Maloba Were, Roseline Akinyi Oganyo, and Paul Kipkosgei Rotich. The High Court bench also welcomes Dickson Odhiambo Onyango, Alex Kimanzi Ithuku, Martha Wanzila Mutuku, Benard Wafula Murunga, Francis Nyungu Kyambia, and Letizia Muthoni Wachira Rwiga, following a fiercely contested shortlisting process.

Further additions to the High Court roster are Kennedy Lenkamai Kandet, Richard Kipkemoio Koech, Emmanuel Omondi Bitta, David Wanjohi Mburu, Dominic Kipkemoio Rono, Ms. Winnie Narasha Molonko, and Ms. Judith Chelangat Mutai. Joseph Kipkoech Biomdo, Anne Mary Auma Okutoyi, Abdi Mohamud Hassan, Nabil Mokaya Orina, Patricia Naeku Leparashao, and Catherine Akaigwa Kassim complete the 24-judge slate for the High Court.

### Environment and Land Court Additions

Thirteen judges have been appointed to the Environment and Land Court (ELC). These include John Walter Wanyonyi, Jecinta Atieno Orwa, Peter Muneeno Musyimi, Cyprian Mugambi Ngyathari, and Charity Chebii Oluoch.

The remaining ELC positions have been filled by Elena Gathoni Nderitu, Gerhard Gitonga Muchege, Charles Nchore Ondieki, Dr. Robert Omondi Owino, Bellinda Akoth Akello, Lillian Tsuma Lewa, Ben Mark Ekhubi, and Josphat Ngeria Kuyioni.

### Rigorous Recruitment Process

These appointments follow an extensive and competitive recruitment drive orchestrated by the Judicial Service Commission (JSC). The JSC initially advertised the judicial vacancies on June 9, 2025, covering positions in the High Court, Court of Appeal, and Environment and Land Court. The advertisement attracted widespread interest from the legal fraternity, with hundreds of applications submitted.

The High Court received 377 applications, leading to 100 shortlisted candidates. The Court of Appeal saw 95 applications, with 35 advancing to the next stage. For the ELC, 243 applications were submitted, resulting in 50 shortlisted candidates. All shortlisted individuals underwent a thorough public interview process before the JSC finalized its recommendations.

Earlier, on January 28, President Ruto formally appointed 15 nominees to the Court of Appeal. These promotions subsequently created additional vacancies in the High Court and ELC, with four High Court judges and two ELC judges elevated to the Court of Appeal. An additional ELC judge retired during the recruitment process, contributing to the openings now filled by this recent batch of appointments.

The JSC affirmed that these strategic appointments will substantially augment the number of judges. The High Court will increase from 91 to 115 judges, while the Environment and Land Court will grow from 48 to 61 judges, significantly enhancing the judiciary's capacity to provide timely and effective service.
