## Kenya's Entrepreneurial A-List Unveils Game-Changing Fursa Index

Kenya's entrepreneurial scene just got a major upgrade with the glitzy launch of the Fursa Index at SalesFest Africa. This pioneering index, shaped by over 10,000 MSMEs, reveals how ready Kenyan businesses are for structured markets, promising a vibrant future for our economic stars.

### The Fursa Index: Charting a Course for Success

The nation proudly unveiled its first MSME Opportunity Readiness Index, dubbed the "Fursa Index," during the energetic SalesFest Africa. This significant milestone for the entrepreneurship ecosystem took center stage at the Trinity Auditorium, All Saints Cathedral in Nairobi. Developed through the extensive Chanuka Jipange national engagement, which spanned all 47 counties, the index is designed to measure the preparedness of Kenyan micro and small enterprises to seize genuine economic opportunities and actively participate in structured markets. Over 10,000 MSMEs across the country contributed their invaluable insights, forming the backbone of this groundbreaking report. This drive for structured systems resonates with broader efforts to strengthen institutions across the country, much like Chief Justice Martha Koome's establishment of new Court of Appeal and Small Claims Court stations, aiming to bring justice closer to the people and streamline legal processes.

### Power Players on the Podium

The launch saw some of Kenya's most influential figures in the MSME sector take the stage. Hon. Susan Auma Mang'eni, Principal Secretary for Micro, Small and Medium Enterprises Development, warmly welcomed the findings, emphasizing their timely arrival as the government undertakes a crucial review of the national MSME policy framework. She challenged the audience with critical questions: How can markets be structured for essential sectors like boda boda operators? And how can informal businesses, such as hawkers, be seamlessly integrated into organized market systems?

Eunice Maina-Mburu, CEO of 20X Entrepreneur and the visionary founder of the Chanuka Jipange movement, shared the inspiring journey of engaging entrepreneurs nationwide. Her most striking discovery? Kenyan entrepreneurs are working incredibly hard, but many remain caught in a "transitioning hustle economy," where immense effort doesn't always translate into structured market access. While our entrepreneurs are navigating their own real-life challenges, far more compelling than any [I'm A Celebrity South Africa 2026: More Than Just British Celebs in the Bush, Mate?](https://zandani.co.ke/article/im-a-celebrity-south-africa-2026-local-buzz-celebrities) drama, they are truly shaping the nation's future. Adding a scientific perspective, behavioral scientist and leadership coach Irene Njoroge underscored that genuine economic transformation occurs when systems actively remove "friction" for entrepreneurs. She powerfully quoted an insight from the session: "The secret of change is not to push harder, it is to remove friction. Structure removes friction that keeps people stuck. When systems are structured people trust." Njoroge also pinpointed three common mindsets that often hinder entrepreneurs from entering structured markets, advocating for systems that reward consistency, quality, and accountability.

### Industry Stars Share Secrets

A panel of industry experts offered practical wisdom on the workings of structured markets in successful sectors. Clement Tulezi, CEO of the Kenya Flower Council, illuminated how the flower industry stands as one of Kenya's most organized agricultural sectors. His philosophy was refreshingly simple: "A flower is not a product. A flower is a process."

Contrasting this, livestock expert David Maina highlighted the largely disorganized nature of the livestock sector despite its immense economic potential. He passionately argued for introducing grading systems and establishing feeding hubs, much like the Galana Kulalu model, where livestock could be temporarily housed for feeding, weight improvement, and standardized grading before market entry. Such structures, he posited, would unlock significant opportunities for pastoralists and smallholder livestock farmers.

Other speakers showcased how policy and technology act as powerful market enablers. David Muiru, Managing Director of Glimco Limited, illustrated how the Affordable Housing Program is injecting greater structure and professionalism into the real estate sector. Meanwhile, Jimmy Bett, Managing Partner at Dibon Co Ltd, stressed the critical role of technology-enabled records, tracking, and customer experience systems for businesses aiming to transition from a pure hustle to thriving in structured markets. The launch of the Fursa Index signals the exciting start of a national conversation dedicated to building structured opportunity systems that empower Kenyan enterprises to scale, attract vital capital, and truly compete on the global stage.
