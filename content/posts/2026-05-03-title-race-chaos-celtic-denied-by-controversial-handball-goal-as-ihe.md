Celtic's Scottish Premiership title aspirations suffered a significant setback after a controversial 'handball' goal denied them a crucial victory against Hibernian. The defending champions now trail leaders Hearts by three points, despite the notable returns of Kelechi Iheanacho and Kieran Tierney in a chaotic encounter.

## Title Race Intensifies Amidst Controversy

The Scottish Premiership title race has taken an unpredictable turn, with defending champions Celtic now three points behind leaders Hearts following their contentious draw against Hibernian. The match featured a highly disputed leveller from Newell, which stood despite the midfielder seemingly using his arm to control the ball. Former Celtic and Hibs player John Collins expressed strong disbelief, stating, "Surely VAR has to have a look? It's as blatant a penalty as you'll ever see." The on-field decision, however, was upheld due to "insufficient evidence of a handball to overturn" it. This outcome has profound implications as Celtic's impressive run of three consecutive league wins, previously the longest ongoing streak in the Scottish Premiership, was halted.

## Key Players Return to Action

The match saw the anticipated return of experienced striker Kelechi Iheanacho, who was brought on for Yang Hyun-Jun. Iheanacho is described as a "goalscorer and a man for the big moment", and his presence injects significant attacking prowess into the Celtic squad. Kieran Tierney also featured prominently, getting a powerful shot on target that was saved by Jordan Smith, and earlier scoring a screamer against Falkirk last week. Daizen Maeda initially made the visitors' dominance count by scoring from a low Johnston delivery, but later struggled to get a shot away after being released by Luke McCowan.

### Hibernian's Disciplinary Struggles Continue

Hibernian's discipline emerged as a significant concern, with McGrath sent off for a dangerous tackle on Johnston. This marked Hibs' fourth red card in just three games, matching the total they had received in their previous 59 Scottish Premiership matches combined. Additionally, two Hibs substitutes were booked within minutes of coming on, further highlighting their struggles with maintaining composure.

### Statistical Context and Recent Trends

Historically, Hibernian could have secured back-to-back league victories over Celtic for the first time since September 2007 under John Collins, following their 2-1 win in February. However, the draw prevented this milestone. Celtic, conversely, have a strong recent record at Hibernian, winning three of their last four league visits, a significant improvement from their previous 15 encounters where they achieved the same number of wins. Hibernian had lost their last two league games but managed to avoid a third consecutive defeat, a streak they last experienced in October 2024.
