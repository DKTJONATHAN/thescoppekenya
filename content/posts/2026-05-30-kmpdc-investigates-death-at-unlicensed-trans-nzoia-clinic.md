## Can You Spot a Licensed Clinic?

The Kenya Medical Practitioners and Dentists Council is currently conducting a formal inquiry into the death of Brian Simiyu, who passed away following a surgical procedure performed at an unregistered facility in Trans Nzoia. This tragic event highlights a dangerous gap between national health oversight and local enforcement.

### Why do unlicensed clinics keep popping up?

The rise of rogue health facilities often stems from a jurisdictional tug of war. While the Kenya Medical Practitioners and Dentists Council (KMPDC) sets the national standards and registers practitioners, the physical environment of a clinic is often monitored by county health departments. When these two arms of government fail to synchronize their inspections, gaps emerge. Rogue operators exploit these administrative blind spots, setting up shop in residential areas where they bypass the rigorous certification process required for surgery. Unlike the oversight seen in major political transitions like the [recent party leadership changes](https://zandani.co.ke/article/2026-03-27-odm-elects-oburu-odinga-as-party-leader-at-special-delegate), the regulation of rural health centers often lacks the same level of centralized scrutiny, leaving the burden of verification on the patient.

### How can you protect yourself before a procedure?

Verification is the single most effective tool a patient has to ensure their safety. Before agreeing to any medical procedure, especially one involving surgery, you must confirm that the facility is legally recognized. You can do this by accessing the official KMPDC online portal. Enter the name of the facility to see if it holds a valid license for the specific services it claims to offer. If a clinic does not appear in the registry, do not proceed. Legitimate centers are legally required to display their registration certificates in a visible area, usually at the reception desk. If you cannot see a current license or if the staff cannot provide one, walk away immediately. Trusting your health to an unverified facility is a risk no patient should take, regardless of how urgent or minor the procedure might seem.

### What are the red flags of a rogue facility?

Not all dangerous clinics look like abandoned buildings. Some operate within functional neighborhoods, but they exhibit clear warning signs that suggest a lack of professional oversight. First, observe the environment. A facility meant for surgery must maintain high standards of hygiene, including sterilized equipment and proper waste management. If the surroundings appear cluttered, dirty, or disorganized, these are immediate red flags. Second, be wary of practitioners who are hesitant to explain their qualifications or provide details about their professional registration. Genuine medical professionals are always willing to share their credentials. Third, if a facility offers major surgical services but lacks basic life-support equipment or a clear referral system for complications, it is likely operating outside its legal mandate. Many patients in rural areas trust local providers without question, similar to how voters might rely on local representatives, but healthcare requires a higher level of skepticism. Just as citizens scrutinize leaders, such as the [late David Kiaraho](https://zandani.co.ke/article/2026-03-29-ol-kalou-mp-david-kiaraho-dies-at-nairobi-hospital), they must be prepared to audit the clinics they choose for their care.

The safety of every patient relies on verifying the legality of their clinic through the official KMPDC portal before stepping into an operating room.
