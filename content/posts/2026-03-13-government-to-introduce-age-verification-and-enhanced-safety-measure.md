## Government Outlines Enhanced TikTok Safety Measures Information, Communication and Technology Cabinet Secretary William Kabogo announced new government initiatives to strengthen TikTok oversight. Measures include improved user protection, stronger cybersecurity, new age verification systems, and increased awareness of data privacy, addressing digital challenges highlighted at the TikTok Safer Internet Summit 2026.

Strengthening User Protection and Cybersecurity The government intends to enhance online safety by introducing improved user protection tools. These measures will safeguard individuals from harmful content, online scams, and harassment. Collaboration with technology firms is expected to ensure more effective reporting systems are implemented. Concurrently, efforts will focus on strengthening cybersecurity frameworks to combat rising cybercrime cases linked to digital platforms, through detecting and preventing threats affecting both users and digital businesses. This proactive stance aims to address misinformation and the protection of personal data.

Introducing Age Verification and Digital Literacy A major platform change involves the introduction of age verification systems. These systems are designed to ensure minors are protected from inappropriate content, a critical step given the platform's significant reach among younger demographics and the ongoing discussions surrounding their online experiences, as explored in articles like [Gen Z Sire Kids: The End of an Era?](https://zandani.co.ke/article/gen-z-sire-kids). Furthermore, Cabinet Secretary Kabogo announced plans to promote greater awareness of data privacy and digital literacy. This initiative aims to equip users with a better understanding of how personal information is utilized online and how to maintain safety while engaging with TikTok.

Summit Highlights and Digital Future Commitment Kabogo's remarks were made during the TikTok Safer Internet Summit 2026, held between March 10 and March 11. The event convened policymakers, regulators, technology leaders, and civil society organizations from across Africa. During the summit, Kabogo acknowledged the increasing role of social media platforms, including TikTok, in fostering digital creativity, innovation, and economic opportunities, particularly for young people. He emphasized the rapid growth of the digital creator economy, noting how platforms like TikTok are opening new avenues for entrepreneurship, storytelling, and cultural expression. The government reiterated its commitment to cultivating a digital environment that empowers creators, supports innovation, and unlocks the full potential of Africa’s digital future.
