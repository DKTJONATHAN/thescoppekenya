President William Ruto announced government support for boxing in Kenya on April 5, 2026, after amateur sensation Portifas Odipo, known as Majembe, defeated Ferdinand Omondi, alias Mbavu Destroyer, in the Vurugu Championship held at the Kasarani Indoor Arena. His pledge follows the highly anticipated bout that revitalized interest in the sport.

## Presidential Commitment to Boxing

President Ruto lauded both boxers on April 5 via an X post, congratulating Majembe on his victory and describing the Vurugu Championship bout as entertaining. The President acknowledged their performance inside the ring, noting the closely contested match captivated the audience from start to finish. He stated, “Congratulations, Majembe (Portifas Odipo), on your victory against Mbavu Destroyer (Ferdinand Omondi) in yesterday’s Vurugu Championship. Both of you put up a spirited fight, delivering an entertaining match that kept us on edge, too close to call.”

President Ruto affirmed that the duel reignited passion for boxing, restoring public belief and excitement in a sport that had long struggled for visibility. Building on the momentum generated by the match, the President pledged to nurture boxing in the country. "We commit to sustain this momentum by supporting the sport, investing in talent, and creating opportunities to grow boxing across the country," he added. This commitment follows a previous pledge of Ksh1 million to the winner, relayed through Lang'ata MP Phelix Odiwuor, popularly known as Jalang'o.

### Vurugu Championship Overview

The Vurugu Championship, held on April 4 at the Kasarani Indoor Arena in Nairobi, concluded in the fourth round. Majembe secured a technical knockout victory after Mbavu Destroyer could no longer continue. Although Majembe had been leading on points, some spectators noted a blow that appeared to land below the belt in the final moments of the contest. Mbavu Destroyer had gained significant public recognition through his viral "Weka Mawe" slogan, while Majembe's internet popularity translated into genuine crowd favoritism. The fight attracted extensive viewership both at the venue and online, reflecting a substantial public appetite for grassroots sporting entertainment.

### Broader Sports Development Initiatives

President Ruto's administration has made various commitments to sports development nationwide. These include substantial investments in sports infrastructure, targeting 22 new stadiums and 30 grassroots football academies. The 60,000-seat Talanta Sports City Stadium is on track for completion before the Confederation of African Football (CAF) deadline of July this year. Additionally, athlete welfare has seen significant improvements, with daily allowances increasing from Ksh7,500 to Ksh26,000, gold medal bonuses reaching Ksh3 million, and government-subsidized housing provided for 2025 championship medallists. Kenya also jointly hosted the CHAN championships with Tanzania and Uganda last year, and the FIFA Series Championships for women are set to be hosted at Nyayo Stadium this month, all in preparation for the AFCON 2027 tournament.
