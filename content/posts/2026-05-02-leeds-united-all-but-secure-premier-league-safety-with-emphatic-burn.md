By Jona Munyi

Leeds United has all but secured Premier League safety for the 2026 season with an emphatic 3-1 victory over already-relegated Burnley. This crucial win moves Daniel Farke's side to 14th place, significantly easing their relegation anxieties and intensifying the battle for survival among other struggling clubs.

## Leeds United's Resurgence and Farke's Tactical Masterclass

Leeds United's recent form has propelled them to a strong position in the Premier League, accumulating 43 points on the board. Their dominant 3-1 win against Burnley at Elland Road on Friday saw goals from Anton Stach, Noah Okafor, and Dominic Calvert-Lewin. This victory places them nine points clear of 18th-placed Tottenham, who currently occupy the final relegation spot. Manager Daniel Farke has been lauded for his leadership and tactical acumen, with the club's board vindicated in their decision to retain him.

### The Turning Point Against Manchester City

While recent victories have been pivotal, Farke's tactical adjustments earlier in the season proved instrumental in Leeds' turnaround. A 3-2 defeat to Manchester City in late November, despite the loss, became a defining moment. Farke's decisive switch from a 4-3-3 to a 3-5-2 formation, introducing Calvert-Lewin and Jaka Bijol from the bench, provided extra midfield presence and attacking support. This tactical blueprint, despite the late defeat to City, saw Leeds bounce back by taking four points from their subsequent two games against Chelsea and Liverpool. Since the start of December, Leeds has lost only four of 19 league games, marking the ninth-best record in the division.

## Relegation Scramble Intensifies for Tottenham and Rivals

Leeds' almost-secured safety heaps pressure on the teams still battling to avoid the drop. Tottenham, West Ham, and Nottingham Forest are the clubs most under threat from relegation, alongside already-relegated Burnley and Wolves. Tottenham, who suffered a "Pyrrhic triumph" against Wolves recently, now sit in 18th place. They are just two points behind West Ham and four behind Nottingham Forest, with each of these teams having games in hand on Leeds. Spurs face a daunting schedule, including matches against Aston Villa, Leeds, Chelsea, and Everton.

### The Statistical Outlook for Survival

The statistical outlook for Tottenham is grim, with Opta's super-computer giving them a 59.8% chance of relegation. West Ham is the second most likely to join Burnley and Wolves in the Championship next season, with a 38% chance, while Forest faces a much longer shot at 2%. Historically, 36 points have been the average needed for Premier League survival, though this season's projections suggest 37 points might be necessary. Notably, no team has ever been relegated from a 38-game Premier League season with 43 points or more, a significant reassurance for Leeds United. However, West Ham holds the record for the most points collected by a relegated team in a 20-team Premier League season, dropping with 42 points in 2002-03. The average points tally for the 18th-placed team has been 34.5, although this figure has decreased to 29.6 points over the last five years.
