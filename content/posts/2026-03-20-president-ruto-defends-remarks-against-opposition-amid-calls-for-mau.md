President William Ruto on Friday, March 20, defended his recent strong statements against the United Opposition during an Eid-Ul-Fitr luncheon in Kisumu, rejecting criticism that his remarks were unpresidential. This comes as the family of late blogger Maureen Githaiga Kinyua formally requested a fresh inquest into her disputed 2019 death.

## President Ruto Addresses Opposition Criticisms

Speaking in Kisumu, President Ruto dismissed claims that his remarks against the opposition were unpresidential, stating he merely advised them "to watch their weight" and focus on policy. He affirmed his resolve to continue confronting the opposition, asserting that they had failed to engage on matters of national priority. Ruto reiterated that he would address what he termed "tribalists and hate mongers" who he claimed have consistently insulted him for two years. He linked his continued strong messaging to unresolved issues, including past killings, unidentified bodies found in rivers like River Yala, and the disinheritance of widows.

### Demands for Maureen Kinyua Death Inquest Intensify

Meanwhile, the family of Maureen Githaiga Kinyua formally petitioned the Office of the Director of Public Prosecutions (ODPP) on Thursday, March 19, requesting a new inquest into her 2019 death. Lawyers representing the family submitted a letter outlining six reasons why Maureen's death in the Mataga area, officially recorded as a road accident, may have been a premeditated killing.

The petition details that Maureen was allegedly lured to Mataga by a female friend with suspected connections to a senior political figure from the Mount Kenya region. She remained there until after 6 PM. When Maureen attempted to leave, the friend reportedly contacted her brother, a boda boda operator, providing specific instructions on where to drop Maureen, suggesting prior coordination. The boda boda rider reportedly vanished immediately after the incident, reappearing only when summoned by the area base commander. Crucially, the motorcycle allegedly involved showed no visible damage, contradicting the official accident narrative.

The family's letter also highlights a penetration wound on Maureen’s forehead that was not acknowledged in the official autopsy report. Furthermore, Maureen’s mobile phone disappeared after her death. Suspicion was heightened by the alleged visit of the senior political figure’s wife to the scene shortly after the incident, briefly surveying the area before departing, which the family believes points to coordination and a potential cover-up. This renewed push for accountability reopens a case nearly seven years after the initial incident. Former Deputy President Rigathi Gachagua, leader of the Democracy for Citizens Party, has publicly denied any involvement, maintaining that Maureen's death was an accident near Sagana Catholic Church on May 24, 2019.
