COTU Secretary General Francis Atwoli has issued a stern warning to employers concerning persistent salary delays and reluctance to implement agreed-upon wage adjustments. Speaking today, Saturday, April 18, at a COTU shop stewards’ meeting in Nairobi, Atwoli criticized private sector players for failing to honor commitments, threatening exclusion from the upcoming Labour Day celebrations.

## Employer Non-Compliance Sparks Union Outrage

Atwoli specifically accused the Federation of Kenyan Employers (FKE) and other private entities of deliberately delaying the implementation of Collective Bargaining Agreements (CBAs) despite mutual negotiation and signing with worker unions. He emphasized that these delays disproportionately affect employees grappling with the rising cost of living and inflationary pressures in 2026.

### Labour Day Exclusion Threat Looms

As a direct consequence of this non-compliance, the COTU boss announced that errant employers risk being barred from participating in this year’s Labour Day celebrations. The significant event, scheduled for May 1 in Vihiga County, is expected to be presided over by President William Ruto, offering a platform for unions to advocate for improved pay and working conditions. Atwoli underscored that employers who do not prioritize worker interests would not be welcome at union-led events.

### Allegations of Sidelining Unions and Tax Evasion

Further criticisms included accusations that employers sideline unions in crucial decision-making forums, even while accepting invitations to union-organized meetings. Atwoli stated that negotiations are often prolonged for years, with salary increments only implemented under duress and frequently accompanied by accumulated arrears. The union leader also raised concerns over alleged tax evasion by some companies, accusing them of utilizing shell entities to under-declare income and avoid their tax obligations, deeming such practices "unacceptable."

### Public Sector Workers Demand Wage Hikes

Atwoli’s remarks coincide with increasing pressure on the Salaries and Remuneration Commission (SRC) regarding new wage demands from public sector workers. The Kenya Independent Commissions Workers Union, representing civil servants from 19 independent commissions, has demanded salary increases ranging from 20 to 50 percent. Citing economic hardship and delayed reviews, the union has issued a 14-day ultimatum for negotiations to commence, warning of potential escalation of labour tensions across critical government institutions if their demands are not addressed promptly.
