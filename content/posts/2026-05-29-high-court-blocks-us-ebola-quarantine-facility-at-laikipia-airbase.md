By treating the Laikipia Airbase as an extension of U.S. territory rather than a sovereign asset, the administration failed to anticipate that the Katiba Institute would leverage the constitution to force a halt. This is not merely about a health facility; it is about the KES 1.74 billion price tag attached to the implicit erosion of our territorial authority. The government negotiated this arrangement in total secrecy, effectively treating our national security infrastructure as a bargaining chip to stave off a threatened $423 million reduction in health aid.

The pressure from U.S. officials like Marco Rubio to secure an evacuation point for their citizens has clearly forced the hand of the Ruto administration.
