The Narok County Government has dismissed allegations that international tourists were unlawfully detained at a lodge within the Maasai Mara National Reserve. In a statement issued by the County Secretary, officials termed the claims false, misleading, and aimed at causing alarm.

## Maasai Mara Tourist Detention Allegations

### County Dismisses Hospitality Group Claims
The Office of the County Secretary responded to reports circulated by MGM Muthu Hotels regarding recent disruptions at the facility. The county categorically denied that any guests were restricted from leaving or that management was barred from the premises. Officials stated that all local and international tourists are safe and free to continue their stay or depart according to their travel plans. 

The statement, issued on April 23, clarified that the claims of unlawful detention were inaccurate. The county government emphasized that no security threats exist at the lodge and that visitors are currently under the protection of county rangers and the Tourism Police Unit.

### Labor Unrest and Service Standards
Disruptions at the lodge were attributed to a labor dispute between management and employees rather than a property conflict. Workers cited harassment, poor working conditions, and low morale as the primary reasons for their strike. This situation follows broader regional discussions on employment and management, such as [CS Ruku's government's plan to move civil servants to contract terms ignites debate](https://zandani.co.ke/article/cs-ruku-governments-plan-move-civil-servants-contract-terms) within the public sector.

Za Ndani established that the deteriorating condition of the lodge also contributed to the unrest. According to county officials, the facility has failed to maintain the service standards required by the Maasai Mara National Reserve Management Plan 2023-2032. 

### Ownership and Security Protocols
The county government clarified that the lodge remains under its ownership, refuting claims of a property dispute made by the hospitality company. Security operations are currently being overseen by the Chief Park Warden in collaboration with other security agencies within the reserve to ensure the safety of all guests.

While the hospitality firm attributed the incident to a dispute over the premises, the county maintained that the core issue remains the internal management of the lodge and its failure to adhere to reserve regulations. Tourists currently at the facility remain under the supervision of park authorities.
