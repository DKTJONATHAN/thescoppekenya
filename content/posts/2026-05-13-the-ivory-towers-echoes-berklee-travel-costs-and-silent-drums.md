### The Philanthropic Facade

### The Unseen Ledger of Progress

Yet, beneath this gleaming veneer of benevolence lay a truth steeped in unimaginable horror. The "progress" was measured in tonnes of rubber and ivory, extracted from the Congolese forests through a system of forced labour that beggared belief. Villagers were given impossible quotas, their lives reduced to a relentless pursuit of wild rubber. Failure to meet these quotas resulted in unspeakable atrocities: beatings, torture, the burning of villages, and the infamous severing of hands, a gruesome tally of "missed targets." An estimated ten million Congolese perished during Leopold’s reign, a silent testament to the true cost of his "philanthropy." The Free State was anything but free; it was a vast, privately owned forced labour camp, its wealth built on the blood and suffering of its people.
