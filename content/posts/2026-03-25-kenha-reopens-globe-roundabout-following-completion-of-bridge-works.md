By Celestine Nzioka The Kenya National Highways Authority (KeNHA) today, March 25, 2026, confirmed the full restoration of traffic flow at Globe Roundabout along the Thika Superhighway. This reopening allows unimpeded travel for thousands of commuters navigating a major route into Nairobi’s Central Business District, bringing significant relief after recent disruptions.

Globe Roundabout Traffic Resumes In a notice issued on Wednesday, March 25, KeNHA announced that motorists can now resume normal travel along the section. The Globe Roundabout is a critical junction connecting major roads leading to the CBD, Thika Superhighway, University Way, Kipande Road, Limuru Road, Kirinyaga Road, and the OTC area, handling substantial daily traffic.

Temporary Closure for Bridge Reinstatement The roundabout had been temporarily closed on March 12, 2026, to facilitate essential reinstatement works at the Nairobi River Bridge. This critical link had significantly slowed movement between Nairobi's Central Business District, Kipande Road, and the Thika Superhighway. The repair works were deemed necessary by KeNHA to enhance the structural integrity and safety of the bridge infrastructure.

Impact of Disruption on Commuters and Businesses During the closure, commuters faced extended travel times due to traffic snarl-ups as they scrambled to utilize alternative routes along the Thika Superhighway, Kipande Road, and surrounding roads leading into the CBD. Public transport operators adjusted their routes and schedules, leading to delays and increased fares for passengers. Businesses operating within and around Nairobi’s city centre also felt the impact, with delays affecting the movement of goods and services.

Anticipated Ease of Movement With traffic now fully restored at Globe Roundabout, movement is expected to improve gradually, alleviating the pressure that had built up on alternative routes during the closure. KeNHA has advised drivers to proceed carefully when approaching the area and to adhere to instructions from traffic police and marshals stationed along the route to ensure smooth flow. KeNHA Director General Eng. Luka Kimeli thanked motorists and residents for their patience and cooperation throughout the repair period.
