## Çelebi Aviation Strategy Shifts to Kenya Cargo Market

Turkish ground handling giant Çelebi Aviation is concentrating its East African operations on Kenya following a KSh 5.2 billion acquisition. The firm has exited the Tanzanian market to prioritize Nairobi’s Jomo Kenyatta International Airport, targeting high-growth logistics flows and regional trade dominance.

### Strategic Consolidation at JKIA
The acquisition of Transglobal Cargo Centre Ltd provides Çelebi immediate operational control at Jomo Kenyatta International Airport (JKIA), the busiest aviation hub in the region. This facility currently processes approximately 20 percent of Kenya’s annual air freight. By shifting focus from Tanzania after its concession agreement expired, the company is prioritizing capital efficiency in a market with a 5 percent projected annual growth rate. This focus on established infrastructure comes as [Kenya finalizes regulatory framework for virtual assets](https://zandani.co.ke/article/kenya-finalizes-regulatory-framework-for-virtual-assets), signaling a broader national push toward modernized, regulated commercial environments that attract foreign direct investment.

### Regional Logistics and Market Realignments
Çelebi’s departure from Tanzania reflects a global transition toward high-value hubs rather than broad, uniform regional footprints. The Turkish firm, which is listed on Borsa Istanbul, aims to leverage Kenya’s position as a regional logistics gateway to service hundreds of airline customers. This strategic realignment highlights how global service providers are increasingly selective with management attention and capital expenditure. As Kenya expands its professional service exports, seen in initiatives like the [KMTC Special Courses](https://zandani.co.ke/article/kmtc-special-courses-new-deal-how-the-namibia-mou-changes-medical-trai) deal with Namibia, the aviation sector is similarly evolving to capture specialized trade volumes through targeted infrastructure investments.

### Impact on East African Trade Flows
The move by Çelebi Aviation underscores the competitive nature of ground handling and cargo services in East Africa. By securing a dominant position in Nairobi, the company gains a foothold in a market that serves as the primary entry point for goods into the hinterland. The decision to exit the Tanzanian market was based on a review of operating conditions, moving away from markets with lower long-term value. This data-driven approach ensures that the company remains lean while scaling its operations across its existing footprint in Europe, Asia, and Africa. The KSh 5.2 billion investment reflects a long-term bet on Kenya’s ability to sustain its role as the logistics heartbeat of the continent.
