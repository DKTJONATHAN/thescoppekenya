Rapper Khaligraph Jones thrilled fans on Friday, April 17, 2026, by unveiling plans for a new business headquarters in Ruaka, Kiambu County. The "Mazishi" hitmaker showcased a construction site slated for his ventures, notably the official Omollo Merch store, marking a major entrepreneurial step.

## Khaligraph Jones Unveils Ruaka Business Headquarters

### New Commercial Hub in Ruaka
The award-winning rapper, who also serves as Blue Ink Corp president, ignited social media with hints of a significant real estate undertaking. In a video posted on his Instagram, Khaligraph Jones was seen supervising a construction site in Ruaka Estate with his team. He initially suggested the site could become a new mall, encouraging entrepreneurs to secure early rental spaces. However, Khaligraph later clarified that the project is a strategic headquarters for his diverse business ventures, prominently featuring the official Omollo Merch store for his customized apparel and brand merchandise. He captioned his post, “Cooking with the boys, Omollo Merch store in 2026,” indicating a collaborative effort.

### Luxury Tech Acquisition Sparks Interest
Beyond the construction update, the video also highlighted Khaligraph's acquisition of a Samsung trifold smartphone, valued at approximately Ksh 600,000, signaling his entry into an elite tier of tech owners. Prominent businessman Sam K delivered the device, and Khaligraph celebrated the milestone over a meal with friends, including veteran P-Unit member Gabu. This high-value acquisition adds another layer to the public's fascination with celebrity investments and their personal narratives, a curiosity often seen in discussions like Anne Kansiime's past statements regarding her divorce.

### Fans Applaud, Others Ponder Investment Pace
The "OG’s" recent moves have garnered widespread admiration from followers, who commend his shift from music to large-scale entrepreneurship. Many consider him a blueprint for Kenyan artists aiming to build sustainable wealth beyond the recording studio. Nevertheless, the swift succession of costly acquisitions, from the luxury smartphone to the Ruaka construction, has also sparked curiosity. Some netizens have raised questions regarding the rapper's various income streams and the remarkable speed of his recent investments. Despite the chatter, Khaligraph Jones remains steadfast, maintaining his distinct "OG" persona as he prepares to make his mark on both the music charts and the commercial landscape of Ruaka in 2026.
