The latest meteorological bulletin indicates that heavy downpours will affect a wide belt stretching from the Rift Valley into the Lake Victoria basin and the Central Highlands. While the weather patterns vary from moderate showers to intense thunderstorms, the cumulative volume of water is significant. This persistent precipitation is expected to influence commercial activity across almost every county in the nation.

For the agricultural sector, the timing of the rains is largely favorable. Counties such as Kericho, Nandi, Trans Nzoia, and Uasin Gishu stand to gain as soil moisture levels remain optimal for cereal crops and tea production. These regions are projected to see a boost in output, provided that the ground remains stable enough to avoid localized landslides or waterlogging of sensitive cash crops.

Conversely, the transport and logistics corridor faces mounting pressure. Nairobi, Mombasa, and the routes connecting them are vulnerable to delays caused by storm-related flooding. Logistics operators are already adjusting schedules to account for impassable rural roads, which drives up the cost of delivering goods to market. This economic friction threatens to offset some of the agricultural gains by inflating local prices for essential commodities.
