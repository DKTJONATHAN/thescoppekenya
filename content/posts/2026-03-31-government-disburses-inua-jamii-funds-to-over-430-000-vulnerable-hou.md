Over 430,000 households supporting orphans and vulnerable children will receive Ksh2,000 each starting today, Tuesday, March 31, 2026. The government has disbursed Ksh860 million under the Inua Jamii cash transfer programme, confirmed by the Ministry of Gender, Culture, and Children Services.

March 2026 Inua Jamii Disbursement Commences

Fund Details and Access The Ministry of Gender, Culture, and Children Services, through the State Department for Children Services, confirmed the release of Ksh860,396,000 on Monday, March 30, for 430,198 beneficiaries of the Cash Transfer for Orphans and Vulnerable Children (CT-OVC) Programme households. Each registered household receives a flat Ksh2,000. Payments are processed via mobile money through the e-Citizen platform, with beneficiaries able to access their funds by dialling *222# on their mobile phones. This system is designed to ensure ease of access even in remote areas.

Programme Objectives and Impact Principal Secretary Carren Ageng'o stated the funds are designed to keep orphans and vulnerable children within their own families, fostering opportunities for better education, health, and nutrition. The CT-OVC Programme specifically encourages the fostering and retention of these children within their families and communities while promoting their human capital development. Beyond direct financial aid, the programme actively promotes improved school enrolment, enhanced nutrition, food security, and stronger child protection at home. These broader efforts to secure community well-being align with initiatives focusing on long-term resource management, such as the recently launched landmark guide on the conservation of African livestock genetic resources. Inua Jamii's Reach and History The CT-OVC Programme operates under the wider Inua Jamii initiative, which targets the most vulnerable citizens, including the elderly, people with severe disabilities, and children who are orphaned or at serious risk. Enrolled households typically receive Ksh4,000 every two months, paid in two separate Ksh2,000 instalments. This current disbursement specifically covers one of those monthly portions for March 2026. According to the World Bank, approximately 3.6 million children are classified as orphans or vulnerable, accounting for nearly one-fifth of the population under 18. The government indicates that families receiving these transfers are better equipped to keep children in school, ensure proper nutrition, and shield them from exploitation, early marriage, and child labour within their communities. The CT-OVC programme has significantly expanded its reach since its inception in 2004 as a modest pilot covering only 500 households, initially established to address the thousands of children orphaned by HIV/AIDS.
