Muthurwa Market traders obstructed Jogoo Road on Saturday, March 28, dumping garbage to protest the Nairobi City County Government's prolonged failure to collect waste. The demonstration caused significant traffic disruptions for commuters travelling to and from the Nairobi Central Business District, forcing diversions and leading to widespread frustration.

Traffic and Public Health Concerns Escalate

Commuter Disruptions and Diversions The protest, which commenced on Saturday, March 28, directly impacted traffic flow along Jogoo Road, a critical artery connecting residential areas to the Nairobi CBD. Motorists were forced to seek alternative routes, diverting onto already congested roads such as Outer Ring Road and Mombasa Road. This resulted in considerable delays for city residents attempting to navigate their daily commutes. The immediate impact underscored the vulnerability of Nairobi's transport infrastructure to civic unrest stemming from unaddressed service delivery issues.

Traders Allege Negligence by County Leadership Traders stated the demonstrations were a direct consequence of weeks of inaction by Governor Johnson Sakaja's administration regarding waste collection at Muthurwa Market. Despite repeated pleas, mounting garbage piles have attracted scavengers, including stray pigs and dogs, posing significant health risks to traders, customers, and nearby residents. Eyewitnesses confirmed that garbage was deliberately emptied onto the road to draw attention to what traders termed persistent negligence from the county government. The incident highlights growing concerns over the city's persistent waste management crisis. Several estates and business areas frequently contend with uncollected garbage and blocked drainage systems. During periods of heavy rainfall, clogged drainage has repeatedly led to localised flooding, disrupting transport and elevating public health risks.

Inter-Governmental Agreement Under Scrutiny The protests occur barely a month after the national government and the Nairobi City County Government signed a cooperation agreement aimed at improving service delivery in the capital. The agreement, reached at State House, Nairobi, on February 17, saw the national government assume responsibility for critical services, including garbage collection and disposal. Governor Sakaja had previously maintained that this new framework was designed to enhance collaboration and strengthen service delivery through intergovernmental support. Pressure is now mounting on the county to urgently address these waste management challenges before the situation further escalates. The broader political landscape within Nairobi often sees various groups advocating for improved governance and service delivery, as evidenced by recent political activities within the city. Officials have yet to issue a detailed statement on the protests.
