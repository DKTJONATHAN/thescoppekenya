## Uhuru Kenyatta Politics: Former President Defies Retirement Calls

Former President Uhuru Kenyatta stated on Friday, May 1, that he would not resign as Jubilee Party leader, challenging critics who want him to quit active Kenyan politics. He defended his constitutional right to political engagement, accusing the current administration of undermining multiparty democracy. This declaration was made during a phone call at the homecoming ceremony of Jubilee Secretary General Moitalel Ole Kenta.

### Defense of Political Rights

Uhuru Kenyatta firmly rejected calls for him to withdraw from public life, insisting that retirement does not remove his right to participate in politics. He questioned why he is being pressured to remain silent, drawing parallels with past leaders like Daniel Arap Moi who continued to be politically active after leaving office without facing similar demands. The former Head of State argued that he retains the liberty to speak and belong to the Jubilee party, emphasizing his constitutional right to freedom of expression. This continued involvement in Uhuru Kenyatta politics, he maintained, is a legitimate exercise of his democratic freedoms, a topic often discussed in the context of [Kenyan laws define everyday actions that could result in arrest](https://zandani.co.ke/article/kenyan-laws-define-everyday-actions-that-could-result-in-arrest).

### Accusations Against Current Administration

During his remarks, Uhuru Kenyatta appeared to criticize President William Ruto, accusing him of interfering with several political parties. He claimed that such actions undermine multiparty democracy in the country and could set a dangerous precedent. The former President stated that he has the right to stand with Jubilee, noting that the alleged interference extends beyond his party to others, potentially aiming to suppress them.

### 2027 Endorsement

The event also served as a platform for Uhuru Kenyatta to openly endorse former Interior Cabinet Secretary Fred Matiang’i as Jubilee’s preferred presidential candidate for the 2027 General Elections. This move signals Jubilee's intention to challenge the current administration, with Matiang’i vowing to address policies implemented by the present government if they come to power. The ceremony saw various Jubilee leaders, including Jeremiah Kioni and Fred Matiang'i, echoing Uhuru’s sentiments and expressing their intent to collaborate with other political parties.
