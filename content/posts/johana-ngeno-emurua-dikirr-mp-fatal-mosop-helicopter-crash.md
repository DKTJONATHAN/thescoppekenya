## Johana Ng’eno, Emurua Dikirr MP: Final Hours Before Fatal Mosop Helicopter Crash The nation is grappling with the profound loss of Johana Ng’eno, the Emurua Dikirr MP, who tragically perished alongside five other individuals in a helicopter crash in Mosop, Nandi County, on the evening of Saturday, February 28, 2026. This incident has prompted immediate investigations by the Kenya Civil Aviation Authority (KCAA) and the Aircraft Accident Investigation Department (AAID) to ascertain its precise cause.

The Emurua Dikirr MP's Engagements on Saturday The final day of Johana Ng'eno's life was marked by a series of public engagements and community service, reflecting his dedication as the Emurua Dikirr MP. His itinerary on Saturday, February 28, commenced with active involvement in search and rescue operations at the Mara River. Here, he joined efforts to locate two individuals whose vehicle had been swept away by floodwaters in the preceding days. Later, the legislator journeyed to Endebes to participate in a cultural celebration honouring a veteran Kalenjin musician. In what would become one of his last public messages, Ng'eno shared on social media, expressing his enthusiasm for joining friends and leaders in celebrating the musician's 35th anniversary in the industry. The post featured a video depicting the MP in high spirits, engaging in song and dance just hours before the tragic incident.

Deteriorating Conditions Leading to the Fatal Mosop Helicopter Crash It remains unclear when exactly the Emurua Dikirr MP departed Endebes. However, it is confirmed that he subsequently boarded a helicopter, registration 5Y-DSB, with five other occupants for onward travel. The aircraft, an Airbus Helicopters H125, had been utilised earlier in the day for his movements between various engagements. As the helicopter approached Mosop Sub-County in Nandi County, weather conditions reportedly deteriorated significantly, with mist and heavy rains severely reducing visibility and creating challenging flying conditions. Witnesses reported that the chopper made an emergency landing near Chepkiep Forest in Mosop, where the pilot briefly interacted with locals while assessing the situation. After a short pause, the helicopter reportedly attempted to take off again as conditions appeared to improve marginally. Provisional reports indicate that the fatal Mosop helicopter crash occurred at approximately 4:45 pm. Moments after the second takeoff, the aircraft reportedly lost stability, struggled in the air, and then crashed near a forested area, bursting into flames upon impact. All six individuals on board perished in the accident.

National Response and Investigation into the Fatal Mosop Helicopter Crash In the immediate aftermath, Nandi County Police Chief Samuel Mukuusi confirmed the incident, attributing bad weather as a possible contributing factor. The identities of the other victims have been confirmed as pilot George Were, Kenya Forest Service officer Amos Kipngetich Rotich, cameraman Nick Koskey, high school teacher Carlos Keter, and Narok County Government employee Wycliffe Rono. The bodies of the victims were initially transported to Moi Teaching and Referral Hospital (MTRH) in Eldoret, with some reportedly burnt beyond recognition, necessitating forensic identification. National Assembly Speaker Moses Wetang'ula has since formed a special parliamentary team to coordinate with the late MP's family on funeral preparations and offer support. The team, led by Tinderet MP Julius Melly and Buuri MP Mugambi Rindikiri, has been dispatched to facilitate arrangements. President William Ruto, along with other political leaders, has mourned the late Johana Ng'eno as a "progressive and devoted servant of the people." The Kenya Civil Aviation Authority (KCAA) has formally announced the launch of investigations into the crash through its Aircraft Accident Investigation Department (AAID) to determine the definitive cause of this national tragedy.
