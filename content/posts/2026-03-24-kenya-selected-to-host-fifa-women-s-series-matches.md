By Celestine Nzioka, Za Ndani Kenya has secured the rights to host the 2026 FIFA Women's Series matches, marking the first time the country will stage a FIFA global football event on Kenyan soil. The announcement was made Tuesday, March 24, 2026, by Cabinet Secretary for Youth Affairs, Creative Economy and Sports Salim Mvurya, with matches scheduled for April 11-15 at Nyayo National Stadium in Nairobi.

Kenya's Inaugural FIFA Global Event

A Vote of Confidence Cabinet Secretary Salim Mvurya described Kenya's selection as a strong vote of confidence in its proven hosting capability. He stated that this decision affirms the nation's consistent ability to successfully plan, organise, and deliver world-class sporting events. Kenya will host Australia, India, and Malawi for Group 4 of the 2026 FIFA Women's Series.

Harambee Starlets on Home Turf The national women's team, the Harambee Starlets, will carry the nation's hopes as they compete on home turf. This provides local football fans a rare opportunity to watch their team compete in a FIFA-led international series. The Harambee Starlets will face India in their opening fixture on April 11.

Broader International Engagement Beyond the women's matches, Kenya has also been selected to participate in the FIFA International Friendly Series, a tournament that will see more than 40 countries take part in. CS Mvurya extended a formal welcome to the participating nations-India, Australia, and Malawi-and the global football community, affirming the country's readiness to host this hard-to-come-by tournament.

Men's Team Engagements While the Harambee Starlets prepare to host at home, the men's national team, Harambee Stars, have already departed for Kigali, Rwanda. They are set to participate in the FIFA Series Men's matches, which will kick off from March 25 to March 31. Harambee Stars will face Estonia on March 27, before playing either Rwanda or Grenada on March 30.

Proven Hosting Credentials Kenya's track record in hosting major sporting events was cited as a key factor in the selection. The country has previously staged events such as the HSBC World Rugby Sevens Series, the WRC Safari Rally, the Magical Kenya Open, and the African Nations Championship.

Infrastructure Development and AFCON 2027 The Government is investing heavily in sports infrastructure, including the development of the Raila Odinga International Stadium at the Talanta Sports Complex and the ongoing modernisation of Kasarani and Nyayo National Stadiums to meet FIFA standards. This reflects a wider commitment to national development and public service enhancement, as seen in the strategic expansion of services like the Unclaimed Financial Assets Authority at Huduma Centres across the country. CS Mvurya highlighted that hosting the FIFA Women's Series feeds into Kenya's larger AFCON 2027 preparations. The nation is set to co-host the Africa Cup of Nations alongside Uganda and Tanzania from June 19 to July 18 next year, making the event an instrumental litmus test for infrastructure, systems, and operational readiness.
