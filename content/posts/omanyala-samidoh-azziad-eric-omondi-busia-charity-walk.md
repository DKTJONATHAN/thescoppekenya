## Omanyala, Samidoh, Azziad, and Eric Omondi’s Busia Charity Walk: The Usual Suspects

Nairobi witnessed its usual Monday morning gridlock compounded by a celebrity spectacle as Ferdinand Omanyala, Samidoh, and Azziad Nasenya flagged off Eric Omondi’s 466km Busia Charity Walk. The walk aims to raise funds for the Sisi Kwa Sisi Help and Rescue Centre.

Another day, another grand philanthropic gesture from Eric Omondi, and naturally, the usual suspects of Kenyan celebritydom were right there for the cameras. On Monday, March 2, 2026, Nairobi's Waiyaki Way became a stage for Africa's fastest man, Ferdinand Omanyala, Mugithi crooner Samidoh, and digital darling Azziad Nasenya, all ostensibly supporting Omondi’s latest "humanitarian mission." One must wonder if these high-profile appearances are truly about the cause or simply another opportunity to bask in the reflected glow of manufactured goodwill.

### The Spectacle of Omanyala, Samidoh, and Azziad's "Support"

Ferdinand Omanyala, known for sprinting, not marathon walking, was prominently featured, giving the event a "high-octane boost." It’s a convenient narrative, really: the speedster lends his brand to endurance, perhaps hoping some of that "endurance" rubs off on his public image beyond the track. Meanwhile, Samidoh "traded his guitar for walking shoes," a temporary inconvenience for what must surely be a significant public relations win. This "Sisi Kwa Sisi" movement, constantly begging for "community spirit," seems to thrive more on celebrity endorsements than genuine, quiet contributions. Azziad Nasenya, the "online sensation," offered her encouragement, undoubtedly ensuring the event trended across the digital landscape, where "small, individual donations" often translate into millions for campaigners. One can't help but ask, is the fanfare surrounding Omanyala, Samidoh, and Azziad's involvement genuinely about the vulnerable, or is it merely about maintaining visibility for their own brands?

### Eric Omondi’s Endless Walk to Busia: A Never-Ending Story?

Eric Omondi, the self-proclaimed activist, is embarking on this "gruelling 466-kilometre walk" to the Busia border, yet again, to complete the Sisi Kwa Sisi Help and Rescue Centre. This follows his "successful" 484km "Walk of Love" to Mombasa just weeks prior. If the Mombasa walk was such a triumph, why is a second, equally arduous journey needed to secure the "remaining resources" by May 2026? It paints a rather bleak picture of the centre's progress, or perhaps, the perpetual need for public attention. Promises of "free healthcare, education, and emergency medical services" from this sanctuary for vulnerable Kenyans sound grand, but the continuous fundraising walks suggest a project perpetually on the brink of incompletion. Much like the constant reforms in our education sector, where changes from KNEC to KNEAC are hailed as progress without addressing underlying issues, Omondi's perpetual walks feel like a band-aid on a gaping wound, designed for optics rather than tangible, lasting solutions. Omondi continues to urge "well-wishers" to contribute "anything from cash to construction materials," which, for a facility supposedly on the verge of completion, sounds like a never-ending plea for public handouts.
