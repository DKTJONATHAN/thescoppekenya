## Wajir's Moment: Re-budgeting Our National Priorities

Wajir County hosting Madaraka Day symbols a profound reorientation of national priorities. It marks a critical fiscal shift, channeling resources from historical containment towards active infrastructure development, finally integrating Northern Kenya's pastoral economy into the broader national GDP.

Dear Wajir, and indeed, to the collective conscience of Kenya,

The decision to host national Madaraka Day celebrations in Wajir County is more than a symbolic gesture; it is a tangible re-allocation of national attention and, crucially, national capital. For too long, the narrative of Northern Kenya has been framed by historical marginalization, a region viewed primarily through a security lens rather than as a vital economic frontier. From the colonial-era "Northern Frontier District" designation to the scars of the Shifta war, the Wagalla massacre, and the later tragedies of Garissa Gubaay and Malkamari, investment priorities here have often centred on containment rather than cultivation of potential. Now, the transformation of a local field into a modern stadium for a national event signals a visible shift in the ledger, moving budgets from maintaining a 'security zone' to actively fostering an 'economic gateway'.

### The New Corridors of Opportunity

This commitment is concretely expressed through mega-projects like the LAPSSET Corridor Project and the Horn of Africa Gateway Development Project (HoAGDP). These initiatives are not simply lines on a map; they are arteries designed to pump economic life into regions historically starved of capital. LAPSSET, connecting Lamu to South Sudan and Ethiopia, along with HoAGDP, targeting infrastructure improvements across Garissa, Mandera, and Marsabit, are directly aimed at integrating the pastoral economy into the national framework. Better roads and logistical hubs mean improved market access for livestock and other regional products, increasing the financial resilience of local communities. This strategic investment in connectivity mirrors the ongoing efforts to attract private investors for crucial infrastructure upgrades, such as the enhancements sought for Moi Airport passenger services, ensuring a broader network of national economic nodes.

### From Containment to Economic Gateway

The transition from viewing Northern Kenya as a remote 'frontier' to a dynamic 'economic gateway' is pivotal. The region, with its strategic position bordering Ethiopia and Somalia, holds immense potential for trade and renewable energy development. Projects like the Lake Turkana Wind Power Project demonstrate the capacity for this region to contribute significantly to national energy grids and diversify the economy beyond traditional livestock. This integrated approach elevates the conversation from mere ceremonial inclusion to genuine economic integration, fostering an environment where residents can actively participate in and benefit from national development. It provides avenues for local entrepreneurs, including women seeking financial independence as Akothee often advocates, to seize opportunities arising from these new economic flows.

### Sustaining the Momentum of Inclusion

While the Madaraka Day celebrations in Wajir are a powerful statement, the true measure of their impact will be the sustained commitment that follows. This cannot be a one-off spectacle. The historical wounds of neglect and violence, from 1963 to recent memory, demand consistent, principled investment that trickles down to every household. The focus must remain on ensuring that the pastoral economy is not just recognized but actively supported with appropriate infrastructure and policies, turning the promise of internal self-rule into tangible economic self-determination for every citizen in these counties. The building of stadiums and roads must be matched by investments in education, healthcare, and water resources that empower local populations to fully leverage these new opportunities.

Will the spirit of inclusion and fiscal re-prioritization demonstrated by Wajir's hosting of Madaraka Day extend beyond the cameras, ensuring every shilling budgeted genuinely reaches its intended recipient for the long term?
