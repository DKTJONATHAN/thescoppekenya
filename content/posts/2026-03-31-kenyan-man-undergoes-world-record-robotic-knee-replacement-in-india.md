A man from the country has achieved a world record following a robotic bilateral knee replacement in Faridabad, India. The groundbreaking surgery, performed on July 20, 2025, recognized Victor as the tallest patient to receive the largest knee implant, as announced by Marengo Asia Hospitals.

Pioneering Robotic Orthopaedic Procedure Victor, measuring six-foot-six, had been experiencing debilitating pain in both knees for several months, with walking becoming increasingly challenging despite receiving treatment locally. After consulting various facilities in the country, he travelled to India for advanced medical care. Detailed imaging and evaluation at the Indian facility confirmed a diagnosis of advanced bilateral knee osteoarthritis, necessitating surgical intervention.

Surgical Precision and Record Recognition The complex operation, led by Dr. Anurag Aggarwal, Clinical Director of Orthopaedics and Joint Replacement, utilized robotic-assisted technology. Dr. Aggarwal emphasized that the technology was crucial due to Victor’s exceptional height and intricate anatomy. Robotic CT-based planning facilitated precise assessment of implant sizing and alignment, ensuring optimal outcomes for this challenging case. The surgery was successfully completed, and Victor began walking within 24 hours. Dr. Aggarwal received the World Records of Excellence award for performing the surgery on a 197-centimetre-tall patient, deploying an extra-large femoral component and a size-8 tibial tray. This marks a rare instance of bilateral robotic knee replacement surgery on a patient of such stature.

Advantages of Robotic-Assisted Surgery Hospital officials highlighted that Victor’s case demonstrates the significant advantages of robotic-assisted procedures. These benefits include smaller incisions, reduced blood loss, faster rehabilitation, and improved accuracy in implant placement. Medical Director Navneet Singh Chhabra stated that robotic technology enables handling even the most complex anatomical challenges with unmatched precision, focusing on faster recovery, minimal pain, and superior functional outcomes. This case reflects a growing trend of individuals from the country seeking specialized medical care abroad for complex procedures, particularly when local treatment options are limited or have not yielded desired results. Such international pursuits align with broader opportunities for individuals from the country to engage with global resources, including fully funded scholarships available, as recently reported.
