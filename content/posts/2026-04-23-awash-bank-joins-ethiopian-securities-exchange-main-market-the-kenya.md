## Ethiopian Securities Exchange Capital Growth

Awash Bank S.C. has officially listed on the Ethiopian Securities Exchange, signaling a significant shift in regional financial markets. As the country's largest private lender, its entry provides institutional depth to the nascent equity market while setting a precedent for upcoming banking listings.

### Institutional Depth via Listing by Introduction

The listing of Awash Bank follows the 2025 debuts of Wegagen Bank and Gadaa Bank. Unlike a traditional initial public offering, this listing is by introduction. No new shares were issued, allowing the existing 12,000 shareholders to trade through licensed brokers. This move provides the Ethiopian Securities Exchange (ESX) with the scale necessary to attract larger institutional investors. ESX CEO Tilahun Esmael Kassahun noted that the inclusion of the largest private commercial bank by assets and profitability anchors the equity market. This development comes at a time when regional transparency and regulatory standards are under heavy scrutiny, reflecting concerns similar to those raised when [Martha Karua Demands Answers After Reports of RSF Financier Holding Kenyan Passport Sparks National Concern](https://zandani.co.ke/article/martha-karua-demands-answers-rsf-financier-holding-kenyan-passport) over cross-border integrity.

### Financial Performance and Asset Quality

Awash reported a gross profit of ETB 25.67 billion for the fiscal year ending June 30, 2025. This represents a 137 percent increase year on year, the highest ever for a private Ethiopian bank. Net profit reached ETB 18.71 billion, supported by total assets of ETB 442.6 billion and deposits exceeding ETB 358 billion. The bank maintained a non-performing loan ratio of 1.8 percent, significantly lower than the 5 percent ceiling mandated by the National Bank of Ethiopia. Earnings per share rose to ETB 783 per ETB 1,000 par value, reflecting strong underlying value for its shareholder base. The bank also mobilized over US$2 billion in foreign exchange, a 25 percent increase that highlights its role in the country's broader trade economy.

### Strategic Pipeline for 2026

The ESX plans to complete nine listings before the end of the Ethiopian fiscal year on July 7, 2026. Dashen Bank and Bank of Abyssinia are the next expected participants, having completed their registration with the capital market authority. Abay Bank, Anbesa Bank, and Amhara Bank are also in advanced regulatory stages. This rapid expansion of the exchange provides a structured exit and entry point for capital, which is essential for regional economic integration. As the Ethiopian market matures, it offers a new frontier for East African financial services. This shift toward institutional investment stands in contrast to other sectors where funding remains a challenge, a sentiment echoed in the [Magical Kenya Open: Junior Players 'Inspired,' But Where's the Real Dough, Darlings?](https://zandani.co.ke/article/magical-kenya-open-junior-players-inspiration-cash-gossip) report which questions the availability of liquid capital for local talent. Awash Bank's 989 branches and 15 million customers provide a massive foundation for the exchange as it seeks to drive national economic growth.
