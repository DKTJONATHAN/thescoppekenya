## Social Health Authority Urges Employees to Verify Health Fund Deductions

The Social Health Authority (SHA) has issued a directive urging all employees to confirm the remittance of their monthly health insurance contributions. This mandate, released on Thursday, March 12, 2026, emphasizes employers' legal responsibility to deduct and submit contributions by the ninth day of the following month to ensure continuous access to healthcare services. The Authority stressed that non-compliance affects employee benefits.

### Employer Compliance and Remittance Deadlines

Employers are legally obligated to deduct and remit employee contributions to the Social Health Authority on or before the ninth day of the month following salary payments. The SHA warned that failure to adhere to this deadline could result in employees being denied access to medical services under the scheme. This requirement ensures that workers maintain uninterrupted healthcare coverage, a critical component of national well-being. The Social Health Insurance Act 2024, which replaced the National Health Insurance Fund (NHIF), mandates a 2.75% deduction from an employee's gross monthly salary without an upper limit. ### Verifying Contribution Status

Employees are strongly encouraged to frequently check their contribution status to verify that deductions reflected in their payslips have been accurately received and recorded by SHA. Several convenient methods are available for this verification. Workers can access their accounts through the Afyayangu portal or use the Afyayangu mobile application. Alternatively, dialing *147# on their mobile phones provides an immediate way to check records. ### Reporting Non-Compliance

The SHA has advised employees to immediately report any instances where their contributions have not been deducted, remitted, or updated in their statements. Such complaints should be submitted via the SHA customer service email to enable the Authority to follow up directly with the respective employers. This proactive approach by employees is crucial in ensuring accountability and maintaining the integrity of the social health insurance scheme.
