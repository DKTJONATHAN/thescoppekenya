### Navigating the Reality of Urban Encounters

The sight of a snake in a suburban driveway has become an increasingly common narrative as Nairobi’s real estate expansion pushes further into peripheral scrublands and grasslands. As concrete structures rise in areas once occupied by natural vegetation, the displacement of local wildlife is inevitable. When snakes are pushed out of their traditional burrows, they often seek refuge in areas that provide warmth or shelter, leading them directly into human-inhabited spaces.

For many residents, these encounters are becoming a recurring challenge. Whether it is the result of shifting weather patterns or the fragmentation of local habitats, the proximity between high-end estates and natural ecosystems is narrowing. Homeowners now face the reality that a quiet suburban life does not necessarily exempt them from the presence of local fauna. While digital engagement remains a common first response, the situation necessitates a shift toward practical safety measures and a better understanding of how to coexist with or deter these uninvited guests.

### Safety Protocols for Property Owners

When a reptile is spotted on private property, the immediate instinct to record the moment for social media often overlooks the potential danger. Handling or approaching a snake is inherently risky, particularly if the species is venomous. The defensive nature of a trapped animal often leads to aggression, and attempting to relocate or harm the creature significantly increases the possibility of a bite.

The Kenya Wildlife Service (KWS) advises a disciplined approach to these encounters. Instead of engaging with the animal, residents should prioritize clearing the area of children and pets. The most effective course of action is to maintain a clear line of sight from a protected distance while securing the perimeter. Rather than attempting a DIY removal, property owners should reach out to licensed professional handlers or the KWS emergency services. These specialists possess the training and equipment necessary to capture the animal without causing injury to themselves or the wildlife.

Preventative maintenance is equally essential. Snakes are often drawn to properties that harbor rodents, which serve as their primary food source. By maintaining tidy gardens, managing waste effectively, and sealing small gaps under doors or within perimeter walls, residents can make their compounds significantly less appealing to reptiles.
