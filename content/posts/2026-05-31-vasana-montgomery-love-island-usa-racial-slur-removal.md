## Love Island USA Faces Renewed Vetting Scrutiny After Vasana Montgomery Removal

Days before its Season 8 premiere, *Love Island USA* has removed contestant Vasana Montgomery following the resurfacing of videos showing her using a racial slur. This marks a concerning second consecutive year the popular dating reality show has faced such a pre-season casting scandal, raising serious questions about its vetting processes.

Vasana Montgomery, a 25-year-old business owner from Beaverton, Oregon, has been removed from the cast of *Love Island USA* Season 8, just two days after her participation was publicly announced. The swift decision came after multiple videos of Montgomery using a racial slur widely circulated online. Her removal occurred less than a week before the show's highly anticipated premiere on Peacock, scheduled for June 2.

This incident mirrors a similar controversy that plagued the reality dating competition last year, indicating a systemic vulnerability in the production's pre-screening efforts. In *Love Island USA* Season 7, contestant Yulissa Escobar was ejected from the villa shortly before the season's launch for identical reasons, after social media users unearthed videos of her using a racial slur. Escobar later issued an apology two days after her removal.

Further compounding the show's difficulties in its previous season, Cierra Ortega was also removed from *Love Island USA* Season 7, approximately a month after Escobar's departure. Ortega's removal followed allegations and evidence of her using a racial slur referencing the Asian community in social media posts from 2015 and 2023. The repeated pattern of contestants being removed for racial slurs from *Love Island USA* casts a stark light on the persistent gaps in the show's background checks.
