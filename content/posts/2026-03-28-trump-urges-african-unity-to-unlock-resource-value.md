U.S. President Donald Trump, speaking at the Saudi Investment Forum in Miami on Friday, March 27, urged African nations to unify. He highlighted the continent's substantial value in land and natural resources as key to unlocking its economic potential.

Africa's Resource Potential and U.S. Strategic Interests

Trump's Call for Unity and Resource Development During the forum, attended by a delegation including African business leaders and Sierra Leone President Julius Maada Bio, Trump emphasized Africa's "tremendous potential" and "tremendous value underground" in minerals. He asserted that a unified approach among African nations is crucial for fully realizing this wealth, noting that private investors are already exploring opportunities across the continent.

Critical Minerals and Strategic Partnerships Trump's remarks underscore a renewed U.S. focus on securing access to critical minerals, which are essential for technological development and economic growth. This interest follows a 2025 mining agreement with the Democratic Republic of the Congo (DRC) under a resources-for-security framework, granting the U.S. access to the nation's minerals. Despite the promotion of these deals as major economic opportunities, local communities in DRC mining regions continue to contend with poverty and armed conflict. The 2026 Critical Minerals Ministerial, hosted by the U.S. in February, brought together representatives from 54 countries, including Kenya and the DRC, to discuss building secure and resilient supply chains for these vital resources.

Kenya's Strategic Engagement and Geopolitical Context Kenya is actively pursuing a bilateral trade agreement with the U.S. to partner on mineral exploration and processing, having hosted the U.S.-Kenya Critical Supply Chains Conference in February. This engagement occurs as President Trump signals interest in African countries amid the ongoing U.S.-Israel war on Iran, after several traditional allies in Europe and Asia declined to provide support. Concurrently, Kenya has been reinforcing defence cooperation with the United States. General Charles Kahariri, Chief of the Defence Forces, recently held talks in Nairobi with the Commander of U.S. Naval Forces Europe and Africa, underscoring deepening military ties during the Middle East conflict.
