By Celestine Nzioka, Za Ndani

World Athletics announced on Tuesday, April 7, 2026, a significant change for elite long-distance running, confirming marathons will be removed from its World Championships after 2030. This strategic shift introduces a standalone World Marathon Championships from 2030, impacting top athletes globally, particularly dominant distance runners from the region.

## World Marathon Championships to Launch

The governing body confirmed that a standalone World Marathon Championships will commence in 2030, effectively separating the marathon race from the traditional World Athletics Championships. This move reshapes how elite marathon competitions are organized worldwide going forward. World Athletics stated, "World Athletics has outlined a landmark strategic roadmap for the future of distance running with the creation of a World Athletics Marathon Championships from 2030." This strategic re-evaluation reflects a broader trend of significant institutional adaptations across various global sectors, much like recent reports on the [US Military's deployment of Claude AI in Middle East operations](https://zandani.co.ke/article/us-military-deploys-claude-ai-middle-east-operations).

### Transition Period and New Structure

Marathons will continue to be a part of the World Championships in 2027 and 2029, providing athletes and federations a short transition window. From 2030, the event will move to its own dedicated global championship structure. Effectively, from 2031 onwards, the marathon, or any other road running distance, will no longer be part of the World Athletics Championships. The new World Marathon Championships will be held annually, with men and women competing in alternating years, maintaining global competition frequency while giving the marathon its own focused platform separate from track and field events.

### Strategic Vision and Global Appeal

World Athletics indicates this change aims to further grow the marathon by aligning elite racing with its global mass participation appeal, which sees millions of runners inspired by professionals in major city marathons. The race has been awarded Elite Label status, recognizing its unique place in sporting history and its long-term potential within the international calendar. Full details about collaborations with key stakeholders who have expanded marathon running globally are expected in the coming months. Concurrently, the World Athletics Road Running Championships will persist as a separate annual event, ensuring other road races, such as half marathons, retain an international stage despite the marathon’s separation.

### Athens Eyed for Inaugural Event

Discussions are currently underway with Athens to host the first edition of the standalone World Marathon Championships in 2030, a move that underscores the city’s historic link as the birthplace of the marathon. The Athens Marathon, known as The Authentic, has also received Elite Label status, acknowledging its global importance and positioning it as a key race in World Athletics’ new strategic direction. World Athletics President Sebastian Coe stated that the marathon deserves its own spotlight, adding that the new format will create a global celebration dedicated entirely to the discipline, aligning with its long-standing heritage. "This is an opportunity to create a dedicated global celebration of marathon running, held in a setting that honours its heritage while shaping a modern championship that reflects the scale and spirit of the global running community," President Coe affirmed.
