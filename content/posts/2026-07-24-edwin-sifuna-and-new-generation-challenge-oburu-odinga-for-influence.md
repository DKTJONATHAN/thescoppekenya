Internal friction within the Orange Democratic Movement and the Azimio coalition reached a breaking point in July 2026. As grassroots support shifts toward Senator Edwin Sifuna and the Linda Mwananchi faction, the influence of veteran stalwarts like Oburu Odinga faces an unprecedented challenge.

## A Generational Divide Within ODM

### The Old Guard Faces a Rude Awakening
Political tides in Kenya are notoriously fickle, and the latest shifts within the Orange Democratic Movement are no exception. For decades, party stalwarts associated with the founding guard have enjoyed undisputed authority. However, current trends indicate that the party base is no longer interested in legacy politics. Observers note that the era of relying solely on historical seniority is expiring. The political climate has turned unpredictable, leaving those who refuse to adapt to the new reality increasingly isolated from the party core.

### Grassroots Power Shifts to Sifuna
The emergence of the Linda Mwananchi faction has redefined the standard for party leadership. Senator Edwin Sifuna has successfully captured the public imagination by articulating the frustrations of the common citizen. While the older generation clings to traditional hierarchical structures, the younger leadership speaks directly to the needs of the electorate. This movement has gained enough traction to make seasoned veterans appear disconnected from the challenges faced by the modern voter.

### The Cost of Ignoring the Street
There is growing criticism directed at the old guard, with some critics using harsh, hyperbolic language to describe the perceived political irrelevance of these veterans. By likening the old guard to archaic figures, these critics suggest that relying on past glory is a failing strategy. As the party grapples with these internal divisions, it is becoming clear that the narrative surrounding who commands the base is being reshaped by the significant volume of support for the younger, more vocal leadership. This shift confirms that influence within the party is no longer inherited through tenure, but must be earned through active, daily engagement with the grassroots.
