## Government Allocates Ksh2 Billion for Protest Victim Compensation

President William Ruto today announced a Ksh2 billion allocation in the supplementary budget to compensate victims of protests from 2017 to 2024. The compensation process, managed with the Kenya National Commission on Human Rights, is targeted for completion by June 2026.

### Presidential Directive on Compensation

Speaking during a joint parliamentary group meeting between the United Democratic Alliance (UDA) and the Orange Democratic Movement (ODM) parties on Tuesday, March 10, President Ruto confirmed the government's commitment to the initiative. He stated that the Ksh2 billion has been included in the supplementary budget forwarded to Parliament for approval. This allocation aims to provide redress for those affected by past protests.

### Identification and Profiling of Victims

The President highlighted that the Kenya National Commission on Human Rights (KNCHR) has been instrumental in identifying and profiling the victims. A comprehensive list of individuals affected by protests from 2017 to 2024 has been compiled, ensuring a broad scope for the compensation exercise. Ruto expressed confidence that the exercise should conclude before June 2026, contingent on parliamentary approval of the allocated funds.

### Mandate of the KNCHR

This proclamation follows a previous directive by the President, issued via a gazette notice, instructing the KNCHR to develop and submit a framework for guiding the compensation of victims of human rights violations and protests. The KNCHR was given 60 days from the date of the proclamation to complete this task and publish a detailed report, a process that is likely already concluded. This directive underscores the KNCHR's constitutional mandate in overseeing and redressing human rights violations.

### Historical Context and Political Agreement

The compensation of protest victims was a key component of the 10-point agreement signed between the ODM and UDA parties. This agreement was forged following the widespread protests of 2024, which ultimately led to the formation of the broad-based government. Earlier attempts by President Ruto to establish a compensation panel in 2025 were dismissed by the courts, which cited the panel's illegitimacy and affirmed that the appropriate channel for such compensation lay with the KNCHR.
