By Celestine Nzioka The Energy and Petroleum Regulatory Authority (EPRA) today, Tuesday, April 14, 2026, increased the prices of Super Petrol and Diesel for the April to May cycle, concluding weeks of public anticipation. Kerosene prices, however, will remain unchanged, with the revised rates becoming effective at midnight for the next 30 days.

Max pump prices increase

Price Adjustments Following EPRA's latest fuel review released on Tuesday, April 14, 2026, the maximum allowed petroleum pump prices for Super Petrol and Diesel have increased by Ksh28.69 per litre and Ksh40.30 per litre respectively. Super Petrol will now retail at Ksh206.97, Diesel at Ksh206.84, and Kerosene at Ksh152.78. These new prices are effective from April 15 to May 14, 2026.

VAT Reduction and Government Cushion To cushion consumers from the high landed cost of petroleum products resulting from escalated international market prices, EPRA announced a reduction in the Value Added Tax (VAT) rate on Super Petrol, Diesel, and Kerosene from 16 per cent to 13 per cent. The government will further utilise approximately Ksh6.2 Billion from the Petroleum Development Levy (PDL) Fund to stabilise pump prices.

Excluded Fuel The regulator clarified that Super Petrol delivered by One Petroleum through the MT Paloma was not included in the computation of the new fuel prices, in adherence to an earlier government directive.

Landed Cost Increases EPRA reported a significant rise in the average landed cost of imported petroleum products. The landed cost of imported Super Petrol increased by 41.53 per cent, from Ksh75,266.82 per cubic metre in December last year to Ksh106,526.39 in January 2026. Similarly, the landed cost of Diesel increased by 68.72 per cent from Ksh82,292.99 per cubic metre to Ksh138,764.76 per cubic metre, while Kerosene saw a 105.15 per cent increase from Ksh82,684.76 per cubic metre to Ksh169,632.55 per cubic metre over the same period.

Regional Variations Motorists in Mombasa will encounter Super Petrol retailing at Ksh203.69 per litre, Diesel at Ksh203.56, and Kerosene at Ksh149.49. For Nakuru, Super Petrol will retail at Ksh206.03 and Diesel at Ksh206.25. Kisumu will see Super Petrol at Ksh206.85 and Diesel at Ksh207.06 for the coming month.
