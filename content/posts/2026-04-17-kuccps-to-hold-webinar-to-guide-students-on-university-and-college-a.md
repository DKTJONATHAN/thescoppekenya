The Kenya Universities and Colleges Central Placement Service (KUCCPS) will host a comprehensive webinar on Saturday, April 18, 2026, at 10am. The virtual session aims to guide Form Four leavers, parents, and guardians through the current 2026 university and college application process, simplifying the journey for thousands selecting courses.

KUCCPS Application Guidance Webinar

Webinar Objectives and Guidance The virtual forum comes as thousands of students navigate the KUCCPS application portal, which is currently open for submissions. Designed to streamline the application process, the webinar will offer step-by-step instructions for applying to both university degree programmes and Technical and Vocational Education and Training (TVET) courses. This comprehensive guidance is particularly beneficial for first-time applicants. Participants will gain crucial insights into selecting appropriate courses based on their Kenya Certificate of Secondary Education (KCSE) grades. KUCCPS emphasizes that aligning course choices with individual academic strengths is paramount to enhancing placement chances. The session will also address the importance of using previous cut-off points as a reference, given that current-year cut-offs are not yet available, and understanding cluster subject requirements for preferred courses.

Interactive Session Details KUCCPS Chief Executive Officer Dr. Agnes Mercy Wahome will facilitate the session, explaining the application process in detail and addressing common applicant concerns. The webinar will include a live question-and-answer segment, providing attendees an opportunity to seek clarifications on any unclear areas. The session is scheduled for Saturday, April 18, 2026, at 10am. It will be accessible via Zoom using ID 865 9036 0480 and passcode 703608, with live streams also available on YouTube, Facebook, and X.

Timelines and Recommendations With the application window expected to close in early May 2026, KUCCPS strongly encourages all eligible candidates to participate in the webinar. This proactive engagement is crucial for avoiding application errors and maximizing opportunities for placement.
