Health Cabinet Secretary Aden Duale stated on Sunday, April 19, that former President Uhuru Kenyatta lacked the parliamentary support required to impeach William Ruto during his 2017-2022 deputy presidential term, directly responding to recent remarks by former Deputy President Rigathi Gachagua regarding their past political relationship. Duale’s assertions add new dimensions to the ongoing political discourse.

Duale's Impeachment Revelation Speaking in Nairobi, Duale confirmed that Uhuru undermined and humiliated President Ruto during his tenure as Deputy President and also directed similar treatment towards Gachagua between 2017 and 2022. Duale, who served as the leader of the majority in the National Assembly, asserted that former President Kenyatta intended to impeach Ruto but was unable to due to a lack of sufficient parliamentary numbers. "Let me make it very clear, Uhuru did not have the numbers to impeach William Ruto. I was the leader of the majority, and I was leading over 145 MPs, solid behind Ruto that made Uhuru not to impeach Ruto," Duale elaborated on Sunday.

Gachagua's Counter-Claims Duale's statements followed Gachagua's warning against criticizing Uhuru, with Gachagua emphasizing that Uhuru never orchestrated his impeachment despite their political differences. Gachagua alleged that President Ruto later betrayed him by facilitating parliamentary efforts to remove him from office, a tactic Uhuru reportedly never employed against Ruto. "President Uhuru Kenyatta never paid members of Parliament to impeach William Ruto as Deputy President," Gachagua stated during a church service in Murang'a on Sunday. Gachagua also claimed that since leaving office, President Ruto has engaged in a sustained campaign against him, including sending law enforcement and hired individuals to his home and disrupting his children's business ventures.

Historical Context and Current Dynamics The relationship between Uhuru Kenyatta and William Ruto significantly deteriorated in 2018 following the handshake between Uhuru and the late former Prime Minister Raila Odinga. This shift sidelined Ruto from many government decisions, leading to widespread speculation about a potential impeachment, which ultimately did not materialize. Duale now attributes this to the considerable number of Members of Parliament loyal to Ruto, exceeding Uhuru’s parliamentary influence at the time. In a parallel situation, after President Ruto assumed office and Gachagua became his deputy, their relationship reportedly worsened after public protests, leading to Gachagua's impeachment. Duale attributes this impeachment to Gachagua's focus on ethnic politics, noting that even Gachagua's own MP and women's representative voted in favour of his removal. Gachagua, during his tenure as deputy, also pointed out that Uhuru allowed Ruto extensive freedom to travel across the country, including frequent visits to the Mount Kenya region, without obstruction, contrasting this with his own perceived restrictions on holding rallies.
