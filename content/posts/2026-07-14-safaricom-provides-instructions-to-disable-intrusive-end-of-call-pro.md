## Safaricom provides instructions to disable intrusive end-of-call promotional pop-ups

### A screen-hogging inconvenience
For months, mobile users have endured a barrage of unwanted service prompts and marketing messages that appear the moment a call ends. These flash notifications often require a user to interact with the screen just to clear them. Many customers view this design choice as a predatory tactic that encourages accidental subscriptions to costly premium services. This specific user experience issue has reached a breaking point, as many subscribers feel their devices are being used to serve advertisements against their will.

### Reclaiming your mobile space
The frustration stems from the lack of transparency regarding how these services are enabled, as many customers report being opted into promotional content without providing clear consent. To disable these notifications, users must navigate to the SIM toolkit menu located within their phone settings. From there, users should select the Safaricom menu and locate the flash or alert settings. By choosing the deactivate option within this sub-menu, customers can permanently stop the intrusive pop-ups from appearing after their calls.

### Corporate silence meets user outrage
The silence from the service provider regarding this issue has historically deepened resentment among the subscriber base. Users frequently expressed that their mobile screens were being held hostage for marketing purposes. By forcing these pop-ups into every interaction, the network risked alienating a loyal base that expects a clean user experience. Moving forward, providing clear, accessible instructions for deactivating these services is seen as a necessary step for the company to maintain customer trust and improve overall satisfaction with its network services.
