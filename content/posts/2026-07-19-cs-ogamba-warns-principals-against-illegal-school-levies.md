## Why Your Child’s School Fees Feel Like A Moving Target CS Ogamba Warns Principals Against Illegal School Levies is the central subject of the update.

Education Cabinet Secretary Julius Ogamba has officially declared war on the hidden financial burdens placed on Kenyan households by school administrations. Speaking at Mbiriri Comprehensive School in Nyeri, the ministry chief signaled that the days of unchecked fee hikes are over, linking these extra charges directly to the current wave of student frustration and property destruction in institutions like Nyabisase Mixed Secondary.

### Which extra school charges are actually illegal?

Parents across the country are well acquainted with the sudden emergence of "project fees" or "extra tuition levies" that arrive via text message or handwritten notes in student bags. Officially, the Ministry of Education mandates that school fees must align with government guidelines. Anything outside this structure is technically unauthorized.

Common illegal levies often disguised as legitimate expenses include charges for school infrastructure development, extra instructional materials, or even supplementary teacher allowances that have not been vetted by the ministry or school boards of management. When a school principal demands payment for "boarding facilities improvement" or "teacher motivation funds" without a signed, verified approval from the ministry, that is a red flag. These costs often balloon to thousands of shillings per term, effectively pricing lower-income families out of quality education while providing no clear accountability on how the money is spent.

### How do these charges trigger student unrest?

There is a direct psychological link between how a school manages its resources and how students behave. When parents are squeezed for funds that never seem to translate into improved services, the students are often the first to notice. A student who sees their parents struggling to pay an illegal levy for a laboratory that remains empty, or for cafeteria improvements that never materialize, naturally feels cheated.

This environment breeds resentment. When learners feel their education is being commodified and mismanaged, the resulting frustration often manifests in destructive behavior. The recent incidents in Kisii and other regions underscore a deeper malaise. When administrators focus on extracting funds rather than maintaining open communication with the student body, the school environment becomes volatile. Instead of fostering an academic atmosphere, the institution becomes a pressure cooker where minor grievances escalate into arson and vandalism.

### How can parents push back against unauthorized fees?

If a school insists on payments that do not appear on the official government-issued fee structure, parents have a clear path to seek recourse. The first step is to demand a copy of the approval letter from the Ministry of Education that sanctions the specific levy. If the administration cannot produce this documentation, the demand is illegitimate.

Parents should then document the request, keeping screenshots of messages or physical receipts of demand letters. This information can be reported directly to the County Director of Education. These offices are tasked with investigating fee structures and curbing administrative overreach. By organizing through Parent Teacher Associations (PTAs), guardians can create a collective voice to challenge these demands. Strength in numbers ensures that individual parents are not targeted or victimized for questioning the status quo.

As the country watches the tension between administration and household budgets unfold, one thing is certain: education is a right protected by government policy, not a blank check for school administrators.

An educated child is a right, not a profit motive for school administration.
