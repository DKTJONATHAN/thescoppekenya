Nyaribari Chache MP Zaheer Jhanda is openly defending his decision to double cash handouts to constituents, increasing them from KSh500 to KSh1,000. The move comes after videos of the distribution circulated widely online, sparking both public approval from residents and criticism from political observers and civil society.

Increased Handouts Spark Debate MP Jhanda has publicly addressed the viral videos, stating that his practice of distributing cash to constituents predates his election. He noted he began giving KSh500 to individuals as a "personal investment" in the community in 2022, prior to securing his parliamentary seat. Upon his election, Jhanda stated the increase to KSh1,000 per person was inevitable, further urging constituents to demand similar amounts from rival politicians seeking support in Nyaribari Chache. He maintains the funds are sourced from his personal resources, including his MP salary, business earnings, and contributions from friends who support his work. Jhanda also reiterated a commitment, made before his election, to dedicate half of his parliamentary salary to the people of Nyaribari Chache, with the remainder supporting his family and himself.

Political Strategy and Public Concern The distribution of cash handouts has shifted public discussion from personal generosity to a perceived political strategy. Critics argue that Jhanda is not merely rewarding loyalty but actively using financial incentives to solidify his political base ahead of the 2027 elections. Civil society organisations and some constituents have expressed concerns that these distributions undermine the dignity of citizens and could be seen as early voter bribery. Such practices are scrutinized for their long-term impact on democratic processes, especially in a climate of deepening fiscal strain and eroding purchasing power for many [The Kenyan Wallstreet: A Precarious Rally Amidst Deepening Fiscal Strain and Eroding Purchasing Power].

Eyeing the 2027 Elections Despite the backlash, the lawmaker remains unapologetic. He refutes claims that his current actions constitute an early campaign for his 2027 re-election bid. Jhanda has officially announced that his re-election campaign launch is scheduled for May 1, 2027.
