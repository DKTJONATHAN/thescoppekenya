The Siaya County Assembly has put its top executive officials under a glaring spotlight, demanding answers for a catalogue of stalled development projects. With only two months left in the 2025/2026 financial year, the dramatic summons carries a stern warning: deliver or face the political music, as public frustration reaches a fever pitch across the county.

Siaya's Political Soap Opera: Executive Officials on the Hot Seat During a charged plenary session, Members of County Assembly (MCAs) did not mince words, pointing to what they termed a "systemic failure" in project implementation. A significant number of projects, already approved under the current budget, are allegedly languishing, having neither been procured nor executed. This comes after a December 2025 consultative meeting had seen the executive commit to tendering all documents by the close of January 2026, a deadline that has reportedly been missed.

The Unmet Deadlines and Public Outcry Central Gem MCA Silas Mandingu, who tabled the motion that brought this high-stakes showdown into the public arena, criticised the executive's response to status requests, describing it as "inadequate" and devoid of clear timelines or accountability measures. Mandingu's push has placed County Executive Committee (CEC) members, chief officers, and procurement directors squarely in the Assembly's crosshairs, demanding their appearance to explain the persistent delays.

Voter Fury and Abandoned Sites The political heat is certainly on, with several MCAs highlighting how the stalled projects are impacting their standing with constituents. South East Alego MCA Scholastica Madowo articulated the widespread sentiment, noting that residents are blaming elected leaders for the implementation failures, despite the MCAs' primary role being oversight. Compounding the issue, South Sakwa MCA Eunice Ndolo brought to light concerning instances where contractors have abandoned sites, including a critical Early Childhood Development (ECD) project, reportedly due to non-payment. These payment delays for contractors could be further exacerbated by wider economic pressures, such as the Strait of Hormuz tensions, which have raised concerns over fuel and shipping costs in Kenya, potentially impacting project budgets and viability for local businesses.

Infrastructure Woes and Water Project Mystery The saga deepens with concerns over sluggish infrastructure development. East Gem MCA Seth Baraka starkly contrasted Siaya's stalled road works with the visible progress in neighboring counties, underscoring the deteriorating state of local infrastructure. In West Uyoma, MCA Justus K'oguta cited a particularly perplexing case: a Sh47 million water project that was publicly announced but has bafflingly not even reached the procurement stage.

The Constitutional Threat The gravity of the situation prompted North Sakwa MCA Oliver Arika to call for the Assembly to consider invoking Article 181 of the Constitution of Kenya. This constitutional provision outlines grounds for the removal of county officials, with non-performance being a significant basis. Speaker George Okode has since directed the executive to submit a comprehensive report addressing all the raised concerns before any further action is taken. In a move towards greater transparency, the Assembly also resolved to open upcoming deliberations on this matter to the public, civil society, and the media, ensuring all eyes are on Siaya's unfolding political drama.
