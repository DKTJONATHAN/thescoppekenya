India has donated a 1.5 Tesla Magnetic Resonance Imaging (MRI) machine to the Kenya Defence Forces, strengthening military healthcare services. The agreement, formalized recently in Nairobi, enhances diagnostic capacity for KDF personnel, with the machine destined for Kenya Navy Hospital in Mtongwe.

## Enhanced Military Healthcare Capabilities

### Strategic Donation Bolsters KDF Medical Readiness
Defence Principal Secretary Patrick Mariru confirmed the donation of the 1.5 Tesla MRI machine by India, emphasizing its role in strengthening military healthcare services and defence cooperation. The formalization of this agreement took place at the Defence Headquarters in Nairobi, witnessed by PS Mariru and Indian High Commissioner to Kenya, Adarsh Swaika.

“This marks a significant step in strengthening our military medical capability and deepening defence cooperation between Kenya and India,” Mariru stated. The MRI machine's installation at the Kenya Navy Hospital in Mtongwe, Mombasa, is set to boost diagnostic capacity for KDF personnel. This will enable timely and accurate diagnoses, improving access to specialized medical care for service members.

### Deepening Defence Ties
The current initiative is expected to bolster overall medical readiness and resilience as military exercises commence. Mariru described the donation as "not merely an equipment transfer but a strategic investment in the health, resilience, and operational readiness of our servicemen and women."

The partnership underscores growing defence ties between Kenya and India, particularly in areas of technical and institutional support. The signing ceremony saw attendance from senior military officials from both countries, including Indian Navy’s Flag Officer Commanding-in-Chief Krishna Swaminathan, alongside senior KDF officers such as Major Generals Edward Rugendo and Frederick Leuria.

Further demonstrating this robust cooperation, the Indian guided-missile frigate, INS Trikand, docked at the Port of Mombasa on Tuesday for a four-day port call. The Indian Embassy noted that INS Trikand, a frontline guided-missile frigate of the Indian Navy, is on an operational deployment to strengthen maritime ties with African nations, aligning with India's MAHASAGAR vision.
