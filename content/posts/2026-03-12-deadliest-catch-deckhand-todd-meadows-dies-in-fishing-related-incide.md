Deadliest Catch Mourns Young Deckhand Todd Meadows The *Deadliest Catch* community is saddened by the passing of 25-year-old deckhand Todd Meadows on February 25, 2026. His death, a fishing-related incident during filming, left Captain Rick Shelford and the *Aleutian Lady* crew heartbroken. Meadows, a father, quickly earned respect for his work ethic and smile.

A Devastating Loss at Sea Captain Rick Shelford of the *Aleutian Lady* shared the devastating news of Todd Meadows' passing via Facebook, calling February 25, 2026, "the most tragic day in the history of the Aleutian Lady on the Bering Sea". Meadows, though new to the crew, quickly became like family, endearing himself with his strong work ethic and infectious laughter that resonated through the wheelhouse. Captain Shelford expressed profound sorrow, highlighting Meadows' deep love for his children and family, and requested prayers for them during this incredibly difficult time. A GoFundMe has been established to support Meadows' three young sons and family.

Discovery Channel's Heartfelt Condolences Discovery Channel also confirmed the tragic loss, describing it as "devastating". A spokesperson for the network extended heartfelt condolences to Meadows' loved ones, his crewmates, and the wider fishing community. While specific details surrounding Meadows' cause of death were not released by Shelford or Discovery, reports indicate he died in a fishing-related incident during filming. The U.S. Coast Guard reported that Meadows fell overboard about 170 miles north of Dutch Harbor, Alaska, and was recovered unresponsive ten minutes later. Efforts to resuscitate him were unsuccessful.

The Bering Sea's Continuing Toll Meadows' death adds to the grim history of the Emmy-winning series, which showcases the perilous world of crab fishing in the Bering Sea. In 2024, deckhand Nick Mavar, who appeared in 17 seasons, passed away at age 59 in Alaska following a medical emergency. The series also lost deckhands Nick McGlashan and Mahlon Reyes in 2020. *Deadliest Catch* celebrated its 20th anniversary in 2025 and premiered season 21 in August 2025, continuing its run on Discovery Channel and streaming on Discovery+.
