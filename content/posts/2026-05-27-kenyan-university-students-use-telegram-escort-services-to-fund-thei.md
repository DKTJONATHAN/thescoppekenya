### The irony of the lazy youth narrative
While government officials and the older generation frequently complain that the youth are simply lazy and have refused to work, the irony of this statement is glaring. These students have not rejected labor; they have simply found that the formal job market is a vacuum. With no internships or entry level positions available, encrypted applications have become the new marketplace for survival. This trend is not a result of moral decay, but rather a calculated response to an economy that has left the youth with no other way to secure their next meal.

### Education meets economic desperation
Obtaining a university degree in 2026 has become a luxury that many can no longer afford through traditional means. Between skyrocketing tuition fees and the recent increase in public transportation fares, the financial requirements for an average student are impossible to meet. Scholars who should be focusing on their studies are instead managing digital profiles to keep their school portals active. They are forced to balance the pressure of upcoming exams with the demands of a hustle where the degree they seek might eventually pay less than the work currently funding it.

### Systemic failure and the moralist critique
The public commentary surrounding this trend often misses the point by focusing on individual morality rather than systemic collapse. Critics are quick to judge the character of these students, yet they offer no solutions for the skyrocketing cost of living. It is easy to demand dignity when your own bills are paid and your stomach is full. For a student watching their peers drop out due to a lack of funds, these services are seen as a necessary survival tactic in a country where the doors to traditional employment remain firmly locked.
