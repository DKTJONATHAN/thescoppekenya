Kenyan agricultural exports will gain duty-free access to China starting Friday, May 1, 2026. This significant development was confirmed by the Ministry of Agriculture today, Monday, March 16, 2026, following a crucial meeting between Cabinet Secretary Mutahi Kagwe and Chinese Ambassador Guo Haiyan.

Expanded Market Access for Agricultural Produce Farmers cultivating tea, coffee, fresh and frozen avocados, macadamia nuts, flowers, fresh horticultural produce, vegetables, and herbs are projected to be the primary beneficiaries of this new trade agreement. The Ministry of Agriculture stated that the move marks the implementation stage of trade agreements secured during President William Ruto’s state visit to China in 2025.

Implementation of Presidential Agreements Cabinet Secretary Mutahi Kagwe highlighted that this initiative actualizes agreements championed by President William Ruto during his visit to China last year, at the invitation of President Xi Jinping. This opens a substantial opportunity for exporters to access one of the world's largest consumer markets. While China’s broader duty-free policy for African goods traditionally favored least developed countries, initially excluding developing nations such as Kenya, Kenya initiated discussions to secure comparable trade privileges.

Removal of Significant Tariffs Under the new agreement, Kenyan agricultural products entering the Chinese market will no longer incur import taxes or tariffs, which previously inflated costs and diminished competitiveness. Prior to this, tea and coffee products faced tariffs generally ranging between 6 and 15 percent. Nuts, including macadamia, attracted tariffs of approximately 10 to 15 percent. Fresh horticultural produce and vegetables often incurred tariffs between 10 and 25 percent, while cut flowers had tariffs of about 4 percent.

FOCAC Framework and Future Cooperation Ambassador Guo Haiyan reaffirmed China's commitment to strengthening agricultural cooperation with Kenya within the framework of the Forum on China-Africa Cooperation (FOCAC). This framework will support enhanced market access for products, bolster agricultural value chains, and improve technical cooperation and capacity building. Cabinet Secretary Kagwe encouraged businesspeople to collaborate with Chinese companies to establish agro-processing industries locally, specifically for exports destined for China.
