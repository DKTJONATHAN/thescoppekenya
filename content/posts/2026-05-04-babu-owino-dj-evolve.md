Embakasi East MP Babu Owino has made a startling admission this Monday, May 04, 2026, stating that he belongs in jail under a strictly retributive legal framework. His comments revolve around the high-profile **Babu Owino DJ Evolve** case, where he instead championed restorative justice, revealing substantial financial contributions towards the ongoing recovery of the popular entertainer.

## Babu Owino DJ Evolve: A Call for Restorative Justice

Embakasi East MP Babu Owino recently put the nation's legal pundits and entertainment enthusiasts in a spin with his candid reflections on the highly publicised **Babu Owino DJ Evolve** saga. Speaking forthrightly, the legislator posited that under a strictly punitive legal framework, he would undeniably face imprisonment. However, he swiftly pivoted to advocate for restorative justice, a path that focuses on rehabilitation, reconciliation, and addressing the harm caused, rather than solely punishment. This perspective adds a fresh layer to the protracted discussions surrounding the incident.

### The Weight of Retribution vs. Restoration
Owino's bold admission signals a fascinating shift in the public discourse surrounding justice, especially from a political figure. Rather than maintaining a defiant stance, his openness about the potential for retributive consequences, yet his insistence on a more healing-oriented approach, is sparking robust debate across various platforms. The move is viewed by some as an effort to reshape the narrative, focusing on future well-being and reconciliation over historical blame.

### The Sh70 Million Tab and Beyond
Perhaps the most eye-opening revelation from the MP concerned the staggering financial commitment he has made towards DJ Evolve's recovery. Owino disclosed that he has already disbursed approximately Sh70 million for the entertainer's ongoing medical care and accumulated debts. This substantial figure underscores the profound and long-term impact of the incident, requiring a financial outlay that dwarfs many individual annual incomes in Kenya. The sum is a stark reminder of the financial burdens associated with complex medical cases, sometimes echoing the massive financial outlays seen in other sectors, such as when [Kenya Power incurs KSh 12.8 billion in annual costs as system losses exceed regulatory targets](https://zandani.co.ke/article/kenya-power-incurs-ksh-12-8-billion-in-annual-costs-as-system-losses). This ongoing support is integral to the restorative path Owino champions.

### What’s Next for the Case?
As the calendar rolls into May 2026, the case involving Babu Owino and DJ Evolve continues to hold public fascination, not least because of these new admissions. The MP’s advocacy for restorative justice, backed by his significant financial contribution, presents a complex ethical and legal quandary. Will this approach gain more traction within the Kenyan legal system, or will public sentiment demand a more conventional retributive outcome? Only time will tell how this high-profile case, now punctuated by a very public confession and an appeal for healing, will ultimately unfold in the annals of Kenyan celebrity and legal history.
