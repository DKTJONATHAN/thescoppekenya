## Energy CS Addresses Fuel Supply Amidst Middle East Tensions Cabinet Secretary for Energy and Petroleum, Opiyo Wandayi, today, March 10, 2026, convened an emergency meeting with oil marketers. This follows earlier discussions with government-to-government (G-2-G) oil suppliers, addressing mounting concerns over Kenya's fuel supply stability amidst escalating Middle East conflicts.

Urgent Consultations Underway CS Wandayi held discussions with oil marketers on Tuesday, March 10, shortly after concluding talks with companies supplying oil under the G-2-G agreement. During the official listing of the Kenya Pipeline on the Nairobi Stock Exchange (NSE) on the same day, Wandayi dismissed concerns of an impending fuel shortage. He affirmed that the government is actively engaging oil-producing nations with G-G deals, including Saudi Arabia's ARAMCO, to ensure sufficient oil reserves through strategic contingency planning.

Geopolitical Impact on Supply Security The emergency meetings are a direct response to the critical situation in the Middle East, specifically Iran's declared closure of the Strait of Hormuz. This vital maritime passage accounts for 21 percent of the global oil supply, and its potential closure due to regional conflict poses a significant threat to international energy markets. Wandayi confirmed close engagement with government-to-government suppliers, including Saudi Aramco, ADNOC, and ENOC, on contingency measures. He assured the public that there is "no cause for alarm" and that Kenya possesses "security of supply" in the short to medium term, with continuous monitoring of the situation.

Local Repercussions and Price Anticipation The developments occur as Kenya closely monitors a Red Sea tanker transporting crucial fuel from the Middle East, vital for the nation's economy. Neighboring South Africa is also preparing for increased pump prices, reflecting broader supply shock fears across Africa due to the US-led conflict with Iran. The Energy and Petroleum Regulatory Authority (EPRA) is scheduled to announce new pump prices on Saturday, March 14, amidst ongoing public apprehension regarding fuel availability despite official reassurances. Despite these immediate energy concerns, Kenya's long-term economic outlook remains a focus, with ongoing efforts to support local innovation, mirroring broader positive trends seen across the continent where [African Startups See $4.18M Boost [CcHUB Report]](https://zandani.co.ke/article/african-startups-see-4-18m-boost-cchub-report) reported earlier this year.
