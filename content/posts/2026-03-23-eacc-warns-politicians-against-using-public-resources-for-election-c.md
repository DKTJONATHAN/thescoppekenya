The Ethics and Anti-Corruption Commission (EACC) today warned political aspirants against utilizing public resources and state offices for campaigns ahead of the 2027 General Election. Speaking during a training forum in Nakuru, EACC Chairperson David Oginde emphasized the need for ethical conduct as political activity intensifies across the nation.

EACC Regulates Campaign Conduct

Misuse of Public Resources Prohibited EACC Chairperson David Oginde, during a training forum held in Nakuru, explicitly cautioned both incumbent leaders and prospective officeholders against misusing taxpayer-funded resources for political gain. He reiterated that public resources and offices are strictly prohibited for campaign purposes, signaling an early push to regulate conduct before the official election period begins.

Addressing Corruption as a National Challenge EACC Chief Executive Officer Abdi Mohamud highlighted corruption as a critical national challenge, ranking it second only to youth unemployment among the country's most serious issues. Mohamud advocated for a collaborative, multi-agency approach in tackling the problem, stressing that no single institution can effectively address it alone. He urged stakeholders to collaborate in developing practical and sustainable solutions to curb corruption, a concern that continues to feature prominently in public discourse, much like the focus on public figures and their engagements.

Call for Ethical Campaigning and Accountability Tharaka Nithi Member of Parliament George Murugara urged politicians to prioritize service delivery instead of engaging in premature campaigns. Murugara cautioned leaders against hate speech and incitement, noting that such actions are prohibited under the law and undermine national cohesion. He called on law enforcement agencies to remain vigilant and ensure that those who violate laws are held accountable as political activity gathers momentum ahead of the 2027 polls.
