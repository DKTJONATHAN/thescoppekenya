Veteran Nigerian rapper and activist Eedris Abdulkareem recently intensified his criticism of President Bola Tinubu's handling of the Jos attacks, publicly labeling him "the worst president." Speaking at an event in Edo State, Abdulkareem condemned the President's airport visit to victims, sparking widespread discussion across the continent.

Rapper Eedris Abdulkareem Blasts President Tinubu Eedris Abdulkareem, known for his politically charged music, did not hold back in his recent address. "Tinubu is the worst president. Anyone supporting APC, may Nigeria happen to you," he declared, before directly criticizing the President's visit to Plateau State following recent massacres. Abdulkareem stated, "Commander-in-Chief, wey get all the security go Jos no fit enter town to go console those wey die from terrorist wey him and him government arrange us. Tinubu na biggest fraud. And anybody wey support am, may Nigeria happen to them." The crowd responded with resounding shouts of "amen." His pointed remarks, delivered to an applauding crowd, cut through the typical entertainment chatter that often surrounds cultural events like "Mummy 4: Fraser & Weisz Return, a Millennial Delusion", underscoring the rapper's long-standing commitment to political commentary.

A History of Dissent and Unwavering Criticism Eedris, widely regarded as a pioneer of Nigerian hip-hop, has a long history of using his platform to challenge leadership. He famously took aim at government failures with 'Jaga Jaga,' a track released over two decades ago. That he continues to voice similar arguments today resonates strongly with many. His continued relevance in national discourse highlights the vital role artists play in public life, a dynamic often shaped by the industry itself, as examined in "Nollywood's Limiting Exhibition Model: Crushing Dreams and Stifling Economic Growth - The Untold Gossip!"

The Jos Attacks and Presidential Response Abdulkareem's comments come amidst a recent wider controversy following severe attacks in Plateau State. On March 29 of this year, gunmen attacked a community in Jos, killing approximately 28 people. In a separate incident, more than 50 individuals lost their lives when assailants invaded Kimakpa village in Bassa Local Government. President Tinubu visited Plateau State shortly after the incidents but addressed victims at the airport rather than traveling to the directly affected communities. He cited a tight ten-minute window before the airport's lack of electricity would prevent departure. The Presidency later explained that the visit was arranged overnight following a security briefing, and that a high-level bilateral meeting with Chadian President Mahamat Idriss Déby Itno had extended longer than anticipated, delaying the trip. Despite this explanation, criticism has continued to mount. Former Vice President Atiku Abubakar was among those who publicly faulted the visit, stating that the President offered neither comfort to families in hospitals nor any clear policy direction on preventing further attacks. For Eedris Abdulkareem, the image of a sitting president addressing grieving victims from an airport tarmac before departing spoke volumes, rendering further context irrelevant.
