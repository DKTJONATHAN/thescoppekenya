## Securing Your iTax Profile Against Recycled Digital Identities

Can you prove you are the real owner of your tax profile if your registered phone number now belongs to a complete stranger? With the June 30 filing deadline looming, thousands of Kenyans are finding their iTax accounts locked because their OTPs are going to recycled SIM cards, creating a major security blind spot for taxpayers.

In Kenya, mobile numbers are recycled by telecommunications providers after a period of inactivity. If a taxpayer stops using a SIM card without updating their KRA records, that number eventually finds its way into someone else's hands. When that happens, the new owner could theoretically gain access to sensitive tax data or prevent the original owner from filing their returns. This creates a hidden liability where your digital identity is essentially untethered from your actual control.

### Why does a recycled number stop me from filing?

The iTax system relies on One Time Passwords (OTPs) sent to the mobile number on file to verify the user. If that number has been reassigned, the security mechanism that is meant to protect your tax history becomes a gatekeeper that locks you out. 

To resolve this, KRA has moved away from purely manual office visits. Taxpayers who can no longer access their old phone number need to visit the official KRA portal to initiate an update. This process often involves submitting identification documents to prove ownership of the PIN, effectively performing a digital audit of your account. By verifying your identity now, you ensure that your tax obligations remain yours alone and do not become a vulnerability that an unknown third party can exploit.

### How can I bypass the login hurdle?

For those struggling with Za Ndani portal login, KRA has integrated the Shuru chatbot, reachable via WhatsApp at +254 711 099 999. This tool is designed to help users generate eTIMS invoices and navigate the initial steps of filing returns without getting stuck in the traditional browser login loop. 

While the chatbot is efficient for basic inquiries and eTIMS generation, major profile changes, such as updating a registered phone number, require a more formal update through the iTax system. If Za Ndani login fails, taxpayers should not wait for the final week of June to seek help. Visiting an official KRA service center with a national ID card remains the most secure way to rebind your identity to your tax profile once a digital disconnect has occurred.

### Why it matters now

Tax compliance is no longer just about calculating your income; it is about protecting your digital footprint. As business transparency laws tighten, similar to the scrutiny currently surrounding [minority shareholder rights at WPP Scangroup](https://zandani.co.ke/article/minority-shareholders-push-for-wpp-scangroup-board-removal), maintaining control over your tax data is essential for individual financial security. If you leave your old phone number linked to your PIN, you are leaving the door open for potential data breaches or administrative nightmares that could lead to heavy penalties for non-filing.

### What to watch next

The current situation highlights a growing friction between mobile data recycling and state administrative security. Watch for further KRA updates regarding biometric verification or deeper integration with the eCitizen platform, which would aim to unify identity across government services. For now, prioritize checking your contact details on the iTax portal today. Do not assume your digital identity is safe simply because you have not changed your habits.

Your tax profile is your digital footprint, so ensure only you have the keys to unlock it.
