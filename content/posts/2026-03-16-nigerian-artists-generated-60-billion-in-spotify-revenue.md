Nigerian artists collectively generated over ₦60 billion in Spotify revenue in 2025, according to insights from the platform's annual Loud & Clear report. This significant financial milestone highlights the rapidly expanding music ecosystem and growing global appeal of Nigerian sounds across the continent and beyond.

Nigerian Music's Financial Ascent Spotify's 2025 Loud & Clear report reveals that revenue for Nigerian artists from the streaming giant alone grew by more than 140% over the last two years. The figures underscore both the increasing global appeal of Nigerian music and the expanding economic opportunities for artists across the market. This financial growth is a key indicator of the industry's health, creating a more robust environment for creators. For some artists, navigating these financial opportunities and challenges is part of their journey, as exemplified by cases such as the Nigerian singer Chike, who recently faced accusations of fraud over an alleged business transaction.

Expanding Reach and Engagement The report details the substantial listener engagement with Nigerian music on Spotify. In 2025, Nigerian artists amassed 30.3 billion streams and 1.6 billion listening hours on the platform. First-time listeners discovered Nigerian artists over 1.3 billion times, marking a 26% increase compared to 2024. This expanding reach demonstrates the genre's ability to captivate new audiences worldwide, highlighting the diverse pathways artists are taking to build sustainable careers. The journey of artists like Toxic Lyrikali, transitioning from local beginnings to wider recognition, illustrates the potential for growth and discovery within this vibrant musical landscape.

Local Impact and Artist Growth At home, Nigerian music maintains a powerful share of listening. In 2025, Nigerian artists contributed over 80% of the tracks featured on Spotify Nigeria's Daily Top 50, showcasing the strength of local talent in shaping the country's listening habits. Local consumption of Nigerian artists also grew by 170% year-on-year on Spotify. The data further indicates significant growth across key artist segments, with local streams of Nigerian female artists increasing by 55% year-on-year and local streams of Nigerian indie artists growing by 75% year-on-year. Independent artists and labels were responsible for approximately 58% of all royalties generated by Nigerian artists on Spotify in 2025, underscoring their vital role in the country's music narrative.

Independent Voices and Genre Diversity Spotify's editorial ecosystem continues to support artist discovery and amplification. In 2025, nearly 2,000 Nigerian artists were added to editorial playlists on Spotify. Beyond editorial support, Nigerian music resonated strongly with listeners globally, with Nigerian artists featured in almost 320 million user playlists worldwide and over 12 million user playlists in Nigeria. The platform saw more than 60 million user playlists featuring Nigerian artists created in 2025 alone. The latest data also reflects a diverse listening culture in Nigeria, with pop urbaine, alternative pop, anime, emo, and drill showing the highest growth over the last five years, signaling an increasingly varied and evolving music landscape.
