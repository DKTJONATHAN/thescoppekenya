## Weather Wins: Zach Bryan Cancels First Show Ever Country sensation Zach Bryan faced an unexpected challenge on his "With Heaven on Tour" when extreme weather forced the cancellation of his Tulsa concert. This marks the Grammy-winning artist's first headlining show called off in five years, despite his strong desire to perform. He has had quite a 2026 already, though!

The Tulsa Tempest Zach Bryan's Friday concert in Tulsa, Oklahoma, at H.A. Chapman Stadium was abruptly canceled due to severe weather conditions. The country singer-songwriter expressed his frustration on Instagram, stating he was "forced" to call off the show. He revealed this was his first-ever canceled headlining show in five years of touring, noting he had "pissed off plenty people this morning trying to ‘play anyways'". The decision was prompted by predicted wind gusts exceeding 60 mph, hail, and intense lightning, leaving the Oklahoma native with "no choice in the matter".

A Year of Triumphs Despite the recent tour snag, 2026 has been a monumental year for the 30-year-old artist. Kicking off with personal joy, Bryan married his girlfriend, Samantha Leonard, on New Year's Eve at Basílica de Santa María del Coro in San Sebastián, Spain. Professionally, he released his sixth album, "With Heaven on Top," just days later on January 9, 2026, which quickly debuted at No. 1 on the Billboard 200 chart. His "With Heaven on Tour," named after the successful album, commenced on March 6 in St. Louis, featuring Kings of Leon as an opening act for several dates. The extensive 39-date tour will travel across the U.S. and Europe before concluding on October 10 in Auburn, Alabama.
