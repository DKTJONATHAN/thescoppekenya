By Celestine Nzioka Motorists across Kenya are facing unprecedented fuel shortages and escalating prices, a direct consequence of ongoing Middle East conflicts disrupting global oil supply chains. Adapting to this new reality, drivers are now urged to adopt proactive measures to conserve fuel and maximize efficiency from every litre available. Beyond personal efforts in resource management, ongoing national conversations continue to address broader societal challenges, including the scrutiny of systemic failures.

Maximizing Fuel Efficiency in 2026 Amid global oil supply disruptions caused by the ongoing Middle East conflict, which has led to a significant plunge in global oil supply and soaring prices, individuals must prioritize fuel conservation. The International Energy Agency characterized the situation as the "largest supply disruption in the history of the global oil market" as of March 2026. Kenya, like many nations, is feeling the impact despite government assurances of sufficient petroleum supplies until April 2026 through strategic imports and a stabilization fund.

Prioritize Vehicle Maintenance A well-maintained vehicle is inherently more fuel-efficient. Regular servicing, timely oil changes, and clean air filters ensure optimal engine performance. Neglecting these fundamental aspects can lead to increased fuel consumption and potential costly repairs.

Reduce Vehicle Weight Unnecessary items stored in a vehicle's trunk or back seat add weight, forcing the engine to work harder. Removing these items, even small reductions, contributes to improved fuel efficiency over time.

Minimize Engine Idling Extended engine idling, such as while stalled in traffic or waiting, wastes fuel. Drivers are advised to switch off their engines if stopped for more than ten seconds, as modern vehicle designs accommodate frequent restarts efficiently.

Adopt Smooth Driving Habits Aggressive driving, characterized by rapid acceleration and abrupt braking, significantly increases fuel consumption. Gentle acceleration and maintaining a steady speed not only conserve fuel but also reduce overall wear and tear on the vehicle.

Optimize Vehicle Aerodynamics Open windows, external roof racks, and bulky items positioned atop a vehicle create increased wind resistance. At higher speeds, this aerodynamic drag can substantially decrease fuel efficiency. It is advisable to keep windows closed on highways.

Use Accessories Judiciously Features such as air conditioning, heated seats, and charging devices place an additional load on the engine. While essential at times, judicious use of these accessories, particularly during shorter journeys, aids in fuel conservation.

Plan and Consolidate Journeys Frequent stop-and-go driving patterns are inefficient regarding fuel use. Drivers should endeavour to combine multiple errands into single trips and strategically avoid peak traffic hours. Effective route planning can reduce both travel time and fuel consumption.

Practice Anticipatory Driving Observing traffic ahead and anticipating movements allows for gradual deceleration when approaching traffic lights or congestion, thereby maintaining momentum and saving fuel. Maintaining a safe following distance also facilitates smoother driving.

Choose Quality Fuel Not all fuels are manufactured to the same standards. Utilizing high-quality fuel is recommended, as it can contribute to improved engine performance and overall efficiency.

Maintain Correct Tyre Pressure Both underinflated and overinflated tyres increase rolling resistance, diminishing a vehicle's fuel efficiency. Regular checks and maintaining recommended tyre pressure levels are crucial for both fuel economy and safety.
