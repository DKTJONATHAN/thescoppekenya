Vihiga Senator Godfrey Osotsi was violently assaulted inside a Kisumu hotel on Wednesday, April 8, 2026. Harrowing CCTV footage, now widely circulated, depicts multiple attackers pinning the senator to the floor of a small café, inflicting serious injuries before staff intervened.

Violent Assault Captured The four-minute CCTV clip, which has gained significant traction, vividly portrays Senator Osotsi, identifiable by his striped shirt, desperately attempting to shield himself against three to four individuals. These attackers are seen simultaneously slapping and kicking him repeatedly while he calls for assistance. Blood droplets are clearly visible on the white-tiled floor, underscoring the severity of the incident as the senator lay overwhelmed.

Senator Pinned and Attacked The footage reveals the attackers moving in a coordinated manner, effectively cutting off all potential escape routes and surrounding Senator Osotsi from multiple angles. One individual, dressed in dark clothing, is observed striking the senator directly, while another in white attire stands nearby, boxing him in as the assault progresses uninterrupted. During the confrontation, the Senator was dragged across the floor multiple times.

Staff Intervention and Medical Evacuation Four restaurant staff members, identifiable by their waiter uniforms, eventually rushed to intervene. They bravely positioned themselves between the senator and his assailants, attempting to de-escalate the violent situation. Their intervention quickly garnered the attention of other patrons, with a growing crowd joining the staff in shielding the senator and pushing back against the attackers. Following the assault, Senator Osotsi was immediately transported out of Kisumu and airlifted to Nairobi, where he is currently receiving medical attention. The full extent of his injuries remains unconfirmed.

Political Outcry and Calls for Justice The incident has sparked widespread condemnation across the nation, particularly among the political class. Siaya Governor James Orengo stated that such attacks are intended to silence the Linda Mwananchi rallies, in which Senator Osotsi is slated to play a critical role. Orengo commented, "This is a clearly coordinated and organised pattern of intimidation aimed at silencing the voices of the people, coming as a second direct hit after the violent assault at the Grand Royal Swiss Hotel on the eve of our Kakamega Rally." Embakasi East MP Babu Owino also decried the assault, attributing blame to the Ministry of Interior for what he termed "state violence" against Senator Osotsi. Similarly, Suna East MP Junet Mohamed condemned the attack, calling for the swift arrest of those responsible. "I condemn the dastardly assault on Vihiga County Senator Hon. Godfrey Osotsi in Kisumu. Those who planned the attack, alongside those who carried it out, must be quickly arrested and arraigned in a court of law," stated Junet. The calls for immediate legal action against perpetrators resonate with ongoing public discourse regarding judicial processes, including recent developments such as the Court of Appeal suspending a ruling on presidential advisors.
