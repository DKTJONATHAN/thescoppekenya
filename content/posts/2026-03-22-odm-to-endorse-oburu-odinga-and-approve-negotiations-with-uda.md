ODM to endorse Oburu Odinga and approve negotiations with UD is central to this update for Kenyan readers.

Orange Democratic Movement (ODM) will formalize Oburu Odinga's leadership and approve negotiations with President William Ruto's United Democratic Alliance (UDA) during a special delegates' convention on Friday, March 27, 2026. This pivotal decision, following Odinga's coronation today, marks a critical juncture for the party's future direction.

Key Agendas for Upcoming Convention ODM Deputy Chairperson Otiende Amollo outlined the two exclusive agendas for the special delegates' convention, emphasizing its focused mandate. Speaking today, Sunday, March 22, at Kang'o Kajaramogi during Oburu Odinga's coronation as his clan's spokesperson, Amollo confirmed that the convention would not entertain additional matters.

Oburu Odinga's Endorsement Delegates will formally endorse Oburu Odinga as the party leader. This leadership decision has already received unanimous approval from the National Executive Council (NEC), the Central Committee, the National Governing Council (NGC), and the Parliamentary Group. Amollo stated, "Oburu is our party leader, and this is not by mistake. We had already agreed on his leadership as NEC members even before we formally approached him. The only step remaining is the final one, which will be completed next week during the special delegates convention."

Negotiations with UDA Approved The second critical agenda item is the approval for ODM to initiate structured negotiations with President William Ruto and the United Democratic Alliance (UDA) party. Oburu Odinga will spearhead these discussions in his capacity as the newly endorsed party leader. Amollo, who also serves as the Rarieda MP, clarified that the March 27 gathering is a "special delegates convention" convened specifically for these two purposes, distinct from a national delegates convention (NDC).

Internal Dissension and Speculation Amollo cautioned party members against introducing alternative agendas, reinforcing that only the two stipulated items would be discussed. His statement addresses ongoing speculation within the party, including reports that a national delegates convention might consider creating a deputy party leader position for East African Legislative Assembly (EALA) MP Winnie Odinga, following a recent meeting with ODM delegates from Nairobi County. Additionally, rumors regarding Embakasi East MP Babu Owino's nomination for the Secretary General position to replace Nairobi Senator Edwin Sifuna, who was dismissed, have circulated. However, Owino reportedly rejected the offer, expressing interest only in the party leader role. Oburu Odinga has since dismissed these rumors as fake news. The ODM party continues to face internal wrangles, with the Linda Mwananchi faction vowing to boycott the special convention and questioning its legitimacy. This faction, reportedly led by Sifuna and Owino, plans to hold its own Linda Mwananchi rally in Nairobi on the same day as the ODM convention, which is expected to take place at Jamhuri grounds.
