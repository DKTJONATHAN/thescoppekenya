Prominent Rift Valley political figures, including Oscar Sudi and Kipchumba Murkomen, have recently offered public sympathy to Deputy President Rigathi Gachagua amid his ongoing impeachment proceedings. This alignment marks a significant departure from the historically strained relations between these leaders and the Mt. Kenya region.

## The Strategic Shift in Political Alliances

### A Tactical Performance of Solidarity
The sudden public backing from politicians who previously treated the Mt. Kenya region as a primary political rival appears to be a calculated performance. Rather than genuine empathy, this show of support is viewed as a strategic maneuver within the high-stakes environment of national succession politics. By positioning themselves as allies to the Deputy President during his time of crisis, these leaders are attempting to reshape their political relevance as the current administration faces internal instability.

### The Contradiction of Historical Narratives
This newfound alliance is complicated by a long history of tribal tensions. Many of these same politicians previously utilized inflammatory narratives against the mountain region, often referencing the volatile 2007 and 2008 post-election violence to solidify their local power bases. Observers note that the transition from antagonizing the region to acting as the primary defenders of its most senior political representative highlights a profound disconnect between their past rhetoric and their present actions. This situational morality underscores the reality that these political figures prioritize shifting interests over long-term principles.

### Opportunism Over Consistent Governance
The current show of sympathy is increasingly regarded as a purely opportunistic effort. By inserting themselves into the impeachment saga, Rift Valley power brokers are testing their ability to influence national outcomes, effectively acting as potential kingmakers or spoilers. This strategy aims to soften their public image while simultaneously ensuring the Deputy President remains dependent on their intervention. Ultimately, the support appears to be a hollow political gesture, driven by ambition and the desire to gain leverage in future government realignments rather than a commitment to the stability of the executive office.
