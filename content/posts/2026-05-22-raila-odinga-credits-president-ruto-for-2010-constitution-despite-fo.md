Raila Odinga recently shocked the Kenyan public by publicly praising President William Ruto for his role in the 2010 Constitution. This surprising endorsement arrives years after the President led the prominent No campaign against the document during the historic national referendum.

## The Transformation of Political Narratives

### A Contradictory Historical Revision
Observers are struggling to reconcile the current political narrative with the documented events of 2010. During the constitutional referendum, the No campaign, represented by the red color and the orange fruit, was spearheaded by then-politician William Ruto. He famously warned that the proposed constitution would lead the nation toward ruin. Now, Raila Odinga is describing the President as a fundamental architect of that very same document. This rapid shift in rhetoric is viewed by many as a calculated act of political gymnastics that leaves citizens questioning the integrity of established history.

### The Wonders of Moses
The phrase Wonders of Moses is now frequently used to describe these miraculous shifts in political loyalty. The expression highlights the absurdity of witnessing two long-term, bitter rivals abandon decades of open hostility to engage in such overt historical revisionism. This phenomenon illustrates how the political elite can reshape the past to suit their current alliances. While the public remains fixated on these changing dynamics, the spectacle serves as a reminder that political memory is often treated as a flexible tool rather than a fixed record of truth.

### Strange Political Bedfellows
This new alliance confirms that in the world of high-stakes governance, the past is often treated as a disposable commodity. Whether political giants are rewriting the foundational laws of the land or simply aligning to maintain power, the message is clear. Political figures have turned the 2010 Constitution into a fictional narrative where former opponents are rebranded as allies. If the objective of the political class was to challenge public perception, they have succeeded by transforming a historical struggle into a script that can be rewritten whenever a new alliance requires it.
