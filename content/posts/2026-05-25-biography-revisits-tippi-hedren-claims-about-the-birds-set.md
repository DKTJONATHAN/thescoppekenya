## The Birds: Reassessing Alfred Hitchcock and the Attic Siege

A forthcoming biography challenges the established narrative surrounding Tippi Hedren and the infamous attic attack scene in the 1963 classic, The Birds, by contrasting her long-standing allegations of onset abuse with newly surfaced technical testimonies from the film crew.

The film industry has long operated under the shadow of the production of The Birds, particularly the grueling attic siege where Tippi Hedren faced live birds for several days of filming. While Hedren’s 2016 memoir solidified the narrative of a victimized actress at the mercy of a controlling Alfred Hitchcock, historical researchers are now looking past the celebrity anecdotes. The upcoming book, A Century of Hitchcock: The Man, the Myths, the Legacy, set for release on June 9, uses production records to create a multi-perspective reconstruction of the shoot at the Brenner family home.

### Reconciling Conflicting Accounts

Author Tony Lee Moral presents a rigorous examination of the discrepancies between Hedren’s evolving recollections-which shifted in severity across interviews in 1984, 2005, and 2016-and the documented realities of 1963 studio procedures. The central contention involves the duration and nature of the attic scene. While Hedren has described a week of harrowing, real-life trauma, animal trainers like Bud Cardos and Ray Berwick have provided accounts that align with standard, albeit intense, motion picture special effects of the era.

Technical logs and interviews with staff, including make-up artist Howard Smit and hair stylist Virginia Darcy, paint a picture of a controlled environment. The biography highlights how the physical constraints of 1960s filmmaking required a reliance on live animals, yet it argues that these were managed by professionals like Gary Gero, who oversaw the safety protocols. By pitting the lived experience of the lead actress against the mechanical and logistical records kept by crew members like Bob Burks, the narrative shifts from a simple story of abuse to a complex historical inquiry into how movie sets functioned during the mid-20th century.
