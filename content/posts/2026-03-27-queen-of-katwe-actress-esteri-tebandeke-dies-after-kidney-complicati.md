Uganda's vibrant entertainment scene is in mourning this week following the tragic death of acclaimed actress Esteri Tebandeke, known for her powerful role in the globally celebrated film *Queen of Katwe*. Tebandeke passed away at Nakasero Hospital after a prolonged and arduous battle with kidney complications.

## Esteri Tebandeke: A Lasting Legacy

### A Difficult Health Journey
Tebandeke had faced a challenging health battle for several years, spending extended periods in hospital as her condition gradually worsened. A glimmer of hope emerged in 2022 when she underwent a kidney transplant, with her husband, Samuel Lutaata Tebandeke, courageously serving as the donor. The procedure was initially successful, fostering widespread optimism for her recovery. However, her health deteriorated again when her body reportedly began rejecting the transplanted kidney, leading to further complex medical issues. In recent months, her condition became critical, necessitating her admission to the High Dependency Unit at Nakasero Hospital, where she required oxygen support. Medical expenses had reportedly escalated significantly, with daily treatment costs reaching approximately Shs 4 million.

### From Vixen to International Acclaim
Born in 1984, Tebandeke carved a remarkable path in Uganda's entertainment industry. She first garnered attention as a video vixen in Bobi Wine's hit track "Singa," before making a powerful transition to acting. Her career truly soared with her role in the internationally acclaimed Walt Disney Pictures production, *Queen of Katwe*. In the film, she starred alongside Academy Award winner Lupita Nyong'o and David Oyelowo, a role she once reflected on as a catalyst for her bigger ambitions. Her powerful performances solidified her esteemed position within the region's creative landscape.

### A Void in East African Entertainment
Tebandeke's passing leaves a profound void in the East African creative sector, with fans and colleagues across the continent remembering her for her immense talent, unwavering strength, and inspiring resilience. Tributes and condolences continue to pour in for her grieving family and friends. Her indelible mark on film and performance remains a significant part of the region's artistic story, even as the East African music scene celebrates other successes, such as the [Bien and Ali Kiba collaboration reaching one million views in 24 hours](https://zandani.co.ke/article/bien-and-ali-kiba-collaboration-reaches-one-million-views-in-24-hour).
