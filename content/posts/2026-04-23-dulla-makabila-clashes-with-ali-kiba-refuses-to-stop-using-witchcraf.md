Tanzanian Singeli star Dulla Makabila arrived at Jomo Kenyatta International Airport this week for a Kenyan media tour. The artist boldly declared his refusal to stop using traditional spiritual practices for success, despite pressure from fellow musician Ali Kiba to change.

## Dulla Makabila Clashes With Ali Kiba Over Witchcraft

The self-proclaimed King of Singeli made it clear upon his arrival in Nairobi that his reliance on traditional healers is a non-negotiable part of his career. Makabila addressed reports that Ali Kiba had advised him to abandon these practices to maintain a cleaner image. The artist dismissed the suggestion, insisting that his success is a deliberate combination of talent and spiritual intervention. He noted that while technology evolves, such as how [Samsung begins mass production of HBM4 memory for NVIDIA AI systems](https://zandani.co.ke/article/samsung-begins-mass-production-of-hbm4-memory-for-nvidia-ai-systems), his methods remain rooted in tradition.

### Stalled Collaborations and Kenyan Ambitions

Makabila admitted that his stance on "uchawi" has cost him significant professional opportunities. A potential collaboration with Ali Kiba fell through because of their differing belief systems. Rather than compromising, the singer chose to look toward the Kenyan market to expand his reach. He is currently promoting a major project with Savara of Sauti Sol, which he views as a gateway for Singeli to achieve global recognition. During his stay in Nairobi, he expressed a strong desire to work with other local heavyweights including Bien, Khaligraph Jones, and Nadia Mukami.

### Relationships and Financial Confidence

Outside of his musical spiritualism, Makabila spoke candidly about his personal life and his history of two divorces. He claimed that his recovery time after a breakup is exceptionally short, often taking only minutes to move on to a new partner. The artist linked this confidence to his financial stability and career success. His blunt approach to romance stands in contrast to the sentimental gestures often seen in the industry, like when [Adekunle Gold gifts Simi Mercedes-Benz GLE and diamond ring for birthday](https://zandani.co.ke/article/adekunle-gold-gifts-simi-mercedes-benz-gle-and-diamond-ring-for-birt). Makabila remains focused on his brand, intending to find spiritual guidance in Kenya while solidifying his status as a regional powerhouse.
