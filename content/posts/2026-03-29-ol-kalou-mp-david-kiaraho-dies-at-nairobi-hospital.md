By Celestine Nzioka, Za Ndani Ol Kalou Member of Parliament David Njuguna Kiaraho died today, Sunday, March 29, 2026, at Nairobi Hospital while undergoing treatment. National Assembly Speaker Moses Wetang'ula confirmed the lawmaker's passing, noting he had been unwell for an extended period. His death marks a significant loss for the constituency and the National Assembly.

Ol Kalou MP's Passing Confirmed Speaker Moses Wetang'ula officially confirmed David Njuguna Kiaraho's death on Sunday, March 29, 2026. Kiaraho passed away in the early hours of the morning, around 1:20 AM, while receiving specialized treatment at Nairobi Hospital. While the lawmaker had reportedly been unwell for some time, seeking treatment both locally and abroad, the exact circumstances surrounding his death, including the specific illness he had been battling, have not yet been made public.

Funeral Preparations Underway Following the announcement, Speaker Wetang'ula appointed Members to a Funeral Preparation Committee. This committee, tasked with collaborating with the MP's family and coordinating funeral arrangements, will be led by Kinangop MP Kwenya Thuku and assisted by Nyandarua MP Faith Gitau and seven other members. Wetang'ula conveyed his heartfelt condolences to Kiaraho's family and friends on behalf of all Members and staff of the National Assembly.

Constituency Initiatives and Public Appearances Despite his prolonged illness, Kiaraho's office continued to facilitate various initiatives and donations to support the Ol Kalou constituency. As recently as March 25, 2026, a representative received 40 water tanks from the Kenya Wildlife Service, designated to aid schools with water harvesting during the rainy season. The MP's last public appearance was in November 2025, when he conducted an infrastructural inspection tour within his constituency.

Political Tenure and Past Controversies David Njuguna Kiaraho was first elected as Ol Kalou constituency MP in 2013, successfully defending his seat in the 2017 and 2022 General elections. In the National Assembly, he served as a member of the Departmental Committee on Transport, Public Works & Housing, and the Procedure & House Rules Committee. His tenure was not without controversy, as he was questioned in connection with the alleged mismanagement of approximately KSh 120 million in National Government Constituencies Development Fund (NG-CDF) funds between 2013 and 2018. In 2020, the Ethics and Anti-Corruption Commission (EACC) also questioned him over alleged mismanagement of constituency development funds. His work in parliament underscored the importance of legislative oversight in national development, especially as Sub-Saharan African economies increase integration into global trade networks.

Second MP Death This Year Kiaraho's death makes him the second Member of Parliament to die this year. Emurua Dikirr MP Johana Kipyegon Ng’eno was among six people killed in a helicopter crash in Mosop, Nandi County, on Saturday, February 28, 2026.
