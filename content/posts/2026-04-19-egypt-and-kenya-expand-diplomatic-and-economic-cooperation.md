## Strengthening Kenya-Egypt Trade and Investment Kenya's partnership with Egypt underscores a strategic vision for African economic integration. Bilateral trade reached USD 567 million in 2024, driven by key sectors including agriculture, energy, and infrastructure. This collaboration positions Kenya as a vital commercial hub, fostering mutual investment and regional stability across East Africa.

Bilateral Trade Dynamics Trade between Kenya and Egypt recorded approximately USD 567 million in 2024, highlighting the growing economic interconnectedness between the two nations. Kenya's primary exports to Egypt include agricultural commodities such as tea, coffee, and various other agricultural goods. Conversely, Egyptian exports to the Kenyan market feature electrical products, plastics, and diverse food products. This trade composition reflects complementary economic strengths and consumer demands.

Strategic Investment and Sectoral Growth The Egyptian-Kenyan Joint Committee, established in 2016, actively promotes trade and investment across critical sectors, including engineering, agriculture, energy, and healthcare. This framework supports targeted growth and collaboration. Egypt stands as a significant investor in Kenya, particularly channeling capital into infrastructure and transportation projects. Mutual investments are steadily expanding, indicating robust confidence in Kenya's economic prospects. The emphasis on agricultural exports, including traditional goods, aligns with broader strategies to enhance Kenya's global market presence for primary products.

Overcoming Economic Challenges Despite the robust framework for cooperation, both countries face economic challenges, particularly in achieving the full potential of their trade volumes. Structural and financial constraints contribute to these limitations. However, proactive measures are underway to address these impediments through enhanced economic integration, the expansion of joint projects, and the promotion of sustainable investments. This strategic collaboration reinforces Kenya's role as a key commercial hub in East Africa, contributing to broader African integration and regional economic balance.
