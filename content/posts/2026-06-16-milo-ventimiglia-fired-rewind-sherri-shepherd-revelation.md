## How an Early 'Rewind' Rejection Shaped Milo Ventimiglia's Career

We often hear about actors' big breaks, but their early challenges can be just as interesting. Sherri Shepherd recently shared a surprising detail from Milo Ventimiglia's past: he was fired from the 1997 sitcom *Rewind* before it even aired. This previously little-known event shows that even successful actors like Ventimiglia experience major career bumps long before they find fame.

### The Unaired Sitcom Rewind and an Unexpected Exit

Sherri Shepherd brought this story to light on her talk show, casually sharing a memory from almost three decades ago. In 1997, a then-18-year-old Milo Ventimiglia landed a role in a Fox sitcom called *Rewind*. He was set to play a younger version of Scott Baio's character, Rob. Shepherd and Mystro Clark were also part of the cast.

Ventimiglia's time on the show was brief. Shepherd recalled that he left the production very early, right after the initial table read. The specific reasons for his departure were described vaguely as "creative direction" and possibly an issue with his age for the role. This marked an abrupt end to what could have been one of his first big network jobs. *Rewind* itself never made it to air. Fox cancelled the show two weeks before its scheduled premiere, despite having taped six episodes. So, for Ventimiglia, this rejection came from a project that the public never even saw.

### From Setback to Stardom: Milo Ventimiglia's Resilient Path

Getting fired from an unaired show could discourage any new actor. For Milo Ventimiglia, however, this early experience with *Rewind* became just one step in a career marked by significant achievements. After this early dismissal, he kept auditioning, taking on smaller parts, and developing his acting skills.

His persistence eventually led to success. Within a few years, Ventimiglia secured the role of Jess Mariano in *Gilmore Girls*, which connected him with many viewers. He then took on another important part as Peter Petrelli in the popular sci-fi series *Heroes*, making him a more familiar face in the industry. But it was his performance as Jack Pearson in NBC's *This Is Us* that brought him widespread recognition, critical praise, and a dedicated following around the world. His path shows that initial difficulties often precede major successes.

### Hollywood's Tough Lessons and Lasting Success

Shepherd's decision to share this old story highlights a common reality in the entertainment business: rejection happens often. Behind every widely celebrated success, there are usually many auditions, firings, and projects that never saw the light of day. Ventimiglia's early time on *Rewind* illustrates that even well-known actors encountered significant challenges early in their careers, often before the public knew who they were.

That Sherri Shepherd chose to share this long-forgotten detail during a reunion with Ventimiglia, decades later, is meaningful. It shows that an initial setback does not determine an individual's future potential or eventual success. For those who follow actors' careers, learning about these early struggles provides more context to their journey. It reveals the kind of persistence needed to succeed in a very competitive field. Ventimiglia's progression from being let go from an unseen sitcom to becoming a highly regarded actor on television serves as a clear example of overcoming early professional hurdles.
