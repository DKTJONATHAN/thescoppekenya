President William Ruto is set to host internet sensations Majembe and Mbavu Destroyer, alongside other key contributors from the Vurugu Bout Night event, at State House. This follows the highly anticipated bout on April 4 at the Karasani Indoors Arena, which captured presidential attention.

Presidential Recognition for Boxing Success

State House Visit Details Media personality Oga Obinna confirmed a phone call with President Ruto, revealing the President's commendation for the Vurugu Bout Night event and praise for the involved youth. President Ruto, who previously pledged Ksh1 million to each boxer, Majembe (Portifas Odipo) and Mbavu Destroyer (Ferdinand Omondi), is currently reviewing his schedule to arrange a State House visit. This meeting will include the boxers, referees, and all other essential contributors, aiming to celebrate the event's triumph and acknowledge every participant.

Calls for Fair Compensation Obinna urged citizens not to politicize the President's planned visit, emphasizing its significance as a blessing for those involved rather than a political opportunity. During his discussion with President Ruto, Obinna advocated for additional compensation, specifically requesting Ksh1 million for other boxers whose initial payments were deemed insufficient. He also sought Ksh300,000 for referees and officials, recognizing their crucial role in the event's successful execution.

Event Acclaim and Future Plans Obinna extended gratitude to all participants, including contenders, referees, organizers, and supporters, for ensuring a smooth and successful event. He stated that the match demonstrated the capacity of the people to self-organize major sporting activities, labeling it the largest in Eastern Africa. Obinna also appreciated government officials who attended, noting he required politicians to pay for entry rather than using the event as a platform. With eight governors and numerous Members of Parliament praising the match, Obinna plans to expand these boxing events to counties like Busia, Mombasa, and Uasin Gishu, offering more young talent professional competition. A preliminary lineup for the next match includes Majembe versus Somali Boxer, with possible bouts featuring Alfayo.
