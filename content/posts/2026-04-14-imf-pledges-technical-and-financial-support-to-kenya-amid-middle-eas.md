Celestine Nzioka, Za Ndani The International Monetary Fund (IMF) has pledged comprehensive support to Kenya, addressing a potential fuel shortage exacerbated by the ongoing Middle East conflict. Following a high-level meeting in Washington, D.C. on Monday, April 13, the Fund assured policy advice, technical assistance, and financial backing to bolster the nation's economic stability.

Kenya Secures IMF Support Amidst Regional Volatility

High-Level Discussions in Washington IMF Managing Director Kristalina Georgieva met with National Treasury Cabinet Secretary John Mbadi, Principal Secretary Chris Kiptoo, and Central Bank of Kenya Governor Kamau Thugge. The discussions on April 13, 2026, focused on Kenya’s economic performance, financial conditions, inflation, reform priorities, and the impact of global tensions, particularly the Middle East conflict. The Bretton Woods Institution reaffirmed its commitment as a trusted partner, promising support through policy advice, technical assistance, and, where appropriate, financial assistance. Cabinet Secretary Mbadi emphasized the administration's continued close collaboration with the Fund to anchor reforms, strengthen market confidence, and mobilize financing support to effectively respond to external shocks and domestic constraints.

New Funding Program Under Negotiation Yesterday's meeting occurred as the government continues negotiations with the IMF for a new funded program. This follows the expiration of a previous Ksh466 billion ($3.6 billion) arrangement in March 2025. That 38-month deal, structured under the Extended Credit Facility and Extended Fund Facility, lapsed before full disbursement, leaving a tranche worth Ksh110 billion, approximately USD850 million, untranched. Governor Thugge confirmed on April 9, 2026, during this year's second Monetary Policy Meeting, that talks for the new facility were ongoing. An IMF mission visited Nairobi from February 24 to March 4, 2026, and further discussions are slated to continue later this month in Washington.

Strengthening Resilience Against Global Shocks Kenya continues to benefit from IMF support, which has been instrumental in maintaining macroeconomic stability and advancing fiscal consolidation. The ongoing Middle East conflict has significantly disrupted global oil supplies and gas, with repercussions expected to persist throughout 2026, even with a ceasefire. While addressing geopolitical tensions, such strategies also prepare the nation for domestic challenges, as seen with forecasts for intensified rainfall in areas like Nairobi, Kiambu, and Nakuru, highlighting a nation's unaddressed vulnerability. [cite: internal link] The IMF has underscored the need for countries to strengthen fiscal discipline, enhance fiscal credibility, and build resilience to external shocks, supported by improved governance and public sector efficiency.
