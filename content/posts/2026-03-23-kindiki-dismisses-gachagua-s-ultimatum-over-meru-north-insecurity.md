Deputy President Kithure Kindiki has directly challenged his predecessor Rigathi Gachagua, dismissing a recent 10-day ultimatum issued to the government concerning escalating insecurity in Meru North. The public exchange occurred today, Monday, March 23, 2026, highlighting a renewed focus on banditry along the Isiolo and Samburu border.

Kindiki Rejects Gachagua Ultimatum Deputy President Kindiki responded forcefully via a social media post, rejecting Gachagua’s warning to mobilise Meru residents for self-defence if security forces are not promptly deployed. Kindiki accused his predecessor of incitement and questioned his efficacy while previously in office, stating that Gachagua failed to provide adequate support to affected communities during his tenure.

Government’s Security Stance Kindiki defended the government’s comprehensive approach to combating banditry. He referenced past operations he personally coordinated as Cabinet Secretary for Interior between 2023 and 2024. During that period, he stated he spent months in the affected and neighbouring counties, operating from bush camps to coordinate security formations and dismantle criminal networks. Those efforts, Kindiki asserted, successfully suppressed banditry in Meru North at the time. However, the Deputy President acknowledged that banditry has plagued the region for over a decade, noting a recent severe escalation. "Bandit attacks have resumed over the last two weeks, with devastating loss of life and livestock," Kindiki observed. The disruption caused by such insecurity significantly hampers economic activities, including efforts to empower modern agriculture in the region.

Intensified Operation Planned In response to the deteriorating situation, Kindiki reiterated that authorities will launch a fresh, decisive crackdown on banditry. This announcement follows his recent declaration of a two-week timeline for an intensified security operation in Nyambene, Meru County, aimed at restoring stability. The government plans to prioritise robust security deployments, incorporating military personnel, police units, and specialised equipment, including armoured vehicles and aircraft. The public disagreement between Kindiki and Gachagua underscores rising political tensions as leaders increasingly trade public statements in the lead-up to the 2027 elections.
