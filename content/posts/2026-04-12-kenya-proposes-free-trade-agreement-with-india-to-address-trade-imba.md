Kenya is proposing a free trade agreement with India to address a significant Ksh271 billion trade imbalance and boost exports. Prime Cabinet Secretary Musalia Mudavadi hinted at this pact on April 12, 2026, during the 2nd Khumbhabhisheka Mahotsavam ceremony at Sri Kalyana Venkateswara Temple in Nairobi.

## Kenya Proposes Formalised Trade Rules with India

### Bridging a Widening Trade Gap

The proposed free trade agreement seeks to unlock a Ksh271 billion trade opportunity, directly addressing the substantial imbalance where Kenya exports goods worth USD147.4 million, approximately Ksh19 billion, to India while importing products valued at USD2.03 billion, roughly Ksh262 billion. This disparity continues to influence local pricing and market dynamics. PCS Mudavadi emphasised that the agreement aims to elevate cooperation, drawing parallels to ongoing discussions with the European Union, the SEPA, the United Arab Emirates, and a bilateral trade agreement with the United States.

Kenya’s primary exports to India include agricultural products and raw materials such as tea, soda ash, edible vegetables like peas, coffee, and metal scraps including copper and aluminium. Recently, tea has emerged as a top export alongside significant volumes of inorganic chemicals, hides, and skins. Conversely, Kenya largely imports petroleum products, pharmaceuticals, machinery, and rice from India, a major trading partner. Other significant imports encompass refined petroleum, automobiles and parts, plastics, and iron and steel products, often driven by the demand for affordable medicines and industrial equipment.

The government’s push for this agreement signals a strategic shift towards deeper economic ties, formalising trade rules that will directly impact businesses, traders, and consumers across both economies. The push to formalise trade rules signals a broader government effort to streamline economic interactions and prepare businesses for evolving market dynamics, including discussions around advanced trading methodologies. ### Strategic Alignment and Community Contributions

Mudavadi stressed that the proposed agreement would strategically align India with other key partners like China, where Kenya already benefits from preferential trade arrangements under the Forum on China-Africa Cooperation (FOCAC). This initiative forms part of the country’s broader global trade strategy.

The Prime Cabinet Secretary also acknowledged the profound contributions of the Indian community, recognised as the 44th tribe of Kenya in July 2017. Their enduring impact spans vital sectors such as health, education, investment, and philanthropy, strengthening the nation’s social and economic fabric.

This trade initiative comes shortly after a joint military operation confirmed on April 7, 2026, when the Indian guided-missile frigate, INS Trikand, docked at the Port of Mombasa for a four-day port call. This visit aimed at strengthening military cooperation between India and Kenya, reflecting the broader scope of bilateral engagement. India is actively pursuing Operational Turnaround (OTR) strategies across various sectors to enhance efficiency, reduce costs, and stimulate economic growth.
