Kangaru Boys Secondary School in Embu County has been closed indefinitely following a student strike that erupted on Sunday, March 22. The incident prompted concern among local authorities as a majority of learners exited the school premises during the night.

## Student Exodus and Town Disruption

The precise cause of the latest unrest at Kangaru Boys Secondary School remains undisclosed, with both local authorities and the school administration yet to issue an official report. Students were observed walking along the Embu-Meru Highway, reportedly seeking transport home. Others gathered in significant groups within Embu town, equipped with whistles, leading to considerable traffic disruption. It is presently unknown whether school property sustained any damage during the incident. This event follows a similar walkout several months prior, which students attributed to a teacher's alleged assault on a colleague.

### Nationwide Crackdown on School Disruptions

The incident at Kangaru Boys Secondary School contributes to an increasing number of school disruptions reported across the country since the beginning of 2026. Two months ago, on Wednesday, February 4, the Ministry of Interior announced a comprehensive crackdown to address school unrest in 15 counties. Principal Secretary Raymond Omollo confirmed the initiative following a monthly meeting of Principal Secretaries chaired by the Head of Public Service, Felix Koskei. The crackdown targets counties including Kericho, Nandi, Nakuru, Kitui, Tharaka Nithi, Kakamega, Narok, Kisii, Nyeri, Nyamira, Kajiado, Migori, Kirinyaga, Trans Nzoia, and Nairobi. The Ministry of Interior stated its collaboration with the Ministry of Education to monitor situations, respond to emergencies in learning institutions, and apprehend those responsible for property destruction. Security agencies are committed to acting firmly to address evolving risks nationwide, ensuring learning continues seamlessly.
