Africa Magic has confirmed the return of its Viewers' Choice Awards for the 12th edition, set for Saturday, May 9th, 2026. The announcement on Thursday, March 26th, at MultiChoice Studio in Lagos, named veteran actress Joke Silva as Head Judge, signaling a major continental celebration of film and television excellence.

## AMVCA Returns with Expanded Recognition

This year's Africa Magic Viewers' Choice Awards (AMVCA) introduces significant enhancements, broadening its scope and recognition across the continent. The 12th edition will feature 32 award categories, with 18 determined by a jury, 11 open to public voting, and three special recognition awards, including Lifetime Achievement and Trailblazer honors. The expansion of Indigenous Language categories, with the addition of Best Indigenous Language, North Africa and Best Indigenous Language, Central Africa, underscores a commitment to celebrating diverse linguistic cinematic achievements across the region.

### Veteran Actress Joke Silva to Lead Judging Panel

Esteemed actress Joke Silva has been appointed Head Judge for the 12th AMVCA. Bringing decades of experience in both acting and production, Silva's leadership in the judging process is poised to uphold the awards' reputation for recognizing outstanding craftsmanship and talent within the African entertainment industry.

### Cultivating an Ecosystem of Excellence

The AMVCAs, which commenced in 2013, have evolved into a cornerstone event for African entertainment. The awards have fostered substantial economic growth, creating over 30,000 jobs across various sectors, from production design and screenwriting to sound engineering, highlighting the ceremony's significant impact beyond the red carpet. Atinuke Babatunde, Executive Head of Content and Channels at MultiChoice West Africa, emphasized the event's dedication to acknowledging the "ecosystem of excellence" behind the beloved stories. This commitment to the industry's varied professionals continues to be a driving force, celebrating not only the stars but also the extensive talent pool that contributes to African cinema, a community that also remembers figures like Queen of Katwe actress Esteri Tebandeke, who passed away after kidney complications.

### Sponsors Drive Pan-African Celebration

The 12th AMVCA is headlined by Don Julio as the lead sponsor, with additional crucial support from partners such as Guinness, MTN, Indomie, QuickTeller, Verve, and Lush Hair. These collaborations underscore the growing interest and investment in the African creative economy, enabling the large-scale recognition of artistic achievement across the continent.

### Anticipation Builds for Nominations

The nominee announcement is scheduled for Sunday, March 29th, airing live on Africa Magic channels from 7 PM. Hosted by AMVCA-winning actor Chiemezie Imo, the reveal will set the stage for the highly anticipated ceremony in May, offering viewers a first look at the projects and individuals vying for top honors this year.
