Kenya Ports Authority (KPA) Managing Director William Ruto was hospitalized after a tragic road accident in Taita Taveta on Friday, April 3, 2026. His daughter tragically succumbed to injuries from the incident, while Ruto remains in stable condition under medical supervision.

Heightened Road Safety Concerns Mark Easter Travel

KPA Confirms Tragic Incident The Kenya Ports Authority confirmed that its Chief Executive Officer, Capt. William K. Ruto, and his family were involved in a tragic road accident yesterday evening, Friday, April 3, 2026, while travelling around Taita Taveta. The authority announced with deep sadness that Ruto's daughter did not survive the accident. Capt. Ruto, who has led the KPA since 2023, is currently in stable condition and receiving close medical supervision. KPA expressed hope for his full and swift recovery and stated that it is collaborating with investigative authorities, including the police, to establish the full circumstances surrounding the crash.

Multiple Crashes Reported Amidst Holiday Rush The accident involving the KPA MD occurred on a Friday night that saw numerous road incidents across the country as travelers headed upcountry for the Easter holidays and schools closed for the term. One severe crash on the Nairobi-Nakuru highway at Kariandusi, near Gilgil, claimed the lives of 11 individuals after a 14-seater matatu collided with a stalled trailer in the early hours of Saturday morning. Gilgil Sub-County Police Commander Winston Mwakio reported that the trailer had mechanical issues, leading to the high-speed matatu losing control and crashing into it. Such incidents highlight ongoing concerns regarding vehicle maintenance and public transport safety, drawing parallels to operational safety lapses within the sector, as previously reported by Za Ndani. Another significant incident reported by the Kenya Red Cross Society occurred at approximately 5:20 AM at the Tunnel area in Fort Tenan, Kericho County. This multi-vehicle crash involved two buses and two lorries, carrying a total of 66 passengers. While no fatalities were reported in this particular incident, 20 casualties required medical attention. Five individuals sustained serious injuries, with two transferred to higher-level facilities, while six children were among those receiving treatment at Fort Tenan Sub-County Hospital. The frequent occurrence of such large-scale accidents underscores the urgent need for stringent adherence to road safety regulations and robust vehicle inspection protocols, a matter the Transport Ministry has been addressing with new initiatives set for July 1 rollout.
