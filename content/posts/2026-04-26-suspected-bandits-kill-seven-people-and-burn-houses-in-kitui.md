Suspected bandits killed seven people and burned several houses in an attack on Saturday, April 25, 2026, at Kamari village in Tseikuru, Kitui County. The assailants, believed to be herders of Somali origin, opened fire on victims working on a farm before escaping into Mwingi Game Reserve in a Toyota Probox.

## Escalating Tensions in Kitui

The deadly assault, which left one woman and six men deceased, also resulted in significant property destruction as numerous homes were set ablaze, displacing many families. Kitui County Commissioner Erastus Mbui confirmed the incident, noting that one man survived with gunshot injuries and is currently receiving treatment at Tseikuru Level IV Hospital. Villagers have since sought refuge in nearby bushland, fearing further attacks.

### Retaliatory Attacks Amidst Banditry Crisis

Authorities indicate this incident forms part of a series of retaliatory attacks between pastoralist groups and local residents. A police officer stated, "It is retaliation after a herder from one community killed a herder from another in the game park. This appears to be a counterattack." The region has experienced intensified banditry, leading to livestock theft and loss of life.

### Government Deploys Full Machinery

This latest attack occurs as the government intensifies its efforts to combat banditry across the North Rift and upper Eastern regions, specifically in areas like Meru, Isiolo, Samburu, and Turkana counties. Following a recent incident where bandits killed two locals in Meru, Deputy President Kithure Kindiki affirmed that the operation would take precedence over other government assignments. Kindiki emphasized the deployment of all state machinery, including the military, armored vehicles, and aircraft, to restore peace. He stated, "The government of Kenya will use all instruments of war: our military, police officers, tanks, and aircraft to confront the bandits in Nyambene. The President has directed that both the police and the Kenya Defence Forces suspend other duties and focus on fighting criminal gangs in this region". While the government prioritizes security in these troubled regions, other political developments, such as party responses to various probes, continue to unfold across the nation.
