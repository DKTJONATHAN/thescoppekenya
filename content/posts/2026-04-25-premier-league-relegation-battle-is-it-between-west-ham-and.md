**TITLE: Premier League relegation battle: Is it between West Ham and Tottenham?**

Nottingham Forest delivered a stunning 5-0 victory over Sunderland on Friday, propelling them to a comfortable 39 points and significantly boosting their Premier League survival hopes for 2026. This emphatic win has reshaped the relegation battle, narrowing the fight for the final spot to just two clubs: Tottenham and West Ham.

## Premier League Relegation: The Battle for Survival Narrows

Nottingham Forest's monumental 5-0 thrashing of Sunderland on Friday has dramatically altered the Premier League relegation landscape. Moving to 39 points, Forest now sit eight clear of 18th-placed Tottenham and six ahead of 17th-placed West Ham, placing them on the brink of securing a fifth consecutive season in the top flight. The question now shifts from who will go down to who *between* Tottenham and West Ham will face the drop.

### Forest's Great Escape: A Statistical Certainty?

Despite manager Vitor Pereira's cautious insistence that 39 points "isn't enough," historical data strongly suggests otherwise. Throughout Premier League history, no team on exactly 39 points after 34 games has ever been relegated. Furthermore, the average points tally for the 18th-placed team in a 38-match season is 34.5, a figure that has dropped to 29.6 over the past five campaigns. Only six teams have ever been relegated with 39 or more points, the last being Birmingham and Blackpool in 2010-11. With Forest unbeaten in eight games in all competitions, their survival seems all but guaranteed, a sentiment echoed by many football pundits who hailed their Sunderland triumph as "the result of the season."

Forest's ruthless display saw Chris Wood score his first goal since the opening day, alongside contributions from Igor Jesus, Elliot Anderson, and Morgan Gibbs-White, whose scoring streak now stands at seven goals in seven games. This newfound attacking prowess suggests a team determined to finish strong.

### The Two-Horse Race: Tottenham vs. West Ham

With Leeds United also considered safe on 40 points, all eyes are now firmly fixed on Tottenham and West Ham. According to leading sports analytics, there is a 99% probability that one of these two London clubs will occupy the final relegation spot. Tottenham are currently deemed 61.01% likely to go down, while West Ham face a 37.98% chance.

Today, Saturday, April 25, 2026, presents critical fixtures for both clubs. West Ham host Everton, while Tottenham visit already relegated Wolves. The outcomes of these matches will undoubtedly swing the percentages once more.
