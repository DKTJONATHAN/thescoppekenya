Cooperatives Cabinet Secretary Wycliffe Oparanya on Friday, April 24, 2026, threatened to withdraw the Western region's support for the Orange Democratic Movement. Speaking in Lugari, Kakamega County, Oparanya cited growing disregard for Western leaders by the party's Linda Groundfaction, warning of a political shift ahead of the 2027 General Elections.

## Oparanya Issues Ultimatum to ODM Leadership

Oparanya's strong remarks underscore increasing dissatisfaction within the Orange Democratic Movement (ODM) over the treatment of Western region leaders. He criticized the party's current leadership, specifically the Linda Groundfaction led by Oburu Odinga, for allegedly sidelining the region. Oparanya contrasted the present situation with the tenure of the late former Prime Minister Raila Odinga, under whom the Western region reportedly received greater recognition and respect. "Raila respected us. Now, those leading ODM after Raila’s death should also respect us. If they do not respect us, we will quit," Oparanya stated.

### Disregard for Western Leaders and Grassroots

The former ODM deputy party leader emphasized that Western Kenya figures were instrumental in building the party. However, they now feel excluded from crucial decision-making processes and power-sharing arrangements within the ODM's current framework. According to Oparanya, the present leadership appears more focused on securing influence within President William Ruto’s administration than on addressing the needs of its grassroots supporters. This perceived shift in focus has fueled discontent among regional leaders who believe their contributions and the needs of their constituents are being overlooked.

### Political Realignment Ahead of 2027

Oparanya warned that continued disregard for the Western region could trigger a significant political realignment in anticipation of the 2027 General Elections. His comments coincide with deepening divisions within ODM, which have led to the formation of a rival Linda Mwananchi faction, spearheaded by Nairobi Senator Edwin Sifuna. While the Linda Groundfaction faces accusations of marginalizing the Western region, the Sifuna-led pact has intensified its grassroots mobilization, particularly in areas traditionally considered ODM strongholds. The Linda Mwananchi movement is scheduled to commence its activities in Vihiga on Saturday, April 25, 2026, before proceeding to Kisumu for a main rally on Sunday, April 26, 2026. These internal party dynamics unfold amidst planned talks between ODM and the ruling United Democratic Alliance (UDA) concerning a new broad-based political arrangement for the upcoming 2027 General Election.
