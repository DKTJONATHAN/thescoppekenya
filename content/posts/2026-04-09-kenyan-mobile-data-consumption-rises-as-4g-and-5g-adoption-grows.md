## Accelerating Digital Transformation: Kenya's Mobile Data Surge Kenya's mobile internet story has evolved from basic access to accelerating data consumption. The Communications Authority of Kenya's October-December 2025 report reveals a significant surge in data usage, predominantly driven by expanding 4G and 5G networks, underpinning the nation's digital economic growth.

Exploding Data Consumption Mobile broadband subscriptions in Kenya rose 9.3% to 51.5 million, with total mobile data subscriptions reaching 61.9 million, a 2.9% increase. Total mobile broadband consumption dramatically jumped 12% in three months, reaching 755,095 TB. This substantial increase indicates a profound shift in user behavior, moving beyond mere connectivity to intensive data utilization.

The 4G and 5G Imperative The generational shift in network technology is the primary catalyst for this consumption surge. Data usage on 4G and 5G networks consistently grew during the quarter, while 3G usage continued its decline. Kenyans are actively migrating to faster networks, a trend indicating that superior connectivity, once experienced, becomes the new baseline. While the average mobile broadband user consumed 14.6 GB monthly, 5G subscribers averaged 46.4 GB per month - three times the average. This gap reflects both early adopter profiles and the enhanced capabilities faster networks unlock.

Transforming Daily Life and the Digital Economy The availability of faster, more reliable connectivity enables significant changes across daily digital interactions. Buffering is less of a concern, leading to increased streaming, downloading, and engagement with data-intensive applications. Mobile video streaming is now genuinely viable for most users, extending beyond major urban centers. Digital payments and e-commerce services also benefit from stable, high-speed connections. This underpins the expanding reach of remote work and online learning. The surge in mobile money users, with Kenya adding 2.7 million in three months to reach 98% penetration, exemplifies the deepening integration of mobile solutions into everyday life, similar to innovations like [M-Pesa introducing tap-to-pay functionality for Android users in Tanzania](https://zandani.co.ke/article/m-pesa-introduces-tap-to-pay-functionality-for-android-users-in-tanz).

Safaricom's Dominant Position Safaricom maintains a commanding market share, accounting for 66.8% of mobile subscriptions, 64.3% of mobile broadband subscriptions, and 89% of mobile money transactions during the quarter. Its extensive network footprint provides a structural advantage as demand shifts towards higher-capacity technologies, a position Safaricom is actively leveraging.

Smartphone Penetration and Future Trajectory Smartphone penetration reached 92.9% during the quarter, as feature phone usage declined. This widespread adoption means nearly every mobile user possesses a device capable of fully utilizing 4G and 5G speeds, a capability increasingly put to use. The CA report underscores that this demand is not decelerating. As 5G coverage expands and more affordable 5G-capable devices become available, average consumption figures are projected to climb further, solidifying Kenya's mobile infrastructure as the foundation of a data-driven digital economy.
