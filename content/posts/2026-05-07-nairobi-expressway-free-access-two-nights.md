## Nairobi Expressway Toll-Free Access 

Moja Expressway Company has suspended toll fees on the Nairobi Expressway between the Central Business District and Westlands for two nights to facilitate traffic flow as ongoing construction on Waiyaki Way intensifies gridlock for local motorists.

The exemption takes effect tonight, Thursday, May 7, and continues through Friday, May 8. Motorists using the designated corridor will be allowed to bypass toll stations without charge during the specific window of 9pm to 6am. This operational shift is a direct response to the mounting pressure on surface-level roads, where current infrastructure improvements have significantly hampered transit times for late-night commuters.

The temporary toll-free period serves as a strategic intervention to redirect traffic away from the congested Waiyaki Way. By opening the elevated lanes, the company aims to reduce the volume of vehicles trapped in construction-related bottlenecks. This ensures that essential travel through the city center remains fluid despite the limitations imposed by the road works below.

Infrastructure managers have emphasized that this is a timed measure designed to balance urban mobility requirements with the logistical demands of the ongoing projects. The corridor between the CBD and Westlands has been specifically targeted due to its status as a primary arterial route that connects the commercial hub with the western residential and business districts.

Commuters are expected to utilize this window to bypass surface disruptions, potentially easing the workload on traffic marshals stationed at intersections along the main road. The decision reflects a growing trend in urban traffic management, where temporary infrastructure access is used as a tactical solution to mitigate the immediate impacts of city development.

### Why it matters now

The management of Nairobi traffic has become a critical economic issue as regional logistics face external pressures. By providing cost-free relief during off-peak hours, the operator is acknowledging the financial and temporal burden that prolonged construction places on the working population. This initiative aligns with broader efforts to maintain economic productivity by ensuring that transport networks remain viable, even when standard routes are compromised by modernization efforts. It acts as a rare instance of infrastructure cooperation that prioritizes driver convenience over immediate revenue collection during high-impact project phases.

### Logistics and operational shifts

The logistics of this toll-free period are simple. Motorists entering the Expressway at the CBD point and exiting toward Westlands, or vice versa, will find the toll plazas open to traffic without payment prompts. Signage at the entry points will be updated to reflect the overnight change. Drivers are cautioned to remain observant of speed limits on the elevated road, as standard safety protocols remain strictly in effect despite the removal of tolls.

For those planning travel tonight and tomorrow morning, the 9pm to 6am window provides a predictable alternative to the unpredictability of Waiyaki Way. ### What to watch next

The long-term success of this traffic mitigation strategy will likely be measured by the reduction in overnight complaints and the shift in vehicle volumes from the ground-level roads.
