## Kenya's Diplomatic Imperative: Safeguarding Citizens Amidst South African Protests

Kenya's High Commission in Pretoria has issued a critical advisory for its nationals in South Africa, urging vigilance amidst escalating xenophobic violence. This directive follows fresh reports of targeted attacks on foreign nationals, placing thousands of Kenyans working, studying, and doing business across various provinces in immediate jeopardy.

### Pretoria's Advisory and Citizen Safety

The advisory, issued on May 4, serves as a direct intervention following a sharp resurgence of xenophobic violence in late April and early May 2026 across Johannesburg, Cape Town, and the Eastern Cape. Kenyans are urged to exercise extreme caution, remain vigilant, and avoid areas prone to demonstrations. The High Commission's directive emphasizes carrying valid identification at all times and maintaining contact with security agencies. In the event of an incident, Kenyan nationals are advised to report to the South African Police Service (SAPS) and subsequently inform the Kenya High Commission for appropriate assistance. This proactive stance by Nairobi underscores its commitment to the welfare of its diaspora, a critical facet of its foreign policy. ### The Call for Accountability and Future Diplomacy
