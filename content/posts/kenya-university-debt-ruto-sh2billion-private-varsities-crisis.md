**The Scoop**
So, word on the street, President William Ruto dropped a pledge on Friday, February 20, ati the government will pay Sh2 billion to Kenya Methodist University (KeMU). You'd think that's a lot, but manze, that's just a tiny piece of the pie. KeMU alone is owed more than Sh5 billion, and the bigger picture? The State apparently owes a whopping Sh60 billion to 32 private universities in total! Can you even imagine? Hii ni pesa mingi vibaya sana.

**The Evidence**
The President made this promise when he hosted the Methodist Church leadership at State House. Apparently, this Sh2 billion is part of the government's capitation debt to KeMU. See, the problem started way back when the government decided to place government-sponsored students in private universities to expand access to higher education. Sounds good on paper, right? But the payments never quite kept up. The program ran until the 2022/2023 financial year, when new placements stopped, and the debt has just been growing ever since, from Sh45 billion to Sh60 billion as students finish their courses.

Kwanza, the numbers don't lie. In 2025, 270,715 students met the requirements for uni admission, an increase from about 246,000 in 2024. More students, more pressure. These private varsities, like Mount Kenya University (owed Sh11.6 billion), KCA University (Sh6.6 billion), and Kabarak University (Sh6.4 billion), are literally struggling because the government hasn't paid tuition fees for students it placed there. It's like sending someone to eat at a vibanda then refusing to pay the bill! Walai, the Kenya Association of Private Universities (KAPU) even said they might sue the State Department.
