The political bedrock of President William Ruto’s administration is showing deep structural cracks as key Rift Valley power brokers turn on one another. High-profile UDA leaders are now locked in public feuds, signaling a brewing revolt within the heart of the president's electoral base in May 2026.

## Internal Rifts Emerge Among UDA Leaders in President Ruto’s Rift Valley Stronghold

### The Boomerang Effect
There is a distinct sense of poetic justice manifesting across the Rift Valley. For years, the region’s political heavyweights fine-tuned the art of creating chaos within rival turfs, specifically targeting the Mt. Kenya region to keep the opposition scrambling. Now, that same strategy of internal sabotage has returned to haunt those who perfected it. Observers often cite the sentiment that the divine does not favor one side indefinitely, noting that the political instability once exported to destabilize others has now become an instrument of local division.

### High Stakes and Shifting Loyalties
Names once synonymous with unwavering unity under the UDA banner, including Oscar Sudi, Kipchumba Murkomen, and Jackson Mandago, are no longer operating in harmony. Public disagreements and political maneuvering have replaced the former displays of solidarity. The grassroots population is not merely observing these fractures; they are responding with growing hostility. As elite politicians prioritize regional supremacy, the internal cohesion of the ruling party continues to erode, forcing the administration to contend with a base that is increasingly disillusioned with the central leadership.

### Grassroots Reality Check
In areas such as Emurua Dikirr, the rhetoric emanating from Nairobi is meeting significant resistance. Constituents are openly questioning why political leaders are importing division into their own backyard while previous campaign promises remain unfulfilled. The era of blind loyalty is fading, replaced by a public that views the current political drama with growing cynicism. As those who built their careers on dividing others find themselves fractured by their own tactics, the electorate is opting to remain disengaged from the turmoil. The consensus among local residents is that the political architects of this instability are now trapped by the very conflicts they initiated.
