### The Policy Gap in Clerical Security

The vulnerability of clergy stems from a fundamental policy gap. Religious institutions often exist in a gray zone regarding property security. They function as private residences, yet they are public-facing entities that command high levels of community trust and, at times, significant financial inflows. 

Local law enforcement typically relies on standard community policing models that fail to account for the specific risk profiles of high-profile clergy members. When these leaders live on-site, they are expected to manage the security of their own environment without professional training or adequate infrastructure. While Nakuru County officials remain focused on initiatives like providing free uniforms for public ECDE learners, the safety of clergy who serve the spiritual needs of these same communities remains a secondary priority in local governance discussions.
