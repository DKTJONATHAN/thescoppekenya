The Social Health Authority (SHA) will cover the initial 24 hours of emergency medical care and associated ambulance costs for all citizens, Health Cabinet Secretary Aden Duale announced Thursday, April 2, 2026, in Nairobi. This initiative, part of SHA’s Emergency, Chronic and Critical Illness Fund, aims to significantly expand urgent care access.

Nationwide Emergency Healthcare Initiative Speaking at Avenue Hospital in Nairobi during the onboarding of the National Police Service and Kenya Prisons Service to the Public Officers Medical Scheme Fund, CS Duale detailed the rollout. The government is preparing to launch a nationwide ambulance dispatch service by June 2026. This system will enable individuals experiencing an emergency to contact a central dispatch center, which will then send the nearest ambulance to their location for transport to the closest hospital.

Streamlined Emergency Response The forthcoming dispatch center will ensure that an ambulance is sent promptly to any location, be it a home, office, or street, following an emergency call. The SHA will fully cover the cost of this ambulance transport, removing financial barriers for urgent patient evacuations. Mercy Mwangangi, SHA Chief Executive Officer, previously outlined this plan in September 2025, stating that the President was expected to launch the service by the end of that month. She indicated that SHA would reimburse ambulance providers Ksh4,500 for evacuations within 25 kilometers, with an additional Ksh75 per kilometer for longer distances. This service is poised to substantially improve emergency response times.

Elimination of Upfront Payments A critical aspect of the new plan is the directive for hospitals to admit emergency patients without demanding an upfront deposit. This measure is designed to remove immediate financial obstacles, ensuring that critical care is not delayed. Interior Cabinet Secretary Kipchumba Murkomen reiterated this, stating that emergency medical treatment is free in the country for 24 hours, applicable to situations like vehicular accidents or other life-threatening events.

Comprehensive 24-Hour Coverage Upon admission, all costs for the first 24 hours of treatment, including necessary procedures such as surgery, will be fully funded by the Social Health Authority. This comprehensive coverage underscores the administration's commitment to health transformation, ensuring that essential medical interventions are accessible during the most critical period of an emergency. The improved response framework, addressing challenges like congested urban pathways, is expected to enhance access to urgent care across the country.
