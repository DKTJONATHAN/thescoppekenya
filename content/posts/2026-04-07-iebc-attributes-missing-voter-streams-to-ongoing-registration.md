The Independent Electoral and Boundaries Commission (IEBC) has clarified why individuals verifying their voter status are not seeing their voting streams. This issue, observed today, April 7, 2026, is due to the ongoing nationwide voter registration exercise, with streams to be finalized upon its conclusion.

Polling Stream Clarification

Provisional Status of Voter Information The IEBC stated that error messages encountered by those verifying their voter details are a direct consequence of the continuous voter registration exercise across the nation. The commission explained that once this registration phase concludes, it will segment registration and polling centres into smaller polling stations, known as streams. Each stream will accommodate a maximum of 700 voters. The IEBC confirmed that the final register will detail individual-specific polling stations and corresponding stream numbers.

Public Concerns and Verification Challenges Earlier today, individuals voiced their concerns on social media platforms after attempting to view their voting streams and receiving error messages. Beyond the missing stream details, some users, particularly newly registered voters, reported difficulties in accessing and utilizing the online verification portal. Over the past weekend, presidential aspirant Reuben Kigame notably criticized the IEBC regarding his wife's missing voter information. The commission subsequently clarified that such discrepancies can only be rectified by visiting the respective constituency offices.

"Niko Kadi" Initiative Progress The IEBC continues to encourage public participation in voter registration through its "Niko Kadi" initiative, which has already facilitated the registration of over 250,000 new voters countrywide. This voter drive is unfolding against a backdrop of active political engagement across the country. For example, Member of Parliament Ndindi Nyoro recently opened his Kahuro office [Ndindi Nyoro Opens Kahuro Office [Admin Boycott]](https://zandani.co.ke/article/ndindi-nyoro-opens-kahuro-office-admin-boycott) - an event reportedly marked by an administrative boycott. In figures released on March 23, 2026, Nairobi leads the registration exercise with 49,055 new registrants, followed by Kiambu County with 20,404. These two counties collectively represent 27 percent of all new voters recorded thus far, indicating a stronger turnout in urban and peri-urban areas, attributed to higher population densities and broader access to voter awareness campaigns. Prior to these renewed mass voter registration efforts, the nation's total registered voter population stood at approximately 22.3 million. The IEBC aims to add more than 6 million new voters to this figure.
