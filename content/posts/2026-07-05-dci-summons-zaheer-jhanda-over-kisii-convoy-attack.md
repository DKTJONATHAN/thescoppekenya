## DCI Escalates Probe into Kisii Political Violence

The Directorate of Criminal Investigations has summoned Nyaribari Chache Member of Parliament Zaheer Jhanda to record a statement at the Kisii County DCI headquarters following a violent attack on a Linda Mwananchi convoy.

This summons marks a significant shift in how law enforcement is handling political volatility in the Gusii region. By targeting a sitting legislator, the DCI is signaling a departure from the traditional handling of local skirmishes, moving toward institutional accountability. The investigation centers on the chaotic events of July 3, when a convoy led by political figures was ambushed while en route to Keroka.

The incident resulted in extensive property damage and placed high-profile individuals in the line of fire. Among the vehicles struck by projectiles was an SUV associated with former Chief Justice David Maraga. The inclusion of the former judiciary head's vehicle in the crossfire has raised the stakes, transforming what might have been viewed as a standard political disagreement into a test of the rule of law.

Law enforcement officers have intensified their search for those responsible for the violence. Detectives confirmed today that the total number of arrests has risen to seven. These four additional suspects are expected to face charges related to incitement and destruction of property as investigators piece together the sequence of events that led to the blockade near Keumbu.

### Institutional Independence at Stake

The move to compel a member of the National Assembly to report to investigative authorities reflects a rigid application of the penal code. Legal experts observe that the summons places the legislative office under direct scrutiny, challenging the perception of political immunity during civil unrest. This proactive approach by the DCI aims to discourage the use of organized youth groups to disrupt political activities across the country.

Previous incidents of regional instability have often ended in dropped charges or political settlements. The current handling of the Keumbu attack serves as a direct counter-narrative to that history. The Inspector General of Police, Douglas Kanja, has maintained that the service will remain non-partisan in its pursuit of those who orchestrate violence, regardless of their political standing or legislative position.

### Political Friction and Local Stability

Reaction to the summons has been polarized, reflecting the deep divisions currently affecting the region. Local stakeholders have called for calm, emphasizing that the economic recovery of the area, which has faced recent climate-related infrastructure challenges similar to those seen in the [Nairobi Floods: 4 Dead and City Gridlocked by Heavy Rain](https://zandani.co.ke/article/nairobi-floods-death-toll-chaos), relies on stability. The disruption to the Linda Mwananchi convoy is seen by many residents as a deterrent to the freedom of movement required for political discourse.

Supporters of the investigative process argue that the summons is essential to clearing the air surrounding the events in Kisii. They contend that if the judicial system is to retain public confidence, it must treat every citizen, including MPs, with equal measure under the law. This sentiment is echoed by those who worry that failure to hold political leaders accountable now could set a dangerous precedent for future regional competition.

The coming days will determine the direction of the criminal case as investigators finalize their files. Observers will be monitoring the Kisii County DCI office to see if the legislator complies with the summons and what evidence authorities present to the court. The outcome of these proceedings will likely influence the enforcement of public order during political tours for the remainder of the year.
