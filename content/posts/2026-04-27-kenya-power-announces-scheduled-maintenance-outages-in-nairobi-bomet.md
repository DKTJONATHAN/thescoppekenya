Kenya Power announces scheduled maintenance outages in Nairo is central to this update for Kenyan readers.

Kenya Power has announced planned electricity interruptions for Monday, April 27, 2026, impacting prime areas across Nairobi, Bomet, and Nandi counties. These essential maintenance works, scheduled throughout the day, will temporarily dim the vibrancy of several bustling neighborhoods, including those frequented by Nairobi's social scene and key economic hubs, as the utility company undertakes critical upgrades.

## Kenya Power Outages Darken Popular Nairobi Haunts

Kenya Power's routine maintenance on Monday, April 27, 2026, will see several prominent locations in Nairobi without electricity, directly affecting businesses and residents alike. From 9:00 a.m. to 5:00 p.m., the lights will be out in various parts of Nairobi West, a locale known for its vibrant activity and essential services.

### Nairobi West's Glitzy Grind Halted

The power interruption in Nairobi West encompasses Msufu Road, Sumba Road, Naimei Road, and stretches of Mombasa Road, particularly near ICC Church. PAA Crescent, Kifaru Crescent, Swara Crescent, Ghandi Avenue, Kisauni Road, Muthaiti Avenue, and Muhoho Avenue are also on the list. This means key institutions such as Nairobi West Hospital, Hotel Rio, Summerdale Hotel, and the ABSA Bank Nairobi West branch will experience the outage. One can only imagine the scramble for power banks among those keen on maintaining their digital presence or running crucial errands, especially as Kenyans continue to question spending habits amid economic considerations.

### Madaraka's Bright Lights Dim

Further disruptions are expected around Kodi Road, Geta Road, and Masaba Road, near the iconic Nyayo National Stadium. Residents of Ole Sankale Road and Madaraka Estate should also prepare for the interruption. Serene Court, Foundation Court, Ramsey Court, Siwaka Estate, Strathmore University, Langata Technical and Vocational College, and Madaraka Primary School, along with their numerous customers, will also be affected. Many will be adjusting schedules for online classes or digital work, temporarily shifting their routines.

### Upcountry Unplugged: Bomet and Nandi Feel the Pinch

Beyond the capital, areas in Sotik, Bomet County, will also face power interruptions from 9:00 a.m. to 5:00 p.m. Affected locations include Gorgor, Gelegele, Abosi, Kapchumbe, Njiship Market, and Shartuka Market, including surrounding environs. Meanwhile, parts of Nandi County, specifically Kaimosi and Chepkumia, will experience outages from 10:00 a.m. to 5:00 p.m. Residents in Kiborgok, Yala, Chepsonoi, Kaimosi Tea Factory, Kaimosi Friends University, Chepkumia Tea Factory, Sirwo, and Tindinyo are advised to plan accordingly for the temporary lack of electricity. Kenya Power assures customers that supply will be restored once maintenance is complete.
