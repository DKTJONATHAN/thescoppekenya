The current negotiation table is built on a thirty-six-shilling deficit that neither side seems willing to close.

An operator running a 14-seater van across the Thika Superhighway now pays significantly more for every kilometer covered than they did six months ago. 

The government's proposed ten-shilling reduction is an insult to the sector's operational costs.
