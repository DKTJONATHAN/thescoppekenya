## Gachagua Cites Principles for Absence at Ng'eno's Burial Former Deputy President Rigathi Gachagua did not attend the burial of Emurua Dikirr Member of Parliament Johana Ng'eno, stating that his principles prevented him from sharing a platform with individuals he accuses of tormenting the late lawmaker during his lifetime. The funeral service, held in Emurua Dikirr, saw the attendance of several senior national leaders, including President William Ruto and Deputy President Kithure Kindiki.

Gachagua's Justification In a lengthy tribute, Gachagua described Ng'eno as a close friend, confidant, and political ally, mourning his death as the loss of a "fearless and outspoken leader." He praised Ng'eno's dedication to championing his community's interests despite facing political hostility and isolation. However, Gachagua explicitly stated his refusal to attend the burial, citing his abhorrence for the "hypocrisy" of Ng'eno's alleged tormentors. "I am abhorred by the hypocrisy of your tormentors. I have seen your tormentors who hunted you like an antelope shedding crocodile tears and shamelessly pretending to mourn your death," Gachagua stated. He added that his principles would not allow him to "sit with those who tormented and persecuted you in life as they pretend to love you in death."

Details of Ng'eno's Death and Prior Claims Johana Ng'eno died on February 28 when a private helicopter, registration 5Y-DSB, crashed in the Chepkieb area of Mosop, Nandi County, at approximately 4:45 PM. The crash claimed the lives of all six people on board, including Kenya Forest Service ranger Amos Kipngetich Rotich, photographer Nick Kosgei, teacher Robert Kipkoech Keter, Narok County government Protocol Officer Wycliffe Kiprotich Rono, and Captain George Were. A preliminary report released on March 1 by the National Police Service and Interior Principal Secretary Raymond Omollo indicated that the helicopter was flying at a very low altitude before it struck trees and crashed. Gachagua had previously addressed Ng'eno's death during a Church Service at AIPCA Nyandarua North Diocese on March 1. He made claims regarding threats allegedly used to coerce Ng'eno during his own impeachment process. Gachagua recounted Ng'eno visiting him at 2 a.m., distressed and carrying messages from a senior government official threatening dire consequences if he refused to sign the impeachment motion. Gachagua advised Ng'eno to sign, reasoning that President Ruto already had the necessary numbers and that resistance would endanger Ng'eno and his family. Ng'eno, according to Gachagua, signed the motion in tears. The late MP was among the 281 National Assembly members who voted to impeach Gachagua on October 8, 2024.

Funeral Attendees The funeral service for the late MP was attended by several prominent national figures. Among those present were President William Ruto, Deputy President Kithure Kindiki, Speaker of the National Assembly Moses Wetang'ula, Defense Cabinet Secretary Siopan Tuya, and Narok Governor Patrick Ole Ntutu, who is Ng'eno's father-in-law. Other senior national leaders were also in attendance.
