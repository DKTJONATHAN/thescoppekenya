## The Disparity in Relationship Expectations

### The Price of Existence
A prevailing sentiment has emerged suggesting that men are measured solely by their ability to provide, whereas women are often valued for their mere presence. This perceived double standard has sparked intense conversations in urban centers throughout the country. While many aspects of life in Kenya are undergoing significant structural shifts, the social expectations placed upon men remain rigid and unforgiving.

### Financial Productivity as a Merit Badge
Many men now argue that their worth is being unfairly commodified within the dating market. If an individual faces a temporary financial setback, he is frequently viewed as obsolete rather than a partner deserving of support. This pressure contrasts sharply with the social grace often afforded to women, whose inherent value is rarely dictated by their bank balance. This atmosphere of scrutiny reflects a broader trend where public opinion often demands high performance from men as a prerequisite for social acceptance.

### The Dating Market Fallout
This friction is fueling an environment where dating often feels like a business transaction rather than a genuine connection. The current climate is thick with skepticism as men openly question why they should bear the weight of unconditional financial responsibility when they rarely receive a similar level of emotional or economic support in return. As the cost of living continues to rise in 2026, the insistence on outdated gender roles is meeting stiff resistance, with more men choosing to opt out of relationships that they perceive as financial traps rather than equitable partnerships.
