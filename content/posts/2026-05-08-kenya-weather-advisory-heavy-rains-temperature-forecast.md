### What are the health risks of this 28-degree variance?

The transition between freezing night lows and sweltering daytime highs is a recipe for respiratory and physical distress. Staying warm is not just about comfort; it is a health requirement. Ensure that bedding is kept dry and that living areas are ventilated to avoid mold growth, which often accompanies heavy rainfall.

For those in the arid North and the humid Coast, the 37°C heatwave conditions bring risks of dehydration and heat exhaustion. Symptoms like dizziness or persistent headaches should not be ignored.
