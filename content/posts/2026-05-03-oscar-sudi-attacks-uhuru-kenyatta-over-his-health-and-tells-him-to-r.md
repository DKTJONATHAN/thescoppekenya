Political temperatures across Kenya intensified this week as Kapseret Member of Parliament Oscar Sudi reportedly urged former President Uhuru Kenyatta to withdraw from active politics, advocating he allow the current administration to govern. Sudi's May 2026 remarks add to an ongoing debate about Kenyatta's post-presidency influence.

## Oscar Sudi Attacks Uhuru Kenyatta Over His Health And Tells Him To Retire And Wait For Death

In a move that caught many by surprise, Oscar Sudi did not mince words when addressing the former Head of State. Speaking with a directness characteristic of his political style, Sudi publicly advised Uhuru Kenyatta to cease his involvement in current political affairs, suggesting it was time for him to withdraw from the public eye and allow the current leadership to govern. This sentiment echoes Sudi's previous statements from September 2025, when he explicitly told Kenyatta to "relax" and permit his "younger brother" (President William Ruto) to lead the country, adding, "You can't do anything else now. We supported you, and you went home. Let others lead.". The pronouncement has been widely debated, with some condemning its perceived insensitivity, while others view it as a raw reflection of ongoing political jostling.

### The Unpacking of Sudi's Unfiltered Counsel

Sudi's comments come amidst persistent discussions regarding the former President's continued presence and influence within the political landscape, nearly four years after relinquishing power. Indeed, as recently as May 1, 2026, Uhuru Kenyatta staunchly defended his right to remain politically active, citing his constitutional freedom and drawing parallels with former global leaders such as Barack Obama. Calls for Kenyatta to embrace a statesman's role and avoid partisan engagement have intensified from various leaders, including National Assembly Speaker Moses Wetang'ula. While the precise trigger for Sudi's latest remarks remains a subject of speculation, it is clear the Kapseret MP intended to deliver a potent message. His use of such stark language has not only drawn attention to Uhuru Kenyatta but also reignited discussions about political decorum and the boundaries of public discourse in Kenya. The political class is currently awash with various interpretations of what Sudi's true intentions might have been.

### The Public's Take on Political Jabs Amidst Daily Struggles

While politicians continue to trade high-stakes barbs, ordinary Kenyans often find themselves grappling with more immediate concerns. Public commentary around Sudi's remarks has been a mix of outrage, amusement, and weariness with endless political drama. Many Kenyans are more focused on the escalating cost of living, with recent reports highlighting how Nairobi Water and Sewer Bills jumped by Ksh23 and Ksh15 for low consumption households as new tariffs took effect, and Easy Coach announcing increased bus fares across major routes following fuel price hikes. Such economic realities often overshadow the theatrics unfolding in the political arena, creating a stark contrast between the concerns of the elite and the daily struggles of citizens.

### Uhuru's Silence and the Political Chessboard

As expected, former President Uhuru Kenyatta has yet to respond directly to Sudi's statements regarding his retirement. His silence, a familiar tactic in the face of political provocations, leaves room for endless speculation about his next move or whether he will choose to acknowledge the remarks at all. Regardless, Sudi's bold statements have undeniably added another layer of intrigue to Kenya's already complex political chessboard, ensuring that the former President's shadows continue to loom large over the current administration's activities.
