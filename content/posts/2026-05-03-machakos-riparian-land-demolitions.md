## Machakos Riparian Demolitions Target Mavoko Estates

Machakos County government has announced immediate demolitions targeting structures on riparian land in Mavoko and surrounding areas, including 360 Estate, Kicheko, and Kincar. This action follows recent sustained downpours and aims to restore blocked drainage channels, thereby reducing the risk of widespread flooding in the affected residential zones.

### County Takes Action

Mavoko Municipal Manager Josylyn Kauta confirmed that areas frequently hit by floods, such as 360 Estate, Kicheko, and Kincar, have reported water entering homes, damaged roads, and disrupted transport. The county noted a recent emergence of perimeter walls and housing units erected on riparian land during the dry season, obstructing waterways and causing flooding along key infrastructure.

In response, the Machakos County Government has reached an agreement with a developer to commence opening waterways immediately. Enforcement of the Machakos riparian demolitions will begin from the B-Energy petrol station, extending through Kicheko and Silanga. Affected property owners have been served notices and are expected to remove their structures before county authorities intervene.

### Widespread Flooding Concerns

The decision for these Machakos riparian demolitions comes as flooding continues to cause significant damage across Nairobi and its environs, with Machakos County bearing a substantial impact. Utawala residents have also faced severe flooding, resulting in impassable access roads and property losses from stormwater. Since late April, 18 flood-related deaths have been recorded, with the government identifying several areas in Tana River as potential flood-risk zones.
