Nairobi delegates of the Orange Democratic Movement (ODM) on Tuesday, March 17, 2026, endorsed EALA MP Winnie Odinga for the Deputy Party Leader position. This decision, made amid ongoing internal party wrangles, also saw her assume the mantle of Nairobi's number one party delegate.

ODM Leadership Endorsements

Odinga's New Party Role Winnie Odinga, daughter of the late former Prime Minister Raila Odinga, received backing for a significant leadership position within the party. This role is poised to influence major political decisions as younger members increasingly vie with the older generation. The delegates' meeting, overseen by Makadara MP and Nairobi branch chairperson George Aladwa, confirmed Odinga's ascension. She is expected to hold a seat on the National Executive Council (NEC), the Central Committee, and function as one of the Deputy Party Leaders. She officially assumes the title of ‘Nairobi Delegate Number One', taking over the mantle from her late father. ODM chairpersons from Nairobi’s 17 constituencies publicly affirmed their support for Odinga as the county’s delegate in the party’s national leadership.

Aladwa Endorsed for Secretary-General In the same session, Nairobi ODM delegates endorsed George Aladwa for the position of party secretary-general. This endorsement comes ahead of the forthcoming National Delegates Convention (NDC). The party's Secretary-General role is presently held by Busia Women Representative Catherine Omanyo in an acting capacity, following the disputed removal of Nairobi Senator Edwin Sifuna.

Shifting Political Alignments The endorsement of Winnie Odinga marks her first public alignment with the Oburu-Odinga-aligned faction, after previously appearing at odds with the camp. In January, during a rally in Kamkunji, Odinga publicly contradicted statements by party leader Oburu Odinga concerning members who wished to leave ODM. She insisted that no one should be removed, stating that the party belongs to the people. Odinga previously appeared to align with the younger generation within ODM, including a splinter faction allied to Linda Mwananchi, Sifuna, and Babu Owino. However, following a family meeting convened by her uncle Oburu in February, she has largely moderated her public statements and maintained a lower profile in party affairs.
