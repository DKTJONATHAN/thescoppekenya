## DCI Arrests Man for TikTok Currency Mutilation The Directorate of Criminal Investigations (DCI) has arrested a man seen on a viral TikTok video intentionally tearing Kenyan banknotes. Maximilian Motara was apprehended by the Banking Fraud Investigations Unit (BFIU) and is currently in custody, facing charges for wilfully defacing currency, an offence under Kenyan law.

DCI Confirms Arrest and Charges On Tuesday, March 10, 2026, the DCI confirmed the arrest of Maximilian Motara, who gained notoriety after sharing a 27-minute video on social media depicting him tearing bundles of Ksh100 notes. Detectives from the specialized Banking Fraud Investigations Unit (BFIU) conducted the operation, emphasizing the DCI's commitment to combating financial crimes and upholding the banking sector's integrity. Motara is presently in police custody and is undergoing processing for arraignment.

Legal Framework on Currency Integrity Kenyan law strictly prohibits the deliberate defacement or mutilation of currency notes. According to Section 367A of the Penal Code (Cap. 63), any individual found wilfully tearing, cutting, defacing, or otherwise mutilating currency notes commits a criminal offence. Violators face penalties of up to Ksh2,000, imprisonment for a term not exceeding three months, or both. This legal provision extends to any wilful destruction, including writing on, stapling, or folding notes in a manner that causes damage. The Central Bank of Kenya (CBK) is mandated with safeguarding the national currency. On February 2, 2026, the CBK issued an official warning against using Kenyan shilling banknotes for decorative purposes, such as cash bouquets, citing damage caused by folding, glueing, and stapling. Such practices compromise the integrity of the banknotes, rendering them unfit for circulation and causing harm to cash-handling equipment like ATM machines. The CBK also prohibits tampering with coins to diminish their weight, which constitutes a felony punishable by up to seven years in prison. The DCI's resolute action serves as a strong reminder of accountability for public conduct, particularly in the digital realm, a principle that applies across various aspects of public life and discourse, including discussions surrounding prominent personalities and their public image.
