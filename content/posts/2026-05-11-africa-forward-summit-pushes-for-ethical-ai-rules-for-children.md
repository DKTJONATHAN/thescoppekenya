## A Call for Safeguards in Kenya’s Digital Evolution

To the delegates and policymakers of the Africa Forward Summit, the polished brochures and high-level proclamations regarding artificial intelligence overlook a fundamental priority. Our children are not merely tomorrow’s consumers; they are navigating an unregulated digital frontier today, one where corporate profit frequently overrides their fundamental well-being.

### Bridging the Gap Between Policy and Practice

The discourse surrounding the UNESCO Recommendation on the Ethics of Artificial Intelligence and the objectives outlined in UNEP/EA.7/Res.9 carries weight in formal chambers. However, for families across Nairobi, Kisumu, and our growing rural connectivity hubs, the experience is vastly different. We observe our children consuming algorithmic content engineered to maximize engagement at the expense of genuine intellectual enrichment. These platforms prioritize data extraction over the healthy development of young minds.

While leadership emphasizes environmental sustainability in technology, we remain largely silent on the cognitive pollution encroaching upon our classrooms. There is a profound dissonance between the rhetoric of rapid digital expansion and the absence of meaningful digital literacy programs. Educators are currently expected to weave technology into their curricula without a secure framework, forced to manage the consequences of AI-driven misinformation and predatory programming independently. Much like the state’s reactive efforts to repair essential infrastructure, such as the Iten-Kabarnet road following recent landslides, we are bracing for a systemic failure in our children’s digital safety before committing to the necessary protective measures.

### The Dangers of an Unregulated Digital Space

Protecting our youth cannot remain an auxiliary pillar of AI policy. The current regulatory void permits global corporations to deploy aggressive features within our market, often without the oversight or safety standards they are required to implement in the Global North. This lack of scrutiny creates instability, reminiscent of the economic anxiety triggered by recent mass layoffs and the dissolution of 117 corporate entities. By prioritizing the rapid adoption of AI without establishing a robust ethical framework, we are sacrificing the long-term digital health of our youth for fleeting technological prestige.

Furthermore, our youth are being conditioned by machines that reflect social biases we have spent decades working to dismantle. Without a stringent mandate requiring transparency in how AI influences juvenile behavior, we are essentially delegating the guidance of our nation’s children to foreign-developed code. True digital literacy extends far beyond the technical ability to operate a device. It requires a deep understanding of the ethical implications of the systems beneath those screens, a subject increasingly relevant in modern critiques of digital combat ethics.

### The Urgency of This Moment

Failing to anchor our AI development in child-centric ethics risks the manipulation of an entire generation. We are currently pouring the foundation of our digital future. Should we fail to integrate protections for children at this stage, we will inevitably face a costly and complex demolition process in the years to come. 

### A Mandate for Oversight

Pay close attention to the forthcoming legislative sessions in the National Assembly. Seek out specific line-item funding for classroom-based AI safety initiatives and binding requirements that force technology firms to demonstrate the safety of their platforms for users under the age of eighteen. Do not be satisfied with vague commitments to ethical standards. Demand clear, enforceable mechanisms that hold corporations legally accountable for the software they release into our schools and homes.

To the architects currently designing this future, I pose a single question: if your policy is incapable of shielding a child from predatory algorithms, on what grounds can we trust it to govern our society?

The requirement is clear. We need the Africa Forward Summit to transcend empty talking points. We demand a mandate requiring every AI product launched in Kenya to undergo a comprehensive child safety impact assessment. We call for the funding of a national curriculum designed to teach children that the machine is a tool to be mastered, not a master to be followed. Progress must be measured not by the speed of adoption, but by the safety of our children.

Sincerely, 

A Concerned Citizen
