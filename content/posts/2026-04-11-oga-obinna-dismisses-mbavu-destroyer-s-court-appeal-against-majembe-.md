Promoter Oga Obinna has firmly rejected boxer Mbavu Destroyer’s formal appeal against Majembe's recent victory, filed with the Sports Disputes Tribunal this week. Obinna urged Destroyer to abandon the lengthy legal challenge, emphasizing that true resolution for the disputed match lies solely within the boxing ring.

## Oga Obinna Dismisses Mbavu Destroyer's Court Appeal

### Promoter Advocates for In-Ring Resolution

Oga Obinna reiterated his stance that Mbavu Destroyer's formal appeal to the Sports Disputes Tribunal is an unnecessary drain on resources, urging the boxer to seek a rematch in the ring. Obinna consistently champions the spirit of fair competition, stating that challenges to a fighter’s triumph, such as Majembe's controversial victory on April 4, 2026, should be settled inside the squared circle. He believes prolonged legal disputes detract from the integrity and excitement of the sport, further highlighting another major boxing event he has scheduled for July 4, 2026. Mbavu Destroyer had filed his appeal seeking to annul the fight's result, be declared the winner, or secure an immediate rematch, with the case certified as urgent and set for mention on April 15, 2026.

### Legal Arenas and Sporting Showdowns

The promoter's dismissal echoes broader sentiments regarding the appropriate forums for dispute resolution across different sectors in Kenya. Just as the Political Parties Tribunal recently dismissed a case challenging Edwin Sifuna's removal as ODM Secretary General, Obinna suggests that sports-related disagreements are best handled within the sporting framework rather than through extensive legal battles. He firmly encourages Mbavu Destroyer to channel his energy into rigorous training for a decisive encounter, rather than pursuing what he deems a futile legal battle against Majembe's hard-won victory in the highly publicized "Vurugu Night Fight". The Sports Disputes Tribunal itself continues to handle a range of cases, from anti-doping violations to appeals against decisions made by national sports organizations, underscoring its role in maintaining order within Kenya's sports landscape.
