Veteran journalist Jeff Koinange revealed details of his highly anticipated new podcast on Monday, April 6, confirming acclaimed radio presenter Maina Kageni as a featured guest. A teaser video shared on social media offered a preview of the show's relaxed, unrestricted interview style, sparking significant public interest.

## Podcast Format and Initial Guests Confirmed

Koinange’s new podcast, initially hinted at a month prior, has now provided a clearer vision for his audience. The video trailer, posted on Monday, April 6, showcased a departure from his usual news desk demeanor. The podcast is set to adopt a more relaxed and open tone, featuring explicit questions less constrained by conventional media norms. Viewers observed Koinange casually interviewing guests while smoking his signature Cuban cigar.

Among the first personalities confirmed for the podcast is veteran radio presenter Maina Kageni, widely recognized for his Morning Drive show on Classic 105. Kageni's inclusion was anticipated, given Koinange's earlier hints about featuring renowned figures and their established friendship within the media industry.

### Unfiltered Conversations and Personal Revelations

The teaser provided glimpses into the unreserved nature of the discussions. Jeff Koinange is seen inquiring about Maina Kageni’s prolonged single status and whether he identifies with the LGBTQ community, a question Kageni reportedly met with laughter and an unfazed demeanor. Another segment features Koinange revealing a personal detail to Kageni about recently discovering he has a 30-year-old daughter, a revelation that visibly surprised Kageni.

Another prominent media personality, Cess Mutungi, who shares a history with the Citizen TV anchor, is also slated to appear as a guest on the podcast.

### Public Reception and Industry Trends

The unveiling of Koinange’s podcast has generated considerable excitement among the public. Social media comments indicate an eagerness to experience the new offering, particularly as it enters a media landscape increasingly populated with similar content. One fan commented, “Jeff's transition from ordinary to extra ordinaire is so good to watch.” Another admirer from Ghana expressed, “It's my prayer, and wish to meet you one day. You're an idol in the media fraternity. The best journalist of our time (21st century) in Africa and beyond. You're a legend.”

Koinange joins a growing list of media personalities entering the podcasting arena, including Alex Mwakideu, Oga Obina, Caroline Mutuku, and Sheila Kwamboka, also known as Kwambox. This trend reflects an evolving digital media environment. The release of the teaser suggests the podcast’s official debut may occur sooner than anticipated, though an exact air date remains unconfirmed.
