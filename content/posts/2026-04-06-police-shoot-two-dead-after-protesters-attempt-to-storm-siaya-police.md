Two individuals were shot dead by police in Gem-Ramula, Siaya County, on Monday, April 6, after a protest against an alleged eviction from a lucrative gold-mining site escalated into an attempt to storm a local police post. Authorities responded with live ammunition to disperse the agitated crowd.

Protests Turn Deadly in Siaya

Confrontation Over Mining Site The fatal incident occurred after a group of irate individuals protested an alleged eviction from a gold-mining site. Area police boss Charles Wafula confirmed the incident, stating that the protestors were determined to curb what they described as an illegal resettlement of a mining company and attempted to storm the Ramula Police Post. Wafula revealed that the individuals had organized a demonstration but did not notify police.

Police Account and Damage According to Wafula, police officers moved in to contain the situation, but the group began attacking both officers and the Ramula Police Post. Protesters also damaged several crucial pieces of infrastructure at the police post, including police vehicles. In the wake of the melee, a tractor, which had been parked near the police post, was torched as residents accused authorities of ignoring their calls to have the mining company quelled. Police resorted to live ammunition after feeling the matter could get out of hand, leading to the shooting deaths of two youths. The rest of the crowd subsequently dispersed in panic.

Official Condemnation and Prior Incidents Siaya County Commissioner Norbert Komora condemned the incident, subtly faulting the protesters as he blamed unnamed elements for inciting youth to violence. Komora remarked, "Last year, they did the same at the police did not act," adding that such incidents cannot be allowed to continue. This incident adds to recent local challenges, with Za Ndani reporting on other pressing community concerns such as [Several people feared trapped after building collapses in Shauri Moyo](https://zandani.co.ke/article/several-people-feared-trapped-after-building-collapses-in-shauri-moy).

History of Police Post Storming Incidents of ordinary citizens storming police stations with fatal repercussions are not uncommon. At the height of Gen Z protests in June 2025, several protesters in Matuu were shot as they attempted to storm Matuu Police Station. Witnesses at the time reported that the group of protesters embarked on a vandalism spree, leading to police firing live bullets to attempt to scare them off.
