The National Transport and Safety Authority (NTSA) has clarified the distinctive KTW registration prefix for three-wheeler vehicles, commonly known as tuk-tuks, which are prominent across urban centers like Nairobi, Mombasa, and Kisumu. This unique numbering system ensures precise identification and categorisation within Kenya's growing transport sector in 2026.

Explanation of the KTW Prefix

Categorisation and Identification Tuk-tuks are officially classified by the NTSA under the three-wheeler motorcycle category. The KTW prefix is specifically allocated to these vehicles, distinguishing them from private cars, buses, trucks, and standard motorcycles in official registration records. This measure aims to maintain order and facilitate easy identification among the millions of vehicles operating on the country's roads daily.

Decoding the Prefix Each letter within the KTW prefix carries a specific meaning, aiding in the classification of these vehicles. 'K' represents Kenya, 'T' denotes Three, and 'W' stands for Wheels. This systematic coding helps to clearly identify and separate this transport mode from other conventional vehicles on the roads.

Extended Registration Series Unlike private cars that typically use three-letter prefixes such as KAA or KCA, or motorcycles that begin with letters like KM, tuk-tuks adopt a four-letter sequence such as KTWA or KTWB. This extended format helps to significantly prolong the lifespan of the country's standard vehicle registration series, according to NTSA.

Theft Prevention Measures The NTSA also implemented the distinct KTW prefix as a strategy to curb vehicle theft. Previously, there were instances where number plates were removed from tuk-tuks and illicitly attached to motorcycles or cars, and vice-versa, complicating identification and recovery efforts. The unique prefix makes such illicit transfers more difficult to conceal. This proactive stance by NTSA complements broader efforts to address illicit activities and maintain public order, drawing parallels with investigations by the Directorate of Criminal Investigations into political influence and potential arming of followers. Number Plate Colours and Meanings The colour of a tuk-tuk's number plate also conveys specific information regarding its purpose, ownership, and registration type, as designated by NTSA. Standard tuk-tuks, predominantly used for commercial purposes such as public transportation, feature yellow number plates at the rear and white plates at the front, both with black lettering. Conversely, green number plates, often starting with the prefix KD, are designated for garage use. These are typically used for temporary identification when a new tuk-tuk is being moved from a showroom to a customer or for test drives. In a significant update for 2026, green plates have also been designated as "New Generation Green Plates" for electric vehicles, including electric tuk-tuks. This policy aims to signify their zero-emission status, aligning with the country's commitment to sustainable transport. The official launch of these green number plates for electric vehicles occurred on February 3, 2026.
