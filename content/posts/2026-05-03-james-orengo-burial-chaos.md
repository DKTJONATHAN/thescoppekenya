## James Orengo Burial Chaos Mars Seme Funeral

Siaya governor James Orengo broke his silence Saturday, April 2, following his departure from the funeral of Seme MP James Nyikal's father in Kisumu County. Orengo accused the state of orchestrating the widespread James Orengo burial chaos that disrupted the solemn occasion, which saw rival political factions clash at the event.

### Governor Alleges State Orchestration

Governor Orengo released a statement Saturday evening, claiming the state was behind the unrest that led to the early conclusion of the funeral service. He described the incident as "deeply troubling" and a "national disgrace," noting that clergy were forced to cut the service short, calling for an immediate burial without speeches to restore order and dignity.

“It is both shameful and a national disgrace that a solemn send-off could be disrupted… The situation became so tense that the clergy had to cut the service short, calling for an immediate burial without speeches to preserve order and dignity,” Orengo stated.

### Linda Mwananchi Movement Unbowed

While apologizing to mourners, Orengo, a key figure in the Linda Mwananchi movement, affirmed that his camp would not be intimidated. He declared that no amount of intimidation, tear gas, "hired chaos," or desperation would silence the movement. The incident began when a group of youths, reportedly ODM ultras opposed to the Linda Mwananchi faction, heckled and confronted Orengo at Ridore Primary School, prompting his departure.

During the escalating tensions, Kisumu governor Anyang’ Nyong’o intervened to calm the situation. Seme MP James Nyikal also appealed for calm from both parties. ### Orengo Denies Being Chased

Contrary to initial reports, Governor Orengo denied being chased from the venue. He clarified that his departure was out of respect for the occasion, as the presiding Bishop and Seme MP James Nyikal had already left to proceed with the burial. Orengo maintained that his sole purpose was to offer condolences and solidarity with the grieving family.

Among other dignitaries present at the event were Kisumu Woman Representative Ruth Odinga and Homa Bay governor Gladys Wanga. Both avoided commenting on the tensions, choosing instead to express their condolences for the deceased on their social media platforms.
