The Directorate of Criminal Investigations (DCI) has warned former Deputy President Rigathi Gachagua over "false and malicious" allegations he made on Sunday, April 5. Gachagua claimed the DCI and President William Ruto were involved in a Ksh500 million recovery linked to a Ksh4 billion substandard fuel scandal, prompting the DCI to launch an investigation into his remarks.

DCI Rejects Gachagua's Fuel Scandal Allegations

Gachagua's Claims Against State Officials Speaking at AIPCA Gakoe Church in Gatundu North, Kiambu County, on Sunday, April 5, Rigathi Gachagua alleged that a Ksh4 billion fuel scandal, involving the importation of substandard fuel, was a "deal gone sour". He claimed that former Principal Secretary Mohamed Liban, former Kenya Pipeline Company Managing Director Joe Sang, and former Energy and Petroleum Regulatory Authority Director General Daniel Kiptoo Bargoria were being "punished for attempting to go behind the government’s back". Gachagua further alleged that Ksh500 million recovered from the officials' homes, purportedly proceeds from the scandal, had been surrendered directly to the President.

DCI Initiates Legal Action Review The DCI swiftly dismissed Gachagua's claims, describing them as "false, unfounded, and malicious". The agency stated that his assertions regarding recoveries and exhibits in the ongoing probe "lack any factual or evidentiary basis" and are designed to erode public trust in law enforcement. Maintaining its professional integrity, the DCI announced it is actively reviewing Gachagua's full utterances, particularly those made in the Gikuyu language. This review aims to assess if the statements constitute offenses under the National Cohesion and Integration Act or other provisions of the Penal Code, including hate speech, ethnic contempt, and incitement, with a view to taking legal action.

Government Response and Public Pressure The DCI's warning follows the April 2 arrest of Petroleum Permanent Secretary Liban Mohamed, Kenya Pipeline Company (KPC) Managing Director Joe Sang, and Energy Petroleum Regulatory Authority (EPRA) Director General Daniel Kiptoo. These officials face allegations of procuring Ksh4 billion in substandard fuel and manipulating fuel stock data to justify emergency purchases outside official government-to-government contracts, with the flagged fuel reportedly brought in at significantly inflated prices. President Ruto, speaking at a church service in Kilgoris on April 5, condemned side deals and reaffirmed his administration's commitment to fighting cartels infiltrating the economy. Meanwhile, public and political pressure continues to mount, with calls for the immediate resignation of Energy and Petroleum Cabinet Secretary Opiyo Wandayi, as many believe a scheme of such magnitude could not have occurred without his knowledge. As national issues, including essential services, continue to face challenges, for instance, Nairobi and neighboring counties are set to face a five-day water supply disruption, the public remains keenly observant of government accountability.
