## Rebuilding Trust: Adan Mohamed’s Digital Mandate at the KRA

Adan Mohamed has officially taken the helm as Commissioner General of the Kenya Revenue Authority. While government officials focus on his ability to meet ambitious revenue targets, the public has a different priority. Small business owners are watching to see if this leadership transition marks the end of the technical breakdowns that have plagued the agency’s digital platforms for years. 

Humphrey Wattanga has moved on to a diplomatic role in Canada, leaving behind an agency that expects taxpayers to operate within a digital environment that is often unstable. For the average Kenyan entrepreneur, the efficiency of iTax and eTIMS matters more than headline collection figures. When these platforms crash during tax filing windows, the burden falls on the user, who risks penalties despite their best efforts to comply. The real test for the new Commissioner General is whether he will view the KRA’s digital infrastructure as a vital public service rather than a secondary concern.

### The Cost of Digital Instability

Tax compliance has become an unpredictable ordeal. Whether a user is attempting to generate a mandatory electronic receipt via eTIMS or filing an annual return on iTax, frequent system outages are a recurring hurdle. This is not merely an inconvenience; it is a structural failure that creates a hidden tax on private business.

When a portal is inaccessible, a business owner cannot generate the legally required documentation to move goods or finalize sales. This forces entrepreneurs to pause their operations, creating a compliance gap triggered by the agency’s own technical negligence. If Mohamed wants to increase national revenue, he must first address the reliability of the tools he provides. An aggressive focus on collection is counterproductive if the foundational systems cannot handle the load. Relying on high-pressure enforcement while providing faulty tools only serves to alienate taxpayers and damage the relationship between the state and the private sector.

### Beyond Trendy Features

There is a growing temptation for public agencies to focus on flashy technological upgrades, such as AI integration, while ignoring the core maintenance of their existing networks. However, Kenyans do not need more complex software that drains their processing power. They need platforms that actually function.

Mohamed enters the role with a background in strategic technical implementation. This suggests a potential departure from the purely administrative focus of his predecessors. His primary challenge is to shift the agency’s perspective. He must transition the KRA from a culture of rigid enforcement to one of service enablement. Instead of pushing buggy updates that cause widespread crashes during peak hours, the focus should be on server uptime, intuitive user interfaces, and responsive support. Treating 19 million registered taxpayers as valued customers rather than just data points is the only way to modernize the agency.

### Why System Reliability Is an Economic Necessity

The Kenyan economy is currently under significant strain, and small businesses are struggling to stay afloat. When the KRA’s digital tools fail, the agency actively hinders the very economic activity it relies upon to fund the national budget. Mohamed’s success will not be measured by the total billions gathered during his tenure, but by his success in reducing the administrative friction that prevents taxpayers from filing their returns on time.

### Future Benchmarks for the Commissioner General

Observers should monitor KRA system notifications closely over the coming quarter. Meaningful progress will look like substantial investments in server architecture to prevent peak-hour crashes. Furthermore, the agency needs to implement a transparent policy that automatically waives penalties for taxpayers during periods of verified system downtime. 

If the KRA continues to demand strict compliance while keeping its own digital doors locked, the current level of taxpayer resentment will only deepen. It does not matter how many leadership changes occur if the operational issues remain unaddressed. The true indicator of success for Adan Mohamed will be his willingness to prioritize the user experience of the Kenyan taxpayer before chasing the next phase of revenue growth.
