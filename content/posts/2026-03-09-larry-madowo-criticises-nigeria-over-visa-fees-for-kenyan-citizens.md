## Kenyan Journalist Larry Madowo Challenges Nigerian Visa Policy CNN journalist Larry Madowo has openly criticized Nigeria's visa fees for Kenyan citizens, citing a significant disparity with Kenya's visa-free policy for most African nations. His complaint, made on Monday, March 9, 2026, detailed charges exceeding Ksh 10,000 for a single-entry visa, sparking public debate.

Visa Fee Disparity Highlights Larry Madowo voiced his concerns on social media regarding the financial burden placed on Kenyan passport holders seeking to visit Nigeria. He reported being charged Ksh 10,332 (80 US dollars) for a single-entry Nigerian visa. Madowo further detailed that the visa itself costs approximately Ksh 3,200 (25 US dollars), but additional charges include Ksh 6,457 (50 US dollars) for online application and an extra Ksh 645 (5 US dollars) for the payment provider. These layered fees significantly inflate the overall cost. He also raised concerns that applicants for multiple-entry visas are often issued single-entry visas instead. Such hidden charges underscore broader concerns about online transparency and consumer protection, echoing warnings like those from KURA about fake job scams.

Kenya's Open Borders Versus Nigeria's Restrictions The journalist's criticism stems from a notable imbalance in travel policies between the two nations. In July 2025, President William Ruto expanded Kenya's visa-free policy, allowing citizens from nearly all African countries, including Nigeria, to enter Kenya without requiring traditional visas. This initiative aimed to boost tourism, trade, and regional integration across the continent. Currently, all African countries can visit Kenya visa-free, with the only exceptions being Somalia and Libya. In contrast, Nigeria's visa framework requires citizens from most countries to obtain a visa prior to travel. Exceptions are made for nationals of the Economic Community of West African States (ECOWAS) member states, who enjoy visa-free movement, and a select few African countries outside the bloc, specifically Angola and Ethiopia, which have visa abolition agreements with Nigeria.

Calls for Reciprocity Madowo's social media post ignited an online discussion, with many participants calling for Kenya to implement reciprocal visa fees. The public debate urged Kenya to impose similar charges on African countries that continue to require Kenyans to pay for visas when visiting their territories.
