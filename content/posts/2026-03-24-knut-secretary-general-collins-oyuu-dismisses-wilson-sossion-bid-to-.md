Kenya National Union of Teachers (KNUT) Secretary General Collins Oyuu today, Tuesday, March 24, 2026, dismissed former SG Wilson Sossion's intent to contest the upcoming April 3 union elections. Oyuu stated that KNUT's constitution prohibits former members from reclaiming leadership positions, urging Sossion to cease external interference in union affairs.

KNUT Leadership Bid Faces Constitutional Challenge

Sossion Declares Intent to Reclaim Position Wilson Sossion revealed his desire to reclaim his position as the KNUT Secretary General on Tuesday, March 24, during an interview with Radio Generation. Sossion, who led the union between 2013 and 2021, asserted that his return would reinvigorate the union's vibrancy. He likened his comeback to Moses, stating he has "heard the cries of my teachers in Egypt" and will return. Sossion also challenged Oyuu to retire, citing age as a disqualifying factor for holding office and advocating for leadership by individuals active in the teaching profession.

Oyuu Dismisses Bid on Constitutional Grounds Hours after Sossion's declaration, Collins Oyuu addressed the press, dismissing Sossion's bid as unconstitutional. Oyuu, elected in June 2021 for a five-year term, emphasized that the KNUT constitution does not permit former members to "recycle" themselves and reclaim seats. "I'm not even aware if he can vie or not. The constitution of KNUT does not allow that, and we will follow the constitution to the letter," Oyuu stated, adding, "If you are not a member, please talk from outside the fence."

Eligibility and Age Become Key Contentious Points The dispute has intensified with both parties raising questions of eligibility. Oyuu reiterated that Sossion is no longer a KNUT member and should refrain from meddling in the union's affairs. Conversely, Sossion maintained that Oyuu is past retirement age and should vacate office, arguing that retirees cannot effectively represent active teachers. Oyuu, however, dismissed claims about his own age eligibility, advising critics to "go read the constitution, it is a 29-page document, talk to it." The upcoming union elections on April 3 are expected to be closely watched as these constitutional and eligibility debates unfold.
