### The High Cost of Bureaucratic Hesitation

Beyond the immediate tactical fallout, the Premier League faces severe reputational risks. The competition markets itself as the world’s most intense, fast-paced football product, yet fans are increasingly subject to long stretches of dead air. 

VAR was designed to rectify clear and obvious errors. However, it has evolved into a mechanism for second-guessing minor physical interactions within the six-yard box. The intervention regarding the challenge on David Raya reveals a deeper malaise: a lack of confidence in the officials on the pitch to make real-time, decisive judgments. When the process becomes more prominent than the players, the governing bodies have failed their primary responsibility to protect the flow of the 90 minutes.

Instead of officiating the game, the current protocol acts as a bottleneck. Like traffic gridlock stalling a high-speed transit route, these extended reviews prevent the game from reaching its natural conclusion.
