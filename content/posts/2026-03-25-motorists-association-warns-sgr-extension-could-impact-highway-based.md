The Motorists Association of Kenya (MAK) issued a stark warning on Wednesday, March 25, 2026, regarding the ongoing construction of the Naivasha-Kisumu-Malaba railway. The association argues that the Standard Gauge Railway (SGR) extension poses a significant threat to the nation's highway-based economy, potentially rendering current road infrastructure upgrades unnecessary.

## SGR Extension Threatens Highway Economy

The Motorists Association of Kenya contends that the operationalization of the Naivasha-Kisumu-Malaba railway is poised to absorb the majority of transit cargo currently transported by road along the Northern Corridor. This shift in freight movement from highways to rail, according to MAK, will weaken the economy, particularly for businesses and communities reliant on long-distance road transport.

### Economic Disruptions Anticipated

Towns that have historically flourished due to transit traffic, including Mai Mahiu, Naivasha, Nakuru, Eldoret, and Webuye, could experience a sharp economic decline if long-haul trucking activity diminishes. MAK stated that once fully operational, the railway will absorb almost all transit cargo, causing towns such as Mariakani, Mazeras, Samburu, Makinon, Mtito Makindu, Emali, Mai Mahiu, Naivasha, Kikopee, Nakuru, Mau Summit, Eldoret, Webuye, and Malaba to "gradually die a natural death" and become "ghost towns." This anticipated economic disruption underscores broader concerns about regional economic stability, a factor that various businesses carefully consider. For example, Carbacid's H1 2026 earnings were positively influenced by strategic investment gains.

### Questioning Road Infrastructure Investment

The lobby group also questioned the rationale behind planned investments in road expansion projects, specifically highlighting the Rironi-Mau Summit dual carriageway. This project is currently under construction and slated for completion by June 2027. MAK argues that the highway could become underutilised if the railway establishes itself as the dominant mode of cargo transport. The association stated, "Investing billions in expanding highways while simultaneously developing a parallel railway corridor raises serious concerns about duplication and value for money."

### Strategic Risks and Foreign Involvement

Beyond economic concerns, MAK pointed to emerging strategic risks associated with foreign involvement in major infrastructure projects. The association cited the roles of foreign firms, particularly those from China, in developing key transport corridors. The motorists warned that increased reliance on externally financed and executed projects could compromise Kenya's control over its vital infrastructure.

President William Ruto officially launched construction of the Standard Gauge Railway from Naivasha to Kisumu on Thursday, March 19, 2026, with a final extension planned to Malaba. The new railway line will be developed in two phases: the Naivasha-Kisumu section, covering 264 kilometres, and the Kisumu-Malaba stretch, extending 107 kilometres. This brings the total length to over 370 kilometres at an estimated cost of Ksh 500 billion. Once complete by June 2027, the SGR Phase 2B will traverse nine counties and is expected to enhance connectivity across the Great Rift Valley, Nyanza, and Western Kenya regions.
