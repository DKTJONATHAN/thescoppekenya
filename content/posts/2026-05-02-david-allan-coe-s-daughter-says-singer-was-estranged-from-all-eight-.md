## David Allan Coe's Family Rift Revealed After His Passing

The passing of legendary country musician David Allan Coe at 86 on Wednesday, April 29, 2026, has uncovered deeply personal family struggles. His daughter, Tanya Montana Coe, has shared that the controversial artist was estranged from all eight of his children, revealing a poignant distance that shadowed his final years.

### A Daughter's Painful Discovery

Tanya Montana Coe, a musician herself, made headlines this week after expressing on social media that she was not informed of her father's death nor given a chance to say a final goodbye. Speaking to Entertainment Weekly, she elaborated on the heartbreaking reality of their distant relationship. "The last time I 'spoke' with him was when I was texting with him when he was in the hospital after the car accident" in 2013, she recalled, unsure if it was truly him on the other end. This stark confession paints a picture of a family longing for connection with a man known for his iconic hits like "The Ride" and "Take This Job and Shove It," but also for his often provocative and controversial lyrics.

### The Web of Estrangement

Tanya emphasized that the estrangement was not unique to her. "None of us eight children had access to him," she shared, highlighting a collective family experience. She and her sister Carla even conducted wellness checks on him in the past 18 months, attempting to bridge the divide. Despite the distance, Tanya maintains a deep love and pride for her father's artistic achievements, acknowledging his difficult childhood and hoping he found peace in the end. She later clarified on Facebook that the "estrangement" was not her choice, but rather her father's decision to stop reaching out, making it impossible for her to connect with him. His son, Tyler Mahan Coe, also confirmed the long-standing estrangement, revealing he hadn't spoken to his father in over a decade.

### Voices from His Inner Circle

David Allan Coe's former manager, Ken Madson, corroborated the family's account, stating Coe was already estranged from his children when they began working together. Madson, who also lost touch with the singer, extended his sympathy to the family. Booking agent David Wade confirmed Coe's death on Thursday, April 30, 2026, remembering him as a "complicated man [and] outlaw, and a great songwriter, singer, and showman". The consensus from those closest to him paints a consistent portrait of an artist whose public persona was as complex as his private life, leaving behind a legacy both musically rich and personally intricate.
