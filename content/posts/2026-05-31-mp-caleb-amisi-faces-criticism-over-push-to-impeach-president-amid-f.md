Member of Parliament Caleb Amisi has launched a formal campaign seeking to impeach the President during a period of widespread public unrest. As protests intensify over the 2024 Finance Bill, critics argue this legislative maneuver serves as a calculated diversion from fiscal policy.

## The Impeachment Theatre Performance

Critics are dismissing the calls for legal volunteers and public mobilization as a well-timed illusion. While the public remains rightfully agitated over looming tax burdens, the sudden push for presidential removal appears to be a strategic distraction. It serves to shift the national conversation away from the specific details of the budget and onto the high-stakes drama of leadership change.

### A Masterclass in Political Opportunism

The move suggests that some political figures are more interested in chasing temporary influence than in mounting a genuine legislative opposition. By presenting a near-impossible constitutional hurdle as a simple community project, these officials are effectively leading the public into a blind alley. This confusion obscures the individuals responsible for legislative decision-making, leaving the average citizen to focus on performative politics while their economic interests are sidelined.

### The Quiet Passage of the Finance Bill

While citizens focus on the noise emanating from the legislature, the Finance Bill continues its steady progression toward becoming law. Experienced observers warn that the public should not be misled by the theatrics of impeachment bids. History indicates that when political circuses command the headlines, the legislative process often advances without the necessary scrutiny. This remains a stark reminder of the cynical games played in Nairobi, where constituents must look past flashy distractions to see the harsh economic realities affecting their finances.

### Illusion Versus Economic Reality

Despite recent headlines regarding fluctuations in currency values, the common person feels no financial relief at the grocery store. The push for impeachment acts as a convenient smokescreen, ensuring that those in power face minimal resistance while they finalize tax hikes. Kenyans are currently being asked to support a political contest that is clearly choreographed to ensure the legislative outcome remains in favor of the governing body. When the impeachment discussion eventually fades, the Finance Bill will likely remain, ready to impose additional costs on the populace.
