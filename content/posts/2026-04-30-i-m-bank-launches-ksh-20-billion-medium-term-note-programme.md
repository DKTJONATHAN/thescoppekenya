## Kenyan Corporate Bonds Surge: I&M Bank Launches KSh 20 Billion Note Programme

I&M Bank Limited has initiated a KSh 20.00 billion medium-term note program, with KSh 10.00 billion sought in its initial public offer. This strategic move capitalizes on Kenya's currently booming corporate bond market, driven by falling interest rates and robust investor demand, aiming to bolster the bank's lending capacity and capital strength.

### I&M Bank's Strategic Note Programme

I&M Bank Limited has publicly launched an offer for fixed-rate Medium-Term Notes, with the first tranche targeting KSh 10.00 billion under a broader KSh 20.00 billion program. The proceeds are designated to support onward lending activities, enhance the bank's funding resilience, and strengthen its capital position, aligning with its strategic priorities. The bank clarified that the funds will specifically contribute to long-term growth and Tier II capital strengthening. These notes are structured as unsecured subordinated obligations, holding an equal ranking with other subordinated creditors but junior to depositors and general creditors.

### Dynamic Kenyan Debt Market Conditions

The launch arrives amidst significant activity within Kenya's debt capital markets. The Central Bank of Kenya has reduced its benchmark rate ten times since August 2024, culminating in a cumulative 425 basis points reduction to 8.75%. This aggressive monetary policy has compressed funding costs across the market, subsequently attracting a considerable queue of corporate issuers. In February 2026, average commercial bank lending rates stood at 14.78%, while deposit rates averaged 7.00%. This differential creates a compelling yield window, rendering corporate paper particularly attractive to both institutional and retail investors.

### Recent Corporate Bond Momentum

The current cycle of corporate bond issuances commenced in November 2025 with EABL, which successfully raised KSh 16.76 billion against an initial KSh 11.00 billion target, achieving a 152% oversubscription on five-year notes priced at 11.80%. Safaricom followed, introducing Kenya's largest-ever corporate bond program, a KSh 40.00 billion Medium-Term Note. Its inaugural KSh 15.00 billion green tranche was listed on the NSE in December 2025, offering a tax-exempt rate of 10.40%. More recently, the Kenya Mortgage Refinance Company launched its KSh 3.00 billion sustainability note on April 28, 2026, just one day prior to I&M Bank's offering.

### I&M Offer Details and Market Positioning

I&M Bank's notes feature a 12.20% coupon rate. This rate positions the offering above the prevailing 10-year government benchmark yield, which currently ranges between approximately 12.70% and 13.00%, thereby offering a credit spread consistent with its subordinated status. The minimum subscription for the offer is set at KSh 500,000, primarily targeting institutional and high-net-worth investors. The offer officially opened on April 30, 2026, and is scheduled to close on May 15, 2026, with the listing on the Nairobi Securities Exchange anticipated for May 21, 2026.

### Facilitating Key Advisors

The successful execution of I&M Bank's medium-term note program is supported by a consortium of key financial and legal advisors. Standard Investment Bank is serving as the lead arranger and placing agent for the offer. The Co-operative Bank of Kenya has been appointed as the receiving bank, facilitating the subscription process. Legal counsel for the transaction is provided by Walker Kontos Advocates, while KPMG Kenya is acting as the reporting accountants, ensuring transparency and compliance.
