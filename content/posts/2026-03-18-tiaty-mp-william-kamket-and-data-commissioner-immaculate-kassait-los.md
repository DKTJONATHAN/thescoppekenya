Tiaty Member of Parliament William Kamket and Data Commissioner Immaculate Kassait are mourning the loss of their 13-year-old son, Bill Ballot, who passed away on Tuesday, March 17, 2026. The circumstances surrounding the young boy's death remain undisclosed, leaving the family in deep sorrow.

Tiaty MP, Data Commissioner Lose Son Bill Ballot

Tributes Pour In Following Tragic Loss The news of Bill Ballot's passing was confirmed by Antonellah Kakuko, a digital strategist for the ruling United Democratic Alliance (UDA) party. Kakuko extended condolences to both Kamket and Kenya's first Data Commissioner, Immaculate Kassait, acknowledging the profound grief facing the family. "We leave it all to You, Dear Lord. Go well, Bill Junior Ballot. Your light will forever remain in our hearts. May the Almighty Father grant my brother-in-law, Hon. William Kamket (MP, Tiaty), and his wife, Immaculate Kassait (Data Commissioner), the strength, comfort, and peace to endure this painful loss,” Kakuko stated. She added, “May they find grace to mourn their beloved son and courage to carry on in his memory.”

Kamket's Political Stature and Recent Statements William Kamket has held the position of MP for Tiaty in Baringo County since 2004, serving from 2004 to 2007 and then returning to the National Assembly in 2017, where he continues to serve after being elected on a KANU ticket. He also held the role of Speaker of the Baringo County Assembly between 2013 and 2017. In recent months, Kamket has become a prominent defender of President William Ruto's administration. On January 5, 2025, Kamket sparked public discussion by threatening to extend President Ruto's tenure amidst growing criticism of the Head of State. Speaking at an interdenominational service attended by the President in Kerio Valley, Elgeyo Marakwet, the legislator warned critics, asserting that continued opposition could lead to an extension of the presidential term, referencing former President Daniel Moi's 24-year stay in power. Fatal Road Accident in West Pokot The mourning period for the Kamket-Kassait family coincides with other tragic events reported across the nation. In neighboring West Pokot County, two individuals died following a serious road accident in Makutano town, Kapenguria, on Tuesday night. A lorry transporting clinker from Sebit Cement reportedly lost control, colliding with several vehicles, bringing down electrical cables, and crashing into a local supermarket. Among the deceased was a West Pokot County government staff member, while numerous others sustained serious injuries.
