Global Latin superstar Bad Bunny's legal team is pursuing over $450,000 in fees from emPawa Africa, following the dismissal of a copyright infringement lawsuit. The suit, initiated by Nigerian producer Dera, alleged an uncleared sample from his 2019 track, 'Empty My Pocket'.

Bad Bunny Pursues Fees from emPawa Africa The collapsed lawsuit had been building since May 2025, when Nigerian producer Ezeani Chidera Godfrey, known as Dera, claimed Bad Bunny's track ‘Enséñame a Bailar’ featured an uncleared sample of 'Empty My Pocket', a song Dera produced for Joeboy. Initially, Mr. Eazi's emPawa Africa joined as a co-plaintiff after Mr. Eazi publicly accused Bad Bunny's team of copyright infringement and alleged repeated stonewalling regarding proper credit for Dera and Joeboy.

Lawsuit's Dramatic Unraveling The legal proceedings began to unravel in early 2026. In January 2026, Dera's lawyers withdrew from the suit, citing "irreparable differences" over legal strategy, adding a dramatic turn to the high-profile case. Subsequently, emPawa Africa was dismissed as a plaintiff due to missed filing deadlines. Dera himself then failed to meet a March 6, 2026 deadline to continue the case and also missed a February 5, 2026 discovery hearing. This series of events culminated on March 9, 2026, when Judge Otis Wright of the federal court dismissed the suit entirely, stating that Dera had "abandoned this case" and that allowing it to proceed "would prejudice defendants and would be against the public interest." The complex and often public nature of such disputes can indeed feel like a captivating drama unfolding, reminiscent of a ['Family Movie' First Look: Bacon & Sedgwick with Real Kids](https://zandani.co.ke/article/family-movie-first-look-bacon-sedgwick-with-real-kids) for industry observers.

Clearance Claims and Internal Disputes Bad Bunny's team consistently maintained that the sample in question had been legitimately cleared through Lakizo Entertainment, a distributor of Dera's original song. Dera's lawsuit, however, countered that Lakizo was never authorized to grant such clearance. This dispute reportedly sparked a separate legal battle between Lakizo and emPawa, resulting in ‘Empty My Pocket’ being completely pulled from streaming platforms. With the copyright infringement suit dismissed and a substantial $456,312 fee demand now on the table, the situation appears to have concluded far less favorably for the plaintiffs than it began.
