## Local-based Players Lead Harambee Starlets into WAFCON 2026 Campaign

Kenya's Harambee Starlets are set to make their mark at the 2026 Women’s Africa Cup of Nations (WAFCON), with head coach Beldine Odemba unveiling a robust 25-player squad. The team, significantly bolstered by local talent, departed for the Ivory Coast yesterday, Saturday, February 28, for crucial pre-tournament friendlies and a training camp in France before heading to Morocco for the continental showpiece.

### Odemba Names 25-Player Harambee Starlets Squad with Local Talent

Head coach Beldine Odemba has officially named the 25-player final Harambee Starlets squad that will proudly represent Kenya at the 2026 WAFCON. The selection underscores a strong commitment to homegrown talent, with locally based players forming the backbone of the team. Thirteen players from Kenyan leagues have made the cut, complemented by talents from neighbouring Uganda and Tanzania. This strategic focus on local players is a clear testament to the growth and development of women's football within the country.

However, the journey to the final squad saw nine locally based call-ups released from the provisional list. Those dropped include Medina Abubakar, Merycline Mejor, Lorna Faith, Purity Akinyi, Elizabeth Mideva, Diana Shakava, Mary Majeso, Vivian Shiyonzo, and Kayole Starlets forward Euphraiser Shilwatso. Despite the strong local presence, the squad boasts significant international experience, with six players plying their trade in Europe and Saudi Arabia. Goalkeeper Lilian Awuor, of Romania's Farul Constanța, leads this contingent. Other European-based stars include Dorcas Shikobe (Sirens of Grevena, Greece), Vivian Nasaka (Hakkarigücüspor, Turkey), Enez Mango (Farul Constanța, Romania), and Mwanalima Adam (HK Køge, Denmark). Violet Nanjala completes the international lineup, playing for United Eagle FC in Saudi Arabia.

The full 25-player Harambee Starlets squad for WAFCON 2026 is as follows:
**Goalkeepers:** Lilian Awuor, Annedy Kundu, Juliet Adhiambo.
**Defenders:** Ruth Ingosi, Dorcas Shikobe, Enez Mango, Vivian Nasaka, Elizabeth Ochaka, Diana Ochol, Lorine Ilavonga, Elizabeth Muteshi.
**Midfielders:** Lydia Akoth, Vidah Akeyo, Lorna Nyarinda, Fasila Adhiambo, Martha Amunyolet, Mwanalima Adam, Shaline Nambengele.
**Forwards:** Violet Nanjala, Elizabeth Wambui, Jentrix Shikangwa, Shalyne Opisa, Airin Madalina, Valerie Nekesa, Eglay Mukhwana.

### Harambee Starlets WAFCON 2026 Preparations Underway

The Starlets' road to WAFCON 2026 is meticulously planned. The team departed on Saturday, February 28, for the Ivory Coast to engage in two crucial international friendlies. Their first challenge will be against Benin on March 2 at the Félix Houphouët-Boigny Stadium in Abidjan, with kick-off at 8 PM EAT. This will be followed by a clash against hosts Ivory Coast on March 4 at the Alassane Ouattara Olympic Stadium in Ebimpé at 10 PM EAT. These matches are vital for Coach Odemba to fine-tune tactics and assess player combinations.

Following the West African friendlies, the squad will travel to Miramas, France, for a residential training camp running until March 15. This camp is designed for tactical refinement, recovery, and strengthening team cohesion before the continental showpiece. The meticulous planning and strategic selections by Coach Odemba reflect a broader national commitment to excellence, much like the thoughtful considerations behind the [Nairobi Cooperation Agreement](https://zandani.co.ke/article/nairobi-cooperation-agreement-not-a-power-grab-constitutional-wisdom) which aims to strengthen our capital's governance.

### The Road Ahead for Harambee Starlets at WAFCON 2026

The 2026 WAFCON tournament, the 14th edition, is set to be hosted in Morocco from March 17 to April 3, 2026. This marks only Kenya's second appearance at the final stage of the Women’s Africa Cup of Nations. In their maiden outing in 2016, the Starlets faced a tough group, finishing bottom of Group B after losses to Ghana (3-1), Mali (3-1), and Nigeria (4-0).

This time, Kenya finds itself in Group A, a challenging but exciting prospect. The Harambee Starlets will kick off their Group A campaign against hosts Morocco on March 17. They will then face Senegal on March 20, before concluding the group stage against Algeria on March 23. The tournament has expanded to 16 teams, with the top four semi-finalists securing automatic qualification for the 2027 FIFA Women's World Cup. This adds another layer of motivation for the Starlets to perform exceptionally in the WAFCON 2026. The nation watches with anticipation as our Harambee Starlets embark on this significant journey, ready to showcase the vibrant talent of Kenyan football on the continental stage.
