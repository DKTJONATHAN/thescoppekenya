Interior Cabinet Secretary Kipchumba Murkomen has directed Inspector General of Police Douglas Kanja to immediately accelerate investigations into the growing instances of political violence and "goon" attacks nationwide. Speaking today, Sunday, April 12, 2026, during a church service in Kitale, Trans-Nzoia County, Murkomen affirmed the Inspector General has full authority to address perpetrators decisively.

## Government Takes Stance Against Political Goonism

Murkomen explicitly stated that the Inspector General of Police has his complete support and clear policy directives to act firmly against any political leader found sponsoring violent groups, without fear or favor. The Cabinet Secretary emphasized that political considerations must not impede efforts to resolve national challenges. He further asserted that "the greatest threat to national security is not the citizens, it is those who are wearing suits and are seeking high offices," suggesting that some politicians actively mobilize citizens with weapons, undermining governmental security provisions.

### IG Kanja Receives Direct Orders

This directive follows a series of violent disruptions across the country. Murkomen's remarks also come on the heels of a statement he posted on his X account on Saturday, April 11, 2026, condemning political violence and thuggery, and reiterating that such acts have no place in the nation's politics. This firm stance aims to bring accountability to political figures involved in instigating unrest.

### Recent Surge in Incidents

The country has witnessed several troubling incidents in recent days. On Saturday, April 11, chaos erupted in Kikuyu Constituency during an event attended by former Deputy President Rigathi Gachagua, which was reportedly disrupted by confrontations and police intervention. Wiper Patriotic Front Party leader Kalonzo Musyoka alleged that police diverted his convoy onto the Nairobi-Nakuru highway during the rally.

Separately, Vihiga Senator Godfrey Osotsi sustained serious injuries from an assault on Wednesday, April 8, 2026, in Kisumu, an attack he claims was politically motivated. Murkomen had previously condemned this attack and issued directives regarding the menace of goons. Further incidents of chaos were reported in Kakamega during a burial ceremony on Saturday, April 11, where rival political factions clashed, resulting in property damage, injuries to mourners, and one stabbing.

### Opposition Alleges State Interference

In response to these events, leaders allied with the opposition have accused the government of employing police officers and hired groups to disrupt their political gatherings. Former Deputy President Gachagua, speaking at a rally in Kajiado, condemned what he termed "infiltration and intimidation" during their meetings. He likened the emerging pattern of organized violence to dangerous militia groups, stating, "Yesterday, they used teargas and used gunfire on us, but we are the sons of Mau Mau. We battled with the police, and our meeting went on."
