Former Petroleum PS Mohamed Liban, ex-KPC MD Joe Sang, and former EPRA DG Daniel Kiptoo were released on police bail Monday, April 6, 2026, following their implication in a controversial Ksh4.8 billion fuel importation scandal. The trio awaits further investigation and potential arraignment, with the pursuit of charges against them currently unclear.

## Bail Granted in Fuel Import Probe

Lawyers representing Liban, Sang, and Kiptoo stated on Monday that their clients maintain no wrongdoing in the Ksh4.8 billion scandal. The legal team asserted that the former government officials were acting on recommendations issued by the National Security Council Committee (NSCC) on March 9. Documents indicate the committee allegedly advised sourcing 'emergency' fuel from alternative channels to shield the nation from potential severe impacts of the Middle East conflict, which continues to threaten Kenya's economy through rising fuel prices and trade disruptions.

### Intensifying Investigations and Allegations

The probe into the fuel importation controversy has expanded, with at least 20 other individuals presenting themselves to the Directorate of Criminal Investigations (DCI) to record statements. A Managing Director from one of the country's prominent petroleum companies is a key person of interest, particularly after it emerged that his company was involved in the shipment of 69 million litres of sub-standard fuel into Kenya. Investigations are examining the alleged falsification of fuel stock data to create a false impression of a supply crisis, which authorities believe was used to justify emergency fuel procurement outside the Government-to-Government (G2G) framework. Preliminary findings suggest the consignment may have been overpriced by over Ksh4 billion and failed to meet required standards.

### Government Response and Leadership Changes

Following the arrests of the three officials during the Easter period, various agencies, including the Kenya Pipeline Company (KPC) and the Energy and Petroleum Authority (EPRA), have released statements to reassure the public that no public resources will be misused. The Energy Ministry confirmed that the substandard fuel could have contributed to a Ksh43.4 per-litre variance compared to the established G2G deal. In response, the government, through the United Democratic Alliance (UDA), has recommended a substantial Ksh15 billion fine against the importers of the sub-standard fuel to protect taxpayers from projected financial losses. UDA Secretary General Hassan Omar indicated that harsh sanctions, five times the projected losses, are under consideration.

Amidst the unfolding scandal, Daniel Kiptoo's resignation from the Director General position at EPRA led to the appointment of Joseph Aketch, who is scheduled to be sworn in mid-week. As of yet, KPC and the Petroleum State Department have not announced replacements for their respective former heads.
