## The Legal Labyrinth of Union Leadership High Court Refers COTU Election Dispute to Labour Court is the central subject of the update.

Justice William Musyoka officially transferred the challenge against the March 14 COTU leadership elections to the Employment and Labour Relations Court on June 4. This procedural pivot signals yet another chapter in a long-standing cycle of jurisdictional shuffling that historically stalls substantive reform within Kenyan labor movements.

The Centre for Public Policy and Research had sought to invalidate the recent polls, but the High Court determined the matter fell outside its mandate. By redirecting the petition to the specialized Labour Court, the judiciary has effectively reset the clock on a dispute that serves as a hallmark of union succession battles. This pattern of moving cases between divisions-often referred to as jurisdictional ping-pong-acts as a strategic wall for incumbents. By the time a specialized court hears the merits of a challenge, the political climate of the union has often shifted or the disputed term has progressed, rendering the initial petition secondary to the established order.
