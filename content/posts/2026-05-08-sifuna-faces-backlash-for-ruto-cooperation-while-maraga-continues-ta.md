## The hypocritical divide on political cooperation

The current backlash against the ODM Senator reflects a broader struggle within the opposition over pragmatic governance. Detractors argue that engaging with the Kenya Kwanza administration undermines their political platform. However, observers note a stark contrast between this recent outrage and the reception of previous cross-party collaborations, suggesting that political loyalty is being applied with selective, and at times contradictory, rigor.

The inconsistency of these purity tests is most evident when contrasted with the appointment of former Chief Justice David Maraga. At that time, those currently demanding Sifuna’s isolation offered no condemnation of the former judge for accepting a state appointment. This history suggests that the opposition’s moral outrage is inconsistent, as the same individuals who tolerated a state-sanctioned role for Maraga are now pathologizing standard political interactions.
