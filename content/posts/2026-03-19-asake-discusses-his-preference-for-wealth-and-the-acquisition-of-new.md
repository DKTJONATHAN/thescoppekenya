Nigerian music sensation Asake recently elaborated on his affinity for money and lavish spending in an interview with indie filmmaker and podcaster Korty EO. The artist, known as Mr. Money, returned to Lagos in December 2025 and has since made headlines for acquiring multiple luxury vehicles, solidifying his superstar status in 2026.

## Asake's Passion for Prosperity and Posh Rides

### The Freedom of Fortune
Award-winning Nigerian musician Asake shared his profound love for wealth during a recent interview, explaining that money grants him significant freedom. The artist, whose moniker is Mr. Money, revealed that while wealth does not solve every problem, it addresses "80% of my problems," a sentiment he expressed in Yoruba as he discussed his enjoyment of both earning and spending money.

### A Fleet of Festivities
Asake's lavish lifestyle is evident in his recent acquisitions, particularly since his return to Nigeria in December 2025 after a year abroad. The musician, who maintains five cars in Lagos, has reportedly spent billions of naira on new luxury vehicles for his family and himself. He surprised his mother with a Mercedes-Benz G Wagon, valued at over 500 million naira, for her birthday, and gifted his father a Toyota Prado Land Cruiser, exceeding 200 million naira in cost. Demonstrating his peak industry status, Asake also treated himself to a Rolls-Royce and an additional G Wagon, cumulatively worth billions of naira.

### From Campus Star to Chart-Topper
During his discussion with Korty EO, Asake reflected on his journey to stardom, which began at Obafemi Awolowo University, Ile-Ife, Osun State, where he studied Dramatic Arts and became a campus music star. Korty also interviewed Asake's mother, who disclosed her initial plans to send him to the United Kingdom for studies. However, Asake insisted on attending OAU Ife, a decision supported by their family friend, actor Muyideen Oladapo, known as Lala, who was then pursuing a Dramatic Arts degree at the university and pledged to mentor Asake.

The year 2026 has already started robustly for Asake, marked by the release of 'REAL, Vol. 1,' a four-track joint EP with Wizkid, which debuted at No. 6 on the Billboard World Albums chart and topped the UK and Nigerian Apple Music Albums Charts. Every track from 'REAL, Vol. 1' currently sits in the Top 10 of the Billboard U.S. Afrobeats Songs chart, with "Turbulence" reaching No. 2. This collaboration highlights the vibrant spirit of partnerships within the Pan-African music scene, similar to the recent joint project "Agaba Romantic" by Joeboy and Wizard Chan. Asake is additionally anticipated to release his fourth full-length album, titled 'Money', later in 2026, following the recent single "Lagos Vibes Only".
