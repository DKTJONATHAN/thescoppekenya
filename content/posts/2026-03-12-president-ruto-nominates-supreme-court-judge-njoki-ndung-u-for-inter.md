## President Ruto Nominates Supreme Court Judge Njoki Ndung'u for ICC Seat President William Ruto has officially nominated Supreme Court Judge Njoki Ndung'u as the nation's candidate for the upcoming International Criminal Court (ICC) judicial elections in December 2026. Judge Ndung'u, a Supreme Court Justice since 2011, is poised to compete against a field of international nominees for a position at the ICC in The Hague, Netherlands.

Judge Njoki Ndung'u's Candidacy Judge Ndung'u's nomination leverages her extensive judicial and parliamentary experience. She was appointed as a Judge of the Supreme Court on June 16, 2011, and assumed office on August 26, 2011, as part of the inaugural bench. Prior to her judicial career, she served as a nominated Member of Parliament in the National Assembly for the 9th Parliament, representing the National Alliance Rainbow Coalition (NARC) from 2003 to 2007. Over her tenure on the Supreme Court, Ndung'u has participated in numerous complex cases, including presidential election disputes. Notably, she was part of the panel that ruled on the 2017 presidential election petition, which led to the annulment of the initial election and the call for a repeat vote. If successful, Judge Ndung'u will assume responsibility for cases involving severe international crimes, such as genocide, war crimes, and crimes against humanity.

International Competition Judge Ndung'u will face competition from other global candidates for the ICC judgeship. Nominees include Deo John Nangela from Tanzania, Rosette Muzigo from Uganda, Everlyn Ankumah from Ghana, Guénaël Mettraux from Switzerland, Yoshimitsu Yamauchi from Japan, and Diana Carolina Olarte from Colombia. The elections for six new judges are scheduled to take place during the 25th session of the Assembly of States Parties from December 7 to 17, 2026, in New York, USA.

Implications for the Judiciary The potential departure of Judge Ndung'u would reduce the Supreme Court's bench by one judge, creating a vacancy. This move coincides with an ongoing process by the Judicial Service Commission (JSC) to appoint another Supreme Court judge following the death of Justice Mohamed Ibrahim, who passed away on December 17, 2025, after a prolonged illness. The JSC announced on Wednesday, February 25, 2026, that it had received six applications for the vacant position by the February 17, 2026, deadline. Among the applicants are Justice Katwa Kigen, Justice Joseph Kiplagat Sergon, Justice Warsame Mohammed, Justice Francis Kipruto Tuiyot, Anne Waceke Kiratu Makori, and Lilian Wanjiku Wachira. The JSC plans a stakeholder engagement on March 23, 2026, ahead of the shortlisting process.
