## Timaya's Candid Perspective on Marriage and Fatherhood

Nigerian music luminary Timaya has once again ignited discussions across the continent with his recent pronouncements on matrimony. The artist openly advises younger men to prioritize having children over formalizing partnerships, asserting that the institution of marriage, in his view, frequently falls short. This stance reflects a broader conversation within contemporary African society regarding evolving family structures.

### A Consistent View on Matrimony

Inetimi Timaya Odon, widely known as Timaya, recently shared his unfiltered thoughts in a video circulating online, featuring fellow artists Peter Okoye and Phyno. His succinct advice to young men was, "Just born pikin. Pikin go grow. This marriage thing no dey work." This direct statement, delivered in Nigerian Pidgin, underlines his long-held skepticism about marriage. His perspective is not new; back in 2022, he hinted at a lifelong bachelorhood, even drawing a parallel to Jesus's marital status, suggesting a "Christ-like quality" in his own disinclination toward marriage. Further elaborating in a 2024 interview on Naija FM, the musician clarified that he personally never felt compelled to marry, despite being a father. He maintained that marriage simply "never really appealed to him," though he acknowledged that his sentiments could evolve over time.

### Fatherhood Beyond Convention

Despite his reservations about marriage, Timaya is a devoted and proud father to four children from different relationships. His two daughters, Emmanuella Odon and Gracey Odon, were born from his relationship with interior designer Barbara Fumnaya Nwaokolo. He later welcomed his son, Emmanuel Odon, with Tamar, and his youngest, Maya Odon, with businesswoman Dunnie Onasanya. Timaya frequently shares tender moments with his children on social media, celebrating their milestones and often stating that fatherhood is a profound joy in his life. This open approach to family life, while unconventional by some traditional standards, resonates with a segment of African celebrity narratives that challenge traditional relationship expectations. Such transparency in personal life offers a contrast to more guarded celebrity personas, prompting reflections on how public figures navigate their relationships and family dynamics, akin to broader discussions on celebrity dating and relationships across different entertainment industries.
