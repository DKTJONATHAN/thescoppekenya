Civil society, faith organizations, and governance actors have jointly issued a strong statement to President William Ruto, raising significant concerns this week over his public remarks and the escalating State House expenditure amidst severe economic conditions in March 2026. The groups, following a retreat in Lukenya, Athi River, highlighted a critical national moment.

## Scrutiny of State House Conduct and Spending

### Eroding Dignity and Public Trust

Lobby groups, including Inuka Kenya Ni Sisi, Centre For Multiparty Democracy (CMD), and Transparency International-Kenya, have condemned what they term as reckless public remarks by President William Ruto. They argue that the conduct of leaders, especially from the highest office, is undermining the dignity of the Presidency and eroding public confidence in government institutions. The coalition emphasized that the Presidency, a symbol of national unity under the Constitution, demands restraint and respect for the rule of law. They insisted that leaders must uphold integrity and dignity in both language and conduct as required by Chapter Six of the Constitution. Their statement arrives as the country shows early signs of engagement in 2027 campaigns, marked by tense exchanges between President Ruto and the United Opposition throughout this week.

### State House Budgetary Demands Amidst Strain

The groups have also sharply criticized the increasing State House budget, deeming it a misplacement of priorities when critical sectors like health and education face severe strain. They explicitly demanded that national resources be redirected toward health, education, and youth opportunity, asserting that the State House budget should not expand while public services falter. On Monday, State House Comptroller Katoo Ole Metito informed the Administration and Internal Security Committee that the presidential office received Ksh8.58 billion for the 2025/26 financial year, a significant shortfall against the projected requirement of Ksh18.33 billion. Moreover, State House is now seeking an allocation of Ksh20 billion for the 2026/2027 financial year. Supplementary budget papers submitted to Parliament reveal a request for an additional Ksh8.4 billion, primarily to cover increased travel, hospitality, and other operational expenses. This comes as other government entities, such as KEMSA, report surpluses amidst financial scrutiny.
