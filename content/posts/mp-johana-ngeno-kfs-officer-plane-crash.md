## MP Johana Ng'eno Among Six Killed in Devastating Plane Crash Emurua Dikirr Member of Parliament Johana Kipyegon Ng’eno was among six individuals who tragically died in a helicopter crash in Mosop, Nandi County, on Saturday, February 28, 2026. This devastating plane crash, which also claimed the life of an officer from the Kenya Forest Service (KFS), has led to an outpouring of national grief and prompted immediate calls for a thorough investigation by authorities. The aircraft, an Airbus Helicopters H125 with registration number 5Y-DSB, reportedly attempted an emergency landing due to adverse weather before crashing and bursting into flames around 4:45 p.m.

Initial Details Emerge from the Plane Crash Scene The fatal incident occurred in Chepkiep, Mosop Sub-county, where eyewitnesses reported that the helicopter initially made an emergency landing due to heavy rainfall and poor visibility. However, after a brief respite, the pilot reportedly attempted to take off again, only for the aircraft to hit a tree and crash approximately 300 metres after liftoff. Nandi County Police Commander Samuel Mukuusi confirmed the incident, stating that all six individuals on board perished. Authorities have since recovered mobile phones and SIM cards belonging to the late MP Johana Ng’eno and Amos Kipngetich Rotich, a Kenya Forest Service (KFS) officer, from the crash scene. Other confirmed fatalities include pilot George Were, photographer Nick Kosgei, teacher Robert Keter, and Wycliffe Kiprotich Rono.

Official Reactions and Investigation into the KFS Officer and MP's Demise The Kenya Civil Aviation Authority (KCAA) has launched an investigation into the circumstances surrounding the fatal plane crash. The Aircraft Accident Investigation Department (AAID) is leading the probe, with forensic teams already recovering critical flight instruments, maintenance logs, and onboard equipment for detailed analysis. President William Ruto expressed deep sorrow, leading the nation in mourning the tragic loss of MP Ng'eno, who served three consecutive terms. Deputy President Rigathi Gachagua called for a thorough investigation, noting that Ng'eno had previously faced threats. National Assembly Speaker Moses Wetang'ula officially notified Parliament of the legislator's demise and appointed a special team to coordinate funeral arrangements. Wiper Party leader Kalonzo Musyoka also urged a swift inquiry and called for support for the bereaved families.

The Political Legacy of MP Johana Ng'eno The sudden death of MP Johana Ng'eno leaves a significant void in Kenya's political landscape. Ng'eno, who was 53 years old, was first elected in 2013 and successfully defended his Emurua Dikirr parliamentary seat in both the 2017 and 2022 General Elections, demonstrating remarkable political resilience and grassroots support. At the time of his passing, he chaired the Departmental Committee on Housing, Urban Planning and Public Works, a crucial role in overseeing the government's Affordable Housing Projects and reviewing the 2026 Budget Policy Statement. His long-standing commitment to public service also saw him serve on the Departmental Committee on Justice and Legal Affairs and the Public Investments Committee during previous parliamentary terms. Ng'eno was known for his vocal stance and dedication to his constituents, often advocating fiercely on issues of land rights and community interests. The nation now awaits the findings of the ongoing investigation, as leaders and citizens alike reflect on the impact of this tragic plane crash.
