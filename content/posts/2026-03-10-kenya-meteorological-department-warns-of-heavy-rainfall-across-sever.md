## Kenya's Forecast: When Even the Stars Can't Outshine the Rain

Get ready, Kenya! The Met Department has dropped a seven-day forecast predicting serious downpours across the nation, March 10-16. From Nairobi's glitzy streets to Lake Victoria's shores, everyone, including our beloved celebrities, might need to trade designer shades for umbrellas and wellies this week.

### Showers Over Star-Studded Western and Rift

Looks like Western Kenya and the Rift Valley are set for a soggy spectacle. Counties such as Siaya, Kisumu, Homa Bay, Migori, Kisii, and Nyamira are on high alert, alongside Trans Nzoia, Baringo, Uasin Gishu, Elgeyo Marakwet, Nandi, Nakuru, Narok, Kericho, and Bomet. Even Kakamega, Vihiga, Bungoma, Busia, and West Pokot are in for a wet week. Imagine the drama as morning showers give way to afternoon thunderstorms - perfect for a dramatic movie scene, or just a very wet commute! For those daring to drive in such conditions, remembering the importance of properly adjusted rear mirrors is crucial, especially for our commercial vehicle drivers who might be ferrying equipment or even VIPs around.

### Highland Hues and Nairobi News

The Central Highlands are not spared, with Nyandarua, Laikipia, Nyeri, Kirinyaga, Murang’a, Kiambu, Meru, Embu, Tharaka Nithi, and the bustling Nairobi County all expecting rain. Mornings might start with a gentle drizzle, perfect for a cozy coffee moment, before the afternoon brings more intense showers and even thunderstorms. Our city dwellers and highland retreat seekers should prepare for those sudden downpours - perhaps a good time for our stars to reflect on their carbon footprint, or at least ensure their luxury rides have passed the latest NTSA vehicle inspection requirements before hitting the slick roads.

### Coastal Cool Downs and Lowland Drama

Down in the South-eastern Lowlands, Kitui, Makueni, Machakos, Kajiado, and Taita Taveta will see a similar pattern of morning cloudiness leading to afternoon showers. And for our fabulous coast-goers in Mombasa, Kilifi, Lamu, and Kwale, the beach might be more about splashy strolls than sun-bathing. Expect light morning showers intensifying into the afternoon.

### Temperature Talk: Hot Spots and Chill Zones

While the rain reigns, don't expect a complete cool-down everywhere. Daytime temperatures above 30°C are still on the cards for the Coast, South-eastern Lowlands, and parts of North-eastern and North-western Kenya. Perfect for that steamy, dramatic look - if you can find a dry spot! Meanwhile, the Central Highlands, Central Rift Valley, and areas around Mount Kilimanjaro will experience cooler nighttime temperatures, dipping below 10°C. So, while some are sizzling, others will be snuggling.
