FIFA will introduce its first-ever World Cup final halftime show on July 19, 2026, at MetLife Stadium in New Jersey, to be rebranded as New York New Jersey Stadium. Coldplay's Chris Martin is curating the spectacle, which promises multiple artists and a significant entertainment event across New York City.

Chris Martin to Curate Historic World Cup Halftime Show

A Global Entertainment Extravaganza FIFA President Gianni Infantino confirmed the 2026 FIFA World Cup final will feature a groundbreaking halftime show, describing it as "a historic moment for the FIFA World Cup and a show befitting the biggest sporting event in the world". The performance, organized in association with Global Citizen, will showcase "more than one" artist, with Chris Martin and Coldplay manager Phil Harvey leading the curation. Beyond the main event, FIFA plans a "takeover" of Times Square for the final weekend of the tournament, which is co-hosted by the United States, Mexico, and Canada. This comprehensive entertainment strategy aims to amplify the global appeal of football's ultimate match.

Afrobeats Ascendancy Expected on Football's Biggest Stage Nigeria possesses a formidable history of cultural representation on global platforms, fueling high expectations for Afrobeats artists to feature prominently in the 2026 World Cup final halftime show. Past involvement by Nigerian talents in major football-related musical events has consistently showcased the genre's powerful global presence. Artists like Tems, Davido, and Burna Boy have previously brought their distinctive sound to major football stages, solidifying Nigeria's cultural impact on sport's most-watched occasions. The precedent for Nigerian artistry on such a grand scale is clear, making their potential inclusion a highly anticipated aspect of the upcoming spectacle.

The Coldplay Connection and Collaborative Synergy The curatorial role of Coldplay's Chris Martin introduces another layer of excitement, particularly given the band's history of collaborations with African artists. Ayra Starr, for instance, performed "Hot Body" with Coldplay during their Music of the Spheres World Tour and also collaborated with Martin on the track "Good Feelings." She served as a support act for select North American and UK dates on what became the best-selling tour in history. This existing synergy between Coldplay and African talent reinforces the potential for a diverse and impactful lineup, promising a truly Pan-African flavour to the final's entertainment.
