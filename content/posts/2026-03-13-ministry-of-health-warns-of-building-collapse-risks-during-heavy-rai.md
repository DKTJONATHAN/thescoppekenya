## Ministry Warns of Building Collapse Risks Amidst Heavy Rains The Ministry of Health has issued an urgent advisory regarding the heightened risk of building and infrastructure collapses during the ongoing March-April-May (MAM) long rains in 2026. Health PS Mary Muthoni cautioned that prolonged rainfall weakens foundations and damages essential infrastructure, posing significant threats to life and public safety. The government urges vigilance and adherence to safety measures to mitigate associated injuries and fatalities.

Advisory Details On Friday, the Ministry of Health, through an advisory by PS Mary Muthoni, highlighted that continuous rainfall severely compromises the integrity of buildings, roads, and bridges. This degradation of structural stability presents substantial dangers to public welfare. The Ministry stressed the importance of remaining vigilant and observing safety protocols to reduce the risk of injuries and loss of life from collapsing structures.

Government Directives and Cautionary Measures In response to these risks, the government has called for extreme caution. The public is advised to avoid any buildings exhibiting visible signs of structural weakness, including cracks, flooding, or compromised walls. Property owners and landlords are mandated to conduct thorough inspections and undertake timely repairs to reinforce vulnerable structures, ensuring their stability.

Warnings for Property Developers Property developers have also received a stern warning against initiating new construction projects on unstable slopes, in flood-prone areas, or within riparian zones. These locations are particularly vulnerable to landslides and severe structural damage during heavy rainfall, significantly increasing the likelihood of collapses. Za Ndani emphasizes the critical nature of adhering to these guidelines to prevent future tragedies.

Public Reporting and Immediate Action The Ministry has further urged residents and the wider public to promptly report any structural hazards or damaged infrastructure. This immediate reporting enables disaster response teams to implement preventive measures swiftly. In the unfortunate event of a building or infrastructure collapse, the public is advised to evacuate the area immediately to avoid falling debris and secondary collapses.

Recent Collapse Incidents in Nairobi (2026) This advisory follows several fatal building collapse incidents within Nairobi alone in early 2026. On February 11, six individuals sustained injuries when a building under construction collapsed at the junction of Racecourse and Kirinyaga Road in Nairobi CBD. Earlier in January, two lives were lost after a multi-storey building under construction in South C estate's Shopping Centre area collapsed. Days later, on January 10, two more individuals died and three were critically injured when another building under construction opposite Karen Community Church along Ngong Road collapsed.
