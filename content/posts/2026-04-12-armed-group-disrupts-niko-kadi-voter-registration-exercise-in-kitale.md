An armed group disrupted the Niko Kadi voter registration exercise in Kitale on Saturday, April 11, temporarily halting the initiative after goons infiltrated the event. Leaders of the drive, which attracted hundreds of youths, have attributed the chaos to political interference aimed at impeding voter registration efforts across the region.

## Allegations of Political Interference

The voter registration exercise, a key initiative by Niko Kadi, faced a significant setback when individuals armed with crude weapons took control of the proceedings. Leaders from the initiative publicly blamed politicians for orchestrating the disruption, citing the worrying trend of using youths to incite disorder. One leader stated, "Our initiative was simple: talk to the youth to register as voters. But along the way as they registered, some hired goons disrupted us." Another leader characterized the incident as "a planned disruption," highlighting the challenge of engaging youths who are simultaneously exploited to disrupt such gatherings.

### Kitale Turnout and National Momentum

Allans Ademba, a Niko Kadi Initiative leader, reported that the Kitale meeting witnessed the largest turnout to date, affirming the success of their "watu wakue Kadi" objective despite the political infiltration. Ademba urged youths nationwide to register and actively participate in the decision-making process for the 2027 general election. The incident in Kitale occurred as the Niko Kadi drive continues to gain momentum across the country, contributing to a significant increase in new voter registrations recorded by the Independent Electoral and Boundaries Commission (IEBC).

### IEBC's Registration Drive and Future Plans

The Independent Electoral and Boundaries Commission has documented over 875,000 new voters in the past two weeks since the mass voter registration commenced. Nairobi leads with 96,897 new registrations, followed by Kiambu with 46,265. In response to the ongoing demand, the IEBC announced a voter registration exercise in the Nairobi Central Business District for today, Sunday, April 12, urging the capital's youth to register between 8 AM and 5 PM. The commission mandates that citizens present their national identification cards or valid passports for registration. This requirement comes amidst ongoing discussions concerning the issuance of national identification documents [cite: internal link for Gachagua article]. Concurrently, the Niko Kadi Drive is scheduled to conduct a similar registration exercise in the CBD on Tuesday, April 14.
