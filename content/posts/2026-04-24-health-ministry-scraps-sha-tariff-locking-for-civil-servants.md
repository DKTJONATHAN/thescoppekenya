Health Ministry Scraps SHA Tariff Locking for Civil Servants is central to this update for Kenyan readers.

## Kenyan Healthcare Policy Shift

The Ministry of Health abolished tariff-locking within the Social Health Authority system. This move directly addresses civil servants' healthcare access issues, ensuring they receive services without out-of-pocket payments at contracted facilities, a policy adjustment intended to restore confidence in the new health scheme.

### Immediate Policy Reversal

On Thursday, April 23, 2026, Health Cabinet Secretary Aden Duale announced the immediate withdrawal of the tariff-locking mechanism within the Social Health Authority (SHA) system. This decision followed reports of civil servants being denied services or compelled to pay out-of-pocket at healthcare facilities. Effective immediately, all SHA-contracted facilities are prohibited from charging civil servants any fees for medication or services. Additionally, provisions for strict tariff application have been suspended until nationwide tariff negotiations are finalized. The Ministry also affirmed a strict "walk-in, walk-out" policy, disallowing co-payments for civil servants. Violations of this directive by contracted facilities risk penalties, including de-contracting.

### Addressing Civil Servant Grievances

The Ministry's intervention directly responds to widespread grievances from public servants, who reported being turned away from hospitals or forced to pay cash for services covered by their salary deductions. This crisis significantly impacted patients requiring dialysis, cancer treatment, diabetes management, and emergency care. Prior to this resolution, the Union of Kenya Civil Servants (UKCS) had threatened legal action against the Social Health Authority over perceived unilateral changes to medical insurance benefits, highlighting a growing discontent among civil servants regarding their healthcare provisions. To mitigate immediate challenges, a joint rapid response desk will be established, comprising representatives from SHA, the State Department of Public Service, and the UKCS. This desk will facilitate the release of civil servants detained over outstanding medical bills and process refunds for any unauthorized charges incurred.

### Broader Healthcare Commitment

This policy shift aligns with the government's broader commitment to enhancing healthcare access and achieving Universal Health Coverage, a key agenda item for Health Cabinet Secretary Aden Duale. Duale has consistently emphasized the importance of a resilient and people-centered health system, outlining priority investments in health policy and regulatory frameworks. While addressing the immediate concerns of civil servants, the Ministry of Health is also embarking on nationwide tariff negotiations with private and faith-based healthcare facilities classified between Levels 3 and 6, commencing April 28, 2026. These negotiations aim to establish a harmonized, evidence-based reimbursement framework to ensure the long-term sustainability and equitable delivery of healthcare services under the Public Officers Medical Scheme Fund (POMSF).
