In recent weeks, Deputy President Rigathi Gachagua and Oscar Sudi have publicly threatened to revoke former President Uhuru Kenyatta's retirement benefits. These escalating warnings, delivered across various public forums, aim to silence his perceived political activity and criticism, but Kenyatta remains conspicuously unbothered.

## DP Gachagua and Oscar Sudi Threaten Uhuru Kenyatta's Pension Revocation

The political climate in Kenya has intensified, with senior figures in President William Ruto's administration targeting the retired former Head of State. Deputy President Gachagua, alongside the outspoken Oscar Sudi, have publicly declared that Kenyatta risks losing his retirement emoluments if he continues to engage in political affairs or criticize the current government. These pronouncements have become a staple at recent public gatherings, including solemn church services, prompting questions about the true intent behind such aggressive rhetoric.

### Persistent Echoes of Influence

Despite the loud declarations, seasoned political observers suggest these persistent, almost ritualistic, attacks against the former President might reveal more about the accusers than the accused. It is widely noted that the sustained badgering of Uhuru Kenyatta by the current administration's so-called 'attack dogs' is less about genuinely enforcing rules and more about a lingering apprehension over his enduring political influence. Kenyatta, for his part, has maintained a remarkably consistent strategy: he dismisses his critics as "worthless people" or "good-for-nothings" without ever deigning to address President Ruto directly. This approach sidesteps the primary figure and renders his proxies insignificant.

### Counterproductive Public Confrontations

The strategy of public shaming, particularly when aired in forums like church services, appears to be proving counterproductive, especially among Kenyatta's traditional political base, the Kikuyu community. Far from diminishing his standing, these attacks are perceived by many as validation, strengthening his popularity and resolve. Such public broadsides often serve to remind the populace of Kenyatta's stature as a former President, implicitly contrasting this with how similar attacks might affect an individual like Deputy President Gachagua. The very public nature of these warnings, seemingly intended to intimidate, is instead fortifying Uhuru’s image as a resilient figure standing against perceived bullying.

### A Strengthened Resolve

Ultimately, in the court of Kenyan public opinion, the threats intended to silence are perceived to be bolstering Kenyatta's position rather than diminishing it. The former President's consistent refusal to engage directly with his accusers, combined with his dismissive stance, reinforces an image of an unperturbed and influential figure. This approach suggests that the attempts to curtail his political activity may inadvertently be enhancing his public standing.
