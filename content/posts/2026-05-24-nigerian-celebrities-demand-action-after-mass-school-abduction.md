## The Power of the Spotlight: Can Nollywood Influence Change?

When forty-six people are abducted from classrooms in Oyo State, it raises a difficult question: can a viral hashtag actually force the government to act? As Nigeria faces a surge in insecurity, the entertainment industry has begun to push back. We are left wondering if the social media influence of celebrities translates into real policy changes or if it simply remains digital noise.

The recent tragedy in the Oriire Local Government Area serves as a dark turning point. Bandits stormed three schools in Yawota and Ahoro-Esinele, leaving two dead and dozens of students and staff missing. While official channels have been quiet, Nollywood figures like Yvonne Jegede, Ruth Kadiri, Toyin Abraham, Opeyemi Aiyeola, Kehinde Bankole, and Ayo Ajewole are speaking out. They are using their large platforms to call for immediate security action. The big question remains: does this public advocacy change how the country is governed?

### Shifting from Mourning to Demanding Action

Nollywood stars are no longer just sharing messages of grief. They have shifted toward a more strategic approach. Their first move is to ensure that the forty-six victims are not forgotten. By sharing names and photos across their social media channels, they stop the news cycle from pushing the incident into the background.

Second, these stars are seeking direct accountability. Rather than making general pleas, figures like Ruth Kadiri and Toyin Abraham are tagging official government accounts, including those connected to President Bola Ahmed Tinubu. This strategy moves beyond simple sentiment. They are building a public record of their demands, compelling government officials to acknowledge the crisis in real-time. Much like how countries prioritize data infrastructure to secure their digital future, these celebrities treat public safety as a vital service that requires constant government oversight.

History shows this is not the first time the entertainment industry has engaged in politics. During past periods of unrest, Nollywood advocacy helped keep international attention on local struggles. This pressure often forces the state to seek diplomatic or security solutions. When stars speak with one voice, they reach a massive audience that politicians cannot afford to ignore when election season arrives.

### The Real Impact of Celebrity Activism

People often debate whether this activism is effective. Some critics believe that celebrity influence is just for show, offering a way for fans to vent frustration without actually challenging the people in power. However, Nigerian icons have successfully shaped national conversations in the past. By using their platforms to highlight the abduction of students, these actors are daring the state to fulfill its most basic duty: protecting its citizens.

The biggest hurdle is that digital outrage tends to be short-lived. The government is aware that public interest often fades quickly. If the outcry does not last, it loses its impact. To drive real legislative reform, these influencers must find a way to take their efforts from the internet to the real world. While social media runs on temporary attention, changing laws requires long-term persistence.

### Why This Matters

This situation highlights a growing tension between the Nigerian state and its most famous citizens. When schools turn into conflict zones, the agreement between the government and the people is shattered. The involvement of these stars shows that many Nigerians are no longer willing to treat insecurity as a distant issue. They want the government to tackle the roots of banditry before the entire education system is destroyed.

### What Comes Next

Moving forward, it will be interesting to see if these celebrities change their tactics. Will they form formal advocacy groups, or perhaps seek direct meetings with security officials? If they maintain this pressure for several weeks, it could represent a major change in how political influence works in Nigeria. However, if the noise dies down quickly, it will show that even the loudest digital megaphone has its limits against a stubborn bureaucracy. Ultimately, the success of this movement will not be measured by the volume of the protest, but by the ability of these stars to keep the pressure on until the government finally moves the needle.
