President William Ruto today, Monday, March 30, unveiled concrete measures to shield the nation from the escalating anxiety surrounding anticipated rises in fuel and essential commodity prices. These interventions aim to mitigate the economic impact of the ongoing Middle East conflict on livelihoods, following a comprehensive multi-agency briefing.

Mitigating Economic Shocks

Fuel Price Stabilization Efforts President Ruto confirmed that a functional Government-to-Government fuel procurement deal remains active, effectively absorbing the impact of rising international oil prices at the pump. This strategic intervention has reportedly mitigated price increases and ensured security of supply, acting as a crucial buffer against external shocks. The Ministry of Energy and the National Treasury are further tasked with preparing additional interventions should international fuel prices continue their upward trend due to the Middle East conflict.

Food Security Assurances Citizens can anticipate stable food supplies for the immediate future. President Ruto assured the nation that fertilizer supplies are adequate and no disruptions are expected through September this year, fully covering the ongoing rainy season. This commitment comes as the Kenya Met Department recently issued heavy rainfall warnings for Nairobi and surrounding counties, underscoring the importance of agricultural preparedness.

Sectoral Performance and Export Challenges Despite initial fears, Kenya's tea sector, a significant foreign exchange earner, has demonstrated a 6 percent growth. This resilience is attributed to diversification into new markets and strengthened ties with existing buyers. The ports of Mombasa and Lamu are also experiencing increased cargo activity. Lamu, in particular, has seen a sharp rise in throughput, including the handling of over 4,000 high-value motor vehicles destined for Gulf markets via transhipment. However, the meat export sector has faced significant setbacks due to logistical and freight challenges stemming from the conflict. To address this, President Ruto announced that the Ministries of Trade and Agriculture will collaborate to explore alternative solutions to support affected exporters.
