## Students Feared Dead After Kapsabet School Septic Tank Collapse Several learners are feared dead after 11 students at the Kapsabet School for the Deaf in Nandi County reportedly fell into a septic tank on Thursday, March 12, 2026. The incident occurred as students gathered to observe President William Ruto's motorcade passing by the school, with many rushed to Kapsabet County Referral Hospital. Initial reports indicated 11 fatalities, though one death has been confirmed by hospital authorities, with ten others injured.

Tragic Incident Unfolds The catastrophic event transpired early Thursday morning when students assembled atop a 20-foot-long septic tank, attempting to view President William Ruto's motorcade en route to a visit at the nearby Kapsabet Girls' High School. The weight of the learners caused the tank to cave in, submerging them. This sudden collapse led to immediate distress, trapping the students within the facility.

Rescue Efforts and Medical Response Following the collapse, community residents swiftly initiated rescue operations. Police personnel later arrived at the scene to assist. Scores of learners were successfully extricated and promptly transported to Kapsabet County Referral Hospital for urgent medical attention. While initial accounts indicated that at least 11 students had died, hospital authorities have since confirmed one fatality, with ten other students receiving treatment for their injuries. Za Ndani has not independently verified the initial higher fatality reports.

Presidential Condolences and Support President Ruto, alerted to the disaster, made an unscheduled stop at the Kapsabet School for the Deaf. He conveyed his profound regret over the tragedy and announced a contribution of Ksh5 million towards infrastructure development at the affected institution. The President's motorcade then proceeded to its original destination at Kapsabet Girls' High School.
