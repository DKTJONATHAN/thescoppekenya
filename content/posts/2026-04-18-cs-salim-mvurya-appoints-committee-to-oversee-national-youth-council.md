CS Salim Mvurya Appoints Committee to Oversee National Youth is central to this update for Kenyan readers.

Sport and Youth Affairs Cabinet Secretary Salim Mvurya has initiated the long-anticipated National Youth Council (NYC) elections, gazetting timelines and appointing a National Steering Committee on Friday, April 17, 2026. This move sets the stage for nationwide polls, marking the first such elections in over a decade.

Committee Appointed to Oversee National Youth Council Elections The Cabinet Secretary, through a gazette notice, confirmed the appointment of members to the National Steering Committee, tasked with overseeing the upcoming National Youth Council elections. This committee aims to ensure a seamless electoral process across the country.

Election Timelines Announced CS Mvurya outlined a three-phase election schedule, commencing in July 2026. Ward-level elections are slated to begin on July 5, 2026, followed by constituency-level elections starting July 16, 2026. The electoral process will culminate with the National Youth Congress elections on July 27, 2026. These elections will be conducted in adherence to the National Youth Council Act, which governs the election of youth representatives at various administrative levels.

National Steering Committee Members The newly appointed National Steering Committee will be chaired by George Marcel Osaro. Its members include Mohammed Barre, Beverly Anyona, Mary Mbugua, Mukomoni Osero, and Wilkistar Oluoch. Additional members appointed to the committee are Gideon Balang, Juma Salim Shibe, Ken Koech, Debora Malwa Mackazi, Elijah Kalama Kafani, Hellen Tuwei, and Sophy Nekoye Walialua.

Committee's Mandate The committee is mandated to coordinate all aspects of the elections, including the handling of disputes and the verification of candidate nominations. It will ensure strict compliance with electoral guidelines and oversee the deployment of digital systems for voter registration, voting, and the transmission of results. A key responsibility also includes safeguarding the integrity of members' data throughout the process.

End to a Decade-Long Wait The last National Youth Council elections occurred in 2012, making the latest announcement significant for youth across the country who have waited over a decade to elect their representatives. The prolonged absence of regular polls had previously raised concerns regarding weakened youth representation and accountability. Members of the public, particularly young people, are urged to note the timelines and actively participate in this crucial process. The re-establishment of these elections is anticipated to strengthen youth engagement and advocacy at various levels.
