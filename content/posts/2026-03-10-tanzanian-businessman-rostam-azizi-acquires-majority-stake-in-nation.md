## Rostam Azizi Takes Majority Control of Nation Media Group Tanzanian businessman Rostam Azizi has acquired a 54.08 percent majority stake in Nation Media Group (NMG), marking a significant ownership transition for the East African media giant. This 2026 acquisition concludes the Aga Khan Fund for Economic Development's 66-year association with NMG, shifting control to Azizi's investment vehicle.

Historic Ownership Transition Rostam Azizi, a prominent Tanzanian businessman and former politician, has secured a majority stake in Nation Media Group (NMG) by acquiring the 54.08 percent shareholding previously held by the Aga Khan Fund for Economic Development (AKFED). This transaction, executed through Azizi's company, Taarifa Ltd, involved the purchase of 100 percent of NPRT Holdings Africa Limited. NPRT was the investment vehicle that managed AKFED's controlling interest of 92,618,177 ordinary shares in NMG. The financial terms of this significant East African media transaction, one of the largest in decades, have not been publicly disclosed. Azizi's extensive business interests span multiple sectors, including mining, telecommunications, agriculture, real estate, energy, and construction.

End of an Era for AKFED The sale signals the end of AKFED's extensive 66-year involvement with NMG, a partnership that began in 1959 when Prince Karim Aga Khan IV established East African Newspapers (Nation Series) Ltd and acquired the Swahili weekly Taifa Leo. Over the decades, Nation Media Group evolved into a formidable multi-platform media conglomerate, currently operating over 30 brands across East Africa and reaching more than 62 million digital users, according to recent statistics. This acquisition occurs as NMG has undergone operational realignments, including the closure of its Mombasa bureau in March 2026 as part of a strategy to become a digital-first organization and consolidate operations. Azizi's acquisition represents a return to the media landscape for him, having previously co-founded a communications company whose influential Tanzanian publications were later acquired by NMG.

Commitment to Independence and Digital Growth Despite the changes inherent in ownership transitions, Mr. Azizi has publicly committed to upholding NMG's editorial independence. He also pledged investments in digital transformation and a strategic expansion of NMG's regional presence. "We are honoured and deeply committed to becoming the majority shareholder of Nation Media Group," Mr. Azizi stated. "NMG is an institution of profound importance to East Africa, and we will uphold its editorial independence while investing in its continued success as the region’s leading independent media organisation." This focus on digital initiatives aligns with NMG's ongoing North Star Strategy and the broader regional emphasis on robust digital infrastructure, a critical factor also highlighted by recent discussions surrounding topics such as Kenya's digital health crisis, underscoring the need for reliable digital systems in an increasingly interconnected East Africa.

Regulatory Approvals Pending The acquisition is currently awaiting regulatory approvals. These necessary clearances are anticipated to be finalized within the next three to four months. The transaction's cross-border nature implies review by multiple authorities, including the recently operationalized East African Community Competition Authority (EACCA), which began receiving applications for cross-border mergers and acquisitions from November 1, 2025. This introduces an additional layer of regulatory compliance for deals involving multiple EAC member states.
