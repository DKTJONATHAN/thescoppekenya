## Kenyan Shilling Navigates Marginal Slip Amidst Rising Remittances The Kenyan shilling experienced a slight depreciation against the U.S. dollar during the week ending March 12, 2026, despite a significant surge in diaspora remittances, which reached Ksh53.4 billion (USD 412.7 million) in February 2026, breaking a three-month year-on-year decline.

Shilling's Exchange Rate Movement The shilling concluded the week of March 12, 2026, exchanging at Ksh129.30 per dollar, a marginal slip from Ksh129.20 recorded on March 5. This 10-cent increase in the exchange rate maintains the shilling's position below the Ksh130 mark against the U.S. dollar for approximately 19 consecutive months. The Central Bank of Kenya (CBK) bulletin noted this movement, highlighting a gradual drift that has characterized recent weeks, with the unit averaging Ksh129.23 against the dollar from March 6 to 12.

Global Economic Pressures The shilling's performance occurred amidst a strengthening U.S. Dollar Index, which rose by 0.4 percent. This global appreciation of the dollar was largely influenced by elevated geopolitical risks and increased demand for safe-haven assets. The ongoing U.S.-Israel war on Iran has notably contributed to these complexities in global trade, creating an environment of market caution.

Diaspora Remittances Rebound February 2026 saw diaspora remittances climb to Ksh53.4 billion (USD 412.7 million), marking a notable increase of Ksh3.9 billion, or approximately 8 percent, compared to the Ksh49.43 billion received in February 2025. This rebound is crucial for the country's foreign exchange earnings and signals a recovery, breaking a three-month streak of year-on-year declines. Previous inflows had contracted by 8.2 percent in November 2025, followed by a 2.2 percent dip in December 2025, and a 0.3 percent decline in January 2026 compared to the same months a year earlier. Month-on-month, however, the figures show a slight retraction. February's Ksh53.4 billion came in Ksh1.9 billion lower, a 3.4 percent drop, compared to the Ksh55.3 billion (USD 427.4 million) recorded in January 2026, suggesting the rebound may still be finding its footing even as inflows hold above the USD 400 million threshold. Cumulative remittance inflows for the 12 months leading up to February 2026 reached Ksh653.3 billion (USD 5,051 million), reflecting a 1.9 percent increase from Ksh641 billion (USD 4,956 million) over the corresponding period in 2025. The CBK emphasized that "Remittance inflows remain a key source of foreign exchange earnings and continue to support the balance of payments."

Foreign Exchange Reserves and Global Inflation Kenya's foreign exchange reserves demonstrate a steady position, standing at Ksh1.8 trillion (USD 14,461 million) as of March 12, 2026, equivalent to 6.2 months of import cover. This comfortably exceeds the CBK's statutory minimum threshold of four months. Reserves had momentarily spiked to Ksh1.89 trillion (USD 14,597 million) the previous week before easing slightly. Globally, inflation concerns persisted during the week, impacting market sentiment. Volatility in international oil prices, primarily linked to the ongoing Middle East conflict, kept markets on edge. Murban crude oil prices surged to Ksh11,916 (USD 92.13) per barrel on March 12, up from Ksh9,862 (USD 76.25) just a week prior.
