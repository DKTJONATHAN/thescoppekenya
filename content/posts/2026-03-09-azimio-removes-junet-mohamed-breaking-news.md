## Azimio Coalition Reshuffles Leadership The Azimio la Umoja coalition party today, March 9, 2026, removed Junet Mohamed as its parliamentary group leader in the National Assembly. This decision follows a significant leadership shake-up, with Suba South MP Caroli Omondi immediately appointed to the position amidst broader changes affecting several prominent politicians within the coalition.

Junet Mohamed Replaced In a statement issued on Monday, March 9, the Azimio la Umoja coalition party announced the immediate termination of Junet Mohamed's appointment as the Leader of the Azimio Coalition Parliamentary Group in the National Assembly. Suba South MP Caroli Omondi has been appointed to the role with immediate effect. The statement, signed by Azimio coalition leader Kalonzo Musyoka, detailed these significant changes following a joint meeting of the coalition council and the National Coalition Executive Council.

Extensive Leadership Changes The coalition also announced the removal of several other key political figures from its leadership structures. Those removed from the Azimio Coalition Council include Cabinet Secretary for Mining and Blue Economy Hassan Joho, nominated MP Sabina Chege, Abdi Noor Omar, and Junet Mohamed. Additionally, changes within the Azimio National Coalition Executive Council saw the removal of members such as CSs Wycliffe Oparanya and Opiyo Wandayi, along with MPs Adan Keynan, David Pkosing, Mishi Mboko, Abdul Haji, Isabella Wangechi Githinji, Caroline Wanjiru Karugu, and Solomon Kuria.

Reasons for Removal According to the coalition's statement, these individuals forfeited their membership in the councils by virtue of accepting public appointments or having joined competing political coalitions. This decisive action underscores Azimio's commitment to internal cohesion amidst a period of significant political realignment. The coalition stated it will soon unveil replacements for the affected positions, as part of an expanded coalition structure.

Coalition Dynamics and Future These changes unfold weeks after Kalonzo Musyoka assumed the leadership role of the coalition, following the demise of its former leader, Raila Odinga. Many of those removed from positions within the coalition are members of the Orange Democratic Movement (ODM) party who have recently been affiliated with President William Ruto. Despite these removals, the ODM party has previously declared Azimio inexistent and indicated its intent to formally initiate the process to officially leave the coalition.
