## Jose Chameleone and Daniella Atim Finalize Child Support Agreement

Musician Jose Chameleone and his estranged wife, Daniella Atim, have settled on a mediated child support arrangement in Kampala. The agreement concludes a five-year separation and establishes a formal financial structure for their five children as the pair moves toward divorce proceedings.

This resolution, reached this week, transitions their long-term separation into a codified legal framework. Both parties opted for mediation to manage the logistics of split households rather than pursuing a contested public court battle. The settlement is currently awaiting the final signature of Justice Jane Okuo Kajuga, providing a clear path forward for the legal dissolution of a marriage that has effectively functioned across two continents for half a decade.

### The Financial Mechanics

The settlement features a multi-currency payment structure designed to address the challenges of maintaining a family in both Uganda and the United States. The monthly obligation is set at a value equivalent to approximately Ksh 260,000, split into two distinct tranches. Chameleone, legally named Joseph Mayanja, is scheduled to pay UGX 7.5 million for local maintenance in Uganda alongside USD 2,000 to support the children currently residing in the U.S.

By bifurcating the payment in this manner, the couple has addressed the instability often associated with fluctuating exchange rates and income volatility in the music industry. The structure aims to insulate the children’s standard of living from broader economic shifts. For the parents, this fixed arrangement removes the ambiguity that frequently characterizes post-separation financial support, allowing for more predictable budgeting and long-term planning.

### Judicial Oversight and Co-parenting

The role of Justice Jane Okuo Kajuga has been central to moving the process toward a conclusion. Her judicial oversight helped guide the parties toward a sustainable arrangement that avoids the common risks of post-divorce litigation. Under the terms of the agreement, primary physical custody of the children remains with Daniella Atim. However, the document includes specific provisions for Chameleone’s visitation rights, ensuring the musician maintains consistent contact with his children despite the significant geographic distance between them.

The five years preceding this settlement were marked by public uncertainty and informal arrangements. By finalizing these terms now, the couple has established a permanent structure that replaces the previous impasse. The transition to a judicially recognized contract allows both individuals to pursue their personal and professional objectives without the complication of ongoing legal disputes. This movement toward private, mediated settlements suggests that high-profile couples are increasingly prioritizing stability and confidentiality over traditional court proceedings.

### The Path Toward Finalization

The legal status of the settlement hinges on the final signature from Justice Jane Okuo Kajuga, which is anticipated by early next month. Once the document is formally endorsed, it will serve as the legal foundation for the subsequent divorce decree. 

Legal observers are now monitoring how these terms will be enforced in practice. While the current agreement creates a roadmap for financial responsibility, the real-world application of visitation rights and the potential for future adjustments regarding currency fluctuations remain critical variables. The resolution marks the end of a long period of ambiguity for the Mayanja family. By choosing a structured, private agreement, both parties have secured a tactical framework intended to minimize conflict and provide the necessary support for their children, effectively closing the chapter on years of uncertainty. The focus for both parents now shifts from negotiating the terms of their separation to the execution of this long-term co-parenting plan.
