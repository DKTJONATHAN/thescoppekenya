Kenya Railways issued a stern warning on Monday, April 20, 2026, cautioning the public against a fraudulent online job advertisement falsely listing over 251 vacancies. The transport corporation identified impersonation tactics used to extort unsuspecting job seekers across the nation.

## Kenya Railways Battles Online Recruitment Fraud

### False Vacancies and Impersonation Tactics
The fraudulent advertisement listed more than 250 positions, including Sales and Marketing Manager, General Manager Finance, Electrician, Mechanical Technician, CCTV Operator, Clearance and Forwarding Officer, and Office Assistant. Other roles advertised in the fake notice included Data Entry Clerk, Driver, Cleaner, Receptionist, Store Manager, and Legal Services Manager. Kenya Railways has firmly denied the existence of these vacancies.

A significant red flag in the scam was the application email address, which directed applicants to send documents to tokenyarailwaysjobs@gmail.com, a generic Gmail account with no official link to Kenya Railways. The legitimate communication for Kenya Railways exclusively uses the domain @krc.co.ke. Additionally, the fraudulent advert attempted to gain credibility by referencing Kenya Railways' genuine 2023 to 2027 Strategic Plan, framing the mass hiring as part of the Corporation's national growth agenda.

### Official Recruitment Policy and Channels
Kenya Railways reiterated that it does not charge any fees at any stage of its recruitment process, including application, shortlisting, interviews, or appointment. The corporation stressed that any individual or entity demanding payment in the name of Kenya Railways is fraudulent.

The transport body urged the public to rely solely on its credible communication channels for job opportunities. All legitimate vacancies are published exclusively on the official website, www.krc.co.ke, and in national daily newspapers. Applications are submitted strictly through the official Job Link available on the website.

### Teachers Service Commission Also Targeted
Kenya Railways is not the sole entity facing fraudulent recruitment attempts. The Teachers Service Commission (TSC) also flagged a fake recruitment drive on Monday, April 20, 2026, which purported to seek over 2000 teachers on permanent and pensionable terms for Junior Schools.

TSC warned the public that authentic job vacancies, recruitment guidelines, adverts, and official information are available only on its official website, verified official communication channels, and recognized national media outlets. This incident follows previous reports of fraudulent schemes involving fake TSC recruitment letters.
