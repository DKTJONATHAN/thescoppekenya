Prophet Victor Kanyari, the leader of Salvation Healing Ministry, recently sparked discussion after moving into his KSh 90 million Runda mansion only to find it isolating. The controversial preacher soon abandoned the luxury abode, revealing his preference for the vibrant community life he experienced in areas like Kayole.

Prophet Kanyari's Runda Reversal

Luxury's Lonesome Lullaby Prophet Victor Kanyari recently made headlines with his candid reflections on life inside his new KSh 90 million Runda mansion. The Salvation Healing Ministry leader, known for his controversial sermons, acquired the high-end property complete with modern amenities, including a swimming pool. However, the opulent surroundings quickly proved unfulfilling for the preacher, who confessed to becoming bored within just two days of moving in. He revealed to his congregation that the quiet, gated environment of Runda starkly contrasted the bustling atmosphere he was accustomed to in other parts of Nairobi.

The Allure of Community Kanyari openly described his experience as isolating, cautioning his followers that immense wealth without genuine social connections can often lead to a profound sense of emptiness. "Do not desire the life of wealth; it is a lonely life. It is better to have that money but eat with the people of Dandora and Githurai," he advised. He particularly missed the vibrant street life prevalent in areas like Dandora, Githurai, and Kayole, lamenting the absence of local street foods like mutura and mshikaki, and the everyday interactions where residents might address him as 'Mheshimiwa'. This sentiment echoes the kind of personal revelations that often capture public attention, much like [Professor Hamo's candid discussions on his personal life](https://zandani.co.ke/article/professor-hamo-shocking-infidelity-lessons-2024).

Kayole Calling Home Despite his daughter's platforms showcasing the grand mansion, Prophet Kanyari admitted the magnetic pull of his former lifestyle was undeniable. He revealed that shortly after his brief stay, he instructed his driver to return him to Kayole, where he found comfort and a stronger sense of belonging. "I told the driver, 'I am going back to Kayole. It is good here,'" he recounted, emphasizing that happiness is not solely defined by luxury but by community, connection, and a sense of belonging.
