As the afternoon progresses, the collective focus shifts from maintaining sales to protecting hardware. This digital blackout has forced a temporary, widespread return to analog systems. In stalls where sleek, touchscreen payment terminals usually reign supreme, owners have reverted to physical ledgers and chalkboard pricing. It is a striking reminder of how deeply electricity anchors the modern Kenyan economy. By tomorrow, the power will likely be stable again, and the screens will flicker back to life. But for today, the traders of Kiambu, Nyeri, and West Pokot are demonstrating that commerce does not simply collapse in the dark.
