Athlete Sebastian Sawe, from Kenya, shattered the world record at the London Marathon on Sunday, April 26, 2026, becoming the first man to officially run under two hours. His remarkable finish in 1:59:30 redefined distance running, marking a new era for the sport in a historic day for athletics.

## Historic Sub-Two-Hour Achievement

### Sawe's Record-Breaking Performance
Sebastian Sawe, a pre-race favorite, concluded the London Marathon with an unprecedented time of 1:59:30. This performance officially surpassed the previous marathon world record of 2:00:35, which was set by the late Kelvin Kiptum at the Chicago Marathon in 2023. The achievement marks a significant milestone in professional marathon running.

### Double Sub-Two-Hour Finish
Adding to the day's historic significance, Yofin Kejelcha of Ethiopia also completed the race in under two hours, clocking 1:59:41. This made Kejelcha the first Ethiopian runner to achieve this monumental feat. It was also the first occasion in history that two competitors officially finished an international marathon race in under two hours.

### Official vs. Unofficial Milestones
While Sawe's run is an official world record, Eliud Kipchoge previously ran a marathon distance in under two hours in Vienna, Austria, during the INEOS 1:59 Challenge in 2019, finishing in 1:59:40.2. This achievement was not formally recognized by World Athletics due to specialized pacing and a non-open competition format. This informal milestone, however, inspired athletes like Sawe globally to challenge established limits.

### Sawe's Consistent Excellence
Sawe's world record follows a period of notable achievements. He was crowned the World Marathon Majors (WMM) series champion for 2025 after securing victories in both the London and Berlin marathons within that calendar year. His 2:02:05 performance in Valencia in 2024 was also recognized as the second-fastest marathon debut in history at the time, trailing only Kelvin Kiptum's 2022 performance.

### Assefa Defends Title in Women's Race
In the women's elite race at the 2026 London Marathon, Ethiopia's Tigst Assefa successfully defended her title. Assefa set a new women-only world record of 2:15:41 in a compelling finish on April 26, 2026. She overcame her rivals Hellen Obiri and Joyciline Jepkosgei, both from Kenya. Degitu Azimeraw from Ethiopia and Catherine Reline Amanangole completed the top-five overall standings.
