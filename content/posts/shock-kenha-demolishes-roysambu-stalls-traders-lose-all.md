## KeNHA Demolishes Roysambu Stalls, Traders Face Devastation Traders operating along the Thika Superhighway in Roysambu incurred significant losses after the Kenya National Highways Authority (KeNHA), supported by security personnel, executed a pre-dawn demolition of structures built on the road reserve this Thursday morning. The operation rendered numerous individuals reliant on these businesses for their livelihoods without income, as roadside kiosks, stalls, and small shops were flattened.

The Demolition Exercise The demolition commenced at approximately 1:20 AM, with KeNHA officers and a large contingent of security personnel utilizing heavy machinery to bring down various structures. Families watched helplessly as their businesses, located near the Shell petrol station in Roysambu, were destroyed. Witnesses reported the deployment of hundreds of officers to secure the area, with the exercise extending into the morning hours. The activity also caused heavy traffic along sections of Thika Road as motorists slowed down to observe the unfolding events.

Authority's Justification and Future Plans KeNHA stated that the demolished structures had encroached on the road reserve, posing a serious safety risk to motorists and pedestrians utilizing the busy highway corridor. The authority confirmed that the demolition followed the expiry of an earlier notice issued to traders, instructing them to vacate the area voluntarily. This exercise is part of KeNHA's broader strategy to reorganize public transport operations along Thika Road. The plans include the construction of a modern bus park, similar to the one proposed for the Githurai area. KeNHA maintains that these developments aim to establish designated pick-up and drop-off points for buses and matatus, thereby improving order, enhancing commuter safety, and easing congestion along the vital transport artery.

Public and Political Reactions The demolitions have drawn criticism from some leaders and residents. Irungu Nyakera, Nairobi DCP Patron and gubernatorial aspirant, accused the government of carrying out the exercise without empathy for the affected traders. Nyakera highlighted a perceived disconnect between government promises of transforming Nairobi into a modern city and the reality of traders losing their income sources through abrupt demolitions. He commented on the cooperation agreement between the Nairobi City County Government and the National Government, suggesting it enables widespread demolitions without compassion. This incident follows a similar exercise in Githurai weeks prior, which led to protests, clashes between traders and police officers, and bonfires that temporarily disrupted transport along parts of the Thika Superhighway. Despite the backlash, KeNHA insists that the ongoing crackdown on unlawful structures will continue in other areas, including Kahawa West, as part of its efforts to reclaim road reserves and enhance safety along one of the country’s busiest transport corridors.
