The government has initiated the seizure of a blue Toyota TownAce vehicle following a High Court order issued on Friday, March 13, 2026. This forfeiture stems from a suspected money-laundering case, with Justice Wilfrida Okwany declaring the vehicle an instrument of crime in a ruling delivered February 9, 2026.

Court Orders Vehicle Forfeiture The forfeiture order, issued in the Anti-Corruption and Economic Crimes Division Case, mandates the surrender of the vehicle to the State. The Assets Recovery Agency had sought to have the car, chassis number SR40-0017560, declared proceeds of crime. Justice Okwany's ruling confirmed the vehicle's role in the commission of an offense, thereby making it liable for forfeiture to the Government of Kenya.

Judicial Basis for Seizure In her February 9, 2026, ruling, Justice Wilfrida Okwany explicitly stated, "This Honourable Court be pleased to issue orders declaring Motor Vehicle Chassis number SR40-0017560 as an instrument or vessel used or intended for use in the commission of an offence, therefore liable for forfeiture to the Government of Kenya." The court's directive ensures the transfer of the vehicle to the Assets Recovery Agency on behalf of the government.

Regulatory Directives Issued Following the court's decision, the National Transport and Safety Authority (NTSA) has been directed to register the forfeited vehicle in the Assets Recovery Agency's name within the national motor vehicle registry. This administrative step solidifies the government's claim over the asset, as outlined in the gazette notice issued on Friday, March 13, 2026.

Legal Framework for Forfeiture Under Kenyan law, the Proceeds of Crime and Anti-Money Laundering Act provides the legal basis for such forfeitures. Beyond money laundering offenses, vehicles can be forfeited if used in the commission of other crimes, if taxes or duties remain unpaid, or in cases of serious or repeated traffic violations. Money laundering is legally defined as any act concealing or disguising the nature, source, location, disposition, or movement of property that constitutes or forms part of the proceeds of crime. The Assets Recovery Agency possesses the authority to pursue forfeiture of property suspected to be linked to criminal activities, even when the property itself, rather than an individual, is named as the respondent in legal proceedings.
