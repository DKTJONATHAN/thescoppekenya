Treasury Cabinet Secretary John Mbadi and youth activist Rosemary Alionya encountered intense backlash from youth representatives during a forum regarding the 2025/2026 Finance Bill this week. The event, intended as a consultation, quickly became a heated confrontation over the government’s proposed tax strategies.

## Tensions Rise Over Economic Policy

### A Chasm Between Officials and Citizens
The atmosphere inside the venue was charged with frustration as government officials attempted to advocate for the new tax proposals before a skeptical audience. While officials utilized complex, bureaucratic language to frame their agenda, the young attendees rejected the narrative entirely. This disconnect highlighted a recurring issue where leaders appear to operate from a script that ignores the daily economic struggles of a generation burdened by rising costs of living.

### The Problem with Performative Engagement
The forum was widely criticized by participants as a performative exercise lacking substantive results. Attendees mocked the session as a gathering of elite figures who favor empty rhetoric over tangible solutions. Rosemary Alionya faced direct scrutiny from youth leaders who argued that the government is prioritizing policy jargon over necessary tax relief. This sentiment reflects a growing impatience with town hall settings that fail to address the core grievances of the public.

### Escalating Threats of Public Unrest
The mood among the attendees signaled that the era of passive engagement is over. The discourse moved away from policy questions and toward blunt demands for government accountability. The youth representatives made it clear that if the current tax burdens are enforced without relief, the potential for renewed public protests is high. As the government continues to navigate public pressure, the message from the floor remains consistent: policy decisions made behind closed doors will face significant resistance on the streets if they continue to exacerbate the struggles of the average taxpayer.
