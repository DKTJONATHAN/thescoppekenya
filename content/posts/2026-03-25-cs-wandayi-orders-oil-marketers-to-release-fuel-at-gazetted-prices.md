Energy and Petroleum Cabinet Secretary Opiyo Wandayi on Wednesday, March 25, 2026, ordered oil marketers to immediately release all withheld petroleum products. He warned that hoarding fuel in anticipation of higher prices is exploitative and will face legal action and regulatory sanctions from the government.

Government Mandates Fuel Release Amid Hoarding Concerns

Marketers Accused of Hoarding Cabinet Secretary Opiyo Wandayi, speaking during a press conference, accused a segment of oil marketers and retailers of deliberately withholding fuel. This action, he stated, is an attempt to profit from future price increases and constitutes a breach of licensing conditions. Wandayi affirmed that the government would pursue appropriate legal action against any entities found responsible for such conduct.

Assured Supply and Gazetted Prices The Energy and Petroleum Cabinet Secretary assured the public of sufficient national fuel reserves, dismissing fears of an impending shortage and advising against panic buying. He stressed that all licensed marketers must ensure a consistent supply of petroleum products to the local market. Products must be sold strictly at prices gazetted by the Energy and Petroleum Regulatory Authority (EPRA), with non-compliance potentially leading to regulatory sanctions, including license suspension or revocation. The ministry also confirmed no immediate plans exist to adjust pump prices.

Regional Market Stability The ministry highlighted that government-to-government fuel supply agreements remain robust. Partners such as Saudi Aramco, Abu Dhabi National Oil Company, and Emirates National Oil Company have not indicated any disruption to existing supply arrangements. In the neighboring nation, Uganda's Energy Minister, Ruth Nankabirwa Ssentamu, also reassured its citizens of a stable fuel supply, cautioning oil companies against unwarranted price increases. Uganda, which largely depends on the port of Mombasa for its imports, reports having sufficient stocks for at least 21 days. Currently, petrol sells at approximately Ksh173 per litre and diesel at Ksh164 per litre in Uganda. In Nairobi, super petrol is priced at Ksh178.28 per litre, diesel at Ksh166.54, and kerosene at Ksh152.78.
