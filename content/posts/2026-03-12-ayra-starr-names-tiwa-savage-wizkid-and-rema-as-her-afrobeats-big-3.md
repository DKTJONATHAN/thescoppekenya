## Celebrating Afrobeats: Ayra Starr's Esteemed Triumvirate Nigerian music luminary Ayra Starr recently unveiled her Afrobeats 'Big 3' during a New York interview, naming Tiwa Savage, Wizkid, and Rema. This selection highlights the diverse talent shaping the genre's global appeal and underscores the interconnectedness within Africa's vibrant music community.

Ayra Starr's Revered Selections During a promotional appearance on New York's HOT 97 FM for her latest single, 'Where Do We Go,' Ayra Starr thoughtfully presented her top three Afrobeats artists: Tiwa Savage, Wizkid, and Rema. “My Big 3, I will say Tiwa Savage, obviously, Wizkid, Rema. That’s my solid big 3,” she stated. This choice resonates with her consistent public admiration and deep respect for each artist, who collectively represent significant facets of the Pan-African music scene. Her selections offer an insightful perspective on the influential figures currently propelling Afrobeats onto the world stage, contributing to the rich tapestry of [African Celebrity News & Gossip: Beyond the Global Front Rows](https://zandani.co.ke/article/african-celebrity-news-gossip-global-front-rows).

Collaborations and Connections Ayra Starr's musical journey has seen meaningful collaborations with her chosen "Big 3." Her bond with Wizkid is particularly notable; he featured on "2 Sugar" from his fifth album, 'More Love, Less Ego', and she later featured him on her 2025 track 'Gimme Dat'. This collaboration earned them a nomination for Best African Music Performance at the 2026 Grammys. Her admiration for Tiwa Savage, an iconic figure in Nigerian pop, led to their 2024 collaboration on 'Gara.' This song was part of the soundtrack album for Savage's acting debut, 'Water & Garri.' Perhaps the most public and closest connection is with Rema, her label mate at Mavin Records. Their friendship has been openly shared, with Rema contributing a guest verse to Ayra Starr's 2025 track 'Who's Dat Girl.' The music video for 'Who's Dat Girl' sparked romance rumors, which both artists have consistently denied.

The Artistry Behind "Where Do We Go" and Upcoming Album In her HOT 97 FM interview, Ayra Starr also provided insights into her latest single, 'Where Do We Go.' She revealed the song was inspired by a secret, emotionally uncertain relationship she described as unhealthy. Starr characterized the track as an attempt to "trauma dump," exploring the cyclical nature of returning to a relationship despite knowing it is not beneficial. Looking ahead, the award-winning artist confirmed plans to release her third album later this year. She indicated a departure from her previous numerical album titles, '19 and Dangerous' and 'The Year I Turned 21,' signaling a new creative phase. This evolution in her artistic approach reflects the dynamic and constantly evolving landscape of Afrobeats, where artists continually explore new narratives and sounds. Like Omah Lay's recent album announcement which attributes Afrobeats origins to specific cultural roots, Ayra Starr's journey exemplifies the genre's diverse artistic expressions across the continent.
