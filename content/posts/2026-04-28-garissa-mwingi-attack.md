## Garissa Mwingi Attack Halts Highway Transport

Suspected attackers ambushed a passenger bus on the Garissa-Mwingi highway in Kitui County on Tuesday evening, April 28, 2026, causing fears of injuries and temporarily halting transport operations due to escalating community tensions. The **Garissa Mwingi attack** forced other vehicles to stop amid safety concerns along the busy route.

### Bus Ambushed Near Ukasi

The passenger bus, traveling between Garissa and Mwingi, came under a stone attack near the Ukasi area. Videos seen by a local publication showed a speeding bus with its windscreen completely shattered from the pelting. Despite the assault, the driver reportedly continued in an attempt to escape the incident. Following the ambush, fears spread, leading other vehicles operating on the Garissa-Mwingi highway to halt their journeys.

### Escalating Tensions Trigger Halt

The incident prompted dozens of traveler buses to stop mid-journey as a safety precaution. Drivers and passengers stated they would not proceed with travel until their security was assured, citing escalating tensions between two warring communities in the area. This disruption to transport echoes other challenges in the sector, such as when the [NTSA Seeks Injunction After Court Suspends Instant Traffic Fine System](https://zandani.co.ke/article/ntsa-seeks-injunction-after-court-suspends-instant-traffic-fine-syst) affected traffic management. The current unrest follows reports of two individuals killed in the area by unknown assailants in what are believed to be revenge attacks.

### Police Respond to Retaliatory Killings

The **Garissa Mwingi attack** occurs two days after seven residents were killed in Tseikuru, Mwingi, Kitui County, on Sunday, April 26, in what police described as retaliatory attacks. Police reports indicate the attackers stormed the village, opened fire, and killed the victims before fleeing. The National Police Service stated that these attacks stem from a series of earlier clashes between residents and a pastoralist community. Following the Sunday incident, Inspector General of Police Douglas Kanja dispatched a high-powered security team, led by Deputy Inspector General Eliud Lagat, to the Kwa Kamari area in Tseikuru. Kanja has called for calm and peace, emphasizing intensified efforts to find a lasting solution.
