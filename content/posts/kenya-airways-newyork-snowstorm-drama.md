## What's the Latest on Kenya Airways' New York Drama?

Kenya Airways has cancelled two crucial flights on its Nairobi-New York route, KQ002 and KQ003, due to a severe blizzard warning that has forced John F. Kennedy International Airport (JFK) to suspend operations on Sunday, February 22, 2026, and Monday, February 23, 2026. This sudden disruption has left passengers stranded on both sides of the Atlantic, facing significant delays.

### The Scoop

Honestly, it's a right old mess at JFK, by all accounts! Our lovely Kenya Airways, bless 'em, had to make the tough call to ground their Sunday flight from Nairobi (that's KQ002, if you're keeping tabs) and the return trip from New York for Monday (that'll be KQ003). All because a monster snowstorm has literally shut down the Big Apple's main airport. (What a nuisance for everyone, eh?). Passengers are, of course, absolutely fuming, stuck in limbo on one of the world's longest non-stop routes.

### The Evidence

The airline themselves put out a statement, confirming the "blizzard warning and severe weather conditions" and that JFK's operations are suspended from Sunday, February 22, 2026. And let me tell you, it's not just a bit of a flurry; New York is bracing for a truly enormous snowstorm, with reports of up to two feet of snow expected! They've even got travel bans in place, for goodness sake. This isn't just a slight delay, it's a full-on, potentially historic blizzard, according to the boffins!

This Nairobi-New York route, you see, is a big deal. Kenya Airways is the *only* airline that does a non-stop flight, saving travellers hours of faffing about with connections through places like London or Amsterdam. Since 2018, they've whisked over 300,000 people and tonnes of cargo across those 7,351 miles. It's usually a smooth 15-hour journey on their fancy Boeing 787-8 Dreamliner. But not this time, mate!

And get this, it's not the first time KQ has faced weather woes in recent weeks. If you recall [what happened last week with their Rwanda flights](#), they had to ground those too due to poor visibility. Honestly, it's just one thing after another for them!

### The Verdict

Kenya Airways has apologised profusely, naturally, for all the bother. They're hoping to get KQ002 back in the air on Tuesday, February 24, and KQ003 on Wednesday, February 25, all subject to the weather gods smiling upon New York and the airport bigwigs giving the all-clear. Passengers have been told to keep an eye on the airline's website, which is always sound advice. (Wouldn't want to turn up and find yourself in another pickle, would you?). The airline insists that customer and crew safety is top priority, and you can't really argue with that, can you? It sounds like New York really is taking a battering, with thousands of flights cancelled across the East Coast, not just KQ's. So, our Kenyan friends are certainly not alone in this winter wonderland nightmare! Fingers crossed everyone gets where they need to be soon, without any more dramatic twists and turns!
