## KNEC Publishes Comprehensive 2026 KCSE Examination Timetable The Kenya National Examination Council (KNEC) has officially released the timetable for the 2026 Kenya Certificate of Secondary Education (KCSE) examinations. The rigorous national assessment is set to commence on October 19 and will conclude on November 20.

Early Start with Oral and Practical Assessments The 2026 KCSE examination period will officially begin on Monday, October 19, with oral and practical papers. Subjects scheduled for this initial phase include French, German, Sign Language, and Music. Following these, practical examinations for Home Science, Art and Design, Building Construction, Electricity, and French will take place between Wednesday, October 21, and Monday, October 26, ensuring an early start to the assessment process.

Theory Examinations Kick Off in November Theory examinations are scheduled to begin in earnest on Monday, November 2. The day will feature English and Chemistry papers, each lasting two hours, with a three-hour and 30-minute break separating the two sessions. On Tuesday, November 3, candidates will undertake the mandatory first Mathematics paper, lasting two hours and 30 minutes, followed by the English Grammar paper beginning at 2 p.m. Wednesday, November 4, will see the second Chemistry paper administered as the first examination of the day, followed by the English Composition and Essay paper from 2 p.m. to 4:30 p.m. Thursday, November 5, is designated for the first Kiswahili paper (Lugha) from 8 a.m. to 10:30 a.m., with the Kiswahili (Insha) paper following from 2 p.m. to 4:15 p.m.

Second Week Intensifies with Core Subjects The second week of examinations will open on Monday, November 9, with the second Mathematics paper, followed by the third Kiswahili paper (Fasihi) in the afternoon. Tuesday, November 10, will feature Religious Education examinations from 8 a.m. to 10:30 a.m., succeeded by the first Biology paper from 2 p.m. to 4 p.m. On Wednesday, November 11, the second Religious Education paper will be administered for two hours and 30 minutes, followed by the first History and Government paper in the afternoon. Thursday, November 12, will include the second Biology paper from 8 a.m. to 10 a.m., and the second History and Government paper from 2 p.m. to 4:30 p.m. The second week will conclude with Biology practicals, scheduled for the entire day, with each learner allocated a one-hour and 45-minute period.

Final Stretch and Conclusion The final segment of the examinations will commence on Monday, November 16, with a Geography paper, followed by the first Physics paper from 2 p.m. to 4 p.m. On Tuesday, November 17, candidates will sit for Business Studies examinations, with Agriculture following in the afternoon, each paper taking two hours. Wednesday, November 18, will see the second Geography paper, followed by the second Physics paper in the afternoon. Candidates undertaking Agriculture and Business Studies will complete their second papers on Thursday, November 19. The 2026 KCSE examinations will officially conclude on Friday, November 20, with a Physics practical session, lasting two hours and 30 minutes for each student.
