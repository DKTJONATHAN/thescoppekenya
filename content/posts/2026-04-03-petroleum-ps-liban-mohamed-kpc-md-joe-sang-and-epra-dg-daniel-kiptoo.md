Petroleum Principal Secretary Mohamed Liban, Energy and Petroleum Regulatory Authority (EPRA) Director General Daniel Kiptoo, and Kenya Pipeline Company (KPC) Managing Director Joe Sang were among four senior government officials arrested Thursday evening. The arrests stem from allegations of procuring substandard fuel, raising significant national concerns about fuel quality standards in 2026.

Substandard Fuel Procurement Allegations Detectives from the Directorate of Criminal Investigations (DCI) apprehended the officials and subsequently transported them to the DCI Headquarters. There, they underwent grilling regarding claims of approving the procurement of fuel that reportedly failed to meet required quality standards. The DCI has not yet released comprehensive details regarding the ongoing saga.

Investigation Details Unfold Sources indicate that the investigative agency is actively probing the procurement processes, supply chains, and potential collusion among the senior officials. The ongoing investigation is expected to encompass how substandard fuel entered the country and whether established due processes were bypassed during the procurement stages. If found culpable, the implicated government officials could face severe charges, including potential abuse of office.

Public Concerns and Regulatory Scrutiny These arrests come at a time when motorists have consistently voiced concerns about fuel quality, with various reports linking engine damage to contaminated fuel. EPRA has previously provided assurances to the public regarding strict compliance measures designed to uphold fuel standards. However, these latest developments cast doubt on the regulator's effectiveness in maintaining these critical standards within the country. This incident underscores broader challenges in ensuring public safety and regulatory compliance, much like the recent efforts by KeNHA to improve urban infrastructure, as evidenced by the reopening of the Globe Roundabout following the completion of bridge works.
