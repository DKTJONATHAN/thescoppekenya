Residents of Kibra, Westlands, Kilimani, and Dagoretti, represented by the Upper Hill District Association (UHDA), have given Interior Cabinet Secretary Kipchumba Murkomen and Inspector General Douglas Kanja 14 days to reverse the planned closure of Capitol Hill Police Station. They warned on Friday, April 10, 2026, of a critical security gap in Upper Hill.

Upper Hill Security Under Threat The Upper Hill District Association, led by Chairperson Justus Mungai, voiced concerns that the station's closure could significantly compromise security in Upper Hill, an area crucial for thousands of workers, residents, hospitals, courts, and businesses. The facility is deemed critical for crime prevention and rapid emergency response, supporting key institutions like Kenyatta National Hospital and Milimani Court. The Ministry of Interior had announced the official degazettement of the Capitol Hill Police Station, leading to the displacement and redeployment of more than 50 police officers just three days prior, on April 8, 2026.

Allegations of Constitutional Violations The residents accused the Ministry of Interior of violating the Constitution by proceeding with the closure without conducting a public participation exercise. The Association cited Articles 10 and 35 of the Constitution, which uphold public involvement in decision-making and ensure access to information. They argued that the degazettement could result in the irreversible loss of this public facility, preventing its future recovery for security purposes in the high-density, high-value Upper Hill area.

Call for Action and Ministry's Stance Failure to meet their demands within the 14-day period, the Upper Hill District Association warned, would compel them to pursue legal and civic action to protect the community’s constitutional rights. Meanwhile, in a notice issued on April 8, 2026, former Officer Commanding Station (OCS) Tusca Opondo directed investigators and the public to collect pending case exhibits and cash bail deposits from the station. Opondo urged multi-agency investigators to immediately collect exhibits, including vehicles, currently held at the station. The Ministry stated that the degazettement is part of a broader restructuring effort aimed at improving policing efficiency and redistributing resources across Nairobi County. The Upper Hill District, home to Milimani Court and numerous law firms, emphasizes the vital role the Capitol Hill Police Station plays in upholding law and order, extending beyond simple patrols to supporting complex legal processes and investigations, as seen in cases involving public interest.
