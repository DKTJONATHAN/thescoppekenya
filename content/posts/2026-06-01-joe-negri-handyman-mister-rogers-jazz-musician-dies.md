## Jazz Maestro Joe Negri Death Marks End of an Era

Pittsburgh, PA - Joe Negri, the acclaimed jazz guitarist who also endeared himself to generations as Handyman Negri on 'Mister Rogers' Neighborhood,' has died. He was 99. Negri passed away peacefully on Saturday, May 30, just days before his centennial birthday on June 10, his daughter Lisa Negri confirmed, citing natural causes. His death marks the close of a remarkable life dedicated to music, education, and quiet influence.

### A Dual Legacy: Television and Jazz

Negri's career seamlessly blended high-caliber musicianship with accessible children's entertainment. As Handyman Negri in the 'Neighborhood of Make-Believe,' he served as a comforting presence from 1968 to 2001, often using his guitar to communicate on the beloved children's program. Concurrently, Negri cultivated a formidable reputation in the jazz scene. A virtuoso on guitar, he performed with numerous legends throughout his career, including Pittsburgh's own jazz piano maestro Johnny Costa, and toured extensively. His understated dedication to his craft later influenced contemporary masters including Yo-Yo Ma and Wynton Marsalis, a testament to his profound musical impact.

Beyond his public performances, Negri committed decades to arts education, shaping countless students. He held respected adjunct professorships at several prominent Pittsburgh institutions, including the University of Pittsburgh, Duquesne University, and Carnegie Mellon University. His nearly 50-year tenure at the University of Pittsburgh concluded in 2019, followed by his retirement from Duquesne University in 2022. During these roles, he not only taught instrumental technique but also instilled a broader appreciation for music theory and performance ethics. This enduring dedication to nurturing young talent highlighted his deep conviction in music's transformative power and the importance of mentorship.
