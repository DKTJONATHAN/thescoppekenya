<h2>Another Resignation in Marseille Rocks Olympique Ahead of Lyon Clash</h2> Olympique Marseille finds itself in yet another leadership crisis as president Pablo Longoria has resigned, a move confirmed just a day before a pivotal Ligue 1 clash against rivals Lyon. This sudden departure, which saw Alban Juster appointed interim chairman, plunges the club into further instability at a crucial juncture of their season. The news broke on Saturday, March 1, 2026, that Pablo Longoria, who had served as president for five years, had stepped down. His exit comes amidst reports of diminished powers, with his responsibilities reportedly restricted to institutional representation within French and European governing bodies. Taking the helm as interim chairman of the management board is Alban Juster, previously the club's Chief Financial & Compliance Officer, a decision made by the Supervisory Board. This interim arrangement is in place as the club searches for a permanent president. <h3>Why the President Is Gone: Power Struggles at the Core</h3> The latest resignation in Marseille highlights a deeper, more troubling narrative of power struggles and instability at the club. Longoria's departure, coming after his powers were reportedly curtailed, points to significant internal dynamics at play. This follows a period of notable upheaval, including the earlier departure of coach Roberto De Zerbi on February 11, 2026, by mutual agreement after a 5-0 thrashing by PSG and a Champions League exit. Adding to the drama, sporting director Medhi Benatia had also tendered his resignation, only to be persuaded by owner Frank McCourt to remain until the end of the season. Benatia, who was appointed Director of Football in January 2025, has since been granted more authority, personally selecting Habib Beye as the new coach. Benatia has publicly denied any "intrigue or power grab," stating his decision to stay was out of responsibility and loyalty to the club. However, the sequence of events paints a picture of intense behind-the-scenes maneuvering that ultimately led to the president being gone. <h3>The Impact of Another Resignation on Marseille's Champions League Hopes</h3> This latest non-football event has undoubtedly cast a long shadow over the squad, just as they prepare for a monumental clash against Olympique Lyon. New coach Habib Beye, appointed on February 18, 2026, after Roberto De Zerbi's exit, is tasked with navigating this turbulent period. Beye's first game in charge was a defeat from Brest, a less-than-ideal start to his tenure. Marseille currently sits five points behind Lyon, who are third in Ligue 1, making today's match crucial for securing a direct Champions League spot. A draw or, worse, a defeat, would severely damage their qualification chances, a scenario the club is desperate to avoid after their past struggles in the qualification rounds.
