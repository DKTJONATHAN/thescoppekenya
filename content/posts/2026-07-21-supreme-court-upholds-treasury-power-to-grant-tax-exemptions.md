## The Tax Fairness Gap: Navigating the Supreme Court’s Treasury Ruling Supreme Court Upholds Treasury Power to Grant Tax Exemptions is the central subject of the update.

The Supreme Court recently upheld the Treasury’s authority to grant tax exemptions to foreign investors through executive gazette notices. For the average Kenyan entrepreneur, this decision confirms a two-tier tax environment. While international firms operate under a system of fiscal sanctuary, local businesses remain responsible for the full spectrum of mandatory tax obligations.

The legal proceedings focused on whether the Treasury requires parliamentary oversight to override established tax codes. However, the practical consequences fall squarely on the balance sheets of local suppliers. When the government commissions major infrastructure initiatives, such as those at the Dongo Kundu Special Economic Zone or Olkaria, contracts often include broad tax concessions. Consequently, foreign entities gain the ability to underbid local competitors who must account for VAT, corporate taxes, and standard regulatory fees in their pricing models.

### An Unequal Economic Landscape

SME advocacy groups have long warned that these exemptions force an artificial cap on market prices. Because a foreign firm can use a gazette notice to bypass income tax, they gain a structural advantage that allows for lower service rates on government projects. Conversely, a local contractor providing logistics or construction materials cannot opt out of their tax liabilities.

This ruling solidifies the standing of 16 existing bilateral agreements that faced previous legal challenges. These deals, often tied to loans or grants, grant the Treasury the legal mandate to waive significant tax revenue. Supporters suggest this approach is a necessary incentive to lure foreign capital into the country. Nevertheless, the reality for local SMEs is that they often find themselves locked out of large-scale procurement. When domestic firms are unable to compete with tax-subsidized pricing, they lose their role in the very projects intended to drive national development.

### The Consequences of Executive Authority

Petitioner Eliud Karanja Matindi sought to challenge these exemptions, yet the court found insufficient evidence of discriminatory impact to override existing executive powers. Regardless of the legal technicalities, traders under the strict oversight of the Kenya Revenue Authority feel the financial strain daily. The chasm between an effective tax rate of zero for certain international entities and the rigorous compliance demanded from a local enterprise creates a significant market distortion.

This disparity serves as a silent barrier to entry. Local businesses are expected to demonstrate superior efficiency and innovation while simultaneously managing a cost structure that is inherently more expensive than that of their global peers. Just as legal systems strive for greater efficiency in areas like magistrate court claims, there is a growing need for a tax framework that provides clarity and equity rather than penalizing domestic operators.

### Strategic Survival in a Strained Market

Now that the legal framework is established, the focus for small business owners must shift toward advocacy for transparency. While the court has affirmed the Treasury's authority, the demand for public accountability remains critical. If tax holidays are truly essential for attracting investment, the government should provide verifiable data showing that these losses are offset by measurable local job creation or technology transfer.

For business owners pursuing state-supported contracts, it is essential to integrate these market distortions into long-term strategic planning. Start by auditing your supply chains to identify if you are in direct competition with firms benefiting from gazetted protections. If your industry is saturated with foreign entities holding these exemptions, attempt to pivot toward specialized service niches. 

In these areas, the advantages of local proximity, cultural insight, and rapid response times can often outweigh the price-cutting power of larger, foreign-owned firms. While the legal environment currently favors international tax exemptions, the long-term sustainability of your business relies on maximizing the value of your local market expertise. By focusing on areas where agility outshines raw capital, you can carve out a viable space despite the structural hurdles present in the current economy.
