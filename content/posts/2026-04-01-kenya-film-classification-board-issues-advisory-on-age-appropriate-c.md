The Kenya Film Classification Board (KFCB) has issued a critical advisory to entertainment operators and parents concerning age-appropriate digital content. This directive arrives as children commence their April school holidays in 2026, addressing heightened screen time and potential exposure to harmful material, urging vigilant content monitoring.

## KFCB Reinforces Content Vigilance for April Holidays

### Guidelines for Entertainment Operators

The KFCB has cautioned video show and gaming operators, alongside other film exhibitors, against admitting children into their premises unless the content is rated as General Exhibition (GE). This measure aims to protect minors from material that could negatively influence their behavior and thinking during their break.

### Parental Responsibility in Digital Consumption

Parents and guardians are urged to assume a more active role in monitoring their children's digital consumption throughout the holiday period. Key recommendations include setting screen-time limits and activating parental control features on televisions, streaming platforms, and other digital devices to restrict access to inappropriate material. The board also encourages families to watch content together, fostering discussions and guiding children. Guiding children towards educational and value-based programming that promotes creativity and critical thinking is also advised. The diverse range of online content available, including celebrity news such as [Njugush's interactions with Celestine Ndinda’s social media post amid relationship speculation](https://zandani.co.ke/article/njugush-interacts-with-celestine-ndinda-s-social-media-post-amid-rel) or personal announcements like [Fatma Banj's separation from Xtian Dela](https://zandani.co.ke/article/fatma-banj-announces-separation-from-xtian-dela), underscores the ongoing need for parental guidance.

### Public Reporting and Digital Literacy Initiatives

The public is encouraged to report any content believed to be harmful or unsuitable for children directly to the KFCB for review and appropriate classification. The advisory also emphasizes the importance of parents remaining aware of their children's whereabouts during their free time. The KFCB pointed to its Parents’ Digital Literacy Programme (PADIL) as an ongoing effort to equip guardians with the necessary skills to navigate the ever-evolving digital landscape, empowering them to monitor and guide children in accessing and consuming content across various digital platforms.

### Collective Responsibility for Child Protection

The board emphasized that protecting children from exposure to inappropriate content is a collective responsibility. This calls for cooperation among parents, entertainment operators, and regulatory bodies to ensure a safe digital environment for minors.
