Nairobi Senator Edwin Sifuna's Linda Mwananchi rally in Narok Town faced a temporary disruption today, Sunday, March 22, 2026, after a teargas canister was deployed amidst the gathering. The incident briefly halted proceedings before leaders urged calm and continued the engagement with the crowd despite adverse conditions.

Narok Rally Faces Disruption

Pre-Rally Tensions Mount Ahead of the planned rally in Narok Town, following a church service at Covenant Church International (CCI) Ntulele earlier today, police reportedly ordered the removal of speakers. Senator Sifuna's team, however, maintained their defiance, asserting that authorities had received prior notification regarding the Sunday event.

Teargas Deployment and Continuation During the rally, a teargas canister was thrown into the eager crowd. Images and videos obtained by Za Ndani depict Senator Sifuna alongside Embakasi East Member of Parliament Babu Owino enduring the teargas and adverse rainy conditions, as they encouraged attendees to remain composed. Following the brief interruption, both leaders proceeded with the rally, engaging the public amidst the lingering smoke.

Pattern of Disruptions to Linda Mwananchi Tour Since its inception in February, the Orange Democratic Movement (ODM) party's Linda Mwananchi tour has been repeatedly marred by disruptions. Prior incidents include a teargas deployment in Kitengela, which abruptly ended a rally there. Similar chaos was reported at meetings in Kakamega and Mbale towns. These disruptions have, on occasion, tragically led to loss of lives. This concerning trend underscores broader public safety challenges. Efforts to enhance public order and accountability are crucial, aligning with calls for stricter measures to protect citizens, reminiscent of appeals for improved safety frameworks.

Allegations and Counter-Allegations Organisers of the Linda Mwananchi tour have attributed these disruptions to what they term "state-sponsored goons" and police interference. Conversely, police investigations have suggested that rally organisers deliberately hired individuals to instigate chaos, an accusation vehemently denied by the faction led by Senator Sifuna.
