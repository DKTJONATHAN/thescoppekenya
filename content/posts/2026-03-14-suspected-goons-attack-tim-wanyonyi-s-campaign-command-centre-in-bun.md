## Campaign Centre Attacked in Bungoma Suspected assailants targeted Westlands MP Tim Wanyonyi's campaign command centre in Makutano, Kanduyi Constituency, Bungoma County, on Saturday, March 14, 2026. The incident at the Eninga Command Centre resulted in injuries to security personnel and property damage, prompting a police investigation into the coordinated attack.

Details of the Incident Chaos erupted early Saturday morning when a group of suspected goons stormed the Eninga Command Centre. Security personnel stationed at the facility were forced to defend themselves as attackers threw stones and vandalized property, shattering windows. Several security officers sustained injuries during the assault. Police officers from Makutano swiftly responded to the scene to restore order and safeguard the property.

Police Response and Investigation Makutano police have initiated investigations into the incident, with authorities vowing to identify and apprehend those responsible for the attack. As of the time of publication, no arrests had been made. Wanyonyi, who was attending a fundraising event, had not yet issued a public statement regarding the matter.

Political Context and Wanyonyi's Aspirations The Eninga Command Centre, known for its association with Tim Wanyonyi, has reportedly been a source of contention in some political circles. Wanyonyi, the sitting Westlands MP, is currently focusing his efforts on Bungoma County, where he is actively campaigning for the gubernatorial seat in the 2027 general election. He secured the Westlands parliamentary seat in 2022 under an Orange Democratic Movement (ODM) ticket. His brother, Moses Wetang'ula, leads FORD-Kenya, a party with significant influence within Bungoma County. For much of 2025, political analysts and residents in Nairobi had anticipated Wanyonyi would contest the capital's top seat. However, in June of last year, the lawmaker announced his disinterest in the Nairobi gubernatorial position, confirming his intention to run for Bungoma's governorship instead. He expressed optimism that serving his home county would provide a fitting culmination to his political career. Wanyonyi's campaign for the 2027 gubernatorial seat will likely focus on issues affecting residents, including economic stability at a time when many continue to feel the impact of inflation. Wanyonyi is expected to face strong competition in the upcoming elections. Other key contestants who have declared their interest include MP Didmus Barasa, former Governor Wycliffe Wafula Wangamati, and current Deputy Governor Jenipher Mbatiany.
