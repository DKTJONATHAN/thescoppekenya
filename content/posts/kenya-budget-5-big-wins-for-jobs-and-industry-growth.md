## Kenya's Budget Blueprint: Five Key Boosts for Employment and Enterprise Kenya’s 2026/27 budget outlines key economic strategies. Projected 5.3% growth, driven by increased spending, targets critical sectors: housing, manufacturing, energy, and grassroots enterprises. These efforts aim to energise job creation and industry, fostering a robust, stable economy.

Affordable Housing Drive The government's focus on affordable housing is set to significantly stimulate the construction sector. This initiative will not only address the acute housing shortage but also generate substantial employment opportunities and drive urban development across the country. This pillar remains central to the government’s economic strategy.

County Aggregation and Industrial Parks A crucial move towards decentralised industrialisation involves the implementation of 47 County Aggregation and Industrial Parks (CAIPs). These parks are designed to boost manufacturing and agro-processing, promoting value addition, reducing post-harvest losses, and creating jobs directly within rural production zones.

Expanded Electricity Generation Kenya plans a substantial increase in electricity generation capacity by 10,000 megawatts, leveraging geothermal, solar, wind, and hydropower sources. This expansion is vital for supporting industrialisation ambitions and reinforcing the country's leading position in renewable energy.

Grassroots Enterprise Support Government financing programmes such as the Hustler Fund, Youth Enterprise Fund, Women Enterprise Fund, and NYOTA are continuing to provide essential support to Micro, Small, and Medium Enterprises (MSMEs). These initiatives address the common challenge of accessing formal credit, thereby nurturing business growth and employment at the grassroots level.

Strong Economic Growth Trajectory The economy is projected to grow to 5.3% in 2026, a notable increase from 4.7% in 2024, underpinned by improved macroeconomic stability. This robust growth is expected to be primarily driven by key sectors including agriculture, construction, tourism, transport, and financial services, creating a fertile environment for job expansion and industry development.
