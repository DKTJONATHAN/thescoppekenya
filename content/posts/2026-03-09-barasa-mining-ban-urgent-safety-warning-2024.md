## Kakamega Governor Halts Mining Operations Amidst Flood Risks Kakamega Governor Fernandes Barasa has mandated a temporary cessation of all mining activities within the county, citing the imminent dangers posed by ongoing heavy rainfall and widespread flooding. This urgent directive aims to protect miners, particularly in Ikolomani and Shinyalu, where environmental conditions significantly heighten the risk of mine collapses. The governor's action underscores a critical focus on public safety and preventative measures across the region.

Governor Barasa's Safety Directive Speaking at a recent public engagement, Governor Barasa emphasized that torrential rains and subsequent flooding render mining sites extremely vulnerable to collapse, thereby imperiling the lives of those working within them. He issued a direct appeal to miners in Ikolomani and Shinyalu, instructing them to suspend their operations until the adverse weather conditions abate. "I just want to ask miners in these areas to stop mining because if you continue, you are putting your lives at risk," Barasa stated. Beyond the mining sector, the county chief also urged residents residing in flood-prone low-lying areas to evacuate to higher ground as a precautionary measure. He stressed the importance of prevention during the current flood season, advising against remaining in areas susceptible to inundation to avert potential loss of life.

County-Wide Precedent Set by Kisumu This announcement from Kakamega follows a similar prohibition enacted by Kisumu Governor Anyang Nyong'o approximately one month prior, specifically on February 8. Governor Nyong'o's ban on all mining and quarrying activities was prompted by the tragic drowning of four boys. Nyong'o's directive specifically targeted murram and sand harvesting operations within Kisumu County. He clarified that only quarry and mining operators possessing valid legal approvals and upholding substantial safety standards would be considered for resuming operations. The Kisumu governor also announced the formation of a joint task force between the county government and the National Environment Management Authority (NEMA). This task force is charged with reinforcing oversight of mining activities and preventing future tragedies. Governor Nyong'o further stipulated that no mining or quarrying activity would be permitted without a clearly approved, time-bound land rehabilitation and restoration plan to ensure excavated sites do not remain hazardous to the public. The task force is mandated to conduct a comprehensive audit of all existing quarries, enforce full compliance with legal statutes, and initiate firm legal action against any individuals or entities found operating unlawfully or in violation of established safety and environmental regulations. This robust approach to enforcement aligns with the increasing scrutiny on adherence to legal frameworks concerning land use and property rights.
