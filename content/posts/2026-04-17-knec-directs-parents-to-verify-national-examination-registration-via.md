The Kenya National Examinations Council (KNEC) has directed parents and guardians to confirm candidate registration for the 2026 national assessments and examinations. Issued today, April 17, 2026, this directive applies to KCSE, KPSEA, and KJSEA candidates, requiring SMS verification by April 30, 2026, to preempt examination period issues.

National Exam Registration Verification Parents and guardians can now verify their child’s registration status through a dedicated SMS service. This involves sending the candidate’s assessment or index number to 20076. According to KNEC, the verification process is essential to ensure that all candidates are accurately captured within the system well in advance of the examination period.

Key Information for Confirmation KNEC has outlined several critical details that parents must meticulously confirm. These include the correct spelling and order of names, accurate gender identification, and the precise date of birth. Additionally, parents are required to verify the specific school where the candidate is registered, along with their citizenship status, differentiating between Kenyan and non-citizen. For KPSEA and KJSEA candidates, subject selection is a crucial component to verify, particularly choices involving Kiswahili or Kenyan Sign Language, and Religious Education options such as CRE, IRE, or HRE. KCSE candidates must similarly confirm their optional subjects to prevent any last-minute discrepancies that could impact their examination records.

Deadline and Rectification Process KNEC has set April 30, 2026, as the definitive deadline for parents to complete this essential confirmation process. The council has issued a cautionary note, emphasizing that any errors not corrected by this date may prove difficult to rectify at a later stage. Parents who identify concerns or discrepancies during the verification are advised to contact their respective school heads immediately for assistance and clarification. This proactive measure follows earlier statements by KNEC Chief Executive Officer David Njengere, who previously highlighted the council’s frequent receipt of complaints too close to examination periods, often revealing that some candidates were never properly registered. Njengere reiterated that early verification through the SMS platform will significantly help eliminate such cases and streamline examination planning for thousands of candidates nationwide.
