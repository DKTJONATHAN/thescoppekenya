Public debate intensifies around the Talanta Sports City stadium, fueling criticism against President William Ruto's administration. This highlights a perceived disparity between ambitious national development pledges and the persistent lack of basic services in local areas such as Kapsinendet.

## President Ruto faces criticism as Talanta Sports City progress highlights unfulfilled development pledges in the Rift Valley

President William Ruto's extensive development tours across the nation, including numerous visits to the South Rift region, have consistently been marked by grand pronouncements on infrastructure, housing, and other critical projects. He has repeatedly reassured citizens of his government's unwavering commitment to delivering these promises. This steadfast assurance contrasts sharply with the ongoing construction of the flagship Talanta Sports City, a project that has drawn significant attention not only for its progress but also for an audit revealing an unexplained cost variation.

### Local Realities and Unmet Needs

Yet, away from the national spotlight and the gleaming renders of new stadiums, the ground-level reality in many local communities paints a starkly different picture. In places like Kapsinendet, for instance, the notion of children from their Early Childhood Development (ECD) centers receiving a "fully sponsored Free Education Program" to Singapore serves as a poignant, almost painful, exaggeration of their daily challenges. The truth for residents is far removed from such lofty ideas, with patients still reportedly carrying mattresses to public hospitals, parents forced to fundraise for school fees, and essential roads remaining impassable.

### Unfulfilled Local Pledges

This perceived disconnect between high-level government rhetoric and the lived experiences of ordinary citizens in underserved areas forms the core of a growing critique. The unfulfilled promise of a "22km tarmac road promised by Kasongo" serves as a poignant reminder of countless local pledges that seem to evaporate into thin air. Many residents observe that despite the government's warnings to motorists on major expressways against overloading, their fundamental infrastructure needs remain unaddressed.

### The Perceived Disconnect

While the distant and costly Talanta Sports City takes shape, many lament that this faraway stadium seems to be the only "development" reaching the people of the Rift Valley, despite the President's assurances that progress would reach every corner. As the Talanta Sports City project continues to make headlines, a cynical question resonates among the populace: "All that development, for whom, if we are still struggling down here?" This sentiment underscores a widespread feeling of being overlooked amidst grand national projects.
