The Kenya Revenue Authority (KRA) has announced significant changes to its tax systems, integrating customs data with the iTax platform for export Value Added Tax (VAT) returns. Effective May 2026, this move aims to streamline filings and enhance compliance for exporters and their agents across various trade zones.

## New Framework for Export VAT Returns

### Integrated Systems and Prefilling Automation
The KRA is synchronizing export VAT return data from the Integrated Customs Management System (iCMS) with the iTax platform where taxpayers file their returns. This integration will automatically prefill validated export values into VAT returns once relevant export documents are issued by Customs. This change applies to exports destined for the Single Customs Territory, other foreign markets, Export Processing Zones (EPZs), and Special Economic Zones (SEZs). Furthermore, exports of taxable services will also see prefilling in iTax, based on TIMS/eTIMS invoices generated and transmitted within the relevant tax period. This automated data flow is part of KRA's broader digital transformation strategy.

### Enhanced Compliance Requirements for Exporters
The new system introduces stricter compliance requirements for exporters and clearing agents. To ensure successful prefilling of VAT returns, exporters and their clearing and forwarding agents must capture the exporter's Personal Identification Number (PIN) alongside a valid TIMS/eTIMS zero-rated invoice number when lodging export documents in iCMS. Under this program, only export transactions that are validated in iCMS and properly linked to the taxpayer's PIN and invoice will be accepted in VAT returns. This measure is set to tighten the linkage between customs documentation and tax declarations, ensuring only verified export values are reflected in VAT filings. The emphasis on eTIMS compliance extends beyond VAT, as KRA has also begun validating income and expenses in tax returns against eTIMS data from January 1, 2026.

### KRA's Broader Automation Initiative
This initiative is a component of KRA's ongoing reforms aimed at deepening automation and integrating tax systems. The objective is to enhance efficiency, transparency, and overall revenue collection efforts.
