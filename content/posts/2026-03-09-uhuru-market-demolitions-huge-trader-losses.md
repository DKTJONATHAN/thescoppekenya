## Uhuru Market Demolitions Spur Trader Outcry

Traders at Uhuru Market along Jogoo Road are counting significant losses following night demolitions that flattened their stalls. This operation, carried out despite claims of a court order, came after a 72-hour vacation notice issued by the Nairobi City County Government on March 4, 2026, for planned redevelopment. Vendors now face the challenge of finding alternative spaces in a competitive city market.

### Controversial Demolition Exercise

The demolitions commenced days after traders had protested on Jogoo Road on Friday, March 6, claiming the government was defying a temporary court order halting the process. Confrontations escalated as police dispersed crowds, using tear gas while protesters scattered. Days later, bulldozers were observed at the busy market, dismantling numerous stalls along the highway to facilitate various county projects.

### Land Disputes and Housing Projects

The incident at Uhuru Market follows closely after a nearby furniture market, also in the Jogoo Road area, was destroyed by fire on February 14, 2026. Traders linked this fire to an ongoing land ownership dispute with authorities and plans for affordable housing construction. Adjacent to the disputed market lies a substantial 400-acre tract where residential units were demolished last year to clear space for a government affordable housing project.

Traders at Uhuru Market assert their legitimate occupancy, citing years of land rate payments to the Nairobi City County Government. They argue these payments reinforced their belief in the legal protection of their businesses within the market area. Following the demolitions, affected vendors highlight the immediate challenge of securing new trading locations, facing fierce competition for limited stalls and roadside selling points.

### Legal Challenges and Past Incidents

Vendors have questioned why the demolition proceeded despite a pending court case concerning the disputed land. They argue that this action has heightened concerns that powerful development interests are increasingly sidelining small businesses. Such concerns arise amidst broader governmental initiatives aimed at improving public services and infrastructure. For example, recent reforms have seen the government create four new ICT directorates, reflecting a drive for more streamlined operations.

This event echoes similar demolitions earlier in February 2026, where traders along Thika Road, specifically at Githurai and Roysambu Roundabouts, suffered a similar fate. In Githurai, traders were forcibly evicted to pave the way for the construction of a modern bus park. These demolitions occur months after President William Ruto cautioned against forced evictions without proper relocation or resettlement plans for affected families.
