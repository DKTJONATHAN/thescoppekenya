## Runway Revelations: Blunt and Tucci Spill on *The Devil Wears Prada 2* Character Twists

The fashion world is abuzz, and rightly so! *The Devil Wears Prada 2*, which premiered on April 20, 2026, and hit theaters nationwide on May 1, 2026, is serving up high drama and unexpected turns. Stars Emily Blunt and Stanley Tucci recently dished to *Entertainment Weekly* about their characters' wild developments, promising a sequel that's anything but basic.

### Emily's High-Fashion Coup

Emily Blunt's character, Emily Charlton, returns 20 years later as the head of Dior's United States division, a true ascent on the couture ladder. However, her ambitions haven't softened. Blunt revealed her character is "on a quest for success at her own undoing," secretly plotting with her billionaire boyfriend, Benji (Justin Theroux), to acquire *Runway* and dethrone Miranda Priestly. Blunt views Emily as more "desperate than cruel," driven by a relentless self-definition through her career. This cutthroat ambition eventually leads to a necessary "comeuppance" for Emily, paving the way for a surprising, earnest friendship with Andy after two decades. It seems even fashion's fiercest can find a path to resolution.

### Nigel's Enduring Loyalty

Stanley Tucci's Nigel Kipling delivers a "moving twist" that will leave audiences talking. Since the first film, Nigel has maintained a soft spot for Andy. As *Runway* faces a credibility crisis in 2026, Elias Clarke Publications' head, Irv Ravitz (Tibor Feldman), brings back a now-renowned journalist Andy (Anne Hathaway) to revive the magazine. However, the real masterstroke is revealed in the film's final moments: Nigel orchestrated Andy's return all along.

Tucci explained Nigel's actions, noting his awareness of Andy's "significant inroads to the world of journalism" and his understanding that *Runway* was "on quicksand." His line, "Forever my girl," to Andy at the movie's close, even gave his real-life sister-in-law, Emily Blunt, "goosies" on set. Tucci admired Nigel's "bold move," calling it "beautifully done" and "so simple" yet unexpected. It's clear Nigel's loyalty and admiration for Andy proved to be *Runway*'s unexpected saving grace.
