## Orengo Challenges Ruto on Nyanza Offer, Cites Stalled Agenda Siaya Governor James Orengo has publicly urged President William Ruto to present a clear, unequivocal offer to the Orange Democratic Movement (ODM) and the Nyanza region if seeking their political alignment. Speaking on March 10, the Governor also criticized the broad-based administration for failing to implement a crucial 10-point agenda established last year.

Call for a Direct Proposal Governor Orengo, addressing a seed distribution event in Alego Usonga, called for an end to what he described as mixed signals from the President. He emphasized the necessity of a direct and "irresistible" proposal from President Ruto to ODM and its supporters, stating that any genuine interest in the Nyanza people must be demonstrated through a clear offer that cannot be refused.

Stalled 10-Point Agenda The Governor additionally criticized the broad-based administration for the non-implementation of the 10-point agenda, a Memorandum of Understanding signed in March 2025. This agreement was intended to address significant governance and economic issues. Orengo noted that while meetings between the United Democratic Alliance (UDA) and ODM were underway to assess progress, his clear assessment indicated that the agenda had not been achieved.

Governance and Economic Concerns Orengo highlighted several pressing issues, including the escalating cost of living and the nation's increasing public debt, as unresolved concerns by the government. He further detailed ongoing abductions, extrajudicial killings, and alleged government interference with public meetings, all contributing to his assessment that the agreed-upon agenda remains largely unfulfilled. These challenges are particularly pertinent as the [Kenya Meteorological Department warns of heavy rainfall across several regions](https://zandani.co.ke/article/kenya-meteorological-department-warns-of-heavy-rainfall-across-sever), potentially exacerbating economic strains on citizens.

Allegations of Intimidation In recent months, Governor Orengo has emerged as a vocal critic of President Ruto's administration, accusing it of undermining democratic values. He specifically cited the arbitrary arrest and detention of several of his staff members on March 8, 2026, in Siaya and Nairobi, ahead of the presidential visit to Siaya. Orengo condemned these actions as egregious conduct by the police, asserting such behavior has no place in a constitutional democracy, and demanded their immediate release.
