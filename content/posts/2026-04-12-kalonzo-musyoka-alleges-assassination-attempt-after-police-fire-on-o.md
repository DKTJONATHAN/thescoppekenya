Wiper Party Leader Kalonzo Musyoka alleges police attempted to assassinate him on April 11, 2026, after officers fired on an opposition convoy heading to Kikuyu, according to a statement released today, April 12. The convoy, including Rigathi Gachagua and Eugene Wamalwa, was intercepted at Gitaru-Kikuyu Interchange.

Opposition Convoy Intercepted The confrontation occurred at the Gitaru-Kikuyu Interchange as the convoy, carrying Kalonzo Musyoka, Democracy for Citizens Party (DCP) leader Rigathi Gachagua, and Democratic Action Party-Kenya (DAP-K) leader Eugene Wamalwa, approached Kikuyu town. Musyoka stated armed officers in official police vehicles initiated the interception.

Allegations of Live Fire According to the Wiper Party, officers opened fire with live ammunition, teargas, and rubber bullets against the convoy in broad daylight. This action, intended to disperse the opposition gathering in Kikuyu, split the convoy, with one section continuing towards Kikuyu and another diverted towards Mai Mahiu amidst intensified police action. Musyoka's group, including Wamalwa, was reportedly pursued along the highway, with live rounds and teargas canisters deployed. Musyoka unequivocally described the incident as a deliberate assassination attempt, stressing it extended beyond crowd control and directly endangered lives. The assault also reportedly targeted media crews and their vehicles accompanying the convoy, resulting in injuries to some journalists who are currently receiving treatment.

Gachagua's Defiance in Kikuyu Despite the confrontation, Rigathi Gachagua's team proceeded to Kikuyu and successfully held a rally. The event drew crowds in what is considered a political stronghold for Kimani Ichung'wah, the local Member of Parliament and Leader of the Majority in the National Assembly. Police later intervened to disperse the gathering, deploying teargas and live bullets, leading to running battles. Gachagua maintained a defiant stance, declaring, "I am a Mau Mau son; I am not intimidated," as security forces tightened control around the venue. Reports indicate several individuals were injured during the clashes, with damage also noted on vehicles, campaign materials, and nearby property. The incident stems from broader political tensions, particularly following the burial of the late Ol Kalau MP David Kiaraho in Nyandarua. During that event, Gachagua, who was impeached on October 24, 2024, confronted President William Ruto and accused Ichung'wah of sponsoring goons and negatively influencing the President.
