The Kenya Wildlife Service announced Tuesday, March 31, 2026, an initiative granting 10 pupils free entry for every 100 paying students on school trips to all national parks, game reserves, and sanctuaries. This offer, valid from March 1 to June 30, 2026, aims to promote local tourism among learners across the country.

KWS Wild Scholars Initiative

Eligibility and Duration The new KWS offer is specifically for schools and students, encompassing all educational levels from nursery institutions through to universities. In addition to the group discount, children under six years old will receive free admission to all parks. KWS encourages schools to utilize the upcoming April holiday to enable learners to experience wildlife. "Enjoy the Wild Scholars offer: 10 free entries for every 100 students, and turn the last days of term into an unforgettable experience. Come, the wild is waiting," KWS stated in its notice. The corporation emphasized that increased student numbers would result in more free entry opportunities.

Promoting Local Tourism This initiative represents a significant reduction in the financial burden typically associated with educational trips to wildlife areas. Standard student entry fees often range from Ksh200 for sanctuaries and many reserves to as much as Ksh750 for premium parks such as Amboseli and Lake Nakuru National Park. By providing these concessions, KWS seeks to make wildlife experiences more accessible, fostering a deeper connection to nature among young people.

Historical Context of KWS Promotions The current "Wild Scholars" offer follows a series of similar promotions by the agency. In September 2025, KWS facilitated free entry to national parks, reserves, and sanctuaries for all citizens to commemorate World Tourism Day. Later, in December 2025, KWS introduced limited-time festive promotions that included free entries for specific children and adults based on group size and vehicle type, alongside special accommodation deals for park lodges. These prior campaigns demonstrate a recurring strategy by KWS to increase public engagement with wildlife conservation efforts.
