## Dan Stevens' Devilish Turn: From Eurovision Stage to Psychiatric Ward

Dan Stevens sheds his singing persona for a terrifying turn as Pepper in *The Terror: Devil in Silver*. Premiering May 7, 2026, on AMC+ and Shudder, this season promises a chilling exploration of institutional horrors with a stellar cast and a unique contemporary twist. Prepare for a frightfully good time!

### Ridley Scott's Unexpected Muse

Who knew that a hilarious turn as Russian singer Alexander Lemtov in the 2020 comedy *Eurovision Song Contest: The Story of Fire Saga* would land Dan Stevens his latest horror lead? Turns out, the film caught the eye of none other than Ridley Scott, whose company, Scott Free, produces *The Terror*. Stevens revealed that Scott was such a fan of his *Eurovision* performance that he "wouldn't shut up about it," making Stevens the perfect choice for the role of Pepper.

### New Hyde's Horrifying Secrets

In *The Terror: Devil in Silver*, Stevens portrays Pepper, an impulsive drummer turned moving man from Queens, N.Y., who finds himself incarcerated at the fictional New Hyde Psychiatric Hospital. The twist? Pepper is committed not due to mental illness, but because it's a "cheaper and easier" alternative for the police than processing him through the criminal system. This grim, under-resourced institution holds a dark secret: a mysterious, malevolent force terrorizing its inmates. Stevens highlights the show's deeper question: what is truly more monstrous, the creature or the system that traps vulnerable individuals?

### A Hauntingly Familiar Role

Fans of Stevens' starring role in the FX series *Legion* will find some eerie parallels in *The Terror: Devil in Silver*. The actor himself admits that "institutions lend themselves to horror quite naturally" and that there are "definitely some parallels" between the two shows. While *Legion* explored mutant abilities within a psychiatric setting, this new series leans into the raw, literalized violence of its devilish premise.

### Behind the Scenes of Silver

The new season boasts a scarily talented supporting cast, including Aasif Mandvi, CCH Pounder, and Stephen Root as hospital workers, with Judith Light portraying a patient who has been institutionalized for decades. Light's character, Dory, represents the long history of institutionalization and how it has evolved. Behind the camera, Victor LaValle, author of the original 2012 *Devil in Silver* novel, serves as showrunner alongside *Halt and Catch Fire* co-creator Chris Cantwell. The first two episodes were skillfully directed by Karyn Kusama, known for her work on horror films like *Jennifer's Body* and *The Invitation*. The series was filmed at the decommissioned Arthur Kill Correctional Facility on Staten Island, adding an extra layer of chilling authenticity to the production. Stevens even recalls encountering "demonic" goats wandering around outside the facility!
