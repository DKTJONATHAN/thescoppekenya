## African Financial Backing Fuels Industrial Scale Dangote Petroleum Refinery and Petrochemicals FZE has secured a significant $4 billion syndicated term loan, with Afreximbank providing $2.5 billion. This facility aims to strengthen the refinery's financial position, optimize its capital structure, and support its extensive long-term expansion, underscoring African financial institutions' commitment to continental industrial growth.

Catalysing Continental Industrialisation The $4 billion senior syndicated term loan represents a strategic infusion for the Dangote Petroleum Refinery. African Export-Import Bank (Afreximbank) underwrote the largest portion, contributing $2.5 billion to the total facility. Both Afreximbank and Access Bank served as co-mandated lead arrangers for this five-year financial arrangement. The primary objective is to consolidate existing debt obligations and enhance the refinery's capital structure as it scales operations. With a capacity of 650,000 barrels per day, the Dangote refinery stands as Africa's largest, illustrating the continent's potential for large-scale industrial innovation.

Strategic Financial Support for Energy Security Afreximbank's substantial commitment highlights its ongoing strategy to promote import substitution, foster intra-African trade, and enhance energy security across the continent. Since refining operations commenced in February 2024, Afreximbank has consistently supported the project, including a prior $1 billion working capital facility and advisory roles in initiatives like the Naira-for-Crude program. Afreximbank President George Elombi affirmed the bank’s dedication to African-led industrialisation, stating that backing African enterprises is fundamental to achieving long-term economic independence. This approach aligns with broader efforts to build robust African institutions capable of supporting monumental projects.

Foundations for Economic Expansion The successful securing of this syndicated loan demonstrates the increasing confidence of both local and international lenders in large-scale African industrial ventures.
