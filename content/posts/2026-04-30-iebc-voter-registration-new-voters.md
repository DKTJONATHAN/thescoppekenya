## IEBC Voter Registration Sees 2.3 Million New Voters Enrolled

The Independent Electoral and Boundaries Commission (IEBC) confirmed registering 2.3 million new voters nationwide during its Enhanced Continuous Voter Registration (ECVR) exercise, which concluded on April 28 after running for 30 days from March 30. This initiative aimed to bolster electoral participation ahead of the 2027 General Election.

### Milestone Achieved

IEBC Chairperson Erastus Ethekon described the outcome as a significant achievement, noting that an average of more than 500,000 new voters were registered each week. While the exercise fell short of the commission's 30-day target of 2.5 million registrations, the IEBC considered it a success, citing improved performance compared to previous large-scale registration initiatives. For instance, in 2016, the IEBC registered 1.4 million voters within a similar 30-day period, falling considerably short of its 4 million target. In 2021, registration numbers also remained significantly lower than anticipated.

### Operational Enhancements Drive Success

Ethekon attributed the improved performance of the ECVR exercise to operational changes that enhanced accessibility and efficiency. A key innovation was the introduction of the open kit strategy, which allowed eligible voters to register at any convenient center, eliminating geographical restrictions that previously hindered participation. This was particularly beneficial for individuals working away from their designated polling stations.

The registration network was also expanded beyond ward levels to include institutions such as universities, Technical and Vocational Education and Training (TVET) institutions, and other colleges. This broader reach aimed to engage more young people and students, aligning with the 'Tuko Kadi' campaign that mobilized them to register.

For the first time, IEBC integrated voter registration services into Huduma Centres across the country, establishing registration units in all 57 centers. This move underscores a broader governmental commitment to accessible public services and administrative efficiency, a topic also highlighted in discussions surrounding public service conduct, such as the recent suspension of a Siaya County Officer over alleged defamatory remarks against Governor Orengo.

Another crucial shift involved the utilization of national ID data sets and real-time performance monitoring. The IEBC provided weekly updates on registration data, enabling accurate tracking of progress and influencing implementation. This contrasted with past years where projections were based on static census data.

### Registration Continues

Despite the conclusion of the 30-day enhanced period, the IEBC has ruled out an extension. However, IEBC voter registration remains ongoing at all Huduma Centres and IEBC constituency offices nationwide.
