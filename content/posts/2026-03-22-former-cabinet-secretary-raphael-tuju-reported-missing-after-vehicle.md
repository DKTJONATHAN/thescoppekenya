Former Cabinet Secretary Raphael Tuju has been reported missing, his family confirming his last sighting on Saturday, March 21. His vehicle was discovered abandoned this Sunday morning, March 22, on Miotoni Lane in Nairobi's Karen area. Tuju was reportedly en route to an interview.

## Investigation Underway After Karen Vehicle Find

Concerns about Tuju's whereabouts escalated after repeated attempts by his family to contact him proved futile. His son, Mano Tuju, informed authorities, leading to a search for the former CS. On Sunday morning, Mano Tuju received a call from the Officer Commanding Station (OCS) at Karen Police Station, confirming the discovery of his father's abandoned vehicle. It remains unclear whether Tuju made it to his scheduled interview on Saturday evening. Reports indicate Tuju had filed a police report on Saturday, March 21, stating he was being followed by an unmarked white Land Cruiser.

### Ongoing Property Disputes

Tuju, a former Jubilee Party Secretary General, has been at the center of public discussion regarding significant property disputes in recent weeks. He has been embroiled in a legal battle with the state over the auction of his properties, a case that previously resulted in a dramatic eviction from his Karen residence. Tuju has openly accused unnamed powerful individuals of orchestrating his downfall. This high-profile situation attracting considerable public interest is a common feature in the lives of prominent figures.

In an interview on March 17, Tuju recounted acquiring his first Karen property over four decades ago, financed by a loan from the National Bank of Kenya. He also expanded his real estate portfolio to areas such as Upper Hill, where property values have significantly increased. He later acquired a 20-acre parcel in Karen, developed into high-end residential and hospitality projects, financed in 2015 with a Ksh1.2 billion loan. Tuju has consistently expressed his willingness to settle his debts, provided transparency is maintained by all parties involved in the ongoing financial and legal entanglement. The High Court recently granted a temporary reprieve to Tuju by blocking any transfer or assignment of his disputed Karen property.
