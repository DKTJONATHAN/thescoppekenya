## Manchester United's Lucumi Chase: More Transfer Window Chaos

Manchester United's reported "move" for Bologna's Jhon Lucumi reeks of indecision. Despite central defence not being a top priority, the club is seemingly back to old targets, adding another name to an already sprawling shopping list. It's less a strategic pursuit and more a familiar scramble in the current transfer market.

### Deja Vu and Dubious Priorities

United's supposed interest in Bologna's Jhon Lucumi signals a familiar muddled approach, not shrewd strategy. *The Athletic* confirmed earlier today that central defence sits well below midfield, striker, and left-back on Michael Carrick's summer wishlist. Yet, here we are, hearing about a 'move' for a centre-half. This isn't efficiency; it's a contradiction. Furthermore, the report notes United eyed Lucumi two years ago. Recycling old scouting reports and then acting on them only when primary targets prove elusive hardly inspires confidence. It suggests a reactive rather than proactive recruitment drive, a hallmark of their recent windows. While individual talents like [Bruno Fernandes might pick up accolades](https://zandani.co.ke/article/2026-05-08-bruno-fernandes-named-football-writers-association-player-of-year) for their performances, the club's transfer dealings consistently undermine on-field progress.

### The Vague 'Move' and Bologna's Leverage

The Italian press claims United have 'made a move' for the 28-year-old Colombian, but the specifics are conveniently absent. Is it a concrete offer? Or merely a casual enquiry with his agent? Knowing United's track record, it's safer to assume the latter. This ambiguity leaves Bologna with all the leverage. Their CEO, Claudio Fenucci, openly stated they promised Lucumi a departure this year for a 'suitable offer' - but quickly added they'd be 'happy to keep him' if the market remains quiet. That's not an invitation for a serious bid; it's a challenge. United are positioned as just another name on a 'long list of suitors,' hardly a position of strength when trying to secure a €22million-rated asset. They're waiting for the transfer window to 'take off' when they should be dictating terms.

### A Familiar Recruitment Circus

This Lucumi rumour perfectly encapsulates Manchester United's summer transfer activity: a broad net, vague intentions, and conflicting priorities. They've brought in Andrey Santos and Youri Tielemans for midfield, a backup goalkeeper in Karl Darlow, and an academy prospect, Tynan Thompson. Yet, they still need a starting midfielder (Kone), a left-back (Lewis Hall), a left-winger, and, critically, a centre-forward. The club reportedly has a 'top target' for the number nine role, yet the article lists five wildly disparate 'possible solutions' from Dusan Vlahovic to Danny Welbeck. This isn't focused scouting; it's a frantic casting call. Without a clear strategy, United risks another season of patchwork recruitment, failing to address core weaknesses effectively and leaving their squad unbalanced and reliant on individual flashes of brilliance.
