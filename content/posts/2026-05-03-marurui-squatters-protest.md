## Marurui Squatters Protest Paralyzes Northern Bypass

Marurui squatters in Nairobi's Kasarani area recently paralyzed transport along the Northern Bypass, staging protests against alleged land grabbing by a private developer. Over 6,000 residents blocked the major roadway, vowing to remain on land they have occupied for decades. They appeal to Governor Sakaja and President Ruto for intervention.

### Road Blockage and Protesters' Demands

The **Marurui squatters protest** brought traffic to a standstill as demonstrators used significant physical barricades. Footage showed dozens of men blocking main sections of the Northern Bypass with huge tree trunks. Some protesters wore helmets, masks, and reflective vests, indicating a level of coordination. Another section of the road was completely obstructed by burning tyres and logs, generating thick black smoke and intense flames, with stones scattered across the tarmac.

Protesters voiced their long-standing claim to the land, asserting they have lived there for many years without previous eviction attempts. “I was born here, my whole family is here, and I am raising my kids here as well. If they chase us from here, I have no other place to go,” one protester stated. ### Decades of Occupation and Land Disputes

The Marurui area is characterized by a complex mix of private property, public utility land, and extensive squatter settlements. Several high-profile land disputes remain active and unresolved across the region. Large sections of Marurui have been occupied by squatters for decades. The Jua Kali settlement, for instance, houses over 6,000 residents on a 7-acre parcel, a settlement established more than two decades ago.

### Call for Intervention and Legal Battles

Much of the land in Marurui remains contested between private entities and the government. A significant 4-acre parcel near Windsor Golf Club is currently at the centre of an ongoing court battle. The family of the late Super Minister John Michuki claims ownership of this parcel, while the Nairobi County Government maintains the land was surrendered for public use. In April last year (April 2025), the Environment and Land Court (ELC) issued a halt order on construction on this contested land. However, conflicts and further protests among the involved parties have continued despite the court's directive, underscoring the deep-seated nature of land issues in the area, a situation that the latest **Marurui squatters protest** has once again brought into sharp focus.
