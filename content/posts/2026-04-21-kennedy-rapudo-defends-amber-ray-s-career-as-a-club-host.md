Businessman Kennedy Rapudo has strongly defended his partner, Amber Ray, amidst brewing online debate regarding her vibrant career as a professional club host. Speaking on Tuesday, April 21, 2026, Rapudo dismissed the stigma surrounding the nightlife industry, highlighting it as a lucrative and often misunderstood profession for Kenyan public figures.

## Kennedy Rapudo Champions Amber Ray's Professional Path

### Debunking Nightlife Stereotypes

Rapudo emphasized that Amber Ray's career was well-established long before their relationship began, clarifying that her role is centered on brand appearance and guest engagement. He firmly stated, "When I met Amber, she was hosting. That was her job. She goes there, stays for a short time, interacts with people, and leaves. That is a job, and there is nothing wrong with it." He described the role as strictly time-bound and professional, underscoring her status as one of Kenya’s most sought-after influencers, making her a high-value asset for event organizers.

### A Lucrative Niche in Entertainment

Addressing her financial success, Rapudo revealed that Amber Ray ranks among the highest-paid club hosts in the country. He argued that her influence and ability to draw crowds position her as a legitimate business player in the entertainment industry. He likened her role to any other corporate or entertainment booking, where a public figure is paid for their presence and influence. This economic reality is seen across various segments of the entertainment industry, from celebrated club hosts to comedians like [Kabugi, whose parodies, such as his recent mimicry of musician Kevin Bahati](https://zandani.co.ke/article/comedian-kabugi-mimics-musician-kevin-bahati-in-parody-of-diss-track), also draw significant public engagement. Her popularity allows her to work with various clients across different industries, proving her versatility as a businesswoman.

### Calling Out Double Standards and Unwavering Support

Rapudo was particularly critical of the double standards often applied to female entertainers in Kenya. He pointed out that while global celebrities are frequently praised for their lifestyle choices and nightlife ventures, local women often face harsh moral judgment for the very same activities. "Public figures are often accepted abroad for similar lifestyle choices but judged locally," he remarked. The businessman concluded by reaffirming his unwavering support for Amber's autonomy, describing her as a disciplined, independent woman who understands her brand. Rapudo maintained that his role is not to "change" her or interfere with her established career, but rather to provide a supportive environment as she continues to grow her business empire. "My role is to support her as long as she is not doing anything wrong," he added, signaling that the couple remains unbothered by the court of public opinion.
