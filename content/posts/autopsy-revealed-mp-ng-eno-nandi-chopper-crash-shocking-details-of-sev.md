**AUTOPSY RESULTS: Severe Injuries and Burns Led to Death of MP Johana Ng’eno and Five Others** **By Celestine Nzioka** Government Pathologist Dr. Johansen Oduor has released a harrowing report following the post-mortem examination of Emurua Dikirr MP Johana Ng’eno and five other victims who perished in Saturday’s helicopter tragedy. The examination conducted on Tuesday morning confirms that the victims succumbed to a combination of traumatic injuries and severe burns after their aircraft crashed and burst into flames in Chepkiep, Mosop Sub-county. Addressing the media after the conclusion of the forensic exercise, Dr. Oduor stated that the impact of the crash caused catastrophic damage to the vital organs of the deceased. The victims suffered from extensive spinal, chest, and head injuries. According to the pathologist, the intensity of the fire that engulfed the helicopter resulted in varying degrees of burns, with some bodies being more severely affected than others. The forensic process was critical in officially identifying the remains of the six individuals, a necessary step that now allows their families to proceed with burial arrangements. A special committee established by the Speaker of the National Assembly, Moses Wetang’ula, is currently coordinating the funeral plans for the late legislator and his companions. The crash claimed the lives of MP Johana Ng’eno, Kenya Forest Service ranger Amos Kipngetich Rotich, photographer Nick Kosgei, teacher Robert Kipkoech Keter, Narok County Protocol Officer Wycliffe Kiprotich Rono, and the pilot, Captain George Were. Preliminary findings from the National Police Service indicate that the helicopter, registered as 5Y-DSB, was flying at an unusually low altitude before it clipped trees and went down. Interior Principal Secretary Raymond Omollo noted that poor weather conditions in the Nandi region are being looked at as a primary factor that may have contributed to the pilot’s inability to maintain safe clearance. Transport Cabinet Secretary Davis Chirchir has confirmed that the investigation has entered a critical phase following the recovery of the helicopter’s flight data recorder, commonly known as the black box. This device will provide investigators with the technical data needed to reconstruct the final moments of the flight. The Aircraft Accident Investigation Department is working in tandem with the International Civil Aviation Organisation to ensure the probe meets global standards. A preliminary report detailing the exact cause of the mechanical or environmental failure is expected to be made public within the next 30 days. As the nation mourns the loss of the vocal legislator and the five professionals on board, the crash site remains cordoned off for further analysis by aviation experts. The government has assured the public of a transparent investigation into the tragedy that has sent shockwaves across the political and social landscape of the country.
