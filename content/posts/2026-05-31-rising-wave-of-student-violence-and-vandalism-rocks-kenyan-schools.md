## Rising Wave of Student Violence and Vandalism Rocks Kenyan Schools

### From Mischief to Criminality
The era of harmless classroom pranks has been replaced by a systematic breakdown of authority. Recent incidents demonstrate that student behavior has shifted from minor disruptions to organized acts of violence. When learners set fire to dormitories or physically attack their educators, the behavior transcends childhood mischief. These acts mirror the tactics of criminals, suggesting that existing disciplinary structures within schools have suffered a total collapse that requires immediate intervention to protect lives and property.

### The Erosion of Respect
The current state of chaos reflects a significant cultural shift where personal accountability has become optional. Teachers are increasingly vulnerable, finding themselves defenseless in an environment that has transitioned from a place of learning into a battleground. This lack of order is a grim indicator of how far schools have drifted from traditional standards of academic integrity. As the classroom becomes an unregulated space, the physical and psychological safety of the workforce responsible for educating the next generation is at severe risk.

### Addressing the Moral Crisis
While technological advancements like artificial intelligence are being explored to improve sectors such as healthcare, surveillance alone cannot fix a broken moral compass. There is a growing consensus that society can no longer dismiss these violent outbursts as typical adolescent behavior. By failing to hold students accountable for acts of vandalism and assault, the country risks grooming a generation that views destruction as a valid form of expression. Urgent measures are required to confront this trend of criminality before the stability of the entire educational system is permanently compromised.
