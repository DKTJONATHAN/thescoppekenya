Micro, Small, and Medium Enterprise (MSME) Principal Secretary Susan Mang’eni has confirmed that the second phase of the NYOTA business support fund will be disbursed to beneficiaries immediately following the completion of the nationwide mentorship program. This tranche will provide an additional Ksh 25,000, bringing the total support per beneficiary to Ksh 50,000.

## NYOTA Fund Second Phase Disbursement Details

### Mentorship Program Nears Completion

The crucial mentorship program, designed to equip beneficiaries with essential business management skills, is nearing its end. PS Mang'eni indicated that over 94 per cent of first-tranche recipients have successfully completed their mentorship, with 98 per cent having already launched their businesses. The remaining participants are expected to conclude the program by April 8, 2026, following a brief extension provided to accommodate latecomers. This rigorous training underpins the government's commitment to fostering sustainable business growth among beneficiaries.

### Second Tranche and Total Support

Upon finalisation of the mentorship, beneficiaries will receive the second tranche of NYOTA Business Support startup capital, amounting to Ksh 25,000. This sum includes Ksh 3,000 designated for savings under the Haba Haba scheme by NSSF, aiming to instill crucial financial discipline from the outset. This additional capital will bring the total support per beneficiary to Ksh 50,000. President William Ruto initiated the first phase in early February, distributing approximately Ksh 2.5 billion to over 101 youth across the 47 counties, with each recipient receiving Ksh 25,000 initially. Effective management of these funds is critical for the long-term success of these ventures, highlighting the importance of understanding varied investment strategies as businesses scale up.

### Business Expertise Classroom Training Set for April

Following the completion of the mentorship program, the second Business Expertise Classroom Training is scheduled to commence on April 15, 2026. This training will be conducted across all constituencies, serving as a preparatory step before the actual disbursement of the second fund tranche. This structured approach aims to further solidify the entrepreneurs' business acumen before they receive additional capital.

### World Bank Funding and Youth Empowerment Initiatives

The NYOTA program is substantially supported by the World Bank, which has contributed to a total project cost of approximately Ksh 25.8 billion (USD 200 billion). Beyond direct business support, these funds are also channeled into the On-the-Job Experience (OJE) program. This initiative focuses on apprenticeships and workplace learning, targeting 90,000 youth. Under the OJE program, each beneficiary receives a monthly stipend of Ksh 6,000 for six months, fostering practical skills and industry-relevant expertise through hands-on experience with employers.
