## Rigathi Gachagua 2027 Strategy to Unseat President Ruto

Deputy President Rigathi Gachagua, speaking in Kajiado on Sunday, May 3, affirmed his commitment to opposition unity for the 2027 General Election, vowing not to divide efforts to choose a single presidential candidate against President William Ruto. Gachagua's **Rigathi Gachagua 2027** strategy prioritizes consensus, even as he pursues the top seat.

### Unity and Individual Presidential Bids

Gachagua, leader of the Democracy for the Citizens Party (DCP), assured Kenyans that his personal presidential ambition would not hinder the united opposition's goal of consolidating support behind one flag bearer. He stated, "I want to promise the people of Kenya that Rigathi Gachagua will not be a dividing factor in the quest for one presidential candidate. I believe and pray they pick me, but if we agree on someone else, I will support him." Declarations by other leaders for the top seat are part of a strategy to build individual support bases before a collective decision is made. This push for unity echoes broader calls for solidarity across the continent, vital for harnessing Africa's potential and resources. ### Choosing the Flagbearer

The Deputy President indicated that all United Opposition officials are currently consolidating support nationwide. They will soon convene to determine who is best suited to fly the opposition's flag. Popularity, public support, and consensus will guide the final decision on the presidential candidate. This framework outlines a clear path for the **Rigathi Gachagua 2027** campaign within the broader opposition movement. Other declared candidates for the 2027 presidential race include Jubilee Party's Fred Matiang'i, PLP's Martha Karua, Wiper's Kalonzo Musyoka, DAP-K's Eugene Wamalwa, and DP's Justin Muturi.

### Defending Former President Kenyatta

Gachagua also defended 4th President Uhuru Kenyatta against criticism from government leaders regarding his recent political comments. Gachagua asserted that Kenyatta retains his constitutional rights, including freedom of expression and the right to associate with any political formation of his choice. "He is our leader who is retired, but his mind is not retired. He still has an opinion and retains his right to express his opinion, to have a political choice, freedom of expression, and freedom of assembly. Leave Uhuru alone," Gachagua stated.

### Legal Challenges for Gachagua

Gachagua faces an active court case challenging his removal from office. Should he be cleared, the former Mathira MP will be eligible to vie for the presidency in 2027. His legal team has stated in court that he is seeking compensation for lost remuneration, damages, and other losses, rather than reinstatement as Deputy President.
