The Judicial Service Commission (JSC) on Friday, March 27, 2026, announced the shortlisting of five candidates, including Court of Appeal Judge Justice Katwa Kigen, for a vacant Supreme Court judge position. This competitive selection process aims to fill the apex court's vacancy created by Justice Mohamed Ibrahim's death.

Supreme Court Shortlist Announced

Shortlisted Candidates and Professional Backgrounds Among the five candidates shortlisted for the Supreme Court judge position is Justice Katwa Kigen, who previously served as President William Ruto's lawyer. The other individuals on the list include Anne Waceke Kiratu Makori, Justices Joseph Sergon, Francis Kipruto Tuiyott, and Abdulahi Mohammed Warsame. Justice Kigen's shortlisting follows his appointment and swearing-in as a Judge of the Court of Appeal on January 27, 2026. His past legal work includes representing President Ruto as part of his defense team at the International Criminal Court (ICC) and during the 2022 presidential election petition.

Selection Process and Public Engagement The Judicial Service Commission initiated this process after receiving six applications for the vacant Supreme Court post. Following a review for compliance, the JSC reduced the list to the five aforementioned candidates who will now proceed to the interview stage. Interviews for these candidates are scheduled for April 28 and 29, 2026. In line with its commitment to transparency and public participation, the JSC has invited members of the public to submit any reservations regarding the shortlisted candidates. Information can be sent in writing to 020 2739180 or recruitment@jsc.go.ke. The Commission also noted that it might interview individuals who submit information, assuring that such submissions will remain confidential.

Vacancy and Appointment Process The vacancy at the Supreme Court arose from the death of Justice Mohamed Ibrahim, who passed away on December 17 last year after an extended illness. Upon the completion of the interviews, the Judicial Service Commission is expected to forward its nominee to President William Ruto for appointment, in accordance with Constitutional requirements.
