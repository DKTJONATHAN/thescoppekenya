Lawyer Tom Ouya Imbukwa Postmortem Reveals Severe Injuries is central to this update for Kenyan readers.

The family of slain Nairobi advocate Tom Ouya Imbukwa has revealed preliminary postmortem findings, indicating he suffered extensive and severe injuries before his death. Speaking at the Kenyatta National Hospital farewell home on Friday, family lawyer Njanja Maina confirmed the examination showed significant trauma, with the exact cause pending forensic analysis.

## Postmortem Reveals Severe Trauma

Family lawyer Njanja Maina disclosed that the postmortem examination established severe injuries on the right side of Imbukwa's body. While the examination provided a disturbing picture, it did not conclusively identify the precise cause of death. Investigators from the Directorate of Criminal Investigations (DCI) have collected samples for forensic analysis to determine what led to the fatal injuries. Maina emphasized the extreme severity of the documented injuries.

### Investigations Ongoing

Despite the grim findings, the family urged against premature conclusions, noting that investigations are still active. They stated that establishing the exact cause of death requires further forensic work. The family plans to meet DCI officers next Tuesday, where detailed documentation from the scene where Imbukwa’s body was discovered, along with an analysis of CCTV footage, is expected to be presented.

### Legal Fraternity Demands Action

The killing has provoked widespread condemnation within the legal fraternity. Law Society of Kenya (LSK) President Charles Kanjama described the incident as a tragic and shocking loss, asserting that early reports suggest the advocate was assaulted and tortured before his death. Kanjama emphasized that the circumstances surrounding Tom Imbukwa's death demand unequivocal condemnation and immediate action. He urged individuals with any relevant information to come forward, assuring them protection, and warned that all responsible parties, including any potential state actors, must be held fully accountable.
