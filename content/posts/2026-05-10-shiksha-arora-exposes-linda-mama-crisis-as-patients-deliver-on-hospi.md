Journalist Shiksha Arora recently released a harrowing video report documenting a crisis at a public hospital, where a woman was forced to give birth on a bare floor due to a severe shortage of maternity beds, medical equipment, and essential healthcare staff.

## The Systematic Failure of Maternal Healthcare

The footage highlights the deteriorating state of the Linda Mama program, a government initiative originally designed to provide free maternity services to expectant mothers. Despite official claims regarding the advancement of universal healthcare, this incident illustrates a collapse in basic service delivery. Hospitals across the country are currently struggling with persistent corruption allegations and a lack of funding, which has left medical facilities ill-equipped to handle routine deliveries.

### The Cynicism of State Absence

Public frustration has reached a boiling point, leading to ironic commentary that mocks the state of the nation. In a display of biting sarcasm, some have publicly thanked Shell Kenya for supposedly standing with mothers during their time of need. This mockery highlights the absurdity of the current crisis, as citizens imply that the government has become so absent from its core duties that expectant mothers are essentially left to fend for themselves in a landscape where state support has vanished.

### A Pattern of Institutional Neglect

The incident is not an isolated event but rather a symptom of a wider, systemic neglect within the public sector. Patients arriving at state-run facilities often encounter empty pharmacies and indifferent systems, despite the existence of national programs meant to cover their costs. Accountability for infrastructure funds remains elusive, leaving facilities in dilapidated states that offer no dignity to those seeking care.

### The Collapse of Public Infrastructure

The failure of the healthcare system mirrors a broader decline in national service delivery. Just as hospitals fail to provide basic life-saving environments, other critical sectors are showing similar signs of disintegration. Whether it involves the inability to maintain safe, flood-free roadways or the failure to manage essential labor wards, the average citizen is increasingly forced to navigate a country that is struggling to provide the most fundamental necessities. The sight of a woman delivering a child on a hospital floor serves as a grim indictment of a government that has seemingly vacated its responsibilities to the public.
