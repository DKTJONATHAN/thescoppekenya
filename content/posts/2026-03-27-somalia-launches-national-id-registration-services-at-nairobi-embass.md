By Celestine Nzioka The Somali Embassy in Nairobi, under Ambassador Jabril Ibrahim Abdulle, initiated national identification card registration services for its citizens residing in Kenya on March 27, 2026. This new National Identification and Registration Authority (NIRA) office aims to streamline the ID acquisition process, significantly reducing travel burdens and delays for Somali nationals living abroad.

Expanding Identity Services Amid Border Reopening The establishment of this NIRA office in Nairobi marks the 30th such facility operating outside Somalia's borders, demonstrating a significant expansion of the country's identification program to ensure diaspora citizens are integrated into national systems. Ambassador Jabril stated that Somalis in Kenya can now register and obtain their national ID card at the embassy, facilitating access to this crucial document. The NIRA national ID card is a biometric document featuring a unique 11-digit number assigned for life, designed to ensure secure identification across various platforms and government systems without duplication. Applicants provide biometric data, including fingerprints and photographs, which are stored in a secure database to support verification in both physical and digital environments. Authorities intend to introduce digital and smartcard versions of the identification card, enabling seamless integration with modern systems for banking, travel, and access to government services. The national ID card is set to become mandatory for international travel starting January 2026, though this was delayed to allow more time for registration. Somalia's government has also enforced its use for domestic flights as of March 1, 2026. This development precedes the planned reopening of the Kenya-Somalia border in April 2026, after more than a decade of closure due to security concerns linked to Al-Shabaab threats. President William Ruto announced the reopening, with an intention to boost cross-border trade, strengthen regional integration following Somalia's entry into the East African Community, and reconnect communities in North-Eastern Kenya. Somalia officially joined the East African Community as its eighth member in March 2024. The phased reopening, focusing on key crossing points, is expected to foster economic revival and reunite families separated by years of restricted movement. While the reopening aims to enhance economic ties, some express skepticism regarding a potential resurgence of attacks on infrastructure and people. President Ruto, however, has maintained that the approach will be intelligence-based, with all security apparatus on high alert.
