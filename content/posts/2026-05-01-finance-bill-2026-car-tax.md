## Finance Bill 2026 Car Tax Targets Classic Vehicles

The National Treasury on Thursday, April 30, published the Finance Bill 2026, proposing a 50 percent excise duty on high-end classic cars in Kenya. This measure, introduced by President William Ruto's administration, aims to boost government revenue by taxing wealthy car owners and dealers, with new provisions expected to take effect from July 1, 2026.

### New Excise Duty Details

The proposed legislation specifies that antique, vintage, or classic vehicles will incur a 50 percent excise duty on their excisable value. To qualify for this new tax, a vehicle must be at least 30 years old from its first registration date and have a value of not less than Ksh10 million, exclusive of depreciation. This targeted approach focuses on a smaller segment of the market comprising affluent individuals and vehicle importers or traders dealing in rare and high-value automobiles. The Finance Bill 2026 car tax seeks to double the existing duty on such high-end vehicles.

### Revenue Generation and Broader Amendments

The government's primary objective with this new classic car tax is to generate additional revenue for the national budget. This proposal is one of several amendments included in the Finance Bill 2026, which addresses various tax segments, including income tax, Value Added Tax (VAT), and excise duty. If Parliament passes the Bill and the President assents to it, the new taxes are slated for phased implementation, with most provisions becoming effective from July 1, 2026.

### Historical Context of Luxury Vehicle Taxation

This is not the first instance of the government proposing increased taxation on luxury vehicles. Two years prior, the Finance Bill 2024 introduced a motor vehicle circulation tax. This earlier proposal mandated a tax rate based on the car's value, with a minimum of Ksh5,000 and a maximum of Ksh100,000, payable during the acquisition of an insurance policy. However, President Ruto later rescinded the 2024 Finance Bill following widespread public protests across the country.
