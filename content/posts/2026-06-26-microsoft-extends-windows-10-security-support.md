Microsoft recently updated its support timeline, pushing the final retirement date for Windows 10 to October 12, 2027. This change grants a three-year window for users stuck with older computers that cannot handle the strict security hardware requirements of Windows 11. For many, this is a welcome development. Microsoft Extends Windows 10 Security Support for Older Devices is the central subject of the update.

Across Kenya, a significant number of professionals and students rely on refurbished or second-hand laptops. These machines are often excellent for drafting documents, managing spreadsheets, or handling academic research. However, they frequently lack the TPM 2.0 security chips that Microsoft now mandates for its latest operating system. Before this announcement, many business owners feared their machines would soon become digital liabilities once security patches were discontinued. This extension acts as a buffer, ensuring that perfectly functional equipment remains safe to use for a few more years.

### Understanding the Cost of Security

There is a catch to this reprieve. While the software remains operational, Microsoft is introducing a paid licensing program known as Extended Security Updates (ESU). To receive these critical patches, users must pay 30 dollars annually, which translates to roughly 3,800 Kenyan shillings. This subscription covers a single year of support, and you will need to renew it every twelve months until the 2027 deadline.

Deciding whether to pay this fee requires a simple look at your budget. A new, Windows 11-compliant laptop from a local electronics retailer typically costs between 35,000 and 50,000 shillings. If your current device runs smoothly and serves your business well, paying 3,800 shillings for another year of safety is a much smarter move than purchasing new hardware. Conversely, if your laptop is already showing signs of physical decay, such as a dying battery or a failing screen, you should think twice. Spending money on software for a machine nearing its mechanical end is not a good use of your capital.

### Preparing for Your Next Hardware Upgrade

View this three-year period as a countdown rather than a permanent fix. Technology moves fast, and eventually, older processors will struggle to run modern web browsers and the latest versions of standard office applications. Even if your operating system remains secure, the underlying hardware will eventually hit a performance ceiling where it can no longer keep up with modern demands.

If you run a small or medium enterprise, now is the time to adjust your financial planning. Business efficiency often hinges on how quickly and reliably you can process information. You now have a clear timeline to save up for a modern machine that meets current industry standards. If your work involves sensitive financial records, operating on an unpatched system is a risk you simply cannot take. If you decide that the 30-dollar fee is not worth the expense for an aging machine, start moving your files to cloud storage now and begin scouting for a budget-friendly hardware replacement long before 2027.

For students and personal users, the priority should be routine maintenance. Check that your Windows 10 system is fully patched today and monitor official channels to confirm if your specific device is eligible for the personal ESU program. If your laptop still handles your daily tasks with ease, this extension provides the flexibility to avoid a rushed purchase in the busy electronics markets of the CBD. Use these upcoming years to monitor market trends. When the time finally comes to retire your older device, you will be in a better position to invest in a machine that will provide reliable service for the next five to seven years, rather than forcing a short-term solution.
