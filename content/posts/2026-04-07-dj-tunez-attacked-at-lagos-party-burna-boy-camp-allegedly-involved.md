Nigerian-American DJ Tunez was reportedly attacked by individuals allegedly affiliated with Burna Boy's entourage at a private Lagos gathering recently. Videos circulating across social media platforms appear to show the physical altercation at Obi Cubana's residence, igniting widespread fan speculation and concern across Pan-Africa.

## Alleged Scuffle Puts Afrobeats Rivalry in Spotlight

### The Incident Unfolds
The confrontation reportedly began when DJ Tunez, who was performing at the event, accompanied legendary Afrobeats artist D'banj to Burna Boy's VIP section. An argument allegedly erupted between Burna Boy and DJ Tunez, escalating into a scuffle before Burna Boy's security quickly intervened to remove him. Social media clips purportedly show DJ Tunez on the floor being kicked by multiple men, though the video's authenticity remains unverified. As of April 2026, neither artist nor their representatives have issued official statements regarding the situation, and no police reports have emerged concerning injuries or involvement. Amidst the speculation, DJ Tunez posted a cryptic message on X, stating: "OGBAFIA NUMBER 1 DON WAKE OOO!"

### Lingering Tensions Surface
This incident is not an isolated event; underlying tensions between DJ Tunez and Burna Boy's camp have been simmering for some time. Last November, Burna Boy faced heavy criticism after ejecting a sleeping fan from one of his concerts. In what was widely perceived as a public relations move to address the backlash, he subsequently began distributing coffee to concertgoers at subsequent shows. DJ Tunez publicly mocked this effort on X, posting a meme announcing that no coffee would be served at his own "Blackout" show and that fans were free to sleep if they wished, further stoking their apparent rivalry. Some sources suggest DJ Tunez had been "poking fun" at Burna Boy online in recent weeks, indicating a build-up of tensions.

### Roots in the Big 3 Feud
The friction between DJ Tunez and Burna Boy is also seen as an extension of the long-standing feud between Burna Boy and Wizkid. Years ago, Burna Boy controversially stated he would have "punched Wizkid in the face" if they were not friends, a remark made in response to criticism from Wizkid's fanbase. This statement is widely considered the catalyst for the enduring animosity between the two superstars. Since then, both artists have traded veiled barbs online, often amplified by their respective fanbases. This latest scuffle is another regrettable episode in the ongoing "culture war" among the famous Afrobeats Big 3-Davido, Burna Boy, and Wizkid-and their supporters, battling for supremacy in the vibrant Pan-African music scene. While the industry sees other collaborative ventures, such as the critically discussed [Joeboy Wizard Chan Joint EP 'Agaba Romantic': A Calculated Fusion or True Artistry?](https://zandani.co.ke/article/joeboy-wizard-chan-joint-ep-agaba-romantic), these high-profile altercations continue to draw significant attention.
