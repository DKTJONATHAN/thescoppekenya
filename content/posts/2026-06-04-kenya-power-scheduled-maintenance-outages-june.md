## Eight Counties Face the Great Power Shutdown

The morning rush today took a sharp turn for small business owners as Kenya Power confirmed an extensive blackout window spanning eight counties. From 8:30 a.m. to 5:00 p.m., entrepreneurs across the country are scrambling to protect their bottom lines from this forced downtime.

### The Digital Standstill Panic

Social media is currently a battleground of frustration as business owners wake up to the reality of an eight-hour disconnect. In Nairobi, the chatter is dominated by cyber cafe managers wondering how to handle pre-booked sessions, while Nakuru shopkeepers are loudly complaining about the timing of the maintenance. The general consensus online is one of exhaustion. One salon owner tweeted that she is already rescheduling all her blow-dry appointments, while a welding workshop operator in Kiambu expressed disbelief that such a long window was necessary for maintenance. The mood is tense, with many feeling that the timing is hitting their productivity exactly when they need it most.

Entrepreneurs are not just sitting idle, though. Many are busy setting up contingency plans to ensure they do not lose their entire day of earnings. For those running cafes, it is all about finding alternative power sources or shifting their data-heavy tasks to the evening. Some local businesses are even pivoting their operations to offline work, using the day to catch up on paperwork or manual repairs that do not require electricity. It is a classic Kenyan hustle, pivoting quickly to ensure the lights-or at least the cash flow-stay on.

### Planning for the Long Haul

The maintenance schedule covers Nairobi, Nakuru, Uasin Gishu, Kakamega, Kisii, Kiambu, Kilifi, and Kwale. Business travelers heading into these areas should expect minimal services in commercial centers, as many shops have opted to close their shutters entirely for the day rather than operate in the dark. For those who must remain open, the current best practice among small and medium enterprises involves offloading non-essential tech equipment to prevent power surge damage when the supply eventually returns.

Data loss is a massive concern. Business owners are being warned to back up their files to external drives or cloud services before the 8:30 a.m. cutoff. Pro-tips circulating on community forums include unplugging sensitive electronics early and checking for potential backup generator fuel levels. As the sun sets, the hope is that these infrastructure upgrades will lead to more stability in the long run. Until then, most shop owners are just counting the minutes until the current returns. It is a quiet, frustrating Thursday for the country's backbone, proving that even a single workday without power is enough to rattle the entire local economy.
