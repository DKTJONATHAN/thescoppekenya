A surprising observation is gaining traction in May 2026: former President Mwai Kibaki’s administration reportedly oversaw more extensive road infrastructure projects in Kalenjin County than the combined tenures of former President Daniel Moi and current President William Ruto, sparking significant discussion across Kenya.

## Mwai Kibaki’s Road Infrastructure Record in Rift Valley

The sentiment highlighting this unexpected reality has gained traction throughout Kenya. During his presidency, from 2002 to 2013, Mwai Kibaki, a Kikuyu president, appears to have left a more pronounced mark on the road network in areas commonly referred to as Kalenjin land, compared to the combined efforts of former President Daniel Moi and incumbent President William Ruto. Kibaki is widely recognized for significantly expanding Kenya's road network, a legacy that continues to serve as a benchmark for national development.

### Key Projects Under Kibaki's Watch

Concrete examples underpin this ongoing discussion. The 53-kilometer Kipsigak-Serem-Shamakhokho Road, a crucial artery connecting Nandi County through Vihiga, was completed between 2004 and 2006 by China Wu Yi, having commenced in 2002. This project highlights his administration's commitment to regional connectivity. Other significant projects include the Chebarbar-Lessos-Napkoi road, developed between 2003 and 2004 by Issaco from Israel, and the 21-kilometer Himaki-Nandi Hills Road, constructed from 2004 to 2005. Further enhancing accessibility in the region was the 17-kilometer Chepterit-Baraton-Kimondi Road, which President Kibaki commissioned in late 2011.

### Recarpeting and Upgrading Initiatives

Beyond new constructions, Kibaki’s government also prioritized improving existing routes. The re-carpeting of the Eldoret-Kapsabet-Nandi Hills-Awasi stretch considerably enhanced travel and trade within the agricultural heartland. These initiatives were part of a broader national strategy that channeled substantial investments into road construction and maintenance across rural Kenya, linking previously isolated towns and trading centers. These well-maintained roads continue to serve the region, a point frequently noted by observers comparing past and present development paces.

### Setting the Bar for Future Administrations

The discussion often draws a sharp contrast with current infrastructure efforts. While President William Ruto's administration is advancing its ambitious infrastructure agenda, including the significant Rironi-Mau Summit road, projected for completion by 2027, the sheer volume and widespread impact of Kibaki’s projects in Kalenjin areas remain a prominent talking point. President Ruto has also announced a comprehensive 10-year program to dual 2,500 kilometers of highways and tarmac 28,000 kilometers of roads nationwide. However, as works progress on current projects, the benchmark set by Kibaki's era continues to highlight a period when tarmac expanded rapidly across the country, leading many to reflect on whether the "home advantage" always translates into greater local development.
