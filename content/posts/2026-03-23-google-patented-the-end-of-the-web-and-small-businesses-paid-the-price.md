<p>By March 23, 2026, Google’s transition from a helpful search tool to an extractive digital dictator is complete, as the tech giant utilizes aggressive AI Overviews and predatory patents to redirect the entire internet’s value into its own coffers.</p> <h2>The Great Traffic Heist</h2> <p>In Nairobi’s tech hubs from Westlands to Kilimani, the silence is deafening. Local publishers and digital entrepreneurs who spent a decade building search authority are watching their traffic graphs crater. This is not a market shift; it is a calculated heist. Google’s AI Overviews, which now dominate almost every search query, are designed to do one thing: keep the user on Google’s page. By scraping the hard-earned research, data, and creative output of Kenyan writers and businesses, Google provides a summarized answer that satisfies the user’s curiosity without ever sending a single click to the source material.</p> <p>For a business operating out of River Road or a niche consultancy in Upper Hill, this is a death sentence. If you run a travel site explaining the intricacies of the SGR booking system or a legal blog detailing Kenyan land laws, Google’s AI now takes your expertise, rewords it, and presents it as its own. You provide the labor, the server costs, and the expertise. Google takes the ad revenue. This parasitic relationship has turned the "open web" into a closed loop where the creator is the only one who loses money.</p> <h2>The Patent That Murdered the Link</h2> <p>The recent patent filings from Mountain View reveal a chilling endgame. Google has effectively patented the mechanism to end the outward-bound link. Their goal is to transform from a search engine—a gateway to the world—into a destination engine. This patent outlines a system where the AI doesn't just summarize; it predicts the next three questions a user might have and generates those answers too. By the time a user is finished, they have spent twenty minutes on Google and zero seconds on the websites that actually provided the underlying facts.</p> <p>This is the behavior of a monopoly that has grown too large to care about the ecosystem it inhabits. When a single entity controls 90 percent of the search market, they no longer need to innovate; they only need to extract. By patenting "the end" of the traditional search journey, Google is signaling that it no longer views website owners as partners. We are now merely free data entry clerks for their machine learning models. If you think your business is safe because you have "good SEO," you are mistaken. You are simply organizing your data so it is easier for Google to steal.</p> <h2>Digital Colonialism in East Africa</h2> <p>The impact in East Africa is particularly devastating because our digital economy is built on the promise of visibility. Kenyan SMEs rely on being found. When Google decides to hide organic results below a wall of AI-generated text and three layers of sponsored ads, they are effectively taxing the visibility of every business in the country. This is a new form of digital colonialism. The intellectual property of Kenyan creators is being harvested to train models that will eventually make those same creators redundant.</p> <p>I have spoken with several boutique digital marketing firms in Nairobi who report that organic reach has dropped by as much as 70 percent in the last six months alone. This isn't because the content got worse. It’s because the "answer engine" has replaced the "discovery engine." When a user searches for "best insurance rates in Kenya," they no longer see a list of brokers. They see a Google-generated table that may or may not be accurate, but is certainly convenient enough to prevent a click-through. This destroys the lead generation pipeline that thousands of local businesses depend on.</p> <h2>How to Survive the Dictatorship</h2> <p>The only way to survive this is to stop playing Google’s game. The era of relying on "organic search" as a primary business strategy is over. If your business depends on Google’s whims, you do not own a business; you own a temporary lease on a digital plot that can be revoked at any second. To avoid the business bullshit of this monopoly, you must pivot to direct-to-consumer models that Google cannot touch.</p> <p>This means building high-value email lists, fostering communities on platforms like Telegram or WhatsApp, and investing in brand recognition that compels users to type your URL directly into their browser. If people are searching for "your brand name" rather than "a service you provide," you still have a chance. But if you are waiting for the algorithm to save you, you are already dead. We are seeing a return to the old-school importance of direct relationships. The middleman has become a predator, and the only solution is to cut the middleman out entirely.</p> <h2>The Business of Destruction</h2> <p>Google is going to destroy business as we know it by devaluing information. When information is "free" and served by an AI, the quality of that information begins to degrade. Without the financial incentive to create deep, investigative, or highly specialized content, creators will stop producing it. We will be left with an internet filled with AI-generated sludge—recycled, low-quality data that Google’s bots feed back to users in a feedback loop of mediocrity.</p> <p>The search giant is betting that you are too addicted to their ecosystem to leave. They bet that you’ll keep paying for Ads even as your organic reach hits zero. They are wrong. Smart businesses are already diversifying. They are moving to platforms that respect the click, or better yet, they are building their own platforms. The monopoly only works if we continue to feed the beast. It is time to stop optimizing for a dictator and start building for our own audiences.</p>
