Chief of Defence Forces General Charles Kahariri and Major General Joyce Sitienei received prestigious French military honors aboard the French amphibious assault ship DIXMUDE on Friday, March 13, 2026. General Kahariri was awarded the Legion of Honour, while Major General Sitienei received the National Defence Medal - Gold Level, recognizing their significant contributions.

French Military Honors Bestowed The ceremony, presided over by French Ambassador to Kenya Arnaud Suquet, saw General Charles Kahariri receive the Legion of Honour, France’s highest order of merit. Established by Napoleon Bonaparte in 1802, this award acknowledges outstanding service in both military and civilian capacities. General Kahariri's decoration specifically recognizes his exemplary leadership, his pivotal role in strengthening defence cooperation between France and the nation, and his contributions to promoting regional security.

Recognition for Strategic Achievements At the same event, Major General Joyce Sitienei, who serves as Deputy Vice-Chancellor at the Centre for Strategic and Security Studies of the National Defence University-Kenya, was awarded the National Defence Medal - Gold Level. This medal, established in 1982, acknowledges individual merit, professional achievements, and dedication to both national and international security. Ambassador Suquet highlighted Maj Gen Sitienei’s exceptional contributions to international peace support training, security cooperation, and the robust defence partnerships between the two nations and allied forces. The awards were conferred just hours before over 800 French military personnel docked at the port of Mombasa. This contingent is in the country as part of an ongoing defence partnership, with their mission focused on training and regional maritime security cooperation, aiming to improve stability across the Indian Ocean region. During their deployment, the French military will conduct joint training exercises with the Kenyan Navy, particularly with units responsible for safeguarding the nation’s coastline. The program will emphasize maritime operations, coordination at sea, and modern naval tactics designed to enhance security within the region's waters.
