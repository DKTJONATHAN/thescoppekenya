Asake announces release of fourth studio album titled MNEY is central to this update for Kenyan readers.

Afrobeats superstar Asake is set to release his highly anticipated fourth studio album, "M$NEY," on May 1, 2026. The project marks a significant moment in his career, following his meteoric rise since 2022 and showcasing his unwavering ambition on the global stage.

## Asake's M$NEY Album: A New Era of Afrobeats Dominance

Asake’s upcoming album, "M$NEY," is anticipated to be his most assertive work to date, a bold statement from an artist at the peak of his powers. Following the success of his third album, "Lungu Boy," which showcased his global aspirations with heavyweight collaborations, "M$NEY" is poised to further solidify his position as a leading voice in Afrobeats. His recent Red Bull Symphony Orchestra performance in New York and strategic collaborations with French popstar Tiakola and global producer DJ Snake underscore his relentless pursuit of new frontiers.

### Flaunting Unmatched Success
Listeners can expect "M$NEY" to be a confident and boastful declaration of Asake's current status and wealth. Known for his self-assured lyricism, famously calling himself the "landlord" on his sophomore effort, Asake is set to flaunt the luxurious lifestyle his success has afforded him. Recent reports of his substantial investment in luxury cars in the weeks preceding the album's release perfectly encapsulate the album's flamboyant spirit. This project is expected to reaffirm his commercial impact as an unparalleled force in Afrobeats.

### Crafting Inevitable Chart-Toppers
Asake has consistently delivered era-defining records that have dominated charts and shaped the Afrobeats soundscape over the past four years. "M$NEY" is expected to continue this trend, offering a collection of party-starters and captivating tunes destined for widespread popularity on social media and the streets. His proven ability to craft infectious melodies and compelling rhythms remains a core strength.

### Global Feature Extravaganza
The album promises a lineup of international guest appearances that reflect Asake's expansive reach. His previous album, "Lungu Boy," featured an impressive roster including UK hip-hop stars Stormzy and Central Cee, Brazilian sensation Ludmilla, American rapper Travis Scott, and Afrobeats icon Wizkid. With Tiakola and DJ Snake already contributing to pre-release singles, fans can anticipate more high-profile international collaborations on "M$NEY," showcasing his extensive network.

### Bold Sonic Exploration
Asake's pre-release singles offer a glimpse into the album's diverse sonic palette. "Badman Ganster" featuring Tiakola blends hip-hop and pop, while "Why Love" incorporates familiar Amapiano elements and House music. The recent "Worship" with DJ Snake marries Fuji-tinged melodies with dance music. This willingness to experiment, first demonstrated on "Lungu Boy," suggests "M$NEY" will feature a range of genres, underscoring Asake's desire to take his music to a global audience and display his artistic versatility.

### Pioneering New Soundscapes
Asake, alongside producer Magicsticks, significantly impacted Nigerian pop music by popularizing Amapiano log drums, a sound that has since shaped numerous hits and launched new stars. With Three-step Afro-House emerging as the next influential sound from South Africa, Asake is uniquely positioned to integrate Nigerian sonic identities into this evolving genre. "M$NEY" could provide a blueprint for Afrobeats artists looking to craft hits from this burgeoning sound.
