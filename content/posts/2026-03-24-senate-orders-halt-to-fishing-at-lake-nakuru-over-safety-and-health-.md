By Celestine Nzioka The Senate Committee on National Security, Defence, and Foreign Relations, led by Isiolo Senator Fatuma Dullo, has ordered an immediate cessation of all fishing at Lake Nakuru as of Tuesday, March 24, 2026. This directive follows allegations of fishermen disappearances and official warnings regarding the fish's unsuitability for consumption, sparking health and safety concerns.

Senate Orders Immediate Halt to Lake Nakuru Fishing

Committee Intervenes Amid Disappearance Claims The Senate Committee's directive came during a meeting with the Kenya Wildlife Service (KWS) and other stakeholders. This session was prompted by a petition alleging abuses by KWS officers at Lake Nakuru National Park, including reported disappearances of fishermen. Isiolo Senator Fatuma Dullo, chair of the Committee, explicitly stated, "Any fishing at Lake Nakuru must stop immediately, KWS must ensure that there is no fishing taking place at the Lake". Petitioners presented claims to the Committee that residents relying on fishing for their livelihoods have faced intimidation and violence from KWS officers. However, KWS has vehemently denied any involvement in forced disappearances, torture, or intimidation of fishermen at Lake Nakuru. KWS Director General Erustus Kanga affirmed the agency's openness to specific, documented complaints but stated, "We have not received any formal reports, complaints, or administrative notifications of forced disappearances of fishermen at Lake Nakuru National Park".

Urgent Health Crisis Declared Beyond safety concerns, the KWS raised alarms regarding public health, asserting that fish harvested from Lake Nakuru are unfit for consumption. KWS warned the public, especially those in Nairobi, against consuming these fish. Director General Kanga described the situation as a "national health crisis in the country." He added, "People are taking fish that have been declared unfit for consumption. We are killing people". The Senate questioned why, if KWS claims were accurate, fishing had been permitted to continue for so long. Senator Dullo reinforced the immediate cessation, asking, "If, based on the submission here, that Nakuru fish were declared unfit for human consumption, why are people being allowed to fish and supply the condemned fish to markets, this must stop today".

Calls for Accountability and Policy Reform The petition to the Senate seeks thorough intervention, including an investigation into the reported disappearances, particularly any involvement of KWS officers. Petitioners are also advocating for measures to strengthen community engagement and safeguard human rights. Furthermore, they have called for a comprehensive review of KWS policies and operational protocols to curb potential abuse of power and enhance accountability, fostering dialogue among the agency, local fishermen, and other stakeholders to resolve ongoing disputes over fishing rights. Such reforms are crucial for ensuring institutional integrity and protecting against power-hungry syndicates passing off as political parties that undermine democracy.
