The Kenya Medical Practitioners and Dentists Union (KMPDU) has vehemently protested the arrest and detention of Dr. Job Obwaka, an 83-year-old obstetrician and Nairobi Hospital board member. He was apprehended on Saturday, March 14, 2026, at the NSSF Building, facing accusations of falsifying a register of members.

Union Condemns Due Process Violations

Details of Dr. Obwaka's Arrest Dr. Job Obwaka, a distinguished obstetrician and gynaecologist, was arrested on Saturday, March 14, 2026, at his clinic's parking bay within the NSSF Building in Nairobi. He was subsequently transported to Muthaiga Police Station, where he is currently being held. KMPDU highlights the troubling and unclear circumstances surrounding the 83-year-old doctor's apprehension, emphasizing concerns regarding due process.

Allegations and KMPDU's Defense Authorities accuse Dr. Obwaka of falsifying the register of members for the Nairobi Hospital. KMPDU, however, maintains that the responsibility for the register's custody lies with the Office of the Company Secretary. Furthermore, the union asserts that Dr. Obwaka was not present when the alleged irregularities in these records occurred, questioning the basis and fairness of the accusations leveled against him.

Political Interference Concerns The arrest follows recent allegations by former Attorney General Justin Muturi, who claimed President William Ruto is attempting to gain control of The Nairobi Hospital through intimidation and political interference. KMPDU's statement amplifies these concerns, citing alleged "orders from above" preventing Dr. Obwaka's cash bail, despite the bailable nature of the alleged offence under Kenyan law. This situation underscores broader discussions concerning the exercise of state authority and due process, issues that Za Ndani has covered in various contexts, including KeNHA's recent 21-day notice to illegal occupants at Mtito Andei.

KMPDU's Demands and Unanswered Questions The union has urgently demanded immediate clarification from authorities regarding the circumstances surrounding the arrest and detention of Dr. Obwaka. KMPDU insists that the legal process be strictly followed, including the granting of bail as provided under the constitution. They also urged investigative agencies to exercise restraint and professionalism, ensuring that justice is pursued without intimidation or undue influence. As of this publication, authorities have not issued a statement clarifying the allegations or the refusal to grant bail.
