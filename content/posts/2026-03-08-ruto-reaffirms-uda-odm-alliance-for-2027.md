## Ruto Reaffirms UDA ODM Alliance for 2027

President William Ruto has reaffirmed the cooperation between the United Democratic Alliance (UDA) and the Orange Democratic Movement (ODM) party. The partnership, aimed at fostering national unity and ending divisive politics, is advancing talks for a collaboration leading to the 2027 general elections. A report detailing progress on their 10-point agenda is expected soon.

### Alliance Reaffirmed Amidst Dissent

Speaking at ACK St. Michael's and All Angels Cathedral in Bondo, President Ruto stated the cooperation is founded on mutual respect, characterizing it as a relationship of equals. He committed to fidelity within the partnership, expecting a win-win outcome. This reaffirmation comes as a dissenting faction within the ODM party critiques the relationship, with some members cautioning that the party risks being marginalized if negotiations with UDA proceed.

### Progress on 10-Point Agenda

The head of state insisted the government has made progress on the agreed cooperation agenda and will present a report on Tuesday, March 10. This report is expected to detail achievements to members of both political parties and the public. While some ODM members accuse Ruto of ignoring the initial 10-point agenda signed with former Prime Minister Raila Odinga, the President asserted the report reflects national realities and will guide future collaboration. The committee overseeing the agenda's implementation is slated to present its findings to President Ruto and ODM leader Oburu Odinga.

### Regional Gains and Future of the Pact

Leaders present at the church service highlighted the benefits of the one-year cooperation under the broad-based government for the region. Homabay Governor and ODM chairperson Gladys Wanga cited projects such as the Rironi-Mau Summit dualling, the Standard Gauge Railway (SGR) extension to Kisumu, and the upgrade of Jaramogi Odinga Odinga Hospital to a level 6 facility. Oburu Odinga affirmed the agreement has secured regional development, indicating ODM's intention to remain in the partnership to bolster growth through government support. Ruto's clarification addresses concerns about the cooperation's continuity after its initial one-year MoU period. Nairobi Senator Edwin Sifuna, an ODM party member, has declared the cooperation concluded following the lapse. Conversely, Oburu Odinga and Governor Wanga maintain no timeline exists for the cooperation's end, intending to celebrate its one-year anniversary. President Ruto, however, did not clarify ODM's demand for the deputy president's position in the ongoing talks.
