The Kenya Universities and Colleges Central Placement Service (KUCCPS) clarified university placement criteria on Saturday, April 18, 2026, announcing that cut-off points will not be the primary determinant for this year's placements. Instead, decisions will strictly follow cluster points, course baskets, and institutional capacity, addressing student confusion during the ongoing application process.

KUCCPS Streamlines Placement Application Process

Placement Criteria Shift KUCCPS CEO Agnes Wahome, alongside Director John Oluoch, confirmed during a placement webinar that while cut-off points are visible on the portal, they will serve only as a guide for eligibility. The final placement decisions will be based strictly on cluster points and weights, course baskets, and the available capacity within programs and institutions. Wahome advised students to confidently apply for programs they qualify for based on cluster points, irrespective of the indicated cut-off figures.

Understanding Cut-Off Point Fluctuations The agency clarified that cut-off points naturally fluctuate annually, influenced by the overall performance in the Kenya Certificate of Secondary Education (KCSE). Stronger candidate performance leads to higher cut-off points due to increased competition for limited slots. Conversely, reduced performance results in lower cut-off points, reflecting decreased competition across various courses.

Optimizing Application Choices Applicants were advised to fully utilize all six available application choices to spread risk and enhance their chances of placement. KUCCPS cautioned against selecting the same program multiple times within the same institution. However, the same course can be chosen across different universities. The ongoing digital application process requires careful attention to detail, highlighting the evolving digital landscape, particularly for institutions and media in the region. Second Revision and Transfers For those who do not secure placement in their initial choices, KUCCPS will initiate a second revision process. Applicants will be contacted via SMS using the phone numbers provided during the initial application. This second revision will be free of charge, requiring students to reuse their initial Ksh1,500 payment code for verification. Accurate and accessible mobile numbers are crucial for receiving timely notifications. Students wishing to transfer from private to public universities can only do so through the official inter-university transfer window, which will be announced at a later date.

Security Warning KUCCPS issued a stern warning regarding portal security, advising students against sharing their passwords, even with cyber café attendants. This caution comes amid a rising number of reported cases of account tampering and fraud, underscoring the importance of personal data security during the application period.
