## Inspector General Douglas Kanja and the Imperative of Election Security

The recurring instability across our cities demands a shift from reactive policing to proactive governance. As we approach the next electoral cycle, the failure to address structural gaps in law enforcement readiness threatens the very foundation of our national peace.

Inspector General Douglas Kanja,

The current state of our national security is not merely a matter of operational logistics. It is a direct reflection of administrative foresight. As the person responsible for the safety of every citizen, you hold a burden that transcends the day to day management of the police force. The recent public transport boycotts and the subsequent unrest among our youth serve as a clear warning. These are not isolated incidents of hooliganism. They are symptoms of a citizenry that has lost faith in the ability of state institutions to maintain order without crossing the line into state-sponsored chaos.

### The shadow of past administrative failures

We must look back at the June 2024 Parliament demonstration incidents as the primary case study for what happens when preparation fails. During those events, the disconnect between crowd control strategies and the protection of constitutional rights was glaring. The images from that day are still etched in the public memory, serving as a reminder that when the police are ill-equipped or poorly instructed, the resulting friction harms the state more than the protestors.

You recently briefed Parliament on the critical issue of officer shortages. This is not just a payroll concern or a recruitment statistic. There is a direct institutional link between these shortages and the readiness of the IEBC to conduct free and fair elections. If the police force is spread thin, the security of polling stations and the integrity of the ballot transport chain become vulnerable. Security for the election is not a task you perform after the votes are cast. It begins with the logistical presence required to ensure that every Kenyan feels safe enough to participate in the democratic process without fear of intimidation or unchecked violence.

### Accountability over political maneuvers

The political climate remains heated, as evidenced by the ongoing [clashes over zoning proposals for political strongholds](https://zandani.co.ke/article/2026-04-06-odm-and-uda-clash-over-zoning-proposals-for-strongholds). Your office must remain above these partisan fraying points. When the police force is perceived as an extension of political ambition, the peace we enjoy becomes fragile. True security is built on the predictable, professional application of the law, not on the tactical shifting of resources to favor one faction over another.

We are reminded that as far back as May 21, 1780, societies understood that public order is the bedrock of civilization. While our context is modern Kenya, the principle remains. If the administration cannot guarantee a secure environment, citizens will find their own ways to voice their frustrations, and those ways are rarely peaceful.

### Why it matters now

The volatility we see in our streets today is the barometer for the upcoming election. If the gaps in policing and the shortages in officer deployments are not resolved immediately, the risks to our democratic integrity multiply. We cannot afford another June 2024 scenario where the state appears caught off guard by its own people.

### What to watch next

Observe the budget allocations for police training in the coming quarter. Watch for transparent communication from the National Police Service regarding the specific deployment protocols for the electoral period. If these remain opaque, we remain at risk.

Inspector General, the peace of this nation is currently held together by little more than hope. You have the authority to turn that hope into a structured, reliable security plan. I ask that you stop treating security as a response to crisis and start treating it as the primary condition for a functioning democracy. Provide the transparency our citizens deserve.

Release a comprehensive, public-facing timeline for the recruitment and strategic deployment of officers dedicated specifically to electoral security.
