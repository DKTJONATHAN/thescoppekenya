## Strengthening Kenya's Livestock Health for Food Security Kenya is enhancing its capacity to detect livestock diseases, vital for safeguarding farmer livelihoods, food security, and public health. A recent workshop united stakeholders to develop accessible, affordable diagnostic solutions, aiming for early disease identification, effective outbreak response, and robust animal health systems nationally.

Protecting Farmer Livelihoods from Disease Livestock diseases such as Foot-and-Mouth, Peste des Petits Ruminants (PPR), and various parasitic infections continue to significantly threaten Kenyan farmers. These diseases reduce productivity, limit access to markets, and can spread from animals to humans, causing zoonotic outbreaks. Many small-scale livestock producers and pastoralist farmers struggle with a lack of affordable and reliable tools for early disease detection, hindering their ability to respond effectively.

Boosting Diagnostics for Faster Response A high-level workshop recently convened in Nairobi, bringing together government officials, veterinarians, researchers, development partners, private sector actors, and farmer representatives. The meeting focused on practical solutions to make disease detection more accessible, affordable, and effective for livestock keepers throughout Kenya. Strengthening veterinary diagnostic capacity is considered central to early disease detection, outbreak response, surveillance, food safety, and trade assurance. The Principal Secretary in the State Department for Livestock Development, Hon. Jonathan Mueke, commended ongoing efforts to enhance Kenya's veterinary laboratory network, improve disease reporting, and expand technology use in animal health. Dr. Allan Azegele, Director of Veterinary Services, emphasized that early and accurate disease detection is crucial for protecting livestock productivity, safeguarding human health, and ensuring food security. Improved diagnostics enable faster responses to outbreaks, reduce drug misuse, and guide targeted vaccination campaigns.

Forging Partnerships for a Healthier Herd The workshop, co-organized by the Directorate of Veterinary Services (DVS) and the Transforming Animal Health Solutions and Services for Low- and Middle-Income Countries (TAHSSL) platform, underscored the importance of partnerships. Collaborations between government, research institutions, the private sector, and communities are considered essential for building an effective disease detection and surveillance system. Discussions focused on closing the gap between laboratory capabilities and the realities faced by farmers, especially in smallholder and pastoralist systems. Participants identified priority diagnostic needs, explored innovative delivery models, and co-developed a roadmap to integrate reliable disease testing into Kenya’s livestock health programs. This initiative aligns with regional and global goals, including the eradication of PPR by 2030 and strengthening One Health approaches that link animal and human health systems. Following the workshop, a report and roadmap will be produced, outlining key actions, partnerships, and investment opportunities to keep animals healthy, strengthen farmer livelihoods, and better protect Kenya against future outbreaks.
