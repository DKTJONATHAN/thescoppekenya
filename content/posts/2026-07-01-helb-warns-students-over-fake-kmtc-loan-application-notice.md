## Protecting Your Education Funding from Digital Scams HELB warns students over fake KMTC loan application notice is the central subject of the update.

The Higher Education Loans Board has flagged a viral notice circulating on social media regarding 2026/27 Kenya Medical Training College application windows as entirely fake. As students rush to secure financing, scammers are weaponizing this anxiety to harvest personal financial data.

### Why are students specifically being targeted now?

The digital space has become a hunting ground for fraudsters who understand exactly when students feel the most pressure. Academic calendars create predictable moments of desperation. When students see a false notice claiming that a deadline is approaching, they often act without verifying the source to avoid missing out on limited funds.

Scammers use this urgency to bypass critical thinking. They mimic the branding and tone of official government agencies to create a false sense of security. This is particularly dangerous as the country continues to expand its local data infrastructure, which makes digital transactions and information sharing faster but also creates new avenues for identity theft. Just as consumers must be cautious with new financial policies like the proposed 16 percent VAT on mobile money transactions, students must treat any link demanding personal details with extreme skepticism.

### How can you distinguish between a scam and a real alert?

Official communication from the board follows a strict rhythm. For the 2026/2027 academic cycle, the actual loan application process for continuing students opened on June 1. However, first-time applicants should note that the legitimate portal for their specific intake is not scheduled for release until the second week of July. Any notice suggesting otherwise is a clear attempt to mislead.

If you encounter a link that looks suspicious, do not click it. Genuine government notifications are never sent through unofficial social media channels or sent as forwarded messages on instant messaging platforms. If you need to verify the status of your application or check for new updates, only access the official board portal directly through a browser, rather than clicking links embedded in unsolicited messages. Protecting your data is just as important as securing your tuition fees.

### What should you do if you encounter a fake link?

The board has provided a direct line for reporting these fraudulent activities. If you see a suspicious notice or a link that claims to represent the board, send the details to 0711052000. This number acts as the primary contact for flagging predatory behavior. Reporting these scams helps the authorities track the origin of the misinformation and prevents other students from falling into the same trap.

Always remember that legitimate portals will not ask you to pay a fee to access an application form or demand sensitive login credentials via text. When you hear about new developments, check the official website first. If the information does not appear there, treat it as noise. Staying informed is your best defense against those looking to profit from your education goals. As the digital environment evolves, students must prioritize verifying their sources to ensure their financial future remains secure.

Verify every loan notification directly on the official board portal before sharing or acting on any online claim.
