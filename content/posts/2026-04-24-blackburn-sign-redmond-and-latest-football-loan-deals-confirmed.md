## Griezmann's American Dream Takes Shape

The footballing world witnessed a significant transatlantic shift as French superstar Antoine Griezmann, at 35, committed to Major League Soccer side Orlando City SC. Announced on March 24, 2026, Griezmann is set to join the Lions in July after concluding his season with Atlético Madrid. This move underscores a growing trend of established European talents opting for the burgeoning appeal of MLS.

### The MLS Lure

Griezmann's signing as a Designated Player, with a contract extending through the 2027-28 season and an option for 2028-29, highlights the increasing ambition and financial power of MLS clubs. This follows another notable transfer from February 27, 2026, where American international Josh Sargent completed a record-breaking move from Norwich City to Toronto FC. Sargent, at 26, signed through the 2030-31 MLS season, becoming the most expensive American player in MLS history.

## European Clubs Bolster Squads and Future Prospects

Back in Europe, clubs continue to fine-tune their rosters, securing experienced players and investing in promising youth.

### Redmond Lands at Blackburn

Blackburn Rovers confirmed the signing of former Southampton winger Nathan Redmond on March 27, 2026. The 32-year-old, a former England international, penned a short-term deal, bringing a wealth of Premier League and Championship experience to Ewood Park as Blackburn aims to solidify its position in the league. His arrival was made possible by special dispensation from the EFL to alter their 25-man squad due to long-term injuries.

### Permanent Moves and Emerging Talents

Elsewhere, Moroccan international Bilal El Khannouss's loan to Stuttgart was made permanent on April 15, 2026, with the German club activating an €18 million purchase option from Leicester City. This reflects Stuttgart's confidence in the 21-year-old midfielder's impressive performances. Additionally, Barcelona secured the promising 16-year-old English winger Ajay Tavares from Norwich City, a deal that was ratified after bureaucratic hurdles were cleared around February 23, 2026. Liverpool also saw young winger Trent Kone-Doherty complete a permanent move to Norwegian side Molde on February 27, 2026, seeking more regular first-team opportunities.
