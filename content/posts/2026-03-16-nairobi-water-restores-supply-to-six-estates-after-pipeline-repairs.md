Nairobi Water has confirmed the restoration of water services to six major estates today, March 16, 2026, following extensive repairs to a transmission pipeline along Outering Road. The disruption stemmed from severe damage caused by the recent mid-March floods, impacting thousands of homes across eastern Nairobi.

## Water Supply Resumes After Pipeline Repairs

Nairobi Water confirmed the resumption of water services in six major Nairobi estates following critical repairs to a damaged transmission pipeline along Outering Road. This pipeline had been severely compromised by recent floods, leading to a disruption of supply for thousands of homes and necessitating emergency water deliveries. The utility company stated the major transmission pipeline along Outering Road is now fully repaired and restored. The affected areas now receiving water include Buruburu Phases 1 and 2, Kariobangi South and North, Dandora, and parts of Mathare.

### Repair Operations and Affected Zones
The restoration involved intensive welding and fabrication on the damaged pipeline section, particularly near a river crossing where strong floodwaters had weakened infrastructure. Engineers completed these key transmission repairs despite heavy rains and rising river levels. Before repairs concluded, residents were advised to order emergency water via the company's service menu by dialling *260# and selecting Option 5. While services have returned to several estates, repair crews are still working on distribution lines serving Kiambiu and Korogocho, where engineers anticipate restoring full water flow later today. The disruption had extended beyond Eastlands, impacting Eastleigh Section Three, Kiambiu, Jerusalem, Uhuru, Jericho, Ofafa, and Harambee estates, as well as Korogocho, Lower Kabete Lane, and Brookeside Drive, causing days of dry taps.

### Nairobi's Ongoing Water Challenges
The capital continues to face chronic water shortages, with daily demand surpassing 900 million litres against an available supply of nearly 525 million litres. Nairobi primarily relies on water from the Thika, Sasumua, and Ruiru dams, which is treated at the Ng’ethu plant and then distributed across a significant portion of the city's network. To mitigate future damage, Nairobi Water teams are lifting sections of the new pipeline above river level, a strategic move designed to protect critical infrastructure from future heavy rains and flooding along the Outering Road corridor. ### Broader Flood Impact and Government Response
The severe mid-March 2026 flooding in Nairobi has tragically resulted in at least 33 deaths within the city and over 66 fatalities nationwide. The heavy rains have caused extensive property damage, including submerged homes, swept-away vehicles, and disrupted infrastructure, particularly in low-lying informal settlements. On Monday, Nairobi Governor Johnson Sakaja initiated a 48-hour action plan to address worsening flooding, drainage failures, and damaged roads across the city, directing officials to map hotspots, estimate repair costs, and prioritise urgent interventions.
