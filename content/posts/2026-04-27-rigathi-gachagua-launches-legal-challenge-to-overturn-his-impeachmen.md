Former Deputy President Rigathi Gachagua is back in the spotlight, launching a high-stakes legal challenge this week to overturn his 2026 removal from office. The charismatic leader, speaking Sunday in Mombasa, declared his impeachment "fraudulent and illegal," confident the judiciary will deliver justice despite his family's initial reservations about renewed public scrutiny.

## Rigathi Gachagua's Legal Offensive Takes Center Stage

Rigathi Gachagua, leader of the Democracy for the Citizens Party (DCP), has initiated a fresh legal battle to contest his ousting from the Office of the Deputy President in 2026. He maintains his impeachment was a "fraudulent and illegal" process, violating constitutional provisions. Gachagua’s legal team has diligently prepared a comprehensive case, aiming to demonstrate that his removal lacked constitutional merit. This return to the courts, he asserts, is a pursuit of justice for himself and his dedicated supporters.

### Family Concerns and Unwavering Confidence

Despite the public humiliation Gachagua's family experienced, he expressed unwavering confidence in the judiciary's capacity to "rectify the situation." He recounted discussing the matter with his children, who initially opposed his legal comeback due to the trauma they endured from the public embarrassment. Gachagua, however, trusts that "three judges will do what is right and apply the law."

### Explosive Bribery Allegations Surface

Wiper Party leader Kalonzo Musyoka, who accompanied Gachagua to the coast, has made significant claims concerning the conduct of the National Assembly and the Senate during the impeachment proceedings. Musyoka alleged that some lawmakers were offered substantial sums, between Ksh 10 million and Ksh 20 million, to vote in favour of the impeachment. He further asserted that these "illegalities" would be brought to light during the court sessions, claiming certain legislators have already confessed to receiving bribes. This comes at a time when discussions around financial integrity in public service are always prevalent, with entities like the Kenya Human Rights Commission actively pursuing legal action against parties for alleged misuse of public funds.

### The Impeachment That Rocked the Nation

The contentious battle surrounding Gachagua’s removal has been plagued by procedural disputes since October 2026. A major point of contention revolves around the formation of the bench that originally heard his petition. Deputy Chief Justice Philomena Mwilu appointed Justices Eric Ogolla, Anthony Mrima, and Freda Mugambi to hear the case, but Gachagua’s legal team argued this was unconstitutional, emphasizing that only the Chief Justice holds the authority to empanel such a bench. A Court of Appeal decision in May 2025 previously postponed proceedings, leaving Gachagua’s political status in a unique legal limbo. This ongoing legal discourse highlights the crucial role of the judiciary in Kenya's political landscape, especially as public figures increasingly engage in such challenges. The Supreme Court of Kenya, for instance, has a record of handling various petitions, some dating back to 2024 and 2025, which are still being processed in 2026.

### A United Opposition's First Salvo

Rigathi Gachagua holds the unique distinction of being the first Deputy President impeached under the 2010 Constitution, serving the shortest term in that esteemed office. Having moved on from the Kenya Kwanza administration, Gachagua is now a central figure in a formidable United Opposition front. This coalition has openly pledged to challenge President William Ruto in the 2027 General Election, framing Gachagua's current legal fight as a pivotal initial move to dislodge the existing administration from power. His petition is anticipated to be officially filed between Monday and Wednesday this week, setting the stage for a compelling political drama.
