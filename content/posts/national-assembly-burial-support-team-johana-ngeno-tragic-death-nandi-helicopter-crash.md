## National Assembly Forms Burial Support Team After Johana Ng’eno’s Tragic Death Speaker of the National Assembly Moses Wetang'ula has established a special team to support the burial preparations for Johana Ng’eno, the Emurua Dikirr Member of Parliament, who tragically died in a Nandi helicopter crash on Saturday, February 28, 2026. The team, formed following Ng’eno’s demise alongside five other individuals, will collaborate closely with the bereaved families.

Details of the National Assembly Burial Support Team The special parliamentary team, tasked with coordinating funeral arrangements, is led by Tinderet Member of Parliament Julius Melly. He will be assisted by nominated MP Mugambi Rindikiri, alongside eight other members of parliament. Speaker Wetang'ula conveyed profound sorrow on behalf of the House and the Parliamentary Service Commission, extending heartfelt condolences to the families and friends of Hon. Ng'eno and the other crash victims. This initiative underscores the National Assembly's commitment to supporting its members and their families during times of profound loss.

The Nandi Helicopter Crash: Investigation Underway The tragic incident occurred in Chepkiep, Mosop Sub-county, Nandi County, when the helicopter with registration number 5Y-DSB crashed and burst into flames on Saturday evening, February 28, 2026, claiming all six lives aboard. The aircraft, an Airbus Helicopters H125 operated by Youth Aviation Ltd, had departed Nairobi at 11:02 AM. Reports indicate the chopper made an emergency landing in Mosop due to adverse weather conditions, specifically heavy rainfall, and crashed moments after attempting to take off once the weather reportedly improved. Authorities confirmed the aircraft struck a tree before the fatal impact. The Kenya Civil Aviation Authority (KCAA) has initiated formal investigation procedures through its Aircraft Accident Investigation Department (AAID) to ascertain the exact circumstances leading to the Nandi helicopter crash. Moi Teaching and Referral Hospital (MTRH) in Eldoret has received the remains, with CEO Philip Kirwa confirming that the bodies were severely damaged, complicating immediate identification. Ng'eno’s body is expected to be flown to the Lee Funeral Home in Nairobi. The other confirmed victims include pilot George Were, Amos Kipngetich Rotich (a forester and MP's bodyguard), Nick Koskey (a cameraman), Carlos Keter (a high school teacher), and Wycliffe Rono (a Narok County government employee).

Johana Ng’eno’s Tragic Death: A National Loss Johana Ng’eno, 54, was serving his third consecutive term as Emurua Dikirr Member of Parliament, having been first elected in 2013 and successfully re-elected in 2017 and 2022. At the time of his passing, he chaired the Departmental Committee on Housing, Urban Planning, and Public Works, where he played a pivotal role in reviewing the 2026 Budget Policy Statement and overseeing Affordable Housing Projects. President William Ruto, in his condolence message, remembered Ng'eno as a "focused, vocal, and fearless leader who stood firmly for justice and equity for all". The President particularly highlighted Ng'eno's dedication to supporting local talents, especially within the music industry. Speaker Wetang'ula described the lawmaker as a leader who consistently prioritised the interests of his constituents. Narok Governor Patrick Ole Ntutu revealed he had spoken with Ng'eno just hours before the crash, as the MP was en route to a fundraiser for flood victims in Endebess. The nation mourns the loss of a devoted legislator whose contributions resonated across the political and social landscape. The ongoing investigation by KCAA’s AAID is critical to providing clarity on the accident's cause and informing future preventative measures. The loss of a sitting MP and five other Kenyans underscores the need for stringent adherence to aviation protocols and continuous assessment of flight conditions. As the nation awaits official identification of all victims and the full report from the investigation, the incident serves as a stark reminder of the inherent risks in air travel and the profound impact such tragedies have on families and national leadership alike.
