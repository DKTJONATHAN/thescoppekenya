Former Cabinet Secretary Raphael Tuju is facing accusations from the Directorate of Criminal Investigations of orchestrating his own disappearance. After resurfacing on Monday, March 23, 2026, from a tense two-day absence, the DCI alleges Tuju faked his kidnapping from his Karen residence, sparking national controversy and legal proceedings.

DCI Unravels Raphael Tuju's Alleged Staged Kidnapping

Inside the DCI's Forensic Investigation The Directorate of Criminal Investigations has officially accused former Cabinet Secretary Raphael Tuju of fabricating his own kidnapping. These allegations surfaced just days after Tuju’s dramatic reappearance on Monday, March 23, 2026, following a two-day disappearance that had caused widespread concern for his safety. According to DCI Director Mohamed Amin, a thorough forensic analysis and intelligence report have entirely disproved Tuju’s claims of being abducted by unknown assailants. The DCI firmly maintains that Tuju remained at his Karen residence throughout the entire 48-hour period he was reported missing. Investigators further allege Tuju deliberately powered off his mobile phone on March 21, 2026, and stayed indoors, allegedly crafting a false narrative to garner "unwarranted sympathy" for either political or personal reasons.

Family's Non-Cooperation Raises Eyebrows The DCI's suspicions were heightened early in their search operations. Director Amin highlighted that Tuju’s family reportedly declined to cooperate with officers, initially refusing them entry into the Karen home. This perceived lack of cooperation is now viewed by authorities as a critical indicator of a coordinated effort to mislead both the public and law enforcement agencies. Tuju had initially claimed he went into hiding after noticing an unmarked vehicle trailing him, prompting him to abandon his car on Miotoni Lane in Karen to evade his alleged pursuers. However, the DCI now categorizes this incident as part of a "worrying trend" of fabricated abductions. This alleged attempt to gain "unwarranted sympathy" for political motives mirrors the public's keen interest in any perceived strategic maneuvers within the political sphere, much like the scrutiny surrounding claims of a [Homa Bay, Turkana County Pay Rise February: A Cynical Ploy](https://zandani.co.ke/article/homa-bay-turkana-county-pay-rise-february-cynical-ploy).

"Repetitive Theatrics" Says Interior CS Interior Cabinet Secretary Kipchumba Murkomen has publicly weighed in on the unfolding saga, dismissing the entire incident as "repetitive theatrics." Murkomen stressed that such actions not only undermine national security but also squander valuable state resources that could be better utilized elsewhere. The sentiment from the highest echelons of government suggests a firm stance against what they perceive as a waste of public time and effort.

Legal Battle Heats Up for Former CS Following his re-emergence, Tuju was swiftly arrested and booked at the Karen Police Station. He now faces serious criminal charges for allegedly providing false information to public authorities. The legal proceedings took an unexpected turn during court appearances on Tuesday, March 24, 2026, when it was disclosed that Tuju had been admitted to Karen Hospital. While his legal team is vehemently challenging the charges and citing medical concerns, the DCI remains resolute in its commitment to pursue the prosecution. As this high-profile case progresses, all eyes remain on Tuju, whose dramatic shift from a prominent government official to a suspect in a staged kidnapping case has undeniably sent shockwaves throughout Kenya’s political and entertainment circles.
