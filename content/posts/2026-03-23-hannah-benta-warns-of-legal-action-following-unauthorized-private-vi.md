Content creator Hannah Benta issued a stern warning on Sunday, March 22, 2026, threatening legal action against anyone sharing a recently leaked private video. Posting to her verified Instagram account, the mother-of-two condemned the violation of her privacy and demanded immediate accountability from those involved.

Hannah Benta Warns of Legal Action After Private Video Leak

Beyond the Headlines: Benta's Identity and Public Appeal In her first public address regarding the incident, Hannah Benta took a moment to clarify her identity, introducing herself as a mother to her son, Baby K, a daughter, and a sister. She firmly urged the public to disregard various "pseudo" accounts that have falsely claimed to represent her online in recent days. Her statement aimed to re-establish her true persona amidst the circulating unverified information.

Leak Categorized as Gender-Based Violence Benta powerfully framed the unauthorized distribution of her private footage as more than just a breach of trust, explicitly categorizing it as Gender-Based Violence (GBV). She emphasized that the leak constitutes emotional, psychological, and economic abuse, asserting that the content was intended for private eyes only and its public circulation is a violent violation of her privacy.

Ultimatum Issued: Legal Action Looms A direct and urgent ultimatum was issued by Benta to all individuals and platforms currently hosting or circulating the video. She demanded the content be deleted globally across all social media and messaging platforms within a two-hour window. Failure to comply, she warned, would result in aggressive legal action, affirming her commitment to pursuing the matter legally to protect her family's well-being and seek formal justice.

A Call to National Leadership and Human Rights Beyond her personal legal battle, Benta appealed to national leadership and human rights organizations for intervention. She specifically called upon several key entities to address the issue, highlighting the broader societal implications of such privacy violations.

Advocating for Cybercrime Enforcement By unequivocally framing the leak as a criminal act rather than a mere "scandal," Benta joins a growing chorus of public figures advocating for stricter enforcement of the Computer Misuse and Cybercrimes Act. This stance underscores the push for robust legal protections for victims of non-consensual media sharing. The incident adds to ongoing conversations about public figures' privacy, a topic often discussed alongside the high stakes faced by personalities, much like the anticipation surrounding potential contestants for shows such as 'I'm A Celebrity South Africa', where public scrutiny is immense.
