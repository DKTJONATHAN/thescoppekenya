## Liverpool's Costly Rebuild: Tillman's Sky-High Price, Jones' Anfield Exit

New Liverpool boss Andoni Iraola has wasted no time demanding a staggering €50 million-plus outlay for Malik Tillman, even as homegrown talent Curtis Jones actively pushes for a €20 million move to Inter Milan. This summer is rapidly shaping up as a chaotic and expensive transition for the Anfield club.

### Iraola's Inflated Demands

New managers often bring fresh ideas, but Iraola's immediate request for Malik Tillman smacks of urgency and questionable valuation. Bayer Leverkusen only shelled out €35 million for Tillman last summer, yet now, following a World Cup performance that "completely changed the landscape," Liverpool is reportedly prepared to pay over €50 million. This isn't shrewd business; it's market inflation driven by a perceived scarcity and a manager's immediate desire. Leverkusen, naturally, will play hardball, professing a reluctance to sell while secretly calculating the massive profit. Such high-stakes financial manoeuvring isn't new in football, with ownership changes like [Daniel Kretinsky becoming West Ham United majority shareholder](https://zandani.co.ke/article/2026-06-13-daniel-kretinsky-to-become-west-ham-united-majority-shareholder) often signalling new eras of investment or divestment. For Liverpool, committing such a sum so quickly after a managerial change speaks volumes about their perceived gaps in midfield.

### Jones Jumps Ship

While Iraola plots his big-money acquisition, Curtis Jones seems determined to engineer his exit. Inter Milan's €20 million offer is considered low by Liverpool, who are holding out for €30 million-plus. However, Fabrizio Romano's revelation that Jones is "keen on the move" and has been inquiring about life in Italy tells the real story: the player wants out. This isn't a complex negotiation; it's a departure. Liverpool will haggle for every last euro, but losing a homegrown midfielder who has been part of the senior squad for years for a figure between €20-30 million, while simultaneously eyeing a €50 million replacement, underlines a significant squad overhaul.

### Anfield's Chaotic Summer

The exits of Andy Robertson and Ibrahima Konate on free transfers, coupled with Mohamed Salah's impending departure, already signal a tumultuous summer. Now, with Alisson and Alexis Mac Allister also linked with moves away, and Jones seemingly on his way, Liverpool's squad looks set for an unprecedented shake-up. Iraola's arrival was meant to usher in a new era, but the immediate scramble for high-priced talent like Tillman, combined with the mass exodus of established players, paints a picture of a club trying to rebuild on the fly, driven by desperation rather than calm, strategic planning. The focus should be on stability, not a frantic merry-go-round of ins and outs. This isn't a controlled transition; it's a full-blown reconstruction project with a new architect under immense pressure.
