## Body of Missing Kenyan Student Found in UK River The body of Edna Mmbali, a 31-year-old Kenyan master's student reported missing in the United Kingdom since February 1, 2026, was discovered on March 8, 2026. Her remains were found in a river in Wraysbury, Berkshire. While formal identification is pending, authorities strongly believe the body is that of the York St John University student.

Discovery and Police Involvement Thames Valley Police, in conjunction with the Berkshire, Buckinghamshire and Surrey Lowland Search & Rescue team, located the body during search operations. Edna Mmbali, originally from Kitale, had been residing in Wraysbury while pursuing her Master of Science degree. Her last confirmed sighting was on February 1, 2026, when she departed her home for a walk.

Disappearance and Community Response Following days without communication, Ms. Mmbali's family raised an alarm, emphasizing her consistent contact with loved ones in Kenya. Her disappearance garnered widespread attention and support from various groups, including the human rights organization HAKI Africa, members of the Kenyan diaspora, and the Kenya High Commission, alongside UK authorities. The family described her as soft-spoken and 5 feet, 5 inches tall, making appeals to the public for information on her whereabouts. On Sunday, March 8, 2026, her family had organized a virtual prayer meeting, hoping for her safe return.

Ongoing Investigation “Formal identification of the woman has not yet taken place, but it is believed to be missing woman Edna, aged 31, from Wraysbury, who was reported missing on 1 February 2026. Edna’s next of kin have been informed," a statement from Thames Valley Police Windsor and Maidenhead confirmed. UK authorities have not yet disclosed the cause of death, with inquiries continuing to establish the circumstances surrounding her demise.
