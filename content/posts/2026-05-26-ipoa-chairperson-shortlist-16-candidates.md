## Shortlist for IPOA Chairperson

The Selection Panel has unveiled 16 candidates for the Independent Policing Oversight Authority chairperson position. While big names from the former electoral commission dominate the list, the power now shifts to Kenyans who have 48 hours to vet these choices through formal submissions.

### Who are the candidates vying for this critical security role?

The shortlist features a mix of veteran legal minds and former administrators. Among the most recognizable names are Lilian Bokeeye Mahiri-Zaja and Thomas Letangule. Both served as commissioners at the electoral agency during high-pressure periods in Kenya’s recent history. Their transition from managing the ballot to potentially managing police oversight has raised questions about the type of leadership the panel is prioritizing.

Other notable figures include Demas Tuikong Kiprono, a well-known human rights advocate, and Duncan Ojwang Oburu, a legal scholar often seen analyzing constitutional matters. Elizabeth Atieno Ayoo, who has previously served in county assembly leadership in Homa Bay, also made the cut. These 16 individuals were selected from a larger pool of applicants looking to fill the vacancy left by Isaack Hassan, who resigned to join the Court of Appeal earlier this year.

### How can the public challenge or support these names in 48 hours?

The law does not just allow for public participation; it requires it. The Selection Panel has provided a narrow window for Kenyans to submit any information regarding the suitability of these 16 candidates. This is often called the "Citizen’s Veto." If you have evidence that a candidate lacks integrity or has a history that contradicts the requirements of Chapter Six of the Constitution, this is the time to act.

To participate, you must draft a memorandum. This is a formal letter or document stating your support or objection to a specific candidate. It should be accompanied by a sworn affidavit if you are making serious allegations. These documents must be delivered to the Selection Panel’s secretariat, either physically at their designated offices in Nairobi or via the official email addresses provided in the public notice. With the 48-hour clock ticking, the panel expects all submissions to be received before the public interviews begin. This process mirrors the transparency citizens expect in other sectors, much like the scrutiny seen in [Ruto’s sugar export promises](https://zandani.co.ke/article/2026-04-21-rutos-bitter-sugar-promise-italy-export-delusion) where public accountability remains a central theme.

### Why does the background of these candidates matter for police accountability?

The Independent Policing Oversight Authority (IPOA) is the only civilian body with the power to investigate the police. When an officer is accused of using excessive force or being involved in a mysterious disappearance, IPOA is the agency that must step in. If the chairperson is seen as a political appointee or someone who is too close to the executive branch, the independence of these investigations is compromised.

The inclusion of former electoral officials is a point of debate. These individuals have experience working in high-stakes, constitutional offices. However, the skills needed to manage a national election are different from those required to hold the National Police Service to account. A chairperson must be able to stand firm against pressure from powerful security bosses. They need a deep understanding of criminal law and a proven track record of defending the rights of ordinary citizens who may not have the resources to fight back against state-sanctioned harassment.

### Why it matters now

The chairperson’s seat has been vacant since Isaack Hassan’s departure, leaving the agency in a state of transition during a period of increased public concern over police conduct. A swift and credible appointment is necessary to restore full functional capacity to the oversight body and ensure that pending investigations into police brutality do not stall.

### What to watch next

The Selection Panel will begin public interviews for the 16 candidates immediately after the memorandum window closes. Once the interviews are concluded, the panel will pick the top three names and forward them to President William Ruto. The President will then nominate one person and send that name to the National Assembly for vetting and eventual approval.

Your window to influence the police oversight leadership is open right now, but it closes in two days.
