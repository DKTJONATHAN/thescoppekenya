**Nairobi Traffic Crisis: Heavy Rains Leave Commuters Stranded for Hours Across Major Highways** **By Celestine Nzioka** Nairobi residents woke up to a morning of absolute transport paralysis today as heavy overnight rains wreaked havoc on the city infrastructure. The downpour which began late last night resulted in massive traffic snarl ups that left thousands of motorists stuck for hours on end. The congestion affected nearly all major entry and exit points of the capital city with the situation being described as some of the worst traffic seen this month. The Nairobi Expressway which is typically known for its fast movement was not spared. The high speed corridor was transformed into a slow moving stream of vehicles starting from Mlolongo near the Jomo Kenyatta International Airport. This tailback stretched along the median of Mombasa Road and Uhuru Highway before extending all the way to James Gichuru Road in Westlands. Despite the visible gridlock on the elevated road, the Moja Expressway Company had not issued any formal communication to motorists by the time of this report. On the lower deck of the city road network, the situation was even more dire. Waiyaki Way experienced a total breakdown in traffic flow with vehicles backed up from Kabete Police Station through Westlands and towards the Kenyatta Avenue roundabout. Many motorists attempted to escape the main highway by using feeder roads such as Peponi Road, but these too quickly became overwhelmed by the sheer volume of traffic. Commuters have shared harrowing accounts of their morning journeys. Esther Muthoni, a media practitioner who travels from Kikuyu, narrated how her usual commute became a four hour ordeal. She left her residence at 5:30 am and only managed to reach her destination at 9:30 am. Such delays have become a common occurrence during the rainy season, yet they continue to cause significant loss of productivity for city workers. Thika Road also faced severe congestion particularly on the inbound lanes. The most affected areas included the stretch between Pangani and Utalii College as well as the exit near the General Service Unit Training School. The Muthaiga Roundabout remained a major bottleneck for the better part of the morning while additional delays were reported along Muranga Road. Other parts of the city that felt the pressure include the diplomatic district of Gigiri and the Red Hill area near the United States Embassy. Kilimani residents also reported slow movement along Dennis Pritt Road as vehicles sought alternative paths to the central business district. There was a slight reprieve on Outer Ring Road where traffic flow began to improve by mid morning as the early rush subsided. Motorists are however advised to remain cautious and expect further delays if the rains persist throughout the evening. The meteorological department has warned of continued rainfall across the city and authorities are urging drivers to maintain safe distances and avoid flooded sections of the road. **Published 10 hours ago**
