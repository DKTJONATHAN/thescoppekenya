Paul Thomas Anderson's cinematic triumph, *One Battle After Another*, dominated the 98th Academy Awards on Sunday, March 15, 2026. Held at the Dolby Theatre in Los Angeles, the film secured six Oscars, including the coveted Best Picture, marking a significant night for the acclaimed director.

Paul Thomas Anderson's "One Battle After Another" Dominates 98th Academy Awards *One Battle After Another*, an ironic look at nativism and activism, proved to be the standout feature, clinching six awards throughout the evening. Paul Thomas Anderson himself took home Best Director and Best Adapted Screenplay, a long-awaited recognition in his 30-year career. The film's other significant wins included Best Film Editing for Andy Jurgensen and Best Casting for Cassandra Kulukundis.

Michael B. Jordan Secures Best Actor in a Tight Race Ryan Coogler's *Sinners* made Oscar history with a record-breaking 16 nominations, ultimately earning four awards. Among its victories was the hotly contested Best Actor award for Michael B. Jordan. Jordan's win came after a season of intense competition, having gained momentum with an upset at the Screen Actors Guild Awards following early wins for Timothée Chalamet at the Golden Globes and Critics' Choice Awards. Coogler also won Best Original Screenplay for *Sinners*.

A Night of First-Time Oscar Glory The 98th Academy Awards celebrated numerous first-time recipients across key categories. Seven of the eight major awards went to individuals experiencing their inaugural Oscar win, including Paul Thomas Anderson. Jessie Buckley was honored with Best Actress for her performance in *Hamnet*, a historical drama based on Maggie O'Farrell's novel. Veteran character actress Amy Madigan also earned her first Oscar, taking home Best Supporting Actress for *Weapons*. The Academy Awards evening, a major fixture in the global entertainment calendar, often sparks discussions about celebrity influence and international engagement.

Sean Penn Joins Elite with Third Acting Oscar The only exception to the night's first-timer trend was Sean Penn, who received Best Supporting Actor for his role in *One Battle After Another*. This achievement makes Penn only the fourth male actor to win three acting Oscars, joining the esteemed ranks of Daniel Day-Lewis, Jack Nicholson, and Walter Brennan. Penn notably did not attend the ceremony. Despite his nomination for Best Actor in *One Battle After Another*, Leonardo DiCaprio left the event without an award. Other notable winners included "Golden" by EJAE, Mark Sonnenblick, Joong Gyu Kwak, Yu Han Lee, Hee Dong Nam, Jeong Hoon Seo, and Teddy Park for *KPop Demon Hunters*. *F1* earned the award for Gareth John, Al Nelson, Gwendolyn Yates Whittle, Gary A Rizzo, and Juan Peralta. *Avatar: Fire and Ash* won for Joe Letteri, Richard Baneham, Eric Saindon, and Daniel Barrett. *Frankenstein* secured awards for Tamara Deverell and Shane Vieau in Production Design, and Mike Hill, Jordan Samuel, and Cliona Furey in Makeup and Hairstyling. In a rare occurrence, the award for Live Action Short Film resulted in a tie, going to *Two People Exchanging Saliva*.
