Democracy for Citizens Party (DCP) leader Rigathi Gachagua on Sunday, April 19, 2026, warned Health Cabinet Secretary Aden Duale against criticizing former President Uhuru Kenyatta. Speaking at ACK Sr Mark Maragua Church in Murang'a County, Gachagua vehemently defended Kenyatta against Duale's recent political attacks.

## Renewed Political Tensions

The political dispute reignited after Duale publicly accused former President Kenyatta on Saturday, April 18, of undermining President William Ruto's administration and actively forming new political alliances for the 2027 elections. Duale urged Kenyatta to "go home and rest," alleging he had politically backstabbed his successor and attempted to position himself as a kingmaker. The Health CS also revisited historical grievances, claiming Kenyatta "humiliated and persecuted" Ruto during their time in government and attempted an impeachment in 2019 without sufficient parliamentary backing.

### Gachagua Defends Kenyatta's Tenure

In a swift rebuttal, Gachagua fiercely defended former President Kenyatta, asserting that he never orchestrated actions against his deputy, William Ruto. Gachagua stated Kenyatta never paid Members of Parliament to impeach Ruto as Deputy President, referencing his own impeachment in late 2024. He further claimed Kenyatta did not deploy goons or police to confront Ruto during his deputy presidency, allowing him to serve his full term. Gachagua highlighted that Kenyatta granted Ruto considerable freedom to traverse the country, including frequent visits to the Mt Kenya region, without interference or restrictions from State House. He contrasted this with what he described as President Ruto's "very, very brutal and extremely inhuman" treatment of his own deputy, both during and after office, alleging sustained campaigns including police presence at his home and disruption of his children's businesses. President Ruto is set to visit Italy to strengthen trade and investment partnerships in the coming months, focusing on broader governmental initiatives.

### SHA Financial Crisis Allegations

Beyond the political exchanges, Gachagua shifted focus to the state of the Social Health Authority (SHA), warning of its impending collapse within the next five months. He claimed SHA currently faces liabilities amounting to Ksh98 billion, describing it as a "walking zombie" teetering on the brink of the largest corporate loss in independent Kenya's history. Gachagua further alleged that a Dubai-based company with ties to CS Duale receives Ksh600 million monthly, even as SHA's significant debts remain unpaid.
