## Nairobi and the Promise of the Africa Forward Summit

The coffee in my mug had long gone cold as I watched the motorcades weave through the Nairobi streets this morning. Behind the polished glass and the handshakes at the Africa Forward Summit, the real test is whether these agreements build our future or gather dust in archives.

This inaugural summit marks a high stakes moment for our capital. While global leaders discuss trade and digital transformation, the citizens of this city are looking past the suits and the staged photo opportunities. We have grown weary of summits that offer only vague promises of cooperation. We are now looking for a clear trajectory of how diplomatic handshakes translate into actual capital inflow. If we want to move beyond the theatre of international summits, we must treat these French-funded proposals with a pragmatic lens that prioritizes local industrialization over administrative feasibility studies.

### From Diplomatic Promises to Tangible Assets

The rhetoric flowing from the summit floor is full of grand visions for climate action and digital growth. Yet, Nairobi already hosts a wealth of potential that remains stifled by infrastructure gaps. When we talk about French investment in our energy or tech sectors, the measure of success should not be the memorandum of understanding signed on a stage. Instead, the focus must be on whether these funds bridge the persistent divide between our existing utility grid and the demands of modern manufacturing.

True progress happens when international capital does not just land in Nairobi but permeates the local industrial base. We have seen too many projects where the foreign consultants arrive, the studies are published, and the funding dries up before the first brick is laid. To change this cycle, we need to track how much of this investment is earmarked for actual procurement from local suppliers rather than returning to foreign firms.

### Integrating the Local Private Sector

International deals often falter because they view our local private sector as a footnote rather than a partner. For the Africa Forward Summit to be different, the French investors must move past the idea of Kenya as a mere market for exports. We have local entrepreneurs and engineers who understand the specific logistics of our terrain better than any international firm.

When we integrate our small and medium enterprises into the supply chain of these massive French projects, we ensure that the capital stays within our borders. This is the difference between a project that serves as a temporary injection of money and one that creates long term, high quality jobs. Just as we see in the grit of the sporting world, such as when [Mikel Arteta emphasizes his resolve following Arsenal loss to Bournemouth](https://zandani.co.ke/article/mikel-arteta-emphasizes-his-resolve-following-arsenal-loss-to-bourne), the true worth of any venture is found in the persistence of the follow-up, not the initial excitement of the kickoff.

### Why it Matters Now

We are at a tipping point where Nairobi is being positioned as a global hub. If these investments manifest as roads, digital infrastructure, and manufacturing plants, the city will thrive as an engine for the continent. If they remain bureaucratic exercises, we will simply be left with more meetings and unchanged living conditions for the average Kenyan.

### What to Watch Next

The coming months will be telling. We must watch the specific project timelines that emerge from these talks. Look for the public tender announcements for the infrastructure projects discussed this week. If the process involves local subcontracting requirements, there is reason for optimism. If the agreements stay in the realm of high-level policy dialogue, we will know that the summit was just another exercise in vanity.

Real development is rarely as loud as the speeches given in convention centers. It happens in the quiet, steady delivery of hardware and the sustained employment of local talent. We have heard the promises. Now, we watch for the work.
