Marathoner Albert Korir has been issued a five-year ban by the Athletics Integrity Unit (AIU) for multiple doping violations, effective Monday, March 30, 2026. The athlete admitted to using Methoxy polyethylene glycol-epoetin beta (CERA), a prohibited substance, leading to the disqualification of his results from October 3, 2025, onwards.

Sanctions Imposed for Prohibited Substance Use The AIU confirmed Korir's guilt, detailing that he admitted to using CERA in several out-of-competition tests. CERA, or Methoxy polyethylene glycol-epoetin beta, is a synthetic form of a hormone that boosts red blood cell production. The World Anti-Doping Agency (WADA) classifies it as a prohibited substance because it enhances endurance by improving oxygen delivery to muscles, providing an unfair advantage.

Violation Details and Admission Korir's doping violation was detected after he provided three samples in October 2025. Analysis at a WADA-accredited laboratory confirmed the presence of the prohibited substance. As Korir did not possess a Therapeutic Use Exemption (TUE) for CERA and testing procedures were without error, this was classified as a doping violation. On January 12, 2026, Korir acknowledged the anti-doping violations and accepted the proposed sanctions, which reduced his ban by one year from the standard six years.

Disqualification and Forfeiture of Achievements Following the ban, Korir has been disqualified from all results achieved from October 3, 2025, onwards. This sanction includes the forfeiture of all medals, titles, points, prize money, and appearance fees earned during this period. WADA and the Anti-Doping Agency of Kenya (ADAK) maintain the right to appeal this decision to the Court of Arbitration for Sport. Korir may also file a cross-appeal if either body challenges the ruling.

Impact on a Distinguished Career Before this ban, Korir had a distinguished professional career. His most significant victory was at the 2021 New York City Marathon, where he secured his first Abbott World Marathon Major title with a time of 2:08:22, earning approximately KSH 13 million (USD 100,000). Korir also won the Ottawa Marathon twice, in 2019 and again in May 2025. He consistently achieved podium finishes in New York, including a third-place finish in November 2025. His personal best of 2:06:57 was set at the 2023 New York City Marathon.
