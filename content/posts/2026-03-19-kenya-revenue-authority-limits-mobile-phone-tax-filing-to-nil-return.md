The Kenya Revenue Authority (KRA) confirmed today, March 19, 2026, that taxpayers can utilize mobile phones for filing nil returns exclusively, but only after March 31. This clarification addresses widespread inquiries as the annual tax filing season progresses, offering a specific mobile option under strict conditions for those not earning income or operating a business.

## Mobile Filing Streamlined for Nil Returns

### Nil Return Specifics

Za Ndani learned that the KRA has clarified that filing tax returns via a mobile phone is only possible for nil returns. However, the authority stated that taxpayers must wait until after March 31 to commence filing for nil returns. The KRA explicitly noted that mobile filing is an option for individuals seeking to file nil returns, but the post-March 31 waiting period remains.

### Challenges for Employed and Business Taxpayers

For individuals who are employed or managing a business, the KRA indicates that filing returns through a mobile device may present difficulties due to the inherent complexities. The authority specified that employed or business individuals would find mobile phone tax filing challenging. This response came after a user on a public platform inquired about the possibility of filing tax returns on a phone.

### New Pre-Filled Return System

The KRA's clarification coincides with the commencement of SMS notifications to employed individuals, detailing a simpler and faster method for filing returns. These messages, predominantly dispatched on Thursday, March 19, announced the official rollout of a pre-filled return system. This new system automatically populates employment income details. Consequently, individuals earning a salary will no longer be required to manually enter most of their income information when submitting their annual returns.

### June 30 Filing Deadline

With the deadline for tax filing set to lapse on June 30, the KRA has encouraged taxpayers to file their returns promptly to avoid the last-minute rush.
