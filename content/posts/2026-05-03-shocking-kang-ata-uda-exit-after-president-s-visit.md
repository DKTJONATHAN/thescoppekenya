## Kang'ata's UDA Exit Reshapes Murang'a Politics

Murang'a Governor Irungu Kang'ata declared today he will not seek re-election on a United Democratic Alliance (UDA) ticket in 2027. This decision follows internal party pressure, differing strategic views, and a candid discussion with President William Ruto, marking a significant shift in Mount Kenya's political landscape. He intends to unveil a new platform.

### Governor Kang'ata's Political Realignment

Kang'ata's Sunday, May 3 announcement to depart from the United Democratic Alliance (UDA) for his 2027 gubernatorial re-election bid introduces a new dynamic into Murang'a and Mount Kenya region politics. Speaking hours after accompanying President William Ruto to various functions in Murang'a, Kang'ata cited internal and external party pressure, alongside differing views on strategy and party direction, as catalysts for his decision. He revealed a discussion with President Ruto did not achieve "full convergence," indicating underlying policy or strategic disagreements. Despite his intention to seek re-election on an alternative platform, Kang'ata emphasized his continued loyalty to the President and the UDA party for the remainder of the current term, pledging cooperation with the national government, a stance that aligns with efforts such as the Education CS's recent assurances regarding university fee payments after a funding system review [https://zandani.co.ke/article/education-cs-assures-university-students-of-smooth-fee-payments-afte].

### Implications for UDA in Mount Kenya

Kang'ata's declaration holds particular significance as he becomes the first high-profile leader from the Mount Kenya region to publicly announce a departure from UDA ahead of 2027. This move occurs amidst reports of shifting loyalties among regional leaders. His exit suggests growing dissent or strategic repositioning within a region critical to UDA's national support base. The governor's refusal to publicly declare his unwavering commitment to UDA during recent presidential tours to Murang'a foreshadowed this announcement. His stated intention to remain loyal through the current term while simultaneously planning an exit in 2027 presents a complex political tightrope walk, potentially aimed at mitigating immediate political fallout while securing future options.

### Kang'ata's Future Political Platform

While confirming his intent to defend his Murang'a gubernatorial seat, Kang'ata explicitly downplayed aspirations for the presidency or deputy presidency, stating his focus remains solely on local leadership. His promise to unveil a new political platform at a later date signals a strategic incubation period for his next move. This approach allows him to observe the evolving political landscape and potentially coalesce with other like-minded figures or emerging political movements. The timing and specifics of this new platform will be crucial in defining his political trajectory and its broader impact on Mount Kenya's political allegiances leading into the 2027 general elections.
