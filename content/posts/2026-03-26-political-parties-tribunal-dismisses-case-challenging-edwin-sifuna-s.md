The Political Parties' Disputes Tribunal on Thursday, March 26, 2026, dismissed a case challenging the removal of Nairobi senator Edwin Sifuna as the Orange Democratic Movement (ODM) secretary general. The tribunal cited a lack of jurisdiction, instructing Sifuna and the ODM party to first exhaust internal dispute resolution mechanisms.

## Tribunal Upholds Internal Dispute Resolution

### Jurisdiction and Procedure

Gad Gathu, the tribunal's acting chairperson, delivered the ruling, asserting that the tribunal lacked the authority to hear Sifuna's case. Gathu explained that Sifuna failed to utilize ODM's Internal Dispute Resolution Mechanisms (IDRM) before approaching the tribunal. The tribunal mandated that both the complainant and the first respondent engage in the party's IDRM processes in good faith. Gathu underscored the tribunal's inability to bypass the requirement for IDRM, stating it could not assume jurisdiction under Section 40, Subsection 2 of the Political Parties Act without prior internal attempts at resolution.

### Background to Sifuna's Ouster

Senator Sifuna had sought the tribunal's intervention to prevent his removal from the secretary general position. His appeal followed a resolution by the ODM National Executive Committee (NEC) on February 11, which decided to remove him from office immediately. ODM Acting Secretary General Catherine Omanyo had issued a statement confirming the NEC's decision regarding Sifuna's conduct. A day after the NEC resolution, the Political Parties Disputes Tribunal had issued an order suspending the gazettement of Sifuna's ouster, pending a full hearing and determination of the matter.

### Ahead of Special Delegates Convention

The tribunal's ruling comes just hours before the scheduled ODM Special Delegates Convention, set for Friday, March 27, at the Jamhuri grounds in Nairobi. The convention is expected to address various party matters, including the potential reinstatement of Oburu Odinga as party leader, and Sifuna's ouster was also slated for discussion. However, the Linda Mwananchi faction of the party has announced its intention to boycott the official convention, opting instead to hold a parallel National Delegates Convention on Friday. As political parties navigate internal rifts and leadership challenges, the broader landscape of governance also continues to present significant challenges, including persistent issues like illicit trade and smuggling operations that impact economic stability.
