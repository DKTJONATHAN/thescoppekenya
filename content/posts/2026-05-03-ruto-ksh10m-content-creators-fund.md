## Ruto Ksh10M Offer Targets Content Creators at Kalasha Awards

President William Ruto, on Saturday, May 2, announced a Ksh10 million offer to Kenyan content creators at the 14th Kalasha International Film and TV Market Festival and Awards held at the Kenyatta International Convention Centre. This initiative aims to bolster the creative economy by funding compelling stories centered on national priorities like affordable housing and health.

### Funding for National Narratives

The President specifically committed Ksh10 million each for content produced around affordable housing and health, areas he highlighted as personally significant. He directed the Kenya Film Commission (KFC) to incorporate these categories into their awards. The Kalasha Awards, established in 2009 by the Kenya Film Commission, recognize excellence in the local film and television industry. This year, the event was historic as it marked the first time a Kenyan Head of State attended the ceremony.

### Broader Government Support for Creatives

Beyond the Ruto Ksh10M content creators fund, the President unveiled several other measures to strengthen the creative industry. He announced that 30 percent of all government advertising will now be channeled through creative platforms. This move is intended to boost investment, support young talent in digital creation, film, and music, and ensure the government actively partners with creatives who tell Kenyan stories.

Additionally, Ruto urged Parliament to fast-track the Creative Economy Bill 2026 to enhance copyright protections and ensure adequate funding and safeguarding for creatives. He also directed the National Treasury to explore tax incentives to attract international film producers to shoot content in Kenya. Further, future affordable housing projects will incorporate creative spaces, including fully equipped audio-visual studios. These efforts underscore a strategic push to position Kenya as a significant hub for creative arts, reflecting broader economic development goals, as seen in initiatives like the launch of a national MSME readiness index.

### International Engagements

In a move to strengthen cultural and creative ties between Kenya and the United States, Panos Panay, President of the Recording Academy, which hosts the Grammy Awards, is scheduled to visit Nairobi from May 26 to May 29. The visit, supported by the U.S. Department of State through its Arts Envoy Program, will involve high-level talks with President William Ruto to explore collaboration opportunities.
