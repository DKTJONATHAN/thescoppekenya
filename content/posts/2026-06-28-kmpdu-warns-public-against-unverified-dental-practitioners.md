## How to Check if Your Dentist is Actually Qualified KMPDU warns public against dental care from unverified graduates is the central subject of the update.

The Kenya Medical Practitioners, Pharmacists and Dentists Union has flagged concerns regarding dental services provided by graduates from unverified training programs. With court proceedings currently underway to settle accreditation disputes, patients are urged to confirm their practitioner's credentials before undergoing treatment.

For many Kenyans, visiting the dentist is already an anxious affair. That anxiety is now compounded by a public health advisory from the KMPDU, which warns that certain dental graduates may not have met the rigorous training standards required for safe patient care. While the Milimani Law Court recently declined to issue urgent orders regarding a petition filed by the Consumers Federation of Kenya, the underlying question of who is qualified to treat you remains.

### What is the difference between a degree and a license?

It is common to assume that any individual holding a degree from a university is automatically authorized to perform medical procedures. However, in Kenya, completing a university curriculum is only the first step. A degree is an academic achievement, but a professional license is a legal permission slip to practice medicine.

The dentistry education dispute hinges on whether specific programs meet the curriculum and clinical hours mandated by the Kenya Medical Practitioners and Dentists Council. Even if a university produces graduates, those individuals cannot legally practice or be registered by the council if their training program has not been formally accredited. Without a valid license, a practitioner lacks the oversight, liability coverage, and standardized training protocols that protect you in the chair. The union argues that bypassing these standards poses significant risks, ranging from improper diagnostic techniques to dangerous complications during invasive procedures.

### How to verify your dentist in three simple steps

You do not need to be a legal expert to check if your dentist is legitimate. The KMPDC maintains a centralized digital database that allows any patient to confirm the status of their healthcare provider.

1. Visit the official Kenya Medical Practitioners and Dentists Council website.
2. Navigate to the Practitioner Registration section or the online portal dedicated to license verification.
3. Enter the full name of your dentist as it appears on their practice certificate.

If the practitioner is licensed, their status, registration number, and the scope of their allowed practice will appear. If you search for a name and find no matching record, you should avoid seeking dental services from that individual and report the concern to the council. This digital check takes less than five minutes but serves as a vital safeguard for your health. ### Why does this accreditation debate matter now?

The recent court activity highlights a standoff between training institutions and regulatory bodies, but the impact falls entirely on the patient. Accreditation is not just a bureaucratic hurdle; it represents a guarantee that the person holding the tools in your mouth has passed the necessary clinical examinations to handle medical emergencies.

When programs operate outside of these standardized checks, the quality of care becomes unpredictable. The KMPDU warning serves as a preventative measure to ensure that public trust in the healthcare system is not undermined by questionable credentials. Until the court processes conclude and full regulatory clarity is reached, verify before you sit down. Your smile is worth the extra few minutes of due diligence.

Always verify your dental provider through the official council portal before accepting any form of treatment.
