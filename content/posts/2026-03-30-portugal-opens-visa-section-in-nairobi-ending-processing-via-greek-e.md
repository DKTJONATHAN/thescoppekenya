Portugal's Embassy in Nairobi officially inaugurated its dedicated visa section on March 5, 2026, centralizing Schengen visa processing. This significant development concludes the long-standing arrangement where visa applications for Portugal were managed through the Greek Embassy, promising a more streamlined experience for travellers.

Direct Visa Processing Commences

End of Third-Party Agreements The previous system, which saw the Greek Embassy in Nairobi process Portuguese visa applications under a representation agreement, ceased on March 1, 2026. This pivotal change was confirmed by the Kenya High Commission in Maputo, which notified the public about the new operational procedures. Prior to this, applicants occasionally utilized French consular services or VFS France centers, provided Portugal was declared their primary destination for Schengen visa applications. The new system mandates that all applicants now direct their requests to the Portuguese Embassy in Nairobi, ending reliance on third-party embassies.

Streamlined Application Requirements Applicants for a Schengen visa through the Portuguese Embassy are required to submit a comprehensive set of documents. These include completed application forms, a valid passport, recent passport photos, and comprehensive travel insurance. Proof of financial stability, such as a six-month bank statement demonstrating the capacity to fund the trip, is also essential. Furthermore, applicants must provide confirmed flight bookings, evidence of accommodation or sponsorship, and ensure their passports remain valid for at least three months beyond their intended travel dates. This centralized approach aims to simplify procedures by consolidating services.
