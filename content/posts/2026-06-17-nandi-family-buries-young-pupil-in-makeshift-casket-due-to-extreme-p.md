The community of Kapkures in Nandi County is mourning the tragic passing of Ednah Jepkemboi, a PP2 pupil at Kapkures Comprehensive School. Due to extreme financial hardship, the young girl’s family was forced to bury her in a makeshift wooden box this week.

## A somber reflection on rural poverty

### A hollow dignity in Nandi
The sight of the unpolished wooden box served as a grim reminder that basic rites of passage remain out of reach for some families. While economic progress is often discussed in national forums, the reality for this Nandi household was a desperate struggle to provide their daughter with a final resting place. This incident highlights the widening gap between state rhetoric and the harsh circumstances faced by those living in deep poverty.

### The silence of the safety net
There is a jarring contrast between official government pronouncements and the cold reality of life in rural villages. While the country shifts its focus toward specialized medical services or high-level infrastructure projects, the fundamental welfare of children like Ednah remains precarious. Institutional support failed to reach this family, leaving them to navigate the trauma of their loss and the financial burden of a funeral in total isolation.

### Seeking empathy in the shadow of neglect
Community members watched in silence as the makeshift casket was lowered, witnessing a display of both parental love and absolute desperation. Many residents are now questioning the absence of a social safety net when families need support for basic emergency needs. This tragedy serves as a sharp reminder that while elite political and sporting interests often dominate public headlines, the genuine crises of the common citizen continue to unfold in the quiet, overlooked corners of the country. The community is now calling for a renewed focus on empathy and collective responsibility to ensure that vulnerable families are not left to suffer alone in their most difficult hours.
