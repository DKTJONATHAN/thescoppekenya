National Assembly Speaker Moses Wetang’ula announced Saturday his intent to intervene in the teachers' Social Health Authority (SHA) medical scheme dispute. Speaking from his Kanduyi home, Wetang’ula confirmed he will meet Health Cabinet Secretary Aden Duale next week to seek a truce, aiming to avert a national education crisis amidst growing dissatisfaction.

## Addressing Teachers' Healthcare Concerns

Speaker Wetang’ula's decision to mediate follows persistent complaints from teachers regarding the SHA medical scheme, despite recent government assurances. He disclosed his plans during a meeting with teachers from Bungoma County, acknowledging the legitimacy of concerns, particularly those surrounding access to quality healthcare.

### Grassroots Rejection of Agreement

The intervention comes days after the Ministry of Health and teachers’ unions, including the Kenya Union of Post-Primary Education Teachers (KUPPET) and Kenya National Union of Teachers (KNUT), announced a consensus on issues such as co-payments and facility access. However, a segment of union officials at the grassroots level has rejected this agreement, warning that unresolved concerns could still lead to disruptions in learning nationwide.

### Widespread Grievances Highlighted

Wetang’ula recognized that the challenges presented are serious and require immediate government action. He confirmed that these complaints are not isolated to Bungoma, citing similar grievances from teachers in Kuria, Narok, and Ndhiwa. The Speaker emphasized that these widespread issues are limiting teachers from fully benefiting from the new scheme, though he welcomed the transition to SHA, insisting on its effective operation for all beneficiaries. ### Appeal for Patience Amidst Frustration

Teachers, through their leaders, have voiced frustration with the scheme, contending that it has complicated healthcare access despite government pledges of reform. Augustine Luketero, Bungoma KUPPET Executive Secretary, stated that teachers continue to face high out-of-pocket costs and reduced benefits compared to previous medical schemes, even after the announcement scrapping co-payments. Wetang’ula appealed for patience among teachers, asserting that he has personally undertaken the matter to find a lasting solution and prevent a nationwide strike. This intervention follows a high-level meeting on Thursday in Mombasa involving the Teachers Service Commission (TSC), SHA, and union leaders, which resulted in key changes, including the scrapping of co-payments and expansion of medical cover, temporarily halting a planned strike.
