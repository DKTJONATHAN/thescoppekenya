## Kenya Passport Issuance Drops to New Low
Kenya's government recorded a sharp 36.7 percent drop in passport issuance for 2025, reaching a four-year low of 393,567. This decline, attributed to the removal of affordable booklet options and broader economic pressures, impacts citizens seeking travel documents amidst rising costs in the country.

### Sharp Decline in Travel Document Issuance
According to a report by the Kenya National Bureau of Statistics, passport issuance decreased significantly from 621,794 in 2024 to 393,567 in 2025. This 36.7 percent reduction represents the steepest decline observed over the past four years. The downturn is linked to weaker demand, increased application costs, and general economic pressures affecting Kenyan households. While reforms to streamline processing times to 72 hours for certain applicants have been announced, the overall fall in numbers highlights persistent challenges in securing the travel document.

### Discontinuation of Affordable Passport Options
Public sentiment largely attributes the drop in application numbers to the government's phased removal of lower-cost passport booklets. In July 2025, the 32-page passport booklet, previously the most affordable option at Ksh7,500, was discontinued for new applicants due to operational and supply constraints, without a clear long-term replacement strategy. This left applicants with two choices: the 50-page booklet at Ksh9,500 and the 66-page version costing Ksh12,500.

Further exacerbating affordability concerns, the 50-page booklet was quietly withdrawn from the eCitizen platform for new applicants in February 2026. This move has sparked renewed apprehension regarding the cost and accessibility of travel documents. The increased financial burden of acquiring a passport has directly contributed to the recorded decline in applications. ### Kenya's Passport Strength Improves Globally
Despite the local setbacks in Kenya passport issuance and accessibility, the nation's passport strength has seen an improvement on the global stage. The latest Henley & Partners report, published in March 2026, shows Kenya climbing from position 69 to 64 worldwide. Regionally, Kenya now ranks 10th in Africa, behind countries such as South Africa, Morocco, Seychelles, Botswana, Namibia, Lesotho, Malawi, Eswatini, and Mauritius. Concurrently, Kenya also reported strong growth in air travel, with a significant rise in passenger numbers throughout 2025.
