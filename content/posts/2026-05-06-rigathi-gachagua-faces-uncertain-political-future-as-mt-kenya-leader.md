Following his formal removal from office in 2026, former Deputy President Rigathi Gachagua is navigating a precarious political landscape. As he explores potential paths forward, regional leaders in Mt. Kenya remain sharply divided over whether he remains a viable power broker or a political liability.

## The struggle for influence in Mt. Kenya

### The debate over regional alignment
The political climate in Mt. Kenya is currently defined by intense speculation regarding Gachagua’s next steps. Loyalists argue that he remains a necessary figurehead for regional unity, while skeptics contend that his association with past administrations and his recent ouster have rendered him a political burden. This friction is intensified by concerns that clinging to a former official may distance the region from the current administration, potentially jeopardizing future development projects and national economic interests.

### Challenging the culture of political gatekeeping
A segment of political commentators has adopted an increasingly rigid posture, acting as self-appointed guardians of Gachagua’s reputation. These individuals frequently dismiss any neutral analysis or moderate criticism as a betrayal of regional interests. By branding dissenters as sellouts or paid detractors, these voices have fostered an environment where sober, strategic debate is discouraged. This exclusionary tactic often alienates potential allies and transforms legitimate political questions into shallow conflicts based on personal loyalty rather than regional policy.

### Evaluating the reality of political influence
The public discourse surrounding Gachagua’s ability to disrupt upcoming elections is frequently exaggerated by both his supporters and his adversaries. While his base portrays him as an untouchable force, his opponents appear equally invested in maintaining a narrative of his continued threat level. In reality, this manufactured tension suggests a disconnect between the high-stakes rhetoric found in political circles and the actual influence Gachagua currently wields. As the national political cycle progresses, the sustained obsession with his relevance may ultimately prove to be an artificial distraction from more substantive governance issues.
