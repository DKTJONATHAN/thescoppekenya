## ODM Leadership Crisis Shifts to High Court

Nairobi Senator Edwin Sifuna has filed a formal petition in the High Court to contest his second removal as Secretary-General by the ODM National Executive Committee, triggering a legal battle that threatens to paralyze party operations during a volatile political window.

The legal challenge, lodged early Tuesday, alleges that the NEC bypassed constitutional mandates and established internal party dispute resolution mechanisms during the ouster proceedings. Sifuna and his legal team contend that the session held at Chungwa House lacked the necessary quorum and procedural transparency required to strip an elected official of his executive mandate.

This power struggle coincides with a tactical pivot by the Linda Mwananchi faction, which has delayed its upcoming political rallies in Kisii and Keroka. The events originally scheduled for June 26 are now pushed to July 3 to avoid direct scheduling conflicts with the anniversary commemorations of the Gen Z protests.

The delay reflects a calculated effort by the faction to prioritize the optics of the protest anniversary over internal party friction. By rescheduling, the group seeks to avoid being seen as a distraction while maintaining its leverage in the broader discourse surrounding police conduct and government accountability.

### Legal Grounds and Procedural Friction

Court documents indicate that the petitioner is seeking an injunction to freeze the appointment of any replacement until the merits of his removal can be heard. Sifuna claims the party leadership ignored the Article 47 requirements of the Constitution, which mandate fair administrative action in all institutional decisions.

Observers suggest that the move is designed to force the party leadership into a public disclosure of their internal voting records. By moving the dispute into the judicial sphere, Sifuna effectively ties the hands of the NEC, preventing them from solidifying their new leadership structure while the case remains sub judice.

The Linda Mwananchi faction has expressed significant apprehension regarding the security climate during the anniversary of the protests. Faction leaders, including Caroli Omondi, have cited the ongoing directives from the Ministry of Interior to heighten police alertness as a primary reason for the schedule change.

Concerns remain that the state may utilize the heightened security presence to clamp down on political gatherings that mirror the protest sentiments of last year. By postponing their outreach, the faction claims they are preventing their supporters from being caught in the crossfire of state-sanctioned policing measures.

### Strategic Distraction or Internal Collapse

The timing of the legal challenge suggests that the party is fracturing along ideological lines rather than mere personality clashes. While the ODM leadership insists that the changes at the secretariat are necessary for administrative reform, the Linda Mwananchi group labels the move a purge aimed at silencing dissent against the party's current trajectory.

Political analysts monitoring the situation point out that the struggle for control of the Secretary-General’s office is essentially a fight for the party's signature authority. Without control over the secretariat, the faction risks losing its ability to organize, communicate with branches, and influence the national political narrative ahead of the next electoral cycle.

The intensity of the dispute has diverted focus from recent parliamentary debates on budget allocations and the broader economic reforms that the party once championed. As the ODM leadership navigates this internal crisis, the vacuum in party management creates an opening for other political entities to consolidate their influence within Nairobi and the wider Nyanza region.

While the high-level legal battle ensues, the next 48 hours will be critical for determining whether the faction chooses to escalate its protest-related messaging or focus exclusively on the court proceedings. The judiciary's response to the petition for an interim stay will determine the legitimacy of the recent appointments and set the tone for the party's upcoming national delegates conference.
