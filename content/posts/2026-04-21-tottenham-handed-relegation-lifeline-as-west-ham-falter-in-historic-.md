The English Premier League’s relegation dogfight intensifies, with Tottenham Hotspur facing a historic battle to avoid their first top-flight relegation since 1977. As West Ham United falters and Leeds United and Nottingham Forest gain crucial momentum, the dramatic race for survival promises a thrilling conclusion.

Spurs in Peril: A Historic Struggle Tottenham Hotspur finds itself in a perilous position, only two points from safety after a challenging season. Their recent late equalizer conceded against Brighton on Saturday, April 18, 2026, highlighted their struggles. The club has not secured a top-flight victory in 2026, managing only two league wins since October 26, 2025. This extends their winless league run to 15 matches. On Saturday, April 25, 2026, they must defeat already relegated Wolves to avoid equaling their worst-ever winless streak, last set between 1934 and 1935. Roberto de Zerbi, the Tottenham boss, remains confident his side can win five consecutive games to secure survival, though the season's evidence offers little support for such a turnaround. Since beating Crystal Palace on December 28, 2025, Spurs have collected a mere six points from 15 league encounters. The club's current predicament marks their first time in the relegation zone after 33 league games in 49 years, a situation that ended in relegation during the 1976-77 season.

De Zerbi's Optimism vs. Reality Tottenham's home form further compounds their worries, with only Championship side Sheffield Wednesday having a worse record in the English Football League this term. Their current winless streak to start a calendar year is among the worst in Premier League history; only Derby in 2007-08 (18) and Sunderland in 2002-03 (17) recorded longer runs, both resulting in relegation. Club legends and football analysts, including former striker Dean Ashton and ex-defender Jamie Carragher, have voiced strong concerns, with many predicting Tottenham's eventual drop to the Championship. Fans echo this sentiment, pointing to a perceived lack of fight within the Spurs squad compared to their relegation rivals.

Rivals Find Form: West Ham, Leeds, and Forest While Tottenham falters, their relegation adversaries have found crucial momentum. West Ham United's draw at Crystal Palace on Monday, April 20, 2026, provided Tottenham with a lifeline, yet the Hammers have shown resilience with two wins in their last five matches, accumulating 19 points from their past 12 games. West Ham manager Nuno Espirito Santo believes the battle will extend to the final day. Leeds United and Nottingham Forest were the biggest beneficiaries of the recent fixtures, securing significant home victories. Leeds, led by Daniel Farke, has achieved back-to-back wins, moving them eight points clear of the drop zone and potentially out of the relegation conversation. Nottingham Forest, with two wins in their last three games and an unbeaten run of five, is five points clear, collecting 18 points from their past 13 matches.

The Hammers' Difficult Road Ahead West Ham faces a daunting fixture list, starting with Everton at home on Saturday, April 25, 2026, followed by an away trip to Brentford. A critical home clash against title-chasing Arsenal on May 10, 2026, looms large. Their penultimate weekend fixture involves an away game at Newcastle, before concluding the season at home against Leeds. Despite their recent form, West Ham holds the unwelcome record for the most points garnered by a relegated side, having amassed 42 points in 2002-03, which was still insufficient for survival.

Forest's European Distraction Nottingham Forest's path to safety is complicated by their Europa League semi-final commitments. Their final five league matches are arguably the toughest, featuring away games at Chelsea and Manchester United in May. The match against Chelsea comes just three days before their Europa League semi-final second leg against Aston Villa. Home games against Newcastle and Bournemouth on the final day could be decisive, but a result at Sunderland on Friday, April 24, 2026, would significantly alleviate pressure. Jamie Carragher has suggested that Forest should prioritize securing points at Sunderland to reach 39 points, which would likely ensure their safety.

The Final Stretch: Crunch Fixtures With five games remaining, Tottenham's run-in, on paper, offers a fighting chance. After facing Wolves this Saturday, they host Leeds on May 11, 2026. A potentially tricky match against Champions League-chasing Aston Villa on May 3, 2026, falls conveniently between Villa's two Europa League semi-final legs. However, away matches against rivals Chelsea and a final day home game against Everton, with both teams likely vying for European spots, present considerable hurdles.

Expert Verdicts and Fan Frustrations The consensus among analysts and supporters suggests that 40 points might be the benchmark for safety this year. While 36 points have been enough to survive in every Premier League season since 2015-16, and only six teams have been relegated with 39 or more points in a 38-game season in the past 14 years, this season's intensity may demand more. The tight margins, particularly between London rivals West Ham and Tottenham, indicate the relegation battle will undoubtedly go down to the wire. Pundits and fans increasingly point to Tottenham's lack of momentum and character as a critical factor in their potential demise, predicting their relegation alongside Burnley and Wolves.
