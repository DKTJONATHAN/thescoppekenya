By Celestine Nzioka

The High Court on Thursday issued an order suspending National Assembly Speaker Moses Wetangula’s decision to remove Kitutu South MP Anthony Kibagendi from Parliament. Justice Bahati Mwamuye's ruling at Milimani Law Courts in Nairobi granted a conservatory order, lifting the suspension indefinitely until full determination.

## Parliamentary Suspension Lifted

Justice Mwamuye's ruling directed that the suspension of MP Kibagendi be lifted immediately. The court emphasized that the principle of separation of powers does not prevent the judiciary from intervening in parliamentary matters. This decision effectively halts the Speaker’s directive.

### Genesis of Kibagendi's Expulsion

Speaker Wetangula had expelled MP Kibagendi from Parliament on February 17, 2026, citing "reckless remarks" made by the legislator. During the suspension, Wetangula deemed Kibagendi out of order for alleging that Parliament had been auctioned and that the Speaker and Deputy Speakers had compromised the institution. Kibagendi was excluded from all parliamentary proceedings, committee sittings, and his office until he presented a properly worded apology to the house.

### Contentious Remarks on Citizen TV

The Speaker’s action followed remarks made by MP Kibagendi during an interview on Citizen TV on February 17, 2026. During the broadcast, the legislator claimed that Speaker Wetang'ula, his deputy Gladys Shollei, and MP Duncan Mathenge had transformed Parliament into an appendage of State House. He further alleged that Parliament had been auctioned to the executive. The controversial nature of these statements, often made by public figures during televised discussions, frequently sparks public interest, similar to other high-profile personalities whose actions garner significant media attention. Later that day, Members of Parliament raised concerns during proceedings, prompting Speaker Wetang'ula to eject him.

### Prior Legal Encounters

In January 2026, MP Kibagendi faced a separate legal issue when he was arrested by detectives from the Directorate of Criminal Investigations (DCI) over an alleged assault. He was subsequently released by the Narok High Court on a cash bail of Ksh100,000 after denying the charges.
