The Kenya Rural Roads Authority (KeRRA) has issued a stern warning against fake job interview letters circulating online, urging the public to disregard fraudulent recruitment solicitations. On Thursday, March 26, 2026, KeRRA clarified that these letters, which demand upfront payments, are part of an ongoing scam and do not originate from the authority.

Kenya Rural Roads Authority Scrutiny KeRRA notified the public about a surge of deceptive letters claiming to have shortlisted applicants for interviews. These fraudulent communications falsely indicate that applicants have been selected and require payment in advance to proceed. The authority unequivocally stated that these letters are a scam, emphasizing that no payments should be made to individuals or entities facilitating interviews or recruitment under KeRRA's name.

Fraudulent Communications Identified The circulating fake letters mimic official KeRRA communication patterns, inviting applicants for positions such as engineering technician and requesting payment before an interview can occur. A notable detail in the scam letters is the inclusion of a purported signature of KeRRA's director general without the official's name, confirming their fraudulent nature. KeRRA has reported the matter to the relevant authorities for investigation.

Official Recruitment Protocol The authority firmly stated that it does not require any form of payment at any stage of its recruitment process, including during interview invitations. KeRRA reinforced that its official communications are exclusively issued through its formal channels, specifically its website and verified social media pages. Job seekers are advised to verify all recruitment information through these official platforms and report any suspicious activity. This incident underscores broader government efforts to enhance accountability in public sectors, with initiatives such as the planned rollout of police body cameras to improve oversight and transparency. KeRRA's warning is timely, given the increasing reports of employment-related scams across the nation, where job seekers are frequently targeted through deceptive offer letters and online platforms. The Kenya Urban Roads Authority (KURA) recently issued a similar caution, alerting the public to fraudsters posing as KURA officials and offering false job opportunities.
