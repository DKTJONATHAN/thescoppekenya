A forty-one-year-old Kenyan man identified as Joseph Kabugi has been captured by Ukrainian forces after being recruited to fight for the Russian army. Kabugi claims he was misled into combat roles after seeking technical employment to escape severe financial hardship.

## Recruitment Deception in the Russia-Ukraine Conflict

### Misleading Job Offers
Kabugi, a computer science engineer, left Kenya after an agency promised him a well-paying technical role in Russia. He signed a contract under the impression that he would manage communications or surveillance systems. This situation highlights the desperation many professionals face, particularly as major institutions struggle, such as when [Moi University faces potential auction after court order freezes bank accounts](https://zandani.co.ke/article/moi-university-faces-potential-auction-after-court-order-freezes-ban).

### Deployment and Combat Realities
After signing his contract, Kabugi was forced into three weeks of military training despite having no prior experience. He was deployed near Kupyansk and survived two drone attacks before his unit ran out of supplies. Language barriers and a lack of preparation left him isolated on the battlefield. He noted that he was not prepared to fight a war after such a brief period of training.

### Capture and Warning to Kenyans
Ukrainian forces from the 425th Separate Assault Regiment captured Kabugi while he was searching for food. Now in custody, he has issued a plea to other Kenyans to stay away from the conflict. Za Ndani has established that Kabugi’s capture comes as Defence Intelligence of Ukraine reports that several other Kenyan nationals have already been killed on the frontline while fighting for Russian forces.
