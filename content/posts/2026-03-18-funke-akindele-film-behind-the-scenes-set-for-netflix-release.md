Funke Akindele's blockbuster film 'Behind The Scenes' will premiere on Netflix Naija on April 3rd, 2026, offering audiences across Africa and the diaspora a chance to stream the record-breaking movie. This highly anticipated release follows its phenomenal success in West African cinemas, where it shattered box office records.

'Behind The Scenes' Arrives on Netflix The announcement from FilmOne Entertainment on March 17th confirms 'Behind The Scenes' will be available for streaming from April 3rd, 2026, providing an opportunity for all who missed its theatrical run to finally experience the film. This Netflix Naija landing marks a remarkable continuation for Akindele, who has solidified her position as a dominant force in Nigerian cinema. The streaming release will broaden the film's reach beyond its already impressive theatrical run, making it accessible to subscribers across the continent and in the diaspora.

Record-Breaking Box Office Success 'Behind The Scenes' opened nationwide on December 12th, 2025, to strong attendance across major cinema chains in Lagos, Abuja, and Port Harcourt. It recorded one of the highest opening weekends of the year and maintained its momentum through the high-attendance Christmas and Boxing Day period. The film rapidly became the fastest Nollywood movie to reach the N500 million mark, subsequently crossing the N1 billion milestone at the West African box office within just 19 days of release, setting a new record as the fastest Nollywood film to achieve this feat. Ultimately, the movie grossed over ₦2 billion during its cinema run, making it the highest-grossing Nigerian movie of all time.

Exploring Thematic Depth At its core, 'Behind The Scenes' is a compelling story centered on personal boundaries, emotional labour, and the unique cost of success for women in leadership. The narrative follows Aderonke "Ronky-Fella" Faniran, a successful real estate mogul and mother of two, admired for her generosity. However, as increasing demands from family, friends, and associates deplete her time, money, and emotional energy, Aderonke finds herself giving until there is nothing left. These resonant themes clearly captivated Nigerian audiences during its significant theatrical run.

Creative Visionaries Behind the Film Funke Akindele produced 'Behind The Scenes' alongside Wendy Uwadiae Imasuen. Akindele also co-directed the film with Tunde Olaoye and served as its primary writer, supported by a dedicated team of screenwriters and creative consultants. This multi-faceted involvement underscores Akindele's comprehensive vision and commitment to the project's success.
