Kenya Power's scheduled maintenance on Tuesday, April 14, 2026, brings temporary blackouts to parts of Nairobi, Mombasa, and Elgeyo Marakwet. With upscale Nyari and bustling Githurai among affected areas, many are buzzing about how Kenyan celebrities will manage without power for essential system upgrades.

Nairobi's Star-Studded Neighbourhoods Brace for Power Interruption Kenya Power announced that various Nairobi locales will experience power interruptions for several hours on Tuesday, April 14, 2026. Areas like Githurai are set for an outage from 9:00 a.m. to 5:00 p.m., impacting Maziwa, Maziwa Farmers, Co-op Bank, and Kahawa West Kamae, along with their surrounding customers.

Nyari's Unexpected Digital Detox Meanwhile, the same hours will see parts of Nyari without electricity. Affected locations include the Stabex Petrol Station, sections of Gachie Market, Redhill Baraka School, Muchugia Estate, Bishops Estate, Nyari West, and areas extending along Ngecha Road. This interruption raises questions about how the area's prominent residents will handle a day without their usual amenities. Perhaps it's an opportunity for a forced digital detox, trading social media scrolls for candle-lit conversations or perhaps a fresh read.

Githurai's Energy Pause The vibrant hub of Githurai will also experience an eight-hour pause in its usual electric rhythm. As technicians conduct system upgrades, residents and businesses in Maziwa and Kahawa West Kamae will navigate the day without power. It remains to be seen how the lively commercial activities in these areas will adapt to the temporary blackout.

Mombasa's Coastal Interlude Down at the coast, Mombasa County's Likoni area will also face power interruptions from 9:00 a.m. to 5:00 p.m. this Tuesday. Diani Beach Hospital, Mrima Primary School, Tenant Purchase, Samaki Poa, Ngumungumu, and Kibaki Estate are among the numerous locations listed. Others include Mt. Sinai, Ujamaa, Pungu, Vyemani, Shikaadabu, Taifa Gas, Inspiration, Vijiweni, Consolata, Corner Mpya, Bububu, Soweto, Likoni Secondary School, Kiwerera, Mbuta, and Third World. This planned outage might prompt celebrities vacationing along the pristine coastline to adjust their seaside plans. One can only imagine the scramble for alternative power sources, especially for those planning elaborate events. Could a temporary blackout even delay a planned grand gesture, much like the recent buzz around Kanyari's surprising promise to Marion Naipei regarding a car?

Elgeyo Marakwet's Quiet Afternoon Elgeyo Marakwet County will also see its share of maintenance, affecting the Katalel Centre area from 11:00 a.m. to 3:00 p.m. Locations expected to be impacted include Kipsoen Polytechnic, Salaba Primary School, Kiplus Centre, Boundary Centre, and Silvan Secondary School. While perhaps less celebrity-dense than Nairobi or Mombasa, the rural calm of Elgeyo Marakwet offers its own unique backdrop for a few hours of quiet reflection for any prominent figures seeking respite from city life. Kenya Power has urged all affected customers to make necessary preparations and to consider all electrical installations as live throughout the maintenance period, emphasizing that these interruptions are crucial for enhancing electricity supply reliability.
