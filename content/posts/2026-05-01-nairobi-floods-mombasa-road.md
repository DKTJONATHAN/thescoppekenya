## Nairobi Floods Paralysed Mombasa Road and JKIA

Heavy overnight rains on Saturday, May 1, crippled Nairobi, rendering major transport corridors impassable and stranding motorists for hours. Mombasa Road, the Easter Bypass, and areas near Jomo Kenyatta International Airport (JKIA) experienced severe Nairobi floods, causing widespread disruption and concerns over rising river levels. The National Police Service had previously warned residents to remain vigilant due to these conditions.

### Key Transport Routes Affected

Flooding between Mlolongo and Cabanas on Mombasa Road brought traffic to a complete standstill early Saturday morning. Witnesses reported a traffic jam stretching approximately 3km from Ole Sereni to The Standard Media Group area. Videos circulating online showed stalled trucks and vehicles, with significant flooding also reported around JKIA. Motorists indicated being stuck for hours following the overnight downpour. The Easter Bypass was completely blocked, prompting authorities to urge motorists to use the Nairobi Expressway as an alternative. Traffic extended from Nairobi CBD all the way to Athi River.

### Impact Across the Capital

Serious flooding was also reported along Jogoo Road at the Makadara section and the Church Army roundabout. Downtown Nairobi faced chaos, with Landhies Road, connecting the OTC stage and Kamukunji area, submerged. The Muthurwa Footbridge reportedly overflowed, sending water onto the road. The Nairobi Commuter train service from Syokimau to Nairobi was unable to proceed past Imara Daima Station due to overwhelming floods on the railway line. Along Lang’ata Road at the T-Mall area, a known flooding hotspot, the road was impassable early Saturday morning, though waters receded before 7 am. At least two vehicles reportedly fell into a ditch near the Shell Petrol Station, with one truck fully submerged in the drainage system, necessitating police response.

### Humanitarian Response and River Alerts

The Kenya Red Cross confirmed the widespread chaos resulting from the overnight rains. Its Nairobi team is responding to calls from Mukuru kwa Njenga, where unconfirmed reports indicate missing persons and marooned households. The full extent of damage from the overnight rains remains unclear. The National Police Service had issued a warning on April 29, urging Nairobi residents to remain on high alert, particularly those living near rivers. The advisory highlighted rapidly rising water levels in major rivers, including the Nairobi, Mathare, Ngong, Kamiti, and Athi rivers, all of which drain into the larger Athi River system, which is currently at critically high levels. Kenyans.co.ke is currently assessing the impact on flood-prone areas in and around Nairobi, such as Lang’ata, Ngara, Madaraka, Eastleigh, Kawangware, Kibera, South C, Kasarani, Mathare, Mlolongo, and Syokimau.
