Diaspora remittances to the country surged to Ksh58.15 billion (USD 450.3 million) in March 2026, marking the highest monthly inflow recorded this year. Fresh data from the Central Bank of Kenya reveals a 9.1 percent increase from February, underscoring the vital role of foreign exchange earnings amidst declining national reserves.

Diaspora Inflows Bolster Foreign Exchange

Consistent Inflow Growth The March 2026 figures represent a significant rebound, following a slower start to the year. Inflows stood at Ksh52.65 billion (USD 407.8 million) in January 2026, rising to Ksh53.31 billion (USD 412.7 million) in February, despite the onset of the U.S./Israel war on Iran on February 28. This sustained growth has pushed the nation's 12-month cumulative remittance inflows to Ksh655.9 billion (USD 5,079 million) for the period ending March 2026, a 2.2 percent rise compared to Ksh642.1 billion (USD 4,972 million) recorded in the corresponding period in 2025. This consistent performance highlights remittances as a critical component of the country's foreign exchange earnings.

Bolstering National Reserves The Central Bank of Kenya, in its weekly bulletin dated April 17, reiterated that remittance inflows are essential for supporting the balance of payments, particularly as the country faces pressure on its foreign exchange reserves. As of April 16, these reserves stood at Ksh1.718 trillion (USD 13,306 million), equivalent to 5.6 months of import cover. This marks a decline from Ksh1.813 trillion (USD 14,022 million) on March 26 and Ksh1.845 trillion (USD 14,294 million) on March 18. While still comfortably above the CBK's statutory minimum of four months of import cover, the month-on-month erosion necessitates sustained remittance momentum to stabilize the external position. The Kenyan Shilling maintained broad stability during the week ending April 16, exchanging at Ksh129.18 per U.S. dollar, a marginal strengthening from Ksh129.53 recorded on April 9. The CBK attributes the steady remittances, in part, to the Shilling's strong performance against the U.S. dollar.

Key Corridors and Economic Pressures North America, notably the United States, and Europe continue to serve as the primary corridors for the nation's diaspora remittances. A growing volume also originates from the Gulf states, reflecting an expanding migrant worker population in that region. The Central Bank bulletin, however, did not provide a breakdown of the March figures by specific source corridor. The surge in March remittances arrives at a critical juncture for the domestic economy, concurrent with a significant jump in fuel prices. Petrol is now retailing at Ksh197.60 per litre, with diesel at Ksh196.63 per litre. This is expected to intensify economic pressure and contribute to an anticipated increase in inflation over the coming weeks.
