For Za Ndani by Jona Munyi

Arsenal seized a critical victory against Newcastle, with Eberechi Eze’s exquisite strike moving them three points clear of Manchester City in a gripping Premier League title race. This hard-fought win puts immense pressure back on their rivals, setting the stage for a thrilling conclusion to the 2025/2026 season.

## Title Race Intensifies

Following a disappointing defeat to Manchester City last week, Arsenal captain Declan Rice's viral declaration that "it's not done" has proven prophetic. Mikel Arteta's side, dubbed "a new league" by their manager, delivered under immense pressure, securing a vital 1-0 win over Newcastle at the Emirates Stadium on Saturday, April 25, 2026. This result saw them reclaim the top spot, a position they had ceded for the first time since October after City's midweek victory over Burnley. The relief among players was palpable at the final whistle, marking an end to their recent run of back-to-back defeats.

## Tactical Battle and Key Moments

### Eze's Masterclass
The decisive moment arrived in the ninth minute, when Eberechi Eze unleashed a stunning strike from outside the penalty area, curling past Nick Pope to net the only goal of the match. Since the start of the 2022-23 season, Eze's 10 goals from outside the box stand as a league-high, highlighting his knack for spectacular finishes. Arsenal’s attacking fluidity, however, was not always evident, as their xG of just 0.64 against Newcastle was their second-lowest at home in the Premier League this season. Despite this, the defensive resolve shown in the closing stages proved enough to secure all three points.

### Injury Concerns and Squad Depth
The victory was tinged with concern as both Kai Havertz and goal-scorer Eze were withdrawn due to muscular problems. While Arteta expressed hope for their swift recovery ahead of upcoming fixtures, these injuries prompted early tactical adjustments. Viktor Gyokeres replaced Havertz in the first half, and Myles Lewis-Skelly was introduced for an ill Martin Zubimendi, demonstrating Arteta's reliance on squad depth in this demanding period.

## The Road Ahead

With four league games remaining, Arsenal has a chance to extend their lead to six points next week if they overcome Fulham. Manchester City, currently embroiled in an FA Cup semi-final against Southampton, will not return to league action until May 4, when they face Everton. This potential buffer could be crucial. Opta currently gives Arsenal a 72.44% chance of lifting the Premier League trophy, compared to Manchester City's 27.56%. Former Crystal Palace and Brighton striker Glenn Murray praised Arsenal's unwavering fight on the pitch, a sentiment echoed by captain Martin Odegaard, who acknowledged the "crazy" and intense schedule.

Arsenal's quest for silverware extends beyond the domestic front, with a UEFA Champions League semi-final against Atletico Madrid looming on Wednesday, April 29, 2026. This packed schedule demands every ounce of effort from the squad as they aim to end a major trophy drought that stretches back to the FA Cup triumph of 2020.
