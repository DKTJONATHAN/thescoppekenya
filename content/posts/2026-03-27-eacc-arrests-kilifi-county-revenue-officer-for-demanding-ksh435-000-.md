The Ethics and Anti-Corruption Commission (EACC) arrested a Kilifi County Revenue Collection Officer on March 26, 2026, in Watamu Municipality. The officer was apprehended for allegedly demanding a Ksh435,000 bribe to facilitate construction approvals, highlighting continued efforts against corruption.

## EACC Targets Corruption in Approvals

The arrested individual, a building inspector attached to the approvals section in Watamu Municipality, allegedly solicited bribes for construction clearances. According to the EACC, a complainant reported that county officials visited a construction site in the Timboni area and demanded Ksh73,000 for wall construction. An additional Ksh362,000 was sought for other structures, an amount the EACC stated was deliberately inflated for financial gain and exploitation.

### Details of the Bribe Demand

The EACC received the complaint on March 25, 2026, confirming a bribe demand totaling Ksh400,000. Investigators from the commission acted promptly to prevent further exploitation of citizens seeking legitimate construction approvals. The alleged demand of Ksh435,000 underscores the persistent challenges in public service delivery points.

### The Operation and Arrest

On March 26, 2026, the EACC conducted a targeted operation at the municipal offices, leading to the arrest of the revenue officer suspected of soliciting the bribe. This action is part of broader anti-corruption enforcement measures. The suspect was subsequently taken to the EACC Upper Coast Regional Office in Malindi for processing before being released on a cash bail of Ksh20,000 at the Malindi Police Station.

### Broader Anti-Corruption Efforts

This latest EACC arrest follows other recent enforcement actions, including the apprehension of an impersonator posing as a National Intelligence Service (NIS) officer. That suspect was involved in orchestrating a Ksh15 million fraudulent tender scheme by deceiving a construction firm into undertaking a non-existent project at Kaptis Primary Special School in Hamisi Sub-County, Vihiga County. The impersonator was also released on Ksh20,000 cash bail after being booked at Kisumu Central Police Station. The EACC's ongoing efforts to curb financial exploitation come as public discourse, such as insights from Janet Mbugua, continues to question spending habits amid economic pressures.

The EACC has issued a strong warning to public officers against engaging in corrupt practices and urged the public to remain vigilant against fraudulent traps. The commission reaffirms its commitment to eradicating bribery at service delivery points to enhance access to quality services for all citizens.
