Veteran gynaecologist and Nairobi Hospital director Dr. Job Obwaka died Friday, May 1, 2026, amidst escalating controversy. Police are investigating his sudden demise in Kitengela, with concerns rising about efforts to manipulate the narrative, contradicting details of his troubled final months.

## The Unraveling Truth Behind a Doctor's Demise

The medical community and Kenyans at large are grappling with the untimely passing of Dr. Job Obwaka, who died at 83 years old. While official tributes pour in from various leaders, the real story emerging behind the scenes is one of alleged harassment, a dramatic arrest, and a police investigation now involving a detained individual. This stark contrast fuels suspicions among the public, suggesting an attempt to obscure the full truth behind his controversial end.

### The Troubled Weeks Leading to His End

Dr. Obwaka's final months were anything but peaceful. In a shocking turn of events just six weeks prior to his death, on March 14, 2026, the revered gynaecologist was dramatically arrested by detectives outside his surgery. He was subsequently detained for three nights at Muthaiga Police Station and faced charges of allegedly falsifying hospital records amidst a protracted governance dispute at The Nairobi Hospital. The incident sent shockwaves through the medical community, with many condemning the heavy-handed approach. Adding to the drama, Dr. Obwaka collapsed at the Milimani Law Courts while awaiting arraignment, leading to his admission to Nairobi Hospital’s intensive care unit, where his health reportedly deteriorated over several weeks. Former Law Society of Kenya President Nelson Havi vehemently claimed that government harassment and what he termed "trumped-up charges" directly contributed to the decline of Dr. Obwaka's health and his eventual " untimely death."

### A Sudden Collapse, A Police Probe

The immediate circumstances of Dr. Obwaka's death on May 1, 2026, have intensified the scrutiny. Police reports indicate that he collapsed in Kitengela while on personal errands, in the company of a 45-year-old woman identified as Beatrice Wangare. He was rushed to The Nairobi Hospital but was pronounced dead on arrival at approximately 7:00 PM. In a significant development, Wangare, believed to be a business partner of the deceased, was detained by police on Saturday, May 2, 2026, to assist with ongoing investigations into his "sudden death." While initial findings suggest a history of underlying medical conditions, the exact cause of death remains officially unestablished pending a postmortem examination at Lee Funeral Home. This swift police action underscores the seriousness with which authorities are treating a case now classified as a sudden death under investigation.

### Weaving the Narrative: Tributes and Truth

Following Dr. Obwaka's death, an outpouring of condolence messages flooded public spaces, with prominent figures, including former Chief Justice David Maraga and Fred Matiang'i, eulogizing him as a dedicated professional and a respected medical leader. However, some of these tributes have been met with skepticism. Health Cabinet Secretary Aden Duale's message, for instance, drew sharp criticism from those who recalled his earlier remarks after the doctor’s controversial arrest. Senior Counsel Nelson Havi branded Duale’s condolences as "morbid and cynical," highlighting previous alleged government harassment. This divergence between the official eulogies and the public's awareness of the preceding legal battles and a police investigation suggests an underlying struggle to control the prevailing narrative. The swiftness of official praise, juxtaposed with the suspicious circumstances, paints a vivid picture of powerful forces attempting to steer public perception away from the unsettling realities of Dr. Obwaka's final journey. Many Kenyans are demanding the truth, not a fabricated version of events spun for public consumption.
