**TSC Moves to Map Unemployed Teachers Nationwide as Registry Update Commences** **By Celestine Nzioka** The Teachers Service Commission has officially initiated a nationwide exercise requiring all unemployed registered teachers to update their professional data through the commission's digital portal. This move is designed to create a comprehensive database that will allow the government to assess the current demand and supply of trained educators across the country. According to an official statement released by the commission on Wednesday, the initiative serves as a strategic tool for future recruitment planning and policy decisions. The commission noted that the updated data will be processed and stored under the strict provisions of the Data Protection Act to ensure the privacy of all participating professionals. However, the commission has issued a stern clarification regarding the nature of this exercise. Officials emphasized that the data update is strictly an administrative procedure and does not constitute a formal job advertisement. The commission further warned that participation in the portal update will not serve as a guarantee for immediate employment or placement within the public school system. Teachers who fall under this category are directed to log onto the official TSC website under the careers section to submit their details. The portal requires applicants to provide their identification numbers, TSC registration numbers, and active phone contacts. Particular attention is required for the entry of subject combinations to ensure the records reflect actual teaching qualifications and allow for accurate workforce mapping. The commission has set a deadline of March 16, 2026, for the completion of this exercise. This development coincides with the government’s broader commitment to address the shortage of educators by recruiting 24,000 new staff and transitioning 46,000 intern teachers to permanent and pensionable terms. All qualified teachers are urged to comply with the directive within the two week window to ensure their details are captured in the national database for future strategic use.
