## Filing Taxes Without the Last-Minute Panic KRA Extends Service Hours Ahead of June Tax Return Deadline is the central subject of the update.

With the June 30 deadline looming, taxpayers are feeling the familiar pressure of the annual filing cycle. The Kenya Revenue Authority has officially pivoted its operational schedule, moving away from standard business hours to accommodate the intense surge of activity that typically hits the iTax portal in the final 48 hours. This shift is less about administrative courtesy and more about managing the systemic congestion that often leads to avoidable penalties.

### The Midnight Rush Strategy: Tactical Scheduling

Most Kenyans wait until the final two days to submit their annual returns, a habit that frequently results in server lag and heightened stress. To manage this influx, the authority has extended physical service center hours to 7:00 AM through 7:00 PM on weekdays, effective immediately through June 26. 

If you are planning to visit a physical site, treat this window like a tactical operation. The rush will intensify as we approach the final weekend. On June 29 and 30, the contact centers will remain open until midnight to provide real-time assistance. By shifting your filing window to these off-peak extended hours, you effectively bypass the mid-day congestion that cripples the portal during regular business cycles. Think of these extra hours as a buffer designed to help you avoid the common errors caused by trying to force a submission during high-traffic intervals.

### Navigating Digital Channels Versus Physical Sites

While physical support is available, the iTax system remains the primary mechanism for compliance. Many taxpayers assume that in-person support is the only way to resolve complex filing hurdles, but the current support structure favors digital resolution. If you encounter a login error or a document mismatch, the digital contact channels-including the WhatsApp-based support tools-are your fastest route to rectification.

Physical visits should be reserved for cases where digital verification has failed. If you must head to a Huduma Center, arrive before 9:00 AM. Experience from previous tax seasons shows that afternoon queues often stretch beyond the building capacity, and by 4:00 PM, service staff are heavily focused on closing pending cases rather than initiating new support tickets for walk-ins. Prioritize digital synchronization before committing half a day to a queue that could potentially be avoided with a simple password reset or document upload handled from your home or office.

### Your Final 48-Hour Compliance Checklist

To ensure your return is processed before the midnight cutoff on June 30, execute this sequence:

1. Validate your P45 and other income documents now. Do not wait to see the physical paper printouts on the final day.
2. Clear your digital credentials early. If you have forgotten your password or if your security questions are outdated, reset them before June 27. The system's identity verification process is the single largest cause of last-minute lockouts.
3. Utilize the extended hours on June 29 and 30 for support calls. If the portal hangs while you are uploading, wait ten minutes before refreshing. Rapid-fire refreshing often triggers a temporary security block from the server side.
4. Keep a digital log of your submission acknowledgement. Do not rely on the browser history to prove you filed.

The goal is to move your filing date forward by just 72 hours. Even if you consider yourself a tech-savvy user, the volume of data moving through the tax servers in the final hours can create unpredictable delays. True transparency in your financial reporting is difficult when you are rushed, and as we have explored in reports on [The Price of Transparency](https://zandani.co.ke/article/the-price-of-transparency-broken-claims-fleeting-fame), administrative shortcuts often lead to costly follow-up audits. Aim to be fully compliant by June 28, leaving the final 48 hours for those who genuinely need the extended support services.
