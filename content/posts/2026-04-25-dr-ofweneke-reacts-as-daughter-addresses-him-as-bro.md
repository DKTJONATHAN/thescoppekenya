Kenyan comedian and media personality Dr. Ofweneke recently prompted a lively online discussion after revealing that his Gen Z daughter, Faith Ofweneke, has adopted the casual habit of addressing him as "bro" in their daily conversations. His humorous query on Instagram for parenting advice quickly went viral across social media platforms.

## The 'Bro' Phenomenon in Dr. Ofweneke's Household

Dr. Ofweneke, whose real name is Sande Bush, ignited a wave of amusement and shared experiences among parents this April 2026. He took to Instagram, seeking guidance on whether to "discipline her or let it slide" after his daughter began calling him "brooooo". This lighthearted parenting dilemma resonated widely, turning a personal family moment into a relatable online conversation about modern family dynamics.

### Parents Share Similar Tales

The comedian's post rapidly filled with responses from users across Kenya and beyond, many confessing to similar linguistic quirks in their own homes. Parents recounted instances where their children, irrespective of gender, used terms like "bro," "bruh," or "dude" when speaking to them. Some even shared more unique greetings, including being called "twin" or "baby girl" by their offspring, highlighting how modern slang deeply influences parent-child interactions today. The comment section brimmed with a blend of laughter, surprise, and empathetic nods to the evolving nature of familial communication in a rapidly changing digital landscape.
