## Runway Remix: Stars Who Didn't Make the Final Cut for *The Devil Wears Prada 2*

The highly anticipated sequel, *The Devil Wears Prada 2*, brings back beloved original stars, but not everyone slated for the runway made it to the final cut. From surprising celebrity cameos to pivotal character roles, creative decisions and timing proved to be as cutthroat as Miranda Priestly herself.

### Sydney Sweeney's Dior Moment Derailed

Following her success in last year's *The Housemaid*, actress Sydney Sweeney was a hot topic for the sequel. Speculation about her involvement circulated in 2025, with Sweeney even spotted on set. Her scene, where Emily Charlton dressed her inside Dior offices with Andy, Miranda, and Nigel present, was ultimately cut due to a "creative decision". Despite the controversial *Euphoria* actress playing herself in the three-minute sequence, it was removed for structural reasons, a difficult choice for the creative team, who expressed gratitude for her time.

### Conrad Ricamora's Roommate Role Axed

Conrad Ricamora, known for his role as hacker Oliver Hampton on *How to Get Away With Murder*, was cast as Andy's roommate in *The Devil Wears Prada 2*. However, this character didn't survive test screenings, with audiences questioning the point of his presence. *Variety* reported that his role was cut to prioritize Andy's established friendship with Lily, played by Tracie Thoms. Ricamora acknowledged the decision on Instagram in April 2026, saying his character "didn't make sense in the grand scheme of the film," playfully adding he might have been cut for "being too sexy and hott and my muscles being too big". He harbors no ill feelings, calling his work "one of the best working experiences of my life".

### Anna Wintour: Too Meta for Miranda's World

The formidable Anna Wintour, former *Vogue* editor-in-chief and the undeniable inspiration behind Meryl Streep's Miranda Priestly, was almost in the sequel. Director David Frankel confirmed Wintour visited the set and filmed a take, but her scene didn't make it into the final version. Frankel explained that including Wintour felt "too meta" for the film's parallel world of *Runway* and Miranda Priestly, believing it would be distracting. Another source noted that Wintour "jumped her cue," leading to an out-of-focus shot, and Frankel didn't dare ask for a second take.

### Adrian Grenier's Nate Left in 2006

Though he didn't film any scenes for the sequel, Adrian Grenier's absence as Andy's former boyfriend, Nate, is certainly notable. Director David Frankel revealed that while there were plans for a cameo, scheduling conflicts made it impossible to include him in the production. Grenier himself addressed being left out in a recent Starbucks ad, where he humorously acknowledged Nate's villainous reputation among fans. In the ad, he suggested, "So let's leave Nate in 2006 and keep this good energy going!".
