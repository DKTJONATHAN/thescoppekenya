The Social Health Authority, which replaced the National Health Insurance Fund in Kenya, is struggling with persistent system failures and intense scrutiny regarding a Sh104 billion tender. Critics argue that technical incompetence and potential financial mismanagement are currently undermining the national healthcare transition.

## System Failures and Transparency Concerns Hit Sh104 Billion SHA Tech Contract

### The Digital Mirage of Universal Care
Government officials continue to tout high registration figures as proof of a successful transition to the Social Health Authority. However, these statistics stand in stark contrast to the experience of patients at hospital reception desks. Many citizens find themselves stranded and unable to access services because the new digital portal frequently fails to verify their coverage status. While the government frames the platform as a modern solution for universal healthcare, the operational reality leaves vulnerable patients effectively locked out of the medical services they are legally required to fund.

### The Sh104 Billion Tender Controversy
The massive Sh104 billion technology contract awarded to a Safaricom-led consortium is facing growing skepticism due to significant financial irregularities. Financial analysts have observed that the volume of health claims processed by the authority is vastly outpacing the actual revenue collected through contributions. This widening deficit has sparked allegations that the digital transition serves as a cover for systematic looting. Critics suggest that the chaotic rollout is not merely a technical oversight but a calculated mechanism designed to siphon public funds to well-connected political figures under the guise of modernization.

### Fraudulent Accreditations and Ghost Claims
Concerns regarding the integrity of the system extend to the accreditation process for healthcare providers. Evidence suggests that numerous medical facilities have been granted approval to process claims despite failing to meet basic infrastructure requirements. This environment creates a prime opportunity for fraudulent billing, where phantom operations or unregistered entities can file claims for services never rendered. As these questionable claims continue to drain the fund, the authority remains unable to stabilize its operations, leaving taxpayers to bear the financial burden while actual patients are forced to pay for their medical expenses out of pocket.
