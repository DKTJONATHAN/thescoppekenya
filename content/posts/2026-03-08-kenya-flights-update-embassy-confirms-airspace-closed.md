## Kenya Flights Update: Qatar Airspace Closure Confirmed The Kenyan embassy in Qatar confirmed temporary flight suspensions to Kenya, citing Qatari airspace closure amid Middle East tensions. Safety advisories were issued for citizens in Qatar. However, Qatar Airways signaled limited inbound flights to Hamad International Airport on March 9, 2026, from various international cities, with repatriation flights operating today, March 8, 2026.

Embassy Confirms Airspace Closure and Issues Advisory The Kenyan embassy in Qatar has responded to inquiries regarding flight resumptions, confirming that air operations are temporarily suspended. An advisory released on Saturday, March 7, 2026, attributed the halt in light operations to the closure of Qatar's airspace, following directives from the Qatar Aviation Authority. The embassy reassured the Kenyan diaspora of their safety and provided guidance for emergency situations, advising individuals to immediately move to a safe location inside a building and stay away from windows and glass surfaces upon receiving an alert. Kenyan citizens in Qatar are urged to register their details via an online platform to help officials maintain contact with the diaspora community.

Government Stance on Casualties and Repatriation Days earlier, the Kenyan government confirmed that no Kenyan nationals had sustained casualties or injuries since the conflict began in the region. Most citizens are continuing with their daily routines, including work and studies, depending on the prevailing security situation. The government also stated that Kenyans wishing to return home should make appropriate arrangements through commercial airlines or licensed travel agents, where travel conditions remain safe, as efforts to repatriate those trapped in the Middle East intensify. This situation comes amidst broader national concerns, including demands for answers after reports of an RSF financier holding a Kenyan passport.

Hope Emerges for Limited Flight Resumption Despite the ongoing suspension of scheduled flights, a prospect for resuming travel has emerged. Qatar Airways announced that while its scheduled flight operations remain temporarily suspended due to the closure of Qatari airspace, it is operating a limited flight corridor. The airline operated repatriation flights today, March 8, 2026, departing Hamad International Airport for Amsterdam, Berlin, Frankfurt, London, and Zurich, prioritizing stranded passengers with families, elderly individuals, and those with urgent medical or compassionate travel needs. Furthermore, Qatar Airways intends to operate inbound flights to Hamad International Airport on March 9, 2026, from Amsterdam, Berlin, Frankfurt, London Heathrow, Zurich, and Muscat. These flights are exclusively for passengers whose final destination is Doha and do not represent a full resumption of scheduled commercial operations. Passengers are advised to refrain from traveling to the airport unless they hold a valid, confirmed ticket for travel.
