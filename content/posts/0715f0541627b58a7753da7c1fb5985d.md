Date: Sunday, March 01, 2026 Headline: Kenya's Elite Coffees Secure Top Honours at Nairobi Taste of Harvest 2026, Bolstering Global Standing **NAIROBI, Kenya** – Kenyan coffee has once again affirmed its prestigious position on the global stage, with its producers celebrating a significant quality achievement at the recently concluded Taste of Harvest Coffee Quality Competition held in Nairobi. This triumph unequivocally cements Kenya's reputation as a premier source of world-class specialty coffee. The national competition, meticulously organized by the African Fine Coffees Association (AFCA) in collaboration with the Agriculture and Food Authority (AFA), witnessed a substantial increase in participation. A total of 100 coffee samples were submitted for rigorous evaluation by panels of local and international judges, a marked rise from the 56 samples recorded last year. This surge in submissions is largely attributable to AFA's strategic decision to waive the USD 300 entry fee, thereby encouraging broader participation from coffee estates and societies across the nation's diverse coffee-growing regions. Ten exemplary samples were distinguished across four distinct categories. Kofinaf Co. Ltd earned top honours in the Washed category, while Gachiku Estate led the Naturals category. Ngacha Estate excelled in the Honey Processed category, and Mt Elgon Slopes Coffee achieved first place in the Experimental category. These winning coffees are now poised to represent Kenya at the forthcoming regional Taste of Harvest competition scheduled for early February in Addis Ababa, Ethiopia, where they will compete against the finest coffees from other African producing countries. Addressing a celebratory breakfast event for the triumphant producers and key stakeholders within the coffee sector, AFA Director General Dr. Bruno Linyiru commended the producers whose coffees exhibited remarkable complexity, clarity, and balance. "The outstanding quality showcased by Kenyan coffees in this year's competition highlights not only the exceptional skill of our farmers but also underscores the inherent value of quality-driven production in securing premium markets globally," Dr. Linyiru stated. **Understanding the Taste of Harvest Competition** The Taste of Harvest competition is an annual coffee quality assessment organized by AFCA across its member countries throughout Africa. According to AFCA, the program's core objective is to identify, celebrate, and actively promote Africa's best coffees, while simultaneously forging direct links between producers and specialty buyers. Following national competitions in participating countries, winning coffees progress to regional and subsequent continental platforms. All coffees undergo evaluation using the Specialty Coffee Association (SCA) scoring system, a methodology that ensures transparency, consistency, and international credibility. Beyond mere recognition, winning lots receive prominent showcasing at major coffee events, trade fairs, and online auctions, providing farmers and estates invaluable exposure to premium markets willing to compensate higher prices for superior quality. **Implications for Kenyan Coffee Farmers** For Kenyan coffee farmers, success at the Taste of Harvest transcends the acquisition of trophies. It generates direct market visibility, significantly strengthens their bargaining power, and actively encourages a paradigm shift from volume-based production to quality-driven farming practices. This transition holds the potential to substantially improve farmer incomes. Furthermore, the competition fosters innovation in critical areas such as processing methods, traceability, and post-harvest handling, aspects increasingly prioritized by international buyers. By rewarding excellence, the competition actively assists farmers in aligning their practices with global specialty standards, all while diligently preserving Kenya's distinctive coffee profile. As Kenya continues its concerted efforts to revitalize its vital coffee industry, the stellar performance at the Taste of Harvest Coffee Quality Competition serves as a powerful signal of both confidence and substantial opportunity. With sustained support, enhanced market access, and targeted quality incentives, Kenyan coffee producers are exceptionally well-positioned to strengthen their presence in global markets and secure more equitable returns for their revered coffee. **By Timothy Muli** *Agricultural News Reporter*
