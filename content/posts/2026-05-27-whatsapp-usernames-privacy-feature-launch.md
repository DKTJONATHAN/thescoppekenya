## WhatsApp handles to replace phone numbers in privacy push

WhatsApp is rolling out a feature in its beta versions that allows users to create unique usernames, meaning you will soon be able to chat with people without giving them your private phone number. This update is currently being tested across Android, iOS, and desktop platforms. It marks a shift in how the app handles identity, moving away from the requirement that every contact must have your mobile digits to start a conversation. For many users, this change is not just a technical update but a necessary shield against the frequent privacy intrusions that happen in large digital spaces.

### Why is this happening now for Kenyan users?

The move addresses a long-standing security gap that many in Kenya experience daily. We are a nation of groups. Whether it is a family Chama, a residential estate forum, or a school parents group, the moment you are added to a large group, your phone number becomes visible to everyone else in that list. This has often led to "contact harvesting" where scammers or aggressive marketers collect numbers from public or semi-public groups.

By introducing usernames, the platform is giving you a choice. If you are in a group with 500 people, they will see your chosen handle rather than your mobile number. This level of anonymity is similar to how Telegram or X operates. It ensures that a stranger who happens to be in the same church or office group as you cannot simply save your number and start sending unsolicited messages or making "Flashing" calls at odd hours.

### How will the new username system work?

The system is designed to be straightforward. Users can navigate to their profile settings to select a unique handle. These usernames must be between 3 and 35 characters long. They are unique, meaning once you claim a specific name, no one else can take it. This is why the early rollout in beta is so important, as it allows users to secure their preferred identity before the global release.

Once you have a handle, you can share it with others. If a person wants to add you, they simply search for your username. The app then initiates a chat without ever revealing the underlying SIM card number. This is particularly useful for business transactions. If you are buying something from a seller on a social media marketplace, you can now coordinate the delivery via a handle rather than giving a stranger your primary contact info. This fits into a broader trend of securing digital identities, similar to how the Kenya Revenue Authority monitors offshore accounts through global financial frameworks to ensure transparency. You can read more on how [KRA monitors offshore accounts](https://zandani.co.ke/article/2026-04-19-kenya-revenue-authority-monitors-offshore-accounts-through-) to see how digital footprints are becoming more regulated.

### Is there a risk of people stealing your name?

Impersonation is a valid concern whenever a platform moves to a username-based system. To counter this, Meta is looking to integrate the handles with its Accounts Centre. This would provide a layer of verification, ensuring that official businesses or public figures can be distinguished from parody or scam accounts. In a local context, this could help reduce the "Man-in-the-Middle" scams where someone pretends to be a group admin or a local leader to solicit funds.

The feature is still optional. If you prefer to keep using your phone number as your primary identifier with close friends and family, that remains an option. The handle simply acts as a front-facing mask for your more sensitive data. It allows for a more controlled way of interacting with the wider internet without the fear that your personal number will end up on a marketing database or in the hands of a stalker.
