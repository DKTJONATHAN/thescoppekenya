## Record KSh 17.58 Trillion GDP Signals Kenya's Economic Transformation

Kenya's economy achieved a nominal value of KSh 17.58 trillion in 2025, a record high according to the Economic Survey 2026. Significant shifts are evident, with agriculture strengthening its dominance and the financial sector driving growth, while manufacturing's contribution declined, marking a transformed economic landscape.

### Shifting Economic Pillars

The Kenyan economy's valuation at KSh 17.58 trillion in 2025, as reported by the Kenya National Bureau of Statistics (KNBS) last week, represents its largest nominal value on record. This milestone coincides with notable shifts in the economy's composition over the past 16 years. Agriculture, forestry, and fishing now stand as the largest sector, valued at KSh 4.07 trillion and accounting for 23.2% of total GDP in 2025. This marks a significant increase from 16.3% in 2009 and 18.3% in 2014, reaching the highest share in the current data series. The sector recorded a 3.1% real growth in 2025, its third consecutive year of positive performance after contractions in 2021 and 2022.

In contrast, manufacturing experienced the most substantial compositional shift, declining from 11.5% of GDP in 2009 to 7.1% in 2025. Real estate's contribution eased from 9.8% to 8.2%, and education from 5.5% to 3.8%. Against these declines, financial and insurance activities grew from 5.8% to 8.3%, while accommodation and food services demonstrated a strong recovery, rising from a Covid-19 low of 0.7% to 1.7% of GDP.

### Sectoral Contributions and Growth Drivers

Transportation and storage emerged as the second largest sector, generating KSh 2.08 trillion, or 11.8% of GDP. The sector posted 3.7% real growth, bolstered by increased road and railway activity, with freight transported via the Standard Gauge Railway increasing from 6,531.0 thousand metric tonnes in 2024 to 7,332.0 thousand metric tonnes in 2025. Air transport was an exception, contracting 43.7% and impacting overall sector performance.

Financial and insurance activities contributed KSh 1.46 trillion, 8.3% of GDP, and were the leading source of overall GDP growth in 2025, accounting for 13.8% of total expansion. Real Gross Value Added (GVA) in the sector grew 6.5%, propelled by a 12.9% growth in the insurance sub-sector and a 25.5% rise in net premium income from life insurance to KSh 222.0 billion. Real estate contributed KSh 1.43 trillion (8.2% of GDP) with 3.9% real growth, while wholesale and retail trade generated KSh 1.38 trillion (7.8% of GDP), expanding 3.6%.

Manufacturing accounted for KSh 1.25 trillion (7.1% of GDP), registering a mere 2.0% real growth, its slowest pace in the current series. This was primarily constrained by a 24.8% decline in sugar production and slower soft drinks output.

### Emerging Sectors and Performance Highlights

The construction sector rebounded significantly, contributing KSh 1.14 trillion (6.5% of GDP) with a 6.8% real growth, following a 0.7% contraction in 2024. This recovery was underpinned by a 20.3% increase in cement consumption, a 50.7% surge in imported iron and steel volumes, and a substantial jump in residential housing completions by the State Department for Housing, from 1,655 units in 2024 to 6,738 units in 2025.

Public administration and defense generated KSh 858.8 billion (4.9% of GDP) with 8.3% growth. Education contributed KSh 662.7 billion (3.8% of GDP), growing 2.8%. Information and communication activities generated KSh 404.7 billion (2.3% of GDP), expanding 4.8%. However, the value of mobile money transactions declined from KSh 8,698.0 billion in 2024 to KSh 8,236.5 billion in 2025. Despite this, the consumer electronics market continues to see dynamic activity, with recent introductions such as the [vivo Y31d featuring a 7200mAh battery](https://zandani.co.ke/article/vivo-y31d-launches-in-kenya-7200mah-battery), indicating persistent demand within the broader technology sector.

Human health and social work contributed KSh 307.8 billion (1.8% of GDP), growing 5.5%. Accommodation and food services generated KSh 303.7 billion (1.7% of GDP) and posted the fastest real growth among all sectors at 15.6%, driven by a 6.1% rise in international and regional visitor arrivals. Mining and quarrying, though a smaller sector at KSh 141.7 billion (0.8% of GDP), recorded the second fastest real growth at 14.9%, recovering from a 7.8% contraction in 2024. Electricity supply contributed KSh 250.3 billion (1.4% of GDP), growing 4.8%, with geothermal generation accounting for 44.8% of total electricity output.
