## Safaricom My OneApp Rollout Exposes Operational Faults Safaricom has issued an apology for the problematic My OneApp rollout, acknowledging widespread user dissatisfaction. Key issues include login failures and performance lags, especially for diaspora customers due to a SIM-based activation requirement, prompting concerns about user trust and operational reliability.

Consolidating Digital Services My OneApp was designed to consolidate Safaricom's M-PESA payments and MySafaricom account management tools into a single platform. The strategic goal was to streamline user experience through fewer applications, simpler transaction flows, and enhanced security. This initiative aimed to unify digital services for millions of users, defending Safaricom's fintech dominance.

Core Technical Hurdles The rollout, however, exposed significant operational challenges. Users reported widespread login failures, frozen transfers, sluggish performance, and unexpected logouts. These core issues impacted millions who depend on the M-PESA ecosystem for daily financial transactions, raising questions about the app's initial stability. The company admitted that the experience "fell short" for many.

Diaspora Exclusion and Activation Protocol A major point of contention arose from the My OneApp's requirement for a Safaricom SIM card with active mobile data to complete first-time activation. This protocol disproportionately affected Kenyans abroad, including roaming and diaspora customers, who found themselves locked out of the app. Safaricom states this SIM-based activation is a fraud prevention measure, intended to verify the SIM's physical presence in the device and counter SIM-swap attacks. While theoretically sound, this measure inadvertently excluded a segment of legitimate users unable to meet the conditions from their international locations.

Unanswered Questions on Resolution Safaricom has assured customers that its teams are "working around the clock" to resolve the identified issues, with a stated goal of achieving seamless access for all users. However, the apology notably omits a concrete timeline for resolution. There is no commitment regarding when the diaspora login problems will be fixed, no specific details on app changes, and no clarification on whether activation requirements will be relaxed or redesigned. This lack of transparency impacts user confidence and the perception of corporate accountability. The integrity of a company's operations, particularly in critical service sectors, is vital for maintaining public trust and ensuring market stability. Operational failures, even technical ones, can erode confidence and reflect on a company's broader adherence to business standards, underscoring the importance of consistent compliance for all entities, from startups to established corporations, to avoid significant regulatory or public confidence setbacks, as occasionally seen when the Deputy Registrar strikes off over 1,300 companies from the official register.

User Options and Mitigation For users experiencing persistent issues, particularly Android users, there is an option to revert to the older standalone M-PESA app (version 3.5.9). This requires uninstalling My OneApp completely and disabling automatic updates to prevent re-migration. However, iPhone users do not have this recourse due to Apple's restrictions on sideloading applications. The continued availability of the older MySafaricom App suggests that the full migration process remains ongoing rather than complete.
